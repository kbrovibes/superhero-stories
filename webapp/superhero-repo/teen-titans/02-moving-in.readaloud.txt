Okay — so imagine you just moved into a giant tower shaped like the letter T, with twelve whole floors and a couch the size of a small boat. Sounds incredible, right? Now imagine doing it with four other people who have VERY different ideas about how to set up a home.

That's exactly what the Teen Titans had to figure out!

Victor Stone — Cyborg — took one look at the security system and said, "This is adequate." Then he unplugged it and started over from scratch. BZZT. CLICK. WHIRR. He worked for two entire days, adding biometric locks to every door and an emergency beacon that could reach every communicator in Jump City. When he finished, he crossed his arms and said, "NOW we're safe." And you know what? He was absolutely right.

Starfire turned her room into a gallery of Earth discoveries. She had photographed everything that surprised her since arriving on this planet — bus stops, grocery stores, fire hydrants, a small dog in a sweater — and she hung them all on her walls with tape. She also filled a windowsill with little potted plants. The plants had never been twelve stories up before and looked slightly confused about it.

Raven chose the room at the very TOP of the tower. She stacked her books carefully — there were SO many books — and placed one candle on the windowsill. Every morning she lit it and sat quietly. The others learned very quickly: knock before you open Raven's door. Always.

Robin spent his first night drawing a complete map of the tower — every exit, every corner, every angle of every window. Then he set up a mission room on the fourth floor with maps of Jump City and a whiteboard already covered in notes. Robin did not think this was obsessive. He thought it was being prepared. (Both things were a little true.)

Beast Boy lost his room key on the first day. He borrowed a screwdriver, started fixing the lock — and halfway through, he accidentally turned into a golden retriever. WOOF! He sat there looking very surprised about it. Cyborg made him a new key with a tiny green paw-print tag. "So you can find it," Cyborg said. "I always find it," said Beast Boy. "You literally just lost it," said Cyborg.

By the end of that first week, the tower was full of music and cooking smells and stickers shaped like little green animals under the kitchen table. Raven's candle glowed every morning. Cyborg hummed while he worked.

Strange, messy, loud. Perfectly, wonderfully home.
