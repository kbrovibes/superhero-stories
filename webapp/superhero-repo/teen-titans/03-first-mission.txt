The alert came at three in the morning. A cargo vessel in Jump City harbor had been seized by a group of hired soldiers working for a smuggler named Trident — a mercenary who moved stolen weapons through the city's shipping lanes. Robin was dressed and at the mission board before the alarm finished sounding.

The Titans moved across the water in under four minutes. Robin's plan was simple: split the ship into zones, clear each one, meet on the bridge. Clean, fast, no complications.

The complications began immediately.

Starfire hit the deck of the ship from above, two starbolts flaring green-gold in her palms, and three soldiers scattered backward into the cargo hold. But Trident himself appeared on the upper railing with a weapon that fired sonic pulses, and the first blast shook the whole ship. Cyborg threw up a shield with his forearm and the pulse deflected into the water with a great hiss. "Plan B," he said.

"We don't have a Plan B," Robin said.

"We're making one right now," said Cyborg, and he fired his sonic cannon back across the deck.

Beast Boy turned into a whale — briefly, and in the wrong place, which nearly capsized a smaller boat nearby — and then corrected to a gorilla, which was more useful. He and Cyborg worked together without needing to speak: Cyborg drove Trident's soldiers toward the bow while Beast Boy, still a gorilla, blocked every retreat. It was the first time the two of them had functioned as a pair rather than two separate people, and they both felt it. Something clicked.

Below decks, Raven found the crew locked in the cargo hold. She pressed her fingers against the bolted door and her dark energy — silent and controlled — unthreaded the metal lock from the inside. The crew of eleven came out coughing into the night air. "You're safe," Raven told them, in her quiet way. She said it once. It was enough.

Robin caught Trident on the bridge in the dark, matched him move for move across the cramped space, and pressed his bo staff against Trident's wrists until the sonic weapon clattered to the floor. It took forty seconds. Robin had counted.

On the dock, afterward, they stood in the early morning mist with a villain in handcuffs and eleven rescued crew members. Nobody said anything for a moment.

"That worked," Beast Boy said.

"Parts of it worked," Robin said.

Starfire smiled at both of them. "All the important parts," she said.

Cyborg nodded once. Raven almost smiled. Jump City's lights shimmered in the water around them, and the Teen Titans walked home in the quiet before dawn.
