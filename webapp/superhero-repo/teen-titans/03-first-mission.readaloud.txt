Three in the morning. Pitch dark. The alarm goes off inside Titans Tower — BWAAAAAAMP — and Robin is dressed and at the mission board before the sound even finishes.

Ready? Let's go!

Jump City harbor. A cargo ship called the Sea Arrow has been taken over by soldiers working for a mercenary named Trident, who uses stolen weapons and controls Jump City's shipping lanes. Robin has a plan: split the ship into zones, clear each one, meet on the bridge. Simple.

The plan lasts about forty-five seconds.

Starfire drops from the sky in a flash of gold and green — WHOOSH — and her starbolts scatter three soldiers right back into the cargo hold. But then Trident appears on the upper railing with a sonic pulse cannon — BWHOOOM — and the whole ship SHUDDERS. Cyborg snaps his arm into shield mode and the blast deflects into the harbor with a great wet HISSSSS.

"Plan B!" Cyborg shouts.

"There is no Plan B!" Robin shouts back.

"We're making one right now!" And Cyborg fires his sonic cannon — KRAKOOM — across the ship deck.

Beast Boy turns into a gorilla. Not immediately — first he tried a whale, which was a TERRIBLE idea in the middle of a boat, and nearly tipped over a smaller vessel nearby. BLOOP. But gorilla worked! He and Cyborg moved together like they'd rehearsed it: Cyborg pushing soldiers toward the bow, Beast Boy blocking every escape. They didn't even need to talk. Something had clicked.

Below deck, Raven found the ship's crew locked in the cargo hold. She pressed her fingertips to the metal door — CLICK, hum, soft glow — and the bolt came apart from the inside. Eleven crew members stumbled out into the sea air. "You're safe," Raven said simply. She said it once. It was enough.

And Robin? Robin was already on the bridge. Forty dark seconds with Trident. Bo staff versus sonic cannon. CRACK — CLANG — SWOOSH — and the cannon skittered across the floor. Robin had it counted.

On the dock, afterward, they stood in the pre-dawn mist. One villain in handcuffs. Eleven rescued sailors. Five tired heroes.

"That worked," Beast Boy said.

"Parts of it worked," Robin said.

Starfire smiled at both of them. "All the important parts worked," she said.

Cyborg nodded. Raven almost smiled — and for Raven, that was a standing ovation.

The Titans walked home across the harbor bridge as the first pink light touched the sky over Jump City. Mission one: done.
