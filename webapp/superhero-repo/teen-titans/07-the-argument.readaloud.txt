Here is something important about teams: sometimes the hardest part isn't the villain. Sometimes the hardest part is the argument before you fight the villain.

The Titans had one of those on a Thursday evening because of a criminal named Overload.

Overload was unusual — he could actually merge himself with electricity and inhabit power systems like a ghost living in the walls. He was attacking Jump City's power grid, block by block, growing stronger every time he absorbed another surge of current.

Robin wanted to go immediately. NOW. Hit hard, hit fast.

Cyborg said: no. Walk directly toward Overload and you walk into his power source. He'll absorb everything we throw at him. We need to cut the electricity from each city block first — force him to retreat to smaller and smaller reserves until he's got nowhere left to go.

Robin said that would take too long.

Cyborg said it was the correct answer anyway.

Back and forth. Twenty minutes. Getting louder.

Then Starfire said, very quietly: "While we argue, Overload is absorbing the power plant on Fifth Street."

SILENCE.

They went out — still tense, still frustrated — and the mission was rough. Robin went in first and Overload absorbed two of his explosive discs and GREW. Raven's barriers held the electrical surges back but not forever. Cyborg tried cutting nearby power junctions while Beast Boy watched from above as a bird.

Then Starfire landed in the middle of all of them and did something brilliant.

"Robin," she said. "Cyborg was correct about the method. Cyborg — Robin was correct that we could not wait. Both things are true at the same time."

Everyone stopped.

"So," she continued, "Robin keeps Overload occupied on the street. Cyborg cuts the grid from the rooftops. Yes?"

Yes.

CRACK — Robin hit Overload hard, fast, brilliant, making himself impossible to ignore. Overload chased him through three blocks while Cyborg moved above — ZAP, ZAP, ZAP — cutting the power junction by junction. Beast Boy called out Overload's position from the air. Raven held the line behind Robin.

And Overload shrank. Block by block. Smaller and smaller, retreating toward his last electrical reserve. When he hit a single transformer housing, Cyborg dropped a portable Faraday cage over it — click, locked, done. He had prepared it before the mission. Of course he had.

Overload: contained. Mission: complete.

Walking home across the bridge, Robin said, "Your plan was right."

Cyborg thought for a second. "Your urgency got us there before the power plant collapsed," he said. "We needed both."

Both of them right. Both of them needed. That's what a real team sounds like.
