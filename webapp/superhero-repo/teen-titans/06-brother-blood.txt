Brother Blood had taken over the H.I.V.E. Academy on the outskirts of Jump City — a school for young villains, run with cold discipline and a disturbing amount of mind-control technology. He was using it to build an army, and he had set his sights on the one thing that would complete it: Cyborg's blueprints.

Victor Stone's technology was unlike anything else on Earth. His sonic systems, his interface hardware, his titanium exostructure — if Brother Blood could replicate even part of it across fifty students, he would have a force that no single hero could stop. He had tried three times to steal the data remotely. He decided he needed to take Cyborg himself.

The kidnapping happened at a Jump City robotics exhibition — Brother Blood's agents were dressed as organizers and they moved fast. Cyborg was separated from the team in the crowd before any of them knew what was happening. By the time Robin understood, Victor was gone.

Finding the H.I.V.E. Academy took six hours. Getting inside took thirty minutes of planning and a lot of arguing. The problem was Brother Blood's mind-control frequency: it operated on a specific electromagnetic band that disrupted human neural signals. It worked on anyone with an organic brain. Raven could counter it with her empathic shielding, but only for herself.

Cyborg, it turned out, was immune. His neural architecture was partly synthetic — Brother Blood's signal simply passed through the tech portions without catching. He had been playing along, pretending to be affected, and while the academy believed he was under control, Cyborg had spent twelve hours cataloging its security layout from the inside.

The Titans went in as a single coordinated group. Raven shielded Starfire from the mind-control frequency while Beast Boy moved through the academy's ventilation shafts as a ferret, cutting power to each security junction as he went. Robin broke through the Academy's front gate with the full force of twenty students' worth of attention and held it there — precise, tactical, impossible to ignore.

Cyborg walked out to meet them from inside, carrying Brother Blood's control console under his arm. "I've had it for four hours," he said. "I was waiting for backup."

Brother Blood was arrested in his own command chair. His academy students, freed from the signal, were confused and relieved and sent home. The Jump City police took six hours to process the paperwork.

In the T-car on the way home, Beast Boy said: "Cyborg, you were never actually mind-controlled, were you?"

"Nope," said Cyborg.

"You outsmarted Brother Blood from the inside."

"Yep."

"That," said Starfire, "is exceedingly impressive."

Cyborg smiled — a real one, wide and warm. "I know my own hardware," he said.
