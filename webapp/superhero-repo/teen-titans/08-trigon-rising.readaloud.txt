Raven never talked about her father. The others had learned that in their first months together — not because Raven hid it exactly, but because the subject was sealed tight, like a locked door in a room she still needed to stand in front of every day.

So when the sky above Jump City turned deep red and enormous four-eyed shapes appeared in the clouds looking DOWN at the city — the Titans understood immediately that this was different from anything they had faced before.

BOOM. Buildings warped. Streets twisted. Shadow-creatures — dark, formless shapes — clawed at walls and windows all across the city.

Raven stood on the roof of Titans Tower, eyes closed, absolutely still, her dark energy blazing violet against the red. She was fighting from inside. Her father, a being called Trigon who existed in another dimension, was using her connection to this world as a doorway. When she reached for her power to resist, the connection grew stronger. It was like trying to push a door shut while something pulled it from the other side using your own arm.

"I need forty minutes," she said. "Keep the city from falling apart."

The Titans didn't ask a single question.

WHOOOOSH — Starfire shot into the warped red sky over Jump City, her starbolts blazing gold against the dark, scattering shadow-creatures from every building she passed. She went back and forth, back and forth — nonstop, relentless, a golden light that would not let the dark win.

KRAKOOM — Cyborg broadcast counter-signals on every frequency he could produce, slowing the dimensional warping block by block across the city's grid.

Beast Boy went straight to gorilla — no whale this time, he had learned — and physically dismantled a shadow structure collapsing onto the waterfront, which involved a lot of impressive CRUNCH sounds.

Robin stood at ground level directing all three of them — reading Trigon's pattern in the distortions, routing each Titan to the next critical point. THERE — Starfire. THAT BLOCK — Cyborg. WATERFRONT — Beast Boy.

Forty minutes.

The roof of Titans Tower blazed with Raven's power, violet and absolute, steady and unbreaking.

And then — like a door slamming shut from the inside — the red drained from the sky. The shadow-creatures dissolved. Trigon's four eyes blinked out, one by one, and the clouds above Jump City were just clouds again.

Raven sat down on the roof. The others came up and sat beside her. Nobody spoke for a while.

"I'm sorry," Raven said. "That I never told you."

"You told us what mattered," Cyborg said. "You said you needed time. We gave it to you."

Starfire sat close. Beast Boy said nothing — which, from him, meant everything.

That's what they were for. All five of them, together.
