The letter arrived at four different addresses on the same Tuesday morning. Each one was written by Dick Grayson, a young man who had trained under Batman in Gotham City and now went by the name Robin. The letter was short and direct: Jump City needs us. Meet at the harbor. Come ready.

Robin had fought crime alone for months on the California coast, but he had started to see what one person working alone could not do. When Slade — the masked mercenary who called himself the greatest strategist alive — had nearly cornered him in the warehouse district, Robin realized he needed allies, not just skills.

Koriand'r arrived first, descending from the sky in a warm golden glow. She had traveled from the planet Tamaran and introduced herself with a handshake that launched Robin off his feet. Her people greeted strangers that way. She was smiling before she landed, her long red hair streaming behind her, and she called herself Starfire. She wanted to know everything about Earth, especially pizza.

Victor Stone drove himself, because no public bus could accommodate someone half-covered in titanium armor. His right arm was a sonic cannon. A red optical sensor replaced one eye. Steel plating covered his chest. He crossed his arms and said, "So where's this tower?" Cyborg was not easy to impress. But he was there.

Raven appeared without any noise at all, stepping out of a shadow that seemed darker than the others. Her blue cloak rippled though there was no wind. She said exactly five words: "Let's get this over with." Behind those quiet eyes lived an empathic power that could sense every emotion in the room and move objects with a thought.

Beast Boy arrived last, a green teenager who had already transformed into a pelican to scout the harbor from above. He landed, turned back into a boy, and told a joke so terrible that even Starfire winced. Garfield Logan laughed anyway.

They stood together on the dock and looked at the tall island in the harbor — and the enormous steel tower shaped like the letter T.

"That's ours," Robin said.

It was a strange collection. One human acrobat with a utility belt. One alien princess who threw golden starbolts. One empath wrapped in dark calm. One half-machine teenager still finding his footing. One shapeshifter who processed hard feelings by turning into a duck.

Not a team yet. But Titans Tower was waiting, and Jump City needed every one of them.
