Imagine getting a letter in the mail that says: "Jump City needs us. Come ready." Would you go? Well, FOUR young heroes got that exact letter — and the world was never quite the same after that Tuesday morning.

Dick Grayson — the Boy Wonder himself, Robin — had been fighting crime alone on the California coast for months. He was fast. He was clever. He had Batman's training and his own iron will. But a masked villain named Slade had nearly outsmarted him in the warehouse district, and Robin was smart enough to know what that meant: he needed a team.

So he wrote the letters. And then he waited at the harbor.

First to arrive: Koriand'r, better known as Starfire! She ZOOMED down from the sky in a burst of warm golden light, her long red hair streaming behind her, her smile enormous and honest and bright. She reached out to shake Robin's hand so enthusiastically that — WHAM — he flew backward three full steps. On her planet Tamaran, that was simply how you greeted a new friend. She apologized immediately. Then she asked if there was pizza.

Then came Victor Stone — CYBORG! Half human, half incredible machine, all attitude. His right arm was a sonic cannon that could rattle windows. His chest plate was reinforced titanium. One eye glowed red like a scanner, tracking everything. He drove up to the dock, stepped out, crossed his arms and said, "So where's this tower?" Cool as a refrigerator.

Raven materialized from a patch of shadow without any sound at all. No footsteps. No creak. Her dark blue cloak hung perfectly still even though a breeze was blowing. She looked at everyone with careful, measured eyes and said — very quietly — five words: "Let's get this over with." WHOOOOSH. That was the chill running down everyone's spine at once.

Then — SPLAT — Beast Boy crash-landed from above! He had been a pelican, scouting the harbor from the air, and the landing was not his best work. Garfield Logan popped back into his green human shape, brushed himself off, and immediately told a joke so spectacularly bad that even Starfire, who did not yet know all Earth humor, could tell it was terrible. He laughed louder than anyone else in the whole harbor.

Five young heroes stood on the dock and looked out at the island. On top of that island rose an enormous steel tower shaped like a letter T, gleaming in the California sun.

"That's ours," Robin said.

BOOM. Can you feel it? An alien princess with starbolts. A half-machine powerhouse. A calm, quiet empath. A green shapeshifter with terrible jokes. And one acrobat with the best utility belt in Jump City.

Titans Tower was waiting. And the Teen Titans had arrived.
