Has anyone ever tried to steal your homework? Imagine someone trying to steal YOUR ENTIRE BRAIN. That's basically what happened to Cyborg — and what came after is one of the greatest tricks the Teen Titans ever pulled.

Brother Blood ran the H.I.V.E. Academy — a villain school on the outskirts of Jump City. He wanted Cyborg's technology to build an unstoppable army: the sonic cannon, the titanium plating, the processor interface. He tried to steal the data remotely three times and failed. So at a Jump City robotics exhibition, his agents moved through the crowd — SWOOP — and Cyborg was gone before the Titans knew what happened.

Six hours to locate the academy. Then they found the problem: Brother Blood used a mind-control signal that scrambled organic brain signals. Anyone with a normal brain was vulnerable. Raven could shield herself — but only herself.

So: how do you break into a villain school when the villain can turn your brain against you?

Turns out — you send in the one teammate whose brain is half computer.

Brother Blood's signal passed right through Cyborg's synthetic neural components without affecting him at all. Cyborg had known this from the moment he was brought in. He had spent twelve hours pretending to be under Brother Blood's control — acting confused, following orders — while quietly memorizing every security junction, every guard rotation, and every locked door in the entire H.I.V.E. Academy.

He had made a complete map. In his head. In twelve hours.

When the Titans finally came for him, the rescue was perfectly coordinated. Robin stormed the front gate and drew every student's attention his way — CRASH, CLANG, everything very loud and dramatic. Beast Boy slithered through the ventilation shafts as a ferret, cutting power at each security junction he passed — ZAP, ZAP, ZAP — working from Cyborg's map that Robin had relayed through the comms. Raven walked behind Starfire with one hand steady on her shoulder, keeping the mind-control signal from reaching her.

And Cyborg walked out the front doors carrying Brother Blood's control console under his arm.

"I've had this for four hours," he said. "I was waiting for backup."

Robin stared at him.

"You were never actually mind-controlled."

"Nope."

"You outsmarted him from the inside."

"Yep."

Starfire clapped her hands together. "That," she said with great feeling, "is exceedingly impressive."

Cyborg smiled — the real, full, Victor Stone smile. "I know my own hardware," he said simply.

Brother Blood was arrested in his own command chair. The academy students went home. And the Teen Titans rode back to Titans Tower together, very pleased with how Tuesday had turned out.
