The argument started over a villain named Overload — a criminal who could inhabit and supercharge electrical systems — and ended somewhere more honest than that.

Robin had wanted to go after Overload immediately when he began attacking Jump City's power infrastructure. Cyborg had disagreed. Overload operated by merging with electrical fields, which meant he could absorb energy-based attacks and redirect sonic frequency weapons. Walking directly toward Overload was walking into his power source. They needed a different approach: cut the power to each city block before entering it, force Overload to retreat to smaller and smaller electrical reserves.

Robin said that would take too long.

Cyborg said that was the correct answer anyway.

They had been arguing about it for twenty minutes when Starfire quietly said, "While we argue, Overload is eating the power plant on Fifth Street." She said it without drama and it landed like a dropped weight.

The team went out — separately, still frustrated — and the mission was exactly as bad as Cyborg had predicted. Robin went in first and Overload absorbed two of his explosive discs and grew larger. Raven shielded the team with her barriers but couldn't hold the electrical current indefinitely. Cyborg tried cutting power to adjacent blocks while Beast Boy, as a bird, watched from above to track where Overload moved.

Then Starfire landed between all of them.

"Robin," she said. "Cyborg is correct about the method. Cyborg — Robin was correct that we could not wait. Both things are true."

There was a silence.

"So," Starfire continued, "we cut the power from the roof while Robin keeps Overload occupied, yes?"

That was what they did. Cyborg and Beast Boy worked the city's power junctions from above while Robin kept Overload moving and angry on the street below — brilliant and precise and giving Cyborg the time he needed. Overload shrank with each cut block until he was contained in a single transformer housing and Cyborg sealed it with a portable Faraday cage he had brought because he had planned for exactly this.

Overload was in custody by midnight.

Walking back across the bridge, Robin said, "Your plan worked better."

Cyborg was quiet a moment. "Your urgency got us there before the power plant collapsed," he said. "We needed both."

It was not a dramatic reconciliation. It was two people telling each other a specific, accurate truth. On the bridge, under the city lights, that felt like more than enough.
