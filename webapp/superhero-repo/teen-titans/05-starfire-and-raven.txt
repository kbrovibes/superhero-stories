The mission had seemed simple enough: track a shipment of stolen artifacts to a warehouse in Jump City's old theater district and recover them before they left the city. Robin sent Starfire and Raven together — the others were handling two smaller incidents elsewhere — and the two of them crossed the city in silence, which was not unusual for Raven, though Starfire usually filled silences with questions.

She was nervous. Raven knew that without asking, the same way she always knew what the people around her were feeling, because she could sense it — a warm flutter of worry coming off Starfire like summer heat off pavement. She didn't comment on it.

The warehouse was guarded by twelve of Blackfire's hired soldiers. Blackfire — whose real name was Komand'r — was Starfire's older sister from Tamaran, and she was everything Starfire was not: cold, scheming, willing to use their family connection as a weapon. She had sent these soldiers specifically to draw Starfire out. Raven had deduced this from the location and the timing before they even arrived.

Starfire hit the main warehouse doors like a golden comet and the fight was immediate. She was powerful and fast and brave, and she drove every soldier back with her starbolts and her strong Tamaranean hands. But Blackfire stepped out of the shadows at the back of the warehouse and looked at her with a smile that carried no warmth at all. "Little sister," she said. Starfire hesitated — just for a moment — and that moment was a real thing, a visible thing, something that cost her.

Raven moved behind her without sound. She had not stopped since they entered — pulling soldiers away from Starfire's flanks, lifting crates between them and incoming blasts, keeping the space clear for Starfire to fight without being overwhelmed. When Starfire hesitated, Raven stepped forward and let her dark energy expand outward in a controlled wave, pushing Blackfire and three remaining soldiers backward into the far wall.

Blackfire escaped through a side door. She always did. But the artifacts were recovered, the soldiers were down, and Starfire stood in the middle of the warehouse breathing hard.

"I always hesitate when I see her," Starfire said quietly.

"I know," Raven said.

"That is a problem."

"Not always," Raven said. "Sometimes it means you still hope she'll choose differently. That's not a weakness."

Starfire looked at her for a moment. Then she said, "You sensed that."

"I always do," Raven said. "I don't always say it. But I noticed."

They flew home over Jump City's rooftops in the dark, side by side, and for once neither of them needed to fill the quiet.
