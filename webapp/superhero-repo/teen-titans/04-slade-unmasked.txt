Slade had been watching the Titans since before they moved into the tower. That was the part that unsettled Robin most — not the ambushes, not the traps, but the patience. Slade waited the way a spider waited: still, invisible, and absolutely certain.

The first serious encounter came on a Tuesday afternoon when Jump City's power grid began failing in sections. Each outage happened in a perfect geometric pattern, as if someone was drawing a shape across the city one blackout at a time. Robin recognized the shape immediately: it was a trap with the tower at the center.

Slade had planted three robotic sentinels in the subway tunnels beneath Jump City — large machines covered in his orange-and-black insignia, each one capable of disrupting power and communications within a half-mile radius. When the Titans split to investigate, which Slade had predicted they would do, the sentinels activated and began driving the team apart. One by one, their communicators went dark.

Cyborg was the first to engage a sentinel in the East Side tunnels, and he found it immediately troubling — the machine's armor matched frequencies with his sonic cannon, absorbing most of the blast. He switched to physical contact instead, wrestling the machine's arm joints with sheer strength until the motor housing cracked.

Robin faced the second sentinel alone in the waterfront subway station. Slade himself was waiting there too, in the shadows above the platform, watching with his single visible eye behind the mask and saying nothing. Robin fought the machine, sharp and precise and angry — and then realized partway through that the sentinel wasn't trying to destroy him. It was studying him. Every move Robin made, Slade was cataloging.

Starfire and Beast Boy flanked the third sentinel together in the west tunnel. Beast Boy turned into a ram and drove the machine into the wall repeatedly until its outer casing fractured. Starfire's starbolts finished it.

When the team reconnected at the tower, the city's power was restored and the sentinels were offline. Slade was gone, leaving only a single orange coin on Robin's windowsill.

Robin stood in the mission room for a long time that evening, looking at the coin. Slade hadn't tried to defeat them. He had tried to learn them.

"He'll come again," Raven said from the doorway.

"I know," Robin said.

"Then we need to know ourselves better than he does," she said, and returned to her room.

Robin put the orange coin in the center of the mission board. He was going to figure out Slade before Slade figured out them.
