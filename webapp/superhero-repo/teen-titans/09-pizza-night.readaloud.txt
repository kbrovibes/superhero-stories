Not every great story is about fighting a villain. Some of the best ones are about Friday night.

This one is about pizza.

After three very busy weeks of missions, Jump City had a quiet Friday evening. And when Beast Boy announced, "Pizza night!" — nobody argued. Not even Raven. That was the clearest sign yet that everyone needed a rest.

Four large pizzas arrived from a place called Chico's, which was Cyborg's minimum acceptable order. Everyone found their usual spot in the common room — which was the thing that felt most like home, because they all had a usual spot now without anyone planning it. Cyborg: left side of the couch, nearest the kitchen. Starfire: upright in the armchair, pizza held at the precise altitude she had decided was best for eating it. Raven: on the floor, book open, one slice on a plate beside her. Beast Boy: draped across the right end of the couch like a green throw pillow with opinions.

Robin ate standing up. They had stopped trying to change this.

Beast Boy had chosen a film about a dog who solved crimes, which was an excellent choice. Starfire found it FASCINATING. She asked forty-seven questions. "Why does the dog distrust the mailman specifically?" "Is the bone a metaphor?" "What is the dog's real name before it became a detective?" Beast Boy answered all of them. His answers were creative. They were not always accurate. Cyborg pointed this out multiple times. Beast Boy said his version was better. Then Raven — without looking up from her book — said, "He's right. It is more interesting." Beast Boy made a triumphant noise. Cyborg protested. Starfire clapped.

The film finished. Nobody started another one. The room went quiet in that comfortable way that means everyone is still there, just resting together.

Then Starfire told them about the Blorthog. It was a Tamaranean festival — a day where Tamaraneans honored their closest companions. You brought gifts. You told them exactly what they meant to you. On Tamaran, it was one of the most important days of the year.

"We should DO that," said Beast Boy immediately.

"The Blorthog is usually in spring," Starfire said. "But I believe the spirit matters more than the season."

A small, easy quiet.

"Thanks for the pizza," Cyborg said to Beast Boy.

"Thanks for fixing my communicator fourteen times," Beast Boy said back.

Raven set her book down. She looked at the room — at all of them, arranged in their usual spots, in their home, in the city they protected together.

"Thanks for staying," she said. She didn't say it to any one person. She said it to all of them.

Outside the tower, Jump City glittered in the warm dark.

That was enough.
