One year. Three hundred and sixty-five days. And Jump City had changed.

Not the buildings or the streets — those were the same. But the way the city FELT had changed, because now five young heroes lived in a T-shaped tower on an island in the harbor, and everyone in Jump City knew it.

Citizens watched for Starfire's gold glow when the nights turned strange. They heard Cyborg's sonic cannon echoing off the water and felt their shoulders relax — the problem was being handled. When something small and fast moved across the rooftops at two in the morning, people glanced up, recognized Robin's silhouette, and went back to sleep. That kind of trust takes a year to earn.

Slade had come back four more times. Of course he had. Each attempt was more intricate than the last, each one defeated — sometimes barely — by the five of them working in tight, practiced, EARNED coordination. Robin stopped thinking of Slade as a puzzle to solve. He started thinking of Slade as a reason to keep getting better. The team got better.

And then one autumn night: a warehouse fire in the Jump City port district. Not a villain. Not a scheme. Just a city emergency, and the Titans were closest.

GO!

Cyborg interfaced with the city's fire suppression system remotely and activated it from SIX BLOCKS AWAY — WHOOOOSH. Starfire made a single pass over the burning storage floor and carried three dockworkers out in her arms — WHOOSH WHOOSH WHOOSH — through the smoke and heat, safe in seconds. Raven held a collapsing section of the roof suspended in mid-air with her dark energy — perfectly still, perfectly steady — while firefighters moved beneath it. Beast Boy became a Saint Bernard and searched the smoke for the last two missing workers — found them both — then became a dolphin in the harbor to keep one of them afloat until the rescue boat arrived. Robin directed the entire evacuation from ground level with calm precision until the fire department had it handled.

Nobody was hurt. Nobody had called them. They came because Jump City was their city.

In the T-car on the way home, Beast Boy said: "Were we always going to become this?"

Cyborg thought about it. "No. I don't think it was obvious."

"I only knew I would try," said Starfire honestly.

Raven looked at the city lights passing outside the window. "I think we became it because we kept showing up."

Robin drove for a full block without saying anything. Then: "Yeah. That's it."

Titans Tower glowed ahead — the letter T against the dark sky, warm and steady, a signal that said: someone is here, and they are watching out for you.

They drove toward it together. They always would.
