There are some villains who shout and boast and announce themselves with dramatic speeches. They wear bright colors. They tell you exactly what they plan to do. You almost have to respect the effort, even if you disagree with the goals.

Then there is Slade. And Slade does none of those things.

Slade waits. Slade watches. Slade plans — for a very, very long time — before he ever acts. Which is actually way more frightening, when you stop and think about it. Silence is more dangerous than shouting, sometimes.

The Titans' first serious encounter with him started quietly. Jump City's power grid began failing — one neighborhood at a time, in a perfect geometric pattern. Blink. Blink. Blink. Robin stared at the map on the mission board and went very still. He recognized the shape. Someone had drawn a target — and Titans Tower was in the middle.

Three robotic sentinels were waiting in the subway tunnels beneath Jump City. Big machines. Orange-and-black armor — Slade's colors. KACHUNK. KACHUNK. They activated the moment the Titans split to investigate, just like Slade knew they would. And one by one — ZZZZT — the communicators went dark.

Cyborg found his sentinel in the East Side tunnels. He fired his sonic cannon — KRAKOOM — and... the machine absorbed it. Adapted to his frequency! That was alarming. Cyborg switched plans fast: he grabbed the sentinel's arm joints with both hands and WRENCHED. Motor housing cracked. Machine down.

Robin's sentinel was waiting in the waterfront station. He fought it — fast, precise, furious — CRACK, FLIP, SLAM. But halfway through, Robin realized something horrible: the machine wasn't trying to beat him. It was watching him. Every move Robin made, it recorded. Up in the shadows above the platform, one eye behind an orange-and-black mask watched too.

Slade.

In the west tunnel, Starfire and Beast Boy took on the third machine together. Beast Boy turned into a ram — BAAAAAA — and drove that sentinel into the wall repeatedly until the casing cracked open. Then Starfire finished it with a starbolt: KRAKOOM. Teamwork!

When the Titans reconnected, the city's power was back. The sentinels were offline. Slade was gone.

But on Robin's windowsill, there was a single orange coin.

Robin stood in the mission room for a long time, staring at that coin. The sentinels hadn't been an attack. They had been a study. Slade was learning them.

"He'll come again," said Raven from the doorway.

"I know," said Robin.

"Then we need to know ourselves better than he does."

Robin placed the orange coin in the center of the mission board.

Game on, Slade.
