You might think that Starfire and Raven would be total opposites. And you'd be right! Starfire is sunshine and curiosity and enormous hugs. Raven is quiet and careful and calm. They barely talk on a normal day.

But here's what makes them a perfect pair.

Robin sent just the two of them across Jump City to recover stolen artifacts from a warehouse in the old theater district. The others were busy elsewhere. So it was Starfire and Raven, crossing the rooftops together in the dark.

Starfire was quiet. That was the first sign something was off.

Raven could feel it — because Raven can sense emotions the way you sense sunlight on your face. She felt a warm flutter of nervousness coming off Starfire, and she understood why. The artifacts had been placed there to lure Starfire out. Because the person who'd stolen them was Blackfire.

Blackfire — real name Komand'r — is Starfire's older sister from the planet Tamaran. And she is everything Starfire is not: cold, calculating, cruel, and absolutely willing to use the fact that they are sisters as a weapon.

The warehouse had twelve guards. CRASH — Starfire hit the door like a golden comet and the fight was on immediately! Her starbolts flared brilliant green-gold. Her Tamaranean strength sent soldiers stumbling backward. She was incredible.

But then from the back of the warehouse, a familiar voice said: "Little sister."

Blackfire stepped from the shadows. Cold eyes. That smile with no warmth in it.

And Starfire — just for one moment — hesitated.

That moment was real. Raven saw it. But Raven had been watching Starfire's back the whole time — keeping soldiers off her flanks, lifting crates between Starfire and incoming blasts with dark energy. WHOOOMP. She stepped forward and sent a dark energy wave crashing outward — Blackfire and three soldiers hit the back wall with a tremendous THUD.

Blackfire escaped through a side door. She always does. But the mission was done. Artifacts recovered. Soldiers down.

Starfire stood in the middle of the warehouse, breathing hard. "I always hesitate when I see her," she said quietly.

"I know," said Raven.

"That is a problem."

"Not always," Raven said. "It means you still hope she'll choose differently. That isn't weakness."

Starfire looked at her. "You sensed that."

"I always sense it. I don't always say it. But I noticed."

They flew home over Jump City side by side — two very different people, perfectly quiet, perfectly together.

Some friendships happen when you're exactly alike. Some happen when someone sees exactly what you carry — and stays anyway.
