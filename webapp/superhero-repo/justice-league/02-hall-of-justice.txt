After the battle in Metropolis, everyone agreed that eight heroes who might be called on again at any moment needed a place to meet. Batman suggested a location, then spent three days arguing with everyone about the details. That was how the Hall of Justice was born.

Bruce Wayne quietly paid for the construction of the Hall, a grand building above the Potomac River in Washington. Its white stone facade rose like a fortress, two great doors facing the reflecting pool, a satellite dish on the roof angled toward the sky. Inside, a round table waited in the center of a broad council chamber, eight chairs placed around it — not one at the head, but all equal. Wonder Woman noticed this immediately and said nothing, because it was correct.

Settling in took longer than the first battle had. Superman and Batman argued about security. Clark thought too many locks would signal distrust; Bruce said too few would get someone killed. They compromised. Aquaman brought a saltwater tank for the lower level, because he thought better with the sound of the ocean nearby. Green Lantern Hal Jordan mounted a star chart and used his ring to add several planets that did not appear on any Earth map.

Cyborg spent two full days wiring the Hall's systems into himself, enabling him to monitor seventeen global threats at once from anywhere in the building. The Martian Manhunter, J'onn J'onzz, read the architecture the way a telepath reads a face — sensing where walls held stress, where circuits ran warm — until the Hall breathed like a living thing. Barry Allen ran every corridor at full speed and declared it mapped. Eleven seconds.

Diana chose a room overlooking the river. She had left Themyscira, the island of the Amazons, years before and had not had a home since. She stood at the window and set her golden tiara on the sill, letting the afternoon light catch it.

At sunset they gathered around the round table for the first time — not to fight anything, just to exist in the same room. Clark brought coffee. J'onn was the last to sit, and when he did, the table felt complete.

"We do not all have the same powers," J'onn said quietly. "But we are bound by the same reason for being here."

No one needed to ask what that reason was. Protecting everyone. That was enough.
