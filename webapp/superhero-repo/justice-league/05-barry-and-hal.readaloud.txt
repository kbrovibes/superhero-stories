Barry Allen organizes his coffee mugs alphabetically. His sock drawer is color-coded. He makes very detailed grocery lists.

Hal Jordan once flew an experimental jet upside down just to see if the radio still worked.

These two became best friends. Here is how.

Batman sent them to Coast City — Hal's home city — to investigate a reading from Brainiac's tech. Brainiac, if you don't know him, is an alien machine-intelligence who travels the universe collecting things. Not stamps. CITIES. He miniaturizes them and puts them in glass bottles. He already has Kandor — a whole Kryptonian city, now tiny, trapped in a bottle on his ship. And he wanted Coast City next.

"Reconnaissance mission," Batman said. "In and out."

NOPE.

Barry and Hal arrive at the power station and find: a shrink-ray device, already active, and THREE of Brainiac's combat probes guarding it. No time to call the team. They look at each other.

"Well," said Hal. "Great."

"After you," said Barry.

Problem: the shrink field destabilizes Barry's speed energy. He can't run near the device without slowing down. So he does the only sensible thing — he runs AWAY from the device and pulls two probes after him like a dog with two very confused cats. WHOOSH WHOOSH WHOOSH — through corridors, around corners, up stairways.

Hal handles the third probe — BAM — energy blast from the ring — and builds a containment bubble around the device to slow the power-up countdown. Then he starts cutting the casing with energy shears. Very carefully. The ring glows. His face is tense. One wrong cut and the field inverts and Coast City gets bottled.

No pressure!

Barry leads both probes into the turbine room and — FWOOOOM — spins a vortex that jams their navigation systems. They slam into walls. He SPRINTS back to Hal just as the containment bubble starts to crack.

They look at the device. They look at each other.

"I have an idea," Barry says. "It's a little weird."

He vibrates at exactly the device's power frequency — BZZZZZT — like a tuning fork. The ring picks up the vibration. Hal's expression goes from worried to extremely focused, and he finds the exact seam — THERE — and cuts.

The device goes dark. No boom. No flash. No shrunken city. Just: dark.

They sit on the walkway. Barry has run downtown and returned with two plates of pancakes. Hal tries the syrup and makes a face. Barry says it is perfect. Hal says Barry has terrible taste. Barry notes that Hal flies planes upside down for fun.

By the time the plates are empty they are both laughing hard.

"Next time," says Hal, "let's bring the whole team."

"Absolutely," says Barry.

They both know this is a lie. And they are both happy about it.
