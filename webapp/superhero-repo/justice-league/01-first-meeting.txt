Nobody sent invitations. Nobody called a meeting. The Justice League began the way most great things begin — because the world needed it.

A vast alien warship appeared above Metropolis, blotting out the sun. From its hull poured hundreds of Parademons, winged soldiers loyal to the tyrant Darkseid of Apokolips. They moved in swarms, their red eyes glowing, searching for something buried beneath the city. Clark Kent heard the screaming first — his super-hearing caught every frightened voice — and Superman was airborne before the newscasters knew what to report. He punched through three Parademons with a single sweep and hovered above the chaos, looking for the source.

A black shape dropped from a Gotham-bound aircraft: Batman, grappling line trailing, landing silently on a rooftop. Bruce Wayne had been tracking strange energy readings for two days. He fired a grapple into the nearest Parademon and brought it crashing to the pavement, then studied it with a small scanner before it could scramble free.

The ocean off Metropolis Harbor erupted. Aquaman rode a wave onto the docks, trident in hand, the sea rising at his command. Green Lantern Hal Jordan appeared a moment later, his power ring spinning an emerald net that scooped a dozen creatures from the air at once. Wonder Woman arrived from the skies above Washington, her golden Lasso of Truth already uncoiling. Barry Allen — the Flash — ran up the side of a skyscraper and evacuated an entire floor of terrified workers before any of them had time to blink.

Cyborg, Victor Stone, was the one who noticed the pattern. His built-in sensors scanned the Parademon formations and broadcast his findings to everyone in range: they were tunneling toward a buried Mother Box beneath the city — a living computer capable of opening a Boom Tube straight to Darkseid's world. If it activated, millions would be taken.

Eight heroes who had never trained together faced that truth at the same moment. Superman destroyed the Mother Box before it could power up. The warship, cut off from its signal, retreated through a crackling portal and vanished.

The Parademons were gone. The city was shaken but safe.

The eight heroes stood together on a Metropolis rooftop, looking at each other for the first time. No one had asked them to fight as one. They had simply done what needed doing, each filling the gap the others left. Batman was the first to speak.

"That won't be the last time," he said quietly.

He was right. And somehow, everyone already knew it.
