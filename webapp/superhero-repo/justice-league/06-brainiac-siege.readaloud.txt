Are you ready? Because this one is big. Really, really big.

One ordinary morning in Metropolis — birds singing, people walking to work, a hot dog cart doing brisk business on Fifth Avenue — a silver spaceship the size of a mountain appeared in the sky. From underneath it came a pale green beam — ZZZZHMMM — and eight city blocks began to SHRINK. Streets, buildings, traffic lights, people — all compressed into a tiny glass bottle. Brainiac, the alien machine-intelligence, was adding Metropolis to his collection.

Superman rocketed straight at the ship. WHOOOOSH. Then — CRACK — the Collector Beam grabbed him and held him frozen in the air like a fly in amber. Brainiac had studied Kryptonians. He was READY for Superman.

But he wasn't ready for the Flash. Barry Allen ran along the beam's edge faster than a lightning strike, disrupting its outer field — ZIPZIPZIPZIP — until Superman broke free. Three seconds. Singed boots. Done.

Batman watched from far below with his scanner. Four seconds of scanning. Then he spoke into the comm. "Cooling tower. Port side. Single vent." Green Lantern heard that and his ring blazed EMERALD. Hal Jordan built a drill, bored into the ventilation shaft — KRSHHHHH — and sealed it with a plug of hard light. The reactor overheated. The beam STUTTERED.

Then Brainiac opened the hangar doors. Drones poured out — CLANKCLANKCLANKLANK — silver and precise and hundreds of them. Aquaman, Wonder Woman, and Cyborg hit them in the streets. Arthur's trident CRACKED armor. Diana's Lasso YANKED them down in arcs. Victor's sonic cannon — BWOOOOM — took out a whole column at once.

While everyone fought below, the Martian Manhunter flew up to the ship's hull — and phased right through it. J'onn J'onzz walked through solid metal like a ghost, found the Collector Beam's power core, and tore it apart with his bare hands. BOOM. Lights out.

The beam died.

But eight blocks of Metropolis were already in bottles on that ship. Cyborg intercepted Brainiac's data stream mid-battle and pulled the coordinates of every bottle in the collection. The whole League boarded. Superman found the shelf of captured cities — rows and rows of glass globes. He restored every single person from Metropolis, one by one, until the streets outside were full of blinking, confused, completely-fine citizens.

Brainiac's ship retreated, trailing damage.

Among the bottles still aboard: Kandor. The ancient capital of Krypton — tiny in glass, waiting.

Superman looked at it for a long moment. His eyes were very still.

"We'll go back for them," he said.

Nobody doubted him for one second. Not Aquaman. Not Batman. Not even the Martian Manhunter, who had seen promises made and broken on worlds far older than this one.

Some promises you just know will be kept.
