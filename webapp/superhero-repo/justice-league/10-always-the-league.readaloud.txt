One year ago: a Metropolis rooftop, eight strangers, a battle over, nobody sure what to say.

Today: the Hall of Justice, the same eight people, a round table, eight mugs of coffee.

WHAT A YEAR.

It was Clark's idea to do this. He says it wasn't. It was Bruce who organized everything. He says he only handled the logistics. Barry arrived first — obviously — and spent his extra time arranging the chairs and then putting them back exactly how they were. Classic Barry.

Arthur had carried a sealed container of water from Atlantis the week before and wouldn't say why until today. He poured it into each glass. Nobody pointed out that they could have used tap water. Because it was from ATLANTIS and that is not the same thing.

The ceremony begins, and it is short, because none of these people are comfortable with long ceremonies.

Clark goes first. He talks about Osaka — the tidal wave, three in the morning, forty minutes, all hands working — the first real mission, the one where they found out if this was actually going to work. It did.

Arthur talks about the rooftop. That first moment after the Parademons were gone, when everyone was still there, and nobody moved to leave.

Diana says what she loves is the table. ROUND. Eight equal seats. Nobody at the head. Because here, every voice counts the same.

J'onn speaks quietly. He says he did not expect to belong somewhere again.

Victor — Cyborg — says that before the League, he spent years trying to figure out which part of him mattered more. The human or the machine. The person or the technology. Nobody here ever asked that question. He was Victor. That was enough.

Hal Jordan gives a toast with the completely wrong mug — it has PROPERTY OF THE FLASH printed on it in bold letters — and everyone LOSES IT. Even Batman's mouth does something that is almost a smile.

Batman stands up. He looks at each of them.

"You kept your word," he says. "All of you."

Five words. From Batman. That is more than a speech. That is EVERYTHING.

Barry Allen raises his mug. "Same time next year?"

Of course. OBVIOUSLY. YES.

Outside the Hall, the city is doing what cities do: school children walking, trucks delivering things, the river catching afternoon light. All ordinary. All the reason for all of this.

Eight people who were never supposed to fit: they fit. They chose it. They choose it every morning.

Whatever comes next — and something always comes next — they will face it together.

THE JUSTICE LEAGUE.
