THREE IN THE MORNING. The Hall of Justice is dark and quiet. Then — BEEP BEEP BEEP BEEP — Cyborg's sensors explode to life. His eyes go full blue. His face reads the data in half a second.

Tidal wave. Massive. Heading for Osaka, Japan. Forty minutes.

The Hall lights up like SUNRISE.

Aquaman doesn't even pause. He dives into the Pacific from the west coast and ROCKETS through the water — faster than a torpedo — straight to the fault zone. Twelve minutes. He finds the break in the ocean floor and pushes sideways with his bare hands. Not down, not up — SIDEWAYS, steering that enormous surge of energy into a deep trench where it can scatter and weaken. The wave slows. Not stopped — but slowed.

Meanwhile — ZOOOOOM — the Flash is already in Osaka. Barry Allen hits the coastal streets running and he doesn't stop. He knocks on every door. "Wave coming! High ground! Now!" In every language. JAPANESE. ENGLISH. PORTUGUESE. Twelve blocks cleared. And he has been there less than four minutes.

Up offshore, Green Lantern raises his ring. SHHHHHHHK — three miles of emerald wall, gleaming and solid and ENORMOUS, rises from the water's surface. The biggest thing Hal Jordan has ever built. His whole body shakes with the effort. He locks his fists and holds it.

KRASSSSSSH!

The wave hits the wall. White water EXPLODES upward. The wall holds. It HOLDS. The wave breaks into foam and spray and tumbling chaos.

Superman and Wonder Woman are flying the harbor in tight sweeps — grabbing fishing boats, cargo ships, tankers — and moving them to deep water where the swell won't crush them. WHOOSH. WHOOSH. One ship after another.

Batman has been watching the seismic network the whole time. He spots a second rupture forming to the north. One transmission: "Hal — northwest, three kilometers." Green Lantern is already moving. ZZAP.

And J'onn J'onzz — the Martian Manhunter, invisible as air — sweeps over the neighborhoods below, sensing every panicked mind, every trapped person, guiding rescuers right to them one by one.

When the wave finally hits the shore, it's a wave, not a monster. It floods the low streets. It tips over market stalls. But not one building falls. Not one person is lost.

At dawn, all eight heroes stand in the wet sand. Barry wrings out his sleeve. Arthur listens to the sea settle. Victor Stone looks at the tide retreating and says three words:

"Good mission."

That is more than enough.
