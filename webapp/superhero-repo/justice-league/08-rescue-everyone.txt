Darkseid's Parademons struck simultaneously in twelve locations across six continents. It was not an invasion. It was a harvest.

They were seizing people — scientists, engineers, doctors, mathematicians — carrying them through Boom Tubes toward Apokolips. Two hundred people taken in the first seven minutes. Cyborg tracked every tube's coordinates and broadcast them to the team before the first one closed.

Every hero moved.

The Flash covered North America. Six locations in three minutes, arriving at each Boom Tube as it was closing, pulling people back by their arms, sometimes diving through the crackling portal himself to drag someone free before the connection severed. He came back through the last tube with eleven seconds to spare and a torn sleeve.

Green Lantern swept through Europe. He sealed three Boom Tubes with containment fields and then went through the one in Lisbon himself, entering Apokolips's outer atmosphere for a full forty seconds, ring blazing against the sulfurous air, long enough to locate and carry six captives back before the tube forced him out.

Aquaman protected the coastal extraction points, commanding ocean currents to delay Parademons long enough for civilians to be evacuated by land. He dove with three Parademons through the harbor at Alexandria, pulling them deep, disorienting them in the pressure of the Mediterranean's lower reaches.

Wonder Woman moved through Africa with her sword and lasso, arriving at each extraction before the Parademons could complete their work. Her Lasso bound two lead Parademons in Lagos, and the whole formation collapsed without their commanders.

J'onn J'onzz went to Apokolips directly.

He phased through the Boom Tube wall while it was still active, becoming intangible, invisible, and drifted into the vast processing center on Apokolips's surface where captives were held. He moved from mind to mind, giving each person a single clear image: stay still, help is coming. Then he broadcast all forty-three captives' positions to Cyborg in a single telepathic data burst.

Superman and Cyborg rode through the final Boom Tube together. Clark carried people. Victor kept the tube open past its natural closing time using his own power systems as a conductor. His readings spiked into the red. He held it.

They brought everyone back. Every one of the two hundred and twelve taken.

At the Hall afterward, Cyborg ran diagnostics for an hour. His power cells needed twelve hours of recovery. Barry Allen fell asleep in his chair at the table. J'onn J'onzz sat by the window and looked toward Apokolips — not visible, but he always knew the direction.

"They will try again," he said.

"Yes," said Superman. "And we will come for everyone again."

J'onn turned from the window. "Yes," he said, simply. "We will."
