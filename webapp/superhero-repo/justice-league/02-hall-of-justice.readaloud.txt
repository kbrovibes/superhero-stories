So you know how after a really epic adventure you all go back to someone's house for snacks? The Justice League needed something like that. Except MUCH bigger. And with more security systems.

Enter: the Hall of Justice.

Bruce Wayne — secretly Batman, very quietly — paid for the whole thing. Because when you are secretly Batman you have a lot of money and a lot of opinions about security systems. The Hall was built right into the cliffs above the Potomac River in Washington. White stone. Huge doors. A satellite dish pointed at the stars. And inside? A round table — ROUND, not rectangular, not with one big important chair at the top — with EIGHT equal seats.

Wonder Woman saw it and gave exactly one nod. That meant it was good.

Then came Moving-In Day, which was — FANTASTIC chaos.

Superman and Batman immediately argued. Clark: "Too many locks send the wrong message." Bruce: "Not enough locks and someone gets hurt." They stared at each other. They compromised. Because that is what teammates do.

Aquaman had a saltwater tank installed in the lower level. He says he thinks better with the ocean nearby. Nobody questioned this. Arthur Curry is the KING of Atlantis. He can have a tank.

Green Lantern Hal Jordan hung a star chart in his workspace. Then he used his power ring — SHHHHK — to add seventeen planets that don't appear on any Earth map. The ring glowed green. The extra planets glowed green. It looked amazing.

Cyborg? Victor Stone spent TWO FULL DAYS wiring his own technology directly into the Hall's systems. By the end, the building was practically part of him. He could feel a broken circuit like a splinter. He could monitor SEVENTEEN global threats at the same time from the kitchen, if he wanted to. Incredible.

The Martian Manhunter — J'onn J'onzz — floated through every room, sensing the structure of the walls with his telepathic mind. He said the Hall breathed like a living thing. The others decided to trust his judgment, because he can read minds and has been around longer than most of them.

Barry Allen mapped the entire building at top speed. It took eleven seconds. ELEVEN. He then memorized every exit. Just in case.

And Diana — Wonder Woman — found a room with a view of the river. She set her golden tiara on the windowsill. Quiet. Still. Like something was finally exactly right.

That evening they all sat at the round table.

No mission. No emergency. Just eight heroes, together, in a place that was theirs.

HOME.
