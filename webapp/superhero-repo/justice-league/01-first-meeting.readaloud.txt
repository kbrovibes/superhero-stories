Okay, picture this. ONE enormous alien warship — bigger than a city block — hovering over Metropolis, blotting out the sun like a storm cloud made of metal. And from its belly? HUNDREDS of Parademons. Winged. Armored. Red-eyed. Screeching as they pour toward the streets.

WHOOOOM! Superman rockets from the ground and punches straight through three of them in a single blow. Three! One punch!

CLANG! Batman drops from a transport plane on a grapple line, already firing a scanner at the nearest creature. Because that is Batman. He fights AND takes notes at the same time.

CRASH! The harbor explodes. Aquaman rides a wave onto the dock, trident gleaming, the ocean itself rearing up behind him like a moving wall. The Parademons scatter — but they regrouped. They always regrouped.

ZZZAP! A green net made of pure light drops from the sky — Green Lantern Hal Jordan, his power ring blazing, scooping twelve Parademons at once. His jaw is set, his fist is tight, and the ring burns as green as concentrated determination.

SWISH! Wonder Woman's golden Lasso of Truth snaps through the air like a whip woven from pure sunlight.

And the Flash? ZOOOOM — he's already evacuated an entire skyscraper. Did you even see him? No? That's the point. The people inside that building don't know his name yet. They just know they're suddenly safe on the sidewalk.

But here is the important part. Cyborg — Victor Stone, half-human, half-machine, ALL brilliant — picks up something on his sensors. The Parademons aren't just attacking. They're DIGGING. Tunneling to a buried Mother Box beneath the city. A Mother Box — a living alien computer that can rip open a Boom Tube and bring the tyrant Darkseid himself straight here!

Not on Superman's watch.

He dives straight down — BOOM — through street and stone and earth, and he CRUSHES the Mother Box before it finishes powering up. The warship's signal dies. The Parademons freeze. And then, with a CRACKLING roar, the warship disappears back through its own portal.

The city is quiet. Sirens in the distance. Eight heroes standing on a Metropolis rooftop, catching their breath, looking at each other for the very first time.

No one planned this. No one trained for it together. They just showed up, read each other's moves, and it WORKED. Completely. Everyone safe. Everything stopped.

Batman crossed his arms and said: "That won't be the last time."

He wasn't wrong. He is never wrong. That is extremely annoying, and also extremely helpful.

The Justice League had just begun.
