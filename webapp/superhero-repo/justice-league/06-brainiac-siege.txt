Brainiac came without warning. One moment Metropolis was a city of three million people. The next, a massive silver spaceship hovered over it, trailing a pale green beam that began shrinking eight city blocks at once — buildings, streets, and people compressed into a bottle no larger than a snow globe. The alien machine-intelligence known as Brainiac had already done this to Kandor, the great capital of Krypton, sealing it in glass and adding it to his collection. Now he wanted Earth's greatest city.

The entire Justice League scrambled. Superman flew straight at the ship and was hit by a Collector Beam that locked his movement in mid-air, holding him rigid in a crackling column of energy. Brainiac had prepared for Kryptonians specifically. The Flash arrived in seconds and raced along the beam's edges, disrupting its outer field until Clark could break free. It cost Barry three seconds and a pair of singed boots, but it worked.

Batman studied the ship's hull from a rooftop below. His analysis: the shrink-beam drew power from a central reactor, and the reactor had a single cooling tower on the ship's underbelly. Green Lantern took the task. Hal Jordan built a solid emerald drill around his ring arm and bored straight through the cooling tower's vent, then filled the gap with a plug of constructed material. The reactor began to overheat and the beam stuttered.

Brainiac sent his robot drones. They poured from the ship in waves — silver, methodical, precise. Aquaman, Wonder Woman, and Cyborg held the drones back in the streets below while Martian Manhunter flew up and phased through the ship's hull. J'onn moved through the interior unseen, dismantling the Collector Beam's power core from within, bending the ship's own architecture around it.

The beam died. The eight shrunken blocks were gone — but Cyborg had intercepted Brainiac's own data stream during the attack, pulling the coordinates of his bottle collection from the alien's memory banks. The people of Metropolis who had been miniaturized were on that ship, sealed in glass. The League boarded together. Superman found the bottles. One by one the captive citizens were restored, stepping out blinking into the Metropolis daylight, confused and whole.

Brainiac's ship retreated, damaged, dragging a trail of coolant vapor across the sky. J'onn said, quietly, that Kandor was still aboard — the Kryptonian city, still waiting. Clark was silent for a long moment.

"We'll go back for them," he said.

No one doubted him.
