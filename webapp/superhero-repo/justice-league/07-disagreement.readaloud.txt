Here is something important about being on a team: sometimes the most powerful people in the room still disagree. Even when the clock is ticking. ESPECIALLY when the clock is ticking.

Brainiac was back. Bigger ship. Same terrifying goal: miniaturize Metropolis and put it in a bottle. Eighteen hours.

Superman: "We go to him. Intercept in orbit. He never gets close to the city."

Batman: "No. We draw him down. Use Earth as our weapon. Familiar ground."

These two looked at each other across the round table.

Clock: ticking.

For two hours — TWO HOURS — they argued. Clark was calm but firm. Bruce was controlled but immovable. J'onn J'onzz sat silently and watched everything with those deep eyes, learning more from the argument than the arguers realized.

Diana finally said: "We are running out of time for this."

Arthur Curry had sat through a lot of Atlantean council debates. Kings argue. He knew the difference between arguing about strategy and arguing about something ELSE.

He leaned forward. "Clark. You want to intercept in orbit because if Brainiac gets close to Metropolis he might still hurt it."

Clark was quiet.

"Bruce. You want to draw him down because if Clark goes to orbit, you cannot follow. And that bothers you."

Bruce was quiet.

Arthur looked at both of them. "This isn't really about where to fight, is it."

Oh.

OH.

Two of the most capable people on Earth, each trying to protect the other in the only way they knew how. And they couldn't see it until someone said it out loud.

Twenty minutes later they had a COMBINED plan. Meet Brainiac in the upper atmosphere — the boundary between orbit and Earth. Clark has his powers at full strength there. Hal's ring works perfectly there. Batman's defensive perimeter below is backup for anything that gets past.

Beautiful. Practical. Theirs.

Green Lantern rockets up first — FWOOOSH — ring blazing, and WRAPS Brainiac's landing thrusters in an emerald containment frame so the ship cannot descend.

Superman hits the scanner array with both hands — RRRRIIP — tearing it clean off the hull. No scanners. No targeting lock. No coordinates. No shrunken Metropolis.

Brainiac retreats. He does not look happy. But he goes.

That night at the Hall, nobody says a word about the argument. J'onn makes tea. Barry talks too much, the way Barry does when he's relieved. Diana and Arthur play chess. It's peaceful.

Clark and Bruce sit at the round table. The round table with eight equal seats and no head.

"Good plan," Clark says.

"Yours was good too," says Bruce. "We combined them."

"That's what the table is for."

Bruce looks at the table for a long moment.

"Yes," he says. "Exactly."
