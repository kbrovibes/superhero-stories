Not every story is a fight. Some of the most important ones are two people in the quiet, when the work is done, saying something true.

The mission in Kahndaq is over. J'onn J'onzz and Wonder Woman stay to secure the last of Luthor's equipment. When it's done they sit on the steps outside. The desert sun drops. Everything goes gold.

These are two of the oldest heroes on the team. Diana is an Amazon from Themyscira — she has walked among humans longer than most history books cover. J'onn J'onzz is the Martian Manhunter — the last survivor of an entire world, carrying Mars inside him the way you carry a memory nobody else shares.

They're quiet for a while. The comfortable kind of quiet.

J'onn says he sometimes walks city streets at night in human form — just a person in a coat and hat, in Middleton where he once lived before the League. Just to feel like he belongs.

Diana says she knows that feeling. In Paris she was just Diana once — walking along the Seine with bread and cheese. Not Wonder Woman. Just a person in a city.

Then J'onn says the harder thing. Everyone else on the team has a people. Clark has Krypton in his memory and Earth in his life. Arthur has Atlantis. Barry has Central City. Even Bruce has Gotham. J'onn has one photograph — one framed image of what Mars looked like. And sometimes that weight is more than a room can hold.

Diana doesn't rush to answer. She lets the quiet sit.

Then: "You have a people. They are eight. They eat all the coffee before you get there, and they argue about things that could be settled in one sentence, and they are YOURS."

J'onn is quiet for a moment. "Your island is still there. You could go back."

"I know," Diana says. "And every morning I choose this."

The stars appear over Kahndaq. J'onn lets his human form relax — stops working so hard to look exactly one way. Diana doesn't look away. She just sits with him in the desert dark, like a good friend.

When they fly home, J'onn puts his photograph on the common-room shelf.

Nobody mentions it. But over the next few days: Arthur leaves a seashell from Atlantis. Barry adds a framed playing card he has carried since childhood. Someone — probably Diana — sets a small golden horse beside them.

A shelf of belonging. Built without a single word.

That is what it looks like when you find your people.
