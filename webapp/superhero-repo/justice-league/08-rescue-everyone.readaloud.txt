TWELVE LOCATIONS. SIX CONTINENTS. ALL AT ONCE.

Darkseid's Parademons aren't fighting today. They're TAKING. Scientists. Engineers. Doctors. Mathematicians. Two hundred people in seven minutes, dragged through crackling Boom Tubes toward Apokolips.

But Cyborg knows. Victor Stone tracks every tube — BEEP BEEP BEEP — and broadcasts every coordinate to every League member before the first portal closes.

GO.

The Flash: NORTH AMERICA. Six locations in three minutes. Barry Allen is moving faster than he has ever moved — the world is a blur, Boom Tubes are closing, and he is pulling people back by their hands and arms, one at a time. Twice he dives INTO the portal himself — into that burning orange — and drags someone back out with seconds to spare. His sleeve tears. He doesn't notice. Already at the next tube.

Green Lantern: EUROPE. Hal seals three tubes with emerald containment fields — BAM BAM BAM — and then goes THROUGH the fourth himself. Into Apokolips. Forty seconds. Ring blazing green. Six captives found, wrapped in a bubble, pulled back through. Forty seconds and he is out.

Aquaman: COASTAL POINTS. Arthur commands ocean currents to delay the Parademon teams — the tide rising, the sea pushing back, buying minutes for evacuation. In Alexandria he dives with three Parademons into deep harbor water, taking them down into cold and pressure and confusion. He swims back up alone.

Wonder Woman: AFRICA. Sword and lasso, moving fast. She binds the two lead Parademons in Lagos and the whole formation loses direction. She is four cities away before they recover.

J'onn J'onzz: He phases through a Boom Tube wall while it is still ACTIVE. Invisible. Intangible. He drifts through Apokolips — through heat and machines and orange-dark sky — into the holding facility. He moves mind to mind through forty-three captives, leaving each one the same thought: stay still, help is coming. Then one telepathic burst to Cyborg: all forty-three positions, exact.

Superman and Cyborg take the last tube together. Clark carries people — arms full, back and forth. Victor holds the tube open past its natural closing time using his own power systems as a live conductor. His readings go red. DEEPER red. He holds it.

They come through. Clark sets the last person down.

"How many?" he asks.

Victor checks. "Two hundred and twelve. All of them."

Afterward: Victor needs twelve hours of recovery. Barry is asleep at the table, still in his suit. J'onn stands at the window, facing the direction of Apokolips.

"They will try again," he says.

Superman: "And we will come for everyone again."

Every. Single. Time.
