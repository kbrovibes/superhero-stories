Darkseid came himself this time.

No advance force of Parademons. No Mother Box to signal his arrival. The tyrant of Apokolips simply opened a Boom Tube above Metropolis and stepped through, his gray armor gleaming, his red Omega eyes already sweeping the city below. He was searching for the Anti-Life Equation, a mathematical truth that, if found, could erase free will from every mind on Earth. He believed it was hidden somewhere in the city's data networks.

The League scrambled in under four minutes. They had trained for this.

Cyborg immediately began flooding Metropolis's data systems with harmless noise, drowning the city's networks in digital static so Darkseid's scanners could not lock onto any useful signal. Batman coordinated from a hover-vehicle above, feeding targeting data to every team member through his communications array. He had spent weeks preparing a frequency that could disrupt Darkseid's Omega Beams — the destructive red bolts Darkseid fired from his eyes — long enough to give the team fractions of a second of advantage.

Darkseid descended toward Centennial Park.

Superman met him there. The collision shook the ground for six blocks. Clark was stronger than almost anything in the universe, but Darkseid matched him, then exceeded him, driving Superman into the earth with one hand. Green Lantern hit Darkseid from behind with a concentrated beam — CRAACK — and bought Clark two seconds to recover. Wonder Woman's Lasso of Truth snapped around Darkseid's left arm and she pulled, her heels carving furrows in the grass. He turned toward her with something like interest.

The Flash ran rings around Darkseid — literally, 200 laps around a single point in under three seconds, building a vortex that disoriented the tyrant's targeting systems. J'onn J'onzz entered Darkseid's mind telepathically and found exactly what Batman had predicted: a momentary uncertainty at the center of all that iron will. He did not break it. He simply noted it like a crack in stone, and passed the coordinates to Batman.

Aquaman arrived from the east, the Potomac River rising behind him, water columns hammering Darkseid's armor and grounding his energy weapons. In that instant of disruption, all seven heroes struck together, channeling every power simultaneously.

Darkseid stepped back. One step.

It was enough. The combined force of the League shattered the connection between his scanners and Metropolis's network. The Anti-Life Equation remained hidden. Darkseid looked at them — all of them, at once — with eyes that had not known surprise in centuries.

"This is not finished," he said.

Then the Boom Tube opened behind him and he was gone.

Superman floated down from above, bleeding from his lip. Diana helped him stand. They all stood there, breathing hard, knowing Darkseid was right.

But today, they had held.
