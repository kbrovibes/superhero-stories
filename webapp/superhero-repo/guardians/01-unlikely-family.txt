Peter Quill never planned to be a hero. He was eight years old on Earth when a Ravager ship swept him off a Missouri sidewalk, and for the next twenty-six years he flew the galaxy as a low-level outlaw calling himself Star-Lord — a name most people found amusing. He smuggled, he scavenged, and he danced to a battered Sony Walkman loaded with the only tape his mother had ever made him. Then, on the dusty planet Morag, he found a small silver sphere that would ruin everything and save everything at once.

The Orb he stole from Morag contained the Power Stone, one of six ancient Infinity Stones that Thanos was slowly collecting. The fanatical Kree warlord Ronan wanted it too, and within hours Peter found himself in a four-way chase across Xandar, the gleaming home world of the Nova Corps. Gamora, a green-skinned warrior, tried to take the Orb from him. Rocket — a furious raccoon genius with a blaster almost as tall as himself — and his partner Groot, a towering gentle tree who said only "I am Groot," tried to collect the bounty on Peter's head. When the Nova Corps arrested all of them, they were thrown together into the Kyln prison.

In the Kyln they met Drax the Destroyer, whose tattoos recorded every grief he had ever carried. Drax had lost his wife Ovette and daughter Kamaria to Ronan's cruelty and lived for revenge. He understood sarcasm the way a boulder understands poetry — not at all — but his loyalty was absolute and his strength enormous. Gamora explained she had planned to betray Ronan and sell the Orb elsewhere. Rocket drew a hasty escape plan involving a guard's prosthetic leg and a security watchtower. Nobody thought it would work. It worked.

They stole the Milano — Peter's battered but beloved ship — and broke free of the Kyln together. Five strangers: a homesick outlaw with a Walkman, a green warrior carrying a complicated past, a literal-minded strongman with nothing left to lose, a raccoon sharper than anyone gave him credit for, and a walking tree whose vocabulary ran to exactly three words. They had not chosen each other, and they did not particularly trust each other. But somewhere between the Kyln's alarm klaxons and the cold dark of open space, they were already acting like a team.

The Orb was still in Peter's satchel, the whole galaxy was hunting them, and Ronan's warship was somewhere in the dark behind them. None of that mattered quite as much as this small, loud, argumentative ship full of people who had been left behind by the universe — and had just found each other instead.
