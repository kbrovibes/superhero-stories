Three weeks after Xandar, the Milano was somewhere between Berhert and the Sovereign system, and the team was at each other in a way that suggested the close quarters and shared victory had burned off the polite reserves everyone had been maintaining. Peter wanted to take a contract on Sovereign — good money, legitimate delivery work. Gamora said the Sovereign were arrogant and would demand perfection from every member of the team, which was a concern when certain members of the team had recently blown up a client's best display case on Knowhere. Rocket said he'd like to see Gamora name anyone who hadn't caused some kind of property damage in the last month. Drax said that he had caused no property damage, which was technically true if you did not count Korath's personal warship.

The argument expanded, the way arguments do when people are actually arguing about something else. What they were really arguing about was the question of what they were — a temporary alliance that had achieved its purpose, or something with an actual future. Nobody said that out loud. They said things like "you never asked before you modified the Milano's port thrusters" and "every plan Rocket calls good has at least one explosion in it." Groot sat in the corner growing small leaves and watching them all with the patience of something that had been alive for a very long time.

It was Drax who ended it, because Drax processed emotions in an extremely direct order and had finished processing faster than everyone else. He stood up, which took a moment because the Milano's common area was not really designed for someone of his proportions, and he said: "I did not think I would ever have a family again. I thought it had ended on the day Ronan destroyed mine. I am aware that we are strange. I am aware that we fight frequently. None of that changes the fact." He sat back down. He said nothing else. He looked at his hands.

Nobody moved for about ten seconds.

Peter said, "Okay, we take the Sovereign contract." Gamora said, "We review the delivery terms first." Rocket said he already had the terms and he'd only modified one of them. The argument restarted immediately at a significantly lower temperature. Groot grew three new leaves in quiet celebration.

The Milano banked toward Sovereign. The team flew on — arguing, adjusting, disagreeing about nothing that mattered — held together by the things that did.
