Here is something nobody tells you about saving the galaxy: after you do it, you still have to figure out what you are to each other. For the Guardians, this question surfaced sideways — as an argument about a delivery contract.

Three weeks after Xandar. The Milano somewhere between Berhert and the Sovereign system. Everyone aboard, everyone has been aboard for three weeks, and the polite reserves people maintain during a crisis have fully worn through.

Peter wanted the Sovereign contract — good money, totally legitimate work. "We are heroes now," he announced. "We do heroic things."

Gamora had notes. "The Sovereign evaluate everyone on the team. They hold their contractors to exacting standards. Rocket recently caused a significant explosion in a client's museum on Knowhere."

Rocket looked up from his workbench with great dignity. "That was caused by someone else's specimen. Furthermore, I have HOLOGRAPHIC FOOTAGE of four broken market stalls on Berhert, Gamora. Four stalls. Organized by timestamp."

"That was one stall."

"I. Have. The. Footage."

"You are exaggerating the number of stalls."

"I AM NOT."

Peter tried to say something. Nobody heard him.

Drax: "I have not caused any property damage."

Everyone paused.

Gamora: "You destroyed Korath's personal warship."

Drax: "That was his property. I was standing on someone else's property at the time. My record is technically clean." He looked around to see if anyone could find a flaw in this logic. Nobody could.

But here is what is actually happening. Under all the noise: the real question. Are we a real team, with a future and commitment? Or five people aimed at the same problem, who are now just five individuals sharing a ship?

Nobody said that. They said it through stalls and footage instead.

Groot watched from his corner, growing one leaf at a time, patient as a forest.

Then Drax stood up. Two inches of clearance between his head and the ceiling. He didn't care.

"When Ronan destroyed my family, I thought family was something that happened once and then ended when it was gone. I was wrong. We argue too much. Rocket smells of engine oil. Peter's music plays when people are sleeping. Gamora is alarming before breakfast. I am told I am difficult to have a conversation with." He looked around the table at each of them. "None of that changes what is true. You are my family. All of you. I would very much like to keep you."

Ten seconds of quiet.

Peter: "We take the contract." Gamora: "We read every line of the terms." Rocket: "Already did. Changed one thing." Peter: "ROCKET." Rocket: "You will genuinely appreciate this one."

Groot grew three new leaves at once.

The Milano banked toward Sovereign. The argument continued. Warmer now, and theirs.
