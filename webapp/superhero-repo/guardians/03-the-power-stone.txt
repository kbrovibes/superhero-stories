With the Power Stone in his fist, Ronan pointed the Dark Aster at Xandar — the shining Nova Corps home world, a planet of white spires and open plazas and several billion people who had done nothing wrong except exist in the path of a Kree fanatic's ancient grudge. Peter heard the plan aboard Yondu's ship, the Eclector, and made a decision that was either very brave or very reckless. Often the same thing.

He called Ronan on an open channel and challenged him to a dance-off.

This was not how the battle was ultimately won. But it bought thirty crucial seconds. The plan had three parts, assembled around a table with Yondu's Ravagers and five Guardians who had just finished arguing about everything. The Ravagers and Nova Corps fleet would engage Ronan's fighters outside Xandar's atmosphere. Rocket would fly a modified mining vessel straight at the underside of the Dark Aster to kill its engines. Peter, Gamora, Drax, and Groot would board through the breach and confront Ronan directly. Every piece depended on every other piece.

The battle over Xandar filled the sky with light. Ronan's warships poured out in formation; the Nova Corps scrambled their Star Blasters in tight patterns, weaving through the chaos. Rocket patched the Milano on the fly, blew a hole through the Dark Aster's belly, and killed its engines. The boarding party dropped inside. In the ship's corridors, Nebula and Gamora fought — two sisters, seventeen years of complicated history expressed through blade and fist. Drax found Korath and removed him from the situation quickly. Then Groot stopped and began to grow. His arms stretched and thickened, branches weaving into a living cage around his teammates. "We are Groot," he said to Rocket, very softly, before the crash.

The Dark Aster hit Xandar and shattered. When the dust settled, Groot was gone, and Ronan walked from the wreckage with the Power Stone blazing purple in his hand. Peter caught the Stone bare-handed and nearly died instantly. Then Gamora's hand was on his arm — the energy split between two people. Then Drax, then Rocket, all four forming a bridge, sharing the unbearable so no single person had to carry it alone. The force poured through all of them, and Peter — whose heritage ran deeper than anyone knew — held on and channeled it outward.

Ronan was destroyed. The Power Stone settled calm back into the Orb. Xandar stood. In the rubble, Rocket found a single small green sprout pushing up through the dust.
