The name Thanos was spoken differently on different worlds — with prayer on some, with terror on others, with the flat tone of people who had seen what he left behind and no longer found it useful to feel anything about it. The Guardians had his name in their story from the very beginning: he had sent Ronan on the mission that brought them together, and he had sent Gamora before that, and Nebula before that. All of them aimed at one thing. He was collecting the six Infinity Stones, and had been for longer than most civilizations had existed.

The Guardians understood this threat more clearly than almost anyone else in the galaxy, because they had held the Power Stone — now sealed in a Nova Corps vault on Xandar — and felt what it could do against a single planet. Thanos wanted all six. The size of that calculation made Peter go quiet whenever he thought it through.

The shadow arrived not as a confrontation but as an intelligence report, passed across a market table in the port city of Hrax by a Kree contact of Gamora's. A data drive, no conversation. The data showed troop movements and Stone sightings — the pattern of an ancient plan accelerating. Four of the six Stones were located. The spaces between them were closing.

Gamora laid out the findings in the Milano's common room without softening them. Nebula confirmed the troop figures from her own memory of Thanos's campaign rooms. Drax asked what could be done. Rocket said the real question was whether five people on one ship were equipped for what this actually was — not a job, not a heist, but something much larger.

Peter stood at the viewport for a long time. He was thinking about Xandar. About the Power Stone killing him alone and surviving it only because his team reached for him. That was not luck or coincidence. That was a principle. You share the unbearable. You don't carry it alone.

"We can't stop this alone," he said. "Nobody can. We find allies. We warn the people who hold Stones that Thanos is coming. We start building something bigger than us, and we stay together while we do it." He turned from the viewport. "That's what I've got."

Groot reached a branch into the center of the table. Gamora placed her hand on his. Drax added his. Nebula set hers down quietly, as if deciding something. Rocket looked at the pile, made a face, and added his too. Peter put his last. Two seconds, then Rocket said they were being theatrical. But it had happened, and it counted, and the Milano flew on with its small and unlikely family pointed at what came next.
