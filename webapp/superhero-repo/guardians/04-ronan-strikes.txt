Ronan the Accuser was not the kind of villain who waited at home while lieutenants did his work. He wore black armor that had never been polished — polish being the kind of detail people who believed in the future tended to — and he carried an energy war hammer crackling blue-white that he wielded like a personal argument he had been making his whole life. His hatred of Xandar and the Kree peace treaty was a conviction that had outlasted every rational debate and burned now as pure, unreasoned fury. When he learned that five small-time criminals stood between him and the Power Stone, he decided to handle them personally.

The first time Ronan's forces reached the Guardians, it was Nebula who served as his instrument. She caught Gamora outside Knowhere, stripped the Orb away, and left her sister spinning in the void. Ronan pressed the Power Stone into the head of his hammer and felt it bind. For one moment everyone in the galaxy sensitive to power felt a deep cold tremor — like a struck bell made of ice.

Ronan summoned Thanos to announce that the deal between them was finished. He would destroy Xandar on his own terms and then Thanos next. Telling the most feared being in the universe that he was no longer needed went about as well as you might expect. Thanos smiled without warmth. Ronan set his course anyway.

Peter overheard the transmission on the Eclector and brought the news to the assembled team. He said Ronan was heading for Xandar, that the Nova Corps could not stop the hammer alone, and that they had to go now. Rocket pointed out that the odds were terrible. Drax said he was going regardless — this was the fight he had crossed the galaxy to find. Gamora said she knew the Dark Aster's weak points from when she had walked its corridors as Ronan's ally. Groot said "I am Groot," which everyone understood as agreement. Peter said, simply: it matters.

For this particular group, that was the right argument. They flew to meet the Dark Aster in formation, the Nova Corps alongside them, Xandar's golden sun behind them and Ronan's iron shadow ahead. His voice came across every open channel — certain, cold, immense — reminding every ship in the sky of exactly how large his certainty was.

Not one Guardian turned back.
