Okay. The Power Stone. One of the most dangerous objects in the universe. Can destroy entire planets. Held by Ronan, the most furious Kree warlord alive. Pointed directly at Xandar, the bright, beautiful home of the Nova Corps.

And Peter Quill's response?

He called Ronan on a video channel and challenged him to a dance-off.

I am not making that up.

It bought thirty seconds, which turned out to be enough.

Here is how the Guardians stopped a planet-destroying warlord. Everyone had a job.

Job one: Yondu and the Ravagers engage the Dark Aster outside Xandar. Hundreds of ships, lasers everywhere, BOOM BOOM BOOM, keeping Ronan's fleet busy. The Nova Corps joined in — their Star Blasters in tight formation, flying through the chaos like silver needles through a storm. WHOOOOSH. ZAAAAP.

Job two: Rocket. In a modified mining vessel, alone, flying straight up at the underside of the Dark Aster. Did anyone tell him this was a terrible idea? Many times. Did that help? No. He pushed that little ship until the engines screamed — RRRRRRRR — blasted a hole through the warship's belly, and killed its engines.

Job three: Everyone else boards the Dark Aster.

Inside the crashing ship, Nebula and Gamora fought through the corridors in a battle that was seventeen years of hurt feelings and complicated history expressed through sword and fist. Drax found Ronan's enforcer Korath — a man made of anger and metal — and defeated him. Peter and the rest climbed toward the bridge.

And then Groot made a decision.

The ship was going down. Fast. Groot started growing — arms stretching, branches thickening, weaving around his teammates like a basket, like a net, like a hug that could stop a mountain. He wrapped himself around everyone. "We are Groot," he said to Rocket. His voice was very, very gentle.

KRAAAAAKOOM.

The Dark Aster hit Xandar and shattered.

When the smoke cleared, Groot was gone. And Ronan walked out of the wreckage with the Power Stone blazing purple in his hand, big enough now to level everything.

Peter stepped forward and grabbed the Stone.

The energy ripped through him. He was going to die. Except — Gamora grabbed his arm. The energy spread between two. Then Drax. Then Rocket. Four people, one terrible power, shared like a weight no single person could carry alone.

And Peter — whose father was something older than any of them knew — held on. He turned the power outward.

Purple fire. Ronan: gone.

Xandar stood in the quiet after.

Rocket was kneeling in the rubble, looking at something very small. A tiny sprout. Green. Just waking up.

Groot was not gone. Not completely. Just... beginning again.
