The Dark Aster was the size of a city and dark as a bruise — a warship built to deliver dread before anything else, its hull plated in ancient Kree war-scripture, its shadow wide enough to swallow a district whole. Flying the Milano into that shadow felt like flying into the mouth of something that had never lost. Peter kept the Walkman playing, volume up, because some situations called for music as a matter of necessity.

The boarding happened through Rocket's hull breach, the Milano threading twisted metal while the Nova Corps flew tight formation above and Yondu's Ravagers held Ronan's fighters at the flanks. Once inside, the team split: Gamora moved through the corridors with the certainty of someone who had walked them before, heading for the bridge. Drax went to find Korath, Ronan's enforcer, with the specific focus of a person who has a list. Peter and Rocket went for the power coupling, arguing in hushed tones about junction access panels the entire way — even in a crisis, Rocket had opinions. Groot spread through the corridors like a forest growing in fast motion, quiet and methodical, filling passages and tripping pursuers.

Gamora found Nebula in a long corridor and there was one motionless second between them — two women trained on the same terrible curriculum by the same teacher, who had fought back to back for years before everything split. Then Nebula reached for her weapon and the corridor became a blur of blade and old grievance. Gamora won, barely. Nebula fell through a grate and caught a passing Ravager ship and escaped into the battle below. Gamora kept moving. The bridge was ahead.

Rocket's final assault killed the Dark Aster's engines and the ship began to fall. In the last moments, Groot grew — arms stretching wide, branches weaving, building a protective cage of living wood around his teammates. "We are Groot," he told Rocket softly, just before the impact. The ship hit Xandar and tore itself apart. Groot was gone. Ronan stepped from the wreckage with the Power Stone blazing in his fist.

Peter grabbed the Stone bare-handed and the energy hit him like a wall. Gamora seized his arm and the fire split between two. Then Drax. Then Rocket, running across the rubble, unwilling to lose anything else today. Four wills, one shared impossible force — held until it bent outward. Ronan was destroyed. The Stone calmed. Xandar stood. And four dusty Guardians stood in the quiet, understanding that what they had just done together could not have been done any other way.
