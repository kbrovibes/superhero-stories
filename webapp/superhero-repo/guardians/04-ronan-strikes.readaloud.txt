There are some villains who send their armies and wait at home. Ronan was not that kind.

Ronan the Accuser wore armor the color of deep night and carried a war hammer crackling with blue-white energy, and he had a personal grudge against Xandar that was thousands of years old and showed no signs of shrinking. When the Kree Empire signed a peace treaty with the Nova Corps, Ronan said: I don't care. When everyone celebrated: I don't care. When the galaxy moved on: I REALLY don't care.

He had never stopped fighting a war everyone else had ended. And now he had the Power Stone.

He pressed it into the head of his hammer. CLUNG. The purple light blazed. And the whole galaxy shivered.

Then Ronan opened a channel to Thanos — the titan who had sent him on this mission in the first place — and said, very calmly, "I don't need you anymore. I will destroy Xandar, and then I will destroy you."

Picture telling the most frightening being in the universe that he is fired. That is what Ronan did. Thanos smiled, and it was not a nice smile.

Ronan set course for Xandar. The Dark Aster moved through space like a flying mountain. Below, on Xandar's surface, nobody knew what was coming.

On the Eclector, Peter heard the transmission. He looked at the team.

"He's going to Xandar," Peter said. "He's going to destroy the whole planet. The Nova Corps can't stop that hammer alone. We have to go. Right now."

Rocket: "Have you seen how big that ship is?"

Peter: "Yes."

Rocket: "Have you counted how many fighters are on it?"

Peter: "I'd rather not."

Rocket: "I am just pointing out that these odds are TERRIBLE."

Peter: "I know. I'm still going."

And here is what happened. Drax stood up, because this was the fight he had crossed the galaxy for. Gamora stood up, because she knew the Dark Aster from the inside and she had her own scores to settle. Groot grew three inches taller, which was his version of standing straighter. And Rocket — still muttering about terrible odds — started calculating a flight path.

They flew out to meet the Dark Aster over Xandar, with the Nova Corps fleet joining them in gold and white formation. And in the golden light of Xandar's sun, Ronan's shadow fell over everything like a storm.

His voice rolled across every open channel, reminding them of exactly how large and certain he was.

Nobody turned back.

Not a single Guardian blinked.

The fight was about to begin.
