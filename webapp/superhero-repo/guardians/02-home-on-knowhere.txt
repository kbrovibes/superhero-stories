After the Kyln breakout, the Guardians needed a place off the Nova Corps' charts, hidden from Ronan's scouts, and unusual enough that no reasonable person would search there. Rocket knew exactly the location. He punched coordinates for Knowhere, and the Milano dropped out of warp into one of the strangest sights in the galaxy: a severed head. It was the skull of an ancient Celestial — one of the enormous beings who had shaped whole star systems before civilization could write down their names. The skull was the size of a small moon, hollow, honeycombed with tunnels carved by mining companies, and lit from within by the purple-gold glow of cantinas and market stalls that clung to bone and membrane like a city grown inside a cave.

Their contact was Taneleer Tivan, the Collector, who received them in his museum surrounded by glass cases holding creatures and relics from a thousand worlds. He had white hair swept up like a wave and a fur-trimmed coat and the specific look of someone who collects things most people are afraid to touch. He explained what the Orb truly contained: the Power Stone, one of six ancient Infinity Stones capable of releasing enough energy to destroy an entire planet. Anyone who held it bare-handed was annihilated instantly. The Collector wanted it badly. But before the transaction could finish, one of his captive specimens shattered her case and triggered a purple explosion that tore apart half his collection and sent everyone scrambling for the corridors.

They barely made it out before Ronan's forces arrived, the Dark Aster dropping its shadow over Knowhere like a storm cloud swallowing the sun. Ronan's lieutenant Nebula — blue-skinned, cybernetic, her body rebuilt by Thanos piece by piece since childhood — had tracked Gamora here. Nebula and Gamora were adopted sisters, both taken by Thanos when they were small, both shaped by the same terrible training. The wound between them was old and sharp. Nebula seized the Orb and tore Gamora's ship apart, leaving her spinning in the void outside Knowhere while Peter jumped after her in a spacesuit, triggering an emergency beacon.

Rocket sat in the Milano's pilot seat for one long moment. The Orb was gone. Ronan had it. Peter and Gamora were drifting in empty space and weren't technically his responsibility — not yet. He could run. Any sensible person would run.

Rocket said something unprintable and punched the throttle. He flew into the dark after them. Yondu Udonta's Ravager fleet, drawn by Peter's beacon, scooped everyone up. Nobody said anything dramatic about what Rocket had done. Nobody needed to. Sometimes the decision to be loyal happens before anyone finds the words for it.
