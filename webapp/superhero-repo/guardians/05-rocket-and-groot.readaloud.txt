Every great team has a friendship right at the center of it that holds the whole thing together. Like a keystone in an arch. Pull it out and the arch falls. For the Guardians of the Galaxy, that friendship was Rocket and Groot.

Rocket you already know. Sharp. Clever. Makes weapons out of junk. Trusts approximately nobody. Has opinions about everything and will share all of them whether you want them or not.

Groot you also know. Eight feet tall. Made of wood. Vocabulary: three words. The gentlest being on any spacecraft the Guardians have ever flown.

Together they had been bounty hunters across a dozen star systems before they crashed into the rest of the team on Xandar. And in all that time, not one person had ever figured out why these two were such close friends. The answer, if you watched carefully: Rocket was different around Groot. Softer. Not soft soft — Rocket would be very upset if you said that — but measurably less prickly. He fixed Groot's bark when it cracked. He translated every "I am Groot" automatically, like breathing.

On the station Berhert, after the Milano took damage from a Ravager chase, they needed a new ignition coil. Rocket said "I'll find one" and Groot said "I am Groot" and they vanished together into the station corridors.

The rest of the team heard: a crash. Then a louder crash. Then the sound of heavy machinery being taken apart with great confidence.

Forty-five minutes later: Rocket with the ignition coil. Groot with a small purple flower from a crack in the station's outer wall. He held it out to Rocket.

Rocket looked at it for a second. Then he took it. He didn't say thank you. But he put it in the dashboard holder on the Milano and it stayed there for three weeks.

That is Rocket saying thank you.

During the Kyln breakout, a containment gate came slamming down between Rocket and the exit. There was no time to calculate. No time to find a workaround. Groot reached back — GRRNNK — and held it up. Alone. That gate weighed more than the Milano. He just held it, arm stretched above his head, until Rocket ducked through.

Then the gate slammed. BOOM.

Groot was fine. He always was.

After Xandar — after the crash, after the quiet — Rocket knelt in the rubble and found a small green sprout pushing up through the dust.

He held it in both hands.

He didn't move for a long time.

Drax walked over. Put one big hand on Rocket's shoulder. Walked away.

Rocket didn't shrug it off.

THAT is how you know the team was real. When even Rocket let someone stand beside him.
