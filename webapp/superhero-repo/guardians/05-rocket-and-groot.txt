Nobody on the Milano fully understood the friendship between Rocket and Groot. It had existed long before the rest of the team, built up over years of bounty work across a dozen star systems. Rocket never explained himself to anyone — a habit grown from a life where showing softness had cost him badly — but around Groot he was observably different. He fixed Groot's bark when it cracked in cold vacuum. He explained plans twice without being asked. He listened to every "I am Groot" as if it carried volumes, translating automatically, the way you translate something that lives so deep in you it has become part of your thinking.

Their most revealing moment came on the orbital station Berhert, where the Milano had landed for emergency repairs after Ravager pursuit ships scored a hit on the port engine. Rocket said he could find the needed ignition coil; Groot said "I am Groot" and followed. The two disappeared down a service corridor. What the rest of the team heard over the next hour: a distant crash, a pause, a considerably louder crash, then the sound of heavy machinery being taken apart with great confidence and imprecise tools.

Rocket emerged carrying the coil. Groot followed carrying a small purple flower he had found growing from a crack in the station's outer wall — the kind that blooms in difficult places because it doesn't know it isn't supposed to. He held it out to Rocket without ceremony. Rocket looked at it for a moment that was perhaps a half-second longer than it needed to be. Then he took it and placed it in the little holder on the Milano's dashboard that had never held anything before, and said "Let's get the coil in" and went to work. The flower stayed for three weeks.

During the Kyln breakout, a heavy containment gate dropped between Rocket and the exit. No time to calculate. Groot reached back and held the gate up with one arm, braced against a weight that could have crushed the floor, holding steady until Rocket was through. Then he let it slam — BOOM — and brushed off his hands calmly.

After the crash on Xandar, Rocket found a small green sprout in the rubble. He knelt down and held it very carefully in both hands, not moving. The team gave him space. Drax walked over quietly, placed one hand on Rocket's shoulder for a moment, and walked away without a word. Rocket didn't pull back. That was new. They were not just two bounty hunters anymore, and the small beginning in Rocket's hands was a little more miraculous for it.
