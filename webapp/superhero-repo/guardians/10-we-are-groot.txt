Peter Quill kept a grey box under his bunk on the Milano. It looked identical to six others in the cargo hold, but it never moved and Peter never mentioned it. Inside: a Zune music player loaded with three hundred songs, left in his quarters by Yondu without a note — which was exactly how Yondu expressed things he could not say with words. A photograph folded four times, soft at the creases from years of careful handling. A playing card, the two of hearts, for reasons Peter had kept private. And at the very back, in a small pot of dark soil: a twig with three green leaves.

Baby Groot had been on the Milano for one month. He was six inches tall, had already learned to dance with complete seriousness and closed eyes, and had stolen Rocket's best multitool on at least two occasions. He drew small spirals on the navigation screens with one precise finger. He had almost certainly figured out how the Zune worked and had decided, for his own reasons, not to tell Peter.

Every Guardian tended to the small pot in the way they knew how. Rocket repositioned it whenever the ship hit rough air — stopping mid-sentence or mid-project, moving it carefully, resuming without comment, as though this were just ship maintenance. It was not just ship maintenance. Gamora received a Xandarian moonblossom as a gift from the Nova Corps and placed it next to Groot's pot; Baby Groot examined the flower for a full hour with quiet seriousness. Drax said one evening, to the room rather than anyone in particular, that Groot's act over Xandar had been a warrior's sacrifice and that he had earned a place in the stories Drax would tell when he had a family again to tell them to.

What Groot had said — "We are Groot," not "I am Groot" — carried something the whole team understood without needing to discuss. The we was the point. Not one hero and companions. Not five strangers who happened to be on the same ship. A group that had become something it could not have been separately, bound by what they had shared together.

On a quiet night between missions, Peter put the Zune on the common room speakers. Baby Groot climbed to the center of the table and stood there with eyes closed and danced — very seriously, very completely, to the music. Rocket looked up from his workbench. Drax set down his blade. Gamora lowered her screen. Nebula turned toward the sound. The Milano flew through the dark and for a moment every one of them was exactly where they were supposed to be.
