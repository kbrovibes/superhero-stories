Hey — before we start, I have a question. Have you ever made a friend completely by accident? Like you bumped into them, knocked over their lunch tray, and somehow ended up best friends anyway?

That is EXACTLY how the Guardians of the Galaxy happened. Except instead of a lunch tray, it was an alien prison break. And instead of a school cafeteria, it was the planet Xandar, which is considerably more dramatic but follows the same basic principle.

Our story starts with Peter Quill — he INSISTS you call him Star-Lord, and he says it with such total confidence that you almost don't laugh — pressing play on his Sony Walkman and strutting across ancient ruins on the dead orange planet Morag. He dances when he thinks no one is watching. BOOM — he snatches a shiny silver Orb off its pedestal, shoves it in his satchel, and dances his way back to his ship. Job done.

Except! Inside that Orb is the Power Stone. One of the most dangerous objects in the entire universe. And now EVERYONE is hunting Peter.

On Xandar, the shining home world of the Nova Corps, it all goes sideways at once. Gamora — green skin, sharp sword, a glare that could strip paint — jumps Peter to steal the Orb. Then CRASH, Rocket — that's a raccoon with a modified blaster almost as big as he is — tackles them both. Then Groot — eight feet of friendly living tree — plucks everyone off the ground like they weigh nothing, because compared to Groot, they do.

The Nova Corps arrests all four of them. WHOMP, right into the Kyln, the most secure prison in the galaxy.

And who is waiting inside? Drax. Enormous. Covered in tattoos from collar to ankle. Does not understand jokes. Does not understand sarcasm. Has been burning with grief since Ronan the Accuser destroyed his family, and has been waiting for an opportunity ever since. He decides not to immediately destroy Gamora when she walks in — which, for Drax, counts as a very warm welcome.

Rocket draws a plan on a napkin. It involves one stolen prosthetic leg, a security watchtower, and precise timing. The odds: terrible.

CRASH. BANG. ZOOOM. They blast through the Kyln and race to the docking bay and pile into the Milano — Peter's battered, beloved personal ship — and shoot out into the dark of open space.

Five strangers. One ship. One very dangerous Orb.

None of them chose each other. But here they are. Something is starting. Can you feel it?
