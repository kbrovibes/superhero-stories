Under Peter Quill's bunk on the Milano, there is a box.

It is grey, plain, and nobody has ever been asked to move it. Inside:

A Zune music player with three hundred songs — left by Yondu without a note, which was Yondu's way of saying: I know you miss the music.

A photograph, folded four times, worn soft at the edges.

A playing card: the two of hearts. Reasons private.

And in a small round pot of dark soil: a twig with three green leaves.

THAT is Baby Groot. One month after the battle of Xandar. Six whole inches tall. Absolutely convinced the Milano belongs to him.

He dances already — seriously, with eyes closed, in the way of someone for whom dancing is truth-telling, not performance. He stole Rocket's best multitool twice. He draws spirals on the navigation screens. He has figured out the Zune and decided not to tell Peter.

Everyone on the Milano tends to Baby Groot in their own way.

Rocket repositions his pot whenever the ship hits rough flying. Stops what he's doing, moves the pot carefully, goes back to work. Without a word. Just ship maintenance. DEFINITELY not just ship maintenance.

Gamora received a Xandarian moonblossom as a gift of thanks from the Nova Corps — a flower from Xandar's southern hills. She put it next to Groot's pot. Baby Groot examined it for a full hour, petal by petal, with the solemn attention of someone who respects plant life from deep personal experience.

Drax said one evening, to the room at large: "What Groot did over Xandar was a warrior's sacrifice. He held us when we could not hold ourselves. He has earned a place in the stories I will tell when I have a family to tell them to." Baby Groot's eyes were closed. He may have heard.

"We are Groot." Not "I am Groot" — the announcement, the statement of self. WE. The whole group, plural, all of them together, making something none of them could be separately.

Tonight, Peter put the Zune on the common room speakers. CLICK. Music filled the ship — warm, from Earth, a melody you feel before you name it.

Baby Groot set down the small screw he was examining. Climbed to the center of the table. Stood straight on six-inch legs. Closed his eyes.

He danced. Completely. Seriously. Because the music asked and he answered.

Rocket looked up from his workbench. His hands went still.

Drax set down his blade.

Gamora lowered her screen.

Nebula turned toward the sound.

They stayed where they were. All of them. The stars outside, the music in the air, one small tree person dancing at the center of everything.

We are Groot.
