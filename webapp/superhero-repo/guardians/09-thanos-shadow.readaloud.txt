There is one name that makes the whole galaxy go quiet when someone says it.

Thanos.

Some worlds say it like a prayer. Some say it like a curse. Some say it the way you name a force of nature — not with feeling, just with fact, because feeling about it doesn't change anything.

The Guardians had known this name from day one. Thanos sent Ronan on the mission that brought them together. Before that, he sent Gamora. Before that, Nebula. All aimed at one goal: the six Infinity Stones, which together could change everything. Permanently.

The Guardians had held one of those Stones. Peter still remembered exactly what it felt like — not just through his hands but through all of him, every nerve and thought lit up at once, and it had been killing him after three seconds. It had taken four of them together to survive it. Thanos wanted all six.

The intelligence came through a Kree contact in the port city of Hrax — a data drive, a market table, no ceremony. Four of the six Stones were located. Troop movements closing around each one. An ancient plan accelerating.

Gamora laid it out without softening it: "He's close."

Nebula, who had sat in Thanos's planning rooms and heard him describe this in the flat voice of someone speaking about an outcome that has already happened in his mind: "Closer than that."

Rocket: "I fly fast. I build weapons. I solve problems that most people call impossible. What I do is not fight a centuries-long campaign by the most powerful being alive with one ship and six people."

Drax: "What can be done?"

Peter stood at the viewport and looked at the stars for a long time. He thought about Xandar. The Stone killing him alone. Surviving only because his team reached for him. You cannot carry the impossible alone — not because you aren't strong enough, but because that is simply how the impossible is structured.

"We build allies," he said. "We warn everyone who holds a Stone that Thanos is coming for it. We start making something bigger than just us. And we stay together through all of it." He turned from the viewport. "That's where I am."

Groot reached one long arm into the center of the table, palm facing up.

Gamora placed her hand on his. Drax added his, solid and unhesitating. Nebula waited — deciding — then set hers down quietly. Rocket looked at the assembled hands with deep personal embarrassment and added his anyway. Peter put his on top.

One second. Two. Five hands plus Groot, pointed at the same direction.

"Deeply dramatic," Rocket said.

"Yes," Peter agreed.

They held it one more second. Then went to work.
