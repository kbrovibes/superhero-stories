Okay. Picture the biggest skull you have ever seen. Now make it the size of a moon. Now carve an entire city inside it — market stalls, flickering lights, the smell of engine oil and ten kinds of alien food cooking at once — and float it in deep space.

THAT is Knowhere.

It is built inside the skull of an ancient Celestial — one of the enormous beings that shaped the universe before anyone else was even born. The Guardians flew the Milano right in through the eye socket, which is exactly the kind of sentence that should exist in the world.

Their contact was the Collector, Taneleer Tivan, who kept a museum of rare objects in Knowhere that smelled of old history and new danger. He had white hair swept back like a wave, a fur-trimmed coat, and the very specific expression of someone who collects things that most people are too sensible to touch.

He lifted the Orb in a glass case, and its purple glow lit up the whole room.

"The Power Stone," he said. "One of the six Infinity Stones. Ancient beyond measuring. It can destroy entire planets. Anyone who touches it unprotected is annihilated on the spot." He turned it slowly. "I want it very much."

Everybody in the room thought about the fact that Peter had been carrying this in a regular shoulder bag.

And then — KRAAAAK — one of his captive specimens smashed through her display case. Purple energy exploded everywhere. WHAM. CRASH. The Collector's irreplaceable collection flew in all directions. The whole deal collapsed in about fifteen chaotic seconds.

Then Ronan's ship arrived. THOOM. The Dark Aster's shadow fell over Knowhere like a storm swallowing the sun.

Nebula dropped in — blue skin, mechanical arms, cold eyes — Gamora's adopted sister, both of them once raised by Thanos to fight as warriors. She tore Gamora's ship apart with barely any effort. CRACK. Gamora tumbled into the void of space. Peter grabbed a spare helmet, punched the hatch, and jumped out after her — just jumped, into open nothing — and triggered his emergency beacon.

Back on the Milano, Rocket sat very still.

The Orb was gone. Ronan had it. Peter and Gamora were drifting in the cold dark.

He said something that cannot be repeated at bedtime. Then he turned the ship around.

VROOM. Hard bank. Into the dark after his friends.

Not because it was logical. Not because the math worked. Because Peter had jumped into space for Gamora, and Rocket was not going to be the one who didn't follow.

Yondu's Ravager fleet swept in and scooped everyone up. But the important thing — the real thing — was the turning around. That was when the Guardians became real.
