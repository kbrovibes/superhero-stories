After the snap, Thanos did what he had always promised himself he would do when it was over. He left. Not in triumph, not in haste, but with the quiet purpose of someone who has finished a long, hard task and wants only stillness. He stepped through a final portal and arrived on a small, unnamed planet far from any charted galaxy — a green and warm place he called the Garden.

The Garden was his retirement. He had never wanted an empire. He did not raise monuments or demand that the surviving half of creation bow to his name. He hung the Infinity Gauntlet on a simple post beside a small stone farmhouse and never put it on again. The gauntlet was ruined anyway — the snap had burned through the Uru metal, scorched the gemstone settings, and cracked the golden frame at every joint. It hung there like a war-trophy no one had bothered to polish. Thanos grew vegetables. He watched the sunrise. He was at peace, in the hollow way of someone who has achieved their goal and discovered that achieving a goal is not the same as being whole.

The Avengers found him. Of course they found him. Thor, Captain America, Iron Man, Nebula, War Machine, Rocket, and Captain Marvel arrived on the Garden twenty-three days after the snap, following an energy trace left by the stones. They expected a fortress and found a farmhouse. They expected a warlord and found a man sitting among his crops, thin and quiet, his left arm burned nearly to uselessness by the gauntlet's energy.

Thanos told them the truth: the stones were gone. He had used them a second time to destroy them — reduced them to atoms so no one could ever undo what he had done. He called it merciful. He was convinced, as always, that he had been right. The Avengers standing in his Garden were, to his mind, proof that his plan had worked.

Thor raised Stormbreaker and brought it down, and the Mad Titan — old and burned and alone on his quiet farm — was gone. It was not a triumphant moment, just a stopping. Back on Earth, half the universe was still dust, and five long years would pass before Scott Lang found his way out of the Quantum Realm with an impossible idea. But the Avengers, who never stayed down, were already thinking.
