Close your eyes and listen very carefully, because this part of the story is told in the spaces between sounds. In the quiet before. In the instant after.

In a gleaming laboratory in the golden city of Wakanda, a princess named Shuri worked faster than she had ever worked in her life. Vision lay on the examination table — a being of golden and scarlet and grey, with a yellow stone set in his forehead like the brightest morning star. The Mind Stone. The last stone Thanos needed.

Shuri's hands moved across her holographic panels like music. She was pulling the patterns apart, very carefully, separating the Mind Stone's energy from the threads of Vision's consciousness — his thoughts, his memories, his soul — so that the stone could be removed without destroying the person.

She was close. She was so close.

Outside, the armies of Wakanda held the line.

Captain America fought in the golden fields with everything he had — that vibranium shield flashing in the sunlight, that quiet, steady voice calling commands across the comms. Black Widow moved through the Outrider army like a shadow in silver light. Bucky Barnes — who had once been lost and was now found — fought beside Falcon on the plains. King T'Challa in his Black Panther suit, fast as thought and fierce as lightning, led his Dora Milaje — the greatest warriors in Wakanda — in shining red armor against the tide.

There were so many. The Outriders were a wave that didn't stop.

Then the lightning fell.

KRAKOOM.

Thor came down from the sky like a comet, Stormbreaker in his hand blazing with storm-light. The great axe swept through the army and the thunder rolled across the Wakandan plains and the heroes on the ground felt their hearts lift. The battle was turning.

And then Thanos arrived.

He stepped through a violet portal of Space Stone light, massive and unhurried, and the air around him seemed to quiet out of respect for how certain he was. Five stones glowed on his golden gauntlet. One socket remained empty.

One by one, the heroes tried to stop him.

Sam Wilson dove from the air. Thanos brushed him aside.

T'Challa came in low and fast. Thanos stopped him with one open hand, almost gently.

Steve Rogers ran across the field and grabbed the gauntlet fist with both hands and pushed, his feet plowing furrows in the Wakandan earth, every muscle in his extraordinary body straining against a force greater than anything he had ever tried to stop. He held Thanos for two full seconds.

Thanos looked at him with something like surprise. Then he pushed back, and Steve went down.

Inside the lab, Shuri heard the alarms.

Wanda Maximoff had heard them too. She was the most powerful Avenger on Earth — possibly in the universe — and she understood what was coming. She put herself between Thanos and the lab, and she unleashed everything. Red energy like ropes of fire wrapped around him. The ground cracked. Whole sections of the city trembled. Thanos strained against it.

Wanda held him with one hand.

With the other hand — shaking, weeping, because she understood what she was doing — she reached back toward Vision.

The Mind Stone cracked. It exploded with a burst of golden light. Vision crumpled.

The stone was gone.

Thanos, free at last from Wanda's grip, looked at his gauntlet. The fifth socket was still empty. He looked at Vision's still form in the rubble.

He held up the Time Stone — green, spinning, borrowed from Doctor Strange and given freely.

He turned time backward.

You could see it happen — like watching a film run in reverse. The rubble rising. The cracks sealing. The stone un-shattering, piece by piece flying back together until it sat whole and yellow and glowing in Vision's forehead once more.

Vision stirred.

Thanos reached out. Very gently, almost tenderly, he pressed his thumb and forefinger against the Mind Stone.

It came free.

All six stones. Purple, blue, red, orange, green, yellow. Six colors, six powers, the whole universe held in one golden fist.

Thor's Stormbreaker came across the battlefield like a bolt from a dying star — KRAKOOM — and buried itself in Thanos's chest. The force of it drove Thanos to one knee.

Thanos looked at Thor with those ember-colored eyes. He was hurt. He was breathing hard.

"You should have gone for the head," he said.

He raised the gauntlet. Six stones blazing. The light from them lit up the whole Wakandan sky.

And he snapped his fingers.

One dry click. So small. So quiet. Against all the noise of the battle, you almost couldn't hear it.

Then you could hear nothing else.

On the Wakandan plain, Bucky Barnes looked down at his hand. It was turning grey, dissolving at the edges, drifting away on the wind like ash from a very old fire.

"Steve," he said. Just that. Just the name. And then he was gone.

Around him, on the great golden plain, warriors who had been fighting a moment ago simply stopped. Grey dust drifted between the trees. Sam Wilson dissolved mid-air. T'Challa looked at his hands and fell, quietly, into the Wakandan earth he had sworn to protect. Groot reached for Rocket. Rocket could not reach far enough.

On Titan, far away in the copper-sky ruins, a young man in a red suit looked at Tony Stark with wide brown eyes.

"I don't feel so good," said Peter Parker.

Tony caught him. He held him.

"I don't want to go," Peter said. "I'm sorry."

He was gone.

Half of all living things in the universe. Gone in a moment. Not with noise. Not with fire. With silence.

Thanos opened a Space Stone portal to a quiet orange world somewhere peaceful. He sat down in a field of tall golden grass and watched the sunrise spread slowly across the sky.

He was tired. His gauntlet was damaged — the six stones had been almost too much power for even him to hold. But he had done it.

He had done it.

On Earth, the remaining Avengers stood in the ash-quiet of Wakanda. They looked at each other across the emptiness where their friends had been. Steve Rogers was on one knee in the dirt, hands on the ground, not getting up yet. Natasha Romanoff stood very still, her face doing the thing it does when she is not going to let herself feel something yet because feeling it would break the moment.

The battle was over.

But this was not the end.

Because Doctor Strange — far away on Titan, looking at his own dissolving hands as the grey ash took him — whispered four words.

"It was the only way."

And somewhere in a Quantum Realm the size of an atom, a small clock was already ticking.
