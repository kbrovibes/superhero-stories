Listen.

In the bright fields outside of Wakanda, a war was raging. Captain America, Black Panther, Black Widow, Bucky Barnes, Falcon, and the Dora Milaje — the warrior women who feared nothing and no one — fought side by side against the Outriders, creatures like nightmares given shape. Thor had arrived from the sky with his new axe Stormbreaker and Rocket and Groot beside him, and for one golden moment, the army of Thanos was losing.

Inside Princess Shuri's gleaming laboratory, Vision lay still on a table, the yellow Mind Stone softly glowing in his forehead. Shuri's fingers flew over her holograms, working as fast as she could. If she could remove the stone safely, Thanos could never have it. She was so close.

Then: silence fell over the field. Not because the battle had stopped.

Because Thanos had arrived.

He came through a portal as quietly as a tide coming in. Every hero who threw themselves at him could not hold him back. The Black Order fell aside. He walked with the certain steps of someone who believed they had already won.

Wanda Maximoff did the hardest, bravest, most heartbreaking thing. With one hand she held Thanos back with all her power. With the other hand she reached into the Mind Stone — the stone that Vision had been built around, the stone that was part of his life — and she destroyed it. The gem shattered into bright fragments. Vision went still.

One breath of silence.

Then Thanos raised his other hand. The Time Stone glowed green. The moment reversed. The stone rebuilt itself, grain by grain. Vision rose up again, startled and afraid. Thanos reached out gently, carefully — and pulled the Mind Stone free.

Six colors blazed in the completed Infinity Gauntlet. All six. Every stone.

Thor buried Stormbreaker in Thanos's chest with a sound like a mountain cracking. Thanos looked at him with something that might have been sadness.

"You should have gone for the head," he whispered.

He closed his enormous fist. Raised it. And snapped his fingers.

One dry click.

And half of all living things — on Earth, on Titan, on every planet circling every star — quietly dissolved into grey ash and drifted away on the wind.

It wasn't loud. That was the worst part.

Thanos opened a portal to a quiet planet and sat down to watch the sunrise. Behind him, the remaining heroes stood in the ruins of Wakanda, in a silence that felt like it might last forever.

It didn't. But it took five years to break it.
