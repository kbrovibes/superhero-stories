In the royal city of Wakanda, Princess Shuri worked with trembling hands in her laboratory. Vision lay still on a stone slab, the yellow Mind Stone glowing softly in his forehead. If she could remove it safely, Thanos would never complete his collection. Outside on the great Wakandan plain, Captain America stood with Black Panther, Black Widow, Bucky Barnes, Falcon, and the fierce Dora Milaje — warrior women who feared nothing — as a wall of terrible creatures called Outriders rushed toward them.

"We don't trade lives," Steve Rogers said. And they ran to meet the enemy.

Then a crack of light split the sky, and Thor arrived with Rocket and Groot, the new axe Stormbreaker ringing like a bell. The army began to fall back. For a moment — just a moment — it felt like they might win.

And then Thanos stepped through a portal, quiet as a tide coming in.

Wanda Maximoff did something almost impossibly hard. With one hand she held the Mad Titan back, and with the other she destroyed the Mind Stone — ending Vision, the being she loved, to save everyone else. The yellow gem shattered into bright dust. One breath of silence. They had done it.

Then Thanos lifted the Time Stone and ran the moment backwards. The Mind Stone reassembled itself. Vision rose up, frightened. Thanos lifted him gently and pulled the stone free. Six stones now burned in the golden gauntlet, one of every color. "I am inevitable," Thanos said.

Thor drove Stormbreaker deep into the Titan's chest. Thanos looked at him almost sadly. "You should have gone for the head," he whispered.

He closed his fist and snapped his fingers. The sound was not loud. Just one dry click. And then the world went very quiet. Across Wakanda, across the stars, across everything — half of all living beings turned to soft grey ash and drifted away on the wind. And Thanos stepped through a final portal to watch the sunrise.
