Okay, can we talk about Knowhere for a moment? Because it is one of the strangest places in the entire universe, and you deserve to hear about it.

Imagine a head. A GIANT head. Not the head of a person — the head of a Celestial, which is a being so enormous that its skull is the size of a small city. This head was severed long ago, long before anyone alive can remember, and it floated away to the very edge of the known universe, where the stars get thin and space starts to behave strangely.

And someone — a creature named Taneleer Tivan, who called himself the Collector — moved in and built a museum inside it.

THE COLLECTOR'S MUSEUM. Cases and cages and glass tanks stretching for miles through that ancient skull — one of everything: every species, every artifact, every rare and impossible thing from a thousand galaxies. And right there, at the heart of it all, was a small shifting drop of liquid red light.

The Aether. Also known as the Reality Stone.

It could change matter into whatever the holder imagined. Walls into sand. Floors into sky. An enemy's weapon, bending away just before it hit. Reality itself — BENDING.

Thanos wanted it.

He sent the Black Order in first — CRASH, SMASH, toppling the cases and shattering the glass — and then he walked in himself, patient and steady, through the chaos of scattered exhibits and broken display cases, all the way to the one he had come for.

He reached into the case.

The Aether flowed toward his hand like it had been waiting for him. And then — snap — it crystallized into a small faceted red stone. It settled into the third setting of the Infinity Gauntlet.

THREE stones. Purple, blue, red. The gauntlet was more than halfway done.

Now, the Guardians of the Galaxy had been chasing Thanos — Gamora, Drax, Star-Lord, Mantis, Nebula — and they arrived at Knowhere just in time to be too late. Thanos had the stone. And with the Reality Stone, he could change what was real around him, so nothing anyone saw in that place could fully be trusted.

He showed Gamora. The world flickered and bent and shifted around her — solid things going soft, air going solid. A demonstration of what he now held.

And then he left.

Three settings still empty. But three stones already glowing.

And ahead of him, on a frozen world at the edge of everything, waited the hardest stone of all. The Soul Stone. The one that would cost him something nothing in the universe could replace.
