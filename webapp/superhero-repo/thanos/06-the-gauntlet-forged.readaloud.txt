Okay, here is something important to understand about Thanos: he was patient. He did not just grab and go. Before he could collect even ONE Infinity Stone, he needed something to hold them all — and that something had to be absolutely extraordinary.

He needed the Infinity Gauntlet.

And there was only one place in the whole universe where something like that could be forged.

NIDAVELLIR.

Say it with me: NID-ah-VEER. It is a dwarf forge — the greatest forge in all the nine realms — and it is built around a NEUTRON STAR. Do you know what a neutron star is? It is what is left after a regular star collapses — smaller than a city, but so dense that a single teaspoon of it weighs more than a skyscraper. The heat and gravity of that star is what lets the dwarves smelt Uru metal — the incredible material they used to make Mjolnir, Thor's hammer.

The king of Nidavellir was named Eitri — and when Thanos arrived with the Black Order, Eitri knew right away that this was not a friendly visit.

BOOM. The doors to the forge hall opened.

Thanos walked in, filling the corridor with his purple presence, and made his demand. "Forge me a gauntlet of Uru gold. Six stone settings. Sized to my hand. Do this, and your two hundred dwarves survive."

Eitri had no choice. He opened the ring around the star — the ancient mechanism that flooded the forge with impossible heat — and his smiths worked for three days and three nights. No rest. No stopping. The roar of the forge filled the whole ring-world.

And at the end of those three days, there it was: a golden gauntlet, gleaming and perfect, every joint precise, every groove shaped to channel the power of the Infinity Stones.

Thanos pulled it onto his hand. He looked at it. He opened and closed his huge fist.

Then he destroyed the forge mechanism with a single gesture — CRACK — so no one could ever use Nidavellir to build a weapon against him.

He left Eitri standing alone on the silent ring, his forge cold, his people gone.

Eitri looked at his burned hands. He looked at the empty hall. And he promised himself that one day, someone would come to Nidavellir who was worthy. Someone he could help build something that would fight back.

Thanos had no idea he had just made the biggest mistake of his plan.

The gauntlet gleamed on his fist, six empty settings like six open eyes.

It was time.
