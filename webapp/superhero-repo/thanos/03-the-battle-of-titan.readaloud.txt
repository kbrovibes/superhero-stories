The first great fight with Thanos did not happen on Earth. It happened on the dusty ruins of his own dead homeworld — the planet Titan. And into that fight came a very unlikely group: Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Spider-Man, and the Guardians of the Galaxy — Mantis, Drax, and Star-Lord — all finding themselves in the same place at once.

Doctor Strange held up the Eye of Agamotto — his green Time Stone — and looked forward through fourteen million possible futures. He searched them all, every single one, looking for the path where the heroes won. He found exactly one. He said nothing about what it was.

The plan was daring. Mantis would climb onto Thanos's enormous shoulders and use her power to calm his mind, sending the Mad Titan into a kind of trance. While he was held still, the others would work together to pull the golden Infinity Gauntlet right off his hand. And do you know what? It almost worked. Tony's armor gripped the gauntlet. Spider-Man's webs held the Titan in place. The glove was sliding off, inch by inch.

Then Star-Lord — whose real name was Peter Quill — learned that Gamora was gone. His grief hit him like a wave, and he struck Thanos in the face before anyone could stop him. Thanos's eyes snapped open. The gauntlet slammed back down. Everything fell apart at once.

The battle that followed was fierce and frightening. Thanos pulled a piece of the moon out of orbit and dropped it in chunks onto the heroes. Doctor Strange split himself into a thousand glowing copies. Spider-Man clung to falling debris, terrified but holding on. And Tony Stark fought with everything he had.

It was not enough. Thanos drove a blade into Tony's side and stood over him. Then Doctor Strange raised one hand and let the green Time Stone float away. "One life for the universe," he whispered to Tony. Thanos took the stone, gave them all a long look, and stepped through a portal toward Earth.
