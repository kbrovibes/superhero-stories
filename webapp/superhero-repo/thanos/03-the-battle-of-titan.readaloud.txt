What are the odds of winning a fight against someone who has five Infinity Stones on their fist?

Doctor Strange looked through fourteen million possible futures — every single one — to find out. He sat very still, his eyes moving through things only he could see, sorting through a million bad endings and a million worse ones. Then he opened his eyes.

"One," he said quietly. Just one future led to victory. He didn't say which.

The heroes had come to the ruins of Titan — Thanos's own dead homeworld, all crumbling stone and blown sand — because that was where Thanos was going to be. Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Spider-Man, and the Guardians of the Galaxy had all converged here by different routes: some intentionally, some because things had gone sideways, and one of them because he had grabbed onto a spaceship without permission.

The plan was clever. Mantis — whose power let her reach into someone's emotions and calm them — would climb onto Thanos's enormous shoulders and quiet his mind. While he was dazed, everyone else would pry the Infinity Gauntlet right off his hand. Spider-Man's webs laced across the Titan's wrists. Tony's nanotech armor gripped the glove. It was SLIDING — they were SO close — the gauntlet was actually coming off —

Then Star-Lord — Peter Quill — learned that Gamora was gone. And his grief was bigger than his self-control. He struck Thanos in the face.

The gauntlet snapped back on with a sound like a door slamming forever.

Oh no. Oh, oh no.

Thanos woke up properly angry. He reached out to the sky above, grabbed a chunk of the moon out of orbit — just casually ripped a piece of the moon out of SPACE — and dropped it down in boulders onto the heroes. Doctor Strange split into a thousand shimmering mirror copies to buy time. Peter Parker clung to tumbling debris, terrified, web-slinging from chunk to chunk in the dark.

Tony Stark fought until a blade made from his own nanotech was buried in his side. He lay there, bleeding, and Thanos stood over him.

Doctor Strange lifted one hand and let the green Time Stone float away from his neck.

"One life," he said to Tony, almost apologetic, "for the universe."

Thanos took the stone. Five colors blazed. Then he looked at all of them — beaten, battered, still standing — and stepped through a portal to Earth.

Before he left, he looked at Tony Stark and said: "You have my respect, Stark."

Tony was not particularly comforted by this.
