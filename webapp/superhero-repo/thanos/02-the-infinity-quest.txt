The Infinity Stones were the oldest objects in existence — six fragments of singularities that had survived the birth of the universe. Each held dominion over a single law of reality: Space, Mind, Reality, Power, Time, and Soul. For most beings they were too dangerous to even touch, but Thanos had spent a lifetime preparing himself for them. He commissioned the dwarf-king Eitri of Nidavellir to forge a gauntlet of solid Uru — a golden glove engineered to channel the impossible energy of all six stones at once. With the gauntlet on his hand, Thanos began his quest.

The Power Stone, a glowing purple sphere, was kept inside the Orb on the planet Xandar, protected by the Nova Corps. Thanos sent his Black Order to break the planet open. By the time the Avengers heard the news, Xandar had fallen and the stone was already pressed into the gauntlet. The Space Stone was easier — it lived inside the Tesseract, a glowing blue cube once kept on Asgard. Loki, frightened and clever, surrendered it on the refugee ship from Asgard, hoping to save his brother Thor’s life. Thanos crushed the cube in his fist and slotted the stone next to its purple twin.

The Reality Stone, red and liquid-like, hid inside the body of the Collector, deep in the dusty halls of Knowhere. The Mind Stone, a soft yellow gem, rested in the forehead of the synthezoid Vision, far away on Earth. The Time Stone, glowing green, hung around the neck of Doctor Strange in a circular amulet called the Eye of Agamotto. And the Soul Stone, the strangest of them all, waited on the cliffs of a frozen world called Vormir, watched over by a hooded ghost who whispered a single rule: "A soul for a soul." Thanos took Gamora to that cliff, looked at the daughter he had raised, and threw her into the abyss. He emerged from the snow with the orange Soul Stone in his palm, and tears, for once, in his eyes.

By the time Thanos turned toward Earth, only the Mind Stone and the Time Stone remained outside his gauntlet. Five glowing colors burned along the back of his hand — purple, blue, red, orange, green soon to be added. He was no longer just a warlord. He was becoming something closer to a god of arithmetic, a being who could rewrite the rules of the universe with a thought. And he had decided exactly what equation he wanted the universe to solve.
