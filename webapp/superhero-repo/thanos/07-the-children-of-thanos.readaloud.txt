You know how every hero has a team? Captain America has the Avengers. The Guardians have each other. Even Thor has his friends.

Well. Thanos had a team too.

He called them his children. His family. The Black Order. And they were TERRIFYING.

First: Corvus Glaive. Say that name like it sounds — sharp and final. Glaive. He was Thanos's most trusted general, and he carried a blade — also called a glaive — that could cut through ANYTHING. Not just armor. Not just stone. Anything. Even the molecular bonds that held an Infinity Stone in its setting. If you needed something cut open anywhere in the universe, Corvus Glaive was your person. He moved through battle quietly, efficiently, like the closing of a door.

His partner was Proxima Midnight. She carried a spear that had been forged from a dying star — and when she threw it, it split into THREE shafts of light in midair, each one tracking a different target. Three-in-one. Captain America himself needed backup to fight her. That should tell you something.

Then there was Ebony Maw. Now here was someone interesting. He did not punch anything. He did not need to. Ebony Maw could move things with his mind — telekinesis — reshaping rooms into traps without touching a single object. And he could talk. He could talk his way through any door, past any guard, into any situation. Smooth and quiet and always polite. He called Thanos "my father." He meant it.

And the biggest? Cull Obsidian. Also called Black Dwarf. An absolute COLOSSUS — larger even than Thanos's other children, wearing armor so thick that energy blasts just — THWUNK — bounced off him like rain. His weapon was a chain-axe that could flatten buildings. When Cull Obsidian showed up, the battle was serious.

And then — Gamora and Nebula. You already know their stories. They did not choose this family. They were taken in from conquered worlds and shaped, trained, and loved in a way that cost them both something important. But they were stronger for surviving it, and in the end they each found their own way.

The Black Order believed in Thanos. Most of them, truly. They had seen the worlds he had "saved" — or thought they had. They followed him with real conviction.

They fought for him until the very last battle. Until Doctor Strange's portals opened over Wakanda and every hero came home at once.

And then — one by one — they turned to dust in the sunlight.

Because Thanos's army, no matter how fierce, was no match for an Avenger who had something to fight for.
