After the snap — after the grey dust, after the silence spreading across every galaxy, after the universe changed forever — Thanos did something nobody expected.

He went home.

Not to a palace. Not to a throne room or a victory celebration. He stepped through a final portal and arrived on a small, quiet, unnamed green planet that nobody had ever charted, somewhere far from any galaxy in the files.

He called it the Garden.

No fortress. No army. A small stone farmhouse, a field, and sunrises. Thanos hung the Infinity Gauntlet on a post by the door — CLUNK — and never put it on again.

The gauntlet was wrecked anyway. The snap had burned through the Uru metal, scorched the settings, cracked the frame at every joint. It hung there like something completely used up.

And Thanos — the Mad Titan, the most feared being in the universe — grew vegetables. Watched the sun rise. Sat in the quiet.

Was he happy? No. Not exactly. He was at peace in the way you can be when you have spent your whole life certain you had to do something and you have finally done it. "At peace" is not the same as "happy." It is just very, very still.

He had been there twenty-three days when the Avengers found him.

Thor. Captain America. Iron Man. Nebula. War Machine. Rocket. Captain Marvel. They followed the energy trace left by the stones across the dark and arrived ready for a fortress, ready for a warlord at full strength.

They found a farmhouse.

They found a man sitting in his crops, his left arm burned thin and nearly useless. Quiet. Not running.

He told them the truth.

He had used the stones a second time — right after the snap — to destroy them. Reduced all six to scattered atoms so no one could ever undo his work. He was telling them because he wanted them to understand it was over.

Thor raised Stormbreaker.

The blade came down.

It was not triumphant. Just a stopping — the end of Thanos, not the end of the problem, because the problem was still everywhere, half the universe still dust.

Five long years would follow. Until Scott Lang tumbled out of the Quantum Realm with an impossible idea. Until Tony Stark solved the equation nobody else could.

The Avengers — who never, ever stop — found the way.

But the Garden kept growing.

And on its wooden post, a burned and ruined golden gauntlet waited in the morning air, a reminder that even the most unstoppable plan can be undone by the most stubborn kind of hope.
