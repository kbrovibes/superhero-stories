Five years passed. The world was quieter and sadder than it had ever been. The Avengers who remained kept watch over a world half-empty, carrying the grief of everyone they had lost. Then Scott Lang — the Ant-Man — tumbled out of the Quantum Realm with a wild, impossible idea. Time travel. Use the tiny hidden spaces between seconds to go back, borrow the six Infinity Stones before Thanos ever found them, bring them home, and undo everything. The team called it the Time Heist.

It cost dearly. On the cliffs of Vormir, Natasha Romanoff and Clint Barton each tried to be the one to give their life for the orange Soul Stone. Natasha was faster. Clint came home with the stone in his shaking hands, and a loss that would never fully heal. Tony Stark and Bruce Banner built a new gauntlet — red and gold, sized for a human hand — and Hulk put it on and snapped his fingers. Across the world, the lost came home. Birds returned to the sky. Families were whole again.

But a past version of Thanos had been watching through Nebula's memories. His warship, the Sanctuary II, came out of the sky and turned the Avengers Compound to rubble in one terrible rain of fire.

Then Doctor Strange opened the portals.

One, then ten, then a hundred glowing circles appeared across the broken battlefield, and through them came everyone. Black Panther and his Wakandan warriors. The Guardians of the Galaxy. The sorcerers of Kamar-Taj. Bucky and Falcon and Wanda and Spider-Man. Pepper Potts in her gleaming Rescue armor. And Steve Rogers, lifting Mjolnir above his head, lightning and all, saying the words: "Avengers… Assemble."

The battle was enormous. Wanda Maximoff poured every bit of her grief into fighting Thanos, and it nearly broke him. Captain Marvel punched clean through Sanctuary II. In the final, breathless moment, Thanos seized the gauntlet. Tony Stark lunged at him, bled, and appeared to lose. Thanos raised his fist. "I am inevitable."

Nothing happened. Tony had used his nanotech suit to move all six stones to his own hand.

He looked at the Mad Titan with steady, tired eyes. "And I… am… Iron Man."

He snapped his fingers. Thanos and every soldier he had ever called dissolved into the wind, just like dust. The universe was safe — not because of cold arithmetic, but because of one man's love for it. Tony Stark closed his eyes, and that was how it was.

And that is how Thanos, the Mad Titan who had waited centuries for his moment, was undone — not by a god or a monster, but by a man in an armored suit who chose, one last time, to be a hero.
