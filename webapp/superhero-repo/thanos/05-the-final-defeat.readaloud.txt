Five years. A smaller, quieter world. And then — a plan.

Scott Lang tumbled out of the Quantum Realm with only five hours of memories but five years of world left behind. He had an idea: use the Quantum Realm to travel through time, borrow the Infinity Stones from the past, and undo everything Thanos had done.

It cost dearly. On the frozen cliffs of Vormir, Natasha Romanoff and Clint Barton each tried to be the one to stay. The Soul Stone demanded a soul. Natasha was faster. Clint came home with tears that never quite dried.

Hulk put on the new red-and-gold gauntlet, clenched his fist, and snapped. SNAP. The lost came home — blinking, whole, HERE.

For about thirty seconds, everything was wonderful.

Then past-Thanos found his way through Nebula's memories into the present, and the Sanctuary II came out of a dark portal and bombed the Avengers Compound into rubble. The sky turned the color of smoke.

And Doctor Strange opened the portals.

One. Ten. A hundred. Orange circles blazed across the broken battlefield, and through every one stepped someone who had been lost. T'Challa and the armies of Wakanda. The Guardians of the Galaxy. The sorcerers of Kamar-Taj. Bucky Barnes, Sam Wilson, Wanda Maximoff, Spider-Man. Pepper Potts in gleaming Rescue armor. Giant-Man, big as a building. EVERYONE.

Steve Rogers — carrying Mjolnir, lightning crackling around his arm — looked out at the assembled might of every hero who had ever stood up for this world, and he said the words:

"Avengers. ASSEMBLE."

The battle was so big it shook the ground. Wanda fought Thanos one-on-one and he called in an airstrike on himself rather than keep facing her — that is how terrifying she was. Captain Marvel punched through Sanctuary II like a comet through paper.

And in the final, everything-on-the-line moment, Thanos grabbed the gauntlet and raised his fist to snap. The universe — again, a second time — balanced on the edge of that one sound.

"I am inevitable," he said.

He snapped.

Nothing happened.

Tony Stark had used his nanotech suit to transfer all six stones to his own fingers while nobody was watching. Six stones. A human body. The kind of energy that could kill a god.

Tony looked at Thanos. Quiet. Steady. Tired.

"And I," he said, "am Iron Man."

He snapped his fingers. Thanos and every soldier he had ever called dissolved into the wind. The universe was safe — not because of cold arithmetic, but because a man loved it enough to give everything he had.

Tony Stark closed his eyes.

And the sky above the battlefield was just sky again — wide and quiet and full of stars.
