At the edge of the known universe, where space frays into strange currents and the stars grow thin, floated the severed head of a Celestial — an ancient giant so enormous that its skull was the size of a city. This place was called Knowhere, and inside the bone-cathedral of that great dead head, a creature called the Collector had built the most extraordinary museum in existence. Taneleer Tivan had spent centuries gathering one of everything: every species, every artifact, every oddity across a thousand galaxies. And hidden in the heart of his collection, behind thick glass in a sealed case, was the Reality Stone.

The Reality Stone did not look like a stone. It was red and liquid, shifting like a drop of colored water that refused to sink. Its old name was the Aether, and it could change matter into any form the holder imagined. In the wrong hands, it could make the solid world seem like a dream — walls becoming sand, floors becoming sky, an enemy's weapons bending away mid-strike. The Collector had kept it safely locked away. He knew exactly what it was, and exactly what would happen if someone dangerous came to collect it.

Someone dangerous came to collect it.

Thanos sent the Black Order to Knowhere ahead of him. They swept through the Collector's museum like a tide, toppling cases and shattering glass, the exhibits scattering in chaos. When Thanos himself arrived, he moved through the halls with the same steady patience he brought to everything — past the cages and the dioramas and the shelves of impossible things — until he found what he had come for. He reached into the case and the Aether flowed into his palm, and in a single breath of red light it crystallized into a small, faceted red stone that settled into the third setting of the Infinity Gauntlet.

The Guardians of the Galaxy arrived to stop him — Gamora, Drax, Star-Lord, Mantis, Nebula — but they were already too late. Thanos had the stone. With it, he could change what was real. He could make Knowhere look exactly as it always had, calm and quiet, right up until the moment he had what he needed. He showed Gamora the power of the Reality Stone with something that was almost a demonstration: the world flickering and bending around her like a nightmare that followed its own rules. Nothing she saw in that place could be fully trusted.

Thanos left Knowhere with four stones glowing in the gauntlet — purple, blue, yellow had yet to join, but red burned alongside the others — and turned toward the hardest part of his quest. He still needed the Soul Stone. And the Soul Stone would cost him something no gauntlet could hold.
