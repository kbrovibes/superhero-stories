The six Infinity Stones are the oldest things in the universe — little pieces of the very beginning of everything. Each one holds power over a single great law: Space, Mind, Reality, Power, Time, and Soul. Most living things cannot even touch them safely. But Thanos had been preparing his whole life.

First, he traveled to a forge called Nidavellir, where a dwarf-king named Eitri lived. He forced Eitri to make him something extraordinary — a golden gauntlet of solid Uru metal, designed to hold all six stones at once and channel their impossible power. With the gauntlet on his enormous hand, the quest began.

The purple Power Stone lived inside an object called the Orb, kept safe by the brave Nova Corps on the planet Xandar. Thanos sent the Black Order to take it. By the time anyone could stop them, Xandar had fallen and the purple stone glowed in the gauntlet. The blue Space Stone was easier — it lived inside the Tesseract, the glowing blue cube that had once sat on Asgard. Loki, trying to protect his brother Thor aboard a refugee ship, surrendered it. Thanos crushed the cube in his bare hand and pressed the stone into place.

One by one the stones came to him. The red Reality Stone from a dusty place called Knowhere. Five stones still to go — and then four — and then three. The green Time Stone hung around the neck of Doctor Strange as the Eye of Agamotto, and the yellow Mind Stone shone in the forehead of a being called Vision. But first came the hardest.

On the frozen cliffs of a planet called Vormir, a hooded figure whispered the rule: a soul for a soul. Thanos looked at Gamora — the daughter he had raised, the girl he loved in his strange and terrible way — and he made his choice. He came back with the orange Soul Stone in his hand, and tears, just this once, on his face. Five colors now burned along the back of the golden gauntlet. Only two stones remained.
