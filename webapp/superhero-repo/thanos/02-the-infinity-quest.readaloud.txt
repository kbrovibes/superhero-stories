Six stones. Six colors. Six kinds of impossible power.

Are you ready for the list? Because this is a BIG list.

The blue Space Stone — which could open a door to ANYWHERE in the universe, no matter how far. The yellow Mind Stone — which lived in the forehead of a being called Vision and could reach into any thought. The red Reality Stone — a liquid-like gem that could make anything look like anything else. The purple Power Stone — which could destroy a planet if you held it in your bare hand. The green Time Stone — which could reverse moments and see the future, worn as the Eye of Agamotto by Doctor Strange. And the orange Soul Stone — the strangest and most secret of all, hiding on the frozen cliffs of a planet called Vormir.

Thanos wanted every single one. And he started by commissioning a glove.

He traveled to a place called Nidavellir — a forge powered by a dying star — where a dwarf-king named Eitri lived. Eitri was the greatest craftsman in any galaxy. Thanos forced him to build something extraordinary: the Infinity Gauntlet, hammered from solid Uru metal, designed to hold all six stones at once and survive the impossible energy they would release.

Then the collecting began.

The purple Power Stone was first — destroyed the planet Xandar and its brave Nova Corps. The blue Space Stone was next — it lived in the Tesseract, the glowing blue cube that Loki surrendered aboard an Asgardian refugee ship to save Thor. Thanos CRUSHED the cube in his hand and slipped the stone into place like pressing a gem into a ring. Crunch.

The red Reality Stone came from a dusty place called Knowhere. The Soul Stone waited on the cliffs of Vormir behind a terrible price: a soul for a soul. Thanos looked at Gamora, the daughter he had raised, and made a choice that even he knew was wrong. He came back with the orange stone and tears drying on his enormous purple face.

Five glowing colors blazed along the back of the golden gauntlet. Only the green Time Stone — around Doctor Strange's neck on Earth — and the yellow Mind Stone — in Vision's forehead in Wakanda — remained.

The Avengers were coming. But Thanos was almost done.

And when Thanos was almost done, there was very, very little time left.
