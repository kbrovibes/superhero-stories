The X-Men's first encounter with Erik Lehnsherr — the man who called himself Magneto — was not a quiet affair. Magneto had led his Brotherhood of Mutants to the Statue of Liberty on a cold November night, intending to use a machine of his own invention to rewrite the genetic code of every world leader attending a summit on nearby Ellis Island. His argument, delivered in the ringing voice of a man who believed utterly in his cause, was that humanity would never accept mutants — so mutants must transform humanity first.

Professor Xavier had seen this day coming in Cerebro's readings for weeks. He had tried to reach Erik by phone. Erik had not answered.

The Blackbird set down on the south shore of Liberty Island while the Brotherhood held the observation deck. Magneto's team was formidable: Mystique, who could become anyone; Sabretooth, massive and wild-tempered; and Toad, fast and clinging to every surface above them. Cyclops led the team up, splitting them on Xavier's instruction — Jean and Storm taking the exterior, Cyclops and Beast pushing through the torch access stairwell.

The fight was sharp and complicated. Mystique disguised herself as Storm and almost fooled Cyclops from behind — but Beast spotted the wrong shadow and warned him a half-second before. Jean wrapped Toad in a band of telekinetic force and deposited him, firmly, in the Hudson River. Storm drove a crosswind across the observation deck that staggered Sabretooth long enough for Cyclops to fire once. The great metal struts of the torch platform bent under Magneto's influence, but Jean held the upper platform level long enough for Cyclops to get the angle.

Magneto was powerful — the most powerful mutant any of them had faced. When he raised his hands, every metal surface on Liberty Island groaned and buckled. He was not cruel. He was angry, and he believed his anger was righteous. That was the hardest thing about facing him.

Cyclops hit him with a narrow optic blast, just precise enough to break the electromagnetic ring powering his machine. The device made a sound like a chord struck on a broken piano, and then went dark. Magneto looked at the smoking remains for a long moment.

"The humans will never love us, Charles," he said quietly, though Xavier was not physically there.

Jean heard it in her earpiece and saw Xavier close his eyes on the other end of the line.

"Perhaps not everyone," Xavier said. "But we are not doing this to be loved, Erik. We are doing it because it is right."

Magneto said nothing more. He surrendered and was taken in, and the summit at Ellis Island continued without incident.
