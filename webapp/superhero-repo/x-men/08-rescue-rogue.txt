When Rogue didn't come back from a solo reconnaissance in Montreal, the team knew within forty minutes. Gambit had noticed her empty chair at dinner and had been quiet in a particular way that everyone recognized. Jean confirmed through a faint psychic trace that Rogue was alive but unable to move — held somewhere cold, elevated, in an unfamiliar building. Cerebro narrowed the location: a Sentinel staging facility on the St. Lawrence River operated by a group called the Purifiers.

The facility was designed to stop mutants specifically. Its walls were lined with power-dampening fields in the upper floors that weakened the abilities of anyone inside. Getting Rogue out required every member of the team and a precise sequence.

Nightcrawler went first. Kurt Wagner — who had joined the X-Men three months ago from a circus in Bavaria, a young man with blue-grey skin, a pointed tail, and the ability to teleport with a sound like a thunderclap and the smell of sulfur — could BAMF through solid surfaces. He teleported to the loading dock below the power-dampening floor and unlocked the service entrance from the inside, taking the guards completely by surprise. BAMF. BAMF. BAMF. Three quick teleports through three locked rooms.

Storm and Wolverine came through the service entrance the moment it was open. Storm created a localized fog bank outside the building, reducing visibility for anyone watching the exterior. Logan moved through the ground floor while Storm held the perimeter. Gambit came in from the roof — three charged cards to the security room's panel, and every interior camera went dark.

Jean was already in contact with Rogue through the psychic trace, guiding her awake. "Stay still. Two minutes." Beast found the room from the floor plan and had the door open in thirty seconds. Cyclops held the exit, visor charged.

Rogue was cold and disoriented, her powers suppressed by the dampening field but otherwise unharmed. Beast carried her down three flights and out through the service door in under ninety seconds. Nightcrawler BAMF'd her and Beast to the tree line in two jumps. The Blackbird was warm and waiting.

On the flight back, Rogue sat wrapped in a blanket with Gambit next to her, not saying anything, just present. She looked at the seven people who had come to Quebec on a Tuesday night because she hadn't come home.

"You didn't have to come all together," she said. It sounded like a protest, but it wasn't.

"Everyone's here," Cyclops said simply, from the front.

Rogue pulled the blanket tighter. Outside, the St. Lawrence glittered below them and fell away behind.
