Twelve young mutants had gone missing over three weeks — students from schools in Boston, Chicago, and Philadelphia, all taken within hours of manifesting their powers for the first time. A group called the Mutant Control Agency had begun covertly identifying new mutants and taking them to a facility in the Nevada desert. Professor Xavier learned this through Cerebro, his signal amplified by Jean Grey sitting beside him to extend his range. It was the deepest either of them had ever pushed the machine, and Xavier emerged from the chamber pale and silent.

"We go tonight," he said.

The Blackbird carried all seven X-Men — the original four, plus Wolverine, Rogue, and Gambit, who had joined the school in the months after its founding. Rogue was nineteen, wearing long gloves that she never removed, because her bare skin absorbed anyone's powers and memories on contact. Gambit, a young man from New Orleans named Remy LeBeau, could charge any object with kinetic energy until it exploded. He kept a deck of cards in his jacket at all times.

The Nevada facility was designed to be impenetrable. It was not designed to account for all seven X-Men working in a single continuous sequence. Beast analyzed the ventilation maps on the jet's screen. Storm created a localized blackout in the surrounding grid — just long enough. Gambit charged and threw three cards through a sensor array — they detonated precisely, blinding every exterior camera without damaging a wall. Cyclops and Wolverine went through the front in six seconds.

Inside, the twelve missing students were held in individual containment pods. Jean worked through the corridors, guiding them out with her telekinesis clearing obstacles ahead. Rogue, face to face with a locked control panel, pulled off one glove and pressed her bare hand to the wrist of a facility technician, absorbing just enough of his access codes to open every pod. She put the glove back on immediately and stepped away.

It took Rogue a moment to steady herself. Gambit was already at her elbow. "Take your time," he said quietly. She nodded.

The twelve students were afraid but relieved, and some of them cried. Ororo and Jean walked them to the Blackbird while the others held the exits. The flight home took two hours. By the time they landed at Westchester, Xavier had twelve beds prepared and twelve warm meals ready. He met every student at the bottom of the ramp.

Later that night, Cyclops found Jean on the mansion's back steps, looking at the stars.

"We got all twelve," he said, sitting next to her.

"Yes," she said. "We got all twelve."

A meteor crossed the sky above them, and they watched it quietly together.
