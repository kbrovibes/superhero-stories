Three weeks after the X-Men arrived at the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters, Professor Xavier led them down two flights of stairs to a door made of reinforced steel. Beyond it was a room as large as a gymnasium, its walls lined with hydraulic panels, laser emitters, and sensors packed tightly enough to cover every surface. Xavier called it the Danger Room, and the name was deliberately honest.

"You each have extraordinary abilities," he told them, "but raw power without training is like a match dropped in a library. Today you start learning how to be precise." Scott Summers took his position at the center of the floor, adjusted his ruby-quartz visor, and waited. The lights dimmed. Then six robotic drones the size of beach balls dropped from the ceiling, weaving in unpredictable spirals.

The next hour was humbling. Scott's optic blasts were powerful but too wide — he destroyed two drones and also one panel of the east wall. Jean Grey stopped three drones cold with her telekinesis but lost concentration and sent one bouncing into Hank McCoy's shoulder. Hank, moving with surprising grace for someone his size, vaulted off a wall and dismantled a drone with his bare hands, then slipped on the cable he had just pulled free. Ororo Munroe summoned a localized updraft that pinned two drones against the ceiling — which was excellent — but the wind also swept everyone's footing out from under them, which was less excellent.

Cyclops called for a pause. He pulled everyone into a huddle, voice level and careful. "Scott's blasts cover the top zones," he said. "Jean holds the close-range ones in place. Storm keeps the ceiling clear. Hank on my left flank, cleanup." Jean looked at him steadily. "You mean Cyclops's blasts." Scott blinked. "Right. Cyclops." It was the first time he had used the name seriously, and it fit.

They ran the sequence again. This time the drones never touched the floor. Scott's visor cut three precise beams high and right. Jean pinned two more in midair with a shimmer of concentration. Ororo shaped the wind into a tight column that held the last drone spinning harmlessly in place while Hank plucked it out of the air like fruit from a tree. Then he gently set it down.

Xavier watched from the observation window above, his hands folded in his lap. Cerebro had shown him what these four young people could become. The Danger Room, he knew, would show them.

"Again," Cyclops said, straightening his visor. And the drones dropped from the ceiling one more time.
