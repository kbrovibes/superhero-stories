Here is what you need to know about Magneto before this story goes any further.

His real name is Erik Lehnsherr. He can control metal — every scrap of it, from a paper clip to the girders of a skyscraper — with just a thought. He is brilliant, fierce, and he truly believes he is right. That is what makes him different from most villains: not fun or money.

He is doing it because he is angry, and his anger is complicated.

On a cold November night, Magneto led his Brotherhood of Mutants to the Statue of Liberty. His plan: use an invention of his own to rewrite the genetics of every world leader at the Ellis Island summit. If they won't accept us, his thinking went, we will transform them.

Professor Xavier had been watching this coming in Cerebro's readings for weeks. He had called Erik. Erik had not picked up.

The X-Men flew in on the Blackbird under cover of dark. Magneto's team was waiting: Mystique, who could look like anyone; Sabretooth, enormous and wild; and Toad, who could cling to any surface at any angle. It was a dangerous combination.

The fight started FAST.

Mystique disguised herself as Storm and circled behind Cyclops — but Beast caught the wrong shadow on the wall — "CYCLOPS, LEFT!" — and Scott spun just in time. ZZZAP, optic blast, the illusion shattered.

Jean swept Toad off the observation railing with a band of telekinetic energy — WHOOSH — and launched him in a large arc toward the Hudson River below. SPLASH. Good night, Toad.

Storm shaped a sharp crosswind across the deck — WHHHHOOOO — staggering Sabretooth mid-leap. Cyclops hit him with one clean beam and he went down.

That left Magneto.

The torch platform groaned as every metal surface on Liberty Island bent toward him like iron filings near a magnet. CREAK. CREAK. CRACK. The structure shuddered. Jean planted her feet and held the platform level through sheer telekinetic force.

"Hold just ten more seconds," Cyclops told her quietly.

She nodded. Her hands were shaking. She held it.

ZZZAP — one narrow beam, perfectly placed, right into the machine's electromagnetic ring. The machine made a sound like a broken piano chord — and went dark.

Magneto lowered his hands. He looked at the smoking device for a long, still moment.

"The humans will never love us, Charles," he said.

Xavier's voice came through the earpiece, calm and certain: "We are not doing this to be loved, Erik. We are doing it because it is right."

Magneto was quiet for a moment. Then he surrendered.

The Ellis Island summit continued safely.

And somewhere in the harbor, Toad climbed out of the Hudson, dripping and annoyed.
