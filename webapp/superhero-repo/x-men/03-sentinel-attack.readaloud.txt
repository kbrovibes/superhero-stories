Picture the biggest robot you can imagine. Now make it thirty feet tall. Now put three of them on the streets of Midtown Manhattan with glowing red targeting beams sweeping back and forth across a crowd of terrified people.

Those robots are called Sentinels. They were built by a man named Bolivar Trask to hunt mutants. And right now they were closing in on a twelve-year-old girl named Maya, who had just discovered her powers last night and had absolutely no idea what to do.

Lucky for Maya — neither did they.

SCRAMBLE! The X-Men hit the Blackbird jet in under four minutes. The Blackbird is sleek and black and very, very fast. Storm wrapped it in cloud cover as they came in low over the Hudson River so no one would see them land.

From the west bank, all five X-Men could see the Sentinels clearly — enormous purple-and-grey machines grinding down the street like slow-moving catastrophes.

Cyclops didn't waste a second. "Beast — get the girl. Storm — air cover, keep their heads busy. Jean — freeze the first one's arms. I've got the joints." He said all of that in about four seconds, which is a very Cyclops thing to do.

Jean Grey raised her hands. SHWOOOOM. The nearest Sentinel's arms locked to its sides like it had been glued there, held by her telekinesis. Storm launched into the sky on a column of wind — CRACK — and drove a lightning bolt straight into the second Sentinel's sensor array. The machine's eyes went dark and it stumbled blindly into an empty bus shelter.

Beast? Beast was already there. Twenty meters in two enormous leaps — BOOM BOOM — and he had Maya up in one arm and safe behind a concrete barrier before the first Sentinel even noticed he had moved.

That left Cyclops with the angles he needed.

ZZZAP! One beam — Sentinel number one's knee joint buckled. CRASH. It sat down on the pavement like a very large, very confused purple statue.

The third Sentinel swung at Storm — but Jean had already redirected its targeting array with a flick of her wrist, sending its arm wide into empty air. Storm pressed it back two full city blocks with a downward gust. Cyclops fired once more.

CRACK.

The third Sentinel folded.

The street went quiet. Beast set Maya down carefully. She stared at the crumpled machines around her.

"You're safe," Jean said, kneeling to her level. "And you are not alone."

That evening, a letter arrived at Maya's house. Handwritten. Hopeful.

She already knew who it was from.
