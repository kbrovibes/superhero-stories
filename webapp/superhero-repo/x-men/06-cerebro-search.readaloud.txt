Twelve young people. Missing.

Twelve kids whose powers had just appeared — someone who could walk through walls, someone whose hands sparked electricity, someone who moved at blinding speed — and within hours of each discovery, they were gone. Taken by a covert group called the Mutant Control Agency to a locked facility in the Nevada desert.

Professor Xavier and Jean Grey sat together in the Cerebro chamber beneath the mansion. Jean placed her hands on the console and focused, lending her telepathic signal to Xavier's so the machine could reach farther than either of them could alone. It was the deepest either had ever pushed Cerebro. Xavier emerged from the chamber pale and very quiet.

"We go tonight," he said.

The Blackbird carried all seven X-Men — Scott, Jean, Ororo, Hank, Logan, and two newer members. Rogue was nineteen, from Mississippi, wearing long gloves at all times because her bare skin absorbed anyone's memories and powers on contact. Gambit was a young man from New Orleans, real name Remy LeBeau, who could charge any object with explosive kinetic energy and carried a deck of playing cards for exactly that purpose.

The team hit the Nevada facility in one continuous sequence, no gaps.

ZZZT — Storm created a localized grid blackout, three seconds of pure darkness around the building. While the cameras blinked, Gambit flicked three charged cards through the air — BOOM BOOM BOOM — three precise explosions that shredded every exterior sensor without scratching the walls.

Then Cyclops and Logan went through the front door.

Six seconds.

Inside, the twelve students were locked in individual containment pods. Jean moved through the corridors, her telekinesis sliding obstacles aside as she went. At the main control panel, Rogue stopped, took a breath, and peeled off one glove. She touched the back of a technician's wrist — just for a moment — absorbing enough of his access codes to open every single pod.

She pulled the glove back on and stepped away, breathing carefully.

Gambit was beside her in one step. "Take your time," he said quietly.

She nodded. "I'm fine."

The students poured out of their pods — twelve frightened kids who didn't know these people but understood they were being helped, and that was enough. Jean and Ororo walked them to the Blackbird, and Storm had them airborne before the facility's backup systems came back on.

Two hours later, they landed at Westchester.

Professor Xavier was at the bottom of the ramp. He had twelve warm meals ready in the kitchen. He had twelve beds made up on the second floor.

He looked at every single one of the twelve students when they stepped off that ramp, and he said the same thing to each of them.

"Welcome," he said. "You are not alone."

He meant every word.
