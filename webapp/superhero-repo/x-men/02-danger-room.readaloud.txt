Two flights of stairs below the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters, behind a door made of thick reinforced steel, is a room that would make any superhero nervous.

The Danger Room.

The walls are covered in panels, laser emitters, pressure sensors, and all sorts of gadgets that hum and blink. The ceiling is dotted with trapdoors. The floor is perfectly flat and completely empty — which is the scariest kind of room, because it means the challenge is coming from ABOVE. It also smells faintly of scorched metal, which tells you something about previous training sessions.

"Today," Professor Xavier said from the doorway, "you begin learning to work as a team."

Then he smiled, pressed a button, and stepped back.

Six robotic drones — about the size of beach balls, incredibly fast — dropped from the ceiling. WHOOOMP WHOOOMP WHOOOMP WHOOOMP WHOOOMP WHOOOMP.

Now — round one. Which did not go well. At all.

ZZZAP! Cyclops fired his optic blasts — got two drones, also part of the east wall. Oops. CRASH — Hank McCoy leaped onto a drone, dismantled it with his bare hands, then slid on the cable. "I am fine!" he announced, from the floor. Jean Grey reached out with her telekinesis — SHWOOOM — froze three drones in midair, then sneezed and accidentally flung one into Hank's shoulder. Storm called up a gust of wind that pinned two drones against the ceiling, which was brilliant, but the wind also knocked everyone else over, which was less brilliant.

The drones beeped. They were basically winning.

Cyclops held up his hand. "Stop. Nobody move. Let's think."

He stood in the middle of the room and took a breath. Then he spoke quietly, calmly, like someone who had been running plans in his head his whole life. "Jean holds the close ones still. Storm keeps the ceiling clear. Beast takes the left side. I take the top right. Ready?"

Everyone nodded.

BEEP — drones dropped again. ZZZAP — Cyclops hit two, perfectly. SHWOOOM — Jean froze three before they got near. WHOOSH — Storm's wind column pushed the last one up and up, spinning harmlessly. CLAP — Beast plucked it from the air like catching a ball and set it down gently on the floor.

Silence.

Every drone down. Zero walls destroyed. Zero people knocked over.

Up in the observation window above the Danger Room, Professor Xavier smiled a very small, very satisfied smile.

Cyclops straightened his visor. "Again," he said.

The drones went back up to the ceiling.

The X-Men got ready.

This is what training looks like.
