Dinner. Tuesday. The Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters.

One empty chair.

Rogue's chair.

Remy LeBeau — Gambit — sat very still, looking at the chair, and didn't touch his food. Everyone noticed that. Nobody needed to ask.

Jean Grey closed her eyes right there at the table and reached out with her telepathy, following a faint thread she'd been aware of all day. Cold. High floor. Unfamiliar building. She could feel Rogue — alive, but unable to move. That was enough information.

Cerebro confirmed the rest: a Purifier staging facility on the St. Lawrence River outside Montreal.

"They built it specifically for mutants," Beast said, reading on his tablet while the Blackbird engines spun up. "Power-dampening fields throughout the upper floors. Our abilities will be reduced inside."

"Not all of them," Cyclops said.

He was right. This mission needed everyone.

Here's how it worked, in order, like a perfect chain.

First: BAMF. Nightcrawler — Kurt Wagner, blue-grey, with a pointed tail and a talent for appearing and disappearing in puffs of sulfur smoke — teleported to the loading dock, bypassing the dampening floor entirely. Then BAMF to a locked door. BAMF through a second. BAMF through a third. The service entrance clicked open from the inside.

Second: Storm and Wolverine in through the door the instant it opened. Storm called up a dense fog bank around the whole building — anyone watching from outside lost their sightlines immediately. Logan moved through the ground floor, quick and quiet.

Third: Gambit dropped from the roof. He had three cards already charged in his hand. The security room's camera panel received all three — BOOM BOOM BOOM — and every interior screen went dark.

Fourth: Jean, still on the Blackbird, held her psychic line to Rogue and spoke through it. "Stay still. Two minutes."

Fifth: Beast found the room. He had the floor plan memorized. He had the door open in thirty seconds. Rogue was cold and still, her powers suppressed, but unharmed. Hank picked her up without hesitation and moved.

Sixth: Three flights of stairs, nine seconds each. Service door. Tree line. BAMF — Nightcrawler jumped with Rogue and Beast to safety in two teleport hops.

Total time from service entrance to tree line: four minutes, twenty seconds.

The Blackbird was warm. Seven X-Men waited.

On the flight home, wrapped in a blanket, Rogue looked around at all of them.

"You didn't have to come all together," she said.

"Everyone's here," Cyclops said from the front. Simple. Final.

Rogue didn't argue. She pulled the blanket up a little higher.

Outside, the St. Lawrence glittered below and fell away behind them.
