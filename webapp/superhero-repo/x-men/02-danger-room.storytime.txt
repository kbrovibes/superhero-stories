There are some rooms that look ordinary until you step inside.

The room two flights below the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters looked like a gymnasium. Clean grey floor, high ceiling, walls lined with smooth panels. But if you looked closely, you'd notice the panels all had tiny slits and sensors in them. And if you looked at the ceiling, you'd notice the trapdoors.

Professor Xavier led the X-Men inside on a cool October morning, his wheelchair humming quietly over the threshold. He stopped at the center of the room and turned to face his four students.

"This is the Danger Room," he said. "It will be your classroom for as long as you are X-Men. The lessons here are not about history or mathematics. They are about learning to trust each other completely."

Scott Summers — Cyclops — looked at the walls. He had already spotted eleven of the sensor panels. He suspected there were twenty more he hadn't found yet.

Ororo Munroe — Storm — looked up at the ceiling. She could feel the dry, still air of an enclosed space and had already begun thinking about how she would move it.

Hank McCoy — Beast — had calculated the room's volume, checked the floor's material for grip, and was quietly hoping the panels could survive a direct fall, just in case.

Jean Grey was trying very hard to hear only her own thoughts inside the metal walls. It was a little easier down here, away from the busier parts of the mansion.

Xavier pressed a button on a small remote. "Begin," he said, and stepped back to watch from the observation window above.

Six robotic drones dropped from the ceiling like little metal planets.

The first round, to put it gently, was not tidy.

Cyclops fired his optic blasts — two drones hit, plus a section of wall he had not intended to hit. He winced under his visor. Hank leaped at a drone, caught it, pulled it apart, then stepped on the cable he'd removed and went down hard on his back. "Still fine!" he called cheerfully. Jean reached out with her telekinesis and grabbed three drones out of the air — held them perfectly — and then sneezed, which broke her concentration, and one drone wobbled sideways into Hank's shoulder. Storm pulled in a gust of wind from somewhere invisible and pinned two drones against the ceiling — which was exactly right — but the gust also swept everyone else's feet from under them.

All four of them sat on the floor looking at each other.

The drones hovered above them, blinking patiently.

Scott was the first to stand. He didn't look embarrassed. He looked thoughtful, the way he always looked when he was working through a problem.

"Again," he said. "But this time, here's the plan."

He walked them through it quietly. Zones. Positions. Who would cover what part of the room. Jean holds the close ones still so nobody gets surprised. Storm keeps the ceiling clear but aims her wind upward, not outward. Beast takes the left approach — he's fast enough to cover it. Cyclops takes the far end, high shots, no stray beams.

Hank nodded along, adding one small adjustment — approach from behind a floor panel, not straight across open ground.

"Good," Scott said. "That's better than my version."

Hank looked surprised and pleased.

They reset. The drones went back up. The room went quiet except for the soft hum of Danger Room machinery.

Then the drones dropped again.

This time it was different.

Jean caught three in midair before they descended ten feet — they hung there, trembling slightly, perfectly contained. Cyclops fired two precise beams at the far upper drones — ZZZAP ZZZAP — and both vanished into puffs of sparks without touching a single wall panel. Storm sent a column of air straight up the center of the room, and the sixth drone spun harmlessly in it like a leaf in a fountain. Hank, crouched behind a floor panel, sprang sideways, plucked the spinning drone from Storm's wind column, and placed it on the floor with a small careful click.

Complete silence.

Every drone down. The walls untouched. Nobody on the floor.

Above them, through the observation window, Professor Xavier held very still for a moment. Then he clasped his hands together once, quietly, the way a person claps when something moves them and they are trying to stay composed.

Cyclops looked at Storm. Storm looked at Beast. Beast looked at Jean. Jean looked at all of them and thought — not aloud, just to herself — oh, this is what it's supposed to feel like.

"Again," Cyclops said.

The drones went back up to the ceiling.

The X-Men took their positions.

Outside the deep basement, the October sky had turned the color of warm gold. Leaves spiraled down from the old oaks along the front drive.

And underground, in the Danger Room, a team was quietly becoming one.

Sleep well, and dream of something you worked hard to learn.
