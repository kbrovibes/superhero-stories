Once a year, on the last day of December, Professor Xavier gathers the X-Men in the great hall after dinner. Not for a mission debrief. Not for a threat report. He asks them each one question.

"What surprised you most this year?"

Logan went first this time, because he sat nearest the door and looked like he wanted to get it over with. He thought for a moment and then said: "How quiet it can be here."

Everyone waited. That was all Logan said. It was also quite a lot, for Logan.

Rogue tucked her gloved hands into her lap. "That I can still be surprised," she said. She might have looked at someone when she said it, but that is between her and Gambit.

Nightcrawler — Kurt, blue and pointed-tail and smelling faintly of that particular sulfur from his BAMF — said he had been surprised that nobody asked him to hide any of that. He had grown up in places that did. The Xavier School had just handed him a uniform. That was the whole story.

Jean Grey said joy was louder than worry. She had spent years listening to other people's sadness without meaning to. Happy thoughts, she had discovered, had a completely different sound. She liked that sound considerably more.

Beast said he was surprised the boiler kept breaking. He had fixed it eleven times. ELEVEN. Everyone laughed, and he spread his large blue hands wide in an expression of complete reasonableness. He had a point.

Storm said she missed the mansion when she was away. That the Westchester hills and the old oak trees had become a kind of home-weather — a feeling she carried inside her, that went quiet when she was gone and came back warm when she returned.

And Cyclops was last, because Cyclops always took the longest. He sat with his hands around a mug of tea and looked at the table. Then he looked at the room — at Logan and Rogue and Kurt and Jean and Hank and Ororo — and said that what surprised him was a feeling he didn't have a tactical word for.

"What is it?" Kurt asked, very sincerely.

Scott looked at all of them.

"Family," he said.

The fire in the great hall popped once. Outside, snow was falling on the Westchester lawn, soft and even, covering the grass and the garden and the long front drive.

Professor Xavier looked around the table for a long moment.

"Again next year?" he asked.

"Obviously," said Logan.

Xavier smiled. Outside, the snow fell. And inside the great hall of the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters, the X-Men were home.
