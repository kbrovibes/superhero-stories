It began over breakfast on a Thursday and ended in the Danger Room at midnight, which was not how anyone had planned the day.

Cyclops had drawn up a mission plan to intercept a Brotherhood convoy moving stolen Stark Industries equipment through New Jersey. Wolverine looked at it, set it down, and said it was wrong. Cyclops said it wasn't. Logan said it was and that Cyclops was sending the team into an exposed position. Scott said Logan didn't understand tactical planning. Logan said Scott didn't understand real fieldwork. The table went very quiet.

By the afternoon the mansion was navigating around the two of them. Jean spent an hour in the library not reading a book. Storm went to the greenhouse and carefully repotted six plants. Hank worked three floors below in his laboratory, listening to music at volume.

Professor Xavier did not intervene immediately. He had learned long ago that people needed to move through their disagreements at their own pace. But by evening, when Logan was doing pull-ups in the lower gym and Scott was sitting alone on the mansion roof, Xavier sent Jean a quiet thought: ask them to meet in the Danger Room. Tonight.

Neither of them asked why Xavier wanted the Danger Room. They showed up at eleven.

Xavier had set the room to a simple scenario: two operatives, one objective, no way through except together. The scenario was not subtle. Logan took the north corridor; Cyclops took the south. They were supposed to meet at the center relay point and extract a target before the room's automated defenses reactivated.

Logan reached the relay point first. Cyclops came in from the south thirty seconds later. He had spotted an alternative exit Logan had missed — lower exposure on approach. Logan, for his part, had neutralized a sensor array that would have lit Cyclops up had he tried his route first.

They looked at each other.

"Your approach had a blind spot," Scott said.

"Yours had a sensor," Logan said.

It was quiet for a moment.

"Both routes make the other one safer," Scott said finally.

"That's the point," Logan said.

Xavier's voice came through the room speakers, dry and gentle. "The mission plan needed both of your instincts. That is why you are a team."

They walked out of the Danger Room at midnight and neither of them mentioned the morning argument again. At breakfast the next day, Logan circled a route correction on the convoy plan and slid it across the table. Scott looked at it for a moment, then nodded and made two notations of his own.

The mission, when it went, went cleanly.
