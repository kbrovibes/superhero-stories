Billy Batson had spent most of his life wishing for a family. And when he became Shazam, he discovered something wonderful: the power of the lightning was never meant to belong to just one person.

He shared it. With Freddy Freeman, who knew everything there was to know about heroes and could think his way out of any situation. With Mary Bromfield, the oldest and wisest, who kept everyone steady and on the right path. With Darla Dudley, whose joy was so bright it lit up every room she entered. With Eugene Choi, who could build and think in ways that astonished everyone. And with Pedro Peña, whose strength was matched only by his quiet, steady heart.

Together they became the Shazam Family.

And do you know what happened when they transformed together? Something extraordinary. The power of the gods created a bond between them — they could sense each other's feelings, think together almost like one mind, move in perfect harmony without a single word. They did not just fight for justice. They fought for each other.

Back at their foster home in Fawcett City, between the cosmic battles and the magical threats and the moments of real danger, they were just a family — eating together, talking, laughing, and looking out for one another.

From that day on, Billy Batson understood the greatest truth of his incredible life: the most powerful thing the Wizard ever gave him was not lightning. It was the chance to belong.
