• Teth-Adam — known as Black Adam — was the Wizard Shazam's first champion, chosen from ancient Kahndaq
• Originally fought to liberate his people; loss of family and the corruption of power turned him vengeful
• Draws power from Egyptian gods: Shu, Heru, Amon, Zehuti, Aton, and Mehen
• Views Billy Batson as an unworthy pretender to the legacy; considers himself the true heir
• Philosophy of justice: harsh, ancient, absolute — rule of the strong over the weak
• Battles with Shazam unleash massive energy; the very fabric of reality crackles under their clashes
• Complex motivations: fiercely protective of Kahndaq — occasionally allies with justice when his people are threatened
• Shazam fights Black Adam with hope and compassion, not just strength; grieves what Adam could have been
• Black Adam: a dark reflection of what a champion becomes when power strips away mercy
