When Billy Batson is not flying through the sky as Shazam, he is sitting at a microphone at WHIZ Radio in Fawcett City, telling the truth.

Billy became a reporter because he believed that the power of a good story is just as important as the power of a lightning bolt. He covered everything — local news, human-interest stories, breaking emergencies — always trying to present the facts with honesty and care. His colleagues at the radio station liked him immediately. He had a way of connecting with people that felt warm and genuine, the kind of warmth that comes from truly listening.

Keeping his secret was always a little bit of an adventure. When a story broke and Billy needed to transform, he used the chaos as cover. One moment he was mild-mannered Billy Batson, shuffling his notes and looking apologetically nervous. The next moment — well, the next moment there was a loud crack of thunder and Shazam was already on the scene.

And do you know what journalism taught Billy about being a hero? That asking good questions, following the evidence, and telling the truth clearly can stop a villain just as surely as a bolt from Zeus. When enemies lie and deceive, a hero who knows how to find the truth has a very powerful weapon indeed.

From that day on, Billy Batson understood that a reporter and a champion have the same job: to protect the truth and use it to help the people who need it most.
