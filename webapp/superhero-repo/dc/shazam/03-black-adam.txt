From the ancient sands of Kahndaq, thousands of years ago, came Teth-Adam, the first champion chosen by the Wizard Shazam. Possessed of the same divine powers as Billy Batson, Adam was initially a man of noble intent who sought to liberate his people from the tyranny of their oppressors. However, the corrupting influence of power and the loss of his family turned his heart to stone, transforming him into the vengeful and nearly unstoppable Black Adam. He views himself as the true and only legitimate heir to the Wizard’s power, and he considers Billy Batson an unworthy pretender to the throne.

The rivalry between Shazam and Black Adam is a clash of two different eras and two different philosophies of justice. While Billy represents the hope and the innocence of the modern age, Adam represents the harsh, uncompromising justice of the ancient world. He believes that order can only be maintained through absolute power and that those who are weak must be ruled by the strong. For Black Adam, every battle is a chance to prove his superiority and to reclaim the legacy that he believes was stolen from him. He is a monarch who will stop at nothing to restore his kingdom and his honor.

Black Adam’s connection to the gods of Egypt—Shu, Heru, Amon, Zehuti, Aton, and Mehen—provides him with a level of power that matches Shazam’s own. He is a master of combat and a strategist of the highest order, his centuries of experience making him a formidable opponent for the youthful Billy. Their encounters often result in massive displays of raw energy and physical might, with the very fabric of reality crackling under the intensity of their clash. For Shazam, these battles are a constant test of his resolve and his belief in the inherent goodness of humanity, even when faced with a darkness that is as ancient as time.

Despite his villainous activities, Black Adam is a man of complex motivations. He is fiercely protective of his home country, Kahndaq, and he has occasionally found himself allied with the side of justice when a greater threat to his people emerges. These instances of temporary heroism reveal the flicker of the man he once was, showing that he is more than just a one-dimensional villain. He is a ruler who values his people above all else, even if his methods of protecting them are often at odds with the values of the modern world. He is a survivor who has carved out his own place in history.

Shazam views Black Adam with a mixture of awe and profound sadness. He recognizes the tragedy of Adam’s fall from grace and the incredible weight of the responsibilities they both share. Billy doesn't fight Adam with just his strength; he fights the cynicism and the bitterness that have consumed the ancient champion. He remains steadfast in his commitment to the purity of his intent, hoping that one day he can find a way to reach the man behind the lightning. He knows that Adam will never truly stop, a realization that fuels his eternal watch.

Ultimately, Black Adam remains one of Shazam’s most dangerous and enduring foes, a dark reflection of what a champion can become when their heart is devoid of mercy. He is a reminder that power without compassion is a destructive force and that the struggle for justice is a constant battle against the shadows of the past. As Adam sits upon his throne in Kahndaq, his gaze remains fixed on the Rock of Eternity, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. He is a king who will never settle for anything less than total dominion, a persistent shadow over the future of the Shazam legacy.
