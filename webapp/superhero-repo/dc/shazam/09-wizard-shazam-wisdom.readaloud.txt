Three thousand years is a very long time to wait. But the ancient Wizard Shazam waited that long because he was looking for someone specific — not the strongest person, not the cleverest, not the most famous. He was looking for someone with a truly good heart. And when he found Billy Batson, he knew the wait was over.

The Wizard gave Billy more than just powers. He gave him wisdom. And do you know how the Wizard shared that wisdom? Not with simple answers. He spoke in riddles and parables, little puzzles that made Billy think harder and grow deeper. He taught Billy how to recognize ancient magical artifacts, how to speak peacefully to powerful deities, and how to keep the balance between the light and the shadow of the magical world.

In the quiet halls of the Rock of Eternity, Billy visits the Wizard's spirit whenever he needs guidance. Sometimes it is about a magical threat. Sometimes it is about something harder to explain — a choice between two difficult paths, or a doubt that crept in during a long night. The Wizard listens. He shares stories of his own struggles — centuries of sacrifice and loneliness and hard decisions. He is honest about how heavy the responsibility truly is.

For Billy, who grew up without parents, the Wizard became something he had never had before: a father figure and a friend.

From that day on, Billy Batson carried the Wizard's wisdom everywhere he went, proof that the most powerful champion in the world still needs someone to believe in him.
