Three thousand years is an almost impossibly long time to wait. But the ancient Wizard Shazam waited every bit of it — sitting on his great throne in the Rock of Eternity, watching age after age of humanity, searching for exactly the right person. Not the strongest. Not the cleverest. Not the most powerful.

The one with the purest heart.

When he found Billy Batson, he knew immediately: the wait was over.

But the Wizard did not just give Billy powers and wave goodbye. He gave him something even more valuable: wisdom. And do you know how the Wizard shares that wisdom? Not with simple, easy answers! He speaks in riddles and parables — little puzzles that force Billy to think harder and grow deeper on his own. He teaches Billy how to identify ancient magical artifacts. How to speak peacefully to powerful deities who have been around since before recorded history. How to maintain the balance between the light and the shadow of the magical world!

In the quiet halls of the Rock of Eternity, Billy visits the Wizard's spirit whenever he needs guidance — sometimes about a dangerous magical threat, sometimes about something harder to explain, like a choice between two difficult paths, or a doubt that crept in during a long and lonely night.

The Wizard always listens. He shares stories of his own three thousand years of sacrifice and loneliness and hard decisions. He is honest about how heavy responsibility truly is.

And for Billy Batson — who grew up without parents, who spent years wishing for someone to believe in him — the Wizard became something he had never had before: a father figure, and a true friend.

The most powerful champion in the world still needs someone to guide him, trust him, and believe in him. That is what wisdom is for!
