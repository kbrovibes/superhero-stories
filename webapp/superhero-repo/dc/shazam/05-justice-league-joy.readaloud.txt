The Justice League is filled with the most powerful heroes in the world. Superman. Wonder Woman. The Flash. Heroes of incredible strength and legendary courage. And then there is Shazam — the youngest member, a boy at heart — and he might be the most important one of all.

Billy Batson brings something the others sometimes forget: joy. He is the first one to cheer when a mission goes well, and the last one to despair when things get difficult. He looks up at Superman and Wonder Woman with genuine admiration and is never embarrassed to ask questions or admit when he does not know something. That kind of honesty makes the whole team stronger.

During long tactical meetings, Billy suggests ideas that surprise even the most experienced heroes — creative, unexpected solutions that only someone who still sees the world with fresh eyes could imagine. And do you know what else he does? He reminds his teammates to celebrate. To laugh. To never lose sight of why they became heroes in the first place.

The Justice League protects the world from enormous threats. But without Shazam, they might forget the most important thing they are protecting: the everyday happiness of ordinary people.

From that day on, Earth's Mightiest Mortal proved that the spark of joy is not a small thing. In the right heart, it is the most powerful force in the universe.
