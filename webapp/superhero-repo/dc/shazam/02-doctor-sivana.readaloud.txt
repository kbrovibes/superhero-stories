Doctor Thaddeus Sivana was one of the most brilliant minds in all of Fawcett City. He could build extraordinary machines and unlock secrets that baffled other scientists. But somewhere along the way, his brilliance curdled into something bitter. He believed the world had never given him the respect he deserved. And when he learned that an ancient wizard had chosen a child — an ordinary boy named Billy Batson — to receive the greatest power in the magical world, Sivana was furious.

From his high-tech laboratories, Sivana plotted endlessly. He studied ancient mystical artifacts alongside his machines. He sent drones and invented gadgets and schemed day and night to strip Billy of his powers and claim the Rock of Eternity for himself. He believed that someone with his scientific brilliance was far worthier than any orphan from the streets.

But Sivana could never understand one thing. The Wizard Shazam had not chosen Billy for his muscles or his knowledge. He had chosen him for his heart. And do you know what Sivana's heart was like? Cold. Closed. He saw kindness as a weakness and compassion as something to exploit.

That was why he always lost. Billy Batson did not just fight Sivana with lightning. He fought back against everything Sivana stood for — the cruelty, the hunger for control, the belief that power belonged only to the cleverest and the strongest.

And that is how Shazam proved that the greatest scientific mind in Fawcett City was no match for a boy with a truly good heart.
