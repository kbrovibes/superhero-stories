Here is a question. You have Beast Boy on your team. He can be ANY animal — a T-rex, a whale, a peregrine falcon. What do you have him be?

The answer is: it depends. And knowing the answer is the whole lesson.

Here's the night the Teen Titans tell when someone asks what teamwork really looks like.

A huge storm hits Jump City. Power goes out across the eastern waterfront. And then — BOOM — three emergencies at the same time!

Robin coordinates everything from the Tower. Cyborg races to the bay tunnel, which is flooding, and holds the CEILING UP with his reinforced arms while rescue teams pull people clear. CYBORG IS HOLDING A TUNNEL. Starfire flies to a waterfront warehouse fire, working with the Jump City Fire Department, going into spots too hot for firefighters. Raven goes to the Titan Bridge, which is buckling under debris, and HOLDS IT STEADY with her powers while engineers assess.

Everyone has their job.

That leaves Beast Boy. And his job is: EVERYTHING ELSE.

He becomes a green SAINT BERNARD — big, friendly, nose-to-the-ground — and WOOF WOOF leads a family of four out of a flooded apartment building! DOWN THE BACK STAIRS! Everyone safe!

SPLASH — he's a green DOLPHIN — pulling two capsized sailors out of Jump City Bay! CLICK CLICK (that's dolphin for "grab my fin")!

CLIP CLOP CLIP CLOP — green DRAFT HORSE — helping the sandbag crew on Harbor Boulevard move TWO TONS of bags in forty minutes! STRONGEST HORSE ON THE BLOCK!

And then — nose down, SNIFF SNIFF — green BLOODHOUND — tracking a missing elderly resident THREE BLOCKS from where the search team was looking, found safe and dry in a dry-cleaning shop!

By midnight, the storm is easing. Everyone is safe. The Titans regroup at the Tower, soaking wet and tired. Robin reads out the results on the mission board. Held. Held. Held. Held.

Starfire says she's glad they were all there.
Cyborg says the timing was impossible and they nailed it.
Raven nods — which, for Raven, is very enthusiastic.

Garfield says: "The right shape for every job."

That's the lesson. Not: be the biggest animal you can possibly be. Not: bring the most power.

The RIGHT shape. The HELPFUL shape. The shape the situation actually needs.

He has been a pigeon on a rooftop for three hours. He has been a tiny mouse sneaking across a tile floor. He has been a green cat sitting quietly on a dock. He has been a RHINOCEROS.

All green. All Garfield. All exactly right.

That's what makes Beast Boy so extraordinary.

Not any one shape.

The green that stays the same through all of them.

ROAR, SPLASH, WOOF, SNIFF, NEIGH — and most importantly — himself.
