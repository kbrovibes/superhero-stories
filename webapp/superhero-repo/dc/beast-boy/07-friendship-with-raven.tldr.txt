• Raven is quiet, precise, and private; Beast Boy is loud and spontaneous — on paper, the strangest possible friends
• Garfield makes it a project to find what almost makes Raven laugh: dry observations, absurdist scenarios, one Robin impression
• Raven gives him steadiness — she notices things and can sit in silence without requiring it to be explained
• After a hard mission, Garfield sits on the roof unsure of himself; Raven comes up, reads her book, stays
• After twenty quiet minutes he is himself again; she says "you know what you are" — he says "green" — she says "that too"
• Their differences stay visible: her room is austere, his is chaos; she meditates, he roars in the hallway
• The team has learned: look to Raven's reading corner, and the green boy nearby who is not really reading his comic
• Their friendship is built not on matching personalities but on paying attention
