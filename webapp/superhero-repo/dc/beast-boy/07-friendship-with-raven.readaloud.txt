Okay — here is the world's most unexpected friendship. Ready?

In one corner: RAVEN. She reads ancient books. She meditates in perfect silence. She speaks in a very calm, very precise voice that means business. She has enormous magical powers kept under extremely careful control. She is not a person who laughs easily. Or loudly. Or often.

In the other corner: BEAST BOY. He does impressions. He makes puns — SO MANY puns. He practiced his T-rex roar in the hallway last Tuesday at seven in the morning. He has seventeen bowls of cereal a week. He is very, very loud.

These two became, somehow, the closest pair of friends on the whole team.

Here's how it happened.

From day ONE, Garfield made it a personal project to find the thing that would make Raven laugh. He tried impressions — nope. He tried slapstick — she walked away. He tried animal sound effects — NOPE.

But then — a very dry, very unexpected observation about how Robin fills out mission paperwork? Raven's mouth twitched. JUST the corner. For about half a second. She definitely suppressed something.

Garfield counted that as a win.

And in return, Raven gave him something even better: steadiness. Raven notices everything. She reads carefully and she pays attention and she knows when something is wrong before you've said a word. On the days when Garfield didn't want to talk, Raven didn't ask questions. She just sat in the same room. No pressure. No conversation required.

Then one day a mission went sideways. Garfield took a hit and felt briefly — just briefly — uncertain about himself. He sat on the roof of the Tower afterward, alone, looking at Jump City, feeling a little lost.

Raven came up through the roof access. She sat down two feet away. She opened her book.

She read. He sat. The city moved below them.

Twenty minutes passed.

She closed the book.

She said: "You know what you are."

He thought about it.

"Green," he said.

She said: "That too."

That's it. That's the whole conversation. And it was exactly the right one.

Now — are they still completely different? ABSOLUTELY. Her room is very orderly. His room is an archaeological site of snack wrappers. She meditates in the early morning. He is, at that same moment, doing T-rex roars.

But here's what the other Titans have figured out. When something subtle needs noticing — a small detail, a quiet problem, a pattern nobody else caught — look to the corner where Raven is reading.

And look for the green boy sitting two feet away, pretending he is reading his comic.

He is not reading his comic.

He is paying attention.

That's Beast Boy and Raven. The world's most unexpected friendship.

And one of the best ones you'll ever find.
