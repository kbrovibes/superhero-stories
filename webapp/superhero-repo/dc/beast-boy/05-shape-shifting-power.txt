Beast Boy's power seems simple at first glance: he turns into animals. But standing in the middle of a mission — a building collapsing, the bay filling with water, a villain three times his size — you realize just how layered that ability really is.

The transformation is complete. When Garfield becomes a whale, he breathes water and navigates by sonar. When he becomes a peregrine falcon, his eyes can spot a coin on the pavement from five hundred feet up. He takes on the full senses, reflexes, and biology of every creature he becomes. He once tracked Raven across three rooftops purely by scent while in the form of a bloodhound, which she found deeply unnecessary and he found scientifically interesting.

The library of animals he can become has grown since his childhood in the Congo. He has studied elephants, great white sharks, T-rexes (technically, his knowledge of extinct creatures works just as well as living ones), hummingbirds whose wings beat two hundred times per minute, and an Australian mantis shrimp whose punch generates a shockwave strong enough to stun anything in range. He tends toward the dramatic — when given any option, he will probably become a dinosaur — but he has learned to recognize when small and quick beats large and obvious.

Every form he takes emerges green. Always the same vivid, particular green: not camouflage, not disguise. His teammates can spot him from across a city block in any shape. The color is the one constant in every transformation, as much a part of Garfield Logan as his voice or his laugh. He once tried to turn into a white arctic fox and got a green arctic fox instead, which was arguably more useful because it was easy to find in a snowstorm.

There are limits. Sustained transformation is tiring — after a long mission with many forms, Garfield comes back to himself scraped-elbows-and-empty-stomach tired. He cannot split into multiple animals simultaneously. He cannot become something he has never encountered or studied. These are the rules he works within, and he has spent years learning the edges of them carefully so he knows exactly what he can push and what he cannot.

The power came from Sakutia, from the green-monkey serum, from something in his parents' science that changed him at the cellular level. He did not choose it. But he has chosen, every day since, what to do with it — and what he has chosen is to be useful, generous, and as loud as a green elephant in an enclosed space.
