Before Beast Boy was Beast Boy — before the Titans Tower and Jump City and all of that — there was a very large, very unusual house called Caulder Manor.

And in that house lived the Doom Patrol.

Now I need you to picture the MOST extraordinary team of heroes you have ever imagined. Ready?

First: Niles Caulder. The CHIEF. He sits in a motorized wheelchair and has the most brilliant mind you will ever meet. He gathered the Doom Patrol together because he believed — deeply believed — that being different is not the same as being broken.

Next: Rita Farr. ELASTI-GIRL! She used to be a famous movie star until a special chemical compound turned her into someone who can grow as TALL AS A BUILDING — BOOM — or shrink down to the size of a PENCIL — poof — whenever she wants. She is calm and warm and slightly terrifying when she is annoyed.

And then: Cliff Steele. ROBOTMAN! He has a human brain inside an indestructible metal body — clank clank clank — and he is gruff and loud and absolutely loyal. He taught Garfield to spar. Mostly by not getting hurt when Garfield became an elephant.

And then there's Garfield. The smallest. The greenest. The LOUDEST.

He jokes during training. He makes impressions of the Chief when the Chief is in the other room. He eats an alarming amount of cereal. Rita has to remind him to eat vegetables at least three times a week.

But here's what matters.

The Doom Patrol fights BIG enemies. General Immortus — a villain who has been alive for CENTURIES and is very, very patient. The Brain — that frightening genius in his glass dome. These are not silly villains. These are careful, clever, old villains who have been plotting since before Garfield's grandparents were born.

And the Doom Patrol faces them together. Always together.

There are nights when missions go hard, and the house is quiet and a little sad. Those are the nights Rita comes downstairs in her dressing gown and just sits across from Garfield at the kitchen table while he eats his cereal. She doesn't say anything. She just stays.

That's what family is.

Garfield Logan grew up in Caulder Manor. He grew up surrounded by extraordinary, unusual, brilliant people who showed him that the world is big enough for everyone — especially the green ones.

And when it was time to leave and find his own path, he took all of it with him.

Every bit of it.

ROAR! (An affectionate T-rex roar. The very best kind.)
