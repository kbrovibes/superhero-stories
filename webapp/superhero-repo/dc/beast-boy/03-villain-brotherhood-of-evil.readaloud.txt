Alright — are you ready for the scariest villain Beast Boy has EVER faced?

The Brain. A criminal genius so clever he doesn't even need a body — just his enormous brain floating in a glass dome, thinking of ways to defeat young heroes. Creepy? A little. But mostly just dangerous.

The Brain runs an organization called the Brotherhood of Evil, and they are after ALL the young heroes — everywhere in the world. And one day, when the other Titans are busy elsewhere, their enforcer shows up at Titans Tower.

His name is Monsieur Mallah. He is a GORILLA. Not a normal gorilla — a super-smart, super-strong gorilla wearing a bandolier of weapons, who can think and plan as well as any general. The Brain turned him into exactly that.

CRASH! Mallah smashes through the Tower doors with a whole squad of soldiers.

Beast Boy is alone. So what does he do? He becomes a GREEN ELEPHANT and CHARGES.

THUD THUD THUD! It's extremely dramatic!

Mallah grabs one tusk, uses Beast Boy's own momentum, and WHOOOM — throws him through a wall.

Okay, plan B!

Beast Boy shakes it off and tries again. Green pterodactyl — SWOOP — knocking soldiers away from the control panel! But Mallah swats him down.

Green bear! SLASH! Mallah sidesteps. Green anaconda! Trying to hold Mallah's arms back — but Mallah is too strong. Green wolf! Still too strong!

The soldiers keep filling every gap. It's not working.

Garfield takes a breath. He thinks about his mom, the scientist. She studied chimpanzees by watching their whole group — not just the strongest one. She looked for patterns.

Patterns. Okay. THINK.

He becomes a tiny green starling. Flits up to the ceiling — nobody even notices a small green bird in all that chaos. Finds the fire suppression switch. Taps it with his beak.

WHOOOOOOSH! Foam EVERYWHERE!

In the white confusion, Garfield zips back to himself, hits the emergency beacon — BEEP BEEP BEEP — and slams the security blast doors closed. CLUNK. Mallah is locked inside!

Mallah breaks through eventually — yeah, he's VERY strong. But the beacon is out. The trail is sent. The Titans will find the Brotherhood.

Beast Boy sits down in the foam-covered wreckage of his kitchen — one boot somehow missing, elbows a little scraped — pours himself three full bowls of cereal with the little marshmallows, because he absolutely deserves them, and thinks:

Sometimes being the cleverest animal in the room is better than being the biggest.

Always green. Always himself. Always figuring it out.
