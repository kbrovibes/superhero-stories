Look out across Jump City Bay — all the way to that little rocky island — and what do you see?

A big, gleaming building shaped like the letter T.

That is TITANS TOWER. Home of the Teen Titans. And it might be the coolest place in the entire DC universe.

Let me take you on a tour!

We start with BEAST BOY'S ROOM. Fourth floor, east side. Now — fair warning — it is not tidy. There are posters of bands. There are posters of animals. There are snack wrappers on the desk. There are half-finished comic books everywhere. BUT! On one shelf, organized very carefully by continent, are the field guides Garfield's mother gave him when he was a little boy growing up in Africa. Those are always neat. Those are always where they're supposed to be.

Next: THE COMMON ROOM! Big windows. Enormous wraparound couch with permanent dents from five people sitting in exactly the same spots for years. This is where the team watches films — Beast Boy picks them, Raven occasionally tolerates them. This is where they eat. And where they argue about breakfast.

Cyborg: "A real breakfast needs PROTEIN."
Beast Boy: "Cereal is a grain and grains are a food group, GOODBYE."

(This argument has never been resolved.)

Now — THE TRAINING ROOM! Robin runs drills here that have nothing to do with turning into animals. Reaction time! Teamwork! Finding the leverage point in a fight — not just the biggest target! Garfield has learned more here than almost anywhere.

He and Cyborg have a running obstacle course competition. The score is basically tied. Neither of them will admit it.

THE COMPUTER SYSTEM — Cyborg's masterpiece — tracks every threat in and around Jump City. Garfield checks it every morning. Robin checks it every four minutes. Beast Boy checks it every morning. Robin checks it every four minutes. This is how the Titans stay ready.

And THE KITCHEN. Stocked with — and this is not an exaggeration — enough cereal to last through a siege. Garfield considers this a strategic necessity. Robin considers it a storage problem. The cereal stays.

But here's the real secret of Titans Tower.

It's not the training room or the computers or the amazing view of Jump City Bay.

It's this: five people come home to it.

Every mission, every fight, every long night — when it's done, they come back here. To the same couch. The same kitchen. The same T on the island in the bay.

And that — for Garfield Logan, green from head to toe — is what makes it the best place in the world.

Welcome home, Beast Boy.

ROAR! (A happy, relieved, I'm-home T-rex roar. The very best kind.)
