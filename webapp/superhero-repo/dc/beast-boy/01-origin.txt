Garfield Logan was born into a family of scientists who studied animals in the jungles of East Africa. His parents, Mark and Marie Logan, ran a research station deep in the Congo, cataloguing creatures that few people had ever seen. Garfield spent his early years surrounded by chimpanzees and river birds, more comfortable with animals than with other children. He could identify every primate call before he could read a chapter book.

When Garfield was five years old, a rare disease called Sakutia swept through the research station. The illness was dangerous to humans but harmless to green monkeys. In a desperate bid to save their son, Mark and Marie used an experimental serum based on the DNA of the green monkey — Chlorocebus sabaeus. It worked. Garfield survived. But the serum left a permanent mark: his skin, hair, and eyes turned a vivid, unmistakable green, and he discovered he could transform his body into any animal he had ever studied.

He could become a mouse, small enough to slip under a door. He could become a cheetah and outrun a car. He could become a green whale and dive to the ocean floor. Every form he took was the same bright emerald green, which made blending into a crowd essentially impossible — but Garfield found he didn't mind. He wore his green like a badge. He smiled more than he worried.

After a tragedy left him an orphan, Garfield was taken in by the Doom Patrol — a team of extraordinary people led by the brilliant Niles Caulder, also called the Chief. Alongside Elasti-Girl and Robotman, he learned what it meant to use his powers for others. He was the youngest and loudest member of the Doom Patrol, always cracking jokes to keep the mood light when missions turned hard. Rita Farr, who could stretch and shrink, treated him like a younger brother and called him "Gar."

In time, Garfield set out on his own and found something even better than one family — he found five. In Jump City, he joined a team of teenage heroes who each carried their own strange and difficult stories. Robin had left Gotham. Starfire had come down from the stars. Raven carried a darkness she was always fighting to control. Cyborg was half machine and all heart. And then there was Garfield Logan — green, grinning, and ready to become anything the mission needed. They called themselves the Teen Titans. Garfield took the name Beast Boy. He was exactly where he belonged.
