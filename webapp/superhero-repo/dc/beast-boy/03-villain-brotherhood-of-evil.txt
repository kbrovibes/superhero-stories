The Brotherhood of Evil was the oldest, most dangerous team of villains Beast Boy had ever faced — and the first time he faced them, he faced them alone. The Brain, a disembodied criminal genius whose mind floated in a glass dome, had dispatched hunters across the globe to capture every young hero on his list. In Jump City, Beast Boy was the only Titan still standing when the hunter named Monsieur Mallah arrived at the Tower.

Mallah was a gorilla — but not an ordinary one. He wore a bandolier of weapons across his chest, spoke in a cool, precise voice, and was as strong as anything Beast Boy had ever faced. He had been transformed by the Brain himself into one of the most intelligent and powerful creatures alive. When Mallah crashed through the Tower doors with a team of the Brotherhood's soldiers, Beast Boy transformed into a green elephant and charged.

The charge bought exactly four seconds. Mallah caught one of the elephant's tusks, used the momentum, and threw Beast Boy through the wall of the equipment bay. Garfield hit the floor as himself, scraping his elbows on the metal grating. He got up. He became a green pterodactyl, swooped through the smoke, and raked Mallah's soldiers away from the control panel where they were uploading the Titans' files. But Mallah swatted him out of the air.

Three more forms. A bear, a wolf, a green anaconda trying to hold Mallah's arms back. Each one Mallah countered. Garfield was fast, but Mallah was smarter than he looked, and the Brotherhood's soldiers kept reforming around every opening he created. They were losing.

Garfield thought of a gorilla. He thought of the jungle research station. He thought of how his mother had observed chimps — not just their power, but their group dynamics, the way they talked to each other in small signs and sounds. He stopped fighting alone. He became a small green starling and flicked the fire suppression switch at the ceiling, flooding the bay with foam. In the white confusion, he turned back into himself, hit the emergency beacon, and locked Mallah inside the bay's security blast doors.

Mallah broke through eventually. But by then the signal was out, and the Titans had a trail to follow. The Brotherhood of Evil would not be done with the Teen Titans, not by a long way. But Beast Boy had learned something important about the Brain's organization that day: it was disciplined, and the only way to match discipline was to be smarter about when to fight and when to think.

He filed that lesson away and ate three bowls of cereal while his elbows healed.
