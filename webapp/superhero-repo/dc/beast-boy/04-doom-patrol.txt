Before the Teen Titans, before Jump City, before any of it, there was a big house called Caulder Manor, and in that house lived the strangest, bravest, most unlikely team of heroes Garfield Logan had ever seen. They were the Doom Patrol, and they were his first family after he lost his own.

Niles Caulder — called the Chief — ran the Doom Patrol from a motorized wheelchair, with a mind so brilliant it sometimes frightened even his teammates. He had gathered people the world had not known what to do with: men and women whose lives had been transformed by accidents and science into something new and difficult and powerful. He believed, fiercely, that different was not broken. He built Caulder Manor into a home and a headquarters both.

Rita Farr had been a famous film actor before a chemical compound encountered during a film shoot gave her the ability to stretch and shrink her body to any size. She called herself Elasti-Girl, and she was the team's warmth — steady, precise, a little wry. She treated Garfield like a younger brother from the first week, which meant she fed him proper meals and occasionally confiscated his comic books during training. He adored her for both.

Cliff Steele was Robotman — his human brain transplanted into an indestructible metal body after a racing accident took everything else. Cliff was gruff and complained constantly and had a laugh that rattled the window panes, and he would walk through a wall for any one of his teammates without a second thought. He taught Garfield how to spar, mostly by surviving every form Garfield could throw at him.

Together they faced enemies like General Immortus, who had lived for centuries and grown patient and strange, and the Brain — that disembodied genius in his glass dome — who saw the Doom Patrol as an obstacle to be removed. These were not cartoon villains. They were careful and old and persistent. But the Doom Patrol was persistent too.

Garfield was the youngest member, and the smallest, and the loudest, and he jokes himself into believing none of it scared him. But there were nights in Caulder Manor when the missions had gone hard and the house was quiet and Garfield sat in the kitchen eating cereal, and Rita would come downstairs in her dressing gown and sit across from him without saying anything, just being there. That mattered more than he knew how to say.

He grew up in that house. Not all the way — that took the Teen Titans and more years besides. But enough to know who he was: Garfield Logan, Beast Boy, the green kid who could be anything, from the strangest, best family in the world.
