There is a story the Teen Titans tell about the night a storm knocked out power across Jump City's eastern waterfront and three emergencies happened at once. It is the story they tell when someone asks what it means to work as a team.

Robin coordinated from the Tower's emergency systems. Cyborg drove to the bay tunnel, where three cars had been swept against the wall by flooding — he braced the ceiling with reinforced arms while rescue teams pulled people clear. Starfire flew to a waterfront warehouse fire, her heat resistance allowing her to go into spots firefighters couldn't reach. Raven held a section of the Titan Bridge that had buckled under flood debris, keeping it stable until engineers could assess.

That left Beast Boy to cover everything else.

He became a green Saint Bernard and found a family of four sheltering on the second floor of a flooded apartment building, barking and leading them out the back stairs. He became a green dolphin and pulled a small sailboat's crew of two out of the bay after they capsized in the surge. He became a green draft horse and helped the sandbag team on Harbor Boulevard move two tons of material in forty minutes. He became a green bloodhound and tracked a missing elderly resident three blocks from where the search team was looking, found safe on the second floor of a dry-cleaning shop.

By midnight the storm was easing. The Titans regrouped at the Tower, soaking wet and tired, and Robin reviewed the evening's outcomes on the mission board. Everyone had held.

Starfire said that she was glad they had all been there.

Cyborg said the timing had been impossible and they'd handled it.

Raven said nothing, which meant she agreed.

Garfield said: "The right shape for every job."

That was the lesson: not being the most powerful thing in the room, but the right thing. He had been the green pigeon on the roof and the green cat on the dock and the small green starling who found the fire suppression switch. He had been the loud rhinoceros and the quiet mouse. Knowing which one to be — and choosing correctly — mattered as much as being able to become it.

The Teen Titans were five very different people, and that was the point. Robin had the strategy. Starfire had the fire and warmth. Raven had the depth. Cyborg had the force. Beast Boy had range — earnest when earnest was needed, silly when silly was the right medicine, quiet when quiet was what the moment asked for.

Always the same green. Always himself.

That was the power. Not any single shape — the green that stayed constant through all of them.
