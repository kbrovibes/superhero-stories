The trouble started at Jump City Mall when every television in every store window switched on at the exact same moment. A chubby man in a jumpsuit covered in remote-control buttons pointed a device shaped like an oversized TV remote and cackled at the screens. His name was Control Freak, and his one ambition in life was to pull fictional villains out of movies and use them to defeat the Teen Titans.

Control Freak's remote could do things no ordinary device should be able to do. He pointed it at a hardware store window display and ZZZZT — a giant robot from a cheesy science-fiction film stepped off the screen and onto the sidewalk, trailing sparks. Robin barked orders. Cyborg charged. Starfire launched starbolts. Raven wrapped the robot in dark energy. And Beast Boy — right in the middle — turned into a green rhinoceros and hit the robot low with a CRASH that rattled every car alarm on the block.

The robot went down. But Control Freak was already pointing the remote at another screen — a fantasy film playing in the food court — and three armored dragon-knights came clanking out into the food court. Beast Boy went green hawk, swooped up over them, and then dropped straight down as a green gorilla onto the lead knight's shoulders. WHAM. Two down.

But Control Freak was faster than he looked. He ducked into the electronics department and activated a whole wall of screens simultaneously, unleashing a pixelated army. The Titans were surrounded. Robin called a retreat to the center aisle.

Beast Boy had an idea. He had watched enough movies in the Titans Tower TV room to know that every TV remote had one weakness. He transformed into a green mouse, darted through the chaos unseen, scrambled up Control Freak's jumpsuit leg, and bit the battery compartment of the remote clean off. The remote sparked. The screen-army froze. Control Freak yelped and spun around, but Cyborg's sonic cannon finished the job — BOOM — and Control Freak hit the floor surrounded by the scattered batteries.

When the police arrived, Control Freak was sitting cross-legged on the tile, surrounded by confused movie characters who were rapidly fading back into their screens. He glared at Beast Boy. Beast Boy — now fully green and fully himself again — sat on a display counter, swinging his legs and eating a free pretzel he'd found on the food court floor.

"Not bad for a kid who turns into animals," he said cheerfully.

Control Freak had no answer for that.
