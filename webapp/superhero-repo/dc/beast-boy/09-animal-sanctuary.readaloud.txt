When Garfield Logan isn't being a superhero — and that IS a job, very dramatic hours — he spends his free Thursdays and Saturdays at the Redwood Animal Sanctuary.

Not as Beast Boy. Not with transformations. Just as Garfield — a green teenager who knows a LOT about animals.

The director, Dr. Priya Nair, was skeptical at first. Lots of teenagers volunteer; most quit after two visits. But Garfield kept coming back — rain, patrol emergency, homework crisis — and he knew things.

He knew the correct temperature for a juvenile great horned owl. He knew the harbor seal in pen three liked to be approached from the LEFT. He knew which otters needed space and which one just needed someone nearby for a while.

And here's the thing — the animals TRUSTED him.

Like REALLY trusted him. The owl ate from his hand. A terrified river otter let Garfield pick it up forty minutes after arrival. Other staff needed THREE DAYS with that otter.

He doesn't transform at the sanctuary. The animals trust him as Garfield — just a person, no powers. He comes as a person and they take him at face value.

And then there's Rosalind.

Rosalind is a juvenile California condor. Condors are BIG birds — serious, quiet, with very long memories and absolutely no interest in impressing anyone. Rosalind watched Garfield from across her enclosure for THREE WEEKS without budging.

Then one day she decided he was fine. And from that day on, she treated him with the warm indifference of a bird who has decided you are already known and therefore not interesting.

Garfield found this incredibly satisfying.

One afternoon, Dr. Nair asked him: "Why do you keep coming here? You're a superhero. You could be doing anything."

And Garfield — who is loud and funny and makes puns constantly — was quiet for a second.

Then he said: "I was five years old, running around Africa with chimps and river birds, and the animals trusted me before most people did. And here — I can be anything, I can be a T-rex or a whale or a peregrine falcon — but here I choose to be nothing special. Just a person. And they trust me anyway."

He looked at the hawk enclosure.

"I like who I am here," he said.

Dr. Nair said that was a very good reason.

Garfield nodded, picked up a broom, and went back to cleaning the enclosure.

Some jobs need doing no matter what shape you're in.

(He did turn into a gecko once to reach the back corner. Dr. Nair pretended not to notice. They have a good working relationship.)
