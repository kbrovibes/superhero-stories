Every hero has that one enemy. The one who isn't just strong or fast or big — the one who is SMART. Carefully, patiently, dangerously smart.

For the Teen Titans, that enemy is SLADE.

Slade Wilson. Half-orange, half-black mask. The most measured, controlled voice you have ever heard. He never shouts. He never rushes. He plans. He watches. He has been studying the Titans for months before he makes his move, learning exactly where each of them is strong and exactly where they might break.

He is the most patient person any of the Titans have ever fought.

And when he finally moves — he moves through their world. Their technology. Their records. He turns pieces of what they know into leverage. He always picks the right pressure point.

So what does Beast Boy do against someone like that?

He does what he hates most. He waits.

He becomes a green pigeon and sits on Slade's warehouse roof for THREE HOURS. Coo. Coo. Nobody suspects the pigeon. Garfield hates it but he stays.

He becomes a green spider — tiny, invisible — and crawls through the ventilation of a coastal facility, mapping every corridor, every room, every door, so Cyborg and Robin waiting outside know exactly where to go. SKITTER SKITTER SKITTER.

He becomes a sea lion and swims across Jump City Bay, tracking a supply boat in the dark water until Raven and Starfire can sweep in from above. SPLASH.

All of this is hard and quiet and completely unlike his natural style.

But it works.

Then comes the big moment: the industrial port. Slade himself is there. The Titans go in as a TEAM.

Robin charges Slade directly — the two of them flipping and striking across the dock. Starfire and Raven run overhead, picking off agents with starbolts and dark energy. Cyborg plants himself at the main entrance — BOOM, nobody gets through.

And Beast Boy? Low and fast as a green wolf, driving agents toward Cyborg's line. Then a hawk, cutting off the retreat. Then — when Slade makes for the water —

SPLASH. A green ORCA surfaces right between Slade's boat and open sea.

Slade escapes. He always does. That's the kind of villain he is.

But the network is gone. The operation is shut down. And the Titans did it covering every single angle, no gaps, no hesitation.

Robin calls it a draw. Garfield says it felt like a win. Cyborg says they're both right.

Raven sits down on the dock very quietly. Starfire puts a hand on her shoulder.

Beast Boy sits next to Raven as a small green cat, warm and quiet.

Because sometimes the right shape isn't a T-rex.

Sometimes it's just being there.
