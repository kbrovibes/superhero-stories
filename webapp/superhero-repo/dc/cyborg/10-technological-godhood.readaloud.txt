As the years passed, something remarkable happened to Victor Stone. The alien technology inside him kept growing, kept reaching, kept connecting. One day, Victor realized he could do something extraordinary: he could speak to satellites in orbit. He could sense the pulse of whole cities through their electrical grids. He could hear the whispers of ancient intelligences from distant worlds, as clearly as a conversation across the room.

During a crisis that threatened the whole planet, Victor did something no one had ever done before. He expanded his consciousness outward — into the Justice League's entire lunar base — and used its massive energy reserves to build a planetary shield. In that moment, he was not a person standing in a building. He was the building. He was the planet's defense.

The Mother Box inside him — a fragment of the Source, the deep energy at the heart of the universe — had been guiding him toward this ever since the beginning.

But with that much power came a dangerous problem. The concerns of one small life — one friend's worries, one family's hopes — began to seem very, very small from up so high. Victor knew he had to hold on.

And do you know what he built to keep himself grounded? A secret place inside his mind. A digital room full of memories: the sound of his father's voice, the smell of a home-cooked meal, the laughter of his friends. Whenever the vastness of the universe threatened to swallow him, he went there.

From that day on, Victor Stone was the most powerful technological being on Earth — and he used every bit of it to protect the small, ordinary, precious lives below.
