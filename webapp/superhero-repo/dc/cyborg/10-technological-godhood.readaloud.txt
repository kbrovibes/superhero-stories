Something remarkable kept happening to Victor Stone as the years went by. His Mother Box — that living alien supercomputer fused inside him — kept growing. Kept reaching. Kept connecting to more and more of the universe's vast web of information and energy.

One day Victor noticed he could hear satellites humming in orbit. He could feel the pulse of entire cities through their electrical grids. He could sense ancient intelligences calling from distant worlds, as clearly as a friend calling his name across a room!

Can you imagine that? The whole universe, whispering to you?

During a tremendous crisis that threatened the entire planet, Victor did something nobody had ever done before. He expanded his consciousness outward — into the Justice League's entire lunar base — and used its enormous energy reserves to build a planetary shield around the Earth! In that single blazing moment, he was not just a person. He was the defense of an entire world!

The Mother Box inside him — connected to the Source, the deep energy at the very heart of the universe — had been guiding him toward this all along.

But with that much power came a very serious problem: from up so high, the worries of one small family, the hopes of one neighborhood, began to seem very, very tiny. Victor felt himself starting to drift away from the things he loved most.

So he built something to stop that from happening. A secret room inside his mind — a digital sanctuary filled with his most precious memories: his father's voice, the smell of home cooking, the laughter of his friends, the feeling of a football in his hands.

Whenever the vastness of the universe threatened to swallow him whole, he went there and remembered.

A god who chose to remain a man. And that choice made him a hero.
