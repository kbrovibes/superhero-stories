After the accident that changed his life, Victor Stone felt like he did not belong anywhere. He was too mechanical for the human world and too human for the world of machines. And then he met the Teen Titans.

They were a remarkable group of young heroes — Robin with his sharp leadership, Starfire with her beaming warmth, Raven with her quiet, steady strength, and Beast Boy who could make anyone laugh even on the hardest days. They looked at Victor's cybernetic body and said: we see you. Welcome home.

Their headquarters was Titans Tower, a T-shaped building on an island in Jump City. And for Victor, it truly was home. He was the team's muscle and their technical mind — analyzing threats, firing his Sonic Cannon when the situation demanded, keeping the team informed during the toughest battles.

And do you know what the Hive Academy taught him? That even the best computer cannot do everything alone. When a virus compromised Victor's systems in the middle of a mission, it was Raven's empathetic magic and Beast Boy's perfectly timed distraction that gave him the seconds he needed to reboot. His friends saved him. After that, he never hesitated to trust them.

Off duty, Victor made waffles and played video games and had the kind of ordinary, wonderful moments that he had thought were gone forever.

From that day on, Victor Stone understood the truth that all great heroes eventually learn: being a hero is not about what powers you have. It is about the people standing beside you.
