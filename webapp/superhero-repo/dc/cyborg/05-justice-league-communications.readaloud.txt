When the Justice League is spread across the world — Superman above one city, Wonder Woman defending another, the Flash racing through a third — someone has to make sure they can all talk to each other and work together. That someone is Cyborg.

Victor Stone is the Justice League's communications center. His cybernetic systems link every hero's gadget and earpiece and display, feeding real-time information to wherever it is needed most. He sees everything. He coordinates everything. He is — as his teammates like to say — the digital glue that holds the whole team together.

But Victor does more than connect phone calls. When the League encounters a strange alien device, or a security system that nobody can crack, or a signal that does not seem to make any sense, Victor is the one who figures it out. His supercomputer mind processes data at a speed that would take other scientists weeks or months.

He has used his energy shields to protect Wonder Woman in the middle of a fight. He has opened teleportation routes to put his teammates exactly where they needed to be at exactly the right moment. And do you know what all of that teamwork built? Friendships. Real ones. Heroes who would do anything for each other.

From that day on, Victor Stone proved that no matter how powerful a team is, what makes them truly unstoppable is the one who keeps them connected, informed, and working as one.
