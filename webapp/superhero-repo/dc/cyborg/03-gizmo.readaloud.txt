Guess what showed up in Jump City today! A mechanical spider the size of a refrigerator — SKITTER SKITTER CLANG! A remote-control drone that squirts blue paint — SPLAT SPLAT SPLAT! And a tickle-bot that will not stop tickling anyone who stands still for too long — HA HA HA STOP IT!

That is Gizmo for you. Tiny inventor, ENORMOUS mischief.

Gizmo treats the whole city like a playground for his gadgets, and every single one of those gadgets has the most wonderfully unexpected features. Bubble-blowing lasers! Paint-cannon robots! A machine that honks a tuba every twelve seconds for absolutely no reason! TUBA HONK!

But here is the thing: Gizmo's inventions are actually brilliant. Genuinely, startlingly brilliant — smarter than most grown-up engineers, in fact! And that is exactly what makes these encounters so interesting for Cyborg!

Victor's supercomputer mind has to analyze each wild machine in real time. PROCESSING PROCESSING — PING! Found the weakness! His mechanical fingers work fast, disabling each gadget carefully so nothing gets broken and no one gets hurt. It is like the world's most chaotic puzzle contest — and Victor loves a good puzzle!

But every time Victor shuts down one of Gizmo's creations, he tries something else too. He talks to Gizmo. Suggests a better direction for all that incredible talent. "You could build robots that help hospitals," Victor says. "You could solve real problems with this brain of yours!"

Gizmo usually just laughs and runs away to build something new.

But Victor keeps trying. Because he sees something in Gizmo that Gizmo does not yet see in himself: the potential to be truly great.

And Cyborg never gives up on potential.
