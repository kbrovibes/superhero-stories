Not every villain is frightening. Some are just enormously, exhaustingly inventive — and one of those is Gizmo. Gizmo is small and clever and absolutely obsessed with building things: mechanical spiders, remote-controlled drones, giant stomping robots, and gadgets with the strangest functions imaginable. Bubble-blowing lasers. Tickle-bots. Machines that squirt blue paint at everything in a two-block radius.

To Gizmo, the city was just a big playground, and all of his inventions were toys. The trouble was, some of those toys caused real problems for real people, and that was where Cyborg came in.

Every encounter between Victor and Gizmo was like a contest. Gizmo built something surprising; Victor had to figure out exactly how it worked and find the clever way to shut it down — without causing any damage. Victor's supercomputer mind ran through possibilities at incredible speed. And do you know what helped him most? Not processing power. Creativity. The ability to think sideways, to find the solution nobody expected.

Victor could see something else, too. Underneath all the pranks, Gizmo was genuinely brilliant. Brillianter than most grown-up engineers, in fact. Victor always tried, between stopping the chaos, to suggest a better use for all that talent. Building helpful robots, perhaps. Solving problems instead of causing them.

Gizmo usually laughed and went right back to his gadgets. But Victor kept trying.

From that day on, Cyborg proved that patience and kindness are as important as any supercomputer — even when your rival is building tickle-bots.
