For a long time after his accident, Victor Stone thought he would always be alone.

He had been a star football player at his high school in Detroit, the kind of boy who could run the length of a field without losing his breath. Then one terrible day there was an accident in his father's laboratory at S.T.A.R. Labs, and when Victor woke up, half of his body was made of metal. Gleaming silver plates. Glowing red lights. Wires where there used to be muscles. His father Silas had saved his life with the only tools he had — but Victor did not feel saved. He felt like a strange new creature who did not belong anywhere.

He hid. He stayed indoors. He thought, "Who in the world would ever want to be friends with me now?"

And then, one day, he got an answer.

In a city called Jump City, there was a tall T-shaped tower on a tiny island in the bay. The tower belonged to a group of young heroes who called themselves the Teen Titans. They were not perfect. They were not famous. They were just five kids who were, in their own ways, also a little bit lost.

There was Robin — sharp, serious, the leader, with a domino mask and a cape.

There was Starfire — an alien princess with bright orange skin and emerald eyes, who laughed like sunlight and floated when she was happy.

There was Raven — quiet, hooded, dressed in deep blue, whose magic came from somewhere old and strange.

And there was Beast Boy — green from head to toe, all elbows and grins, who could turn into any animal he had ever seen.

When they met Victor, they did something nobody had done since his accident. They looked at him. They REALLY looked at him. Not at the wires. Not at the glowing parts. At HIM. At Victor.

"Hey, big guy," said Beast Boy, who could not keep a thought to himself for more than five seconds. "You wanna come live in a giant T?"

And just like that, Victor had a home.

The T-Tower was the best place in the world. Victor had his own room, with a bed long enough for his big frame and panels of equipment that hummed in his sleep. He did not have to hide his metal anymore. He did not have to explain. Each Titan brought something different to the team — Robin brought plans, Starfire brought hope, Raven brought stillness, Beast Boy brought laughter — and Victor brought the heavy hitting, the scanners, the firepower, and a sonic cannon that went WHOOOOM and sent bad guys flying.

But more than the missions, Victor loved the in-between moments. The video game tournaments with Beast Boy that went on until three in the morning. The long debates with Robin about which technology was best. The mornings when he made waffles in the big common-room kitchen — golden, fluffy, piled high with whipped cream — and the whole team came stumbling in for breakfast in their pajamas.

He was not just "Cyborg" in the T-Tower. He was Vic. The guy who made the best waffles. The guy who could hack any satellite in orbit before lunch.

There were hard days, of course. There were villains who hunted the Titans, like a clever masked man called Slade who tried to break them apart. There was one mission against a place called the Hive Academy where a sneaky virus crept into Victor's systems, freezing his cybernetic body in place. Victor could not move. He could not fight. He just had to stand there and trust.

And that was when he learned the most important lesson of all.

Raven's magic wrapped around him, soft and dark and warm. Beast Boy turned into a tiny green hummingbird and zipped past the bad guys to distract them. Together they bought him just enough time to reboot. CLICK. WHIRR. BWEEEP. His systems came back online.

He realized then that trusting his friends was not a weakness. It was a STRENGTH.

Years went by. The Titans grew. Some left. Some new ones came. But the core bond never broke. When Victor eventually joined the Justice League and stood among gods and amazons, he carried every Titan with him in his heart.

Now sometimes, late at night on the Watchtower, Victor looked out at the curve of the Earth and remembered the T-shaped tower on its little island.

The Titans had not just saved the world.

They had saved him.

And he would always, always be a Titan at heart.
