Victor Stone was good at almost everything. He was a star athlete and a brilliant student, and he lived in the busy city of Detroit with his father, Silas Stone — one of the world's greatest scientists. Silas worked at S.T.A.R. Labs, a place filled with extraordinary technology, including devices that had come from other worlds entirely.

One day Victor visited his father's lab. And something went very wrong. An experiment had an accident, and Victor was hurt badly — so badly that Silas faced the most frightening choice of his life.

He used everything he knew. Every piece of technology he had studied. He rebuilt Victor's injured body with cybernetic parts, fusing science and human life together to save his son. When Victor woke up, he was different. He had a supercomputer in his chest. His arms could transform into tools. Boosters in his body let him fly. He could connect to any computer anywhere in the world.

At first, Victor worried about how he looked — how different he was from everyone around him. But then something wonderful happened. He realized that his new abilities could be used to help people in ways he had never imagined.

And do you know what he shouted the first time everything clicked into place? "Booyah!"

From that day on, Victor Stone became Cyborg — not just a hero, but a symbol of hope for anyone who has ever felt out of place and found a way to turn that difference into their greatest strength.
