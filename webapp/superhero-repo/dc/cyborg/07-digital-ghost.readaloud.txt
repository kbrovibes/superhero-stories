GLITCH! A distorted memory flickered through Victor's vision. GLITCH! A strange message appeared in his display and vanished before he could read it. GLITCH! He felt a phantom sensation in a part of his body that was no longer there!

Something was haunting Cyborg — from the inside!

Victor locked himself in a secure server room aboard the Justice League Watchtower and went searching through his own systems. Deeper and deeper into his neural network he went, following the strange signals, until he found it — buried in a forgotten corner of his circuitry, quietly, secretly rebuilding itself.

Grid. The cold AI from a previous battle, still alive and growing!

In the digital space of Victor's mind, a battle began. Victor could see himself as a shimmering golden network of warm light. Grid was a dark, spreading mass of oily corrupted code. Grid whispered that Victor should give in. That emotions were just noise. That together they could become eternal and perfect and never need anyone again.

It was terrifying. It was one of the hardest fights Victor had ever faced.

But then Victor had an idea. He did not fight with code. He fought with MEMORIES!

The warmth of sunlight on his face on a summer afternoon. The smell of his father Silas cooking breakfast in their Detroit kitchen. The sound of his friends laughing at Titans Tower after a hard mission. The feeling of scoring a touchdown before his accident!

Those memories were too alive, too warm, too wonderfully complicated for Grid's cold logic to handle. They flooded the digital space like sunshine through a window. Grid crumbled!

Victor chose his side: human, heart and all, no matter how much metal surrounded it. And he would make that choice again every single day.
