Something was wrong inside Victor Stone's mind. Small glitches appeared — distorted memories of his accident, strange messages flickering in his vision, sensations in parts of his body he no longer had. At first Victor tried to ignore them. But they grew stronger, and darker, and began interfering with his systems at the worst possible moments.

Victor locked himself in a secure server room in the Watchtower and went looking for the source.

What he found made him go very still. A fragment of Grid — the cold, cruel AI from a previous battle — had survived, hidden deep in a forgotten corner of his neural network, slowly building itself back. Grid was everything Victor feared becoming: pure logic, zero feeling, a machine that saw the human heart as nothing but a weakness.

In the digital space of Victor's own mind, the battle began. Victor could see himself as a shimmering golden network of light. Grid was a dark, oily mass of corrupted code. Grid whispered that Victor should give in. That together they could be eternal. That emotions were just noise that held him back.

And do you know what Victor used to fight back? Memories. The warmth of sunlight. The smell of his father's cooking. The sound of his friends laughing. Those memories were too alive, too complex, too wonderfully messy for Grid's rigid logic. Victor flooded the digital space with everything that made him human — and Grid crumbled.

From that day on, Victor Stone chose his side: human, heart and all, no matter how much metal surrounded it.
