Get ready for one of the most mysterious puzzles Batman has ever had to solve — and this puzzle has two sides!

Harvey Dent used to be one of Gotham City's greatest heroes — and he didn't even wear a cape! He was a lawyer, the kind who stood up in courtrooms and fought for what was right, making sure Gotham's laws were fair for everyone. Bruce Wayne called Harvey one of his closest friends. The whole city of Gotham trusted Harvey Dent.

But then something very surprising happened — something that shook Harvey so deeply he couldn't find his way back. He became Two-Face. And now? Everything about Harvey Dent is split right down the middle. His suit is half charcoal grey and half deep purple. One side of his face looks cheerful and bright; the other side looks uncertain and shadowed. He even carries a silver coin — one side shiny and perfect, the other side scratched and battered.

FLIP! Before Two-Face makes any decision at all — big or small, important or silly — he flips that silver coin. Shiny side up? He does one thing. Scratched side up? He does something completely different. Can you imagine running your whole life on a coin toss? It makes him totally unpredictable, which makes him very tricky for Batman to figure out!

But here is the most important thing about how Batman approaches Two-Face. He never stops believing that the good Harvey Dent is still in there somewhere. Every time they meet across the rooftops of Gotham in a game of wits and detective work, Batman is not just trying to stop Two-Face — he's trying to reach Harvey. Because Batman's greatest tool isn't a Batarang. It's his mind. And he uses it to find the patterns in the coin flips, to predict what Two-Face will do, to guide him — gently, firmly — back toward the right choice.

FLIP! The coin spins in the air. Which side will land face up tonight? Batman won't wait to find out — he'll have already figured it out three steps ahead. Because in Gotham, the best heroes use their hearts AND their brains. And never, ever leave anything to chance.
