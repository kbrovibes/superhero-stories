Harvey Dent was once a respected leader in Gotham City who worked hard to make sure everyone followed the law. However, after a surprising event, he became the character known as Two-Face. He is easily recognized by his unique suit, which is split right down the middle with two different colors and patterns. This look represents the two different sides of his personality and how he approaches every decision he makes.

Two-Face carries a special silver coin that he uses whenever he has to make a choice. One side of the coin is shiny and clean, representing a happy and certain face, while the other side is a bit scratched, representing an uncertain or grumpy face. Before he does anything, he flips the coin into the air and lets it decide his next move. This makes him very unpredictable, as even he doesn't know what he will do until the coin lands.

Batman often tries to help Two-Face remember the good person he used to be. He knows that underneath the split suit and the coin-flipping, there is still a man who cares about justice. Batman uses his detective skills to figure out which way the coin might land and how to best guide Two-Face back toward a more balanced life. It is a constant game of chance and strategy between the two.

The story of Two-Face teaches us about the importance of making our own choices instead of leaving them to luck. While the coin might seem like an easy way to decide things, Batman shows that it is better to use our minds and hearts to determine what is right. Two-Face remains a fascinating figure in Gotham, a man caught between two worlds, always waiting for the next flip.
