In the great, bustling city of Gotham, there lived a boy named Bruce Wayne. He grew up in a grand home called Wayne Manor, and his very best friend in the whole world was a kind man named Alfred Pennyworth. Alfred taught Bruce to work hard, to be kind, and to always help the people around him.

Bruce wanted more than anything to make Gotham a safer place. So he studied and studied — science and history and puzzles that made his brain ache. He trained his body until he was strong and quick. And then he thought very carefully about what kind of hero Gotham needed. He chose the image of a bat. A bat is often misunderstood, but it is actually a wonderful, helpful creature. Bruce liked that very much.

And do you know what happened next? Bruce became Batman. He had no magic powers — no flying, no super-strength. Just a brilliant mind, a brave heart, and more clever gadgets than you could ever count. He worked side by side with the police and with the people of Gotham, making sure everyone felt safe.

When night fell over Gotham, a great light shaped like a bat would shine up into the clouds. Every citizen who saw it knew: someone is watching over us. And during the daytime, Bruce Wayne used his wealth to build parks and hospitals and schools, so that Gotham could shine in the sunlight just as brightly as it was protected in the dark.

From that day on, Bruce Wayne was two things at once — a man who built his city up by day, and the dark guardian who kept it safe by night. And Gotham was a little brighter because of him.
