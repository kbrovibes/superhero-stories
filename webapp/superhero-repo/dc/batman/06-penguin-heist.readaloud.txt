In the glittering heart of Gotham City stood the Iceberg Lounge, a nightclub owned by a very slippery villain named Oswald Cobblepot — the Penguin. He dressed elegantly, spoke very politely, and beneath it all ran one of the most crafty criminal operations in Gotham. Batman had received a tip from a confidential informant: the Penguin was planning to steal a powerful cold-fusion reactor from a Wayne Enterprises research facility.

Batman moved through the shadows like a ghost. He swung onto the roof of the facility just as the Penguin's trained mercenaries were breaking in. Using smoke pellets and flash-bangs, he appeared and disappeared in the darkness, picking them off one by one. But then the Penguin's voice crackled over the intercom: the reactor was already being moved through hidden tunnels beneath the city, headed toward the Iceberg Lounge!

Batman plunged into the tunnels and found a heavily armored underground vehicle rolling along the track. He planted explosive gel charges on the tracks, and — BOOM — the vehicle ground to a halt. Then the Penguin himself appeared, pointing an umbrella that shot bursts of icy cryogenic gas. Batman rolled away just in time, the floor turning to brittle ice behind him.

And do you know what happened next? Batman threw a Batarang perfectly, jamming the umbrella's mechanism. It backfired, coating the Penguin in a layer of frost from head to toe. Cobblepot sputtered and shivered as the Gotham City Police Department arrived to take him away.

From that day on, the cold-fusion reactor was safe, Wayne Enterprises was protected, and the Penguin learned that no tunnel in Gotham is ever quite dark enough to hide from the Batman.
