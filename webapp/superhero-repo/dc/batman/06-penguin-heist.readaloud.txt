Right smack in the middle of Gotham City stands the fanciest-sounding villain hideout you'll ever hear about: the Iceberg Lounge. Fancy name, right? And running it is Oswald Cobblepot — better known as the Penguin — who wears top hats and talks very politely while secretly plotting some very impolite things!

Batman got a tip: the Penguin was planning to steal a powerful cold-fusion reactor from a Wayne Enterprises research building. And what exactly is a cold-fusion reactor? Something very important that definitely should NOT end up in a villain's umbrella collection! SWOOSH — Batman grappled up to the roof before the Penguin's crew even noticed him coming.

One by one, using smoke pellets and quick thinking, Batman appeared and disappeared in the shadows — POOF — picking off the Penguin's hired mercenaries like chess pieces. But then crackled the Penguin's voice through an intercom: the reactor was already rolling through hidden tunnels under the city! RUMBLE RUMBLE went the armored underground vehicle — but Batman planted explosive gel on the tunnel tracks. BOOM! Grinding halt.

Then the Penguin himself waddled forward, pointing an umbrella — not the regular kind, but a cryogenic umbrella that fired blasts of freezing air! ICY WHOOSH — the floor went brittle with frost! Batman dove and rolled — and then aimed one perfectly thrown Batarang — CLANG — right into the umbrella's mechanism. The gadget backfired! PFFFFFZZT — and the Penguin was suddenly covered head to toe in a thick layer of his own ice!

"I... I am NOT amused," sputtered Cobblepot, shivering. The GCPD arrived to find one very cold Penguin and one very recovered reactor. And Batman? Already back in the Batcave, studying Cobblepot's entire criminal network on the big screens. Because in Gotham, stopping a crime is only the beginning. Understanding it — that's the real victory.
