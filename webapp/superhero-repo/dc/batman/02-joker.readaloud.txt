There is no one quite like the Joker in all of Gotham City. He wears a bright purple suit and an orange tie, his wild green hair poking out in every direction. His enormous red smile stretches nearly from ear to ear. And his laugh? Oh, his laugh — "HA HA HA!" — can be heard all the way down the street before you ever see him coming.

The Joker loves to cause a little mischief. He might fill a whole room with giant balloons, or swap all the statues in the city for funny caricatures. He once turned the Funland amusement park into his personal stage, and another time he sneaked his tricks into the grand Gotham Museum. For the Joker, the whole city is just one big performance, and he is always looking for an audience.

And do you know what happened next? Batman always showed up. Because while the Joker's jokes could be a little overwhelming, Batman made sure they never caused any real trouble. The two of them are like two sides of the very same coin — one very serious, one always laughing. The Joker kept trying to get Batman to join in the fun, and Batman kept right on protecting Gotham.

What is interesting is that the Joker is actually very clever. He builds puzzles and gadgets as tricky as anything Batman has ever seen — he just uses them for very different reasons. Batman never forgot that, and he always treated the Joker fairly, with respect, even while keeping the city's peace.

And that is how Gotham stayed safe — not because the Joker stopped laughing, but because Batman never stopped watching.
