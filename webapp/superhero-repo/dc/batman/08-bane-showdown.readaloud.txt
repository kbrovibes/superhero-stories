Of all the villains in Gotham, none was more frightening than Bane. He was enormous — muscles like boulders — fueled by a powerful chemical called Venom that flowed through special tubes on his back. And he was smart, too. He had chosen the ruins of the Old Gotham Library for their showdown, as if he wanted to make a point about destroying knowledge itself.

Bane stood in the center of the old reading room, waiting. He called Batman by his real name — "Bruce" — because Bane was the kind of opponent who did his homework. The fight was unlike anything Batman had ever been through. Every punch Bane threw felt like a wrecking ball. Batman wove between crumbling bookshelves, fired stun grenades, and used every trick he knew — but Bane powered right through each one.

Batman knew he could not win by trading blows with a mountain. So he had to think. He led Bane toward a section of floor that was old and weak, directly above the library's ancient boilers. As Bane charged, Batman swung away on his grapple — and the floor gave way beneath Bane's tremendous weight.

And do you know what happened next? Bane came climbing back up, angrier than ever. The fight moved into the boiler room, hot and flickering. Batman saw his chance — the regulator on Bane's mask, the device that controlled the Venom. With one precise, heated Batarang, he sliced the delivery tube. Green liquid hissed into the air, and Bane crumpled to his knees.

"You didn't break me, Bane," Batman said quietly. "You only reminded me why I have to keep fighting." And then the sirens of the GCPD came echoing down the tunnel, and Batman slipped away into the dark.
