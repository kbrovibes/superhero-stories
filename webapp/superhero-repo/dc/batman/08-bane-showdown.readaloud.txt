Brace yourself — because tonight Batman faces the biggest, most powerful opponent he has ever encountered. And I do mean BIG!

His name is Bane, and he is unlike any villain in Gotham. He's enormous — muscles like solid boulders — powered by a super-steroid called Venom that flows through tubes directly into his body, making him stronger than a construction crane. But what makes him truly dangerous? He is also a brilliant tactician. And he has done something no villain ever managed before: he figured out that Batman is Bruce Wayne.

Bane chose a very dramatic location for their showdown — the ruins of the Old Gotham Library. Because Bane loves to make a point. He called Bruce by name the moment Batman walked in. CRASH BOOM SLAM — the fight was like nothing Batman had ever experienced. Every punch from Bane sent shockwaves through the stone floor. Batman wove between crumbling bookshelves, using stun grenades, trying every trick — but Bane powered through all of it like a living tank.

Can't outfight a mountain? Then OUTTHINK it. Batman spotted weakened floorboards above the ancient boilers in the basement. WHOOSH — he swung out of the way on his grapple at the last second as Bane charged — CRACK CRUMBLE CRASH — the floor gave way and Bane plunged down!

Down in the hot, flickering boiler room the battle continued. But Batman had already spotted the one weak point: the Venom regulator on Bane's mask. One heated Batarang, aimed precisely — SIZZLE SNAP — the main delivery tube split open. Green liquid hissed away. Bane's tremendous strength vanished. The giant crumpled.

"You only reminded me why I have to keep fighting," Batman said quietly. Then the GCPD sirens echoed through the tunnels, and Batman was already gone — back to the Batcave, already planning for tomorrow.
