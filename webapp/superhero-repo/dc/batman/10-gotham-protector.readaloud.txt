High above Gotham City, standing on the stone head of a stone gargoyle, a dark cape whipping in the night wind — there is Batman. Watching. Listening. Always there.

Do you know what Batman actually does every single night? He tracks the police radio. He monitors Arkham Asylum — Gotham's most secure facility for the city's most dangerous criminals — to make sure no one has escaped and no new schemes are being hatched. He watches the city's most troublesome neighborhoods. He knows every shortcut, every back alley, every rooftop path in all of Gotham. Every. Single. One.

And every night, when Commissioner Gordon needs help — the police chief who works hand in hand with Batman, the law and the symbol working together — he activates the Bat-Signal. A great beam of light shaped like a bat blazes up into Gotham's cloudy sky. And every person in Gotham who sees it takes a small breath of relief. Someone is coming. We are not alone.

Here's a secret about Batman's victories, though. The biggest ones don't always look dramatic. They look like a park where kids can play without being scared. They look like a small business that isn't being bullied anymore. They look like a neighbor who finally felt safe enough to stand up and tell the truth. Those are the things Batman fights for, night after night.

And do you know what Batman's biggest dream is? He hopes that one day, Gotham won't need him. That the people of the city — all of them — will have become brave enough to protect one another. He works toward that dream every single night.

Because Batman doesn't just protect Gotham. He believes in it. And in a city as big and complicated as Gotham, being believed in might be the most powerful force of all. WHOOSH — there he goes, leaping from the gargoyle into the dark sky. The dark knight, still on watch. And Gotham sleeps a little safer for it.
