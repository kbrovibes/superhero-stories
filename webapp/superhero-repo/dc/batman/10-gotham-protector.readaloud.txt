Gotham City is a vast, busy place — full of skyscrapers and shadows, laughter and trouble. To the people who live there it is home. To Batman, it is a responsibility. Night after night he stands on the highest gargoyles and walks the darkest alleyways of the Narrows, watching and listening and keeping the city safe.

He tracks the police radio. He monitors the dangerous criminals kept at Arkham Asylum. He knows every shortcut, every hiding place, every street corner in Gotham. And he works closely with Commissioner Gordon, the city's brave police chief, because the two of them together — the law and the symbol — are stronger than either one alone. When the great Bat-Signal lights up the clouds at night, every citizen of Gotham knows: we are not alone.

But here is what is truly wonderful about Batman. His real victories are not the dramatic ones. They are the quiet ones. A park where children can play safely. A shopkeeper who no longer has to pay money to criminals. A witness who is finally brave enough to tell the truth. Those are the things Batman fights for.

And do you know what he hopes for most of all? He hopes that one day, Gotham will not need him — that the people of the city will have learned to stand up and protect each other. He works every night toward that day.

From that day on — and every day since — Batman has remained on watch, perched high above the city as the first light of dawn creeps over the rooftops, a silent guardian who will never, ever stop caring for the city that is as much a part of him as the cape on his shoulders.
