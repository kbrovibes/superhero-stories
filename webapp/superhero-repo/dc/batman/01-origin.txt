Long before he became Batman, Bruce Wayne was just a small boy in Gotham City, walking home from the movie theater between his parents, Thomas and Martha Wayne. Thomas was Gotham's most beloved doctor; Martha was famous for the pearl necklace she wore everywhere. That night, the family took a shortcut down a narrow lane called Crime Alley. A robber stepped out of the shadows. He took Martha's pearls. He took Thomas's pocket watch. And in a single terrible moment that Bruce would never forget, his parents were gone. Bruce sat on the cobblestones alone, holding the broken pearls, and made a quiet promise to himself: he would not let what happened to him happen to anyone else.

Alfred Pennyworth, the family butler, raised Bruce after that night at Wayne Manor. Alfred was patient and kind, and he taught Bruce that grief could become purpose. Bruce poured himself into learning. He traveled the world to study with martial-arts masters, detectives, and scientists. He learned forensics from Interpol, escape arts from magicians, and meditation from monks in the Himalayas. By the time he came home to Gotham, Bruce was the World's Greatest Detective and one of the most skilled fighters alive — but he still felt invisible to the city he wanted to save.

One night, sitting in his father's study, Bruce wondered how to make criminals afraid the way he had been afraid as a boy. Just then, a bat crashed through the window. Bruce smiled for the first time in a long time. "Criminals are a superstitious, cowardly lot," he said. "I shall become a bat." Beneath Wayne Manor, he discovered an enormous cave full of bats — the Batcave — and turned it into a hidden headquarters. Alfred helped him build the Batmobile, the Batcomputer, and a closet full of Batsuits, utility belts, batarangs, and grappling guns.

Batman never carries a gun, because he knows what guns took from him. Instead, he uses gadgets, his brain, and shadows. By day he is Bruce Wayne, billionaire owner of Wayne Enterprises, funding Gotham's schools and hospitals. By night, when the Bat-Signal lights up the sky above the GCPD rooftop, Commissioner Jim Gordon looks up and knows help is on the way. Bruce became Batman so no child in Gotham would ever have to feel as alone as he did on Crime Alley that night.
