Wayne Manor looks like a very grand, very quiet old house. Tall windows. Ivy on the walls. The kind of place where nothing exciting ever seems to happen. But if you think nothing exciting happens inside Wayne Manor — oh, are you in for a surprise!

Because beneath those polished floors, hidden behind a bust of William Shakespeare in the library (yes, really — you press the nose and a secret door swings open!), lies the Batcave. The nerve center of everything Batman does. And keeping it secret is just as important as any mission Batman runs in Gotham City.

One very stormy night, while Bruce Wayne was out on patrol, a team of professional spies crept through a forgotten side entrance. These were not ordinary burglars — they wore high-tech stealth suits and carried the very latest hacking equipment. They were good. Very good. But they made one enormous, catastrophic mistake: they had not accounted for Alfred Pennyworth.

Now here's the thing about Alfred — he looks like a very calm, very proper gentleman who brings tea and makes witty remarks. But Alfred knows Wayne Manor better than he knows his own reflection, and he defended that house ALONE. He watched the intruders on security screens. With quiet flicks of switches he activated ultrasonic emitters — BZZZZ — that made the spies feel quite dizzy. He triggered localized gas discharges — PFFFT — that stopped them in corridors. The spies crept through the manor and Alfred was one step ahead at every single turn.

One spy reached the library. Reached for the Shakespeare bust. A gloved hand closed around his wrist. Bruce Wayne had come home early after a silent alarm went off in the Batcave. The spy was disarmed. Quietly and completely.

WHOMP. Secret: still secret. Because Wayne Manor has a very good security team. And his name is Alfred.
