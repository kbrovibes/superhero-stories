What has keys but no locks, space but no room, and you can enter but can't go inside? It's a keyboard — and if you figured that out, then you might — JUST might — be smart enough to keep up with Batman tonight!

Edward Nygma, the Riddler, thought he was the cleverest person in all of Gotham. He wore a bright green suit and carried a question-mark cane, and he had rigged broadcast towers all across the city with special transmitters. If Batman couldn't solve his riddles in time? ZZZZT — every phone, radio, and alarm in Gotham would scramble to static! "Solve my puzzles, Batman," crackled the Riddler over the airwaves, "or the city goes silent!"

WHOOSH — Batman was racing. First riddle: he cracked it and zoomed to an abandoned computer lab in the financial district — right into a trap of pressurized gas and hidden turrets. He made it through. Second riddle answered: the harbor! Down into a sunken foundry beneath the dark water he went — floor sensors, tripwires, the works. He made it through that too. Because Batman does not slow down when puzzles get harder. He speeds up.

The final clue pointed to the Hall of Mirrors in a creepy old amusement park. A hundred reflections of Batman bounced off the glass in every direction. "You can't catch a shadow!" laughed the Riddler on a hidden screen.

Do you know what Batman did? He did NOT look at a single mirror. He threw a smoke pellet — FOOM — filling the room with grey mist. Then he went perfectly still. And he listened. He heard breathing. He heard the tiniest hum of a transmitter. He moved through the smoke like a whisper, found the Riddler behind a false wall, and stopped him cold — just before the final switch was thrown.

Gotham's signals hummed safely back to life. And the Riddler went to Arkham Asylum wondering — just wondering — how on earth Batman always finds the answer.
