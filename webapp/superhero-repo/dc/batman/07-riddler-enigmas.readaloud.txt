Edward Nygma — the Riddler — loved puzzles more than anything. He wore a bright green suit and carried a question-mark cane, and he believed with every fiber of his being that he was the smartest person in Gotham City. To prove it, he rigged broadcast towers all across the city with transmitters that would scramble every phone call, every radio signal, every alarm — unless Batman could solve his riddles in time.

Batman raced from the clocktower to an abandoned mainframe in the financial district, then dove into the murky harbor to find a sunken foundry from centuries ago. Every location was a trap — gas jets, hidden turrets, sensors in the floor. But Batman is the World's Greatest Detective, and every riddle the Riddler asked told Batman a little more about where Nygma was hiding.

The final clue led to the Hall of Mirrors in a creepy, long-abandoned amusement park. Hundreds of reflections of Batman bounced off the mirrors in every direction. The Riddler laughed and laughed on his screens: "You can't catch a shadow, Batman!"

And do you know what happened next? Batman did not look at a single mirror. He threw a smoke pellet, filling the room with gray mist. Then he stood very still — and listened. He heard the faint hum of a transmitter. He heard the quiet sound of someone breathing. He moved through the smoke like a shadow himself, found the Riddler hiding behind a false panel, and stopped him before he could press the final switch.

From that day on, Gotham's communications hummed along perfectly, and the Riddler was delivered to the GCPD — proof that in Gotham, the greatest puzzle always has an answer.
