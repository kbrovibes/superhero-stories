J'onn J'onzz can pass through walls like smoke. WHOOSH! He can fly through the vacuum of space. He can lift enormous objects with his bare hands. He can become invisible, read minds, and change his shape into anything he imagines. He might be the most powerful member of the Justice League!

And yet there is one thing — one simple, ordinary thing — that can stop him in his tracks.

A candle flame.

Not because fire is incredibly powerful against a Martian body, but because of what fire MEANS to J'onn. When H'ronmeer's Curse swept across Mars, it burned the Martian people from the inside out — a psychic fire that traveled along their own telepathic connections and took everyone J'onn had ever loved. His wife M'yri'ah. His daughter K'hym. His friends. His whole world.

Fire is not just a danger. It is the face of the worst thing that ever happened to him.

In battle, even the flicker of a small flame can make J'onn's concentration slip. The smell of smoke is enough to make his shape-shifting stutter and freeze. In real heat, his molecular structure can become unstable — SHIMMER — and he can crumple in pain. His enemies know this weakness. They try to use it against him every chance they get.

But here is what makes J'onn J'onzz truly extraordinary: he faces it anyway.

Every single time J'onn sees a flame and does not run — every time he pushes through the fear and does what needs to be done — he wins a private, invisible battle that no one else will ever see or know about.

A hero's greatest strength is sometimes found not in what they can easily do, but in what they are willing to face even when it is terrifying.

J'onn faces his fire every time. Every. Single. Time.
