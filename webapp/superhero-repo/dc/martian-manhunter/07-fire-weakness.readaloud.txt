J'onn J'onzz can fly through the vacuum of space. He can pass through walls as if they are made of smoke. He can lift mountains with his bare hands. And yet there is one simple thing that can stop him where he stands.

A flame.

Not because fire is especially powerful against a Martian body, but because of what fire means to J'onn. When H'ronmeer's Curse swept across Mars, it burned the Martian people from the inside out — a psychic fire that traveled along their own telepathic connections. J'onn felt every single one of those losses. He watched his wife, his daughter, his friends, and everyone he had ever known perish in that inner conflagration.

Fire is not just a threat. It is the face of the worst thing that ever happened to him.

In battle, even the sight of a small candle can make J'onn's concentration slip. The smell of smoke is enough to make his shape-shifting stutter. In the presence of real heat, his body can lose its integrity entirely. His enemies know this, and they use it.

But here is what is truly remarkable about J'onn J'onzz. He keeps fighting anyway. And do you know what? Every single time he faces a flame and does not run — every time he pushes through the fear and does his duty — he wins a private, invisible battle that no one else will ever see.

From that day on, the Martian Manhunter proved that a hero's greatest strength is sometimes found not in what they can do, but in what they are willing to face.
