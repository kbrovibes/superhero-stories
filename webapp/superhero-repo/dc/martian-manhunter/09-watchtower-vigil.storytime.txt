High above the Earth — higher than the airplanes, higher than the clouds, higher even than most satellites — there orbits a great shining station.

It is shaped like a wheel of silver and glass, and its lights twinkle softly as it turns. The astronauts who look out from the space station sometimes see it gleaming above them and smile, because they know what it is.

It is the Watchtower. The home of the Justice League.

And on most nights, when the rest of the team has gone home to their cities — to Metropolis, to Gotham, to Central City, to Themyscira — there is one hero who stays behind.

His name is J'onn J'onzz. The Martian Manhunter. The silent watcher of the world below.

For J'onn, the Watchtower is more than a workplace. It is his sanctuary. It is his vigil. While Superman flies home to Lois, while Batman descends into his cave, while the Flash zips back to Iris, J'onn quietly takes his post by the great curving observation windows and looks down at the blue marble of Earth.

From up here, the world is so beautiful it almost hurts.

You cannot see the borders. You cannot see the wars. You cannot see the small angry arguments humans get into. From this high, the planet is just one beautiful turning thing. Clouds drift in lazy spirals. Storms gather and break. The lights of cities glow like glittering jewelry strung across the night side.

J'onn reaches out, very gently, with his Martian mind. He does not pry. He does not snoop. He just LISTENS. He listens the way you might stand very still in a forest and listen to all the birds at once. He hears the great soft hum of human consciousness rising up from the planet — millions and millions of dreams and fears and hopes, all mixed together like the sound of a faraway ocean.

He listens for the discordant note. The wrong sound. The moment when somebody, somewhere, needs help.

His life on the Watchtower is quiet, but it is not lonely.

He has a small garden up there. Did you know that? Tucked away in a corner near the observation deck, where the great Earth-light pours in through the windows, there is a tray of red Martian flowers, their petals delicate as old paper. J'onn brought a few precious seeds from the ruins of his home, and he grows them lovingly in soil he made himself. He waters them by hand. He talks to them sometimes, in soft old Martian. They are a living link to a planet that no longer exists, and tending them is one of the holiest things in his life.

He has meditation chambers, too — silent rooms where he sits cross-legged and refines his enormous psychic powers, keeping his mind disciplined and his shape-shifting precise.

And he has the Watchtower itself. He manages its complex systems. He monitors the emergency channels. He coordinates the team's responses to crises. If the world needs the Justice League, J'onn is usually the first to know, and the first to call them home.

But the vigil has a cost.

When you can hear the thoughts of an entire planet, you hear EVERYTHING. The silent screams of people in pain. The whispered prayers of the desperate. The dark thoughts of people who want to hurt others. J'onn carries the weight of the world's quiet suffering in his mind like a great heavy cloak, and he wears it without complaint.

There are nights when it threatens to overwhelm him. When the volume of sorrow becomes too loud. On those nights, he goes to his Martian garden. He kneels down. He breathes slowly. He remembers that being a guardian means being a WITNESS — standing in the gap between the world and the darkness that would swallow it.

Then he rises. And he goes back to his post.

His friendships keep him going.

The Flash sometimes zips up to the Watchtower with a big box of cookies, and the two of them sit by the window eating and laughing softly about the silliest little things. Batman comes sometimes to talk in the dark, exchanging long thoughtful philosophical sentences with J'onn that nobody else really understands. Superman drops by simply to put a warm hand on J'onn's shoulder and say, "Thank you for staying."

J'onn knows them. ALL of them. He knows their hopes and their fears better than almost anyone, because his telepathy lets him peek if he ever wanted to. But he never peeks. He respects them. He listens only when they offer their words aloud. They love him for that — for being the one friend who could know everything but chooses, instead, to wait.

As the sun rises over the curve of Earth, casting long golden ribbons of light across the Watchtower's silver halls, J'onn J'onzz remains at his post.

He is the Martian Manhunter.

He is the silent watcher who found, on a world not his own, a reason to keep watching.

The vigil will never end. There will always be new threats. There will always be new lives to save.

But J'onn is not alone. Not anymore.

And as long as he has his friends, his memories, his small bright garden of red flowers, and the beautiful messy world turning patiently below him, he will keep his watch.

Always.
