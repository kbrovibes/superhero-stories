Long before he ever set foot on Earth, J'onn J'onzz was part of something extraordinary. He was a Martian — one of a civilization so ancient and so gifted that their very buildings were not constructed with tools. They were sung into existence. Martian architects would gather, reach out with their minds, and reshape the living rock with pure thought until something beautiful emerged.

Every Martian was connected to every other Martian through the Great Song — the collective telepathic consciousness that wrapped around the whole planet like music. There were no secrets on Mars. Every thought, every feeling, every memory was shared freely. Education happened in the Hall of Memories, where young Martians could experience the lives of their ancestors as clearly as if they had lived them themselves.

It was a beautiful world. And the very thing that made it beautiful — that open, trusting connection — was also what made it vulnerable. When H'ronmeer's Curse struck, the Great Song became a chorus of grief. And when the last voice fell silent, J'onn was the only one left.

He carries every one of those voices inside him. The art. The philosophy. The laughter of billions. He is not just a survivor. He is a living library of a world that no longer exists.

And do you know what J'onn does with that enormous, painful gift? He looks at humanity and sees the same possibility — the same capacity for wonder and the same capacity for tragedy — that his own people once had.

From that day on, J'onn J'onzz vowed: he would protect Earth the way he could not protect Mars. And in doing so, he would keep the Great Song alive.
