What was Mars like before J'onn was the last one left? Oh, let me tell you — because it was one of the most extraordinary places that ever existed in the entire universe!

Mars was ancient and magnificent. The Martian people were so gifted that their buildings were not built — they were SUNG into existence! Martian architects would gather together, reach out with their minds, and reshape living rock with pure thought until something breathtakingly beautiful emerged. Bridges that curled like ribbon. Towers that hummed with psychic energy. Cities that looked like music made solid!

And connecting every single Martian was the Great Song — a vast telepathic web of shared thoughts and feelings that wrapped around the whole planet like the world's warmest blanket. No secrets. Total honesty. Complete connection. Young Martians learned by visiting the Hall of Memories, where they could experience their ancestors' entire lives — their joys, their discoveries, their adventures — as clearly as if they had lived them personally!

But the very thing that made Mars beautiful also made it vulnerable. When H'ronmeer's Curse came, it traveled along those open telepathic connections, and the Great Song became a chorus of terrible grief.

J'onn was the only one left.

He carries every single voice inside him still — every piece of Martian art, every poem, every scientific discovery, every laugh and every dream of every Martian who ever lived. He is not just a survivor. He is a living library of an entire lost world!

And when J'onn looks at Earth and sees its people — messy, beautiful, stubborn, hopeful — he sees both the wonder and the warning. The same magnificent potential. The same precious, fragile connection.

He will not let Earth suffer Mars's fate. Not while he still draws breath.
