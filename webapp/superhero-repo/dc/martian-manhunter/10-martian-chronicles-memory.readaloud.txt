Inside the mind of J'onn J'onzz there is a library unlike any other in the universe. He calls it the Martian Chronicles, and it holds every story, every poem, every piece of art, and every memory of a world that no longer exists.

When J'onn needs to visit, he closes his eyes, and there it is — the city of Z'onn Z'orr shimmering before him, its beautiful psionic-carved streets as vivid as the day he last walked them. He can hear the voices of friends who are gone. He can read the great philosophical writings of Martian sages. He can feel, for a moment, the warmth of the Great Song.

This is not just a refuge. J'onn has made it into a gift. He translates what he knows — the wisdom of a civilization that thrived for millennia — into books and lectures and careful, gentle telepathic glimpses that he shares with those he trusts. He wants humanity to learn what Mars understood: that empathy matters, that honesty strengthens communities, that every living thing is connected to every other.

And do you know what happens every time a human chooses kindness over cruelty, or wisdom over pride? A small piece of Mars lives on.

J'onn is the last son of Mars and the keeper of its flame. He tends those memories the way he tends his little garden in the Watchtower — carefully, lovingly, with the full understanding of how rare they are.

From that day on, J'onn J'onzz proved that a civilization does not truly end as long as one person remembers it with love — and carries it forward into the world.
