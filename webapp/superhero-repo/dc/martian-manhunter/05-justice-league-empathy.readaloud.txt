Superman protects Metropolis. Batman plans every battle down to the last detail. Wonder Woman fights with the courage of a warrior queen. And at the center of all of it, quiet and steady, is J'onn J'onzz — the Martian Manhunter — the heart of the Justice League.

In the heat of a battle, J'onn creates something remarkable: a telepathic web that connects all of his teammates at once. They do not need radios or earpieces. They share thoughts at the speed of light. He feels when someone is in danger. He anticipates the next move. He makes sure nobody is left alone in the dark.

But J'onn's gift to the League is not only tactical. He knows what each of his teammates carries in their hearts. The grief they hide, the fears they do not speak aloud, the weight of being what they are. He offers a quiet word, a steady presence, a willingness to listen without judgment.

And do you know what ritual he has, one of the most ordinary and most beloved? He shares chocolate-sandwich cookies — Chocos — with his friends. He discovered them the week he arrived on Earth and he has loved them ever since. It is his small, wonderful way of saying: I am glad to be here. I am glad to be part of your family.

From that day on, J'onn J'onzz proved that the most powerful force in the Justice League is not any single ability or any single hero. It is the invisible thread of empathy and trust that binds them all together.
