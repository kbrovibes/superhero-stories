Inside the mind of J'onn J'onzz, there is a place no telescope can find.

It is not a planet. It is not a room. It is something stranger and more wonderful than either. It is a great shimmering library, built entirely from memory, where every story, every song, every soul that ever lived on Mars is gently preserved.

J'onn calls it the Martian Chronicles.

When the rest of the universe sleeps, J'onn often slips quietly inside.

If you could go with him — if you could shrink yourself down and step through the doorway of his thoughts — you would find yourself walking along the wide red avenues of a city called Z'onn Z'orr, where J'onn once lived a very long time ago. You would see Martian families gathered in courtyards. You would hear the soft murmur of telepathic conversation, rising and falling like a chorus. You would smell the dry sweet wind of a planet whose sky was always pink.

You would hear them. His daughter. His wife. His friends. The teachers and scientists and poets of his world. All of them still alive in the shimmering walls of his memory, all of them speaking and laughing and creating, exactly as they did before everything ended.

This is the great work of J'onn's life. He is the keeper of the flame. The last librarian of a lost civilization. As long as one person remembers, he believes, a world is not entirely gone.

He walks through the philosophical treatises of the Martian sages, written in their long careful telepathic script. He reads the epic poems of the great Martian artists, where each verse contains not just words but the full color of feeling the poet was feeling when they wrote it. He revisits the scientific discoveries that once made Mars a paradise — gentle weather, peaceful cities, food that grew in the desert, families who lived for many human lifetimes.

To J'onn, these memories are sometimes more REAL than the world outside. They are the grounding force that keeps him connected to the man he was before Earth.

But the Chronicles are not just a private refuge.

J'onn has begun, very carefully, to SHARE.

He has written books — not as the Martian Manhunter, but under his human name, John Jones — gentle slim volumes about Martian philosophy and the importance of empathy. He has given quiet lectures to small groups of scientists and thinkers. He has used his telepathy, with permission, to share brief shimmering glimpses of Martian life with friends he trusts deeply. Wonder Woman has seen the gardens of Z'onn Z'orr. The Flash has heard a few notes of the Great Song. Superman has felt, just for a heartbeat, what it was like to be part of a planet full of connected minds.

J'onn believes the lessons of Mars are too important to vanish. The importance of EMPATHY — feeling what others feel. The value of TRANSPARENCY — telling the truth, gently, even when it is hard. The deep connectedness of all life — the understanding that no creature, no matter how small, lives entirely alone.

He believes that if Earth can learn from his people's successes AND from their failures, perhaps humans can avoid the same sad fate.

This sharing has begun to give J'onn something he never expected.

Closure.

By placing memories of his people into the larger world, he is making sure they continue to ripple outward. He sees their influence everywhere now. Every time a human chooses kindness over cruelty, a small piece of Mars lives. Every time a stranger helps a stranger for no reason at all, the Great Song plays a single quiet note. Every time someone forgives, listens, or holds the door for someone they do not know — Mars is there.

Slowly, slowly, a graveyard is becoming a garden.

The Chronicles also remind J'onn of something he sometimes forgets. His people were RESILIENT. They faced hard times before the end with grace and humor. They had a saying — "Tll arr d'pl" — which roughly means "the night sky still holds stars." When J'onn is weary, when his vigil grows long, he reads those old records and feels his shoulders lift just a little.

His dream, he tells M'gann sometimes, is that one day the Great Song will be sung again. Not just by Martians. By everyone. By every person on every world who believes that gentleness is a strength and that we are all, in the end, connected.

He knows he may not live to see that day. But he is content to be the one who plants the seeds.

On clear nights, when J'onn stands on a hilltop somewhere on Earth and looks up, he can sometimes see a tiny red spark in the sky.

Mars.

His home.

He used to feel only sorrow when he saw it. He felt the great weight of being the LAST.

He does not feel that anymore. Not the way he used to.

Because his people's stories are safe within him. Their wisdom is woven into the lives of his friends. Their kindness is alive in M'gann's laugh and the Flash's cookies and Batman's quiet nods. Their memory is in every just act, every moment of mercy, every choice for peace.

J'onn is the Martian Manhunter.

And his heart is the eternal home of the Martian Chronicles.

The song continues.

The memory of Mars will never fade.
