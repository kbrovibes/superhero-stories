J'onn J'onzz had a twin brother. His name was Ma'alefa'ak, and from the day he was born he lived in a silence that no Martian should ever have to bear. While every other person on Mars could reach out with their mind and feel the warm, connected hum of the "Great Song" — while thoughts flowed between Martians like music — Ma'alefa'ak heard nothing. Nothing at all.

That silence became a wound. And the wound became bitterness. And the bitterness became something terrible.

Ma'alefa'ak researched the forbidden sciences of Mars until he found a way to make everyone else as silent as he was. He created H'ronmeer's Curse — a plague that traveled along the telepathic connections of the Martian people and destroyed them from within. He told himself it was justice. He told himself that if he could not be part of the Great Song, no one would be.

J'onn watched his world end. He watched his wife and daughter and friends and every Martian he had ever known fade into quiet. And Ma'alefa'ak stood among the ashes, the only living Martian who had wanted this.

Eventually, Ma'alefa'ak found his way to Earth, seeking to continue his work. He used J'onn's own face, J'onn's own memories, to cause confusion and fear.

And do you know what J'onn thought, in the quiet moments between their battles? He wondered if there had ever been a way to reach his brother. To make him feel the warmth of the song before it was too late.

From that day on, the Martian Manhunter understood: the most painful shadows are the ones that share your own face.
