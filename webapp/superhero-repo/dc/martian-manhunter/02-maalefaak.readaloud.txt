J'onn J'onzz had a twin brother. His name was Ma'alefa'ak, and from the very first day of his life, he lived in a silence that no Martian should ever have to bear.

Can you imagine? On a planet where every single person shared their thoughts like music — where the Great Song hummed between all Martian minds constantly — Ma'alefa'ak heard nothing. Not a single note. Just silence. Complete and total silence, every day, forever.

While J'onn felt the warmth of millions of connected minds, his twin brother felt only the cold. That silence became a wound. The wound became bitterness. And the bitterness became something truly terrible.

Ma'alefa'ak dug into the forbidden sciences of Mars until he found a way to make everyone else as silent as he was. He created H'ronmeer's Curse — a plague that traveled along the very telepathic connections of the Martian people and destroyed them from within. He told himself it was justice. He told himself that if he could not be part of the Song, no one would be!

J'onn watched his entire world end. He watched his wife, his daughter, his friends — everyone he had ever known — fade into quiet. And Ma'alefa'ak watched too, the only living Martian who had wanted this.

Eventually Ma'alefa'ak followed J'onn to Earth, wearing J'onn's own face, using J'onn's own memories to confuse and deceive.

And in the quiet moments between their battles, J'onn wondered: was there ever a moment when Ma'alefa'ak might have been reached? Might have felt the warmth of the Song before it was too late? Might have chosen differently?

The most painful shadows, J'onn learned, are the ones that share your own face.
