On the red, quiet planet of Mars, a being named J'onn J'onzz lived a peaceful life. He was a Manhunter — a peacekeeper who solved problems with wisdom and kindness. He had a wife named M'yri'ah and a daughter named K'hym, and all around him the Martian people shared their thoughts and feelings the way humans share breath — openly, constantly, beautifully.

Then a terrible plague swept across Mars. It was called H'ronmeer's Curse, and it used the Martians' own telepathic connections against them. One by one, J'onn watched everyone he loved disappear. He was left alone on a silent, ruined world.

Then something very strange happened. On Earth, a scientist named Dr. Saul Erdel was experimenting with a transmatter beam — a device that could reach across the stars. By a great cosmic accident, the beam found J'onn and pulled him across the vast empty darkness between worlds in a single instant. Dr. Erdel did not survive the shock. And J'onn found himself standing alone in a laboratory on a loud, crowded, bewildering planet he had never seen.

He chose a human disguise — the name John Jones, detective — and began to learn about Earth. He found that humans were messy and complicated and wonderfully, stubbornly hopeful. And do you know what he discovered one ordinary afternoon? A chocolate-sandwich cookie called a Choco. He loved it immediately. It became his small reminder that even on a strange world, simple kindnesses matter.

From that day on, J'onn J'onzz became the Martian Manhunter, guardian of a planet that had given a grieving survivor a second home.
