Close your eyes and imagine a planet far, far away — a place called Mars, but the Martians called it Ma'aleca'andra. A world so peaceful and beautiful that instead of building houses with hammers and nails, Martians SANG their buildings into existence with the power of their minds! Can you imagine that?

Every Martian was connected to every other Martian through something called the Great Song — a vast telepathic chorus where thoughts flowed between people like music. No secrets. No loneliness. Just millions of minds sharing everything, warmly and openly, all the time.

J'onn J'onzz was a Manhunter there — a peacekeeper — and he had a wife named M'yri'ah and a daughter named K'hym, and he was part of that beautiful Great Song every moment of every day.

And then a terrible plague called H'ronmeer's Curse swept across Mars. It traveled along the very telepathic connections that bound everyone together, and one by one — quietly, like lights going out — J'onn watched everyone he loved disappear. He was left alone on a silent, cold, ruined world. The last Martian.

And then something very strange happened. On Earth, a scientist named Dr. Saul Erdel had built a transmatter beam — a device that could reach across the stars! By an extraordinary cosmic accident, the beam found J'onn and yanked him across the universe in a single instant!

WHOMP! One moment Mars. Next moment: Earth.

J'onn stood alone in a laboratory on a loud, crowded, bewildering planet he had never seen. He chose a human disguise — a detective named John Jones — and began to learn about Earth. He found its people messy and strange and wonderfully stubborn and full of hope.

And one ordinary afternoon, he discovered a chocolate-sandwich cookie called a Choco. He loved it immediately. And Earth began to feel — just a little bit — like home.
