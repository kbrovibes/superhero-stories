High above the clouds, orbiting in the quiet of space, the Justice League Watchtower glows like a second moon. And standing at its great observation windows, more often than not, is J'onn J'onzz.

The other heroes go home when the mission ends. Superman returns to Metropolis. Batman disappears into Gotham. But J'onn often stays at the Watchtower, looking down at the blue marble of Earth, listening. His telepathic mind reaches gently through space, touching the edges of millions of human thoughts — the hopes, the fears, the small quiet prayers of ordinary people going about their days. He listens for the one discordant note that might signal something wrong.

In the meditation chambers he sharpens his powers and keeps his enormous mental strength carefully balanced. In a corner of the station, he tends a small garden of Martian plants — a little piece of the home he lost, kept alive in the cold silence of space.

The vigil has a weight to it. J'onn hears things that are hard to hear. He carries that weight with grace and with patience, because he believes a guardian must be a witness.

And do you know what he does in the quieter moments? He shares a box of cookies with the Flash, who always arrives in a blur and leaves crumbs everywhere. He sits with Batman and talks about the nature of justice until the sun rises over the Earth below. These small, ordinary moments are the most precious things in the Watchtower.

From that day on, J'onn J'onzz proved that the greatest kind of courage is the willingness to keep watch, night after night, for a world that will never know your name.
