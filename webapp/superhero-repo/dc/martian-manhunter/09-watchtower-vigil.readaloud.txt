Way up above the clouds — high above the highest airplane, high above the weather, high above almost everything — the Justice League Watchtower orbits Earth like a second moon. And standing at its great curved observation windows, looking down at the blue planet below, is J'onn J'onzz.

The other heroes go home when the mission ends. Superman flies back to Metropolis. Batman vanishes into Gotham's shadows. The Flash races home for dinner. But J'onn often stays at the Watchtower — watching, listening, keeping the vigil.

His telepathic mind reaches gently outward through space, touching the edges of millions of human thoughts floating up from the world below: the hopes, the worries, the small quiet prayers of ordinary people going about their ordinary days. He listens carefully — always watching for the one discordant note that might mean trouble is coming.

In the Watchtower's meditation chambers, he practices his powers and keeps his enormous mental strength carefully balanced. And in one small corner of the station, he tends a tiny garden of Martian plants — a living piece of the home he lost, kept green and growing in the cold silence of space. Just a few plants. But they matter enormously.

The vigil has weight. J'onn hears things that are hard to hear. He carries that weight patiently, because he believes a guardian must also be a witness.

But here is the secret — the best part — of the long Watchtower nights: the Flash sometimes arrives in a blur, plops down next to J'onn, and they share a box of Chocos together, getting cookie crumbs everywhere. Batman comes and sits and they talk about the nature of justice until the sun rises over the Earth below.

Small ordinary moments. The most precious things in the whole Watchtower.

J'onn keeps watch for a world that will never know his name. And he does it gladly, every single night.
