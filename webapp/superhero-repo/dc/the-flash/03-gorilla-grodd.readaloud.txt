Deep in the jungles of Africa, hidden from every map and every satellite, there sits a remarkable city. The buildings are tall and gleaming, the streets are clean, and every citizen walks upright, speaks in full sentences, and thinks thoughts sharper than any knife. This is Gorilla City — and its king is a mighty gorilla named Grodd.

Gorilla Grodd was no ordinary creature. He was enormously strong, yes — strong enough to shake the ground with a single step. But his real power lived inside his mind. He was a telepath, which means he could reach his thoughts right into someone else's head, pulling at their memories and filling them with fear. He could make a person see things that weren't there at all, or feel terrors they had never imagined.

And do you know what Grodd wanted most of all? He wanted the Speed Force — the great, invisible river of energy that gave Barry Allen his speed. Grodd believed that gorillas were meant to rule, and that the Flash stood in the way of his grand plan to prove it.

Their battles were unlike anything Central City had ever seen. While the Flash raced across streets and rooftops, Grodd fought from inside Barry's own mind, trying to fill it with doubt and confusion. But Barry had something Grodd could never understand: a heart full of kindness and a clear, steady purpose. And that simple goodness turned out to be the most powerful shield of all.

From that day on, Barry Allen knew that the truest battles are sometimes the ones fought not with your feet — but with the calm, brave, unshakeable quiet of your own thoughts.
