Of all the enemies Barry Allen ever faced, none were more terrifying than the one who wore yellow.

His name was Eobard Thawne, and he came from the 25th century — hundreds of years in the future. He had studied Barry Allen his whole life, admired him, and then, slowly, grown to resent him. He recreated the same accident that made Barry the Flash. But the energy that filled Eobard was not warm and bright. It was cold and dark — the Negative Speed Force. And instead of becoming a hero, Eobard Thawne became the Reverse-Flash.

He was as fast as Barry. He was as clever as Barry. And he had one terrifying power that Barry didn't: he could slide through time and change things. He traveled into Barry's past and moved pieces around like a chess player, making certain sad things happen — hoping to mold Barry into the hero he obsessed over, while also making Barry suffer. It was a twisted, tangled logic that only made sense to a man who had lost his way entirely.

Their battles streaked across centuries, leaving trails of glowing energy in their wake. Barry never fought Eobard just with speed. He fought him with something the Reverse-Flash couldn't replicate: hope. A belief that goodness was worth protecting, no matter the cost. And do you know what that did? It kept Barry running forward, even when the shadow of yellow followed him back through time.

And that is how the Flash learned that the most personal battles are not always about the fastest feet — but about the warmest heart refusing to break.
