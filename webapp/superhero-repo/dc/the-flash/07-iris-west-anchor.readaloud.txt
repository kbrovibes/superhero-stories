Barry Allen can outrun a thunderstorm. He can circle the whole Earth in seconds. He can move so fast that time itself seems to slow down around him. But do you know what the fastest man alive can't do without? A reason to come home!

And that reason has a name: Iris West.

Iris is an investigative journalist in Central City — sharp, brave, and completely devoted to finding the truth. She asks hard questions. She goes where the story is, even when the story is somewhere dangerous. She is not afraid of much. And when Barry told her — just blurted out — the whole story of the lightning bolt and the chemicals and the Speed Force and the impossibly fast running? She did not run away. She didn't laugh. She listened. She HELPED. And from that moment on, Iris West became the most important person in Barry Allen's world.

She loves Barry when he is simply Barry — the forensic scientist who is always late, who forgets dinner reservations, who has evidence bags falling out of his coat pockets. She doesn't need him to be the Flash. She needs him to be himself. And do you know what that gives Barry? Something no amount of speed can create: a place that feels like home no matter how far he's run.

Iris is the tether. The anchor. When Barry reaches deep into the Speed Force — so deep that he risks losing himself in the infinite, dissolving into pure cosmic energy — it is the thought of Iris's face that finds him and pulls him back. Love, it turns out, is faster than the Speed Force.

Her courage inspires him too. As a reporter she walks into crises just like he does — no lightning bolt powers, no friction-resistant suit, just a notepad and the determination to find the truth. Barry watches her do it and is reminded: heroes come in every shape. And the ones without powers? Sometimes the bravest of all.
