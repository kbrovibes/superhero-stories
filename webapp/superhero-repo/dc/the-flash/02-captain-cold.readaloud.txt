Most villains are loud and messy and unpredictable. But today, the Flash is dealing with someone who is the exact opposite: cool as ice, precise as a clock, and absolutely brimming with a very strange sort of honor. His name is Leonard Snart. And in Central City, everyone calls him Captain Cold.

FWOOOOSH — a beam of freezing energy shoots from Captain Cold's special gun, and suddenly the ground is sheets of slippery ice! That cold gun of his can generate absolute zero temperature — the coldest cold that actually exists in science! It can freeze the very moisture in the air. And against someone whose power is all about moving really, really FAST? A sheet of perfectly placed ice on the road ahead is... a pretty good problem. Because even Barry Allen has to be careful not to wipe out at top speed!

But here's what makes Captain Cold so interesting: he's not wild. He's a professional. He plans every job like a brilliant chess match. He leads a whole crew of criminals called the Rogues — Mirror Master, Weather Wizard, Heat Wave, and the Trickster. And Leonard Snart has RULES for his crew. No hurting innocent people. No going after the Flash's family. No crossing certain lines. Period. If you break the rules, you're out of the Rogues. Because Captain Cold has a code of honor — a twisted one, sure, but real!

Do you know what that means? When something truly dangerous threatens Central City — something so bad that even the criminals in town are scared — Captain Cold and the Flash have sometimes ended up on the SAME side! They don't like it. They don't talk about it later. But they do it. Because they both love Central City, even if they show it very differently.

The Flash and Captain Cold: one warm, one cool. One blazing, one frozen. The world's most unlikely rivals — who somehow share more than either would ever admit!
