Not every person who stands in a hero's way is wild or reckless. Some are cool and careful — and none more so than Leonard Snart, the man the people of Central City called Captain Cold.

Leonard was a master thief, and he had one special tool: a cold gun that could freeze the moisture in the very air, dropping the temperature to absolute zero. Against a hero who moved at the speed of sound, that chill could slow everything right down. And that was exactly the point. Captain Cold was not fighting out of anger or cruelty — he was a professional, and he treated every heist like a job to be planned perfectly.

He led a team of criminals called the Rogues. His crew included Mirror Master, Weather Wizard, Heat Wave, and the Trickster — each one with their own gadget and their own style. But Leonard had rules. No hurting innocent people. No going after the Flash's family. If you wore the cold-weather coat and worked for Captain Cold, you followed the code. Do you know what that made him? One of the strangest villains Barry Allen ever faced — one with a twisted sense of honor.

The Flash and Captain Cold circled each other across Central City like two opposite forces: one burning with warmth and motion, the other still as ice. Barry even found himself admiring Leonard's loyalty to his crew. And when something truly dangerous threatened the city they both called home, Captain Cold and the Flash could — just barely, and just for a moment — end up on the same side.

And that is how Central City learned that even the coldest heart can hold something that looks, just a little, like a code of right and wrong.
