There is a moment that comes to every true champion — a moment when the limits they always believed in simply fall away. For Barry Allen, that moment came when the Speed Force stopped being something he ran into and started being something he became.

When it happened, the world looked different. Every atom hummed its tiny song. Every star traced a line of momentum across the dark. Past and present and future shimmered around him all at once, like sunlight on the surface of a river — not frightening, but impossibly beautiful. Barry could feel the connections between all living things, thin and bright and real. He was not just fast. He was motion itself.

In this state, he could do things that defied all explanation. He could steady a crumbling coastline before the earthquake reached the surface. He could guide the tide of a storm so it missed a sleeping city. He was everywhere the Speed Force touched — which was everywhere at all.

But with this wonder came a great danger. The deeper Barry went into the Speed Force, the harder it became to find the way back. He could feel himself starting to dissolve into the light. And do you know what pulled him home every single time? Iris West. The thought of her face. The thought of Central City's streets and the people who walked them. Love, it turns out, is the one force that even the Speed Force cannot outrun.

And that is how Barry Allen learned the greatest lesson of his life: the further you reach into the infinite, the more important it becomes to hold tight to the ones you love.
