KABOOM! ZZZAP! On one very ordinary rainy night in Central City, the whole world was about to change — and it happened so fast that Barry Allen didn't even see it coming!

Barry Allen was a forensic scientist at the Central City Police Department. Do you know what a forensic scientist does? They're detectives who use science — they look at clues and evidence and figure out what happened. Barry LOVED science more than anything. He worked late every night, surrounded by beakers and notebooks and test tubes, carefully studying clues. He was always the last one in the building. He was also — this is absolutely true — always late for everything else in his life. Meetings, dinner, appointments. ALWAYS late. (Don't worry, that's about to change!)

One dark and stormy evening, a bolt of lightning CRACKED through the laboratory window and smashed into a whole shelf of electrified chemicals! WHOOOOOM! The glowing liquid exploded and splashed all over Barry. And in that one breathtaking second — the whole world slowed down around him. Not because the world got slower. Because Barry got FASTER!

He could run faster than sound! He could vibrate his body so fast that he could run right through solid walls — WHOOSH — like a ghost! He could see things happening so quickly that nobody else could even notice them. His mind stayed clear and sharp while the world moved like it was stuck in honey! It was overwhelming at first. Sensation rushing in from everywhere at once!

Here's a fun fact: the first Flash, Jay Garrick, appeared in 1940 — but Barry Allen came along in 1956, and when he met Jay Garrick for the first time in 1961, it introduced the whole idea of the DC Multiverse! Two Flashes from different worlds! AMAZING!

Barry built a special friction-resistant suit that could handle his impossible speed, and became THE FLASH — the Scarlet Speedster of Central City! He discovered his speed came from something called the Speed Force — a cosmic river of energy that flows through the entire universe. And he ran right toward danger. Every time. WHOOOOOOSH — off he goes!
