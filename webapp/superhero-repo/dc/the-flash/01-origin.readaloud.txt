There once was a young scientist named Barry Allen who worked late every night in his laboratory at the Central City Police Department. Barry loved facts and evidence, and he believed the world followed careful, careful rules. He was always the last one in the building, surrounded by his beakers and notebooks — and he was always, always late for everything else.

One dark and stormy evening, something happened that no rule in any book could explain. A bolt of lightning crashed through the window and hit a shelf full of chemicals. The glowing liquid splashed all over Barry, and in that one breathtaking second, the whole world slowed down around him. And do you know what happened next? Barry Allen could run faster than the speed of sound.

He discovered he could zip through streets in the blink of an eye, slip through solid walls by vibrating his whole body, and see things that happened so fast no one else even noticed. It was overwhelming at first — like every sensation arriving all at once. But Barry had a kind heart and a strong sense of right, and he knew what to do with a gift like this.

He stitched together a special suit that could handle the heat and friction of his impossible speed, and he became The Flash — the Scarlet Speedster of Central City. He kept working at the police station as a scientist during the day, and raced into danger as a hero at night. He learned that his speed was connected to something called the Speed Force, a great cosmic river of energy that had chosen him as its champion.

From that day on, Barry Allen was the fastest man alive — and a founding member of the Justice League — proving that the most ordinary person can achieve the most extraordinary things when they choose to use their gifts for good.
