When trouble strikes anywhere in the world, the Justice League races to help. And among all those mighty heroes — Superman, Batman, Wonder Woman, and the rest — there is one who always arrives first. A streak of red and gold lights up the sky, and then, before you can even blink, the Flash is already there.

Barry Allen is the League's rescue specialist. When a great building crumbles or a flood rushes through the streets, he races through the wreckage faster than a human eye can follow, carrying people to safety one after another. He can move thousands of people out of harm's way in the time it takes you to count to ten. And the whole time he's doing it, he's talking — calm, kind, reassuring — because he knows that frightened people need a friendly voice just as much as they need strong arms.

He watches over his teammates too. In the middle of a battle, when a villain's attack comes from an unexpected direction, it's the Flash who steps in front of it. He's pulled Batman back from the edge of disaster. He's caught Wonder Woman when the fight knocked her off her feet. He is everywhere at once, a warm, blurring presence that tells his teammates: I've got you.

And do you know what the other heroes love most about Barry? He never loses his smile. Even in the darkest moments, when the job feels impossible, he cracks a gentle joke or says something hopeful — and just like that, everyone breathes a little easier.

From that day on, the Justice League learned that the fastest man alive was also, in the most important way, its biggest heart.
