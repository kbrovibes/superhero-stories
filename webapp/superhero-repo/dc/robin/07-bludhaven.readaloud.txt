Have you ever heard of Blüdhaven? Probably not — and that's actually the problem!

Most superheroes love big famous cities. Superman has Metropolis — bright and shiny! Batman has Gotham — moody and gothic! Wonder Woman has Themyscira — magical island! But Nightwing? Nightwing picked a city that nobody else wanted.

It's called BLÜDHAVEN. (You say it BLOOD-haven. Don't worry about the dots over the U — they're just there to look cool.) It's a rusty old port city just down the coast from Gotham. And honestly? It's a MESS!

Listen to this list:
• Rusted-out old factories everywhere! CREEEAK!
• Foggy alleys you can barely see through! WHOOOOO!
• The buses are ALWAYS LATE!
• The mayor is CROOKED — he takes bribes!
• The police department is corrupt — half the cops are in the mob's pocket!
• And the mob runs the docks!

Most heroes took one look at Blüdhaven and went "Yikes, no thank you!" But Nightwing? He took one look and said, "PERFECT."

Dick rented a small apartment on a street called Parkthorne Avenue. A brick walk-up with creaky stairs. He hid his Nightwing suit in a secret compartment UNDER the bed.

And then he did something clever. He decided to be TWO things at once.

BY DAY: He joined the Blüdhaven Police Department! As an actual real police officer! He even got a badge — number ONE SIX EIGHT. His partner was a sergeant named Amy Rohrbach, who was very smart, and she figured out pretty quick that her rookie partner was suspiciously GOOD at being a detective. (She didn't know why. Yet!)

BY NIGHT: He was NIGHTWING. Two black escrima sticks spinning in his hands — WHIRRRR WHIRRRR! Swinging between the rusty old warehouses, his blue bird-wings glowing in the foggy moonlight!

Now, Blüdhaven was a different kind of place from Gotham. In Gotham, the bad guys wear bright purple suits and laugh "HA HA HA" and have names like the Joker and the Riddler. In Blüdhaven, the bad guys wear ordinary boring suits. They run trucking companies. They pay off the wrong cops. The danger is QUIETER. And quiet danger is the hardest kind to catch.

So Dick had to be SNEAKY. He sat in diners listening to retired cops. He sat on the docks listening to longshoremen. He drew big maps of who-paid-who. Slow, careful, patient detective work. Just like Bruce taught him.

The biggest bad guy in Blüdhaven was a HUGE man named Roland Desmond. He called himself BLOCKBUSTER. And he was — get this — the SIZE OF A REFRIGERATOR! With shoulders as wide as a doorway! He owned half the city. He looked unbeatable.

But Dick is not afraid of big.

It took TWO YEARS. Two whole years of patient work. Court cases. Raids. Sneaky midnight stakeouts. One little piece at a time, Nightwing took Blockbuster's empire apart. Until one day — POOF! — Blockbuster's own people stopped showing up to work for him!

And little by little, beautiful things started happening in Blüdhaven!

The buses started running on time!
The crooked mayor got ARRESTED!
The docks reopened with honest workers!
The fog still rolled in — but the city felt LIGHTER underneath it.

No newspaper called it a victory. No giant Bat-Signal lit up the sky. But up on a rooftop on Parkthorne Avenue, in the fog, in the dark, a young man with a great spread of blue wings across his chest stood with his escrima sticks crossed behind his back. And he looked down at his city.

HIS city.

And finally, finally, it was finally HOME.

Nightwing of Blüdhaven. Quiet hero of the quiet city.
