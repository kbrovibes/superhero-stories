Here is a story about growing up.

You know how, when you were three years old, you had a little tiny T-shirt with a dinosaur on it that you loved? And then one day you put on the dinosaur shirt and oh no — your tummy stuck out the bottom! And your arms were too long for the sleeves! And the shirt didn't fit anymore? It wasn't because the shirt was bad. The shirt was wonderful. It was because YOU had grown up.

That is exactly what happened to Dick Grayson.

Dick was nineteen years old now. NINETEEN! He was taller than Bruce. He was stronger than Bruce. He had been leading the Teen Titans for four whole years, all the way across the country in Jump City. He had grown up.

And one night, his bright red and yellow and green Robin costume — the one Alfred had stitched when Dick was just NINE — felt like the dinosaur shirt. It wasn't the wrong size. It was the wrong SHAPE. Dick wasn't a Boy Wonder anymore. He wasn't anyone's sidekick.

Dick came home to Wayne Manor for a visit. He sat with Bruce on the back porch, watching the stars come out. And he said:

"Bruce. I think I'm done being Robin."

Bruce was quiet for a long time. Then he said one thing.

"I know."

(Because Bruce always knows.)

Dick told Bruce a story. A few years before, Superman had visited the Teen Titans. And Superman had told Dick about a legend from Krypton — Superman's home planet. On Krypton, there was a hero called NIGHTWING. A great warrior who soared through the bottle city of KANDOR in a long dark cape, protecting people. Superman had said:

"If you ever need a new name, Dick — I think Nightwing is yours. A bird that flies AFTER the sun goes down."

Dick had carried those words in his pocket for years. And now, finally, it was time to use them.

He designed the new suit himself! With Alfred's help, of course. (Alfred ALWAYS helps with the suits.) And here's what it looked like:

The bright red — GONE.
The bright yellow — GONE.
The bright green — GONE.

The new suit was SLEEK BLACK. Armored mesh, slim-fitting, strong but flexible. And right across the chest — listen, this is the cool part — was a single ROYAL BLUE bird shape, wings stretched from shoulder to shoulder! It looked like a flying bird with wings spread wide. The mask was longer and sleeker. The boots were jet black.

And — oh, this is GREAT — instead of Bird-arangs, Dick had two short black sticks clipped to his back. ESCRIMA STICKS! They were martial-arts weapons from the Philippines that Dick had been training with secretly for years. WHACK WHACK! He could spin them like a circus juggler.

He put on the new suit for the first time in the Batcave. He stood in front of a big mirror. And just behind him, lit softly under glass, was the OLD red-and-yellow Robin costume on a mannequin. Dick looked back at the bright little Robin. Then he looked at the tall blue-winged figure in the mirror.

He smiled.

Because here's the thing. He wasn't LEAVING Robin behind. He was CARRYING Robin inside him. The little robin was still there, in his chest, brave and bright. But now the little robin had grown up into something bigger.

A NIGHTWING. A bird with darker feathers — but still bright, still brave, still ready to fly. Even after the sun has gone down.

WHOOOSH! Nightwing was born!
