It was Robin's very FIRST mission as Batman's partner. Are you ready? Because this story is FAST!

It started with a phone call. RIIIING! Commissioner Gordon — the head policeman of all of Gotham City — was calling Bruce Wayne. "There's a bank robbery on Sprang Street," Gordon said. "Six guys. Red ski masks. They call themselves the Red Mask Crew. We need help."

Up on the roof of the Gotham Police Department, Gordon flipped a big switch. CLICK! And up went the BAT-SIGNAL — that giant yellow spotlight, painting a black bat shape right across the clouds! WHOOOOOSH!

At Wayne Manor, Bruce looked at Dick. Dick looked at Bruce. Bruce said two words.

"Suit up."

Two minutes later, the Batmobile — that long, low, dark, super-fast car — roared out of the Batcave. VRRROOOM! Batman behind the wheel. And right next to him — bright red, bright yellow, bright green, grinning ear to ear — sat ROBIN.

When they got to the bank, OH MY GOODNESS. Smoke alarms screaming! WEEEEE-OOOO WEEEEE-OOOO! Glass everywhere! Six robbers in red ski masks stuffing canvas bags full of money! A bank clerk hiding under the counter going SHHHHHH!

Batman swooped in through the skylight on the roof. WHOOOSH! His cape spread wide like enormous black wings. Three of the robbers screamed and RAN. The other three lifted up their bad-guy weapons and pointed them at Batman.

But guess what? They forgot to look BEHIND them.

Because Robin had snuck in through a side window. And Robin was already on the move. He grabbed a chandelier. He swung. He bounced off the marble counter. He flipped THREE TIMES in the air — and he landed in a perfect somersault, right behind the bad guys!

And then — and this is the BEST part — Robin tapped the first robber on the shoulder, just little tap tap, and when the robber spun around, Robin's eyes went big and his cape swirled and he shouted:

"HOLY BANK HEIST, BATMAN!"

The robber blinked. The robber stared. Where had this little bright bird come from? And while he was still blinking, Robin did one perfect flip-kick — just like a circus acrobat — and down went the robber. THUMP!

That night, "Holy something, Batman!" became a Robin tradition. Whenever he saw something surprising, he had to shout it. "Holy mackerel, Batman!" "Holy umbrella, Batman!" "Holy giant rubber ducky full of stolen jewels, Batman!"

You might think Batman would say "Robin, please be quiet!" But you know what? He didn't. Because Batman liked it. The bright, cheerful sound of Robin's voice made the dark cape feel a little less heavy. It reminded Batman that he wasn't ALONE down in the shadows anymore.

When Commissioner Gordon FINALLY arrived at the bank with all his police officers, he walked into the lobby and stopped. He STARED. Because all six robbers were neatly tied up with Bat-rope, in a big pile. And sitting RIGHT ON TOP of the pile, swinging his green boots, was a small grinning boy in red, yellow, and green. He waved.

Gordon — who had been a Gotham cop for TWENTY-FIVE YEARS, and almost never smiled — took off his hat. Scratched his head. And he LAUGHED out loud. A big, real, happy laugh.

Because Gotham City — for the first time in a very long time — had just gotten a little BRIGHTER.

The Dynamic Duo was here.
