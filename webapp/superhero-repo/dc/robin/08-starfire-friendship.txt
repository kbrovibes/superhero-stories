When Dick Grayson moved from Jump City to Blüdhaven, he was nervous about one thing more than any other. He was leaving the Titans. He was leaving Cyborg, and Raven, and Beast Boy. And he was leaving Kori. Kori — Princess Koriand'r of Tamaran — Starfire — had been his best friend on the team for years. She had bright orange skin, long red hair that floated when she flew, and a way of saying his name — "Friend Richard" — that made it sound like a small song. Dick had been worried that moving away would mean losing her.

He did not lose her. Not even a little.

What happened instead was that Starfire and Nightwing built a friendship that worked across two cities at once. Starfire was the fastest flier on the team — she could cover the distance from Jump City to Blüdhaven in about twenty minutes if she really opened up. So every Friday night, after Officer Grayson finished his shift at the Blüdhaven Police Department and changed into his Nightwing suit, Kori would come whooshing down out of the sky over the rooftop of his Parkthorne Avenue apartment, glowing orange in the foggy dark, with a big smile and usually a takeout bag of zorka-berry pies that she had picked up from a small Tamaranean bakery she had found in Star City.

They would sit on the roof. Sometimes they would patrol Blüdhaven together — Starfire flying high to scout while Nightwing swung between the warehouses. Sometimes they would just talk. Kori told Dick about Tamaran, about her sister Komand'r, about the K'tarr'm festival back home where the sky turned nine shades of purple. Dick told Kori about Mary and John Grayson, the trapeze, Haly's Circus. They knew each other's stories the way old friends do.

There was one famous night in Blüdhaven when a gang from out of town tried to attack the police precinct house. Nightwing was outnumbered ten to one. But up on the roof of the precinct, watching the sky, was a long red-haired figure who had been visiting for the weekend. Starfire dropped down out of the night sky like a small comet, green starbolts blazing, and the gang scattered before she even landed. Amy Rohrbach, watching from the precinct window, said quietly to herself, "I really need to ask Grayson about his friend from out of town."

Starfire and Nightwing were never the kind of friendship that needs to be in the same city. They were better than that. They were the kind that calls on the T-communicator at midnight just to say good night across half a continent. They were each other's home base, no matter which roof either one of them happened to be standing on.
