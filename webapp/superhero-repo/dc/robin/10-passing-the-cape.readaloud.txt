Here's the last big secret about Robin that I want to tell you.

There has been more than ONE Robin.

Did you know that? When Dick Grayson grew up and became Nightwing, the little red and yellow Robin costume with the big yellow R didn't just sit in a glass case forever. Oh no. In Gotham, the Robin tunic NEVER stays on a shelf for long. Somebody is always growing into it.

Dick became — and this is the BEST part of his whole story — the BIG BROTHER. To a WHOLE FAMILY OF ROBINS!

There were three of them after Dick. Let's meet them!

ROBIN NUMBER TWO: JASON TODD!

Bruce Wayne found Jason Todd trying to STEAL THE TIRES off the BATMOBILE! With a tire iron! In Crime Alley! (Yes — that exact same alley where Bruce lost his own parents.) Jason was a tough scrappy kid with a quick temper. He talked back. He yelled. He punched a wall once and broke his hand. Bruce brought him home.

Dick came back from Blüdhaven to help train him. Dick taught Jason how to do a flip without breaking a wrist. He taught him that being angry was OKAY — but you could turn that anger into FOCUS, like turning gasoline into a campfire. Jason never got all the way soft. But Jason LOVED Dick. And Dick loved Jason right back. Big brother stuff!

ROBIN NUMBER THREE: TIM DRAKE!

Tim was the SMART one. Listen to this — Tim figured out Batman's secret identity ALL BY HIMSELF. When he was TWELVE!

You know how? He saw old newspaper photos of the Flying Graysons at Haly's Circus. He saw old photos of Robin doing a triple somersault. He said, "Hey! Robin does the EXACT same somersault as the Flying Graysons! And the Graysons' little son was named Dick! And Dick now lives at Wayne Manor with Bruce Wayne! Which means... Bruce Wayne is BATMAN!"

He marched right up to Wayne Manor in the rain and told Bruce, very politely, "The Bat-Family needs a Robin again. I'm pretty good. I can help."

Dick visited Tim almost every WEEKEND while he trained. He took Tim out onto the foggy Blüdhaven rooftops. He taught him the quiet stuff — how to LISTEN for footsteps in fog, how to read a city, how to throw a Bird-arang with just two fingers.

ROBIN NUMBER FOUR: DAMIAN WAYNE!

Damian was the HARDEST. Damian was Bruce's actual SON! He was ten years old. He carried a SWORD on his back. He had been raised by ninjas (the League of Assassins). And he SCOWLED. Always! All the time! At everybody!

Damian did NOT want a big brother. He thought Dick was SILLY! He thought the bright Robin colors were "humiliating." (That's a fancy Damian word that means "embarrassing.")

But Dick was patient. The way only Dick could be. Every morning at SUNRISE, Dick would meet Damian on the Wayne Manor lawn with two practice escrima sticks. WHACK, WHACK, WHACK! They would spar in the wet grass. Sometimes Dick let Damian win — just by a little bit. And slowly, slowly, slowly — Damian started smiling. Just a tiny corner-of-the-mouth smile. But it was a real one.

Years later, somebody asked Dick what the most important thing he had EVER done was. They thought he'd say "leading the Teen Titans!" Or "becoming Nightwing!" Or "wearing the Batman cowl when Bruce was lost!"

Dick thought about it. For a long time. And then he said, very quietly:

"I taught my little brothers how to fly."

Because that's what the trapeze always taught him. The CATCH is the most important part. Dick was the boy who used to be caught — by his father, wrist to wrist. And he had grown up to become the CATCHER. For a whole family of Robins.

Big brother of all the Robins. That was Dick's real superpower.

And THAT, my friend, is the WHOLE story of Robin.
