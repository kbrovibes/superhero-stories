This story is a HARD one. Are you ready? Because it's the story of the day Gotham City lost its Batman.

It happened during a HUGE crisis. Bruce Wayne got lost in time — like, really lost, in a different century! — and nobody knew if he was ever coming back. For MONTHS, Gotham didn't have its Batman.

And without Batman? Things in Gotham got BAD.

The Joker got BRAVER! HA HA HA! He came out laughing in the streets!
The Penguin came out of hiding with his umbrella! WAUGH WAUGH!
Smaller gangs started carving up neighborhoods!
And every night, Commissioner Gordon went up to the roof of the police station and flipped on the BAT-SIGNAL.

But for the first time in TWENTY YEARS — nothing answered.

The yellow light bounced off the clouds. The clouds were empty. Gotham was scared.

So the Bat-Family had a meeting in the Batcave!

Want to meet everybody who showed up?

ALFRED PENNYWORTH — the butler — making tea for everyone, calmer than anybody, but you could see his hands were shaking just a tiny bit when he poured.

TIM DRAKE — that era's Robin, super super smart, sure he could find Bruce wherever he was.

DAMIAN WAYNE — Bruce's actual son! Only ten years old! Scary good with a sword! Always scowling!

BARBARA GORDON — also known as ORACLE — Commissioner Gordon's daughter — watching Gotham from her wheelchair through every security camera in the city.

And all of them turned and looked at one person.

DICK GRAYSON. Standing in the doorway. In his Nightwing suit.

They were waiting. Because they knew. Somebody had to be Batman until Bruce came home. Gotham couldn't stand without one.

Dick sat down in the great stone chair in front of the giant computer. He was very quiet. He thought about his mom on the trapeze. He thought about Bruce kneeling beside him in the sawdust ring, all those years ago, saying just one word: "I know."

He stood up. He walked over to the long glass cases. He picked out a Batsuit — not Bruce's classic one, but a new one, tailored to fit HIM.

And here's the cool part. He kept his Nightwing bird! The bright blue bird stayed right there across the chest, even with the long black cape and the pointy bat ears!

It was a Batman who used to be Robin.

A Batman who could STILL do triple somersaults off a gargoyle.

A Batman who would still shout "Holy something!" in the middle of a fight.

Dick took the cowl in his hands. He looked at it for a long, long time. Then he pulled it on.

And the new Batman? He needed a new Robin.

Damian Wayne — fierce little ten-year-old with the sword on his back — stood beside Dick that very first night on the roof of the Gotham City Police Department. He was wearing a bright Robin tunic that Alfred had altered just for him. He scowled out at the city.

Dick put one big hand gently on his small shoulder.

"Holy first night, partner," Dick said.

Damian rolled his eyes — but he ALMOST smiled.

The Bat-Signal went up against the clouds. The new Batman and the new Robin SWUNG off the rooftop together, capes flying out behind them, into the dark Gotham sky.

WHOOOOSH!

And down below, in the streets, Gotham City — for the first time in many, many scary months — lifted up its head. The Bat-Family had stepped up. The watch was being kept. The Dynamic Duo was still flying.

It just had different wings now.
