Have you ever watched a circus acrobat fly through the air on a trapeze? WHOOOSH! Did you know the most famous Robin in the world started his life exactly like that — flying through the air on a trapeze, in a little red and yellow costume, way up under a giant striped tent?

His name was Dick Grayson. He was eight years old. And he belonged to a family called the Flying Graysons. They were the star act of Haly's Circus — that's "HAY-lees," like the comet! They traveled all over the country in a long, rumbly train, painted with bright pictures of elephants and clowns. CHUGGA CHUGGA TOOT TOOT!

Dick's mom was named Mary. His dad was named John. They taught Dick how to swing, how to tuck, how to flip, and how to catch — wrist to wrist, every single time, never miss. Dick wasn't just good. He was AMAZING. People said he didn't walk on the high wire — he DANCED on it.

But one night, when the circus came to Gotham City, something terrible happened.

A grumpy mobster named Tony Zucco was trying to scare the circus owner into paying him money. When the owner said no, Zucco messed with the trapeze ropes. He damaged them in secret. And during the big show that night, in front of a huge crowd, the ropes gave way. Dick's mom and dad fell.

The big top went very, very quiet.

Sitting right in the front row, in a quiet dark suit, was a man named Bruce Wayne. Bruce had lost HIS parents too, when he was just a little boy, on a sad street called Crime Alley. So when Bruce looked at little Dick Grayson sitting alone in the sawdust, he understood exactly what Dick was feeling.

Bruce walked over. He knelt down. He said one word.

"I know."

That night, Bruce took Dick home with him — to a big house on a hill called Wayne Manor, with a long driveway and the kindest butler in the world, Alfred Pennyworth, who made hot cocoa with EXTRA marshmallows.

Dick was very quiet at first. He missed his mom. He missed his dad. He missed the smell of sawdust and rope.

But one night — listen to this part! — Bruce led Dick to a big old grandfather clock in the hallway. CLICK! The clock swung open like a secret door! Behind it was a long stone staircase going DOWN, DOWN, DOWN into the dark.

Down at the bottom of the stairs was the BATCAVE.

The bats fluttered up in a soft cloud. FLAP FLAP FLAP! Dick's eyes went huge. Computers blinked. The Batmobile gleamed.

Bruce said, "I lost my parents too. I built this so the same thing wouldn't happen to anybody else."

And Dick Grayson, the boy who used to fly, knew right then — he had just found his new home. And his new purpose.
