So Dick Grayson lived at Wayne Manor now. He had Alfred's pancakes for breakfast — pancakes with banana faces! He had Bruce Wayne for a guardian. And he had a HUGE secret. Down behind the grandfather clock, down the long stone staircase, was the Batcave. And every night after dinner, Dick went down there to TRAIN.

Bruce taught him EVERYTHING. How to punch. How to flip. How to throw a Batarang so it WHOOSHED through the air and came right back to your hand! How to pick a lock with two tiny little tools. How to step so quietly that not even a bat could hear you. CREEEEP. CREEEEP.

Dick was AMAZING at it. Because remember? He used to be a circus acrobat! So he could already leap higher and flip faster than almost anyone in the whole world. Bruce just had to teach him the new stuff — like patience. How to crouch on top of a stone gargoyle in the rain for a whole hour without moving. Hard work!

One night, Bruce said, "Dick. It's time."

"Time for what?" Dick said.

"Time to choose your name. If you're going to fight beside me, you need your own name. Not Batboy. Not Mini-Bat. YOURS."

Dick thought about it for a long, long time. He thought about flying on the trapeze. He thought about the WHOOSH of the wind on his face when he let go and somersaulted through the air. And then he thought about his mom, Mary, who used to whisper into his ear before every show. She called him a special name in Italian — "mio piccolo robin." That means MY LITTLE ROBIN. Because robins are the brave little birds that come back first in spring, even when it's still cold and snowy.

Dick looked up.

"ROBIN," he said. "I want to be Robin."

Bruce smiled. Just a little smile, in the corner of his mouth.

Alfred made the costume that very week! And listen to this — it was the BRIGHTEST hero costume you have ever seen in your life. Bright RED tunic. A great big YELLOW letter R sewn right over the heart, on a black circle. GREEN sleeves like wings! GREEN leggings like a circus acrobat! A YELLOW cape lined with black! And a little black mask across his eyes.

When Dick put it on and looked in the mirror, he didn't see a sad boy anymore. He saw a brave, bright bird. Ready to FLY again.

He climbed up the rope all the way to the tippy-top of the Batcave. WAY up. He looked down. There was Bruce, in his long dark cape and pointy cowl, looking up.

Dick shouted one word: "CATCH!"

And he LEAPED.

WHOOOSH! Triple somersault — one, two, THREE! — just like his dad taught him on the trapeze.

Bruce caught him. Wrist to wrist. Just like the Flying Graysons. Perfect.

And that — that very moment, right there in the Batcave with the bats fluttering overhead — was the moment the world's most famous crime-fighting team was born.

Batman.

And Robin.

The Dynamic Duo!
