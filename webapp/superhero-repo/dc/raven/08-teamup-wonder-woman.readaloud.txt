You know what arrives at Titans Tower one ordinary morning? A LETTER. Not from a villain. Not a distress signal. A letter, sealed with the seal of Themyscira — from WONDER WOMAN!

Yes! Diana of Themyscira, princess of the Amazons, one of the greatest heroes on Earth — writing to the Teen Titans and asking for help.

An ancient being called the Dreamwarden had escaped its containment. Its power: pulling entire cities into an endless fog of looping DREAMS. People would walk the same streets over and over, always arriving at the same bright corner, always feeling like everything was fine — even though nothing was fine at all.

Here is the problem: you CANNOT punch a dream-fog! THWACK — nothing. BLAM — nope. Starfire's green starbolts: useless. Cyborg's sonic cannon: no effect whatsoever. Robin's best strategy hit the fog and disappeared without a trace. To get through this, you needed something completely different.

You needed an empath.

Wonder Woman arrived at the dimensional threshold the next morning: Lasso of Truth at her hip, tall and calm as a mountain, expression serious and steady. She greeted each Titan by name. Robin. Starfire. Cyborg. Beast Boy. Then she looked at Raven.

"The monks of Azarath believed that dream-states are navigated by someone who can hold a feeling without being captured by it," she said. She met Raven's steady violet eyes. "That is you."

Raven nodded once.

Into the fog they went. WHOOOOOM.

Oh, it was strange in there. Streets looped. Every corner brought you right back to the beginning — the same three pigeons, the same fountain, the same peacefully wrong feeling. Raven moved with her eyes half-closed, following the Dreamwarden's emotional signature through the maze like a thread through dark water. She found the anchor in a plaza: a small carved stone, humming faintly, holding the whole fog together like a stitch holds a seam.

Outside, Wonder Woman kept the Dreamwarden occupied: CLANG — Themysciran bracelets deflecting attacks! WHOOSH — the Lasso of Truth pulling a wall of fog aside! Spectacular. She was buying Raven exactly the time she needed.

Raven sat before the stone. Breathed slowly. Found the one thread inside the knot — and carefully, carefully loosened it. Not yanking. Not forcing. Just finding the way through.

Twenty-two minutes.

WHOOOOOOSH — the fog lifted. All of it. Gone like a breath. Streets straightened. The city's people blinked at the sky, suddenly back in the right afternoon.

Wonder Woman looked at Raven. "You have remarkable control."

"I have practice," Raven said.

Wonder Woman smiled — a warm, genuine smile. "Those are the same thing."

She was exactly right.
