The letter arrived at Titans Tower on an ordinary morning, which is always when extraordinary things begin. Wonder Woman — Diana of Themyscira, princess of the Amazons, one of the most powerful heroes on Earth — was requesting the Teen Titans' assistance with a situation in a dimension adjacent to Azarath. An ancient being called the Dreamwarden had escaped its containment and was now pulling entire cities into an endless fog of confused, looping dreams. It could not be fought in the usual way: there was nothing to punch, no target for a starbolt, no opponent for Robin's grapple. What was needed was an empath who could navigate the dreamscape and find the anchor point holding the fog in place.

Wonder Woman met them at the dimensional threshold with the Lasso of Truth on her hip and the calm authority of someone who had seen very strange things before and expected to see them again. She looked at the Titans assembled, and then she looked at Raven with particular attention. "I have read what I could find about Azarathian training," she said. "The witnessing practice. The monks believed that the interior of a dream-state is navigated best by someone who can hold a feeling without being captured by it." She paused. "That is you."

Raven nodded once.

Inside the Dreamwarden's fog, the world tilted and looped. The streets of the affected city repeated, always starting over, always leading back to a bright intersection that felt peaceful but wasn't. Raven moved through it with her eyes half-closed, following the emotional signature of the Dreamwarden itself — a dense, heavy feeling, like a very old worry that had become its own weather. She found the anchor point buried in a plaza: a small carved stone, vibrating faintly with concentrated dream energy.

Wonder Woman fought the Dreamwarden's outer shape while Raven worked at the stone — carefully, the way you un-tangle a knot, not pulling hard but finding the thread that was holding everything and gently loosening it. It took twenty-two minutes. When the anchor released, the fog lifted all at once, like a breath going out. The city's streets straightened. The people in them blinked and looked around.

Walking back through the dimensional threshold, Wonder Woman said to Raven, "You have remarkable control." Raven considered this. "I have practice," she said. Wonder Woman smiled. "Those are the same thing," she said.
