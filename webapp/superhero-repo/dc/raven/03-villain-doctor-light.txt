Doctor Light did not believe in Raven. That was his first mistake. The villain arrived in Jump City on a Tuesday, filling the harbor with blinding white beams that cracked windows for three blocks in every direction and turned the afternoon sky into something too bright to look at. Arthur Light — that was his real name, though he preferred the dramatic title — wore a suit of his own invention that bent and shaped light into solid constructs: walls, chains, a blinding spotlight strong enough to stagger anyone who looked directly at it. He had decided the Teen Titans were not worth worrying about.

Starfire hit him twice with her starbolts before he redirected her beams back at her with a mirror construct, knocking her sideways into a parked delivery van. Cyborg fired a sonic blast and Doctor Light split it into a harmless spray of color. Robin's grappling hook tangled in a cage of frozen light, and Beast Boy — trying as a falcon, then a rhinoceros — couldn't get close enough. Doctor Light laughed. He had a very loud, unpleasant laugh.

Raven had been quiet at the back of the group, watching. Not because she was uncertain. Because she was reading the pattern. Doctor Light relied on one principle: everything he did traveled in straight lines. Light was predictable. Darkness was not.

She stepped forward. Her soul-self rose and spread, a deep, swirling shadow that absorbed the beams before they could bounce or redirect. Doctor Light fired his brightest construct directly at her — a solid bar of white light wide as a car. Raven extended one hand and her telekinesis deflected it sideways into the bay, where it hissed out harmlessly in the water. Then she wrapped her shadow around his suit's power coupling — the small glowing node at the center of his chest — and squeezed. Not with anger. Just firmly, the way you turn off a lamp.

The suit went dark. Doctor Light blinked in ordinary afternoon light, without any of his constructs, looking much less impressive than he had ten seconds ago. Cyborg had him contained before another second passed.

Starfire flew back over the harbor, slightly rumpled but smiling, and told Doctor Light cheerfully that in her home world of Tamaran the word for someone who underestimates an empath translates loosely as "a person who has not thought carefully about anything." Raven said nothing. She pulled her cloak straight and waited for Robin to call the Jump City police.
