Brother Blood called himself the High Commander of the H.I.V.E. Academy, a secret school somewhere in the mountains where he trained young people to be the opposite of heroes. His real talent was not combat — it was control. Brother Blood could reach into a person's mind and turn their loyalty around as if it were a key in a lock. It was a power of persuasion, something between hypnosis and absolute will, and it made him one of the most unsettling opponents the Teen Titans had ever faced.

He had tried before to recruit the Titans, or to defeat them, and each time he had failed in ways that surprised him. But when he returned to Jump City with a team of his H.I.V.E. cadets — each one trained, focused, and under his influence — he was certain he had a new advantage. He had studied the Titans' weaknesses carefully. He had read everything he could find about their empathic member. And he had decided that Raven's ability to feel others' emotions was not a strength. It was a door.

He was wrong about that.

When Brother Blood attempted to project his will toward Raven, pressing his mental control against her the way he would push it against anyone else, he met something he had never encountered: a mind that had been practicing stillness since childhood. The monks of Azarath had trained Raven to meet any emotional pressure — rage, despair, coercion — with quiet, steady attention rather than reaction. Brother Blood's influence found nothing to grip. It moved through her awareness like wind through open hands.

Raven used her empathy differently in that moment. Instead of being overwhelmed by his projected will, she read it — the way you read a page, calmly and carefully — and understood exactly what he was doing and how. She then extended that understanding to her teammates, projecting a gentle alert through the team's emotional connection: a quiet, clear signal of where Brother Blood's control was reaching and how to resist it.

Robin broke free first. Then Cyborg. Then Beast Boy and Starfire together. Brother Blood stared at the circle of un-controlled, very determined Titans surrounding him and seemed, for just a moment, to look genuinely confused. The H.I.V.E. cadets, freed from his influence, were confused too — and considerably more interested in leaving than in fighting. Robin took Brother Blood into custody. Raven sat on the steps of the city library afterward, drinking tea from a thermos she had brought in her cloak pocket, and said that the lesson was straightforward: a mind built on manipulation has no idea what to do with patience.
