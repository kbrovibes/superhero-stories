It's 2:47 in the morning. The alarm in Titans Tower is going BRRRRING BRRRRING!

Heroes don't get to choose their timing. Up! Cloaks on! Let's go!

The problem: a creature called the Dissonance. Not a monster with claws. Not a robot. Not anyone in a costume. An entity made of pure hostile emotion — centuries of bitterness swirled together until they became something alive. It had materialized in Jump City's power grid and was GROWING, absorbing the frightened feelings of everyone in the city around it.

Its weapon: waves of directionless OVERWHELM. Like standing inside a thunderstorm made entirely of feelings. People nearby would freeze. Or argue. Or simply forget what they had been trying to do.

CYBORG ran his instruments and they went haywire. BZZZZT — no readings at all. The entity scrambled electronics. BEAST BOY flew up as an eagle, caught a wave of the Dissonance head-on, and landed on the roof in a heap going "wow, that is extremely unpleasant." STARFIRE fired her starbolts — WHOOSH — dissolved before they hit anything. ROBIN threw everything: gadgets, tactics, quick thinking. Nothing stuck.

They all looked at Raven.

Raven was standing very still, which is exactly what she looked like when she was doing her most important work. She was reading the Dissonance with her empathy like reading a complicated page — slowly, carefully, finding what was underneath.

She had found something.

"It has no center," she said quietly. "Noise without a foundation. It cannot absorb something genuine — it has no framework for real feeling."

She paused. "I need each of you to feel something true. Specific. Yours."

Robin thought of Gotham City — the night he decided what kind of hero to be.

Starfire thought of Tamaran, her home star, the view of it from space.

Cyborg thought of his father's workbench — machine oil, neat rows of tools, Saturday mornings.

Beast Boy thought of the tower roof when the sky went three colors and nothing special was happening and it was completely enough.

Raven felt all of it. She added her own: Azarath, the towers, the bells, the water, her mother's voice. She wove all five together and sent them outward — not a blast. A presence. Real feeling meeting something that had never known what real felt like.

The Dissonance FALTERED. Then it broke — pieces dissolving, the whole thing folding inward like a fire going out. WHOOOOSH. Done.

Four minutes.

The five Titans sat on the roof in the dark, and nobody said anything, and it was the very best kind of quiet.
