Here is a question for you: have you ever just KNOWN that a friend was having a bad day, even before they said anything? That feeling — that's empathy. Most people have a little of it. Raven has ALL of it.

Not a little empathy. Not medium empathy. Not even a lot of empathy. For Raven, feeling other people's emotions is not a choice — it is a sense. Like hearing. Like seeing. It just arrives.

When Raven walks through Jump City — dark blue cloak, hood up, very quietly — she feels EVERYONE around her. The businesswoman rushing to a meeting: BZZZZ, a hum of nerves. The little kid at the crosswalk: ZAP, a bubble of impatient energy. The old man outside the bookshop: MMMMM, a slow, contented warmth. She does not choose this. It just is.

So how does she manage all of it without going completely sideways?

The monks of Azarath taught her something called witnessing. It means: you can be fully present to another person's feeling — you can really feel it, really know it — without losing your own footing. Like you can hear a song playing in the next room without forgetting where you are standing.

Raven practices this every single day. In Titans Tower, she feels the whole team at once: Robin's sharp, focused discipline. Cyborg's big, steady, practical warmth. Beast Boy's wild chaotic bounce of energy — she once described it as "what a pinball machine would feel like if it were enthusiastic about everything." And Starfire's joy, which is like standing near a very cheerful sun.

She holds all of this at once. Every single day.

And here is what it means in practice:

Raven often knows when a mission is going sideways before anyone says it. She feels the shift when someone's concentration cracks. She shows up at the right moment with exactly the right thing — a word, a silence, once a cup of chamomile tea for Robin during a long planning session when she was absolutely certain he was about to make a tired mistake. She said nothing about it. She just put the tea on the table beside him.

He made a better decision.

And here is the secret about Raven that surprises people: she is one of the kindest heroes on the team. Not in a loud way. She won't sweep you into a big hug. But when you are sad, she will sit nearby. Not too close. Not making a big deal of it. Just… there. Steady. Letting you know, without a single word, that she knows exactly how you feel, and that you are not alone in it.

Her teammates learned, after a while, that this was the best kind of comfort of all.
