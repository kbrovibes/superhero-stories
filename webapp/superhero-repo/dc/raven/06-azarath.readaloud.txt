Okay, today we are going on a trip. Not to space — though the Teen Titans go to space sometimes too. Not underwater — though that has also happened. Today we are going somewhere even more extraordinary.

We are going to Azarath.

To get there you have to travel between dimensions — slipping past the edge of the regular world into a space that exists in between. Close your eyes and I will describe it.

White towers. TALL ones — smooth as polished stone, rising from water that is deep silver and perfectly still. The sky is a soft grey-blue, like the very last moment before sunrise. Not dark. Not bright. Just… peaceful. The whole place hums with quiet — not silence, QUIET. There is a difference. Silence is the absence of sound. Quiet is the presence of peace.

This is where Raven grew up.

Her mother Arella had a room at the top of one of the towers, and in the mornings Raven could hear water moving softly against the base of the building. There was a library three stories tall full of ancient scrolls and maps of dimensions that don't have names in any language spoken on Earth. And there was a circular meditation hall in the center of it all — no walls, just columns, open sky all around — where the monks sat every morning in absolute stillness.

Raven sat with them from the time she was very small. At first it was hard! Her mind would wander. She'd feel the monks' emotions all around her like little warm lights — focused, peaceful, steady — and she'd want to move or ask questions or think about her books. But slowly, slowly, she learned. The stillness settled into her bones.

When she left Azarath to join the Teen Titans, she brought almost nothing with her. The cloak — dark blue, woven in Azarath from a special fabric that helps focus her power. And one small book of meditations that she keeps on her nightstand in Titans Tower.

Starfire found the book once. She opened it curiously — and the text suddenly appeared in TAMARANIAN, the language of Starfire's home world! Just for a moment. Then it shifted back to Azarathian.

Starfire set it down very carefully and told no one for about thirty seconds, and then told everyone immediately.

Raven just nodded. "It does that," she said.

Here is what to remember about Azarath: Raven carries it everywhere. In the way she breathes when things get hard. In the way she goes still when other people go noisy. In the way she finds the steady place inside herself no matter what is happening outside.

The monks of Azarath said: a place lives in you long after you leave it.

They were absolutely right.
