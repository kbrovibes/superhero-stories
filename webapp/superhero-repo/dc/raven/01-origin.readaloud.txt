Close your eyes for just one second and picture a world where everything is peaceful. White towers. Still water. Soft light. That place is called Azarath, and that is where our story begins!

Her name is Raven. She has violet eyes, a dark blue cloak, and the quietest voice you have ever heard — but don't let that fool you. This girl has MORE power tucked inside her than most heroes twice her size.

Raven grew up in Azarath, a peaceful dimension far beyond the ordinary world. The monks who lived there were gentle and wise, and they taught her the most important skill she would ever learn: how to stay CALM. Not because she was timid — because her feelings could shake the walls! When Raven got frightened or angry, her powers would SURGE and objects would fly and shadows would ROAR. So she breathed slowly. She kept steady. She learned control.

Here is what made Raven extraordinary. She could feel everyone else's emotions. If you were scared, she FELT it. If you were happy, a little warmth bloomed right inside her chest. She could move objects without touching them — WHOOSH, across the room they'd go — and she could send out her soul-self, a shape of dark energy in the form of a raven, sweeping through the air like living shadow. WHOOOOOM!

Now, Raven also carried a secret. Her father was a powerful demon named Trigon. That was a heavy thing to know about yourself. But the monks of Azarath had taught her something important: you do not choose your family, but you absolutely choose your actions. Raven chose kindness. She chose courage. Every single day.

When she sensed a great danger heading toward Earth, she traveled to Jump City and knocked on the door of the bravest people she could find. Robin the leader — always with a plan. Starfire who glows like a comet. Cyborg, half-machine half-hero, strong as a tank. Beast Boy who turns into ANY animal — ROAR, SQUEAK, SPLASH! Together they built a team. Together they moved into Titans Tower, a T-shaped building right on the water's edge.

And the Teen Titans were born!

On their first night, Raven sat in the library with chamomile tea and a book she loved, listening to Starfire singing, Cyborg laughing, Beast Boy trying — and failing — to tell a story straight. She kept her smile tucked inside her cloak where nobody could see it.

But it was there. A real, quiet, warm smile.

Raven doesn't fight because she loves battles. She fights because she loves her team, she loves Jump City, and she believes — deeply, quietly, every single day — that goodness is worth choosing no matter what.

Now THAT is a hero.
