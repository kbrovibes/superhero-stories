In the peaceful dimension of Azarath, where white towers rose above still water and the air hummed with quiet meditation, a girl named Raven grew up. The people of Azarath were gentle and calm, and they taught Raven their ways: how to breathe through her feelings, how to listen before speaking, how to find stillness even when the world was loud and strange. She wore a dark blue cloak and kept her violet eyes steady and thoughtful. For most of her childhood, that peace was enough.

But Raven carried a secret that set her apart from everyone else in Azarath. Her father was Trigon, a powerful demon from a dark realm beyond the reach of ordinary worlds. Arella, her mother, had come to Azarath seeking refuge, and the monks there had helped raise Raven in safety, training her to contain the enormous power that lived inside her. Raven's abilities were extraordinary: she could move objects with her mind, project her soul-self into the world as a shape of dark energy, and feel the emotions of everyone near her as clearly as if they were her own.

That gift of empathy was both a wonder and a weight. When someone nearby felt frightened, Raven felt it too. When someone was joyful, warmth bloomed in her chest. She learned to keep her own emotions deeply calm, because she understood — from years of quiet practice in Azarath — that strong feelings could cause her power to surge in ways that frightened people. So she kept her voice low, her expression still, and her hood drawn close. It was not coldness. It was care.

When Raven was a teenager, she sensed that a great threat was gathering, something that even Azarath could not hold back alone. She traveled to Earth, to Jump City, and sought out other young heroes like herself — people who understood what it was to be different, to carry a power that not everyone understood. She found Starfire, Cyborg, Beast Boy, and Robin. Together they became the Teen Titans, a team that met in Titans Tower and protected the city from dangers too strange and fierce for anyone to face alone.

Raven did not come to the Titans because she was fearless. She came because she had learned, in Azarath, that goodness is a choice you make every single day — especially on the days when it is hard. She had heard the old monks say it many times, and she believed it with every quiet beat of her heart. Whatever shadow lived in her past, the light she chose was entirely her own.
