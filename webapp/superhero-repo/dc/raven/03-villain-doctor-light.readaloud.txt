Okay, here is something you should know about a villain named Doctor Light: he thought he was the cleverest person in the entire city of Jump City. He was VERY wrong about that.

Arthur Light showed up on a Tuesday — because trouble never waits for the weekend — and he filled the whole harbor with BLAZING white light! KSHHHH! FWOOM! Windows cracked. Cars stopped. People threw their hands over their eyes. His special suit could shape light into anything: walls, cages, chains, a spotlight so bright it was like looking straight at the sun.

"Teen Titans?" he said, laughing loudly. "I'm not worried about the Teen Titans."

First mistake.

ZAAAP — Starfire fired her starbolts, and Doctor Light bounced them right back at her using a mirror construct. CRASH — she went into a delivery van. Ouch. Then Robin's grapple got tangled in a cage of frozen light. Beast Boy tried a falcon — blocked! Tried a rhino — too slow! Cyborg blasted with his sonic cannon — BOOM — and Doctor Light split it into a rainbow and laughed some more.

All this time, Raven was standing at the back, watching. She wasn't hesitating. She was READING. Because Raven is the smartest person in the room, and she knew something Doctor Light had not figured out yet.

All his attacks? They went in straight lines. Light always does.

But shadows? Shadows can wrap around anything.

Raven stepped forward. Her soul-self ROSE behind her — that great dark raven, stretching wide — and her shadows SWALLOWED his beams before they could go anywhere. FWOOOM! Doctor Light's eyes went wide. He fired his BIGGEST beam straight at her — a solid bar of light wide as a car!

Raven raised one hand. WHOOSH — telekinesis flicked it sideways into the bay. HSSSSS — it went out in the water like a candle in a puddle.

Then she reached her shadow around his suit, found the little glowing power node at the center of his chest, and simply… turned it off. Like a lamp.

CLICK.

The suit went dark. The lights went out. Doctor Light stood in ordinary Tuesday afternoon sunshine with absolutely no dramatic constructs and looking extremely surprised about it.

Cyborg had him in cuffs in about two seconds.

Starfire flew back over the water — a tiny dent in her boot, but smiling — and told Doctor Light very cheerfully that on her home world of Tamaran, there is a word for a villain who underestimates an empath. She said it was a very long word.

Raven just straightened her cloak and waited for Robin to call the police.

She hadn't said a single word to Doctor Light. She hadn't needed to.
