Listen closely! This story is about a very sneaky villain — and a very smart hero who was ready for him.

His name was Brother Blood, and he ran something called the H.I.V.E. Academy: a secret school hidden in the mountains where he trained young people to cause trouble. But Brother Blood's real power wasn't punching or shooting lasers. No, no. His real power was much scarier than that.

He could reach into your MIND and flip your loyalty around like a light switch.

That is a very unpleasant superpower.

Brother Blood had heard about the Teen Titans. He'd heard about Raven. He had done his homework, reading everything he could find about empaths and emotions and how Raven's gift worked. And he came to a conclusion.

"Her empathy," he said to himself, "is a DOOR."

He thought: she feels emotions, I project emotions, therefore I can control her.

He was SO wrong.

He marched into Jump City with a squad of H.I.V.E. cadets — trained, focused, ready for trouble — and aimed his mental control straight at Raven. ZZZNNNN — like pushing a radio signal into someone's head. He pressed his will forward and waited for it to catch.

And Raven… breathed.

That's it. She breathed slowly, the way the monks of Azarath had taught her for years and years. Her mind was a still pond. His will moved through it like wind over water — leaving absolutely no mark at all.

Raven didn't feel controlled. She felt informed. She was reading his projected thoughts calmly, and now she understood EXACTLY what he was doing and exactly how he was doing it.

She sent a soft signal through her empathic connection to each teammate — a gentle tap on the shoulder, clear as a whisper. Here is what he is doing. Here is how to resist.

SNAP — Robin broke free. Eyes clear, expression determined.

SNAP — Cyborg powered up.

SNAP SNAP — Beast Boy and Starfire together, side by side.

Brother Blood looked around. His cadets, suddenly free of his influence, looked confused and mostly interested in running away. He was surrounded by four very focused and considerably annoyed Teen Titans.

Robin took him into custody. Very tidily.

Afterward, Raven sat on the steps of the Jump City library with a thermos of chamomile tea she'd had tucked in her cloak the whole time. Beast Boy sat beside her.

"A mind built on manipulation," she said, "has no idea what to do with patience."

"That is very smart," Beast Boy said. "Also, you had tea this whole time and didn't offer any to anyone?"

Raven took a sip.

"Correct," she said.
