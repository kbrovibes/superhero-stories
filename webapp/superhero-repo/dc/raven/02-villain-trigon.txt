The most frightening moment Raven ever faced did not come from a stranger. It came from her own past. Trigon was the name whispered only in the oldest books of Azarath — a demon of terrible power who ruled a dark dimension of his own making. When Raven was young, the monks of Azarath had explained very carefully who her father was, and why keeping her emotions controlled was not just discipline — it was the one thing that kept Trigon's influence from reaching through to the world she loved.

For years, she had kept that barrier strong. But Trigon was patient. He waited in his dimension like a storm waiting to break, sending his will forward in small ways: whispers in Raven's sleep, a shadow at the edge of her mirror, moments where her own voice seemed to say things she had never meant to say. Raven recognized the signs. She had read about them in the ancient scrolls of Azarath. She told her team at Titans Tower, her voice quiet and steady even though her hands were cold.

Robin called it a mission. Starfire stood close to Raven, warm and ready. Cyborg ran every scan he had. Beast Boy tried to make a joke — he always did — and Raven actually appreciated it, though she kept her expression level. Trigon's energy appeared in Jump City as a deep red shimmer, distorting the walls of the city, trying to force a passage from his dimension into theirs. He spoke in a voice like a thunderstorm heard from underground, promising power, promising belonging, telling Raven that she was like him and always would be.

Raven closed her eyes. She breathed slowly, the way the monks of Azarath had taught her since she was small. She stretched her soul-self outward, that great dark bird of living shadow, and she pushed back. She did not push with anger — anger was exactly what Trigon was counting on. She pushed with the steady, quiet certainty of someone who had chosen her own path a thousand times already and was choosing it again right now.

The red shimmer cracked. Trigon's voice faded. The city walls stopped shaking. When Raven opened her eyes, the sky above Jump City was clear and ordinary and full of stars. She folded her cloak around herself and said nothing for a long moment. Then she looked at Robin and nodded once. He nodded back. That was enough.
