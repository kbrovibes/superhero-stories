Are you ready for the scariest chapter? Don't worry — Raven is on the case. Take a deep breath. Just like she does.

Now. Every great hero has a challenge that comes from close to home, and for Raven, that challenge had a name that was written only in the oldest scrolls of Azarath: TRIGON.

Trigon was Raven's father. He was a demon — huge and red and full of crackling power — who lived in his own dark dimension and spent his days waiting. Just waiting. Slowly, like a storm building behind a mountain. He sent whispers. He sent shadows. He made Raven's sleep uneasy and made her mirror look wrong at the corner of her eye. Raven knew the signs. She had read about them. She was ready.

Then the attack began FOR REAL.

A deep red shimmer appeared in Jump City — BWOOOOM — and the walls of buildings started to shake. Trigon was trying to force a passage between his world and theirs, like pushing a fist through a locked door. His voice came through the cracks, low and rumbly, like thunder heard from underground. "You are like me, Raven," it said. "You will always be like me."

Raven gathered the Teen Titans. Robin stood straight and made a plan — he always had one. Starfire glowed green and fierce, her starbolts charged. Cyborg ran every scan at top speed — BEEP BEEP WHIRR — tracking the dimensional breach. Beast Boy turned into a bear, thought about it, turned into a rhinoceros, thought again, and then turned back and stood close to Raven instead. Good call.

And Raven?

Raven closed her eyes.

She breathed in. She breathed out. Slowly, slowly, the way the monks of Azarath had shown her a hundred times when she was small. The way her mother Arella had shown her even before that, in a soft voice, with a hand on her shoulder.

Her soul-self rose around her — that great dark raven shape, wings of shadow spreading wide, filling the air with a deep and certain power — and she pushed BACK against Trigon's passage. Not with anger. Not with fear. With the calm, certain knowing of someone who has made her choice and made it and made it again, every single day, for her entire life.

CRACK — the red shimmer broke. CRACK — the passage closed. WHOOSH — Trigon's voice was gone, swept away like smoke on a clean wind.

Raven opened her eyes. The sky above Jump City was full of ordinary stars. The city was quiet. The buildings stood exactly as they had before.

She looked at Robin. She nodded once. He nodded back.

That is what courage really looks like. Not shouts. Not fury. Just quiet, steady, and absolutely sure.
