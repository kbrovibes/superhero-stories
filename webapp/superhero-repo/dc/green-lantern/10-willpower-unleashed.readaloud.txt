There are moments — rare, brilliant, terrifying — when a Green Lantern stops using the ring, and the ring starts using everything the Green Lantern is.

That is what happened to Hal Jordan in the heart of a sentient nebula that was swallowing entire solar systems. The nebula's gravity was crushing — heavier than anything Hal had ever felt. His constructs flickered and failed. The green glow in his ring grew faint. For a moment, the darkness pressed in close.

And then Hal thought about the billions of people in the systems around him. He thought about Earth. He thought about the faces of those he loved. And something deep inside him caught fire.

The ring blazed. Not with the steady glow of a charged battery, but with a wild, searing light that came from Hal himself — a great surge of everything he was and everything he refused to give up. He reached out with that light and built something he had never built before: an emerald sun. It rose between him and the nebula, blazing with warmth and willpower, pushing back the crushing dark until the star system steadied and the threat retreated.

The Guardians on Oa saw what had happened, and it made them uneasy. Power that came entirely from a single person was unpredictable. Uncontrollable. But they also knew the truth: in the greatest crises, the moments when all hope seemed lost, this was what turned the tide.

Do you know what kept Hal from losing himself in all that light? His memories. The people he loved. His ordinary, human life back on Earth.

And that is how Hal Jordan learned that the most powerful force in the universe was never stored in a battery at all — it was always living inside him.
