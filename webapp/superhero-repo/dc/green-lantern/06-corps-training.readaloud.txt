Becoming a Green Lantern is not as simple as slipping on a ring. First, you have to earn it — and that means training on Oa.

Oa is the home of the Green Lantern Corps: a planet-sized city at the center of the universe, where thousands of alien heroes come from every corner of the galaxy to learn their craft. Hal Jordan arrived as a cocky test pilot from Earth, full of confidence. The other Lanterns had a name for new recruits. They called them poozers. And a poozer's job was to learn how much they still didn't know.

The training was hard. The arenas used real gravity — heavy, pressing, punishing. Simulations could conjure something as violent as an exploding sun. And over all of it, booming like a thunderclap, was the voice of Hal's drill instructor: Kilowog of Bolovax Vik. Kilowog was enormous, relentless, and the finest teacher in the Corps. "You call that a construct, Jordan?" he would roar, looming over Hal's trembling effort. But every insult was a lesson, and every hard lesson made Hal better.

Hal also studied in the Great Library of Oa — reading the ancient wars against the Manhunters, learning the laws of the Guardians, understanding the emotional spectrum that fueled the different rings across the galaxy. And then came the hardest part: facing his own fears in psychic simulations the Guardians designed to break recruits down to their truest selves. The Green Lantern Oath — "In brightest day, in blackest night" — became not just words, but a lifeline.

And that is how, on graduation day before the Central Power Battery, Hal Jordan stopped being a poozer and became, truly and completely, a Green Lantern.
