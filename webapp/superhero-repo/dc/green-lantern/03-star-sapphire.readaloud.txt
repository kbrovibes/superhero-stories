The universe runs on many kinds of energy. The Green Lantern Corps harnesses willpower — the determination to never give up. But there is another force in the cosmos, one that the ancient guardians of the emotional spectrum considered among the most powerful of all: love.

The Star Sapphires guard that force. They wield beautiful violet gemstones that glow with a light the color of a twilight sky, and their power flows from the deepest feelings of the heart. Among them is a woman named Carol Ferris — one of Hal Jordan's closest friends right here on Earth.

When Carol becomes Star Sapphire, a stunning violet suit appears around her, and she can shape constructs of purple energy — shields that shimmer, beams of light that fly with perfect precision, and graceful bridges that arc across impossible distances. Her power is extraordinary. But the gemstone, filled as it is with the force of love, can sometimes act on its own — protecting the people Carol cares about in ways she didn't quite plan. And do you know what that sometimes leads to? A rather unexpected and very colorful argument with a certain green-suited test pilot.

In those moments, Hal has to be patient and thoughtful — helping Carol find the balance between her own will and the gemstone's energy. And when they manage it together, green and violet light weaving side by side, the result is something truly beautiful and very difficult to stop.

And that is how Hal Jordan and Carol Ferris learned that even the most complicated friendships become something wonderful when two people choose to face the confusion together.
