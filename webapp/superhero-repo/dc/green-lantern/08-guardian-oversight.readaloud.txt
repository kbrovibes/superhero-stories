The Guardians of the Universe were the oldest beings Hal Jordan had ever stood before. Their skin was blue. Their eyes held the weight of billions of years. They sat in their great chamber on Oa and issued orders in calm, measured voices — and they expected those orders to be followed without question.

Hal Jordan was not always very good at that.

The Guardians valued logic and tradition above all things. To them, feelings were a source of chaos — unpredictable, unreliable, dangerous. They saw their Green Lanterns as agents, not individuals. And they made decisions based on what was best for the greatest number of beings in the universe, which sometimes meant accepting losses that Hal could not bring himself to accept.

One day, the Guardians ordered the evacuation of a star system that was in danger — but refused to send any extra help to save the animals and creatures living there, because they were not considered important enough. To the Guardians, it was a simple calculation. To Hal, it was wrong. He built a giant energy ark with his ring, loaded every creature he could find into it, and flew them to safety — directly against orders. The Guardians were furious. But they looked at what he had done, and they could not pretend it hadn't been right.

That was the kind of Green Lantern Hal was: the conscience of the Corps. He asked hard questions. He uncovered hidden histories. He spoke up when silence would have been easier. And slowly, over time, he became the bridge between the ancient wisdom of the Guardians and the warm, living hearts of the Lanterns they commanded.

And that is how Hal Jordan showed that even the oldest and wisest authorities need someone willing to say — quietly, respectfully, and firmly — "There is a better way."
