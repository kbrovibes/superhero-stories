The Guardians of the Universe are the oldest beings Hal Jordan has ever stood in front of. Their skin is blue. Their eyes hold the weight of BILLIONS of years of wisdom. They sit in their great chamber on Oa and speak in calm, measured voices, issuing orders they expect to be followed. Without question. Immediately. Every time.

And Hal Jordan is not always very good at that.

The Guardians believe feelings are chaotic and unpredictable. They make decisions based on mathematics — what helps the greatest number of beings across the universe, even if that means accepting some losses along the way. To them it is pure logic. To Hal, sometimes it feels very wrong.

One day the Guardians ordered a star system evacuated because it was in danger. But they refused to send any extra resources to rescue the animals and creatures living there — not enough lives to justify the effort, they calculated! It was a simple equation to them.

To Hal Jordan, it was not simple at all.

So Hal built a giant emerald ark with his ring — WHOOOSH! CLANK! — loaded every creature he could find into it, and flew them to safety. Directly. Against. Orders!

The Guardians were furious! But when they looked at what he had accomplished, they could not honestly say he had been wrong.

That is the kind of Green Lantern Hal was: the conscience of the Corps. He asked hard questions. He dug up hidden histories. He spoke up when staying quiet would have been much easier. And slowly, over time, he became the bridge between the ancient wisdom of the Guardians and the warm, beating hearts of the Lanterns they commanded.

Even the oldest and wisest authorities need someone willing to say — quietly, firmly, respectfully — "There is a better way."
