Every good student needs a great teacher. And for Hal Jordan, that teacher was the biggest, gruffest, most demanding alien in the Green Lantern Corps: Kilowog of Bolovax Vik.

Kilowog was famous on Oa. Not just for his size — which was considerable — but for his wisdom. He had trained more Green Lanterns than anyone could count, and he knew how to find the spark in a person even when they were covered in the dust of their latest failure. With Hal, it took patience. And volume. And a very direct approach.

"You're trying to outmuscle them, Jordan," Kilowog rumbled one day during a training session, when Hal was building construct after construct trying to hold back a swarm of simulated enemies. "Don't build a wall when you can build a funnel. Use your head." Hal thought about it — and realized Kilowog was right. From that day on, he stopped trying to overpower problems and started trying to outwit them.

Their bond was truly tested on a real mission, when a sun on the edge of the known universe was about to explode. Hundreds of thousands of lives were in danger. Hal and Kilowog built a great emerald shield together, holding back the solar flares while the evacuation rushed forward. When the worst flare came, Kilowog threw himself into the line of fire. Hal didn't think — he just poured his energy into Kilowog's, and together they held. The planet was saved.

Afterward, Kilowog sat quietly and told Hal about Bolovax Vik, his home world, and the people he had lost. "You've got the spark," was all he said. And for Hal, that was everything.

And that is how a poozer and his drill sergeant became, through fire and honesty and a little impulsiveness, the best of friends.
