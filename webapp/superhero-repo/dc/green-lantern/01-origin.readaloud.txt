There are test pilots, and then there is Hal Jordan. Other pilots were careful and cautious. Hal was the one who pushed further, flew faster, and stayed calm when the instruments screamed and the engine rattled and anyone sensible would have turned back. He worked at a place called Ferris Aircraft, and he was known for two things: his skill in the cockpit and his absolute refusal to be afraid.

One day, near the edge of the desert, a spaceship fell from the sky.

Inside was a creature named Abin Sur — a member of the Green Lantern Corps, an interstellar police force that protected the entire universe. Abin Sur was gravely hurt, and he needed to pass on his most important possession before he was gone: his Power Ring. The ring didn't wait to be told who to choose. It searched the area for someone brave and honest, and it flew straight to Hal Jordan.

When Hal slipped on the ring and spoke the Green Lantern Oath for the first time, something extraordinary happened. The ring glowed. A bright green suit appeared around him. And a connection opened up — between Hal and the most powerful force in the universe: his own willpower.

He traveled to a world called Oa, the home of the Green Lantern Corps, where he met thousands of alien heroes from every corner of the galaxy. They taught him to make "constructs" — solid objects built from pure green energy, shaped entirely by his imagination. Being a pilot, Hal's first constructs looked like planes and shields and great sweeping wings of light.

And that is how a test pilot from Earth became one of the universe's greatest protectors — proving that the most important instrument in any cockpit is courage.
