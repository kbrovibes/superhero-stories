Not every great teacher stays good forever. And the most gifted Green Lantern who ever lived — before Hal Jordan — was a man named Sinestro.

Sinestro was brilliant. He was disciplined. He was so skilled with his Power Ring that other Lanterns would stop to watch him train. He was also the one who first showed Hal how to fight and survive in the vastness of space. In those early days, Hal looked up to him. And then, slowly, Sinestro showed who he truly was.

Sinestro believed that the only way to keep order was through fear. Make people afraid enough, he reasoned, and they would never misbehave. The Guardians of the Universe — the ancient, wise founders of the Green Lantern Corps on Oa — told him that was wrong, and when he refused to stop, they removed him from the Corps entirely. But Sinestro was not finished.

He found a different source of power: a yellow ring, fueled not by willpower but by fear. He built his own army, the Sinestro Corps, and he became Hal Jordan's greatest enemy. When they fought, the sky between them blazed with green light and yellow light, willpower against terror, courage against control.

And do you know what gave Hal his edge? Every time Sinestro tried to fill Hal's mind with fear, Hal had to face it squarely and refuse to blink. The green ring grew stronger the more he stood firm. His courage was not the absence of fear — it was the decision to keep going anyway.

From that day on, their rivalry stretched across the stars, a great argument between two very different ideas of what it means to protect the universe.
