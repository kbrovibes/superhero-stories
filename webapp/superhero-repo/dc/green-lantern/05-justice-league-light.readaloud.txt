Every great team needs someone who can build the things the team needs in the very moment they need them. In the Justice League, that someone is Hal Jordan — Green Lantern.

When a mission calls for a crumbling building to be held upright while people escape, Hal is already building a frame of green energy, strong as girders, with a thought. When an enormous spaceship needs to be towed to safety, he wraps it in a great glowing construct and does exactly that. Batman comes up with the plan. Superman provides the raw strength. But Green Lantern provides the tools — shaped perfectly to the problem, built in seconds from pure imagination and willpower.

In places where it is dark and dangerous — deep underwater, or far out in the cold silence of space — Hal can light the way. His ring produces a warm green glow that reaches as far as the team needs to see. He can build floating platforms so his teammates have somewhere to stand. He can shape shields tailored to whoever needs them, sized and angled just right for the situation at hand.

Do you know what his teammates love about working with Hal? No matter how strange or impossible the problem looks, Hal never says "I can't build that." He tilts his head, squints a little, thinks for just a moment — and then the green light begins to flow. Sometimes it takes the shape of a staircase. Sometimes a dome. Once it was an entire bridge, wide enough for a crowd.

And that is how the Justice League learned that the brightest light on the team isn't always the one who hits hardest — sometimes it belongs to the one who builds what everyone else needs to shine.
