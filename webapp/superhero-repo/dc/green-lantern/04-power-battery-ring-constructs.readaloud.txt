Every hero has a tool that makes them who they are. For Hal Jordan, there are two: the Power Battery and the Power Ring that fits on his finger.

The Power Battery looks like a small green lantern — the old-fashioned kind with a handle and a glowing light inside. Once a day, Hal holds his ring against the battery and speaks the Green Lantern Oath out loud. The words fill the ring with green energy, the color of willpower, the color of hope. When the Oath is complete, the ring glows steady and strong, ready for whatever the universe needs next.

With a charged ring, Hal can build anything he can picture in his mind. He calls them constructs — solid objects made of pure green energy, as hard and real as steel for as long as he believes in them. Being a test pilot, Hal loves building things that fly. He makes sleek green airplanes that carry him faster than any jet. He builds giant wings to glide through a storm. He creates parachutes for falling people and enormous green boxing gloves for a villain that needs a proper nudge. He has built a dam out of green energy to hold back a flood, and a towering hand to catch a crumbling building.

Do you know the secret to making a truly powerful construct? You have to believe in it completely. The moment a doubt creeps in — the moment the mind wanders — the construct flickers and fades. That is why Green Lanterns spend so much time training their thoughts to stay calm and clear even in the middle of the most frightening moments.

And that is how the Power Battery and the ring became the most creative tools in the universe — limited only by the size of one pilot's imagination.
