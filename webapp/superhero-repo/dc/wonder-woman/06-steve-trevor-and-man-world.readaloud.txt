Imagine growing up your whole life on a paradise island where you've never seen the outside world — and then one day a PLANE falls out of the sky right into your ocean. SPLASH! That's exactly what happened to Diana. And the pilot she pulled out of the water would change her life forever.

His name was Steve Trevor — an American military pilot, brave and kind and completely bewildered to find himself surrounded by Amazon warriors on a magical hidden island. He was the first man Diana had ever met. She looked at him curiously. He looked at her with wide eyes. And when he started telling her about the world beyond Themyscira's mystical veil — cities and countries and conflicts and ordinary people just trying to get by — Diana felt something she had never felt before. A PULL. A need to go and help.

Leaving her home to return Steve to his world was Diana's first step into what she calls Man's World. And it was not simple! The world outside was noisy and complicated and sometimes very unfair. Diana saw things that troubled her — people treated unequally, arguments that turned into cruelty, problems that seemed so easy to fix if people would only choose kindness. She was frustrated sometimes. Sad sometimes. Confused by why so many things were so difficult.

But Steve was always there — patient, honest, sometimes grinning at her puzzled face. He helped her understand that even a flawed and messy world is full of remarkable goodness. He showed her the beauty in ordinary human moments: a family sharing a meal, a stranger helping someone who dropped their groceries, a child learning something new. "See?" he'd say. "That's why it matters."

Together they became partners — in military intelligence, in humanitarian work, in the daily effort to make things a little better. Diana became Diana Prince to the world. And Steve Trevor became the first and greatest teacher Wonder Woman ever had: the man who showed her the world was worth saving.
