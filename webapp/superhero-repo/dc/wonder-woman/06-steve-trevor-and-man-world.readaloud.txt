The day a plane came spinning out of the sky and splashed into the waters near Themyscira, everything in Diana's life changed. She swam out through the waves and pulled the pilot to safety. His name was Steve Trevor — an American, brave and kind and thoroughly bewildered to find himself on a hidden island of warrior women.

Steve was the first man Diana had ever met. And as he told her about the world beyond the island's mystical veil — a world of bustling cities, of wars, of ordinary people trying their hardest to be good — Diana felt something she had not felt before: the pull to go and help.

Leaving Themyscira to return Steve to his home was Diana's first step into Man's World. And it was not easy. The world outside was loud and complicated and sometimes very unjust. There were things Diana saw that troubled her deeply — people treated unequally, conflicts that seemed to make no sense. But Steve was always there, patient and honest, helping her understand that even in a flawed world, people carried remarkable sparks of goodness.

Together, Diana and Steve worked — in military intelligence, in humanitarian efforts — always focused on keeping the world a little safer, a little kinder. She became Diana Prince, and he became her most trusted friend.

And do you know what Diana learned from watching Steve, and from the short, precious time they had together? That human beings are fragile and mortal and wonderful. And that protecting them — truly protecting them — means understanding and loving them, not just fighting for them.

And that is how Steve Trevor became the first great teacher of Wonder Woman: the man who showed her why the world was worth saving.
