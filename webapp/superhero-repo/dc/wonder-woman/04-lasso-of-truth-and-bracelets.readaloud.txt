When the gods of Olympus gave Wonder Woman her gifts, they chose very carefully. They did not give her a sword of destruction or a shield built only for herself. They gave her a golden rope and a pair of silver bracelets. And in those two things is all the wisdom of Themyscira.

The Lasso of Truth — also called the Golden Perfect — glows like sunlight and never breaks, for it was forged from the Girdle of Gaea herself. When Diana loops it around someone, something remarkable happens. Whatever lies they were carrying, whatever stories they were telling themselves — all of it falls away, and only the truth remains. The lasso can break illusions and heal troubled minds. Diana uses it not to conquer, but to clear away the fog so that people can finally see clearly.

The Bracelets of Submission are just as remarkable. Forged from the remnants of Zeus's own Aegis, they are virtually indestructible. Diana can move her arms so fast that bullets bounce away harmlessly. Energy blasts meant to level buildings scatter like sparks from a campfire. And when Diana crosses her bracelets together — when she brings them together with purpose and strength — a shockwave of pure divine energy rolls outward, sending even the mightiest foes stumbling back.

And do you know what is most wonderful about these two gifts? They are not separate from Diana. They grow stronger as she does. When her heart is clear and her purpose is true, they become more powerful still.

From that day on, the golden lasso and the silver bracelets have reminded the world that the most formidable heroes are not the ones who destroy — they are the ones who seek the truth and choose to protect.
