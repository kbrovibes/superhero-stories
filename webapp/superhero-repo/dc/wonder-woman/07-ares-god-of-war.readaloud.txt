Long before cities were built, before even the first roads were made, there was a god whose name made all the world shiver: Ares, the God of War. He is the son of Zeus himself, ancient and powerful and hungry for conflict the way a fire is hungry for wood. Wherever people quarrel and nations turn on each other, Ares is somewhere nearby — not because he forces the fighting, but because he whispers the right fears into the right ears, and lets human nature do the rest.

He despises the Amazons. He despises Wonder Woman most of all. Diana stands for peace, for truth, for the belief that people can choose something better. To Ares, that is an insult. He wants to prove, more than anything, that war is simply what the world is — that Diana's hope is foolish, and that the fire of conflict can never truly be put out.

When they clash, the battle is extraordinary. Ares can conjure weapons from thin air and send waves of despair into the hearts of those nearby. He is nearly invulnerable. He can call armies from the shadows. He is as old and as fierce as anything Wonder Woman has ever faced.

But Diana does not fight him just with her golden lasso and her silver bracelets. She fights him with something he has never understood: love. Every time she looks at people who are frightened and lost and still choosing kindness, she grows stronger. Every act of peace in the world weakens him. And that is the truth Ares cannot bear.

And that is how Wonder Woman battles a god of war — not with rage, but with an unbreakable belief that peace is always worth fighting for.
