Every superhero has a greatest enemy. And Wonder Woman's greatest enemy is not a person with a gadget or a stolen power. He is a GOD. Ancient, enormous, and as old as human conflict itself. His name is Ares — and he is the God of War!

Ares is the son of Zeus. He has existed since the very first battle ever fought. He is nearly invulnerable — swords bounce off him, energy blasts scatter around him. He can summon weapons from thin air and conjure armies from the shadows. He can project waves of pure despair into the hearts of people nearby, making them feel hopeless and angry. And he doesn't force people to fight! That's what makes him so sneaky. He just finds the fears already inside people — and whispers to them. Quietly. Patiently. And then lets human nature do the rest.

CRASH! Ares HATES Wonder Woman. And do you know why? Because Diana stands for peace. Every time Diana helps two people who were fighting to understand each other, every time she prevents a conflict or heals a wound — she is directly working against Ares. She is his opposite in every single way. And her mission to make the world kinder? To Ares, that is a personal insult.

He wants to prove, more than anything else, that war is simply what the world IS. That Diana's hope for peace is foolish and naive. That the fire of conflict can never truly be put out.

But here's what Ares never figured out. Diana doesn't fight him just with her lasso and her bracelets. She fights him with LOVE. Every single act of kindness and peace in the world — anywhere on Earth — actually weakens Ares. Every person who chooses cooperation over conflict chips away at his power. And Diana? She fills the world with those moments every day.

That is the truth Ares cannot stand: love is stronger than war. And Wonder Woman proves it every time she shows up.
