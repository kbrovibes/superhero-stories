The Justice League has the strongest person alive. The fastest person alive. The cleverest detective alive. But you know what a team of powerful heroes needs more than anything else? A heart. And that heart belongs to Wonder Woman.

Diana is a founding member of the Justice League — one of the very first heroes to join! And from the very first day, she brought something to the team that no one else could: she made them care about WHY they fight, not just HOW.

At the center of the Justice League stands what people call the Trinity — Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman. They are the three most important. Batman is careful and analytical, always thinking three steps ahead. Superman is hopeful and fearless, always believing the best in everyone. And Diana? She is the wisdom between them. When Batman's plans feel too cold, Diana adds warmth. When Superman's optimism needs grounding, Diana brings steadiness. Together the three of them balance each other perfectly — like three legs of a very strong chair.

Here's something that Diana does that no one else does. After a battle, when the immediate danger is over, Diana stays. She sits with people who are frightened. She listens. She looks into the eyes of someone who has lost everything and she does not look away. Her teammates watch this and they carry it home with them. She makes them better.

Before any big team decision, Diana asks the question everyone else forgets: "What happens to the people after this is over?" Because winning a fight isn't enough if the people caught in the middle are left behind.

Her message to the Justice League — delivered quietly, by example, every single day — is this: power is not a prize. It is a responsibility. And it must always, always be carried with LOVE. Can you imagine teaching that to Superman and Batman? Diana does. And they listen. Because she's right.
