The Justice League has heroes who can fly, heroes who can lift mountains, and heroes who can solve any puzzle in the world. But the one thing the League needs most of all — more than strength or speed or strategy — is a heart. And that heart belongs to Wonder Woman.

Diana does not just fight to defeat enemies. She fights to protect the worth of every single person caught in the middle. When the team faces a choice about what to do, it is Diana's voice that asks the question no one else remembered to ask: what happens to the people after the battle is over?

At the very center of the Justice League stands what people call the Trinity — Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman. Batman is careful and precise, always thinking three steps ahead. Superman is hopeful and fearless, always believing the best of people. And Diana? Diana is the wisdom between them. Her compassion softens Batman's hard edges. Her steadiness grounds Superman's soaring idealism. Together, the three of them are something extraordinary.

Diana is always the first one to arrive when someone is in need. She listens. She sits with people who are grieving. She looks into the eyes of those who have lost everything and does not look away. Her teammates watch her do this, and they carry it with them into their own lives.

And do you know what Diana teaches the Justice League, again and again? That power is not a prize. It is a responsibility. And it must always, always be carried with love.

From that day on, the Justice League was not just a team of powerful heroes. It was a team that understood why it existed — because Wonder Woman never let them forget.
