In the ancient myths of Greece there are stories of a sorceress named Circe — a woman so powerful she could transform people into animals with barely a gesture, a daughter of the Titans who had lived for longer than most people can imagine. Those stories are true. Circe is very real, and she has never stopped scheming.

From her mystical island of Aeaea she watches the modern world, looking for ways to plunge it into chaos. She despises the Amazons and everything they stand for. She despises Wonder Woman most of all. Diana represents order, truth, and peace — and Circe has been at war with all three things for thousands of years.

Her magic is astonishing and terrible. She can make you see things that are not there. She can summon great beasts from ancient mythology. She can reach into the minds of world leaders and whisper the wrong ideas — steering whole countries toward conflict. She is patient and clever and very, very dangerous.

But Wonder Woman has something that Circe's magic cannot touch: a clear mind and a golden rope. The Lasso of Truth — Diana's shimmering golden lasso — cuts right through illusions the way sunlight burns away fog. And Diana's own training on Themyscira has given her a discipline of spirit that resists the pull of dark enchantments.

And do you know what Diana feels when she faces Circe, even after so many battles? Sadness. Because all that extraordinary power, all those centuries of knowledge — and it has been used for nothing but darkness.

From that day on, Diana has always known: the fight against Circe is not just a battle against one sorceress. It is a battle for the very idea that light is stronger than shadow.
