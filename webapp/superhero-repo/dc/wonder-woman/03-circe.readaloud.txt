Do you know what's scarier than a villain who just appeared last week? A villain who has been plotting and scheming for THOUSANDS of years. That's Circe. And today, Wonder Woman has to face her!

Circe is an ancient sorceress — a daughter of the Titans themselves — who has lived longer than most kingdoms have existed. She makes her home on a mystical island called Aeaea, where she has been perfecting her dark magic for centuries. And her most impressive — and most terrifying — power? She can turn people into animals. POOF! A warrior becomes a frog. ZAP! A general becomes a pig. Just like that. She finds it rather funny.

Why does Circe hate Wonder Woman so much? Because Diana represents everything Circe despises: truth, order, peace. Diana's mission — to make the world kinder and more honest — is Circe's worst nightmare! So Circe schemes constantly, weaving modern technology with ancient mystical tricks. She creates terrifying illusions. She summons mythological beasts from old Greek legends. She slips into the dreams of world leaders and whispers the wrong ideas into their sleeping minds. Sneaky? Extremely. Patient? For thousands of years!

But here's what Circe can never understand — and it drives her absolutely mad! When Diana faces illusions, she doesn't get tricked. Her Lasso of Truth — the golden, unbreakable rope forged from divine power — slices right through Circe's deceptions like sunlight burns away fog. SHINK! Illusion shattered. And Diana's Amazonian training gave her a strength of spirit that resists dark enchantments from the inside out.

When Diana looks at Circe, even after all their battles, she feels something unexpected: sadness. Because all that knowledge, all those centuries of magical power — and it's all been used for nothing but darkness.

Wonder Woman's fight against Circe is never just one battle. It's a thousand-year argument about whether light or shadow wins in the end. And Diana has never once doubted which side is right.
