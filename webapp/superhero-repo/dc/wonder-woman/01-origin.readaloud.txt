On a hidden island called Themyscira — sometimes called Paradise Island — where the sea sparkles and white marble temples gleam in the sun, there lived a people called the Amazons. They were warrior women of great wisdom and strength, and their queen was Hippolyta. For a long time, Queen Hippolyta wished with all her heart for a child. And the gods of Olympus heard her.

They breathed life into a figure of clay, and Diana was born — a princess, a daughter of Zeus himself, filled from the very first moment with gifts of strength, wisdom, and boundless courage. She grew up running on Themyscira's warm beaches, learning to fight, to think deeply, to heal the sick, and to seek truth above all things. She was trained by the greatest warriors the Amazons had ever known.

Then one day, a plane fell from the sky and crashed into the waters near the island. Inside was a man — an American pilot named Steve Trevor, the first man Diana had ever seen. She helped him, listened to his stories of a world beyond the island's veil, and felt something stir deep inside her. The world outside needed help.

Diana entered a great tournament to determine which Amazon would become the champion. Her mother, Queen Hippolyta, was worried. But Diana fought with everything she had — and she won. She was given the Lasso of Truth and the Bracelets of Submission and a new name: Wonder Woman.

And do you know what she did next? She left the only home she had ever known to go and help a world full of strangers, because she believed every person was worth protecting.

And that is how Wonder Woman began — not with a wish for glory, but with a heart too full of love to stay home.
