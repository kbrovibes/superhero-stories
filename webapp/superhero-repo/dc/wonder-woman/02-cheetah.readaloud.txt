There was once a very brilliant woman named Barbara Ann Minerva, who loved nothing more than exploring the world and uncovering its ancient secrets. She was a remarkable archaeologist — clever, bold, and full of curiosity. But Barbara wanted more than knowledge. She wanted power. And that wanting led her somewhere very dark.

She found an old ritual in a faraway land, one that was said to give the power of a cheetah goddess. But the ritual required a pure heart, and Barbara's hunger for power had already twisted hers. The transformation went wrong. And when it was over, Barbara had become the Cheetah — fast as a lightning strike, strong as iron, with claws that could cut through nearly anything. She was trapped in a cycle of rage she could not escape.

Now she and Wonder Woman are locked in the most personal rivalry you can imagine. Cheetah looks at Diana and sees everything she envies: divine gifts, grace, love from the world. She fights with a savage, blazing fury. Their battles take them through dense jungles and crumbling ancient ruins, the wild places of the earth that seem to mirror the struggle inside them both.

But do you know what Diana sees when she looks at Cheetah? She sees Barbara. The scholar. Her friend. The woman who got lost. Diana does not fight Cheetah with hatred — she fights with the stubborn, patient hope that somewhere inside the claws and the fury, her friend is still there.

And that is how Wonder Woman's most painful battle is also her most important one — not because she is trying to win, but because she has never, ever given up on the person she is fighting for.
