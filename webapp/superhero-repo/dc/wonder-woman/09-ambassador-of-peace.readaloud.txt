You might expect Wonder Woman's most important battles to be fought with her golden lasso or her silver bracelets. And sometimes they are. But some of her most important work happens in very different rooms — great halls where world leaders sit across tables from each other, trying to decide the future of millions of people.

Diana serves as the Ambassador of Peace — for Themyscira and for the United Nations. She travels to conflict zones where people are suffering, and she sits with them. She speaks at international forums where the biggest decisions are made, and she asks the questions that cut through the noise: What do the people need? What will truly help them? How do we build something lasting?

It is not easy work. The world of politics can be slow and stubborn. Sometimes the powerful seem more interested in their own advantages than in the welfare of those who have nothing. But Diana came from Themyscira — a place that proved a better kind of society was possible — and she is not discouraged by stubbornness. She is patient and clear and absolutely unwilling to give up.

Her work extends even beyond the human world. She speaks with mystical forces. She meets with representatives of other civilizations among the stars. She is the bridge between worlds that might otherwise misunderstand each other and stumble into conflict.

And do you know what her deepest wish is? That one day, ordinary people everywhere will become their own ambassadors of peace — in their homes, their schools, their streets.

From that day on, Diana has carried that hope with her wherever she goes, proof that the most enduring victories are not won in battles, but in the patient, quiet work of building a world worth protecting.
