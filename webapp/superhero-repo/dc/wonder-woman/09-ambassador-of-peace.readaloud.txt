You might expect Wonder Woman's most important battles to happen in jungles and ancient ruins with her lasso and bracelets blazing. And yes, sometimes they do! But some of her most important work happens in very different places — enormous meeting rooms where world leaders sit across long tables from each other, deciding the futures of millions of people. And there, too, Diana shows up.

Diana serves as the Ambassador of Peace for Themyscira at the United Nations — one of the most important jobs in the entire world. She travels to places where people are suffering and just... sits with them. She speaks at big international forums where the fanciest and most powerful politicians argue — and she asks the questions that cut right through all the noise: What do the people actually need? What will truly help them? How do we build something that lasts?

Isn't that incredible? The same person who can deflect bullets with her wrists is sitting in a conference room asking about healthcare and schools and whether children have clean water. Because Wonder Woman understands something that takes most people a very long time to learn: you can't win someone's world by fighting for it. You have to build it with them.

It is not always easy. Politics can be slow and stubborn. Some powerful people are more interested in their own advantages than in helping those who have nothing. But Diana came from Themyscira — a place that already proved a better society is possible — and she is not discouraged by stubbornness. She has fought a GOD. Some difficult politicians are no match for an Amazon princess!

Her work goes even further — she speaks to mystical forces, meets with representatives of civilizations from across the stars, and acts as a bridge between worlds that might otherwise stumble into conflict.

And her deepest wish? That ordinary people everywhere will become their own ambassadors for peace — at home, at school, in every choice they make. Peace isn't the end of fighting. It's the beginning of building.
