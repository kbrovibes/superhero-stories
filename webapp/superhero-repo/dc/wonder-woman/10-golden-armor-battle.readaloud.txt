There are days when Wonder Woman's silver bracelets and golden lasso are exactly enough for the challenge ahead. And then there are the OTHER days — the days when the threat is so enormous and so powerful that the whole world seems to hold its breath. On those days, Diana reaches for something different. Something ancient. Something golden.

SHHHHHIIINK — the Golden Armor!

It was forged by the gods of Olympus — not just hammered from metal, but infused with divine power and the courage of every Amazon champion who ever lived! It gleams like a thousand suns. And rising from its back — enormous golden wings, breathtaking and fierce, that carry Diana through the sky faster than any eagle has ever flown! WHOOOOSH! In battle, those wings can fold around her like the walls of a fortress, forming a nearly impenetrable shield against attacks that would crack a mountain in half. The helmet sharpens her senses in the chaos of battle. And the armor amplifies her strength — her blows land with the impact of a thunderbolt from Zeus himself. BOOM!

But here's the most important thing about the Golden Armor. It is not just about power. It carries a story.

Long, long ago, an Amazon warrior named Asteria wore this very armor. She stood alone — completely alone — against an overwhelming enemy force so that all her sisters could escape and make it to safety. She gave everything she had so that Themyscira could survive. So that the Amazons could live on. So that Diana could one day be born and become Wonder Woman.

When Diana puts on the Golden Armor, she is wearing Asteria's courage. And the courage of every Amazon who came before. She is wearing generations of love.

That means when Diana enters the most dangerous battle of her life in the Golden Armor, she is not alone. She is carrying every hero who ever chose to stand when it would have been easier to run. And that is something nothing can defeat.
