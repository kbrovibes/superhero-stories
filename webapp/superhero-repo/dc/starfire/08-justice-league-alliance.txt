The alert came from the Watchtower, the Justice League's orbital station, on a Saturday morning. A Gordanian fleet — not one warship this time but nine, traveling in formation — had entered the solar system and was broadcasting a demand: Earth would surrender all extraterrestrial individuals living on the planet or face a demonstration of Gordanian firepower. The Justice League, dealing simultaneously with a tectonic event off the coast of Japan, reached out to the Teen Titans for backup. Superman and Wonder Woman would handle the underwater crisis. The Titans would handle the fleet.

It was the largest operation Koriand'r had ever been part of. The Justice League's Martian Manhunter coordinated strategy from the Watchtower. Green Lantern — Hal Jordan, wielding the most powerful ring in the universe — would serve as the primary diplomatic contact, attempting to negotiate while the Titans held position. The plan required someone to board the Gordanian flagship while Green Lantern was talking: someone who knew Gordanian protocols, who understood how their command structure worked, who could speak to their admiral directly.

Robin looked at Starfire. She had already anticipated the question.

She flew to the flagship at speeds that made Green Lantern raise an eyebrow, breached its entry bay with a controlled starbolt, and navigated the corridors from memory. The Gordanian admiral was a scarred veteran named Rek-Var who had fought Tamaraneans before and wore that history in the lines of his face. He did not expect to see a Tamaranean princess walking into his command deck unescorted, hands lit with quiet green fire, eyes calm.

She spoke to him in Gordanian. She told him she understood he was following orders from commanders who had promised him easy victories. She told him that the people of Earth had a habit of surprising those who underestimated them. She told him that she was asking him, directly, to stand down — and that she was asking once, because she respected what he had endured in a long career, even if she disagreed with every order he had ever carried out for the Citadel's empire.

Rek-Var looked at her for a long moment. He said: "You should have been captured when they had you."

She said: "I know."

He signaled his fleet to hold. Green Lantern completed the negotiation, and nine Gordanian ships turned and left the solar system in good order.

Afterward in the Watchtower, Martian Manhunter told Robin that Koriand'r's intervention had been the decisive moment — without direct Gordanian dialogue, the fleet might not have held. Robin nodded and said nothing. He already knew. He had watched her walk out of that flagship the way she walked out of every impossible situation — upright, unhurried, and still warm.
