Nine. NINE Gordanian warships. A whole fleet, traveling in formation, heading toward Earth and demanding that all extraterrestrial residents be handed over immediately.

That is the situation the Teen Titans woke up to on a Saturday morning. The Justice League itself was on the line — the Watchtower, the orbital station, with Martian Manhunter coordinating from above. Superman and Wonder Woman were already needed at a tectonic emergency off Japan. Green Lantern — Hal Jordan, with the most powerful ring in the universe — was going to try to negotiate with the fleet.

The Titans would hold position and back him up.

And they needed someone to do something extremely brave.

The plan was to board the Gordanian flagship while Green Lantern was talking — to get inside and speak directly to their admiral. Someone who knew the Gordanian language. Someone who understood how their command structure worked. Someone who had, unfortunately, spent years aboard their ships and knew exactly how they operated.

Robin looked at Starfire. She had already figured this out thirty seconds ago.

She flew to the flagship at speeds that made even Green Lantern look surprised — and he has seen a lot. She fired one controlled starbolt at the entry bay. BOOM. In she went. Down the corridors, from memory, through the gray metal halls that smelled like old engine fuel and cold space.

The Gordanian admiral's name was Rek-Var. He was scarred and experienced and he had fought Tamaraneans before. He definitely did not expect a Tamaranean princess to walk onto his command deck, alone, hands quietly glowing green, looking completely calm.

She spoke to him in Gordanian. She told him she knew he was following orders from commanders who had promised easy victories. She told him the people of Earth had a habit of surprising everyone who underestimated them. And then she said:

"I am asking you, directly and once, to stand down. I am asking once because I respect what you have endured in a long career — even though I disagree with every single order you have ever followed."

Rek-Var stared at her. He said: "You should have been captured when they had you."

She said, quietly: "I know."

A long silence. Then Rek-Var signaled his fleet to hold.

Green Lantern completed the negotiation in record time. Nine warships turned around and flew away from Earth. The solar system was safe.

In the Watchtower afterward, Martian Manhunter — who is not someone who gives compliments casually — told Robin that Starfire's moment on that flagship had been the one that mattered most.

She had talked a fleet into leaving. With honesty, with respect, and with green fire quietly glowing in her hands just in case.

THAT is a superhero.
