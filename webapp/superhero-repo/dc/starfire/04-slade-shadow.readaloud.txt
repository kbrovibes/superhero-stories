Some villains stomp and shout. Some blow things up just to cause chaos. But the scariest villain the Teen Titans ever faced was very, very quiet.

His name was Slade. He wore a mask — half orange, half black — that covered one whole side of his face. He spoke in a calm, measured voice, like someone who has never in his life been surprised by anything. And the thing that made him truly frightening? He studied. He watched. He planned for months before he ever made a single move.

Slade had spent a long time studying Starfire.

He had figured out something important: her powers run on her heart. Her joy, her love for her friends, her warmth — those feelings make her starbolts stronger, her flight faster, her whole self more powerful. So Slade thought: if I can take those feelings away — if I can make her afraid and alone — she will be much easier to defeat.

He set a trap.

A warehouse near Jump City's waterfront appeared to be collapsing, with people inside. The Titans rushed to help — of course they did, that is what heroes do. But inside, Slade's plan snapped shut. EMP bursts scrambled Cyborg's systems. Walls and corridors shifted, separating Starfire from the others. She was alone.

And then one of Slade's combat robots stepped out of the shadows.

This robot had been built SPECIFICALLY to fight her. It was fast and armored and equipped with special light-dampening shields that made her starbolts fizz and scatter like sparks in the rain. She fired — WHOMP — and the energy dispersed harmlessly. She flew — it tracked her perfectly. She hit the wall. Twice.

Slade was watching on a monitor somewhere, very satisfied.

But here is what he had gotten wrong about Starfire: doubt doesn't freeze her. It makes her think HARDER.

She stopped trying to blast through the shields. Instead she looked at the robot's arm pulling back for a swing — and she CAUGHT it. She used its own momentum and spun it directly into a structural pillar. CRUNCH. The robot crumpled. Done.

Slade had studied her starbolts. He had forgotten to study her Tamaranean strength. And he had completely forgotten to study what her heart does when it is tested.

It keeps going. It always keeps going.

The Titans rescued everyone in the warehouse and regrouped outside. Slade slipped away — he always does. But Cyborg recovered a fragment of the robot and spent three days decoding Slade's tactics from it.

Next time, the Titans would be ready.

And Starfire had learned something that night: her greatest power was never the fire in her hands. It was the fire that refuses to go out.
