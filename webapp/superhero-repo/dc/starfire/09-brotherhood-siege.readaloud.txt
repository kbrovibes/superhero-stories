The most dangerous kind of villain is not the one who is the strongest. It is the one who plans.

The Brain was a criminal genius — and not the kind in a dramatic cape and a loud voice. The Brain was a cold, careful, brilliant mind preserved in a robotic chassis, and he had spent months studying every young hero team on Earth. He knew their locations, their weaknesses, their routines. And then he launched his plan: attack every single Titan team at the exact same moment, so no one could come help anyone else.

Twelve locations. Simultaneously. The Brotherhood of Evil's most ambitious operation ever.

Starfire was in Tokyo when it happened. She was helping a Japanese hero team investigate strange Gordanian probe activity near the Pacific coast when EVERY emergency channel lit up at once. Jump City under attack. Titans East — hit. Titans across four continents — hit.

She turned toward home and flew.

At full speed, in the high stratosphere where nothing was in her way, she blazed across the Pacific Ocean like a green comet. The ocean below was dark and vast. She was faster.

She came over Jump City Bay at full charge and saw the situation instantly: three Brotherhood villains attacking Titans Tower at the same time. Beast Boy was in the courtyard below, going animal by animal against a massive villain called Monsieur Mallah. Raven was holding the building's perimeter with her dark energy, spread thin. Cyborg was at communications, tracking everyone.

Starfire came in LOW and FAST over the bay and dropped two starbolts into the courtyard. BOOM. BOOM. Mallah's advance stopped immediately.

"Starfire!" Beast Boy shouted, and turned into a golden retriever for about two seconds from pure relief before remembering they were in a fight.

The battle at the Tower lasted ninety minutes. Three coordinated villains against the full team. It was hard and it was exhausting, but at the end of it the Titans were standing and the Brotherhood was not.

Then came the hard part: coordinating rescue for every team that had been hit. Robin and Cyborg worked the relay systems for hours, reaching Titan teams across four continents, tracking every villain, making sure everyone was accounted for.

By the next evening, every Titan was operational. Every Brotherhood villain had been neutralized.

Robin stood on the Tower roof. Starfire stood beside him, still faintly glowing.

"Good team," he said.

"The best," she said.

The Brain had mapped every Titan's ability and weakness. What he could never map was what it felt like to know these people were coming for you, no matter how far away you were.

That is not a weakness. That is the opposite of a weakness.

She flew across an ocean for her family. She always will.
