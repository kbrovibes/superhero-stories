Okay, let's talk about the most amazing, the most spectacular, the absolutely INCREDIBLE powers of Starfire — because they are unlike anything you have ever heard of!

Koriand'r is a Tamaranean. And Tamaraneans have a superpower that is honestly just really wonderful: they absorb sunlight. Not in a plants-and-photosynthesis way. In a THIS IS MY POWER SOURCE AND IT IS MAGNIFICENT way. Sunlight goes in, and blazing green energy comes out.

Those emerald blasts are called STARBOLTS. They crackle and glow and hit their target with enormous force. When Starfire fires a starbolt, it looks like a piece of the sun broke off and decided to go somewhere fast.

And guess what? Earth's yellow sun is actually a fantastic charging station for her. She is basically powered constantly, the whole time she is outside during the day. Which means she is usually at full power, which means villains of Jump City have a really tough job.

But that is not even the most interesting part!

Robin noticed something early on. When Starfire was happy — really happy, flying and laughing above the bay — her starbolts were BRIGHTER. Bigger. Stronger. Cyborg tested it properly, and the numbers confirmed it: when Koriand'r is feeling joy and love, her power doubles.

DOUBLES! That is not a small difference!

What this means — and this is so brilliant — is that every single time a bad guy tries to hurt her friends, they are accidentally making her MORE powerful. Threatening the people she loves? Huge mistake. She will blaze right at you with twice the fire.

She also flies. Oh, she flies. No wings, no engine, no jet pack — just pure solar force carrying her through the air. Jump City to New York? Under a minute. Up to the edge of space? She goes there for fun. In actual outer space she does not need to breathe at all — vacuum is like a comfortable swimming pool to her.

The most impressive thing, though — even more than the flying, even more than the starbolts — is how she uses all of it.

She never wastes a shot. She aims at the ENGINE, not the pilot. At the WEAPON, not the hand holding it. She is always thinking past the fight — thinking about the city that will still be standing after it is over, and the people who will walk those streets again.

That is what makes Starfire more than just powerful.

It is what makes her a HERO.

STARBOLT! Watch her go!
