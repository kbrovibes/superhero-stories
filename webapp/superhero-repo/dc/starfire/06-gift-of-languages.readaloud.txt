What would you do if someone arrived in your city from far away and you had no idea what they were saying?

Most people would feel stuck. Confused. Maybe a little bit scared.

But not Starfire. Because Starfire has one of the most extraordinary abilities you have ever heard of, and it has nothing to do with fire or flying.

She can absorb any language. From a single touch.

That is right! One handshake. One honest, friendly touch from someone who is genuinely trying to understand. And boom — the whole language pours into her mind. Every word. Every rule. The rhythm of how the sentences go. Even the way people who speak that language tend to THINK about things.

She learned English from Robin when she crashed in Jump City. One touch, less than a minute, and she was speaking complete sentences.

Since then she has absorbed FOURTEEN languages. That includes Interlac — the big trading language that hundreds of galactic civilizations use when they need to talk to each other. It includes Okaaran, which is spoken by a blue-skinned people from a distant star system. It includes Khund, which is a warrior language full of very short, sharp words. And it includes four more Earth languages, because Robin is not the only person she has wanted to understand.

One afternoon, two alien trading ships arrived in Jump City's harbor in the middle of an argument. Neither captain spoke any Earth language. Neither spoke the other's language either. Everyone stood around looking helpless.

Starfire walked up, touched a hand, waited a moment, touched another hand, waited again. Then she stood between the captains and spent forty-five minutes patiently translating both sides of the disagreement.

The ships left the harbor peacefully.

Robin once asked her what absorbing a language FEELS like.

She thought carefully — she always thinks carefully — and said it felt like a door opening into a room that was always there, always lit, just waiting for her to walk in.

Here is the most beautiful part, though.

In Tamaranean — her home language — "fighting" and "dancing" are the same word. They are the same motion with a purpose. She thinks that is very true.

And her favorite word in English? "Home." Because in Tamaranean the word means "the place where you are warmed." And she thinks they are, ultimately, exactly the same thing.

She values language so deeply because she once went years without one — years as a prisoner on cold ships where no one spoke anything she knew. Those years taught her that connection is the most precious thing there is.

Every word she speaks is a small act of love.

Isn't that something?
