Among all the gifts that Koriand'r carried from Tamaran to Earth, the one that surprised her new teammates most was not her starbolts or her flight. It was the ability she demonstrated one afternoon when a displaced family of Okaarans — a blue-skinned people from a far star system — arrived in Jump City's port with no one able to understand them. Starfire touched the hand of the eldest and spoke to her in fluent Okaaran within ten seconds, then translated everything for Robin and Cyborg without pausing.

The Tamaranean gift of language absorption worked through physical contact and genuine connection. A handshake would work. An embrace worked even better. What was required, beyond the touch itself, was sincerity — a real willingness to understand. Starfire had tested this carefully over her years on Earth. She had absorbed fourteen languages this way, including Interlac (the galactic trade language), Okaaran, Khund, and four human languages beyond the English she had first learned from Robin. Each absorption took less than a minute and left no trace of effort on her face — only a brief moment of stillness, as though she were listening to something very quiet.

What this gift meant in practice was that Starfire was often the most important person in the room when the Titans encountered beings from off-world, or when they worked alongside international heroes who had traveled to Jump City. She once spent an afternoon working through a disagreement between two rival alien trading ships whose captains could not find a common language, and resolved what had been escalating toward a fight in forty-five minutes of patient conversation.

Robin once asked her what it felt like, absorbing a language.

She thought about it carefully — she always thought carefully before answering questions about Tamaran — and said it felt like a door opening into a lit room that had always been there, waiting. The grammar arrived first, then the vocabulary, then the specific rhythm of how the people who spoke that language thought. That last part was the most interesting to her: every language carried a slightly different way of seeing the world inside it. In Tamaranean, there was no separate word for fighting and dancing — they were aspects of the same verb, motion with a purpose. In English, there was a word she found beautiful: "home." Tamaran's word was more like "the place where you are warmed." She thought they were the same thing, ultimately.

Cyborg once joked that she was the best diplomat the planet had ever produced. She had laughed and said she was simply someone who had been without language once, for years — and who had learned, exactly because of that, never to take a word of connection for granted.
