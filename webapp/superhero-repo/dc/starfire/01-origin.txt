Far across the galaxy, past rings of blue gas and fields of silver asteroids, there is a warm planet called Tamaran. It is a world lit by two suns, where orange-skinned Tamaraneans channel sunlight into blazing energy that sparks between their fingers. A princess was born there with auburn hair that floated like a flame and eyes full of kindness. Her name was Koriand'r, and she loved Tamaran's twin suns, its palace gardens, and its ancient stories of warriors who fought for peace.

But Koriand'r had an older sister named Komand'r — called Blackfire — who carried cold envy in her heart. Blackfire could not fly. Blackfire could not summon the starbolts that their people were famous for. When the Tamaranean throne was promised to Koriand'r instead of her, Blackfire made a terrible deal with the Citadel, a ruthless empire that had long threatened Tamaran. She handed her own sister over as a prisoner in exchange for a false promise of power. Koriand'r was taken from her home and held captive on cold metal starship decks for years.

She was not broken. The Citadel ran experiments on Koriand'r, intending to strip her powers away. Instead, her body absorbed the alien energy and amplified it a thousand times. When a guard next opened her cell door, brilliant emerald light exploded outward. She flew — really flew, rocketing through a starship hull into open space — for the very first time. She launched herself toward a distant blue-green planet called Earth.

She crashed in Jump City, California, scorching a crater in a parking lot. She stood up, shook the dust from her hair, and looked around with wondering eyes. She did not know what the humans around her were saying. But she had an extraordinary gift: one honest touch, and she could absorb any language instantly. The first person to reach out a hand was a young hero named Dick Grayson — Robin, the protégé of Batman. Koriand'r touched his hand, and English rushed into her mind like warm sunlight.

She told him her name. She told him she was free. Standing on the cracked pavement of Jump City, the princess of Tamaran made her choice: she would stay on this strange, wonderful planet and protect it. She became Starfire — and from that day forward, Earth had a champion who fought not from anger alone, but from love. Pure, blazing, undefeatable love for the world she had chosen as home.
