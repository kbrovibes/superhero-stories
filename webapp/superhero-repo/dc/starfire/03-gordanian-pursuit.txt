The Gordanians were not the sort of enemy who accepted defeat gracefully. This reptilian warrior race had served the Citadel empire for generations, hunting escaped prisoners across star systems. When they learned that Koriand'r — the Tamaranean princess they had once held captive — was living openly on a planet called Earth, General Yal-Zor dispatched a warship to retrieve her. As far as the Gordanians were concerned, she was still their property.

The warship appeared above Jump City on a Tuesday morning. Scout drones buzzed low over the harbor. General Yal-Zor broadcast a demand on every frequency: surrender Koriand'r within the hour, or the city would feel the power of Gordanian plasma cannons.

Starfire listened from Titans Tower and felt something she had not expected — not fear, but a clear, still anger. She had spent years being told she belonged to someone else. That was done. She turned to Robin, Cyborg, Beast Boy, and Raven and said quietly: "They have come for me. I will not make you fight my battle."

Robin's answer was immediate: "It stopped being just your battle the moment they aimed weapons at our city."

The Titans' plan took twenty minutes. Cyborg hacked the warship's communications and filled its internal channels with Earth radio static, breaking Yal-Zor's coordination with his drones. Beast Boy drew a drone toward the harbor as a bottlenose dolphin and became a rhinoceros just before it got close. Raven's telekinesis yanked its power cell loose. And Starfire flew straight up — blazing green against the sky — drawing Yal-Zor's personal fire so her teammates could move freely.

A plasma bolt clipped her shoulder. She spun, caught herself in midair, and launched a starbolt that struck the warship's main cannon dead center. It sparked and went dark.

Yal-Zor sent his ground forces into the streets. That was his mistake.

The fight in Jump City's docklands lasted forty minutes. When it ended, Yal-Zor stood surrounded — Robin's line wrapped around his weapon arm, Cyborg's sonic cannon humming, Starfire hovering above with her eyes blazing and both hands lit. The general looked up and calculated odds.

She said quietly: "I am not a prisoner. I will never be a prisoner again. Tell whoever sent you."

Yal-Zor's warship left Earth's atmosphere and did not return. Back at the Tower that evening, Cyborg made his famous chili — the enormous batch he saved for real victories. Beast Boy ate four bowls and fell asleep on the couch. Starfire sat at the table with Robin, and the smile that crossed her face came easily and freely, the way starbolts do when you are exactly where you belong.
