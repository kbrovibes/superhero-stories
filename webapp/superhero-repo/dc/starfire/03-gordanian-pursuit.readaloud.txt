Imagine waking up on a perfectly normal Tuesday morning — and then a Gordanian warship parks in the sky above your city. YIKES.

That is exactly what the people of Jump City saw when they looked up from their breakfast. A big, dark space warship hovering over the harbor, with drone aircraft buzzing through the streets and a scary general named Yal-Zor broadcasting on every radio and TV channel: SURRENDER STARFIRE. One hour. Or else.

The Teen Titans heard it in their Tower. And Koriand'r — Starfire — stood very still for a moment.

She had spent years as a Gordanian prisoner. Years on cold ships, far from the twin suns of Tamaran, being told she belonged to someone else. And now they had come for her again, this time threatening HER city. HER home. HER family.

She felt something calm and bright and fierce rise up inside her.

She looked at Robin, Cyborg, Raven, and Beast Boy. She said: "They came for me. You do not have to fight my battle."

Robin looked at the warship out the window. He said: "They aimed weapons at our city. That makes it our battle."

PLAN TIME! The Titans were brilliant at plans. Cyborg hacked the warship's communications and filled every channel with Earth sports radio — incredibly loud and very confusing for Gordanian ears. Beast Boy turned into a bottlenose dolphin and led one of the drones away from a crowded dock area. SPLASH! Then it got close — BOOM — he became a rhinoceros. That drone was done.

Raven reached out with her dark energy and simply pulled a drone's power cell right out of its hull. Clatter. Done.

And Starfire? She went STRAIGHT UP. She blazed into the sky like a green comet heading directly for the warship. She was bait — drawing Yal-Zor's biggest cannon directly at her — while her teammates worked.

A plasma bolt hit her shoulder! She SPUN in the air — found her balance in half a second — locked on. STARBOLT! The warship's main cannon exploded in a shower of sparks.

Yal-Zor sent his soldiers into the streets. That turned out to be a very poor decision.

The Titans fought through the docklands for forty minutes. Then Yal-Zor was surrounded. Robin's line pinned his weapon arm. Cyborg's sonic cannon hummed at full power. Starfire floated above him, eyes blazing, both hands lit with emerald fire.

She said, calm and clear: "I am not a prisoner. Never again. Tell whoever sent you."

The warship left. It did not come back.

That night Cyborg made the big pot of chili. The one he saves for real victories. Beast Boy ate four bowls and fell asleep on the couch before anyone could stop him.

And Starfire smiled — easily, freely, like sunlight.

She was home.
