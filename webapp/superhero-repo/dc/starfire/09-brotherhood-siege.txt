The Brotherhood of Evil had spent months mapping every hero team on Earth before they struck. Their leader, the Brain — a brilliant criminal intelligence preserved in a robotic chassis — had assembled a roster of villains that outnumbered every young hero on the planet. The plan was simultaneous: attack all Teen Titan outposts at once, leaving no one free to help anyone else. It was the most sophisticated assault on young heroes the world had ever seen.

Starfire was in Tokyo when it began. She had been working alongside a Japanese hero team, investigating Gordanian probe activity near the Pacific coast, when the Titans emergency alert lit every channel at once. The Brotherhood had struck twelve locations simultaneously. Jump City's Tower was under siege by three villains at the same time. The Titans East, the Titans South, the honorary Titan network across four continents — all engaged, all alone.

She flew back across the Pacific at full speed, green light blazing in a long streak visible from high altitude, pulling from every bit of sunlight available. In the stratosphere, unobstructed, she was faster than anything the Brotherhood had in the air.

She arrived at Titans Tower to find Robin directing what remained of the home team — Cyborg running communications, Raven holding the perimeter, Beast Boy buying time in the courtyard below against a villain called Monsieur Mallah. Starfire came in over the bay and dropped two starbolts into the courtyard that ended Mallah's advance in three seconds.

The fight at the Tower lasted ninety minutes. Three Brotherhood villains, coordinated and expecting to overwhelm a depleted team. But the Titans were not depleted: they were exactly themselves, and Starfire was among them. When it was over and the Brotherhood's local assault had failed, Robin established a relay with Cyborg and they began coordinating rescue operations for every Titan team that had been hit.

What the Brain had not modeled correctly was the speed of Tamaranean flight over open ocean, the way that Starfire's presence transformed team morale from holding action to genuine counterattack, or the stubborn fact that a team who fought from love rather than calculation was harder to predict than any algorithm. The Brotherhood had mapped what the Titans were. They had not fully understood what the Titans meant to each other.

By the end of the following day, every Titan team was operational again. Every Brotherhood villain had been accounted for. Robin stood on the Tower roof and looked at Jump City and let himself breathe slowly. Starfire stood beside him. She was still glowing faintly — residual solar charge from the stratospheric sprint — and she smelled like ozone and starlight.

"Good team," he said.

"The best," she said.
