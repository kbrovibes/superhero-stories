Have you ever had someone pretend to be your friend when they were actually up to something sneaky? That is exactly what happens in this story — and Starfire has to figure it out the hard way.

It started with a spaceship! A sleek, dark spacecraft dropped right out of the sky and landed in the courtyard of Titans Tower. And out walked a tall woman in dark armor who said, "Koriand'r! Little sister! I have been looking for you everywhere!"

It was Blackfire. Starfire's own older sister. The one who had betrayed her years ago.

But Starfire's heart is made of sunlight, and sunlight always reaches toward warmth. She hugged her sister back. Maybe Blackfire had changed. Maybe THIS time would be different.

Blackfire was SO charming. She beat Cyborg at every video game. She flew racing laps with Beast Boy and won. She called Koriand'r by her secret childhood nickname and laughed the way sisters laugh. The whole Titans Tower felt a little brighter.

Except for Robin.

Robin had trained with Batman — the greatest detective on Earth — and he had learned to watch for the tiny things people try to hide. He noticed Blackfire's eyes sliding toward the Tower's security panels. He noticed how her questions about Starfire's powers were just a little too careful, too precise.

He told Koriand'r. And Koriand'r — even though part of her wanted to believe so badly — listened.

Then Blackfire told the truth herself. She had made a deal with a Gordanian warlord named — well, no matter the name. She would hand Starfire over. Again. In exchange for a Centauri Moon Diamond worth a small star system.

She had never changed at all.

But here is the thing: STARFIRE had changed. She was no longer a captive princess waiting to be rescued. She was a Titan, a warrior, a hero with green fire blazing in her hands.

The battle was INCREDIBLE. Emerald starbolts and violet energy arcs blazing across the night sky! CRACK! BOOM! The whole bay lit up! Blackfire was fast and fierce — but Starfire was fighting for something bigger than a diamond or a throne. She was fighting for her family. Her real family: Robin, Cyborg, Raven, Beast Boy.

She drove Blackfire back. Not with anger — with love that was stronger than anything Blackfire had ever felt.

She let her sister go. She said: if you ever really change, the door is open.

Later, Robin found her on the rooftop, sitting quietly under the stars. He sat right down next to her without saying a word.

Sometimes that is the best thing a friend can do.
