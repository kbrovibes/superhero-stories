Have you ever been somewhere that felt like home — but also realized, clearly and fully, that you have a different home now? That is the story happening here, and it is a beautiful one.

A courier probe arrived at Titans Tower — a small cylinder still warm from traveling through hyperspace, which is the very fast way of getting across the galaxy. Inside was a message from Tamaran. Tamaran! The planet where Koriand'r was BORN.

The message said: ion storms had hit Tamaran, and the ancient energy conduits that helped channel sunlight through the palace district had broken down. Children and elderly people who depended on those conduits were weakened. Could Koriand'r come?

Of course she could. Of course she would.

She and Robin traveled through the Vega system in a borrowed spacecraft — three whole days of flying through the stars. Robin studied navigational charts. Koriand'r looked out the port window and remembered.

And then — there it was. Tamaran.

Two suns, low on the horizon. The whole planet glowing amber and gold in their combined light. BEAUTIFUL. It was always beautiful. She had never forgotten.

The Tamaranean people came out to meet her, and it was the most wonderful surprise: children who had grown up hearing her name, old warriors who remembered her as a young girl training in the palace courtyard. A small girl with eyes like polished copper ran up and pressed a silver-leafed branch into her hands and said nothing, just looked up at her.

Koriand'r kept that branch. She held it very carefully.

Now, the work. The conduits had crystalline energy nodes inside them, and the ion storms had cracked the crystals. Regular repair crews could not fix them — the nodes needed the exact right kind of energy reactivated from the inside.

Starfire flew into the conduit shafts herself. Her body is basically a living solar power source, remember? She flew through each shaft and fired precise, carefully measured starbolts into each broken node — click, activated. Next one. Click, activated. It took a whole entire day of very careful, focused work.

When the last node lit up — BLOOM — warm golden light spread across the whole palace district.

Four hundred Tamaraneans began to hum. It was the harmony of celebration, rising higher and higher, like sunlight in sound.

Before she left, the High Councillor offered her a home on Tamaran. A real place, a permanent one, with all the honors due a princess.

She thanked him. She said no.

Robin was at the ship. They lifted off and Tamaran grew smaller through the port window, the twin suns bright in the distance.

She had the silver-leafed branch. She had her answer. Tamaran made her. Earth chose her. She chose Earth right back.

That is exactly where she belonged.
