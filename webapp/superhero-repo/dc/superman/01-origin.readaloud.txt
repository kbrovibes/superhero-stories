BOOM! Far, far across the galaxy, on a beautiful planet called Krypton, something incredible — and heartbreaking — was about to happen. Are you ready?

Krypton was the most advanced world you could ever imagine. Crystal towers, gleaming cities, scientists who understood the universe better than anyone. And the most brilliant scientist of all was a man named Jor-El. His wife was Lara Lor-Van. And together they had the most precious thing in their lives: their baby son, Kal-El.

But Jor-El had discovered something terrible. Krypton was going to be destroyed. He warned everyone. No one listened. So Jor-El and Lara made the most heartbreaking and most loving decision any parent has ever made. They built a tiny spacecraft — just big enough for one baby — and they sent Kal-El hurtling through the stars toward a little blue planet called Earth. They kissed their son goodbye. WHOOOOSH — the rocket launched into the sky just as Krypton crumbled behind it.

The little spacecraft landed in the wheat fields of Smallville, Kansas — THUMP — and two very kind farmers, Jonathan and Martha Kent, found a baby in the wreckage. They named him Clark. They raised him as their own son. They taught him to be honest and kind and hardworking. And they noticed that Earth's yellow sun did something extraordinary to Clark — it filled him with powers beyond anything they'd ever seen! Super strength! Super speed! X-ray vision!

Did Jonathan Kent let Clark show off? Absolutely not. "These powers are a responsibility," he said, "not a party trick." And Clark never forgot it.

Here's a fun fact: Superman first appeared in 1938 — he was one of the very FIRST superheroes ever created! And when Clark grew up and moved to the big shining city of Metropolis and became Superman, he carried every lesson from Kansas right inside his heart. Because the most powerful being on Earth got his greatest strength from two ordinary farmers who loved him. Can you imagine anything more wonderful than that?
