Long, long ago, on a beautiful planet called Krypton, a baby boy was born. His name was Kal-El, and his parents — Jor-El and Lara Lor-Van — loved him more than all the crystalline towers and scientific wonders of their remarkable world. But Krypton was in terrible danger, and there was nothing anyone could do to save it.

So Jor-El and Lara made the most heartbreaking and most loving decision a parent could ever make. They placed their tiny son in a special spacecraft and sent him toward the distant warmth of Earth. The little ship traveled alone through the cold darkness of space, carrying the last legacy of a proud people to a new home.

The spacecraft landed in the rolling wheat fields of Smallville, Kansas. Two very kind people named Jonathan and Martha Kent found the baby in the wreckage, and they took him in and raised him as their own son — Clark Kent. They taught him to be honest and hardworking and kind. And do you know what happened next? The yellow sun of Earth began to unlock something extraordinary inside him. He became stronger than anything. Faster than a high-speed train. He could even see through solid walls.

Jonathan Kent told Clark gently that these gifts were not for showing off. They were a responsibility — to be used for the good of others. Clark never forgot those words.

And that is how a child of Krypton became the greatest hero of Earth — not because of his powers, but because of the love and wisdom of two ordinary people from Kansas. He grew up, moved to the great city of Metropolis, and became Superman: a protector who chose kindness over conquest, every single time.
