Somewhere in the cold darkness between the stars, a vast ship shaped like a skull moved silently through space. Inside it, shelves upon shelves held tiny glass bottles, and inside each bottle — an entire city, shrunk down to miniature size. The creature who collected them was called Brainiac: the Collector of Worlds. He did not believe in cruelty, exactly. He simply believed that knowledge was precious, and that the best way to preserve a city was to bottle it up — even if no one inside had asked.

One of those bottles held Kandor — the beautiful capital city of Krypton. Superman's home. When Superman discovered this, he felt something he rarely felt: a sorrow that went all the way to his bones, mixed with a fierce, blazing hope that a piece of his lost world still existed.

Now Brainiac had come for Metropolis. He sent drones and traps and robots, and tried to use the Bottled City of Kandor as a bargaining chip, knowing it would hurt. But what Brainiac never understood — what made his cold calculations go wrong every single time — was Superman's heart. Brainiac could process a million scenarios per second, but he could not process love, or the determination of someone who refuses to let the people he cares for become data in a filing system.

And do you know what happened next? Superman fought through every trap, every drone, every trick — and he brought Kandor home. He keeps the Bottled City safe inside his Fortress of Solitude to this day, working to restore it.

And that is how knowledge without kindness will always fall short — because a heart full of hope is the one thing a collector can never put in a bottle.
