Somewhere in the cold darkness between the stars, a ship shaped like an enormous skull drifts silently through space. Inside it — shelves and shelves of glass bottles. And inside each bottle? An entire CITY, shrunk down to the size of a snow globe! The creature who collected them is called Brainiac — the Collector of Worlds — and he is unlike any villain Superman has ever faced.

ZZZAP! Brainiac doesn't destroy cities. He SHRINKS them. He decides a civilization is worth preserving, points his shrink-beam — and ZIP — down it goes into a bottle on his shelf. He's collected thousands of cities from thousands of planets across the galaxy. And one day, Superman discovered something that stopped his heart cold: one of those bottles held KANDOR — the capital city of Krypton. His people. Still alive. Still tiny. Living inside a bottle in a villain's collection!

Can you imagine how that felt? Superman had thought Krypton was completely gone. But here was a piece of it — tiny, fragile, but ALIVE. He felt sadness and joy and fury all at the same time.

Now Brainiac had come for Metropolis — the next jewel for his shelves. He sent robot drones, energy mazes, and psychological traps designed to use Superman's love for Kandor against him. Brainiac can calculate a million outcomes per second. He analyzed Superman's every move. He thought he had the perfect plan.

But Brainiac made one massive mistake. He forgot about heart. Because Superman pushed through every drone, every trap, every trick — not with strength alone, but with love. CRASH BOOM SMASH — one by one the obstacles fell. And Superman brought the Bottled City of Kandor home to safety.

Today, Kandor sits carefully on a special shelf inside the Fortress of Solitude — a tiny city glowing softly, waiting for the day Superman can make them big again. Because some things are too precious to leave in anyone else's collection.
