The Justice League has some of the most powerful heroes who have ever existed. Green Lantern can build anything with pure light. Wonder Woman has the strength of a goddess. Batman is the cleverest person on the planet. Flash can outrun sound itself. And standing right at the center of all of them, in his red and blue suit, is Superman. Not because he's the strongest — though he might be! — but because of something much harder to measure.

Here's a question for you: what makes someone a true leader? Is it being the biggest? The loudest? The one who gives the most orders? Superman shows the answer every single day — and it has nothing to do with any of those things!

Superman leads by being first into danger and last to leave. He leads by kneeling down to talk to frightened people after a disaster. He leads by never asking the team to do something he wouldn't do himself. His red and blue suit moving through the sky during a crisis is a signal to every other hero: we can do this. Keep going. When Superman doesn't give up, no one gives up.

And his friendship with Batman — called the World's Finest partnership — is one of the greatest in all of superhero history! They are total opposites: Batman works in shadows, Superman works in sunlight. Batman plans for the worst; Superman hopes for the best. But together? They complete each other like two puzzle pieces. Each one is better because the other exists.

Superman's biggest message to the Justice League — the thing he reminds them again and again: they serve humanity. They do not rule it. Power is a responsibility to be used with LOVE, not something to show off. Can you imagine telling that to a team of people who could literally move mountains? Superman does. Every single time. And every single time, they listen — because he shows them first, in everything he does.
