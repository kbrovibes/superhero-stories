You might think that the most powerful man on Earth draws his strength from the yellow sun. And the yellow sun does give him a great deal. But if you asked Clark Kent where his deepest strength truly comes from, he would tell you: his family.

Lois Lane is not just Clark's wife. She is his equal in every way. She is brave and fierce and completely devoted to the truth. She knows every single part of who Clark is — every secret, every worry, every hope. On the nights when the weight of protecting the world feels very heavy, it is Lois who reminds him why it is worth it.

Then there is Jonathan. Jon is their son — a boy with the powers of a Kryptonian and the warm, curious heart of a human being. Teaching Jon to use his abilities is one of the most important things Clark has ever done. They fly together over the countryside, father and son, the world spread out below them like a patchwork quilt. Clark tells Jon the same things that Jonathan Kent of Smallville once told him: these gifts are not for showing off. They are a responsibility to be used with love.

The family is even larger than that. Cousin Kara Zor-El is part of their circle, and other trusted allies too. Together they share a heritage and a mission — and none of them ever has to face the darkness alone.

And do you know what Clark knows, more surely than anything else? That the person he is — the goodness inside him — was not built by Krypton. It was built by Lois and Jon and the family that loves him.

And that is how the Man of Steel found the greatest power of all: a place to belong.
