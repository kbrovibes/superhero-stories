You might think the most powerful person on Earth gets his strength from the yellow sun — and the yellow sun does give him quite a lot! But if you asked Clark Kent right now where his REAL strength comes from, he'd smile, and then he'd tell you: his family.

Let's talk about Lois Lane first. Lois is not just Clark's wife. She is his equal in absolutely every way that matters. She is brave in situations that would terrify almost anyone. She is fiercely devoted to the truth. She is also the person who knows Clark completely — every hope, every worry, every secret, every doubt. When the weight of protecting the whole world gets heavy — and it does get heavy — Lois is the one who reminds Clark why it's worth it. She is his anchor. His home. His favorite person.

Then there's Jonathan — Jon for short — their son. Jon has Kryptonian powers and a completely, wonderfully human heart. Teaching Jon to use his abilities is one of the most important missions Clark has ever had. They fly together over the countryside, father and son, the whole wide world spread out below them. "These gifts are a responsibility," Clark tells Jon — the exact words Jonathan Kent of Smallville once told him. The lesson passed forward like a torch through the generations. WHOOOOSH — two superheroes soaring side by side, one teaching the other to be good.

Cousin Kara Zor-El is part of their circle too, and other trusted friends who share in the mission. Together they call themselves the Super Family. They share heritage, purpose, and the knowledge that none of them ever has to face the darkness alone.

Here's the truth about Superman that not everyone understands: the yellow sun is what makes him powerful. But Lois and Jon? They're what makes him GOOD. And good is so much more important than powerful.
