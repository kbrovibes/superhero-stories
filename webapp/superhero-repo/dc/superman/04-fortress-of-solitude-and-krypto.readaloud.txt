Far, far to the north, past where any map ends and the world turns to endless ice and snow, there is a place that shimmers in the pale Arctic light. Great crystalline towers rise from the frozen ground, built from self-assembling Kryptonian crystals that hum softly in the wind. Only one person in the universe can open the door. This is the Fortress of Solitude — Superman's truest home.

Inside, the Fortress holds wonders beyond imagining. There is the Bottled City of Kandor on a carefully lit shelf, the precious miniature city Superman rescued from Brainiac. There are glowing holographic projections of his parents, Jor-El and Lara, who speak to him with kindness and wisdom whenever he needs guidance. And there are the records of Krypton itself — the history of his people, preserved so it will never be forgotten.

But the very best thing about the Fortress might also be the most ordinary thing: a dog. Krypto is a big, white, very happy dog from Krypton, sent into space on a test flight long before the planet was destroyed. He found his way to Earth, and under the yellow sun he became just as powerful as Superman. He can fly! He can see through walls! He is also, without question, still a dog — and loves nothing more than chasing Arctic foxes and playing fetch with titanium-reinforced balls that would crush anything a regular dog might carry.

And do you know what this means? When the most powerful being in the universe comes home to the Fortress of Solitude, he is greeted by a tail wagging so hard the whole ice shelf shakes.

From that day on, the Fortress remained the place where Kal-El could simply be himself — part Kryptonian, part Kansan, and always, always glad to be home.
