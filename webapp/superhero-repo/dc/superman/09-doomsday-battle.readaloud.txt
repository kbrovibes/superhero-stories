The darkest day in Metropolis's history did not begin with a villain in a suit or a spacecraft in the sky. It began with a rumbling in the earth — and then Doomsday burst free, a creature of pure, ancient rage from the prehistoric past of Krypton. It was enormous. It was relentless. And it headed straight for the heart of the city.

The Justice League tried to stop it. One by one, they fell back. This was not like any threat they had faced before. And then Superman stepped forward into the street and met the creature face to face.

The battle that followed was the hardest thing Superman had ever done. Every punch he landed seemed to make Doomsday stronger. The buildings of Metropolis shook. The pavement cracked and split. The fight moved from block to block, past the terrified citizens who had hidden in doorways and behind cars — and their frightened faces gave Superman something that no power under any sun could give him: the absolute determination not to stop.

And do you know what happened next? In front of the Daily Planet building, under the great revolving globe, Superman and Doomsday struck each other with everything they had left. One tremendous, final impact. A shockwave rolled through the city. And when the dust settled, both of them had fallen.

Lois Lane held Clark in her arms on the cold street while the city of Metropolis fell into a great, quiet sadness.

But the story does not end there. The world had seen what one person was willing to give for the people he loved. And from that sacrifice grew something that could never be destroyed: hope. Superman came back. The spirit of a true hero always does.
