BOOM. RUMBLE. CRASH. The darkest day Metropolis has ever seen did not start with a villain in a suit or a machine in the sky. It started with the earth shaking — and then something ancient and terrible burst free.

His name was Doomsday. He came from the prehistoric past of Krypton, a creature built for one single purpose: to be unstoppable. And he was. He was enormous. He was covered in bony spikes that absorbed every blow. Every punch that landed made him tougher than before. He tore through the city leaving a trail of destruction — SMASH CRASH CRACK — and the Justice League threw everything they had at him. One by one, they fell back. Nothing was enough.

And then Superman stepped forward into the middle of the street.

What followed was the hardest battle of his life. Every punch Superman landed made Doomsday stronger — did you hear that? STRONGER. The more Superman hit him, the worse things got! The buildings of Metropolis shook. The pavement shattered. The fight rolled from block to block, past citizens hiding in doorways and behind overturned cars — and their terrified faces gave Superman something that no yellow sun could ever provide: the absolute determination not to stop. Not while one person needed protecting.

The fight reached the Daily Planet — right under the great spinning globe on the roof. Superman could feel himself slowing. Doomsday could feel it too. CRASH — they struck each other with every last bit of strength they had. One tremendous, world-shaking impact. A shockwave rolled through the whole city. And when the dust settled, both of them had fallen.

Lois Lane held Clark on the cold street. Metropolis went very quiet.

But the story never ends there. Heroes return. And the hope that Superman planted in every heart in that city — the courage he inspired — that was impossible to destroy. The spirit of a true hero is eternal.
