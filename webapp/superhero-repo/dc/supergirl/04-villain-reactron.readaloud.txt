Ready for a story about thinking smarter, not just hitting harder? This one's got a NUCLEAR armor suit and a whole lot of strategy!

Ben Krull used to be a soldier and then a test pilot. Then an accident left him badly hurt, and military engineers rebuilt part of him with a nuclear-powered core. They called the result Reactron — a man inside a nuclear armor suit that happened to have one very nasty weapon: gold kryptonite radiation. The one thing that makes a Kryptonian feel absolutely terrible.

He picked a morning when Superman was in Metropolis dealing with something else. Then he drove toward Midvale, blasting craters in the road, loudly announcing himself.

Kara was already in the air before he reached the old rail bridge.

KRAANG — they collided in midair! Kara felt the nuclear radiation from the suit immediately, a deep grinding wrongness in her bones. She backed off fast, floating twenty feet away, watching.

Reactron was powerful. But he was not clever. Every blast was maximum force. Every move was a straight line.

Kara smiled behind her mask. She had a plan.

She started moving in wide swooping arcs, unpredictable as a pinball — WHOOSH here, WHOOSH there — using the rail bridge girders as cover and then abandoning them before he could lock on. ZZZT — his blast hit steel instead of her. She was already on the other side.

She drew him east. Then south. Away from town, toward the old quarry at the edge of Midvale. The quarry had tall rock walls on three sides. She knew exactly what she was going to do.

When Reactron followed her into the quarry — WHOOOOSH — Kara went to full speed, looped behind him in a giant circle, and hit the back of his suit right where the nuclear core housing was. KRAANG!

The shielding cracked. The kryptonite generator went dark.

The suit stopped glowing.

What was left was still a large, angry former soldier. They wrestled for a few more minutes — WHUMP, THUD, CRASH — before Kara pinned him against the rock wall and called the Department of Extranormal Operations on the communicator in her cape.

"I'll be back with better equipment," Reactron said.

"Good," said Kara. "I'll study the suit designs tonight so I'm ready."

She meant it. That afternoon she was at S.T.A.R. Labs, reading everything on file about nuclear armor and gold kryptonite. Because knowing your enemy's tools is just as important as knowing your own.

That's not just super-strength. That's super-smart.
