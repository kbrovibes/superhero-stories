The summons came from the Watchtower, the Justice League's space station orbiting Earth at 22,000 miles altitude. Kara had heard of it but never been inside. When she flew through the thermosphere and the docking bay opened for her, she floated in trying hard to look like this was normal. Batman watched from the corridor shadows, which she had expected. Wonder Woman offered a handshake Kara returned carefully, given what she'd heard about Amazon grip strength. The Flash vibrated his fingers nervously and stopped when he noticed her noticing.

The situation was a dimensional incursion: a being called Maxima from the planet Almerac had opened three simultaneous breach points over different cities, each releasing battle drones into the atmosphere. Superman was assigned to Metropolis. Wonder Woman took Paris. Batman coordinated communications. That left Kara, The Flash, and Green Lantern John Stewart for the Tokyo breach — the largest.

The Tokyo engagement was organized chaos. The drones adapted — they analyzed attack patterns and began countering after the first few strikes. Green Lantern's constructs worked once before the drones anticipated the emerald light. Kara took the front line while the Flash moved in intervals too fast for any drone to track. She drew fire; he disabled power cells from behind. They cleared hundreds of drones in twenty minutes.

The breach required a different approach. Maxima had anchored it with a crystalline resonance device hovering at the center of the dimensional tear. Green Lantern couldn't get close through the drone coverage. Kara put her head down and flew straight into the worst of the fire, taking everything the remaining drones could throw at her, while Green Lantern and the Flash cleared the perimeter. She reached the device, read its structure with x-ray vision in three seconds — her father had spent his career studying alien technology — and used heat vision to sever the four anchor points in precise sequence. The breach collapsed. Every drone lost its command signal and went dark.

On the flight back to the Watchtower, Green Lantern told Kara that her read of the resonance device had been decisive. Batman said nothing. Three days later, Superman mentioned — in a tone of deliberate understatement — that Batman had filed a recommendation for Supergirl's provisional membership to the Justice League roster. Kara flew four loops over Midvale. She was supposed to stop at three.
