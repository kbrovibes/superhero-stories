• The S-shield is the ancient crest of the House of El on Krypton — a glyph meaning "hope," worn for generations by Kara's father Zor-El and his fathers before him
• On Earth, Superman's use of the symbol made it shorthand for "safety is coming"
• Kara sews her own version at the Danvers household and pricks her finger on the needle twice, which is funny given she can stop bullets
• First time she wears it publicly, a little girl in a parking lot calls her "the Superman lady" — Kara is still figuring out her own identity
• One winter night, a dam upstream from Harker's Mill begins to fail, threatening 4,000 people
• Kara works for over an hour: hauling boulders, welding with heat vision, redirecting water with super-breath
• The dam holds; the town is saved; the people of Harker's Mill never know how close they came to losing everything
• Standing waist-deep in mud and water, the S-shield on her chest is the only clean thing on her
• She remembers her father's words: "It means we keep trying. Even when it's hard. Especially then."
