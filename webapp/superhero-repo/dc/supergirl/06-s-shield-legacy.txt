The S-shield was older than any hero. On Krypton, it had been the crest of the House of El for generations — not a letter but a Kryptonian glyph that meant hope, a philosophical concept that Kryptonian scholars said was the first duty of anyone with power. Kara's father Zor-El had worn it on his chest, and his father before him, and Kara had grown up knowing the symbol the way she knew her own name. When Superman wore it on Earth, the planet adopted it as a shorthand for something safe coming to help you.

After a few months in Midvale, Kara sewed her own version from materials she found at the Danvers household. She used scissors at normal speed and pricked her finger twice on the needle, which was funnier than it sounds given that the same finger could stop a bullet. The finished suit was deep blue with a red cape and the S-shield centered on the chest, and the first time she wore it publicly, a small girl in a grocery store parking lot pointed and said "that's the Superman lady." Kara was not offended. She was, after all, still figuring out who she was.

The meaning deepened one winter night when a dam upstream from the town of Harker's Mill began to fail. Kara arrived minutes after the first break and spent the next hour and twenty minutes working faster than any machine: pouring boulders from the nearby valley into the breach, welding them in place with heat vision, using super-breath to redirect water flow around the partial barrier while she built. The town of Harker's Mill had 4,000 people in it and all of them went to sleep that night without knowing how close they had come to losing their homes.

When the dam held and the water stopped rising and Kara stood waist-deep in the flood current, breathing hard, covered in mud, the S-shield on her chest was the only part of her that was still clean. It was almost comical. She looked down at it and thought about her father's notebooks, the carefully inked symbol on every page, the reason he gave when she was seven years old and asked what it meant: "It means we keep trying. Even when it's hard. Especially then."

She kept it in mind on every flight after that — not as a logo, not as a costume detail, but as a promise. The House of El had built that meaning over generations. Kara intended to add to it.
