Okay, here is a story about the time Kara melted the toaster. It does not stay about the toaster. But it starts there!

One perfectly ordinary morning, Kara was standing in the kitchen thinking about breakfast. She glanced at the toaster. And — ZZZAP — two red beams shot from her eyes and the toaster became a glowing puddle.

Heat vision! Uncontrolled heat vision! Oops.

Superman told her the basics: heat vision runs on concentration and emotion — feel strongly, beam gets hot. Freeze breath is the opposite — a super-cold exhale, precise and powerful. Both take practice. Both will cause problems if you lose focus. He also mentioned that he once accidentally froze an entire lake in Manitoba when he was twelve.

That made Kara feel MUCH better about the toaster.

She set up a training area at the Midvale salvage yard and got to work.

For heat vision: tin cans. She started small, learning to warm them without melting them, then melting them without burning the ground, then cutting them in precise lines. She discovered she could narrow the beam down to the width of a needle! ZZZZAP — she engraved her name in Kryptonian script on a steel panel. Then in English, just because it was fun.

For freeze breath: barrels of water. She practiced coating the surface in a smooth sheet of ice — FWOOOOSH — without shattering the barrel. She got so precise she could freeze one side of a barrel without the other side even getting cold.

Then came the real test. A Tuesday in late November. Chemical fire at Midvale Harbor freight terminal!

Ordinary water would make a chemical fire WORSE. The fire department was there but didn't have enough foam. Kara landed and assessed in three seconds: two chemical sections, one electrical section.

Section one, chemical — FWOOOOSH! Freeze breath smothered the flames in a precise cold sheet.
Section two, chemical — FWOOOOSH! Done!
Section three, electrical wiring — ZZZZAP! Heat vision at minimum setting, burning out the fuel source precisely, no spreading.

Four minutes. The whole terminal. Safe.

The fire chief found Kara standing in the steam afterward. He said it was the most precise fire suppression he had EVER seen.

Kara said thank you, and mentioned the toaster.

He looked confused.

She decided not to explain.

Some stories are better left mysterious!
