Are you ready for the BIGGEST story yet? Because this one goes all the way to OUTER SPACE and then comes back to save Tokyo. Hold on!

The Justice League called.

Not a phone call. A signal from the Watchtower — the League's space station orbiting Earth twenty-two thousand miles up. Kara flew through the thermosphere, through actual SPACE, and into a docking bay that opened just for her.

Inside: Batman, watching from the shadows. Wonder Woman, with a handshake that nearly cracked Kara's hand — nearly. The Flash, whose fingers vibrated with nervous energy until he caught Kara noticing and stopped.

The crisis: a being called Maxima, from a distant planet called Almerac, had torn three holes in the dimensional fabric over three cities at once, and battle drones were pouring through every hole. All three cities needed help at the same time!

Superman went to Metropolis. Wonder Woman went to Paris. Batman stayed at the Watchtower running communications. That left Kara, The Flash, and Green Lantern John Stewart for the Tokyo breach — the biggest one.

WHOOOOSH — Tokyo! The city had gone into emergency protocol, streets clearing, emergency lights flashing — and above the skyline, dozens of battle drones swirling like angry machines, firing in every direction.

The drones were SMART. They learned. You hit one with a pattern — ZZZAP — and two seconds later all the others knew how to dodge it. Green Lantern's emerald constructs worked ONCE before the drones adapted.

Kara took the front line, drawing fire onto herself — KRAANG KRAANG KRAANG — while the Flash zipped behind them at impossible speed, disabling power cells one by one. WHOOSH WHOOSH WHOOSH. It was a perfect rhythm. She drew, he disabled. Twenty minutes and hundreds of drones later, the sky was clearing.

But the BREACH. The hole in reality. It was anchored by a crystalline resonance device hovering right at the center — and there were still too many drones around it for Green Lantern to get close.

Kara looked at the drone coverage. Looked at the device. Looked at her hands.

"I'll get it," she said.

WHOOOOOOSH — she flew straight into the fire. KRAANG KRAANG KRAANG — she took every hit, pushing through. She reached the device! Three seconds with x-ray vision to read the structure — she had learned alien technology from her father. Then: heat vision, precise sequence, four anchor points — ZZZT ZZZT ZZZT ZZZT!

The breach folded inward and COLLAPSED. Every remaining drone lost its signal simultaneously and dropped from the sky.

Tokyo: safe.

On the way back to the Watchtower, Green Lantern told Kara she had made the decisive move. She said her father taught her to read alien structures.

Batman said nothing. But three days later, Superman told Kara that Batman had quietly filed a recommendation for her provisional membership to the Justice League.

Kara flew loops over Midvale. She was supposed to fly three. She flew four.

Nobody said anything about the four. They understood completely.
