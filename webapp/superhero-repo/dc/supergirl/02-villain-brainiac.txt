The signal came from deep space — a cold mathematical pulse that no ordinary radio could detect. Kara noticed it first because she had spent years learning the sound frequencies of Krypton's old communication arrays, and this signal used the same wavelength. When she traced it to its source, her blood went cold. The transmission came from a massive green spacecraft shaped like a skull, drifting toward Earth's orbit. She knew that ship. Everyone on Krypton had known that ship.

Brainiac. A being of pure machine intelligence, he had traveled the galaxy for centuries collecting cities. He would shrink entire populations down to miniature size, seal them in glass bottles, and add them to his collection like trophies. Krypton had lost a city this way — Kandor, stolen and preserved under a glass dome before the planet's destruction. Brainiac was precise, logical, and utterly without mercy, and he had decided that Midvale would make an excellent addition to his collection.

Kara flew straight at the ship without hesitation. She hit the hull with both fists — KRAANG — and the impact rang through the craft like a struck bell. Brainiac deployed his skull-drones, hundreds of small robots bristling with energy weapons, and the sky above Midvale became a storm of light. Kara moved through them at full speed, heat vision carving arcs of fire, her cape snapping like thunder. She fought for every inch of altitude, driving the drones back one cluster at a time, and the people of Midvale looked up in awe at the girl in the blue-and-red suit holding back an invasion alone.

But Brainiac had planned for a Kryptonian. He fired a containment beam carrying synthesized kryptonite radiation — a green, sickening energy tuned to Kara's biology. The moment it touched her, her strength collapsed. She dropped hundreds of feet before she could stop her fall, gasping. Below her, the city began to shrink at the edges, buildings folding inward like a collapsing model.

She thought of Kandor. She would not let that happen to Midvale.

Kara angled away from the beam, using the last of her strength to reach the ship's emitter array. Instead of destroying it, she reversed the polarity, turning the shrink beam back on itself. The containment field collapsed, the kryptonite radiation vented into space, and Midvale snapped back to full size with a sound like a thunderclap. Brainiac's ship retreated, calculating the odds against a Kryptonian who had out-thought him. Kara hovered above the city, hands shaking, and watched the skull-ship vanish into the stars.
