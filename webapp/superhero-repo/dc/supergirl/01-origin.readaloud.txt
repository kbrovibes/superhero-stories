Are you ready? Because this story starts on a WHOLE OTHER PLANET — and ends with a girl who can fly faster than sound!

Her name is Kara Zor-El, and she grew up in Argo City on a planet called Krypton. Picture silver towers, two moons in the sky, and a dad who was one of the smartest scientists on the entire planet. That was Kara's world. It was amazing. And then — oh no — Krypton's core started to fail. The whole planet was in danger!

Her dad, Zor-El, had one plan and one chance. He built a small rocket — just big enough for one person. He put Kara inside and programmed the ship to fly to a blue planet called Earth. Her cousin Kal-El was already there, living as a hero called Superman!

WHOOOOSH — the rocket fired. Krypton exploded behind her. And Kara flew alone through the dark between the stars for what felt like forever.

But then — THUD — she landed! A field outside a town called Midvale. And standing right there was Superman himself, her cousin, with those recognizable blue eyes. He handed her a warm coat and said, "Welcome. You're safe now."

And then came the fun part!

Earth's yellow sun? It charged Kara's Kryptonian cells like a super-powered battery. WHOOSH — she could fly! ZZZAP — heat vision that could slice through steel! FWOOOOSH — freeze breath cold enough to stop a river! She was strong, fast, and nearly unbeatable.

But here is the thing — being powerful is not the same as being wise. Kara had a LOT to learn about being a hero on this new planet. English. School lunch. Earth humor. All of it!

So she sewed the S-shield — the symbol her whole family wore back on Krypton — onto a blue suit. She tied on a red cape. She took a deep breath.

And she flew.

BOOM — the sonic boom rattled every window in Midvale. Kara laughed so hard she nearly fell out of the sky!

Far below, the little town of Midvale glowed warm and golden. She was far from Argo City. She was far from home. But she was brave, she was kind, and she was exactly who her mother always said she would be.

She was Supergirl. And she was just getting started!
