Ben Krull had once been a soldier, then a test pilot, and then an accident victim who survived only because military engineers rebuilt part of his body with a nuclear-powered core. The Reactron suit was the result — a suit of armor with a gold kryptonite generator at its center, making Ben Krull one of the few beings on Earth specifically designed to be a threat to Kryptonian biology. He resented Superman intensely for a confrontation years earlier, but when Superman was occupied with a crisis in Metropolis, Reactron decided that the Girl of Steel in Midvale would do just fine as a target.

He hit the outskirts of Midvale at dawn, blasting craters in the road and announcing himself loudly enough that Kara was already airborne by the time he reached the old rail bridge. They clashed in midair, and Kara felt the nuclear radiation off the suit immediately — a deep, grinding wrongness in her cells, different from kryptonite but similarly sapping. She backed off, assessing. Reactron was not subtle: every shot was maximum power, every move telegraphed, a soldier who had traded strategy for raw force.

Kara changed tactics on the third exchange. Instead of trading punches, she began moving in wide, unpredictable arcs, using the rail bridge's steel girders as cover and then abandoning them before Reactron's blasts could follow. She drew him away from the populated area of Midvale in stages, leading him toward the quarry outside town where the echoing rock walls would help her. When he followed, she had the geometry she needed: she looped behind him at superspeed, struck the suit's nuclear core housing from behind with both fists, and the blast shielding cracked without breaching the core, disabling the kryptonite generator.

Reactron in a powerless suit was still a large, angry former soldier, and the fight lasted another few minutes before she pinned him against the quarry wall and held him there while she called it in to the Department of Extranormal Operations. As she waited, Reactron told her that he would come back better-equipped next time. Kara said she would be ready, and she was not being reckless — she had already memorized the suit's design well enough to improve her approach for the next encounter. The DEO arrived and took Krull into custody, and Kara spent the following afternoon at S.T.A.R. Labs studying everything on file about gold kryptonite and nuclear-suit technology, because knowing your enemy's tools as well as your own was part of what it meant to be prepared.
