Picture the sky above Midvale going dark. Not from clouds. From something MUCH bigger. Are you ready? This one is scary — but Supergirl's got it!

A massive green spaceship shaped like a SKULL slowly appeared in Earth's orbit. Kara heard it first — a cold signal, a mathematical pulse on old Kryptonian frequencies. She knew exactly what it was.

Brainiac.

This villain is not like any other. He is a pure machine mind, cold and calculating, and he has spent centuries doing the most terrible thing you can imagine: he flies to planets, SHRINKS entire cities down to pocket size, seals them in glass bottles, and takes them as trophies. He once stole the Kryptonian city of Kandor — shrank it down and put it in a jar! Can you imagine?

And now he wanted Midvale.

Kara did not wait. She flew straight at that skull-ship — WHOOOOSH — and hit the hull so hard — KRAANG — that the whole ship rang like a bell! Brainiac launched hundreds of skull-drones, little robots with energy weapons, and the sky turned into a light show. ZZZT! ZZZT! ZZZT!

Kara shot through them at full speed. ZZZAP — heat vision! WHOOSH — barrel roll! She was incredible. The people of Midvale stared up at the sky as the girl in the blue suit held back an entire alien invasion by herself.

But Brainiac had a trick. He fired a beam loaded with kryptonite radiation — the one thing that makes Kara weak. WHOMP. Her strength vanished. She dropped hundreds of feet, gasping, the city beginning to shrink at the edges below her.

Then she thought of Kandor. The city Brainiac stole from Krypton. The people still trapped in a bottle.

No. Not Midvale. Not today.

Kara aimed for the emitter array — the machine that made the shrink beam work — and she REVERSED it. Turned it back on itself like flipping a switch. KABOOM! The kryptonite vented into space. The shrink beam collapsed. Midvale snapped back to full size with a sound like THUNDER!

Brainiac's ship calculated the odds. Calculated them again. Then quietly turned around and flew away.

Kara hovered in the sky, her hands shaking just a little. Below her, Midvale was whole. Safe. Every building back where it belonged.

She smiled. One for Kara. Zero for the skull-ship.

She was going to need a very long nap after that one.
