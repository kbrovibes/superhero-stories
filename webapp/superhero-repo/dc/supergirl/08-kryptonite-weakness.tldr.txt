• A man named Marcus Drayne acquires a green kryptonite fragment and uses it as a weapon in a hostage situation at Midvale's old textile warehouse
• Kara arrives and is immediately affected — her legs stop working and she drops to one knee at the threshold
• Kryptonite doesn't just hurt: it hollows out the sun-energy from her cells, making her feel small and breakable
• Drayne expected her to collapse; she doesn't — she catalogues her options from the floor
• Lead blocks kryptonite radiation completely; the lead-lined case is twelve feet away on a table
• Three employees are unharmed against the far wall
• Kara crawls across the warehouse floor to reach the case — slowly, painfully, without flying
• She flips the lid shut with one shaking hand; the glow vanishes; her strength begins returning immediately
• She is standing when the DEO arrives; Drayne surrenders; the employees are safe
• Kara reflects: being vulnerable isn't the worst thing — only stopping because you're vulnerable truly defeats you
