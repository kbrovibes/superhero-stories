Siobhan McDougal had been a student at Midvale High before the accident that changed everything. She had found an ancient Irish artifact — the Caoránach Crest — in her family's attic, and the moment she touched it, a spirit older than recorded history poured through her. Her hair turned white as bone, her skin went chalk-pale, and she discovered she could produce a scream powerful enough to shatter steel. She called herself Silver Banshee, and she was furious at the world for what she had become.

When Silver Banshee attacked downtown Midvale, the scream was the worst part. SKREEEEE — each wave cracked windows and knocked police officers off their feet. Kara flew in fast, but the moment the scream hit her Kryptonian hearing, which was ten times sharper than a human's, the sound was overwhelming. She gritted her teeth and pushed forward anyway, absorbing the sonic punishment through sheer stubbornness. Silver Banshee rose into the air on whirling white energy, hurling shards of force at buildings below, searching for something she had lost.

Kara had done her homework. Silver Banshee's scream was calibrated to harm living things, but Kara's Kryptonian cells absorbed it differently. It was painful, not disabling. What she needed was to keep the scream away from bystanders. She flew into a hover directly between Banshee and the crowd, arms spread, using herself as a sound barrier while scanning with x-ray vision. She found what she was looking for: the Caoránach Crest, lying where Siobhan had dropped it in a doorway two blocks away.

Kara told Silver Banshee the truth. She said she knew about the artifact, knew Siobhan hadn't asked for any of this, and told her exactly where the Crest had fallen. The fury in Banshee's face flickered — surprise that anyone had listened instead of just fighting. Kara kept talking, calm and steady, using her freeze breath to contain the raging white energy around Banshee. Siobhan picked up the Crest. The spirit quieted. The white energy dissolved.

They sat together afterward on the curb outside the wrecked shop, not quite friends but no longer enemies. Siobhan said being a monster was mostly just exhausting. Kara said that even a Kryptonian on a strange planet understood something about that. The Caoránach Crest was eventually secured by specialists at S.T.A.R. Labs, and Siobhan started finding her way back. Kara checked in every few weeks, because listening had worked once, and she intended to keep doing it.
