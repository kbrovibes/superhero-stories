The villain known as Killer Moth was often dismissed as a second-tier nuisance, a man in a garish costume who lacked the gravitas of the Joker or the menace of Bane. But for Barbara Gordon, Drury Walker was a persistent thorn in her side. He was obsessed with becoming the "anti-Batman," offering his services to Gotham’s underworld as a protector for criminals. While his moth-themed gadgets were often absurd, his desperation made him unpredictable and, at times, surprisingly dangerous.

Their latest encounter began at the Gotham Museum of Natural History, where Killer Moth was attempting to steal a rare collection of prehistoric lepidoptera. Barbara arrived just as he was deploying his "Silk Cannon," a device that fired a fast-hardening adhesive. She dodged the sticky strands with the grace of an acrobat, her purple cape swirling around her. She had studied Walker’s patterns and knew that he relied heavily on his flight suit to maintain an advantage.

"You really need a new hobby, Drury," Batgirl quipped as she launched a specialized Batarang designed to disrupt his wing stabilizers. The projectile hissed through the air, clipping the moth-themed jetpack and sending him into a dizzying spiral. Killer Moth screeched in frustration, firing a barrage of pheromone bombs that filled the room with a disorienting, sweet-smelling fog. Barbara quickly adjusted her cowl’s filters, her eyes narrowing as she tracked his thermal signature through the haze.

The fight moved into the museum’s main atrium, where a massive whale skeleton hung from the ceiling. Killer Moth attempted to use the structures for cover, darting between the bones like his namesake. Barbara followed him, her movements silent and efficient. She realized that Walker’s primary weakness was his ego; he wanted to be seen as a master criminal, and his focus on theatrics often left him vulnerable to simple, direct tactics. She lured him into a narrow corridor where his wings would be a liability.

As he charged through the hall, Barbara triggered a localized EMP that fried his flight suit’s electronics. He tumbled to the floor, his brightly colored wings twitching pathetically. She was on him in an instant, securing his hands with high-tensile zip-ties. Killer Moth looked up at her, his mask askew, still trying to maintain his persona. "You think you've won? This is just a minor setback for the Moth!" he blustered, though his voice lacked conviction.

Barbara didn't waste time on a lecture. She had already alerted the GCPD and secured the stolen artifacts. She looked at the man on the floor and felt a flicker of pity. Drury Walker was a man so desperate for identity that he had chosen one that made him a laughingstock. But she also knew that even a joke could cause real harm if left unchecked. She was the one who kept the "smaller" threats from escalating, ensuring that Gotham’s citizens didn't have to worry about even the most ridiculous villains.

She vanished into the night before the police arrived, leaving Killer Moth for the authorities. The encounter hadn't been a world-shaking battle, but it was a necessary part of her vigil. Every villain she stopped, no matter how garish their costume, was a victory for order and safety. As she swung back toward Burnside, Barbara reflected on the diversity of her rogues' gallery. From cosmic gods to men in moth suits, she was ready for them all, proving that Batgirl was a hero for every level of the fight.
