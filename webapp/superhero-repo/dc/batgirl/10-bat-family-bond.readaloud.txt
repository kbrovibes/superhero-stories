The Bat-family was not an ordinary family. It was a group of people who had lost something important, who had come together under the shadow of Gotham City and decided to protect each other and everyone else. And right at the center of that family was Barbara Gordon.

Her closest bond was with Dick Grayson — the first Robin, who grew up to become Nightwing. They had trained together in the Batcave when they were young, and they had been each other's anchors ever since. When the nights felt too dark, they were the ones who reminded each other why they kept going.

Barbara was also a big sister to the younger members — a mentor for Tim Drake, a steadying hand for Cassandra Cain, an encouraging voice for Stephanie Brown. As Oracle, she was the voice in all of their ears, calm and clear, even during the most dangerous moments.

And do you know what happened when Bruce Wayne brooded too long in the shadows? Barbara was the one who knocked on his door and said: you do not have to do this alone.

Together, the Bat-family had been through tremendous sadness and tremendous courage. Those shared experiences made their bond stronger than steel. When several of them patrolled Gotham's rooftops on the same night, their silhouettes against the moon brought comfort to the innocent and sent a clear message to anyone thinking of causing trouble.

From that day on, Barbara understood: she was Batgirl, she was Oracle, but most of all, she was part of a family. And as long as they had each other, the darkness would never truly win.
