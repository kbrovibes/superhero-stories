Not every villain in Gotham City wears a frightening costume. Some wear moth wings and call themselves Killer Moth. His real name was Drury Walker, and his plan was to be the anti-Batman — a protector for criminals, for hire.

Tonight, Walker had broken into the Gotham Museum of Natural History to steal a collection of rare prehistoric lepidoptera — ancient moths and butterflies locked behind glass. He brought his Silk Cannon, a gadget that fired fast-hardening glue. But Batgirl was already there, her purple cape swirling as she ducked and rolled away from every sticky strand.

She launched a Batarang — it clipped his wing stabilizers and sent him spinning. Walker fired back with pheromone bombs that filled the room with a sweet, dizzy-making fog. Batgirl calmly switched on her cowl's filters and tracked his body heat through the haze.

The chase moved under the giant hanging whale skeleton in the main atrium. And do you know what Batgirl did? She let Walker think he was winning, then led him straight into a narrow corridor where his big moth wings could barely fit. She triggered a small EMP. His flight suit fizzed and died, and Walker crumpled to the floor in a heap of flickering wings.

She fastened his hands with high-tensile zip-ties, called the GCPD, and made sure every artifact was safe. Then she disappeared into the night, leaving Walker for the police.

From that day on, Batgirl made sure that no villain — no matter how silly the costume — was ever left unchecked.
