Have you ever heard of a villain called Killer Moth? His real name is Drury Walker, and he wears a moth costume and calls himself the anti-Batman — a super-villain-for-hire who protects criminals! Can you believe that?

Tonight, Walker had sneaked into the Gotham Museum of Natural History with a truly ridiculous plan: steal a collection of rare prehistoric moths and butterflies from behind the display glass! And he brought his Silk Cannon — a gadget that fires super-fast-hardening glue strands. SPLURT!

But Batgirl was already there. Her purple cape swirled as she ducked and dodged every sticky strand. WHOOSH! ROLL! LEAP! She launched a Batarang — ZING! — and it clipped his wing stabilizers, sending him spinning like a top!

Then Walker fired pheromone bombs — POOF! — filling the whole room with a dizzy sweet fog! Most heroes would have stumbled. Not Batgirl. She calmly switched on her cowl filters and tracked him using thermal heat vision through the haze. She could see him clear as day!

The chase moved into the main atrium, right under the museum's enormous hanging whale skeleton. And then Batgirl did something sneaky and brilliant: she let Walker think he was winning. She led him backwards, step by step, into a narrow corridor where his enormous moth wings barely fit. Then — ZAP! — she triggered a tiny EMP device. Every circuit in his flight suit fizzled and died!

Walker tumbled to the floor in a heap of flickering wings. Batgirl had him zip-tied and called the GCPD before he even knew what had happened.

Every artifact was safe. Not a single prehistoric butterfly was harmed. And Killer Moth had learned a hard lesson about underestimating Batgirl!
