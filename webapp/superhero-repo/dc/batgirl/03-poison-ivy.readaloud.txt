One morning, the Gotham City Botanical Gardens disappeared behind an enormous tangle of vines and oversized flowers. Thorny bushes blocked every path. Sneezing-powder lilies bloomed in every corner. Poison Ivy had turned the public gardens into her own private jungle.

Batgirl flew her mini-drone above the maze and studied the patterns. She noticed something curious — every time the vines shifted, a soft chime rang in the treetops. Musical chimes were hidden in the branches, and they were controlling the plants. Batgirl played the right sequence of notes on her gauntlet, and slowly, gently, the vines curled back to let her through.

Along the way there were sticky sap pits and clouds of tickle-dust spores. Batgirl's filters and solvent sprays handled each one. And do you know what she found at the very center of the gardens? Poison Ivy, carefully tending a single giant glowing orchid, as quiet as a gardener on a Sunday morning.

Batgirl spoke to her calmly. She told Ivy that the people of Gotham loved these gardens and wanted to help protect them. Ivy listened. She agreed to return everything to normal, as long as the city promised to plant more trees downtown. Batgirl kept that promise, working with the park rangers to restore every path and keep every rare species safe.

From that day on, Batgirl proved that patience and a deep understanding of the world around you can solve even the most tangled puzzle.
