Barbara Gordon grew up in the heart of Gotham City, and she had a front-row seat to something most children never see. Her father, Police Commissioner James Gordon, spent every day trying to keep the city safe. Barbara watched him, and she decided she wanted to help too — but in her own way.

She trained and trained and trained. She learned judo and karate until she could move like a shadow. She spent hours in the Gotham City Library studying forensic science and criminology and computer hacking. And do you know what happened next? She began shadowing her father's investigations from a distance, teaching herself to spot the clues that everyone else walked right past.

When Barbara felt ready, she designed her very own suit and gadgets. No one handed them to her — she built them herself. Then she joined Batman and Robin as Batgirl, bringing something the team had never quite had before: a detective's eye paired with an athlete's body and a coder's mind.

From that day on, Batgirl proved to all of Gotham City that you do not need superpowers to be a hero. All you need is courage, dedication, and a very, very sharp mind.
