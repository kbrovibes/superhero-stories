Close your eyes. Take a deep breath. Now imagine diving down, down, down into the Atlantic Ocean — past the sunlight, past the twilight zone, into the dark blue cold of the deep. And then — WHOA — a city appears!

Welcome to Atlantis!

The towers are built from coral and shimmering glass and they glow with a soft blue light, like being inside a moonlit tide pool. The streets aren't walked — they're SWUM! Children dart between sea turtles on their way to school. Merchants travel in vehicles shaped like colorful fish. The whole city hums with power from the ocean currents themselves — no electricity needed, just the endless energy of the sea. At the center stands the grand palace, where King Arthur and Queen Mera watch over their people. And here's a fun fact: Atlantis is said to have been a legendary real civilization that ancient people believed actually existed — and in the comics, it did!

Now let's talk about the most important object in the whole city: the Trident of Neptune! It's Arthur's legendary golden weapon — forged from a special metal that never, ever rusts no matter how many centuries it spends in salt water. It's beautiful. It glows. And it has power beyond any ordinary weapon.

When Arthur holds the Trident and focuses his will — the ocean LISTENS. WHOOOOSH — he commands a wave and it rises on cue. CRASH — he calms a storm with a gesture. He can forge paths through currents that would crush a submarine. He can trigger geothermal vents deep in the seafloor. He can part walls of water. The Trident is the voice of the sea itself, and Arthur is the one it has chosen to speak through!

The Trident is also the symbol of Arthur's authority as King. When people see it, they know: the true ruler of Atlantis is here. Not because he claimed power — but because the ocean gave it to him. And a true king protects what he rules.
