When people think of the Justice League, they often picture great battles and dramatic rescues far above the ground. But Arthur Curry has a different mission. He calls it the Ocean Patrol.

Every single day, Aquaman swims the world's waters from above and below. He checks on ships that have wandered off course in a storm. He watches for waves building too large, too fast. When a coastal town is threatened, he is already there before the news has spread. He is the guardian of every beach, every bay, every stretch of open sea.

When his Justice League teammates need him, Arthur is ready. If Superman faces a problem deep underwater — pressure too great, currents too strong — Arthur is there to guide him. If Batman needs to reach a remote island without being seen, Aquaman calls on the fish and the dolphins to find the fastest, safest route. His friends in the sea know every current and every shortcut in the ocean, and they are always glad to help.

But Arthur's favorite part of the Ocean Patrol might be the quietest part: visiting coastal towns to talk to children about the sea. He shows them how to return a lost baby sea turtle safely to the waves. He explains why healthy coral reefs matter for every creature on the planet. He lifts a heavy piece of driftwood so a nesting bird can rest undisturbed. Do you know what that makes him? Not just a superhero — but a neighbor.

And that is how the Justice League Ocean Patrol showed that caring for the world, one creature at a time, is the most heroic work of all.
