Right on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean, where the land meets the sea and the waves never stop — there's a lighthouse. And growing up inside that lighthouse was a boy named Arthur Curry who was about to discover he was the most unusual person on the entire planet!

Arthur's father was Thomas Curry, a lighthouse keeper who loved the ocean more than almost anything. Gentle, kind, wise — the kind of dad who would stand with you in a rainstorm just to keep you company. But Arthur's MOTHER was someone extraordinary. She was Queen Atlanna — the Queen of Atlantis, the great hidden city deep beneath the waves! That made Arthur something the world had never quite seen before: half lighthouse keeper's son, half Atlantean royalty. Half the surface world, half the deep ocean, all at once.

From his very first swim, Arthur noticed he was different. He could dive deeper than any Olympic champion and feel completely fine. Freezing cold dark water? No problem! He could hold his breath forever — or rather, he didn't need to hold it at all because he could breathe underwater! SPLASH — down he went to the bottom of the ocean like it was a swimming pool!

And the most magical thing of all? He could hear the sea creatures. Not just hear them — TALK with them! Dolphins circled him in greeting. A blue whale rumbled a slow, warm hello. Even tiny reef fish swam in formations just for him! Here's a fun fact: Aquaman doesn't exactly speak to fish like a telephone — it's telepathy, a kind of mind-to-mind connection across the whole ocean!

Arthur put on a suit of shimmering orange scales and green leggings and took his Trident of Neptune — the legendary golden weapon of the King of Atlantis — and became AQUAMAN. The guardian of seventy percent of the Earth's surface! Can you imagine having that much ocean to look after? That's the biggest kingdom in the world — and it's all underwater!
