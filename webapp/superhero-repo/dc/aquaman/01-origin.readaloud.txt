There is a place on the coastline where the land meets the ocean, and the waves never truly stop. It was at a lighthouse on that shore that a boy named Arthur Curry grew up, learning everything from his father — a kind lighthouse keeper who loved the sea.

But Arthur's mother was someone extraordinary. She was the Queen of Atlantis, the great hidden city beneath the waves. That meant Arthur was something the world had never quite seen before: half the land and half the ocean, all at once.

From the moment he was old enough to swim, Arthur noticed he was different. He could dive deeper than any Olympic champion and still feel perfectly comfortable. The freezing cold of the deep ocean felt no worse than a gentle breeze. And most wonderfully of all, he could hear the fish. Not just hear them — talk with them. A school of dolphins would circle him in greeting. A blue whale would rumble a slow, warm hello. Even the smallest creatures of the reef listened when Arthur spoke.

When he learned that he was the rightful King of Atlantis, Arthur didn't hesitate. He put on a suit of shimmering orange scales and green leggings, took his place between the two worlds, and became Aquaman. Do you know how much of the Earth is ocean? Seventy percent — and every drop of it was under his care.

And that is how Arthur Curry became the guardian of the largest kingdom on the planet, a hero who proved that the greatest strength comes from belonging to two worlds at once.
