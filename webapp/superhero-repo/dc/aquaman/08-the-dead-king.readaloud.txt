Long, long before coral towers, long before Atlantis had glowing streets and friendly sea turtles — there was a first king. His name was Atlan. And his story ended in a rage so enormous that it pulled an entire continent beneath the ocean. The whole civilization sank because of his anger. And then Atlan was buried in the Arctic ice, sleeping through the ages, forgotten... until the day his tomb started to wake up!

Arthur traveled to the Arctic alone. The water up there is brutally cold even for an Atlantean — grinding ice floes, ancient silence, a darkness that feels very old. He found the entrance to the tomb: a towering archway of black obsidian, coated in a frost that never melts even in summer. CREAK — he pushed through the archway. Inside, the walls were covered in murals of a golden age that ended in catastrophe. And sitting at the center, on a frozen throne of kelp that hadn't moved in centuries — was Atlan.

The Dead King's eyes opened. FOOM — cold blue fire blazed in them! He rose from his throne, gripping a scepter carved from dark ancient metal. And then — WHOOOOOM — he drove his grief directly into Arthur's mind! A crushing flood of psychic visions: Atlantis sinking AGAIN. A king failing his people because he could never fully belong to either world — land OR sea!

Arthur fought back with warmth and life. He drove the golden Trident of Neptune into the ice — and LIGHT filled the chamber! "You are a ghost of a failed time!" Arthur cried. But instead of destroying Atlan, Arthur did something much harder: he channeled that ancient rage back into the earth. He let the grief dissolve instead of explode. He didn't meet anger with anger. He met it with understanding.

Arthur escaped the collapsing tomb carrying Atlan's dark scepter — a relic of a lost era — in one hand, his own golden trident in the other. Because a new king can learn from the oldest mistakes. And then choose a completely different path.
