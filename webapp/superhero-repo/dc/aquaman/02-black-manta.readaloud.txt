The ocean holds many secrets — ancient shipwrecks, glittering minerals, and technology so old it has no name. And there is one person who wants to find all of it before anyone else does. His name is Black Manta.

Black Manta has no superpowers. What he has instead is a brilliant, restless mind. He built his own high-tech diving suit entirely by himself. The suit has powerful jet thrusters so he can swim as fast as any Atlantean. It has strong mechanical arms for lifting heavy objects from the seafloor. And most striking of all, it has a large silver helmet with two glowing red eyes — eyes that can fire bright beams of energy straight through the water.

When Black Manta and Aquaman meet beneath the waves, the ocean lights up with their encounter. Manta fires his energy beams while Aquaman uses his great strength and the golden Trident of Neptune to defend himself. Sometimes Arthur calls on his friends in the sea to help — a darting school of dolphins to distract Manta's gadgets, or a great octopus to wrap around his mechanical arms. Do you know what Black Manta never quite understands? The ocean is not just a place to raid. It is a living world full of creatures who look after each other.

Arthur hopes that one day, Manta will realize his talents could do so much good if they were turned toward protecting the sea instead of plundering it. But until that day comes, the King of Atlantis will always be there, ready to defend every reef and every creature from those who take without asking.

And that is how Aquaman showed that the cleverest gadgets in the world are no match for a hero who is truly at home.
