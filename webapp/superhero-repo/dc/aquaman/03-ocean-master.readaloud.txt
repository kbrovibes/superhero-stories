Deep in Atlantis, there was another prince — and his name was Orm. He was Arthur's brother. They had the same royal blood running through them, and they both loved Atlantis with all their hearts. But they loved it in very different ways.

Arthur believed that Atlantis and the surface world needed each other. The land and the sea were one planet, after all, and the only way to keep everyone safe was to work together. Orm disagreed. He thought Atlantis should be the greatest kingdom the world had ever known — proud, powerful, and hidden away from everyone else. He called himself Ocean Master, and he wore a magnificent purple and silver suit with a long flowing cape. He carried a trident of his own, one that could command the currents and spin the sea into roaring whirlpools.

When the two brothers disagreed, they settled it the way Atlantean royals do: with tests of skill and heart. They might race through a glowing field of jellyfish, or see who could reach a stranded whale first and guide it safely home. These were not just contests of speed or strength — they were questions about what kind of king each brother wanted to be.

Do you know what Arthur never stopped believing, even in the middle of their fiercest arguments? He believed in Orm. He knew his brother was brave, that he genuinely loved his people, that underneath the proud purple cape was a good heart that had simply taken a wrong turn.

And that is how Aquaman showed that even when brothers see the world differently, love and patience are the most powerful weapons of all.
