Sometimes the most complicated rivalries aren't with strangers — they're with family. And Arthur Curry's most personal challenge comes from his own brother!

Deep in Atlantis, there is another prince. His name is Orm. He and Arthur share the same royal Atlantean blood, and they both love Atlantis with everything they have. But they love it in very, very different ways. Arthur believes Atlantis and the surface world need each other — one planet, land and sea working together. Orm disagrees. He thinks Atlantis should be the most powerful, most independent kingdom that has ever existed — proud, mighty, and completely separate from the surface world. He calls himself Ocean Master!

And Orm looks the PART of a powerful ocean ruler. He wears a magnificent purple and silver suit with a flowing cape that billows dramatically in the underwater currents. He carries a trident of his own — one that can command ocean currents and create massive spinning whirlpools that could swallow ships! WHOOOOORL! His power is genuinely impressive. He is brave, he is strong, and he is completely convinced he is right about Atlantis's future.

When these two brothers disagree, they settle it the way Atlantean royals do — through tests of skill, strength, AND wisdom. They might race each other through a glowing field of bioluminescent jellyfish WHOOSH! Or compete to see who can reach a stranded whale first and guide it safely back to deeper water. These contests ask a deeper question than who is faster — they ask what kind of KING each brother wants to be.

And here's what makes Arthur different: he never stops believing in Orm. Even in their fiercest arguments, even when Orm is being frustrating and proud and impossible — Arthur can see that his brother genuinely loves his people. That underneath the dramatic cape there is a good heart that took a wrong turn.

Family is the hardest thing to be patient with. But patience — Arthur shows — is its own kind of power.
