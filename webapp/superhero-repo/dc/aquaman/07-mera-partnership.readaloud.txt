Arthur Curry was a powerful king. But beside him sailed someone even more formidable in the deep water: Mera, Queen of Atlantis.

Mera had grown up in the distant realm of Xebel, and she carried the gift of hydrokinesis — the ability to reach out her hands and shape water itself as if it were clay. She could harden it into shields as strong as steel. She could sharpen it into shimmering spears that flew with perfect aim. She was as much a warrior as Arthur, and a sharper tactician than most generals either of them had ever met.

One day, strange whirlpools began appearing in the trade routes between the seven underwater kingdoms — precise, controlled, and dangerous. Arthur and Mera sailed together to investigate. At the center of the disturbance, a group of rogue mercenaries from a forgotten Xebel military unit burst out of hiding, wielding high-pressure sonic cannons. They aimed straight at Mera. They knew she was the real threat.

That was their mistake. While Arthur launched himself at the front line like a living torpedo, Mera turned the entire ocean into a weapon. With a sweep of her hands, she sent crystalline water-spears straight at the mercenaries' cannons. When an attack slipped past Arthur, Mera's shields were already there. When she needed cover, Arthur had already cleared the path. Do you know what their enemies never understood? They weren't fighting two people. They were fighting one.

And that is how the King and Queen of Atlantis showed the world that the truest strength is found not in one great champion, but in two hearts that move as one.
