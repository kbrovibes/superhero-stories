Deep within the archives of Atlantis, hidden behind wards of ancient sorcery, lay the history of the First King. Atlan, the Dead King, was a figure of myth and terror, a ruler whose rage had once sunk the continent and whose legacy still haunted the currents. Arthur Curry had always known that his claim to the throne was contested by the shadows of the past, but he never expected those shadows to take a physical, vengeful form. The awakening of the Dead King’s tomb had sent a shiver through the very soul of the ocean.

Arthur traveled alone to the icy reaches of the Arctic, where the tomb was said to be hidden beneath miles of shifting glaciers. The water here was biting, filled with the grinding sound of ice and the whispers of long-dead spirits. He found the entrance—a massive archway carved from black obsidian, encrusted with frost that never melted. As he stepped inside, the temperature dropped even further, and a pale, ghostly light began to emanate from the walls, revealing murals of a golden age that ended in blood.

At the center of the chamber sat a throne of frozen kelp, and upon it, the desiccated remains of Atlan. But the corpse was not still. As Arthur approached, the Dead King’s eyes flared with a cold, blue fire. He rose, gripping a scepter made of dark, unyielding metal. Atlan didn't speak with words, but with a psychic weight that threatened to crush Arthur’s mind. He saw visions of Atlantis falling again, of the surface world burning, and of a king who failed his people because of his dual heritage.

The battle that followed was unlike any Arthur had ever faced. The Dead King didn't just control water; he controlled ice and the very essence of death. Every strike from Atlan’s scepter sent a wave of numbing cold through Arthur’s limbs. Arthur fought back with his golden trident, the clash of metal ringing through the frozen halls. He had to prove that he was not just a successor by blood, but a leader by choice. He channeled the warmth of the sun and the vitality of the living sea into his weapon.

"You are a ghost of a failed era!" Arthur roared, his voice echoing off the obsidian walls. He slammed his trident into the floor, shattering the ice that Atlan was trying to weave around him. The golden light of the trident flared, pushing back the blue shadow of the Dead King. Arthur realized that Atlan’s power was fueled by his lingering resentment. To defeat him, Arthur had to show that Atlantis had moved beyond the hatred that had destroyed the First King’s world.

Arthur didn't deliver a final blow of destruction. Instead, he used the trident to ground the Dead King’s energy, returning it to the earth and the sea. The ghostly fire in Atlan’s eyes flickered and then faded, leaving only a pile of ancient bones and a silent scepter. The tomb began to crumble, the magical wards finally dissolving. Arthur grabbed the dark scepter—a relic of a bygone age—and swam out of the collapsing cavern just as the ice claimed it once more.

Returning to the surface, Arthur stood on a lone iceberg, the northern lights dancing above him. He held the scepter of the Dead King in one hand and his own trident in the other. He had faced the darkest part of his heritage and emerged with a clearer understanding of his role. He was not just the King of Atlantis; he was the shepherd of its future, tasked with ensuring that the mistakes of the past remained buried beneath the waves.
