Every ocean in the world has its own name, its own creatures, its own ancient secrets. The Atlantic! The Pacific! The Indian! The Arctic! The Antarctic! And two more besides! Together the Seven Seas cover more of the Earth than all the land put together. And the day came when Arthur Curry stood at the Great Altar of Atlantis — GLOWING bioluminescent towers rising all around him — and accepted the most important title of his life: High King of ALL of them!

But how does someone BECOME the king of seven enormous underwater kingdoms, each with their own proud traditions and their own reasons to be suspicious of someone who is also half-human? The answer is: years of swimming from kingdom to kingdom. Years of listening. Years of showing up. Xebel — the fierce warriors. The ancient scholars. The mysterious Brine dwellers. Each kingdom suspicious. Each one gradually understanding, through Arthur's actions, that cooperation is stronger than division.

At the coronation, Arthur raised the golden Trident of Neptune high and the whole city cheered! And the words he spoke echoed through the water and into Atlantean history: "The sea does not belong to the King. The King belongs to the sea." Not "I own all of this." But "I serve all of this." Do you know how rare it is for someone with that much power to say something like that? It takes real wisdom!

He made new laws protecting the coral reefs — those colorful underwater rainforests that thousands of species depend on! He signed treaties with surface nations to reduce ocean pollution. He built a multi-kingdom task force to defend against deep-sea threats. And after all the speeches, after all the ceremonies? Arthur swam out through the city and watched a child laughing in the kelp forests, playing with a curious seal.

THAT was the moment he felt most proud. Not the crown. The child. The sea: a little safer, a little fairer, for everyone who lives in it!
