• Amnesty Bay — Arthur's childhood home; a lighthouse on the Atlantic coast
• Tom Curry — Arthur's father, the lighthouse keeper who taught him integrity and love
• Arthur returns to the lighthouse whenever the burdens of the Atlantean throne grow heavy
• Memory: Tom's calm, protective love when Arthur first revealed he could speak to fish
• Tom never cared about royal bloodlines — only that Arthur was a good person
• The lighthouse is a symbol of hope for those lost at sea; Arthur sees himself the same way for his people
• Storm breaks during Arthur's visit; he sits in the rain, grounded by the familiar feeling of water
• Finds a weathered compass inside — its needle still pointing true north; a symbol of his father's values
• Lesson: you don't need a throne to be a king — integrity, instilled by those who loved you first, is enough
