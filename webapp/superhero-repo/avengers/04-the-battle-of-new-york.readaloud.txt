RIIIIIP.

That was the sound the sky made when Loki opened a portal above Stark Tower in Manhattan. Then came the noise of the Chitauri — thousands of alien soldiers pouring through on flying skimmers and enormous whale-creatures called Leviathans. Fun fact: the word "Chitauri" comes from Chi Tauri — a real binary star in the constellation Taurus. Even the alien army had a cosmic address!

Loki stood on the balcony, smiling. He thought he had already won.

He had not met Bruce Banner yet.

Captain America took command of the streets — calm voice through the comms, sending heroes exactly where they needed to be. Iron Man went streaking into the sky in red and gold, blasting skimmers left and right. Thor called down lightning from Mjolnir, turning alien soldiers into scattered sparks. Black Widow and Hawkeye fought back to back on the street below — no magic hammers, no power suits, just incredible skill and nerves of absolute steel.

Then a battered motorbike pulled up and a man climbed off. Bruce Banner looked around at the chaos, looked at his friends, and smiled a small, quiet smile. "That's my secret, Cap," he said. "I'm always angry." He took one step forward and —

SMAAAASH — the Hulk was there, a mountain of emerald muscle, and he punched a Chitauri Leviathan — a creature the size of a city bus — right in the nose with one swing. It went DOWN.

Oh my goodness. Oh my GOODNESS.

But even the Hulk couldn't fight an infinite army. Far away, the World Security Council — very scared, very bad at patience — authorized a nuclear missile aimed at Manhattan. A rogue pilot launched it. The city had minutes.

Tony Stark didn't hesitate for one second. He flew up under the missile, wrapped his arms around it, pointed himself at the portal, and flew straight through into the darkness of space — toward the Chitauri command ship far above. There was a blinding explosion.

Every Chitauri soldier on Earth dropped instantly. All of them. Because here's the thing about the Chitauri: they were all connected to their command ship like lights on a single cord. Cut the cord — flick, flick, flick — every light goes out at once.

Natasha Romanoff slammed the portal shut using Loki's own scepter.

Tony Stark tumbled back through the closing gap, suit dark, unconscious, falling from the sky. The Hulk caught him in two enormous green hands, gentle as you please.

Tony opened his eyes. The battle was over. The sky over New York City was just sky again.

THEY DID IT.
