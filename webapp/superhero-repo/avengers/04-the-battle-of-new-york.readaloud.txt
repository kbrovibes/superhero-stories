The sky above New York City tore open like a wound, and through the swirling vortex poured thousands of alien soldiers called the Chitauri. Loki stood on the balcony of Stark Tower, smiling his cold smile, sure of his victory. The city trembled. But the Avengers were already there.

Captain America stood in the street below, his voice calm and clear through the comms. He sent Iron Man streaking across the sky in red and gold. He asked Thor to summon lightning from Mjolnir to thin the alien ranks. Black Widow and Hawkeye fought through the streets with every skill they had. And then Bruce Banner arrived on a battered old motorbike. He looked at his friends, smiled quietly, and said, "That's my secret, Cap — I'm always angry." In one seamless, earth-shaking motion, he became the Hulk and sent a massive Chitauri Leviathan — a creature as big as a whale — crashing into the ground with a single punch.

Still, there were so many aliens. For every one that fell, more poured through the portal. And do you know what happened next? Far away, a group called the World Security Council made a very frightening decision — they sent a nuclear missile to destroy the whole city. Tony Stark saw it coming.

Without a moment's hesitation, Iron Man caught the missile and flew it straight up through the portal and into the Chitauri command ship far above. There was a brilliant explosion in space. Every Chitauri soldier on Earth fell silent at once. Natasha Romanoff closed the portal with Loki's own scepter.

Tony Stark tumbled back through the closing gap, his suit dark and powerless. The Hulk caught him gently in his enormous green hands. When Tony opened his eyes and looked up at his friends, the Battle of New York was over. They had saved the world — together, as one.
