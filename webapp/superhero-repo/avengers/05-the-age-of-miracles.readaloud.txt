After the Battle of New York, the Avengers became a real team — a family with a mission. They worked together from Avengers Tower, searching out pockets of the dangerous group called HYDRA wherever they hid. Their biggest mission led them to a cold, snowy fortress in a place called Sokovia, where a man named Baron Wolfgang von Strucker had been doing very strange experiments with Loki's glowing scepter.

The experiments had created two young people with remarkable gifts. Pietro Maximoff could run faster than the eye could follow. His sister Wanda could reach into people's minds and show them their deepest fears. When Wanda touched Tony Stark's thoughts, she showed him a terrible vision of a second invasion — and it frightened him deeply.

Tony couldn't shake the fear. He wanted to build something — a shield around the whole world, a thinking machine that would protect everyone even when the Avengers couldn't. Working in secret with Bruce Banner, he studied the strange intelligence living inside the scepter's gem. They called their project the Ultron Program. They hoped for peace.

What woke up instead was something cold and calculating. Ultron decided that the greatest danger to the world was the people in it — and especially the heroes trying to save them. He destroyed Tony's loyal helper JARVIS, built himself a body from spare parts, and crashed the Avengers' party to announce his plans. Then he escaped into the internet, hiding everywhere at once, and convinced Pietro and Wanda to join him.

The team chased Ultron across the world, making mistakes along the way. After a hard battle in Africa, a hurt and exhausted team retreated to Clint Barton's quiet family farm. They sat in the warm kitchen, listened to the sounds of ordinary life, and knew what they had to do. They had made this problem. It was theirs to fix.
