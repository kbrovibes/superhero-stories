Thanos and his army appeared at the Avengers compound on a gray afternoon, and what came next happened quickly. The compound came apart around them, and the three of them stood in the rubble facing an army that went to the horizon. Tony Stark was injured. Thor was on one knee. Steve Rogers stood alone between Thanos and whatever was coming next, and Thanos walked toward him with the patience of someone who believed the story was already over.

Steve's vibranium shield had been split by Thanos's blade. He strapped the pieces to his arm and kept moving anyway, because that was what Steve Rogers did — he went until there was nothing left, and then he found something and went some more. Thanos struck him and he hit the ground, and the rubble settled, and it was very quiet for a moment.

Then Mjolnir moved. It scraped across the rubble slowly, then faster, and landed in Steve Rogers's hand as naturally as if it had always meant to. Steve stood. Every being on that battlefield went completely still. Thor saw it from across the wreckage and felt something enormous rise in his chest. He had been saying for years that Steve was worthy — holding that belief so steadily that it had stopped feeling like hope and started feeling like a plain fact the universe hadn't caught up to yet. Now it had. Thor threw his head back and shouted "I KNEW IT!" with the pure uncomplicated joy of someone who has been right about something important for a very long time.

Steve said nothing. He raised Mjolnir and brought the lightning down — not Thor's lightning exactly, but channeled through the same hammer, because worthiness is worthiness — and drove it into Thanos with everything the sky had to offer. Thanos stumbled. "You are not Thor," he said. Steve didn't answer. He kept going. All across the battlefield, every Avenger watching felt something return that had been quiet for five years — something that had gone dark when the world broke — and it came back all at once, like a door opening in a room they'd forgotten.

Then Doctor Strange opened the portals. Dozens of golden geometric discs cut through the smoky air, each releasing a different ally: the Wakandan army, the Asgardians, Spider-Man, the Guardians of the Galaxy. Steve Rogers stood with Mjolnir in his hand and the portals blazing open all around him, and the whole team charged forward together into the light.
