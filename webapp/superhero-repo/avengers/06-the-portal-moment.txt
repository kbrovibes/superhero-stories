Tony Stark, Steve Rogers, and Thor stood in the ruined courtyard of the Avengers compound and faced Thanos's army alone. The compound was rubble and smoke. Thanos walked toward them with perfect patience, because patience is easy when you believe the outcome is settled. Tony activated everything he had left, Steve raised his broken shield, and Thor lifted Stormbreaker, and they went forward because going forward was the only direction any of them had ever known.

Then something came through Tony's comms. A voice, easy and familiar, from somewhere that was not this ruined place: "On your left." Sam Wilson's voice — Sam, who ran laps around the Reflecting Pool in Washington every morning and called that phrase out each time he passed Steve, because Sam was faster and liked to be cheerful about it. "On your left" had started as a tease, become a greeting, and then become something that simply meant: I'm here. Keep going. Tony heard it and went still. He had been waiting five years for this. He couldn't speak.

Doctor Strange raised his hands. The first portal opened — a golden geometric disc, turning slowly in the air — and through it came T'Challa at a dead sprint, the Dora Milaje and the Wakandan army streaming behind him. Then Strange opened another, and another, and another, dozens of them simultaneously: Spider-Man swinging through in an arc, the Guardians of the Galaxy tumbling forward, Thor's Asgardian warriors at a full run, Pepper Potts arriving in the Rescue armor she'd built in the quiet years, Wong and the Sorcerers of Kamar-Taj stepping through in their red capes.

The portals stayed open like windows. Through each one you could see somewhere that had chosen to come here — Wakanda, New Asgard, Kamar-Taj — every place that had something worth protecting, sending the people who would not stand back and watch. It was the most beautiful thing Tony had ever seen, and he had built many beautiful things.

Steve Rogers looked at all of it. He raised his shield. He had been waiting his whole long life for a moment that felt exactly like this one. He said two words — "Avengers, assemble" — quietly, the way he said everything that mattered. And they all ran. Every one of them, through every open portal, together.
