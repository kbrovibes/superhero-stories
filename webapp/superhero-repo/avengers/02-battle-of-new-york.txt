Loki arrived at Stark Tower with the Tesseract and a plan he was certain would work. He placed the glowing blue cube on the roof's reactor platform, opened a portal above the Manhattan skyline, and smiled as the Chitauri army poured through — alien soldiers on sleek craft, followed by Leviathans, serpentine creatures the size of city blocks, writhing through the sky. The city went silent for exactly one second. Then the Avengers arrived.

Tony Stark was airborne in under ninety seconds, running coordinates through his comms. His last message, directed to one specific frequency: "Hulk? Smash." Steve Rogers hit the street below at a run and didn't stop — organizing police into cordons, directing civilians to safety, moving through the chaos of Fifth Avenue like he'd been doing it his whole life, because in a way he had. Thor swept into the sky to face the Leviathans, calling lightning from the clouds with Mjolnir, driving the creatures back while thunder rolled across the island. Clint Barton perched on rooftop after rooftop and never missed once.

Then Loki made the mistake of trying to intimidate the Hulk. He stood tall and spoke at length about his godhood and his inevitable victory. The Hulk listened for four seconds. Then he grabbed Loki by the ankle and slammed him into the floor six times in quick succession, set him down in his crater, and walked away. Meanwhile, Natasha Romanoff had fought her way up through Stark Tower, found the scepter, studied it for thirty seconds, and used it to close the Chitauri portal from the inside.

But before she could, a nuclear warhead had been fired at Manhattan. Tony saw it. He said nothing on the comms — he simply intercepted it, wrapped his arms around it, and carried it through the portal into space. The portal sealed. His suit lost power in the dark out there. He fell. He came back through the last sliver of light just before it closed, and the Hulk caught him before he hit the ground.

Later, in a shawarma restaurant that had somehow survived the afternoon intact, six people in torn uniforms ate in complete silence. Thor poked at his food uncertainly. Tony had a bandage on his jaw. Steve sat straight out of habit. Five minutes passed and no one spoke. Then Clint reached for the last piece, and Tony moved his hand to let him take it, and somehow that was enough. The city was quiet outside. They had done it.
