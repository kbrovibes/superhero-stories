The battle against Thanos was fought in two places at once — far away on the dead planet Titan, and close to home in the bright fields of Wakanda.

On Titan, Tony Stark stood alongside Doctor Strange, Spider-Man, and the Guardians of the Galaxy. Doctor Strange used the Eye of Agamotto to look through fourteen million possible futures, searching for the one where the heroes won. He found it. Then Mantis climbed onto Thanos's shoulders and tried to calm the great Titan's mind, while Spider-Man's webs and Tony's armor worked to pull the golden Infinity Gauntlet from his hand. For one thrilling moment, it almost worked. But Peter Quill — Star-Lord — learned that the person he loved most, Gamora, was gone. His grief burst out of him in a single, desperate strike, and the gauntlet snapped back onto Thanos's fist. Doctor Strange traded the green Time Stone for Tony's life, and Thanos stepped through a portal toward Earth.

In Wakanda, Captain America stood beside King T'Challa, Black Widow, Bucky, Falcon, and Okoye of the Dora Milaje, facing a wave of terrifying creatures called Outriders. Then a bolt of light split the sky — Thor arrived with a brand-new weapon, the mighty axe Stormbreaker, forged in a dying star at Nidavellir. For a moment, hope was real.

But then Thanos himself walked through a portal, calm as a storm. Wanda Maximoff made the hardest choice anyone could make — she destroyed the Mind Stone herself to keep it from him. But Thanos turned back time with a twist of the Time Stone and took it anyway. Thor buried Stormbreaker in the Titan's chest. It was not enough.

Thanos snapped his fingers. And across Wakanda, across Earth, across the stars — half of all living things quietly turned to dust and drifted away on the wind.
