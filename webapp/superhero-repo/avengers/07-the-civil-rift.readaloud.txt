After everything the Avengers had been through together, you would think they could handle any problem. Giant robots? Alien armies? A mind-controlling trickster god? No problem! But here is the thing that actually broke them apart: a piece of paper.

Well — a very important piece of paper. The governments of the world had gotten together and written the Sokovia Accords — a new set of rules that said the Avengers had to answer to an international committee before taking action. Tony Stark thought that was fair. Heroes are powerful, and powerful people should be accountable. Steve Rogers thought it was dangerous — what if the committee made the wrong choice, or was too slow, or was secretly corrupt? Two good people. Two good arguments. No easy answer.

Then — BOOM — a bomb went off at a United Nations meeting, and someone very cleverly made it look like Bucky Barnes had done it. Bucky! Steve's oldest friend, once brainwashed by HYDRA into being the Winter Soldier. Steve was absolutely certain Bucky was innocent. Tony was bound by the law.

The two sides of the Avengers met at a grand airport in Germany, and oh — what a fight. Tony had called in Spider-Man, a very eager young hero from Queens who had never seen anything this big before. Black Panther had come, grieving for his father who was lost in the bombing. Two teams of heroes ran at each other in the sunshine, and it was spectacular to watch and terrible to see, because these were people who cared about each other.

But the worst moment came in a cold HYDRA bunker in Siberia, in the dark, where Tony Stark saw footage of who had really murdered his parents all those years ago. It was Bucky — under mind control, not himself, not choosing — but it didn't matter in that moment. Tony fought Steve and Bucky alone, in the dark, with all the grief and shock and fury of a son who had just lost his parents again.

Steve won. He left Tony's armor cracked and dented on the cold floor. He set his vibranium shield down beside it and walked away. He couldn't carry it as someone else's soldier anymore.

The Avengers were broken.

And far, far away — moving quietly through the dark between stars — a being called Thanos had already collected one Infinity Stone. Then two. The universe's clock was ticking, and nobody was answering the phone.
