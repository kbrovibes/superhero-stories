The celebration was very short. A version of Thanos from the past had found his way through the time portal, and his great warship, the Sanctuary II, turned the Avengers Compound into rubble with one terrible rain of fire. But out of the dust and broken stone, three figures climbed to their feet — Iron Man, Captain America, and Thor.

The three of them faced Thanos alone. He was enormous and terrifying, and he fought with a great double-edged blade. But then something happened that made even Thanos stop for a moment. Steve Rogers reached out his hand, and Mjolnir — Thor's magical hammer, which could only be lifted by the truly worthy — flew right to him. Steve Rogers, the most decent and honest person any of them had ever known, stood there with the hammer in one hand and his vibranium shield in the other, lightning cracking all around him.

It was beautiful. It was not enough. Thanos's army was already filling the sky. The heroes were three. The enemy was thousands. And then — the air began to shimmer with orange light.

Portals opened. One, two, ten, a hundred. Through them stepped everyone who had been lost. The Wakandans. The Guardians of the Galaxy. The sorcerers of Kamar-Taj. Falcon and Bucky and Wanda. Spider-Man. Even Pepper Potts in her gleaming Rescue armor. And at the head of them all, Steve Rogers lifted his hammer and said the words everyone had been waiting for: "Avengers… Assemble."

The battle that followed was enormous and fierce. Wanda Maximoff fought Thanos herself, pouring all her grief into it. Captain Marvel punched clean through Sanctuary II. And in the final moment, as Thanos held the gauntlet and declared "I am inevitable" — Tony Stark used his nanotech suit to steal the stones and snap his own fingers. "I am Iron Man," he said quietly.

Thanos and his armies turned to dust. Tony Stark gave everything. The universe was safe.
