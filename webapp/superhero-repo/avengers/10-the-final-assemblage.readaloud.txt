BOOM. BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

Thanos's massive warship — the Sanctuary II — came out of a dark portal and fired everything it had at the Avengers Compound. The whole building crumbled into rubble in about ten seconds. Dust everywhere. Silence. And then — slowly — three shapes climbed out of the wreckage.

Iron Man. Captain America. Thor.

Three people. Against Thanos. Against his double-edged blade. Against an army that was still on its way through a time portal. Not great odds, right?

But then — oh, but THEN — something happened that nobody who saw it ever forgot.

Thanos swatted Thor's hammer Mjolnir across the battlefield with a laugh. Steve Rogers watched it land. He reached out his hand. The hammer — which could only be lifted by someone truly worthy, someone honest and brave and good all the way through — flew to Steve Rogers like a golden greeting. CLANG. He caught it. Lightning crackled up his arm and across his vibranium shield.

Even Thanos looked surprised.

"I knew it," Thor said, grinning. "I KNEW it!"

They fought — three heroes against one titan — and they were extraordinary. But Thanos called his full army anyway: Chitauri, Outriders, Black Order, everything he had, a horizon-filling wall of creatures pouring through the time portal. Three was not enough.

Then the air began to shimmer with orange light.

One portal opened. Then ten. Then a hundred. Then too many to count, glowing across the destroyed battlefield like the most beautiful thing anyone had ever seen. Through them came EVERYONE. T'Challa and the Wakandan armies. The Guardians of the Galaxy. The sorcerers of Kamar-Taj. Falcon and Bucky and Wanda Maximoff and Peter Parker. Pepper Potts in shining Rescue armor. Giant-Man, enormous as a building. Every hero who had been lost — home, alive, HERE.

Steve Rogers looked at all of them, standing behind him and beside him and stretching back as far as the eye could see.

"Avengers," he said into the quiet. He raised the hammer. "ASSEMBLE."

And two armies ran at each other full speed.

Wanda Maximoff fought Thanos by herself and nearly won. Captain Marvel punched clean through Sanctuary II. Spider-Man zigzagged through the chaos protecting the gauntlet with everything he had. And in the final moment, Thanos got the gauntlet and raised his fist and said "I am inevitable" —

Nothing happened.

Tony Stark had stolen the stones. All six. Snapped to his own nanotech fingers.

He looked at Thanos with tired, quiet eyes.

"And I… am… Iron Man."

He snapped.
