What do you do when the worst thing in the world has already happened?

You keep going. You have to. Even when it hurts.

Half of all living things were gone — every planet, every star system, every family. The heroes who remained were left in a world of empty chairs and quiet rooms and playgrounds where children used to laugh. Five long years passed.

Steve Rogers ran support groups, sitting in circles with sad strangers, listening and helping. Natasha Romanoff stayed at the Avengers Compound, watching screens, coordinating the remaining heroes. She didn't sleep much.

Tony Stark did the thing nobody expected: he went to a quiet house in the woods, married Pepper Potts, and they had a daughter named Morgan. He tried very hard to enjoy the peace he had found.

Thor retreated to New Asgard — a small fishing village in Norway. Bruce Banner figured out something amazing: how to be both himself AND the Hulk at once, big and green and gentle and smart. Clint Barton, having lost his whole family in the Snap, put on a dark outfit and became the Ronin, hunting criminals across the world until the grief had somewhere to go.

And then — one ordinary day — Scott Lang fell out of the sky.

Scott had been stuck in the Quantum Realm, a tiny hidden universe-inside-the-universe, when the Snap happened. For him, only five hours had passed. For everyone else: five years. And in those five hours of drifting through the quantum dark, Scott had had an idea that changed everything.

The Quantum Realm could be used like a road through time.

They called it the Time Heist. Go back. Steal the stones before Thanos ever found them. Come home. Snap everyone back. Tony solved the hardest math problem in the history of the universe on a chalkboard while Morgan slept upstairs. Teams slipped into the past — and saw things that made their hearts ache. Thor saw his mother one last time. Tony spoke to his father, young and uncertain, on a cold 1970s airfield.

On the frozen cliffs of Vormir, Natasha and Clint fought each other — not as enemies, but because each one wanted to be the one to stay. Natasha was faster, and braver, and gone. Clint came home with the orange Soul Stone in trembling hands.

Hulk put on the new red-and-gold gauntlet and snapped his fingers. SNAP.

Across the world, the lost came home. Birds came back to empty skies. And the Avengers had exactly thirty seconds to be happy before everything got complicated again.
