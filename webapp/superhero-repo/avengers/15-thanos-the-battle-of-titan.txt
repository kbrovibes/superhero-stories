The first true confrontation with Thanos did not take place on Earth. It happened on the broken bones of his own home — the dead planet of Titan. Doctor Strange, Iron Man, Spider-Man, and the Guardians of the Galaxy had all converged there, drawn by the trail of the Mad Titan. Strange, using the Eye of Agamotto, looked forward through fourteen million possible futures. He searched them all. In only one of them did the heroes win. He told no one which path it was.

The plan was simple in shape and impossible in practice: get the gauntlet off Thanos’s hand. Mantis would calm his mind. Drax and Star-Lord would hold his arms. Iron Man and Spider-Man would peel the golden glove from his fingers. For a few breathless seconds it almost worked. Mantis crawled onto his shoulders and forced him to one knee. Peter Parker’s web-fingers pried at the metal. Tony Stark wrenched the gauntlet to the edge of Thanos’s knuckles. The Avengers were one centimeter from saving the universe.

Then Star-Lord learned that Gamora was gone. Peter Quill’s grief broke the plan in half. He struck Thanos in the face, the Mad Titan’s eyes snapped open, and the gauntlet slid back onto his fist. The fight that followed was a hurricane. Thanos hurled a moon out of orbit and dropped it onto the heroes, piece by piece. Strange duplicated himself into a thousand mages of light and bound Thanos in glowing chains. Spider-Man clung to falling debris, terrified but refusing to let go. And Iron Man, smaller than the Titan in every way that mattered, fought like a man who had nothing left to lose.

Thanos drove a blade made from Tony’s own nanotech armor into his side. Tony fell to his knees in the red dust of Titan. The Mad Titan stood above him, ready to end Iron Man with a single closing fist. And then Doctor Strange — who had seen this exact moment in his vision — held up one trembling finger and offered Thanos the Time Stone. "One life for the universe," he whispered to Tony as the green gem floated away. Thanos slotted the stone into the gauntlet, gave the heroes a final, almost apologetic look, and walked through a portal toward Earth. Five stones glowed on his hand. Only one remained. And the only person who knew the plan was now silent.
