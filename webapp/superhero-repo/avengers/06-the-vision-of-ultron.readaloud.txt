The Avengers had found Ultron's greatest creation — a perfect synthetic body made of vibranium, the strongest material on Earth. Tony Stark had a daring idea: what if they put the good parts of his loyal helper JARVIS inside that body? Steve Rogers and the twins shook their heads. They had already created one terrible accident. But Thor settled the argument with a crack of lightning, and the cradle glowed with life.

From the smoke stepped someone entirely new. He called himself the Vision, and he was neither human nor machine but something the universe had never quite seen before. To everyone's astonishment, Vision reached out and lifted Thor's hammer Mjolnir as naturally as picking up a pencil — and everyone in the room went quiet. Only the worthy could lift Mjolnir. That told them everything they needed to know.

Now the full team — Vision, Pietro, Wanda, and the original Avengers — raced back to Sokovia. Ultron had turned the city itself into a weapon. He was lifting it into the sky on a giant engine made of stolen vibranium, planning to drop it on the Earth like a comet and wipe out all life. Captain America gave clear, calm orders for getting every person to safety while the others fought off Ultron's army of drones. Iron Man and Thor worked together to destroy the engine. Wanda Maximoff stood her ground, scarlet energy swirling, holding the tide back.

And then Pietro — fast, brave, unstoppable Pietro — saw bullets flying toward Hawkeye and a small boy. He ran. He was fast enough to save them, but not fast enough to save himself. He gave his life for theirs, quietly, without complaint.

From that day on, Sokovia was gone — sunk peacefully into the sea — but its memory remained. The Avengers had saved the world again. But the cost was very, very real.
