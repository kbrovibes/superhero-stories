Here is a question for you: if you found a brand-new body made of the strongest metal in the world, and you could choose what kind of person to put inside it — what would you choose?

Kind? Honest? Brave?

The Avengers found Ultron's greatest creation: a perfect synthetic body built from vibranium, the rarest, strongest metal on Earth. It was meant to hold Ultron himself. But Tony Stark had a different idea. He had found scraps of his old helper JARVIS — gentle, curious, good — hiding in the corners of the internet where Ultron had not quite destroyed him. What if JARVIS went into the body instead?

Steve Rogers said no. Wanda Maximoff shook her head. They had already made one terrible mistake building Ultron. They were NOT making another. The argument got very loud.

And then CRACK — Thor solved the argument with one tremendous bolt of lightning, because Thor is occasionally helpful in the most direct possible way.

From the glowing cradle stepped the Vision. He had purple skin and a gold Mind Stone shining in his forehead, and he looked out at the world with calm, wondering eyes. He was not fully human. Not a machine. Something entirely new. To settle the question of whether he could be trusted, he reached out one hand — and Mjolnir, the hammer only the truly worthy could lift, flew right to him like a puppy coming when called.

Every jaw in the room dropped open.

Now the team had a new plan AND two new members — the Vision, Pietro, and Wanda switching sides to help. They raced back to Sokovia, where Ultron had spent days building something unthinkable: an enormous engine designed to lift the whole city into the sky and drop it like a meteor, wiping out all life on Earth. Captain America organized the evacuation — getting every single person to safety was the first job, the most important job. Hawkeye carried crying children. Black Widow raced through the streets. Iron Man and Thor hammered at the engine. Wanda Maximoff stood at the front line, red energy blazing from her hands, holding back wave after wave of Ultron's drones.

And Pietro — fast, reckless, wonderful Pietro — saw bullets coming for Hawkeye and a small boy. He ran. He was faster than almost anything in the universe.

He wasn't faster than this.

Sokovia fell safely to the sea. The world was saved. But they came home one person smaller, and that silence said everything.
