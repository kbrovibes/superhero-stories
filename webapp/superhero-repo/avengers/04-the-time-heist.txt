Scott Lang had found the Quantum Realm by accident and understood it only partially — which turned out to be exactly the right amount to change everything. The Quantum Realm was a subatomic dimension where time moved differently, where past and future existed like rooms in a long hallway. Scott brought the idea to the Avengers, and Tony Stark spent three days alone in his workshop before emerging with a solution, which was typical. He and Scott built quantum time tunnels using Pym Particles, and the Avengers split into teams and went back to find the Infinity Stones before Thanos ever had them.

Tony Stark and Steve Rogers traveled to 1970, to S.H.I.E.L.D.'s headquarters in New Jersey. They moved carefully through corridors full of people who didn't know who they were. Then Tony turned a corner and nearly walked into Howard Stark, carrying a briefcase and frowning at a clipboard. Howard Stark, who built extraordinary things and kept working and had a son he never quite knew how to talk to. Tony fell into step beside him. They talked about fatherhood, and work, and the strange business of loving people who aren't yet old enough to know it. Howard went one way. Tony stood in the hallway a moment before going the other.

Down the same corridor, Steve saw Peggy Carter through a glass window — at her desk, in the late afternoon light, her hair pinned up exactly as he remembered. The war was over for her. She had built a whole life on the other side of it. He kept walking, because the mission required it, and because some moments are meant to be carried rather than entered. Thor and Rocket Raccoon went to Asgard in 2013 for the Reality Stone; Thor became briefly emotional about the place, and Rocket handled it by being relentlessly practical, which worked. Natasha and Clint went to Vormir for the Soul Stone, and what happened there they each carried quietly afterward.

The team returned to the present with all six Infinity Stones and the plan ready. They gathered in the compound and looked at what they'd managed. It had worked. The next part would be harder, and they all knew it, and they stood together anyway.

Each of them had brought something back that didn't make it into any mission report. Tony folded his piece of it carefully and carried it without a word.
