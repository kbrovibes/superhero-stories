The Sokovia Accords acted as a wedge that split the Avengers down the middle. Tony Stark, burdened by guilt and a desire for accountability, supported the treaty. He believed that if the heroes didn't accept limits, they were no better than the villains they fought. Steve Rogers, however, saw the Accords as a threat to their freedom and their ability to do what was right. He couldn't trust a committee to decide when and where the Avengers should intervene, especially when the stakes were life and death. This fundamental disagreement turned friends into adversaries.

The situation escalated when the Winter Soldier, Bucky Barnes, was framed for a terrorist attack at the UN. Steve’s loyalty to his oldest friend blinded him to the political ramifications, while Tony felt compelled to bring Bucky to justice. This led to a massive confrontation at a German airport, where the two factions of Avengers clashed in a battle of ideals and power. The fight saw the introduction of new players: the young, eager Spider-Man, recruited by Stark, and the vengeful Black Panther, seeking justice for his father’s death. It was a spectacular display of abilities, but one tinged with sadness as the team tore itself apart.

The conflict reached its tragic peak in a hidden HYDRA base in Siberia. Tony discovered that Bucky, under mind control, had been the one to kill his parents. The revelation was too much for Tony to bear, and he lashed out in a raw, emotional fury. Steve was forced to fight his teammate to protect his friend, leading to a brutal, close-quarters struggle that left the Iron Man suit crippled and the Avengers’ unity in ruins. Steve walked away from the fight, leaving his shield behind—a symbolic gesture that the man who represented America no longer felt he belonged to the system.

In the wake of the civil rift, the Avengers were a team in name only. Steve and his faction became fugitives, operating in the shadows to handle global threats that the Accords-bound team couldn't reach. Tony remained at the Compound, trying to maintain the appearance of stability while secretly pining for the days of a united team. The world was safer in the short term, but the core of its defense was broken. They were unprepared for the arrival of a threat that had been moving through the shadows for years—a threat that required a level of unity they no longer possessed.

Thanos, the Mad Titan, had finally decided to take matters into his own hands. Tired of his underlings' failures, he embarked on a direct quest to collect all six Infinity Stones. His goal was simple and terrifying: to wipe out half of all life in the universe to restore "balance." He began by decimating Xandar to retrieve the Power Stone and then intercepted the Asgardian refugee ship to take the Space Stone from Loki. The first chapter of the Infinity War began with the death of a god and the destruction of a people, a grim reminder of Thanos’ power.

The warning reached Earth when Bruce Banner crashed through the roof of the Sanctum Sanctorum, gasping out the words: "Thanos is coming." Tony Stark, Doctor Strange, and the newly arrived Peter Parker faced off against Thanos’ lieutenants, the Black Order, in the streets of New York. The battle was a frantic attempt to protect the Time Stone, revealing the scale of the threat. Despite their best efforts, Strange was captured, and Stark and Parker found themselves on a one-way trip into deep space. The Avengers were separated by light-years, fighting independent battles against a common, overwhelming foe.

The coming of Thanos was the ultimate test for a fractured world. The heroes were scattered across the globe and the galaxy, each trying to hold back a tide of destruction that seemed unstoppable. Steve Rogers and his team emerged from hiding to protect Vision and the Mind Stone, leading to a desperate journey to the hidden kingdom of Wakanda. They hoped that the advanced technology of the Black Panther’s home could find a way to save Vision and destroy the stone before Thanos could claim it. The rift between the heroes still existed, but the shadow of the Titan loomed larger.
