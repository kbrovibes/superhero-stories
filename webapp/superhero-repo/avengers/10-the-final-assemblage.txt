The celebration of their success was cut short by a devastating bombardment. A past version of Thanos, having discovered the Avengers’ plan through the Nebula of the past, had followed them through the time portal. His massive warship, the Sanctuary II, unleashed a rain of fire that leveled the Avengers Compound and buried the heroes under tons of rubble. The Titan had come to reclaim the stones and, this time, to erase the universe and start anew. He was a version of Thanos who hadn't yet achieved his goal, and he was more ruthless and impatient than the one they had faced before.

Out of the ruins, the "Big Three"—Iron Man, Captain America, and Thor—rose to face the Mad Titan one last time. The battle was a brutal, three-on-one struggle that showed the sheer power of Thanos even without the stones. He fought with a massive double-edged blade, parrying Thor’s lightning and Stark’s lasers with ease. The fight reached a turning point when Steve Rogers, proving his ultimate worthiness, summoned Mjolnir to his hand. The sight of Captain America wielding the hammer and the shield in tandem was a moment of pure, heroic legend, forcing Thanos onto the defensive for the first time.

Despite Steve’s bravery, Thanos’ army began to descend upon the ruins. Thousands of Chitauri, Outriders, and members of the Black Order filled the horizon, creating a wall of darkness that seemed insurmountable. The three heroes stood alone against the tide, battered and broken but refusing to yield. Just as the end seemed near, a crackle of orange energy appeared in the air. Portals began to open across the battlefield, manifesting the hundreds of heroes who had been snapped away five years prior. The Wakandans, the Sorcerers, the Asgardians, and every Avenger who had vanished returned to the fight.

Captain America, standing at the head of the most powerful army ever assembled, uttered the two words the world had been waiting for: "Avengers... Assemble." The two armies collided in a spectacular, chaotic clash of cosmic proportions. It was a battle of scale and stakes that defied description. Giant-Man crushed Leviathans, Wanda Maximoff nearly tore Thanos apart with her grief-fueled power, and Spider-Man swung through the carnage to protect the gauntlet. The heroes moved with a desperate, unified purpose to keep the stones away from the Mad Titan.

The climax of the battle occurred when Thanos managed to reclaim the gauntlet. He prepared to snap his fingers again, but was intercepted by Captain Marvel, whose raw power matched his own. In the final struggle, Tony Stark lunged at the Titan, appearing to lose the fight. But as Thanos triumphantly declared "I am inevitable" and snapped his fingers, nothing happened. Tony had used his nanotech suit to steal the stones from the gauntlet. With the power of the universe literally at his fingertips, Tony looked at the monster who had haunted his dreams for years and whispered: "And I... am... Iron Man."

The snap of Tony’s fingers released a wave of white light that turned Thanos and his entire army to dust. The threat was gone, but the cost was absolute. The power of the stones was too much for a human body to bear, and Tony Stark succumbed to his injuries on the battlefield. Surrounded by his friends and family, the man who had started it all found his peace. The "Final Assemblage" had been a victory of the highest order, but it left the world mourning its greatest champion. The era of the original Avengers was coming to a definitive, heartbreaking close.

The aftermath of the Battle of Earth was a time of healing and transition. Steve Rogers undertook a final mission to return the Infinity Stones to their original timelines, ensuring the stability of the multiverse. However, instead of returning as the man he was, he chose to stay in the past and live the life he had always wanted with Peggy Carter. When he finally reappeared as an old man, he passed the shield to Sam Wilson, signaling the beginning of a new chapter. The original team was gone—Stark and Romanoff dead, Rogers retired, and the others finding new paths—but their legacy was eternal. The Avengers had saved the universe, and in doing so, they had become legends that would inspire generations to come.
