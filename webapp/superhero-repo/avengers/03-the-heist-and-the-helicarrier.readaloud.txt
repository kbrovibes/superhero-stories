Here is a very important thing to know about Loki, the God of Mischief: he does not need to escape a prison to cause trouble. He just needs to TALK.

Loki sat in a specially built glass cell on the Helicarrier — surrounded by clouds, surrounded by guards — and he smiled. Because he had a plan that didn't require fists or weapons or even moving. He just needed words.

"Has Stark told you about his plan?" he'd murmur. "Does Rogers know what Fury is keeping from him?" Tiny little needles of doubt, slipping between the cracks. And nearby, in a S.H.I.E.L.D. lab, his glowing scepter hummed away quietly — sending out invisible waves of anger and suspicion like a very grumpy radio station that nobody could find or turn off.

Tony Stark — who could never leave a computer system alone — had been poking around S.H.I.E.L.D.'s classified files. What he found made him furious: Nick Fury's bosses wanted to use the Tesseract to build WEAPONS. Steve Rogers was furious too — at Tony, for snooping. Soon everyone was shouting, and the scepter's hum got a little louder, and things were getting very, very bad —

BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

Three Helicarrier engines exploded at once.

Hawkeye — still under Loki's mind control — had led a squad of brainwashed agents right to the engines. The whole enormous ship lurched sideways like a startled whale. Deep in the corridors, Bruce Banner hit the floor, shook his head, felt that familiar rumble in his chest — the one that always meant trouble — and lost the fight with the Hulk.

ROOOOOOAR. Green everywhere.

Thor chased the Hulk through twisting metal hallways, grabbing at enormous green arms, keeping the rampaging monster away from the crew. Iron Man dove into a broken turbine engine — dangling in the howling wind, electricity sparking — and Steve Rogers ran up to the outside of the engine casing to redirect the airflow. Neither of them liked the other very much right then. But they worked together perfectly anyway.

Meanwhile, Natasha Romanoff found Hawkeye. Her oldest friend. The person she trusted most. She couldn't knock him out — she had to SAVE him. She knew exactly the right moment to crack his head against a pipe and jolt the mind-control loose. His eyes cleared. "Nat?" he said. He was back.

But Loki was gone. And Agent Phil Coulson — kind, steady, collector of vintage Captain America trading cards — was gone too.

Nick Fury laid Phil's blood-stained cards on the table. The room went very still. The arguing stopped.

Sometimes it takes losing someone you care about to remember what you're actually fighting for.
