High above the clouds on the great flying fortress called the Helicarrier, something sneaky was happening. Loki sat locked inside a special glass cell made to hold a god — but he was never truly trapped. With soft words and clever whispers, he planted seeds of doubt in everyone's hearts. And nearby, his glowing scepter pulsed with a quiet hum that made everyone just a little angrier than usual.

Tony Stark had been poking around SHIELD's computer systems, and what he found made him furious — Nick Fury had been keeping secrets. The arguing that followed was loud and fierce. Bruce Banner felt something dark stirring inside him. And just when things couldn't get any worse, the whole ship shuddered from a terrible explosion.

Hawkeye — still under Loki's spell — had led a team of mind-controlled agents straight to the Helicarrier's engines. In all the chaos, Bruce Banner lost control and became the Hulk, roaring through corridors and bending metal like paper. Thor tried to hold him back, and the two mighty beings battled through the ship.

Meanwhile, Tony and Steve had no choice but to work together. Tony flew straight into the heart of a broken turbine engine — a very dangerous thing indeed — while Steve gave him precise guidance from the control room. It was the first time they truly acted as one team.

Deep in the catwalks of the ship, Natasha Romanoff faced her friend Clint Barton in a quiet, heartbreaking fight. She couldn't hurt him — she had to save him. With one careful strike, she snapped him free of Loki's control. But Loki had already escaped, and kind Agent Phil Coulson had been lost.

Nick Fury placed Coulson's Captain America trading cards — worn and treasured — on the table before the heroes. Looking at those cards, the Avengers felt something shift inside them. Their friend was gone. And Loki was heading for New York.
