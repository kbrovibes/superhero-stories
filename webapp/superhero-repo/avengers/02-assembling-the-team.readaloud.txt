Picture this: a soldier from 1945. A genius in a flying armored suit. A thunder god from another realm. A scientist who turns into a giant green monster when he sneezes wrong. And two of the world's sneakiest spies. Now put them all in a room together and say, "Please cooperate!"

Ha! Good luck.

Nick Fury needed exactly this team, and he needed them FAST. First stop: a boxing gym in New York City, where Steve Rogers — Captain America — was punching bags so hard the chains rattled. Agent Phil Coulson walked in carrying a file folder and a look that said this-is-serious. "The world needs you," Phil said. Steve Rogers had never in his life said no to that sentence.

Meanwhile, Phil's teammate drove to Stark Tower, where Tony Stark was in the middle of seventeen different projects at once. Tony grumbled. Tony made jokes. Tony read the file about the stolen Tesseract — and those sharp brown eyes went very quiet and focused. Iron Man was in.

Far away in India, Natasha Romanoff tracked down Dr. Bruce Banner, who was working at a small clinic and trying extremely hard to remain calm at all times. Why? Because somewhere inside Bruce lived the Hulk — eight feet tall, emerald green, roaring with a voice like a thunderstorm — and the Hulk came out when Bruce was NOT calm. "I'll try," Bruce said carefully. Natasha hid her relief very professionally.

And then — KRAKOOM — Thor arrived. Nobody invited him. He swooped down from a stormy sky and snatched Loki right off Nick Fury's transport jet, because that is what older brothers do. Tony Stark flew after him. When these two enormous egos met in a dark forest, the trees suffered. Lightning! Repulsor blasts! An explosion that stripped bark off a hundred oaks! Then Steve Rogers arrived and bonged his vibranium shield like a giant gong — CLAAANG — and the fight stopped.

They all climbed aboard the Helicarrier — S.H.I.E.L.D.'s magnificent flying fortress, a warship the size of a small neighborhood that floated above the clouds on four great rotors. It had once briefly been mistaken for a submarine by Bruce Banner, which tells you something about how weird that day was.

Six remarkable people, all convinced they were right, all watching each other sideways.

Not a team yet. But the story was just getting started.
