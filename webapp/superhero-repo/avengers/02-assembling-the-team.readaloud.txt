With the Tesseract gone and Loki on the loose, Nick Fury knew he needed a very special team. Not soldiers. Not generals. He needed remarkable people — the kind the world had never seen all in one place.

First, he found Steve Rogers in a boxing gym, punching bags with the strength of ten men but looking a little lost in the busy, noisy world of the twenty-first century. Fury offered him not comfort, but a mission. That was exactly the kind of thing Steve Rogers could not say no to. Then Agent Phil Coulson paid a visit to Tony Stark's gleaming tower and showed him a drive full of frightening data about the Tesseract. Tony grumbled and made jokes, but the puzzle of the glowing blue cube was just too interesting to ignore.

Far away in India, Natasha Romanoff — the spy known as Black Widow — found Dr. Bruce Banner working quietly by himself. Bruce was brilliant with science, but he carried a secret: whenever he got too angry or frightened, he became the enormous, incredibly strong Hulk. Natasha spoke to him honestly and gently, and eventually he agreed to help.

And then there was Thor. The God of Thunder didn't wait to be invited. He thundered down from the sky on his own, because his brother Loki was causing mischief on Earth, and Thor intended to bring him home. His very first meeting with Iron Man and Captain America ended in a huge crash and a forest full of fallen trees. They were all very strong and very stubborn, and none of them were used to sharing the stage.

From that day on, the Helicarrier — a tremendous flying ship high above the clouds — became their meeting place. They argued, they clashed, and they drove each other a little mad. But they were together. And together, they were the only chance the world had.
