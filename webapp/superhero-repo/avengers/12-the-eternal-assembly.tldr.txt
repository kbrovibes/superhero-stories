• The Avengers story is not a fixed roster but an enduring ideal — the "Eternal Assembly"
• Original six: Steve Rogers (soldier), Tony Stark (genius), Thor (god), Bruce Banner (monster), Natasha Romanoff (spy), Clint Barton (spy/archer)
• The shield, the armor, Mjolnir — symbols of commitment passed from generation to generation
• Steve Rogers passed the shield to Sam Wilson; Tony Stark's legacy carried forward by young protégés
• Monuments in New York, Sokovia, and Wakanda mark a decade of unprecedented heroism
• A young girl in Jersey City discovers her own light; a king defends his people with new wisdom; an archer passes his bow to a new student
• Lessons of the Avengers era: victory requires sacrifice, leadership is service, strength comes from unity
• The story ends where it began — with a choice to step forward when others step back
• Nick Fury's Avengers Initiative realized a thousand times over; the assembly is a network of courage spanning the stars
• Final call: "Avengers Assemble — now and forever"
