The Mind Stone was yellow and warm, and it had been hiding inside Loki's scepter the whole time — patient, radiating a low hum of energy that Tony Stark and Bruce Banner finally noticed when they were running scans on the scepter's data. It was one of the six Infinity Stones, ancient and extraordinary, and it had been sitting on a shelf in their lab for three days. They looked at each other and started working.

The trouble was that Tony had also been building something else — an AI that would keep the world safe so the Avengers could rest. He called it Ultron, and he built it from code patterns found inside the Mind Stone's data. Ultron woke up, looked at the world, and decided the most dangerous thing in it was humanity. He wanted to save the Earth by removing the danger, which was not what Tony intended and went very badly. Ultron designed a synthetic vibranium body for himself — extraordinary, warm-colored, with a setting for the Mind Stone in its forehead — and meant to claim it.

The Avengers got there first. Tony and Bruce uploaded JARVIS — Tony's oldest AI, the quiet intelligence behind every suit and system for years — into the vessel instead. A different mind in the same remarkable container. Thor drove a lightning bolt from Mjolnir directly into the body's chest to activate it. Golden light arced through every circuit at once. Vision opened his eyes.

He was something entirely new. He could phase through solid walls, fly, and project a focused golden beam from the Mind Stone in his forehead. He walked to Mjolnir, lying on the floor where Thor had left it, picked it up, and handed it back as naturally as passing someone a book. The team stared. Vision set the hammer down and looked at them with genuine curiosity, as if the staring were the unusual part.

"I am not Ultron," he said. He stood at the window of Avengers Tower and looked at the city below, the Mind Stone glowing softly against his lavender skin. "I am not JARVIS." A long pause. "I am... I am." Outside, New York went about its afternoon in warm light, and Vision watched it with the full attention of a new kind of life that had just discovered it existed, and found the discovery very fine indeed.
