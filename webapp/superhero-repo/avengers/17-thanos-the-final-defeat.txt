Five years passed in mourning. The Avengers who remained — Captain America, Iron Man, Thor, Black Widow, Hulk, War Machine, Nebula, Rocket — became caretakers of a smaller, sadder world. Then Scott Lang climbed out of the Quantum Realm with an impossible idea: time travel through the spaces between seconds. The plan was simple to say and almost impossible to do. Borrow the Infinity Stones from the past. Bring them home. Snap everyone back. Return the stones before history noticed. The team called it the Time Heist, and it worked — but at terrible cost. On the cliffs of Vormir, Natasha Romanoff and Clint Barton fought over which of them would fall. Natasha let go, and Clint came home with the orange Soul Stone in his shaking hand.

Tony Stark and Bruce Banner built a new gauntlet, this one in red and gold, sized for a human hand. Hulk slipped it on and snapped his fingers, and across the world the dust came home. Birds returned to the sky. Cars on highways suddenly had drivers again. Peter Parker opened his eyes on Titan five years older than the day he had crumbled, and ran toward the only other living face he could see. But back at the Avengers Compound, a younger Thanos — the version from before he had ever held a single stone — had followed Nebula’s memories through the time portal. His warship, Sanctuary II, hung in the sky and turned the Compound to rubble in a single rain of fire.

What rose out of the rubble was the largest Avengers gathering in history. Doctor Strange opened a hundred glowing portals across the broken field, and through them stepped Black Panther and his Wakandan armies, the Guardians of the Galaxy, the Ravagers, the sorcerers of Kamar-Taj, Wanda Maximoff, Falcon, Bucky, Spider-Man, Pepper Potts in her Rescue armor, Scott Lang grown to giant size, and — at last — Steve Rogers lifting Mjolnir into the air, the lightning crackling along his arm. "Avengers… assemble," Captain America said. And the field charged.

The fight was a storm. Wanda lifted Thanos into the air and nearly broke him with her grief. Captain Marvel punched through Sanctuary II from below. Thanos reached for the stones one last time, and Tony Stark, bleeding and exhausted, slipped them off the Titan’s glove and onto his own armored gauntlet. Six stones glowed for the third and final time. Tony looked at Thanos with steady, tired eyes and said, "And I… am… Iron Man." He snapped his fingers. The Mad Titan and every soldier he had ever summoned crumbled to dust in the wind. The universe was, at long last, balanced — not by Thanos’s cold math, but by an Avenger’s love for it. And Tony Stark, with a small, quiet smile, closed his eyes.
