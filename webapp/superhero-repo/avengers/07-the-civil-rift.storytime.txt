Some fights are fought with punches. Some are fought with words. And some — the hardest ones — are fought inside a heart, when two good people look at the same problem and see completely different answers.

After Sokovia, the world's governments sat down and wrote a very important piece of paper. They called it the Sokovia Accords. It said: the Avengers are powerful. Too powerful to go wherever they want, whenever they want, without anyone saying yes or no. From now on, a council of nations would tell the Avengers when to act.

Tony Stark said yes.

He had seen the damage. He had felt the weight of all those buildings and all those lives, and he thought: maybe we need someone watching over us, to make sure we don't make things worse.

Steve Rogers said no.

He had lived through a time when a powerful organization — one that looked very trustworthy on the outside — turned out to be rotten at its core. He believed that when a good person saw something wrong, they couldn't wait for permission to fix it.

Two good men. Two different answers. And a piece of paper between them that felt, suddenly, like a wall.

Then something terrible happened. At a United Nations summit in Vienna, a bomb went off. And the man the security cameras caught near the bomb was Bucky Barnes — Steve's oldest friend, once the Winter Soldier, now trying to leave that darkness behind him.

Steve knew Bucky. He knew Bucky had been used, manipulated, his memories stolen by the very people who had made him a weapon. He couldn't let the world simply decide that Bucky was guilty.

So Steve ran. And Tony, who believed in the Accords, felt he had no choice but to stop him.

The airport in Leipzig, Germany became the strangest battlefield anyone had ever seen — because both sides wore hero suits, and both sides thought they were right.

On Tony's side: Black Widow, Black Panther (the king of Wakanda, T'Challa, who had lost his father in the Vienna bombing and wanted justice), War Machine, Vision, and — just recruited — a very excited teenager from Queens named Peter Parker, who swung into the fight on webs saying "Hey! You're the Avengers! Oh wow!"

On Steve's side: Sam Wilson the Falcon, Wanda Maximoff, Clint Barton, and Scott Lang — Ant-Man — who showed up and told everyone he was a huge fan of Captain America and then turned into a giant the size of a warehouse.

CRASH. BOOM. WHOOSH. ZAP.

Heroes fought heroes across tarmac and control towers and planes, pulling their punches because they didn't want to truly hurt each other, but fighting hard because each side believed they had to. It was sad and spectacular at the same time.

But the real fight was not at the airport.

The real fight was in a cold, dark HYDRA bunker in Siberia. Steve and Tony arrived there together to face a man who had arranged the whole thing — a man named Zemo, who had lost his own family in Sokovia and wanted the Avengers destroyed not from outside, but from the inside, turned against each other.

In that cold room, Tony Stark saw a piece of video footage. It showed the night his parents had died. And it showed who had been there.

Bucky Barnes. Under mind control. Not himself. But there.

Tony looked at Steve. "Did you know?"

Steve looked at the floor.

The fight that followed was not for the world. It was not for a city or a people or a great idea. It was for something smaller and more painful — grief, and trust, and the terrible distance that opens between friends when honesty comes too late.

Steve won. He left his vibranium shield behind on the cold floor.

That shield had been given to him by the government, by the institution, by the system. And maybe, he thought, it was right to leave it there. He was going to keep fighting — but on his own terms now.

The Avengers were not one team anymore.

Half were official, working with the world's governments. Half were fugitives, operating in shadows.

And somewhere far away — in the dark between galaxies — a purple shadow was watching all of this with enormous satisfaction. The heroes were divided. They were weak.

He had been waiting for exactly this.

His name was Thanos. And the next part of the story belongs to him.
