Let me tell you a secret about the Avengers.

They are not a list of names. They are not a building, or a logo, or a team with a fixed number of members. The Avengers are an IDEA — and ideas don't stop just because one story ends.

Here is how it started: six people, all completely different, who argued about everything and saved the world anyway. Steve Rogers, who grew up in Brooklyn before it was fashionable and never once stopped trying to do the right thing. Tony Stark, who wore his armor like armor and his jokes like a second armor and underneath both of those was someone who cared deeply. Thor, who came from another world entirely and learned that being a hero has nothing to do with being royalty. Bruce Banner, who was afraid of himself and chose to protect others anyway. Natasha Romanoff and Clint Barton, who had no powers at all and outran, outfought, and outthought people who did — because courage doesn't need a superpower.

These six people cracked Loki's invasion, survived the helicarrier, saved New York City, stopped Ultron from dropping a city from the sky, held on through five years of grief, traveled through time, and stood together on that final battlefield saying: not today. Not while we're here.

That should have been the end. But it wasn't, because Steve passed the vibranium shield to Sam Wilson, and Sam made it new. Tony's legacy sparked something in a clever kid from Queens. An archer found a new student to pass the bow to. In Jersey City, a girl discovered she could do something no one had ever done before. In Wakanda, a king carried forward the wisdom of everyone who came before him.

Monuments stand in New York and Sokovia and Wakanda — carved stone, no stage lights, just names. Children come and look at those names and understand something important: ordinary people chose to step forward when it was frightening, and it mattered. It always matters.

Nick Fury had a dream, once — a dream he scrawled on a card and called the Avengers Initiative. What grew from that dream turned out to be much larger than any initiative. It was a network of courage stretching from a Brooklyn boxing gym to the farthest edge of the cosmos.

And the story does not end. It just passes to whoever is ready to carry it.

Maybe it passes to you.

Avengers? ASSEMBLE. Now and forever.
