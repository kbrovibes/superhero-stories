BUZZZZZ. CRACKLE. WHOOOOOM.

Deep underground, something was waking up — and it was NOT happy about being kept in the dark.

Scientists called it the Tesseract. It was a little glowing blue cube, about the size of a grapefruit, and it had been sitting very quietly in a secret S.H.I.E.L.D. underground base for a long time. But here's the funny thing — scientists who studied it noticed it almost seemed alive. It pulsed. It listened. One researcher named Dr. Erik Selvig wrote in his notebook that the Tesseract "behaved" like it had a mind of its own. Can you imagine? A magic cube with FEELINGS?

Well. On this particular night, the Tesseract had had enough of sitting still.

WHOOOOSH — the walls cracked. The lights screamed. A spinning portal ripped itself open right there in the middle of the lab, and through it stepped a very tall figure in gleaming gold-and-green armor. He had dark hair, mischievous eyes, and a very long scepter with a jewel at the tip that glowed like a dangerous little star.

His name was Loki — prince of Asgard, God of Mischief — and he had not come to be friends.

TAP. TAP. He pressed his scepter to Dr. Selvig's chest. To Hawkeye's chest — the man whose real name was Clint Barton, the best archer S.H.I.E.L.D. had ever trained. And just like that, their eyes went glassy and faraway, like windows on a foggy morning. They belonged to Loki now.

Loki tucked the glowing Tesseract under his arm, smiled his most dangerous smile, and disappeared into the night with a whole convoy of brainwashed agents marching behind him.

Nick Fury, Director of S.H.I.E.L.D. — the man with the eye patch and exactly zero patience for nonsense — climbed out of the rubble. He dusted off his long black coat. He looked at the empty space where the Tesseract used to sit.

Then he picked up the phone and activated a plan he had been saving for exactly this kind of terrible emergency. He called it the Avengers Initiative. And the signal went out — to a snowy mountain where Steve Rogers was trying to keep busy, to a sunlit lab in Malibu where Tony Stark was rebuilding the world, to a faraway city where Natasha Romanoff was already moving.

The Tesseract was gone. And something ENORMOUS was about to begin.

Are you ready? Good. Buckle up.
