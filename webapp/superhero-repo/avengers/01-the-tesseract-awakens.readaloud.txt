Deep underground, in a secret SHIELD facility, a strange thing began to happen. A glowing blue cube called the Tesseract — one of the most powerful objects in the universe — started to hum and glow all on its own. Dr. Erik Selvig watched with wide eyes as the light grew brighter and brighter. And then Nick Fury, the director of SHIELD, arrived to see something no one was quite ready for.

A beam of blue light shot out of the cube and tore a hole right through the air — a doorway between worlds. And do you know what stepped through? A tall figure in glittering gold and green armor, carrying a glowing scepter. His name was Loki, the trickster god of Asgard. He had a sneer on his face and a plan in his heart, and neither one was very kind.

With a touch of his scepter, Loki turned Hawkeye — whose real name was Clint Barton — and Dr. Selvig into his loyal helpers. Their eyes clouded over like windows on a rainy day, and just like that, they belonged to Loki. Nick Fury tried to stop him, but Loki was too quick, too powerful, and too clever. He took the Tesseract and slipped away into the night with a convoy of SHIELD vehicles behind him.

Nick Fury climbed out of the rubble, dusty and bruised, watching the helicopters disappear. He knew that no ordinary army could stop what was coming. So he reached for an old communication device and sent out a very special signal — a call that traveled to a snowy mountain cabin where Steve Rogers felt the weight of his shield again, to a bustling city in Russia where Natasha Romanoff stood up from her work, and all the way to a gleaming tower in Malibu where Tony Stark saw danger approaching on his screens.

And that is how it began — with a stolen blue cube and a signal that changed everything. The Tesseract had awakened, and with it, the legend of the Avengers was about to be born.
