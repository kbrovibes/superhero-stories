Imagine a school where your superpower is your best subject. Does that sound amazing? Because it IS amazing — and it's called the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters!

The school sits in Westchester County, New York — at the end of a long gravel drive, behind an iron gate, in a big beautiful brick manor house covered in climbing ivy. From the road it looks totally normal. Inside? Totally NOT normal.

Professor Charles Xavier built the school because he had a big dream: give young mutants a safe place where their powers aren't something to be afraid of, but something to understand and grow. Xavier is a telepath — he can sense thoughts — and he spent years quietly finding young people who needed exactly this.

Scott Summers arrived first. First student, ever. And he took that very seriously.

He helped Xavier design the curriculum. He helped set up the DANGER ROOM — an underground training facility that could simulate almost any scenario you can imagine. Fighting robots? WHAM! Crumbling buildings? CRASH! Raging river? SPLASH! The Danger Room threw it all at the students, and the students got better, mission by mission.

Scott ran the sessions. And running them taught him the most important leadership lesson of his life: build your plan around YOUR PEOPLE, not around what you want to do.

He tried it wrong once. He assigned positions based on the scenario, not on who was best suited. BOOM — it went badly. He filed that lesson and never made the same mistake again.

Outside the training room, the Xavier School was wonderfully alive. Want to know what breakfast sounded like? Hank McCoy — enormous, brilliant, blue and furry — arguing about comparative literature between bites of toast. Bobby Drake — whose whole power is making ice — leaving ICE SCULPTURES in the hallway that melted by noon. And Ororo Munroe, whose power is the weather, tending a garden that was more beautiful than any garden in Westchester because the sun was always exactly right and the rain only fell when the flowers needed it.

Scott sometimes walked the grounds at dusk, just listening. The voices. The laughter. The sound of young people figuring out who they were, right here, surrounded by people who understood.

He understood, then, exactly what Xavier had built.

Not just a training facility. Not just a team. A home. A community. A place where the most extraordinary thing you could do was be yourself — all the way.

ZZZAP! Now THAT'S a school worth going to.
