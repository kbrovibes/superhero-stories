Are you ready? Because this is the story of a boy who had to be brave before he even knew what brave meant — and who grew up to lead the greatest team of heroes the world has ever seen!

His name is Scott Summers. And he grew up in Alaska, which is already pretty cool — think snow-capped mountains and wide open sky. His dad was a pilot, and Scott loved everything about planes: the roar of the engines, the way they punched through clouds. Scott wanted to fly someday too.

Then one night — a dark, scary night — something went very wrong with their plane. The engines sputtered and quit. Scott's parents made the bravest decision you could imagine. They strapped one parachute around both boys — Scott and his little brother Alex — and pushed them safely out. Christopher and Katherine were gone. Just like that.

WHOMP. The boys landed. But they were alone.

Scott ended up in an orphanage in Omaha, Nebraska. Far from Alaska. Far from Alex. He kept to himself, trying to be steady, even when steady was hard.

And then — one afternoon — his head pounded like a drum, and KRAKKOOM! Bright red beams blasted right out of his eyes! He couldn't turn them off. They smashed through walls like a wrecking ball.

Scott was scared. Wouldn't you be?

But then he discovered something. Special lenses made of ruby-quartz — a rare red mineral — could hold the beams back. With those lenses built into a visor, Scott could finally open his eyes safely. CLICK. The world snapped back into focus.

Not long after, a man in a wheelchair came looking for him. Professor Charles Xavier. He ran a school in Westchester, New York — the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters — for young people exactly like Scott. People whose bodies had changed in amazing ways. Xavier called them mutants.

"You can learn to control this," Xavier told Scott. "And you can use it to help people."

Scott packed his bag.

At the Xavier School, he trained in the Danger Room — BOOM, CRACK, KRAKK — blasting targets, bouncing shots off walls, threading beams through tiny gaps. He got better every single day.

And Professor Xavier looked at Scott one morning and said, "I need you to lead the X-Men."

LEAD them. Not just join. LEAD.

Scott Summers stood a little taller. He put on his ruby-quartz visor. He looked at his teammates — Jean Grey, Hank McCoy, Bobby Drake, Warren Worthington — and he was ready.

His name was Cyclops now. And the X-Men were ready to change the world.
