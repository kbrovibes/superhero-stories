Did you know that the most important thing Scott Summers owns isn't a weapon? It's a pair of special red lenses. Let me tell you everything about them.

Scott's eyes fire optic blasts. ZZZAP! That's incredible, right? But here's the thing — he can't turn them off. His eyes don't have an off switch. Without protection, those beams fire constantly. He can't even open his eyes without blasting whatever is in front of him.

That's a problem! You can't exactly eat breakfast like that.

The solution is ruby quartz. Ruby quartz is a rare, beautiful crystal — deep red, like looking through a sunset — and it has a very special property: it ABSORBS concussive energy instead of letting it through. The blasts build up in the crystal and stay there. Scott can open his eyes, look around, smile at his friends, walk through a door — all without demolishing anything.

Professor Xavier found a deposit of ruby quartz in the mountains of Madripoor and brought it to a craftsman named Henry Larner. Larner spent THREE WHOLE WEEKS carefully grinding and shaping and calibrating the lenses until they fit Scott's eyes exactly. The result? A sleek curved visor in deep red crystal and polished steel. PERFECT.

Now here is the coolest part. The visor has a control slider on the right temple. Are you ready?

Slider ALL the way DOWN — CLICK — the beam narrows to a pencil-thin line. Super precise! Perfect for picking a lock or stopping one falling object. SHINK!

Slider ALL the way UP — WHOOOOSH — the beam spreads wide, a big fan of force. Good for pushing back a crowd or stopping something enormous. KRAKOOOM!

Right in the MIDDLE is where Scott uses it most — a fist-width beam, powerful and aimed. That's his go-to. BOOM!

Jean Grey once asked Scott what the world looks like through red lenses all the time.

He thought about it.

"Warm," he said.

Everything through ruby quartz is amber and rose and deep red — like sunsets all day long. Scott has never seen the color blue. Jean described it for him: "Cool and wide. Like taking a long slow breath." Scott smiled and said that sounded really nice.

He meant it. No sadness. Because the red-tinged world has always been HIS world — and he finds it beautiful.

First thing every morning: CLICK. Visor on.

Last thing every night: he sets it down carefully on the nightstand.

Not a weapon. A discipline. A choice. Every single day.

And THAT is what makes Cyclops extraordinary.
