Of all the enemies the X-Men have faced, few are as calculating or as unsettling as Mister Sinister. His real name is Nathaniel Essex, a scientist who lived in London more than a hundred years ago and became obsessed with the idea that some people were destined to evolve extraordinary abilities. He studied genetics long before anyone else understood what genes were, and he made dark bargains that transformed his own body into something nearly indestructible. By the time Cyclops was born, Mister Sinister had already been watching the Summers family for years — because something in Scott Summers's genes, and in the genes of the woman Scott would one day love, fascinated him completely.

Sinister's scheme began quietly. He had agents watching the Xavier School, and he arranged for a series of powerful Mutant-Hunting robots called Sentinels to be deployed against the X-Men, hoping to capture and study their genetic material. When Cyclops and Jean Grey fought their way through the attack, Sinister took careful notes. He sent his own team — the Marauders — to intercept the X-Men near the tunnels beneath New York City, known as the Morlock Tunnels. Scott led his teammates through the dark underground passages, calling out directions, keeping everyone moving. When one of the Marauders hit him with a disorienting pulse weapon, he shook it off and kept going.

The confrontation with Mister Sinister himself came in an abandoned laboratory. Sinister stood at the far end of a long room, perfectly still, wearing a dark costume with a diamond mark on his forehead — silver-pale, unnaturally calm. He told Cyclops plainly that he had been watching him since birth, that his optic blasts were among the most powerful ever recorded, and that he intended to keep watching. Cyclops raised his visor and fired — a full-force optic blast that would have knocked most opponents through three walls. Sinister simply absorbed it and smiled.

Scott did not flinch. He had already sent a signal to Jean Grey, who struck Sinister with a powerful telekinetic wave from behind, sending him crashing through the lab wall. The team escaped together through the tunnels, regrouping in the winter air above the city. Sinister had vanished before they could press the advantage.

That night, back at the Xavier School, Scott sat alone in the Danger Room long after the others had gone to bed. He ran the encounter again and again in his mind. Sinister had said he had been watching the Summers family for years. Scott didn't know exactly what that meant yet. But he knew he would find out — and when he did, he would be ready. He always prepared. That was what leaders did.
