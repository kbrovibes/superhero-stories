Okay. Deep breath. Because this villain is BIG. Like, really, enormously, ancient-as-history BIG.

His name is Apocalypse, and his real name — En Sabah Nur — is so old it comes from a language spoken thousands of years ago in ancient Egypt. He was alive when the Pharaohs ruled! He has had more time than almost anyone to practice being powerful, and he used every single second of it.

Apocalypse believes something very wrong: that only the strongest people deserve to survive. He doesn't just want to cause trouble — he wants to REMAKE the whole world according to his rules. And Cyclops? Cyclops will not let that happen.

Apocalypse sent his Four Horsemen — four powerful mutants he had enhanced with his ancient technology — CRASH, THOOM, CRACKLE! — smashing across the rooftops of Manhattan! People below ran for cover. Buildings shook. Car alarms went off everywhere.

Cyclops had spent days studying everything the X-Men knew about Apocalypse: his armor, his tactics, the way he moved. Now he stood on the rooftop and called his team. WOLVERINE — go left! JEAN GREY — cover from above! STORM — pull the weather in from the harbor! His voice was clear and steady, even when the Horsemen came at them from every direction.

KRAKKOOM! Red beams cracked through the air. One Horseman stumbled. Then another. Then a third. Cyclops fired only when it mattered. No wasted shots. Every beam right where it needed to go.

And then — WHOOOOOM — Apocalypse appeared.

He was enormous. Blue-skinned, dressed in ancient armor that looked like it had been worn for centuries. He looked down at Cyclops the way a mountain looks at a pebble.

"You lead with your mind," Apocalypse said. "Wisdom or weakness — I intend to find out which."

He swung his armored arm — CRUNCH! — and knocked three X-Men flying!

But Cyclops never panicked. He fired a full sustained blast — KRAKOOOM — right at Apocalypse's chest! It bought one precious second — just enough time.

"NOW," Scott said quietly into his earpiece.

Jean Grey reached out with her mind. SLAM! Telekinetic force hammered Apocalypse from the side.

Storm raised her arms, and a CRACK of lightning from the harbor clouds hit him dead center — KABOOM!

Apocalypse staggered. His enormous transport shuddered. He retreated into the clouds.

The city was safe. Every single X-Man made it home. Scott sat quietly on the rooftop, looking out at the harbor. He counted his team — one, two, three, four, five — all safe. All there.

They had won together. That was the only way you ever beat someone like Apocalypse. Not alone. Together, with a plan, and people who trusted each other.

ZZZAP! That's how a team defeats an ancient villain!
