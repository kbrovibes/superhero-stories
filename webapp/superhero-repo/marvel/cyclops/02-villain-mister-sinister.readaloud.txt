Okay, listen close — because this villain is one of the creepiest, most patient enemies the X-Men have ever faced. And his name is Mister Sinister.

His REAL name is Nathaniel Essex, and he has been around for a very, very long time — over a hundred years! He studied mutant genes back when no one even had a word for mutants. And he has been obsessing over the Summers family — Cyclops's family — practically since before Scott was even born. Creepy, right?

Sinister's plan was sneaky and patient. He sent enormous robot hunters called SENTINELS — CLANG CLANG STOMP STOMP — crashing through the streets of Manhattan, going after the X-Men. He wanted their DNA. He wanted to run his experiments. He figured the X-Men would be too busy fighting giant robots to notice who was really pulling the strings.

He was wrong.

Cyclops spotted the pattern — the Sentinels were herding the X-Men toward the Morlock Tunnels, the old underground passages beneath New York City. He called his team together and said: "Someone planned this. Let's follow the trail."

Down they went. Into the spooky underground passages, dodging Sinister's hired fighters — the Marauders — at every corner. CRACK! WHOOSH! BOOM! Beams and blasts echoed off the stone walls. Cyclops moved his team through every ambush, calm and precise.

And then — at the end of a long, dim lab — there he was.

Mister Sinister. Silver-pale skin, black costume, and a diamond-shaped mark glowing bright on his forehead. He barely moved. He just watched Scott with cold, calculating eyes like someone who had been waiting a very long time.

"I have studied you since the day you were born," Sinister said.

Scott didn't blink. He reached up to his visor, adjusted the aperture wide, and let loose a FULL BLAST — KRAKOOOM — the most powerful optic beam he could fire, aimed straight at the center of Sinister's chest!

Sinister absorbed it. All of it. And smiled.

For one second, Scott's jaw tightened.

But here is the thing about Cyclops. He never, ever goes in without a plan.

He had already sent a signal to Jean Grey. And Jean — with her incredible telekinetic power — reached out with her mind and HURLED Sinister through the wall behind him. CRASH! Stone and dust exploded everywhere.

The X-Men bolted. They made it out into the cold winter air above the city, every single one of them safe.

Sinister had vanished. He always vanishes. That's what makes him so dangerous — he is patient, and he is always watching.

That night, Scott stayed up late in the Danger Room, running through every clue. Sinister had been studying his family for a hundred years. Scott didn't know exactly why yet.

But he would find out. Cyclops always finds out.
