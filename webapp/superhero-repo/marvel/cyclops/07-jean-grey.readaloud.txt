Okay, I have to tell you something. Scott Summers is brave and steady and precise and smart. He faces Apocalypse without blinking. He stands up to Magneto alone.

But there is ONE thing that makes Scott Summers go completely quiet.

Her name is Jean Grey.

Jean has been at the Xavier School almost as long as Scott. She is a telepath — she can sense thoughts — AND a telekinetic, meaning she can move objects with her mind. LIFT! PUSH! HOLD! She is funny and warm and her instincts in the Danger Room are excellent.

Scott noticed her the moment she arrived. And then he spent SIX WHOLE MONTHS being totally quiet around her because he thought she was completely out of his league.

Here is the thing: Jean is a TELEPATH. She absolutely knew.

She started sitting next to him at training sessions and asking him questions about the scenarios he designed. Why this obstacle here? Why these two teams and not three? Why change the weather setting in the third phase? Scott, who loved thinking about these things, gave careful, thorough answers. The answers got longer. The conversations moved away from training. They talked about everything.

What Jean understood that hardly anyone else did — Wolverine just raised an eyebrow, Hank called Scott "meticulous" — was that Scott's discipline was REAL seriousness, not stuffiness. She saw the difference between being careful because you care and being careful because you're scared. She never mixed the two up.

In the field, they were INCREDIBLE together. Jean's telekinesis could hold a position Scott's beam couldn't reach. Scott's optic blasts could crack open angles Jean needed for a big telekinetic lift. BOOM — angle opens — WHOOOOSH — Jean moves something enormous. They practiced combinations in the Danger Room for months until the rhythm was completely automatic.

Bobby Drake once accidentally took a photograph of them at the edge of the school lake. He was messing with the camera and hit the shutter at exactly the wrong — or maybe exactly the RIGHT — moment.

The photo shows Scott LAUGHING. Actually, fully laughing. His visor pushed up slightly the way it goes when his face moves unexpectedly. Jean is looking at him — not at the camera, just at him — with an expression that is hard to describe but very easy to understand.

Bobby was very embarrassed about the accidental photo.

Scott put it on his desk the next day and it has been there ever since.

ZZZAP! Even the most focused leader in the X-Men needs someone who sees them exactly as they are.
