The oldest and most powerful enemy the X-Men have ever faced is a mutant so ancient that history barely remembers his true beginnings. He calls himself Apocalypse, and his real name — En Sabah Nur — means The First One in an old language older than most nations. He was born thousands of years ago in ancient Egypt, and in all that time he had grown to believe one terrible idea: that only the strongest deserve to survive. He did not want to destroy the world. He wanted to remake it, and Cyclops stood directly in his way.

Apocalypse had spent years collecting and enhancing powerful mutants, turning them into his Four Horsemen — warriors bound to his service and amplified by his technology. When he set his sights on New York City, he moved with devastating confidence, sending his Horsemen ahead of him to weaken any resistance. Cyclops studied everything the X-Men could learn about him before the battle began — his Egyptian armor, his vast physical strength, the way he could alter the size and shape of his own body. A plan came together slowly, piece by careful piece.

The X-Men met Apocalypse's Horsemen above the rooftops of Manhattan in a battle that shook buildings and scattered civilians to safety below. Cyclops directed his teammates with short, clear commands — Wolverine to the left, Jean Grey overhead, Storm pulling weather in from the harbor — while he himself moved through the chaos, sending precise optic blasts to disrupt the Horsemen's coordination. He did not waste shots. Every beam had a purpose.

Apocalypse appeared above the battle on his enormous armored transport, and the sight of him — twelve feet tall, blue-skinned, ancient — was something designed to make people feel small. He looked down at Cyclops with something like interest. "You lead with your mind," Apocalypse said. "That is either wisdom or weakness. I intend to find out which." He swept a massive armored arm across the rooftop, scattering three X-Men at once.

Cyclops fired a sustained blast directly into Apocalypse's chest, buying seconds for his team to recover. Together, Jean Grey hammered him with telekinetic force while Storm summoned a direct lightning strike from the harbor clouds. Apocalypse staggered — not defeated, but forced to retreat his transport before the combined assault brought it down. As the dust settled across the rooftop, Scott pulled off his visor and pressed the bridge of his nose in the quiet that followed. Apocalypse was gone for now. The city was safe. And the X-Men, every one of them, had made it home.
