Okay, picture this: the most precise, plan-every-detail leader in the X-Men — and the most instinct-driven, no-plan-needed fighter in the X-Men — on the same team. Together. Forever.

That is Scott Summers and Wolverine, and it is MAGNIFICENT.

Logan — Wolverine — arrived at the Xavier School without much warning and with a very firm opinion about everything. He has been alive an extremely long time, has a skeleton made of unbreakable adamantium, and has three long retractable claws on each hand. SNIKT! He operates entirely on instinct. He does not like briefings. He definitely does not like long briefings.

And Scott Summers gives VERY thorough briefings.

"Summers," Logan says, with that particular emphasis.
"Logan," Scott says, with no particular emphasis whatsoever.

They disagree on almost everything tactical. Logan: move fast, react fast, adjust on the go. Scott: know the terrain — every room, every exit — before you put a single foot inside. Logan thinks Scott's plans are twelve steps too long. Scott thinks Logan's approach will eventually get someone hurt. They argue about this in training rooms, briefing chambers, and hallways. The other X-Men have learned to find somewhere else to be for the first ten minutes.

BUT — underneath all the arguing is a genuine, stubborn, deep respect.

Proof? Here it is.

Mission: Weapon X facility in Alberta, Canada. The X-Men are extracting three young mutants held there against their will. Things go fine until — CLANG CLANG CLANG — the automated corridor doors start sealing ahead of schedule! Scott and Logan are deep in the building with two young mutants, and the last exit door is slamming shut. Thirty seconds.

Logan looks at the locking mechanism — a ten-centimeter pin, sixty meters away, in a corridor filling with steam from a burst coolant pipe.

He looks at Scott.

"Tell me you can hit that," Logan says.

Scott narrows his visor all the way down to minimum aperture. Ten-centimeter target. Sixty meters. Steam in the air. He takes one breath.

CRACK.

The pin shears clean. The door stays open.

Logan tucks one kid under each arm — just GRABS them — and SPRINTS. Scott fires twice more at the automated sentinels behind them. BOOM. BOOM. They burst through the door into the cold Alberta air.

Four seconds to spare.

Logan sets the kids down. He looks at Scott.

"Good shot," he says.

From Wolverine — a man who does not hand out compliments — those two words were a medal.

Scott said: "Good run."

Logan grunted and walked toward the jet.

End of conversation. Beginning of everything.

SNIKT! ZZZAP! Best rivals in the X-Men, every single time.
