Okay, this story is different from all the others. Are you ready? Because in this story, Cyclops doesn't throw a punch. He doesn't fire a single blast. He does something much, much harder.

He listens.

The villain is Magneto — real name Erik Lehnsherr. He can control metal — ALL metal, every bolt and beam and wire and rail in the world. He had a hard life, full of loss and fear, and it made him believe that mutants could only be safe if humans were afraid of them. He built a dome — a massive solid-steel dome — over the entire island of Genosha, sealing everyone inside.

That's a lot of power. That's a frightening amount of power.

Most heroes would arrive with a battle plan: blast the dome, fight through the guards, confront Magneto head-on. BOOM CRASH WHAM.

Scott did not do that.

He landed on top of the dome ALONE, set down his communication device where Magneto could see it — OUT IN THE OPEN, in plain sight — and he waited. No weapons. No tricks. Just Scott Summers standing on a steel dome in the quiet, waiting.

After a long silence, a seam of metal opened in the dome. Scott dropped inside.

It was dim in there. Blue-white energy crackled along the walls. Magneto stood in the center, his deep-crimson helmet catching the light, his cape drifting in the magnetic field around him. He looked at Scott.

"You came alone," Magneto said.

"I came to listen," Scott said.

And he did. For two whole hours, Scott listened to Erik Lehnsherr — the losses, the years alone, the fear that had hardened into all of this. Scott did not agree with the dome. He said so, very clearly, looking Magneto right in the visor. But THEN he said something that stopped Magneto completely cold:

"Professor Xavier lost his closest friend when he lost you. He has never stopped believing you could come back."

Magneto was very quiet.

The magnetic field around him — which had been humming with tremendous power — surged ONCE — WHOMP — then went perfectly still.

And then Magneto lowered the dome.

Not because Scott beat him. Because Scott SAW him.

CREEEEAK — the steel dome folded back and the sunlight poured in. The people of Genosha walked out, blinking, into the open air. Scott stood beside Magneto and watched them go.

Magneto said: "You have Xavier's stubbornness."

Scott said: "I'll take that."

No blast needed. No battle won. Just a conversation — and a whole island set free.

THAT is what it means to be a true leader.
