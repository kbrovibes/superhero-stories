• The Swordsman: flamboyant circus master and Clint's second major mentor; master of every bladed weapon
• Taught Clint wider combat, showmanship, grace, and timing — a fight as a performance
• Clint admired the Swordsman's skill but was increasingly repulsed by his criminal inclinations
• Breaking point: Clint discovered the Swordsman committing a robbery and refused to stay silent
• Brutal confrontation when Clint tried to stop him — nearly cost Clint his life; solidified his resolve to be a man of integrity
• Years later, paths crossed again when Clint was an Avenger — the Swordsman vacillated between villain and seeking redemption
• These encounters forced Clint to confront his origins and the nature of forgiveness
• The Swordsman's influence: Clint became an expert in hand-to-hand combat and melee weapons beyond archery
• The emphasis on flair translated into Clint's effective, unpredictable battlefield demeanor
• Lesson: talent without character is dangerous — true mastery is guided by the heart that wields it
