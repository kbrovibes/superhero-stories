Friends, I want to tell you about a very dark and very important chapter in the life of Clint Barton. A time when the world cracked in half — and Hawkeye nearly cracked with it.

It began with a terrible thing called the Snap. The villain Thanos gathered all six Infinity Stones and used their power to make half of all living things simply vanish. Clint was standing in a field with his whole family — his wife Laura, his children — and then, in one silent, unbelievable moment, they were gone. All of them. Just gone.

Can you imagine that? The thing that made Clint brave was the people he was protecting. And now they were gone.

Clint put down his bow. He picked up a blade. He put on a mask and black armor and stopped calling himself Hawkeye. He became Ronin — a word that means a warrior who belongs to no one, who follows no rules. He traveled the world, going to places like Tokyo where criminal organizations had survived the Snap while innocent people vanished. He dismantled gang after gang, moving through the shadows like a ghost.

For FIVE YEARS, the name Ronin meant something cold and frightening in the world's darkest corners. But Clint wasn't getting better. He was getting hollower.

And then Natasha Romanoff found him. His oldest friend — the Black Widow, who had known him since their S.H.I.E.L.D. days. She tracked him all the way to Tokyo, stood right in front of him in the rain, and told him something astonishing. She said: we found a way to bring them back. All of them.

Clint almost didn't believe her. But somewhere deep inside the Ronin mask, Hawkeye was still there — waiting. He put the blade down. He came home.

The story of Vormir — the distant, haunting planet where the Soul Stone waited — is one of the saddest and bravest chapters of all. Natasha gave everything so that Clint could return to his family.

He has never forgotten it. He never will. And that is why, when Hawkeye fights today, he fights with something extra fierce in his heart.
