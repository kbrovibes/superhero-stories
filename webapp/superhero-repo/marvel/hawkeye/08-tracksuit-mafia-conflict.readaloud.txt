Not every adventure takes place on a distant planet or in the middle of a giant alien invasion. Some of the most complicated ones happen right on your own street — maybe even outside your own apartment building. And that is exactly how things went for Hawkeye one very eventful holiday season in New York City.

It all started at an auction. Someone was selling a suit — the Ronin suit, the dark armor from Clint's most difficult years, the costume he never wanted to see again. And a criminal gang called the Tracksuit Mafia showed up wanting it badly. These were not robots or alien warriors. They were a persistent, loud, tracksuit-wearing group of troublemakers who said "Bro" after absolutely every sentence — which would almost be funny if they weren't so determined!

Their connections, though, were anything but funny. The Tracksuit Mafia answered to some very dangerous people, including a fearsome villain known as the Kingpin — one of the most powerful criminal bosses in all of New York City.

The gang started showing up everywhere. Outside Clint's apartment building. On the street. In parking garages. ALWAYS in matching tracksuits. And Clint, who had been trying to have a quiet holiday with his family, suddenly found himself outnumbered again and again.

But here's the thing — he wasn't alone! Kate Bishop was right there beside him, and the two Hawkeyes working together were a remarkable team. TWANG — trick arrow! WHOOSH — grappling line across the rooftops! They moved through the city like two notes in perfect harmony.

And then there was Lucky. Oh, we must talk about Lucky! Lucky was a golden dog who had belonged to the Tracksuit Mafia, until he showed Clint some unexpected kindness in the middle of a fight. Clint rushed the injured dog to an emergency vet, paid every bill, and Lucky — now known as Lucky the Pizza Dog — became part of the team. Because Lucky LOVES pizza, which is an extremely good quality in a friend.

The battles moved from cramped hallways to the sparkling lights of Rockefeller Center. It was complicated, it was exhausting, and it required Clint to confront what his Ronin years had set into motion. But he held on — and he got home.

Protecting your neighborhood matters just as much as saving the world.
