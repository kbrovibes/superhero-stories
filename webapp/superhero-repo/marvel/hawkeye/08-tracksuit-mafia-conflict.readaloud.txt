Not every battle Clint Barton fights involves alien armies or world-ending threats. Some of them happen on the streets of his own city, in cramped apartment hallways and on crowded holiday sidewalks. And those battles, it turns out, can be just as complicated.

It started with an auction and a suit — the Ronin suit, the dark armor Clint had worn during the worst years of his life. The Tracksuit Mafia wanted it, because that suit was connected to things in the past that powerful people wanted kept quiet. Their leader, Maya Lopez, was tough and determined, and her gang was relentless. They kept appearing — around every corner, outside his building, always saying "Bro" to each other in a way that might have been funny if they had not been so very persistent about trying to cause trouble.

Clint was outnumbered again and again. But he had Kate Bishop by his side now, and together they were a formidable pair — trick arrows zipping through the air, the two Hawkeyes keeping each other safe and moving.

The battles grew more serious. The Tracksuits were connected to much bigger and more dangerous people: the assassin Yelena Belova, and behind it all, the powerful criminal called the Kingpin. The situation Clint had walked into was larger and darker than it first appeared.

But Clint held on. He protected his neighborhood. He protected Kate. And when the moment came, he chose the harder path — finding a way through rather than simply through fighting — and got home to his family.

From that day on, Clint knew the truth all great heroes eventually learn: every neighborhood deserves a protector who notices and who cares.
