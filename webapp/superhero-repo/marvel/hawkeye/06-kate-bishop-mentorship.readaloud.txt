Kate Bishop had admired Clint Barton ever since she was small. She had seen him fight, all those years ago during one of the Avengers' great battles, and something had settled into her heart: I want to do that. Not the powers — just the pure, precise courage of a person with a bow who refused to give up.

So Kate trained. And she got good. Very good. And then, in the middle of a very complicated situation involving criminals and an auction for a mysterious suit, she and Clint found themselves side by side whether either of them had planned it or not.

Clint looked at her and saw something familiar. The stubbornness. The refusal to walk away when things got dangerous. The sharp, quick eyes that saw exactly where an arrow needed to go. He sighed, and then he started teaching.

He showed her how to choose trick arrows in the dark at full speed. He taught her strategy and patience. He was honest with her about the hard parts of the job — the exhaustion, the close calls, the choices that keep you up at night. He told her about Ronin, the dark chapter of his life that he was not proud of, because he wanted her to go in with clear eyes.

Kate pushed back on him just as hard. She made him remember why he loved being Hawkeye in the first place.

In the end, there were two Hawkeyes — and the name meant more for having been shared.

From that day on, Clint Barton knew that the best thing he had ever done with his bow was teach someone else how to carry it.
