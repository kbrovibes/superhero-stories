In the world of S.H.I.E.L.D. — the secret organization that watched over the world's hidden dangers — there were many skilled agents. But there was one partnership that stood apart from all the rest.

Clint Barton and Bobbi Morse, the agent known as Mockingbird, found each other on a mission that was supposed to be simple and turned out to be anything but. Clint was the archer; Bobbi was a master of hand-to-hand combat who could take down opponents twice her size with graceful, precise moves. Together, they were extraordinary — each one covering exactly the space the other left open.

Their work together turned into friendship. Friendship turned into love. And they got married, two heroes trying to build something warm and real in the middle of a life full of danger and secrets. It was not easy. They co-founded the West Coast Avengers, building a new team and leading it together through victories and losses and all the complications of being both partners and colleagues.

There were hard times too. Bobbi carried secrets from her S.H.I.E.L.D. years — things she had not shared, choices she had made before she and Clint found each other. When those came to light, they hurt. The distance grew. Eventually, they separated, which was quiet and sad the way these things always are.

But they never stopped respecting each other. They kept showing up when the other needed backup.

From that day on, Clint carried Mockingbird's lessons with him: be more than one thing. Be a strategist. Be a fighter. Be someone whose partner makes them better.

And that is how two remarkable people made each other extraordinary.
