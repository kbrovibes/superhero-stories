• The Snap: Clint lost his entire family when Thanos erased half of all life; grief shattered his Hawkeye identity
• Abandoned his bow for a blade and the Ronin persona — black armor and mask that obscured his humanity
• Ronin operated as a merciless vigilante targeting criminal organizations that survived the Snap while innocent people vanished
• Five years of brutal, final violence — methods far removed from Hawkeye's moral compass
• Ronin was self-punishment as much as vigilantism; Clint was punishing the world and himself for his inability to save his family
• Tokyo: Natasha Romanoff tracked him down and offered hope — the chance to reverse the Snap and bring his family back
• Natasha's arrival pulled Clint back from the edge and forced him to confront what he had become
• After the world was restored, Clint carried the heavy memory of his Ronin actions — haunted but transformed
• Ronin gave him a deeper understanding of the fragility of the human spirit and the necessity of community
• Lesson: even the greatest heroes can fall into darkness under unbearable loss — and that redemption requires returning to who you were
