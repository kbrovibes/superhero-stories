All right — buckle up. Because this is the BIG one. This is the story of the day Drax the Destroyer finally came face to face with Thanos himself.

The planet Titan. A world of broken towers and shattered machines, pale sky above ancient ruins, wind blowing through empty hallways. Thanos had once lived here. Now he was coming back to finish something terrible.

And waiting for him? Not one hero. Not two. But a whole team — Iron Man in gold and red armor, Spider-Man swinging between the ruins, Doctor Strange with his glowing cloak, and the Guardians of the Galaxy. All of them. Together. On Titan.

When Thanos appeared on the far edge of the ruined city, Drax took one step forward. STOMP. Then another. STOMP. His daggers were out, his knuckles were tight, and he was about to —

"DRAX." Star-Lord grabbed one arm. Iron Man grabbed the other. "We have a PLAN," Star-Lord said urgently.

Drax stopped. He looked at the horizon. He looked at Star-Lord. He looked at the horizon again. He took a slow breath.

"I am calm," he announced.

He did not sound calm. Not even slightly.

But he waited. He stayed in position. He held his daggers at his side. He stood completely, absolutely still while the team set up their trap — which was an almost impossible thing for Drax the Destroyer to do, because THANOS WAS RIGHT THERE.

And then the battle ERUPTED.

WHOOOOOM — Doctor Strange's golden rings of the Mirror Dimension snapped around Thanos like a cage! THWIP THWIP — Spider-Man's webs tangled the Mad Titan's arm! BOOM BOOM — Iron Man's armor blazed with golden repulsor blasts!

And Drax? Drax LEAPT. Both daggers forward, full strength, every single thing he had. He hit Thanos so hard the whole ruin shook.

Thanos turned and looked at him. Just looked. That grim, serious look that said: you are stronger than I expected.

They were so close. Nebula and Star-Lord's hands grabbed for the Infinity Gauntlet — almost — almost — and then — oh no. Anger. A moment of anger at the very wrong time. And Thanos pulled free.

The battle ended in silence. In Titan's pale dusty air.

Drax stood in the ruins, daggers in hand, not one step backward. He had not won that day. But he had faced the most dangerous being in the universe and looked him straight in the eye.

Not one flinch. Not one step back.

That matters. It really, really matters.

The universe wasn't done writing this story.

Neither was Drax.
