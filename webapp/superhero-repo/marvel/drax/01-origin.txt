Long before Drax the Destroyer had tattoos on his arms and twin daggers at his side, he was Arthur Douglas, a quiet man who drove across the Nevada desert with his wife Yvette and their daughter Heather. The stars burned above them, the road stretched long and empty, and the night seemed ordinary in every way. Then the sky cracked open with purple light. A ship belonging to the warlord Thanos dropped out of the darkness above, and in a single terrible moment, everything Arthur held dear was taken from him.

Thanos could not afford to leave witnesses. He destroyed the Douglas family car without a second thought and flew on, certain the deed was done. But the universe had other plans. A cosmic being named Mentor, whose son was none other than Thanos himself, had watched the tragedy unfold. Desperate to create a warrior powerful enough to challenge the Mad Titan one day, Mentor took Arthur's spirit and gave it a second chance. He poured that spirit into a new body — one built for battle, strong as a planet, covered in ritual green tattoos that marked every battle ahead.

Arthur Douglas was gone. In his place stood Drax the Destroyer.

Drax woke on the planet Titan with fists like boulders and a purpose burning in his chest like a star. He remembered nothing of Nevada, nothing of the car, nothing of ordinary life. He remembered only the name: Thanos. That name was the compass that pointed every step, the reason he trained on Titan until his strength became legend, and the reason he eventually crossed the galaxy searching for justice. Along the way he discovered a second truth that mattered almost as much: his daughter Heather had survived that night. She had been found and raised by a man named Isaac, and she grew up to become the hero called Moondragon. Knowing she was alive lit something in Drax's chest that felt like warmth.

Drax did not understand jokes. If someone told him that it was raining cats and dogs, he looked at the sky and asked which direction the cats were falling. The Guardians of the Galaxy found this endlessly confusing. But they also found that Drax never once told a lie, never once stood back when a friend was in danger, and never once gave up on justice. His tattoos were marks of honor, each one a promise kept. He was not the warrior the galaxy expected. He was something rarer — a destroyer who fought only for the people he loved.
