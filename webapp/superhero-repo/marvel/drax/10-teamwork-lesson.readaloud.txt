The Guardians of the Galaxy got a distress call one day from a place called Vesper Station.

Vesper Station was a small mining settlement orbiting a planet nobody could pronounce — everyone just called it Gravel Planet. Forty-three miners lived there. Twelve of them were children. A crime syndicate called the Sovereignty had decided to take over, and the people had no one to call, so they sent one message into the stars and hoped.

The Guardians picked it up.

Now, the mission got complicated fast. There were more Sovereignty agents than expected. Gamora and Rocket went below the station to disable a weapons array. Star-Lord and Nebula flew the Milano to cut off reinforcements.

That left Drax inside the station. With forty-three miners. And twelve children.

Twelve children.

Drax stood in the main meeting room. They looked at him — this enormous green warrior covered in tattoos, daggers at his side. He took up a genuinely impressive amount of the room.

One child — a little girl about six years old named Mirava — was crying and could not stop.

Drax stood there for a moment. Then he walked over and sat down on the floor right next to her. THUD. A very large thud. He got his eyes down to roughly her level.

"You are safe," he said.

Mirava looked up, still crying.

"There are five Guardians protecting this station," Drax said. "Gamora is below us right now. She is the best warrior I have ever seen. Rocket is a genius — the best engineer in this galaxy or any other. Star-Lord is flying outside. Nebula is with him. And I am here." He looked her in the eyes. "I will not move. Nothing will get past me."

Mirava stopped crying.

"Is the raccoon real?" she asked.

"Yes," said Drax, without any hesitation at all.

She thought about this. "Does he like being called a raccoon?"

A pause. "No. But he is one."

When the mission was over, Rocket came looking for Drax and stopped in the doorway.

Drax was on the floor. Mirava was asleep against his arm. Her small head was resting right on his tattoos. The other children were scattered nearby in the soft sleepy way of kids who have been very scared and are now very tired.

Rocket stared.

"You've got to be kidding me," he said.

"I was protecting them," said Drax, with great dignity, from the floor.

He was right. He was absolutely, completely right.

Mirava grew up. She became a mining engineer — the best in her whole system. Her first deep-core drill? She named it the Destroyer.

She was never afraid of the dark again.

THAT is what it means to be a Guardian of the Galaxy.
