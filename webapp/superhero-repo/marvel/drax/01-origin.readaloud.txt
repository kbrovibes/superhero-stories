Are you ready? Because this story begins in the middle of the desert — a dark, quiet night, a long empty road, a family in a car — and then BOOM. The sky tears open.

His name was Arthur Douglas, and he was just an ordinary man driving through the Nevada desert with his wife and his little daughter Heather. The stars were out. The radio was probably on. Everything felt perfectly, completely normal.

And then? WHOOSH. A ship from SPACE appeared overhead. Not any ordinary ship. The ship of the most dangerous warlord in the galaxy — Thanos. That terrible name. That terrible moment.

Arthur was gone. But the universe wasn't finished with him yet.

A cosmic being named Mentor — who happened to be the FATHER of Thanos himself, if you can believe that — watched everything from high above. Mentor took Arthur's spirit, the very spark of who he was, and poured it into something extraordinary. A brand-new body. Strong as a mountain. Fast as a comet. Covered head to toe in swirling green ritual tattoos that glowed in the starlight.

Arthur Douglas became DRAX THE DESTROYER. KA-BOOM!

Drax opened his eyes on the planet Titan and sat up like a boulder rolling to its feet. He had one thought — one single burning thought filling his chest like a furnace. THANOS. That name. That name. He would find Thanos and he would have justice.

He trained on Titan until his punch could crack a wall of solid rock. CRACK! He trained until he was the strongest fighter on the whole planet. WHAP! And then he leapt into the stars on the trail of Thanos.

But wait — something amazing! His daughter Heather? She had survived that terrible night. A kind man named Isaac had found her and raised her. She grew up to become the hero Moondragon. When Drax found out, the big warrior — who NEVER cried, ever, not once — went very, very quiet for a long moment.

He also takes everything completely literally — every single word, every single time. One time, Star-Lord said something was "a piece of cake." Drax looked around for twenty seconds. "I see no cake," he said, completely serious.

The Guardians still laugh about that one.

But here is the most important thing you need to know: Drax never tells a lie. Drax never backs down. Drax fights for the people he loves — and that is every single Guardian in the galaxy.

THWAM! The Destroyer is ready. Let's go!
