The Battle of Xandar was the day the Guardians of the Galaxy truly became what their name promised. Ronan the Accuser brought the Dark Aster down through Xandar's golden sky carrying an Infinity Stone powerful enough to crack a planet in half. Below, the city of Xandar shimmered in the afternoon light — thousands of people, the great domes and spires of the Nova Corps headquarters, ordinary streets where ordinary lives were happening right up until they weren't.

The Guardians flew in against Ronan's fleet in a battered ship called the Milano. The plan was fast, chaotic, and only barely a plan at all. Rocket climbed into a one-man fighter and took on Ronan's warships in open space with the fearlessness of someone who had decided that fear was simply not useful. Gamora cut her way through Ronan's soldiers while trying to reach the orb containing the Power Stone. Groot formed a barricade of branches to hold a corridor while people fled to safety. And Drax — Drax planted himself at the forward position and held it like a wall.

The rematch with Ronan came. Drax had learned from Knowhere. He did not charge alone this time. He charged when the moment called for it, with the Guardians around him, and the difference was measurable. He hit Ronan hard enough to crack the Universal Weapon's staff and send the warlord skidding across the deck of the Dark Aster. It was the hardest hit Ronan had ever taken from a single being who was not wielding an Infinity Stone, and even through his armor, Ronan felt it. For the first time in the battle, he paused.

What stopped Ronan was not a weapon in the end. It was a song. Star-Lord danced and distracted the warlord in a way so entirely unexpected that every Kree warrior on the ship stopped to stare, and in that pause, the Power Stone was grabbed, and the whole Guardians team held it together — each one of them pushing back against its impossible energy with everything they had — and they turned it on Ronan.

Drax stood in the settling dust of the battlefield and looked at Xandar's sky, now empty of warships, and felt something he had not felt in a very long time. Not peace — that was too quiet a word. Something between peace and readiness, the feeling of a person who has done the most important thing they could do on a given day, and knows more important days are still ahead.
