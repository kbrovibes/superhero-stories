Do you know how the Guardians of the Galaxy first became a team?

In a PRISON. A floating space prison called the Kyln. That is where this story begins, and it does not get less surprising from there.

Star-Lord — real name Peter Quill — had stolen a silver orb containing the Power Stone. Immediately caught. Gamora had come to take the orb for her own reasons. Rocket Raccoon and Groot had come to collect the bounty on Star-Lord's head. And Drax was already there, sitting patiently in his cell, waiting for someone — ANYONE — connected to Thanos.

Drax looked at this group. He narrowed his eyes.

"These are not warriors," he announced.

He was partly right and completely wrong at the same time.

They escaped together. They survived a wild, chaotic battle at Knowhere together. They argued — oh, they argued a LOT — but when Xandar needed defending, they didn't go home. They made a plan.

Now. Let me tell you about Groot.

Groot was nine feet tall and made of wood and said only three words — "I am Groot" — in every situation. Need a fact? I am Groot. Feeling happy? I am Groot. Someone just did something unbelievably dangerous? I. Am. GROOT.

During the battle over Xandar, the Guardians' ship was falling through the sky. It was going to crash HARD. And Groot — big, gentle, magnificent Groot — grew his branches out around the whole group like a cocoon. Wrapped them in wood. Held them safe.

"We will be protected," Groot said. But it sounded like: "I am Groot."

BOOM. The ship hit the ground.

The Guardians climbed out — shaken but safe. Groot was in pieces.

Then Rocket found one tiny twig. Still alive. Still growing.

Drax picked up that tiny twig and held it between two giant fingers, very carefully — the way you might hold a butterfly. He carried it until it was safe. He checked on it every day.

That is the whole Guardian story in one tiny twig.

Gamora taught Drax when to pause. Star-Lord tried to teach him about jokes, with limited success. ("Why is that funny?" Drax would ask. Peter would sigh.) Rocket showed that strength comes in small, spiky, determined packages. And Drax gave all of them one thing no one else could:

When things get very bad, Drax will be standing in front of you. Daggers out. Not moving.

Not. Moving.

That is family. That is the Guardians of the Galaxy.

I am Groot.
