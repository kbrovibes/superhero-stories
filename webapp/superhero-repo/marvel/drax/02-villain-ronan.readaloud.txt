Meet the villain of this story: Ronan the Accuser. Say that name out loud. RO-NAN. It sounds like a rumble of thunder, doesn't it? Good, because that is exactly what Ronan is like.

Ronan wore black armor that covered him from boot to chin, and he carried the Universal Weapon — a stone war hammer so powerful it crackled with blue lightning when he swung it. CRACKKK! He was tall and cold and furious, and he had just done something very, very dangerous.

He had stolen an Infinity Stone. The Power Stone — a glowing purple orb of pure energy that could blast an entire planet to pieces. And he was pointing that power straight at Xandar, the home planet of the Nova Corps.

Now. Drax had been WAITING for someone connected to Thanos. And Ronan — oh, Ronan worked for Thanos. That was all Drax needed to know.

Inside the prison station called the Kyln, Drax stood up tall and SHOUTED his challenge across the whole floating prison. Come and find me, Ronan! I am right here! The other Guardians — Star-Lord, Gamora, Rocket, and Groot — stared at him. Rocket's jaw dropped. "You just told the bad guy exactly where we are!" SHHHHHH!

"He will come to us," Drax said, perfectly calm.

And later, on a floating mining station called Knowhere — which was actually the giant severed head of an ancient Celestial — Drax slipped away and sent Ronan a personal message. Come to Knowhere. I am here. Come face me.

Ronan came. With his whole FLEET.

And the fight? Oh, what a fight. WHAM! CRACK! SLAM! Drax hit Ronan hard enough to shake the ground. He threw his daggers so fast they sang through the air. He was strong, he was fierce, he was absolutely determined.

And then Ronan hit him back. BOOM. Into the yellow liquid pools of Knowhere Drax went, sinking like a stone.

It was Groot who found him — one long wooden arm reaching into the dark liquid — and pulled him out, dripping and coughing. Rocket was furious. Gamora pinched the bridge of her nose. Star-Lord stared at the ceiling.

And Drax looked up at them and said something unexpected. "I was mistaken. I thought I was ready."

No excuses. No arguing. Just the truth.

Because that is Drax. He tells the truth even when it is about himself.

But he also learns from it. And the rematch? Over Xandar, with every Guardian flying at full speed? WHOOOOSH.

That rematch went very differently.
