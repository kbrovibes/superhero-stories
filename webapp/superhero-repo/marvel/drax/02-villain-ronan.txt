Ronan the Accuser stood aboard his warship, the Dark Aster, like a statue carved from iron. He wore heavy black armor and carried the Universal Weapon — a massive stone war hammer that crackled with blue energy. Ronan had made a deal with Thanos and then broken it the moment he found something even more powerful: an Infinity Stone called the Power Stone, glowing inside a small silver orb. With the Power Stone, Ronan could destroy an entire planet. He had chosen his first target — the planet Xandar, home of the Nova Corps, his oldest enemy.

Drax wanted Ronan more than anyone. He had heard that Ronan worked for Thanos, and for Drax that was reason enough to challenge the man directly. On the prison station called the Kyln, when the Guardians were all locked away in separate cells, Drax had shouted a challenge across the floating prison. He told Ronan exactly where he was and what he planned to do. His new friends — Star-Lord, Gamora, Rocket, and Groot — stared at him with expressions that said this was perhaps not the wisest strategy. "He will come to us," Drax explained. The others were quiet for a moment. "Or," Rocket finally said, "we go somewhere safer."

On Knowhere, the severed head of an ancient Celestial floating at the edge of the galaxy, Drax made his move. While the others were distracted, he sent a message to Ronan himself. Come to Knowhere. Drax is here. Come and face me. He sat down and waited. He did not see any reason to whisper the plan. Ronan arrived with his full fleet, and the attack was swift. Drax fought Ronan with everything he had — every dagger, every strike, every ounce of the terrible strength that had been poured into his new body. He hit Ronan hard enough to shake the floor of the mining station.

Ronan looked down at him and hit him harder.

Drax sank into the yellow liquid pools of Knowhere and disappeared. It was Groot who reached in with one long wooden arm and pulled him out, dripping and sputtering. Rocket was furious. Gamora was exasperated. Star-Lord shook his head slowly. But Drax, half-conscious and wrapped in Groot's arms, looked up at the ceiling and said something no one expected: "I was mistaken. I thought I was ready." He had learned something that the strongest warrior always has to learn eventually — that courage and strength alone are not enough. The right moment and the right team matter just as much.

The rematch would come later, over Xandar, with all the Guardians at his side. That time, they were ready.
