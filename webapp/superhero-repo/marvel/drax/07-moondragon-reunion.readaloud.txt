This is a story about something very hard and very wonderful at the same time.

Do you remember how Drax had a daughter named Heather? Do you remember that Heather survived the terrible night in Nevada, and was found by a kind man named Isaac, who raised her on the moon of Titan?

Well. Heather grew up.

Isaac trained her mind like other people train muscles — rigorously, carefully, every single day. She studied and focused and pushed herself until she could do something extraordinary: she could reach into other people's minds with her thoughts alone. She could move objects without touching them. She was a telepath and a telekinetic, and she was remarkable.

She trained at a place called the monastery of the Dragon of the Moon. She shaved her head. She wrapped herself in dark clothing. She became a hero with a cool and precise name: Moondragon.

And then one day — on Titan, in a corridor of its great compound — Drax the Destroyer walked around a corner and came face to face with his daughter.

Neither of them knew what to do.

Now. You might expect this moment to look like a movie — music swelling, people running toward each other, a great enormous hug. But this was Drax, who expresses feelings through action and not words. And this was Heather, who had trained her mind to be calm and controlled and precise. They stood in that corridor and looked at each other.

"You are Heather," Drax said.

"Yes," said Heather.

A pause.

"You are well," Drax said.

"Yes," said Heather.

Another pause.

Neither of them was good at feelings-in-words. Both of them were excellent at feelings-in-actions. And over the following weeks, that was how their reunion happened — in small moments, sidelong, while they were doing other things. In battle, where Heather's telepathy let her read exactly what Drax was about to do a second before he did it, making them the most perfectly coordinated duo the Guardians had ever seen. WHAM-ZING — like they had practiced for years.

They argued too. Heather found Drax's literalness frustrating. Drax found her philosophical way of speaking baffling. Once she said something was "a double-edged sword." He looked at her hands for the sword.

But Drax never called her Moondragon.

He called her Heather. Every single time.

She pretended this was annoying. Her face, when he said it, told a different story entirely.

Two people from the same broken beginning. Both remarkable. Both still here.

That is the whole story.

That is the whole wonderful story.
