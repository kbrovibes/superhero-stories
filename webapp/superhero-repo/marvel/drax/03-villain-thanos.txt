The moment Drax had trained for his entire rebuilt life finally arrived on a planet called Titan — the ruins of a world that had once been Thanos's own home. The Guardians had come together with Iron Man, Spider-Man, and Doctor Strange to stop the Mad Titan from collecting all six Infinity Stones. Titan was a shattered landscape of broken towers and ancient machines, the sky torn and pale above what had once been a great civilization. And somewhere in that wreckage, Thanos was coming.

Drax had to be talked out of charging the moment Thanos appeared on the horizon. Iron Man and Star-Lord both put hands on his arms. "We have a plan," Star-Lord said urgently. Drax looked at the horizon, then back at Star-Lord, then at the horizon again. "I am calm," he announced, in a voice that did not sound calm at all. But he held his daggers at his side and waited. He even managed to sit very still while the team set their trap — which was impressive, because Thanos was right there.

The battle of Titan was the closest anyone had come to stopping Thanos from completing the Infinity Gauntlet. Iron Man's armor crackled with golden energy. Spider-Man used his web-shooters to tangle the Mad Titan's arm. Doctor Strange kept Thanos suspended in a ring of golden shields called the Mirror Dimension. And Drax? Drax drove both his daggers into Thanos with everything he had — hard enough to actually slow the most powerful being in the universe. Even Thanos noticed. He turned and looked at Drax with a kind of grim acknowledgment. For that one moment, the warrior who had been born just to face him had the Mad Titan's full attention.

They came terrifyingly close. Nebula and Star-Lord reached for the Gauntlet with both hands. But anger broke the plan at the worst moment, and Thanos slipped free. The battle ended in dust and silence on the broken surface of Titan.

Drax had not gotten his justice that day. But he had looked Thanos in the eye on the ruins of Titan, and he had not flinched, not for a single second. Justice can take many roads. For Drax, the long road had only made him harder, and braver, and more certain. The universe was not finished with its story. Neither was he.
