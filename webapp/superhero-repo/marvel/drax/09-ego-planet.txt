When Star-Lord's father turned out to be a living planet named Ego, the Guardians discovered very quickly that Ego was not the warm, welcoming father Peter Quill had dreamed of. Ego was an ancient Celestial being who had built himself a human-shaped avatar to walk the galaxy with, and he wanted to use the energy running through Peter's half-Celestial blood to cover every world with his own living matter — absorbing all other life in the process. It was, as Rocket put it, the worst possible version of a family reunion.

Drax had been on Ego's planet during the first phase of the crisis, which he had initially found wonderful. Ego's world was lush and beautiful, covered in flowers that were warm to the touch, and the avatar named Ego was warm and articulate and said all the right things. Drax had liked him. This was embarrassing later. He had described Ego as a "being of grace and honor." He had said this out loud, to Gamora, who had been suspicious from the start. She gave him a look that he still occasionally thought about.

When the nature of Ego's plan became clear, Drax's role became exactly what it always was: hold the line while the people doing the complicated things did the complicated things. While Peter Quill used his Celestial energy to fight Ego in the planet's core, and Gamora and Nebula worked out their terrible shared history, and Rocket rigged explosives to destroy the power source at Ego's center, Drax held off the attackers. The planet itself was trying to stop them. Tendrils of Ego's living matter came at them like enormous roots, and Drax tore through them with his daggers and his hands. The planet screamed.

Baby Groot carried the bomb through Ego's core while everyone tried to keep the living planet from stopping him. Drax watched that tiny walking twig move through an obstacle that would have stopped a less determined being. He felt a specific and fierce pride that he could not have explained in words and did not try to. Groot reached the power source. The bomb was set. The Guardians flew.

Afterward, standing on a hillside as Ego's planet came apart behind them, Drax looked at Peter Quill. Quill had lost something that day, even though what he had lost had been a lie. Drax understood that. He understood that grief does not require the thing you lost to have been perfect. He put one enormous hand briefly on Peter's shoulder and said nothing at all.

Some things, even the most literal man in the galaxy knows better than to put into words.
