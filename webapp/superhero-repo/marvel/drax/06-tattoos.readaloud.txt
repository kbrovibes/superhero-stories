Look at Drax. Really look.

You see that? From his wrists all the way up his arms, across his chest, up his neck — those swirling green and red lines covering every inch? Those are not decoration. Those are not just a cool design. Those are a MAP. A map of everything Drax has ever promised, every loss he has ever honored, every oath he has ever kept.

Let me explain.

Drax's tattoos come from a Kylosian warrior tradition. In this tradition, before you even TOUCH a weapon, you sit down and you receive marks. Marks for tests completed. Marks for oaths taken. Marks for people you are fighting for.

The wise cosmic being named Mentor — the one who built Drax's new body — made sure this happened first. He had a reason. A warrior who fights without knowing WHY he fights is just someone throwing punches in the dark. The marks come first. The "why" goes on the skin before the sword goes in the hand.

So what do Drax's marks say?

The big curving ones across his shoulders? Those are called the Marks of Becoming. They say: once there was an ordinary man named Arthur Douglas, and he mattered, and his life mattered, and none of what came next would be possible without him. Even Drax the Destroyer stands on the shoulders of Arthur Douglas.

The lines running down his forearms? Oaths of Protection. A formal, permanent, completely unbreakable promise: I will stand between the ones who cannot protect themselves and the things that threaten them.

And two marks above his heart. Just two. Drax chose exactly where those went.

They are for Yvette and Heather.

Now — you may have noticed that Drax never, ever wears a shirt. The other Guardians have definitely noticed. Once, on an ice planet called Berhert, Gamora said very politely: "Drax. It is minus forty degrees. Would you like a jacket?"

"I do not feel cold," said Drax.

"I know. But the people here might find it—"

"They will adapt," said Drax, and walked out into a blizzard.

Gamora stood in the doorway for a moment. Then she went back inside for her OWN jacket.

The tattoos are the most permanent thing about Drax. In a body he did not start with, in a life handed to him by a stranger in the stars, the marks on his skin connect him to everything he has ever been. You cannot take them from him. You cannot make him forget them. They are written right there where he can carry them always.

That is the kind of memory you can never lose.
