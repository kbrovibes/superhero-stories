What if you went to find your long-lost father — and your father turned out to be a PLANET?

Not a person who lived on a planet. A planet. That also made a person. Of himself. To walk around in.

This is the situation Peter Quill found himself in, and it just gets stranger from there.

His father's name was Ego. Ego was an ancient Celestial — one of the oldest and most powerful beings in the universe — who had spent thousands of years building a beautiful world for himself, complete with flowers and warm light and a very convincing father-shaped body. "I am your dad!" said Ego. "Come visit! Isn't it wonderful here?"

And here is the most embarrassing part of this story for Drax: he agreed.

The flowers were warm to the touch. The light was golden. Ego was charming and said all the right things. Drax walked around for a while and announced — out loud, to Gamora — that Ego was "a being of grace and honor."

Gamora had been suspicious from the start. She gave Drax a look. He would think about that look later.

THEN: the true plan was revealed. Ego wanted to spread his own living matter across every planet in the galaxy — absorbing everything, replacing all other life with more of himself.

"That is a terrible plan," said Drax.

Yes, Drax. It was.

The battle plan: Peter would fight Ego directly using his own half-Celestial energy powers. Rocket rigged a bomb to destroy Ego's core. Gamora and Nebula handled their complicated family business.

And Drax held the line.

The planet itself was fighting them — enormous tendrils of living golden matter coming from everywhere. CRACK — Drax tore through one with a dagger. SMASH — he shoved another aside with both hands. WHAM — he hit a cluster so hard they scattered like broken pottery.

And through all of it, one tiny figure moved toward the core.

Baby Groot. Small as a houseplant. Carrying the bomb. Moving through the chaos with absolute calm.

Drax watched that tiny walking twig and did not say anything, because he did not have the words for what he felt. He kept fighting.

Groot planted the bomb. The Guardians flew. Ego's planet cracked and crumbled behind them.

Afterward, they stood watching the last light fade. Peter Quill was quiet. He had found his father. Lost him. Both in the same day.

Drax walked over. He put one enormous hand on Peter's shoulder.

He said nothing.

Because some things, even the most literal man in the galaxy understands better than to put into words.

Just: here. I'm here. You're not alone.

That's all. That's everything.
