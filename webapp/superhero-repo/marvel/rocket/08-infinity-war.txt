The darkest day the Guardians of the Galaxy ever faced began with a distress signal echoing out across the stars. Thor — a golden-haired warrior from a realm called Asgard — was drifting alone in deep space, the wreckage of his people's ship surrounding him. The Guardians pulled him aboard, and what he told them changed everything. Thanos — an enormous, grey-skinned titan, the most powerful being in the universe — was hunting the six Infinity Stones. He already held the Space Stone and the Power Stone. If he gathered all six and fitted them into the Infinity Gauntlet, he could end half of all life in the universe with a single snap of his fingers.

Rocket had heard of Thanos. Everyone in the galaxy had heard of Thanos the way you might hear of a terrible storm far away — as a name spoken quietly, as a threat too enormous to make entirely real. Now the storm had arrived.

The Guardians split up. Star-Lord, Gamora, Drax, and Mantis flew toward Knowhere to intercept the Reality Stone. Rocket and Teen Groot flew with Thor to Nidavellir — a forge built around a dying star, a place of such intense heat it could cast weapons of extraordinary strength. Its craftsman was Eitri, a giant dwarf who had made Thor's original hammer, Mjolnir, and other legendary weapons for Asgard. The forge was cold and dark when they arrived. Thanos had been there first, disabling it, crushing Eitri's hands so no new weapon could be made.

Rocket studied the forge's ring mechanism and found the damaged components. He repaired what he could. But to restart the forge, someone had to hold the ring open against a dying star's full output — the kind of energy that should have destroyed any living being. Thor stepped into the beam and held it through sheer will while Eitri cast Stormbreaker: a new battle-axe of Uru metal, ancient and powerful. Groot grew his arm into the handle, completing it.

They flew hard toward Wakanda, where the final battle was already happening. They were not in time to stop the worst of it. Thanos had all six Stones. When the snap came, half of all life vanished — including Teen Groot, who looked at Rocket just before the grey ash took him.

Rocket stood on the grass of Wakanda in the sudden silence, one of the ones who remained. He had survived every fight he had ever been in. He needed to survive this one too — long enough to find a way to bring them back.
