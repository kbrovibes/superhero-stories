Five years. That is how long passes after the snap. Half the universe is gone. The Guardians are scattered. And Rocket — small, brilliant, entirely unwilling to give up — has been at the Avengers compound on Earth, working alongside Nebula, Black Widow, and Captain America, keeping the galaxy from falling apart while he waits.

He is ALWAYS waiting. Always thinking. Always sure there is an answer.

And then Scott Lang walks in with a theory about the Quantum Realm.

The Quantum Realm is a dimension between dimensions — a place where time does not move the way it does everywhere else. Scott says: what if you could use it to travel backwards in time, grab the Infinity Stones before Thanos collected them, and bring them back to now?

Rocket does not need to hear the rest. He is already building.

CLICK! SNAP! WRRRR! Tony Stark cracks the theoretical equation. The EQUIPMENT, though — quantum tunnel emitters, Pym Particle dispensers, a relay that keeps every Avenger's time coordinates locked in sync — all of that is Rocket. He works for days. He barely sleeps. He barely eats. Only he could have built it.

Time Heist! The Avengers scatter across history to collect the Stones. Rocket's assignment: go with Thor to Asgard in the year 2013 and retrieve the Reality Stone while a battle between Asgardians and Dark Elves rages around them.

Thor... has a moment. His mother Frigga is there, in this version of 2013, and he has not seen her in years. He almost completely ruins the timeline trying to get to her. But Frigga — who is brilliant and perceptive — figures out roughly what is happening, and she tells him it is all right, and sends him off with her love.

Rocket thinks this is extremely complicated and also, quietly, very good.

They bring back the Reality Stone. The other teams bring back the others. Tony Stark — yes, THAT Tony Stark, genius, billionaire — builds the Nano Gauntlet. The Hulk wears it. With all six Stones blazing in his fist, he snaps.

The universe comes back. Every person who was gone: BACK. Just like that. In one golden instant, the silence ends.

And then Thanos arrives. From the PAST. With an ARMY.

BOOM! The biggest battle of anyone's life. Every hero, every ally, all of them on that field — Avengers, Guardians, Asgardians, sorcerers, the whole impossible gathering of people who refused to give up.

Tony Stark picks up the Gauntlet. He snaps one more time.

Thanos's army is gone.

Rocket stands in the rubble in the sudden quiet, and looks around, and finds Groot — alive, tall, green, there. He walks over and stands close. He does not make a speech. He does not need to.

For Rocket, standing close is the whole speech.
