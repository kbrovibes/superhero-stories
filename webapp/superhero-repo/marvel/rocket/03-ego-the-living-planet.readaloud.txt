Okay, here is a villain who is going to surprise you. Ready? Because this one is a PLANET.

Yes. An actual planet. With a face. And a plan.

His name is Ego. And he calls himself a Celestial — one of the oldest, most powerful kinds of beings in the entire universe. He made himself a gorgeous home world, with blue mountains and amber meadows and waterfalls that catch the light like ribbons of gold. When he walks among smaller beings, he looks like a kind silver-haired man. He is very charming. He is extremely dangerous. And — here is the twist — he is Peter Quill's father.

Oh boy.

While Peter and the others are inside Ego's world being completely dazzled, Rocket stays outside. His ears are flat. His nose is working overtime. Something here is deeply, thoroughly wrong — he can't name it yet, but he knows.

And he is RIGHT.

Ego's real plan is called the Expansion. For thousands and thousands of years, he has been secretly growing tiny tendrils of living golden matter into the soil of worlds all across the galaxy. Thousands of planets. A hidden network, sleeping, waiting. He wants to use Peter's special Celestial energy to wake all of it up at once — every tendril on every planet blooming simultaneously, absorbing every world into himself. Goodbye, everyone. Goodbye, everything.

NOT TODAY.

Rocket goes to work. CLANK! CLICK! WRIIIRRRR! He builds a bomb using Anulax batteries — widely regarded as the most explosive material in the entire known galaxy. He convinces Yondu — the blue-skinned Ravager captain who trained Star-Lord — to help fly it to Ego's brain, buried deep inside the planet's hollow core. Yondu's whistling arrow cuts their path through solid stone, glowing blue in the dark. Baby Groot rides along on Rocket's shoulder, his eyes enormous and his courage even more so.

The inside of Ego is WILD. Walls that breathe! Tunnels that shift and change! Golden tendrils reaching out through the dark, trying to grab them! Rocket keeps flying, keeps pushing forward, because he did not build this bomb to turn around.

Baby Groot plants the bomb at the core. Tiny hands. The biggest courage in the room. CLICK. Done.

BOOOOOM!

Ego shatters from the inside out. The Expansion dies all across the galaxy. The planet crumbles into stardust. The Guardians blast out in the Milano with approximately zero seconds to spare.

Rocket looks at Baby Groot. Groot looks at Rocket. Both of them slightly scorched. Both of them absolutely satisfied.

That, says Rocket's biggest grin, is how you stop a planet.
