Rocket did not start out as a Guardian of the Galaxy. He did not start out with a spaceship, or a jetpack, or a pile of the most spectacular weapons ever assembled. He started out alone, on a place called Halfworld — a strange, sad planet where scientists had performed experiments on animals, trying to give them human-level intelligence and engineering skills. The scientists were long gone by the time Rocket opened his eyes. But the changes they had made to him were permanent.

He had the mind of an engineer and the reflexes of a fighter. He could look at any machine and understand, in seconds, exactly how it worked and exactly how to improve it. His hands were fast and clever. His nose could track a scent across a crowd of a thousand beings. He was small — the size of an Earth raccoon, which is exactly what he looked like — but he had more raw determination than a being ten times his size. What he did not have was anyone who understood what he was, or where he had come from, or why he felt so different from everyone around him.

Rocket left Halfworld and made his way across the galaxy by whatever means necessary — hired muscle, bounty hunting, quick escapes from even quicker mistakes. He was brilliant and he was prickly, and he liked it that way. If you kept your distance from people, they could not confuse you or leave you. He believed, for a long time, that a partner was a liability.

Then he met Groot.

Groot was an enormous, gentle tree-person from a world called Planet X, a being who could grow branches, smash walls, and glow with soft warm light — and whose vocabulary, for complicated reasons of his own, consisted entirely of the phrase "I am Groot." Somehow, Rocket understood exactly what Groot meant, every single time. The two of them became inseparable. Rocket handled the planning, the weapons, and the talking. Groot handled the heavy lifting and provided a kind of quiet, patient steadiness that Rocket had never had before.

Together they drifted into the orbit of a team that would change Rocket's life: the Guardians of the Galaxy. Peter Quill — Star-Lord — Gamora, Drax the Destroyer, and eventually many others. They were misfits and wanderers, each carrying a story that had not ended the way it was supposed to. Rocket fit right in. For the first time, he was not just a small, strange creature who had been changed by someone else's experiment. He was the most dangerous small creature in the entire Milky Way — and he had a family who knew exactly how to keep up with him.
