There are few enemies in the galaxy more terrifying than Ronan the Accuser. He was a Kree warlord who carried an enormous stone hammer called the Universal Weapon, and he wore black armor so heavy it groaned when he walked. Ronan believed he alone had the right to judge entire civilizations — and he was willing to destroy whole planets to prove it. When he came into possession of the Power Stone, one of the six Infinity Stones, Rocket knew the Guardians were facing the most dangerous opponent they had ever encountered.

The Power Stone was an ancient, violet gem of almost incomprehensible energy, and Ronan planned to carry it to the planet Xandar and use it to wipe out every living person there. Xandar was a gleaming world of white towers and long-standing peace, home to the Nova Corps — the galaxy's greatest peacekeeping force. Rocket had no particular attachment to Xandar, but the idea of a planet full of people being destroyed because one furious warlord decided so made something rise up in his chest that felt a great deal like anger.

The Guardians had the Orb — a protective casing that held the Power Stone — but Ronan had taken it back. Rocket needed a plan, and fast. He spent forty frantic minutes in the Milano's cargo bay, pulling apart every blaster, every spare circuit, every engine component he could reach, and built a device that could punch a hole directly through the Dark Aster — Ronan's enormous warship. The other Guardians flew their fighters straight into the battle above Xandar, engaging the Kree fleet while Rocket focused on the shot that mattered.

The Dark Aster came down. It crashed into Xandar's surface in a cloud of fire and debris. But Ronan climbed out of the wreck with the Power Stone in his hand, more furious than ever. It was Peter Quill who gave Rocket the moment he needed — Star-Lord challenged Ronan to a dancing contest, which was so bewildering that Ronan actually paused — and in that pause, Gamora, Drax, and Rocket lunged. Together they pried the Stone free. The Power Stone blazed violet and terrible, and four Guardians held it together, the energy passing through all of them at once. They held on. The Stone's power cracked through Rocket like lightning, but he did not let go.

Ronan collapsed. Xandar was saved. The Nova Corps took custody of the Power Stone, promising to keep it from those who would misuse it. Rocket stood in the rubble of the Dark Aster, a little singed and considerably satisfied, and decided that saving entire planets was probably worth the very long day.
