Once upon a time, in the vast and humming dark of space, a very bad storm was coming.

His name was Ronan the Accuser.

Ronan wore black armor as heavy as a mountainside and carried a weapon called the Universal Weapon — a great stone hammer whose blow could crack a starship in half. He was a Kree warrior who believed he alone could decide which civilizations deserved to live. For a long time, that was frightening enough. But then Ronan found the Power Stone — one of the six ancient Infinity Stones that the universe had scattered to keep them safe — and frightening became something else entirely.

The Power Stone was a small violet gem, quiet as a held breath, and inside it was stored more pure energy than the heart of a sun. When Ronan closed his hand around it, the violet light blazed between his fingers and the whole Dark Aster — his enormous warship, black as a thundercloud — shook with the force of it.

Ronan set his course for Xandar.

Xandar was a world of white towers and silver parks and wide, bright plazas where children played while their parents watched. The Nova Corps — the peacekeepers of the galaxy — kept their headquarters there, their gold-and-blue ships gleaming in the harbor. It was a good and peaceful place. And Ronan intended to end it.

Rocket heard the news and went very still for exactly three seconds.

Then he went to the cargo bay and started taking things apart.

The other Guardians had a plan — fly their fighters into the Kree armada above Xandar and create enough chaos for one ship to get through. Rocket's job was to make sure that ship did what it was supposed to do. He laid out every spare circuit, every engine coil, every blaster he had been collecting for six months, and he began to build. CLICK. CLANK. WRENCH. His hands moved faster than anyone watching could follow. The machine he built was not beautiful. It was large and held together with confidence and ingenuity rather than proper fasteners. But when he powered it up, the hum it made told him exactly what he needed to know.

It would work.

The battle above Xandar was like nothing Rocket had ever flown through. Laser fire streaked between the stars. Kree warships pressed down in great dark waves. The Milano dipped and rolled, engines screaming, and Rocket's knuckles were white on the controls. He held his breath and took the shot.

BOOM.

The Dark Aster shook. The Dark Aster tilted. The Dark Aster fell.

It came down through Xandar's atmosphere in a spiral of fire and broke apart across a wide green valley outside the city. Dust billowed up in walls of pale grey. The Guardians rushed through the smoke, hearts hammering.

But Ronan was not finished.

He hauled himself out of the wreckage, armor cracked but still standing, the Power Stone blazing violet in his outstretched hand. He lifted the Universal Weapon. He was going to finish this himself.

And then Peter Quill stepped in front of him, pointed a finger, and challenged Ronan the Accuser to a dance contest.

Even Rocket was not sure what to think about that.

But Ronan — ferocious, ancient, terrible Ronan — stopped. Stared. For just one bewildered moment, he did not know what to do.

That moment was enough.

Rocket, Drax, Gamora, and Star-Lord all dove for the Stone at the same time. Their hands closed around it together, and the Power Stone's violet fire surged through all four of them at once, wild and enormous and blazing. It was the kind of energy that was not meant to pass through a living being. Every one of them felt it — felt the light, felt the force, felt the weight of something vast and ancient pressing through their bones.

They held on.

Together. All four of them. They held on.

Ronan fell.

The violet light faded. Xandar was quiet. In the distance, Nova Corps ships were already landing, their lights blinking soft blue through the settling dust. People came out of their buildings slowly at first, then faster, voices rising from silence into sound — relief, and gratitude, and the particular brightness of a city that realized it was still standing.

The Nova Corps locked the Power Stone away somewhere safe and deep, where no single being could ever touch it alone.

Rocket sat down on a piece of the Dark Aster's wreckage, stretched his tail out behind him, and looked up at the sky for a long, quiet moment.

He had built something today that mattered. Not just a weapon — but a chance. And his family had taken that chance together.

He was still singed around the ears. He was extremely satisfied.

Groot settled down beside him, shoulders warm, and said nothing at all. Which was, of course, the exact right thing to say.
