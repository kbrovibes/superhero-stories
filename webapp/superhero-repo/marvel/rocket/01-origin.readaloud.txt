Hey — have you ever felt like you were just a little bit different from everyone else around you? Like maybe you saw things they didn't, or thought about problems in a way no one else quite understood?

Well. Meet Rocket.

Rocket is a small, wisecracking, INCREDIBLY brilliant Guardian of the Galaxy — and yes, before you ask, he does look like a raccoon. He has stripes on his face, a fluffy tail, bright curious eyes, and a brain that can build a superweapon out of whatever is lying on the floor of the nearest spaceship. He is also, quite possibly, the most dangerous small creature in the entire Milky Way galaxy.

But he did not start out that way.

Rocket was made. Not born — MADE. On a faraway planet called Halfworld, scientists ran experiments on animals, trying to give them human minds and engineer's hands. Then the scientists left. And Rocket was alone on that strange little world, feeling like no one in the entire universe was quite like him. So he left.

He bounced across the galaxy — working as a bounty hunter, fixing engines, picking up jobs wherever he could find them. He was FAST. He was BRILLIANT. He could look at any machine — CLANK, WHIRR, CLICK — and know in two seconds exactly how it worked and exactly how to make it better. Or turn it into a cannon. Rocket preferred cannons.

He met Groot on a job that went sideways — actually, most of Rocket's jobs went sideways. Groot is a giant tree-being from a world called Planet X, tall as a house, kind as a sunrise, and he says exactly three words: "I am Groot." Those three words can mean many things, and Rocket is the only being in the galaxy who understands every single meaning. They became best friends immediately and completely.

Then came Star-Lord — that's Peter Quill, a human from Earth who flies a ship called the Milano and listens to the most ancient music in the cosmos. Then Gamora. Then Drax the Destroyer, who is large and serious and takes everything literally.

The Guardians of the Galaxy! A bunch of misfits who somehow, together, are UNSTOPPABLE.

Rocket looked around at this strange crew and felt something he hadn't felt in a very long time. He felt like he was home. He had come from a laboratory on a lonely planet, and now he was here — surrounded by people who needed him exactly as strange and fierce and brilliant as he was.

BOOM! The most dangerous raccoon in the galaxy had found his family. And the galaxy would never be quite the same again.
