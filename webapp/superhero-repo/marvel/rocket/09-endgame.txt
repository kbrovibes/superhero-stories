Five years passed after Thanos's snap. The universe was quieter than it had ever been, with half its people simply gone. Rocket was one of the survivors — one of the few Guardians still present — and he spent those years working alongside Nebula, Natasha Romanoff, and Steve Rogers at the Avengers compound on Earth, doing what could be done in a half-emptied galaxy. He was not at peace with any of it. He was waiting.

When Scott Lang arrived with a theory about navigating the Quantum Realm — a space between dimensions where time moved differently — and suggested they could use it to travel back and retrieve the Infinity Stones before Thanos ever collected them, Rocket was already three steps ahead of the plan. The technology required was extraordinary and almost certainly impossible. Rocket thought it was probably possible. He started building.

He built the time navigation hardware that made the Time Heist real: modified Pym Particle dispensers, quantum tunnel emitters, and a relay system that kept all the Avengers' time coordinates synchronized across different points in history. Tony Stark handled the theoretical breakthrough. Rocket handled every piece of physical machinery that made it mean anything. It took days of sustained work at the kind of pace that required no sleeping and very little eating.

The teams scattered across time. Rocket went with Thor to Asgard in 2013, while the Dark Elves were attacking and the Reality Stone was temporarily loose. Their mission: extract the Stone without disrupting the timeline. Thor nearly broke it — his mother Frigga was alive in this year and he could not quite walk away from her — but Frigga, perceptive and warm, understood more than she should have, and sent him off with her blessing. Rocket watched and felt something complicated and true.

They brought the Stones back. Tony Stark built the Nano Gauntlet. The Hulk wore it and reversed the snap — the universe, all of it, came back in one sunlit instant. Then Thanos arrived from the past with an army, and the battle that followed was the largest Rocket had ever seen: Avengers, Guardians, Wakandans, Asgardians, sorcerers, all of them on the field together.

Rocket fought through all of it. When it was over — when Tony Stark, holding all six Stones, snapped again and Thanos's army was gone — Rocket stood in the ruins of the Avengers compound and felt something vast and certain move through his chest. He looked for Groot. Groot was there: tall, green, alive. Rocket did not say anything particularly moving. He just stood close.

That, for Rocket, was everything.
