Not every enemy comes at you with a fleet. Sometimes the most dangerous threat looks, at first, like a miracle. When the Guardians of the Galaxy received a distress call from a planet being attacked by strange, golden, pulsing tendrils growing up through its soil, they answered. The distress call led them to Ego — a being who called himself a Celestial, who had constructed for himself a beautiful, living planet of blue mountains and amber meadows, and who wore the face of a kind, silver-haired man so that he could walk among smaller beings and seem like one of them.

Ego was Peter Quill's father. That was the part that made everything complicated.

While the rest of the Guardians were drawn deeper into Ego's paradise world, Rocket stayed behind with Yondu — a blue-skinned Ravager captain who was Star-Lord's old mentor — and with Baby Groot, who was regrown from a single twig and approximately the size of a potted plant. They were the ones who noticed, from the outside, that something was wrong. Ego's true goal was to spread his Expansion — a network of living matter he had planted on thousands of worlds — and use Peter's Celestial energy to activate it all at once, absorbing every planet in the galaxy into himself. The tendrils that had been quietly growing through the soil of distant worlds would suddenly bloom, consuming everything.

Rocket worked through the night, reprogramming a mining pod, rewiring its core circuits, and packing it with Anulax batteries — the most explosive material in the known galaxy. Getting the bomb to Ego's brain, deep inside the planet's core, was the only way to stop the Expansion. Yondu's arrow could travel faster than any human hand, guided by his whistled commands, glowing blue as it cut through stone corridors. Together they flew down into Ego's hollow interior, Groot clinging to Rocket's shoulder, the bomb clutched between them.

The battle inside Ego was like fighting the planet itself — walls that breathed, tunnels that shifted, golden matter that reached and grasped. Rocket kept moving. He had not built this device to leave without using it. Groot planted the bomb at the core, tiny brave hands pressing it into place with a quiet certainty that made Rocket's chest hurt with pride. The detonation shook the planet apart from the inside out.

Ego was finished. His Expansion died with him. The Guardians escaped aboard the Milano, Peter Quill feeling the loss of his Celestial power as Ego's influence collapsed — but alive, and free of his father's terrible plan.
