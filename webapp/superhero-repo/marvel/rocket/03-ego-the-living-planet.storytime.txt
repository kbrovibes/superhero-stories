Once upon a time, a distress call came in from a planet being swallowed by something it could not name.

Strange golden tendrils — thick as tree roots, warm as sunlight — were pushing up through the soil of a distant world, spreading through the fields and forests, moving with terrible patience. The people there did not know what it was or where it came from. They only knew it was growing.

The Guardians of the Galaxy answered.

They found Ego.

From a distance, Ego's home world was one of the most beautiful places any of them had ever seen. Blue mountains rose from amber plains. Silver rivers caught the starlight. And on its surface stood a man — tall, silver-haired, warm-eyed, with a voice like the inside of a cathedral — who introduced himself as a Celestial. One of the oldest kinds of beings in the universe. A creature so ancient and so powerful that he had simply willed a whole planet into being around himself as a home.

He had a secret that changed everything. Ego was Peter Quill's father.

Peter had never known his father. He had grown up with Yondu's crew — Yondu, the blue-skinned Ravager captain who was rough and gruff but had always kept Peter alive. Meeting Ego felt like a miracle: a father, a home, a whole glittering legacy, all at once. Peter went inside. Gamora and Drax followed.

Rocket stayed by the ship.

His ears were flat against his head. His nose was working. Something about this world — the way it hummed, the way the air felt too warm, the way Ego smiled just a little too broadly — something was wrong. He could not name it yet. But he knew.

He sat with Baby Groot and with Yondu, and thought very hard.

Then the truth came out.

Ego had not been living quietly on his beautiful planet. For thousands of years, he had been traveling — planting tiny seeds of himself, living tendrils of golden matter, into the soil of world after world after world. Thousands of planets across the galaxy, a hidden network sleeping and waiting. He intended to use the Celestial energy inside Peter Quill to wake it all up at once. The Expansion: every planted world blooming simultaneously, swallowed into Ego, filling the universe where all those lives had been.

Rocket heard this and felt something cold and absolutely clear settle behind his eyes. He knew exactly what to do.

He went to the cargo bay and took out the Anulax batteries — the most explosive material he owned, saved for a special occasion. He spent the rest of the night carefully building a bomb. Baby Groot sat nearby and watched with total attention.

Yondu's whistling arrow was the key. It moved faster than any mechanical drone, guided by the tune Yondu whistled through his teeth, leaving a trail of blue light through the dark as it cut through stone. It would cut their path to Ego's brain — buried deep in the planet's hollow core.

Down they went.

Inside Ego was like being inside a living thing — which, of course, they were. Walls breathed. Corridors shifted. Golden tendrils quested through the dark, reaching and grasping. Rocket flew low and fast, one hand on the controls and one eye on Baby Groot, who clung to his shoulder with both tiny fists, eyes bright with a particular small courage.

"I am Groot," Groot said quietly, as they dove deeper.

"I know," said Rocket. "Just a little further."

The brain — a pulsing, golden, ancient thing at the center of Ego's world — was exactly where Rocket's calculations said it would be. Baby Groot scrambled forward on his tiny legs, bomb in hand, and pressed it into place against the core with the careful seriousness he gave to everything that truly mattered.

CLICK.

They ran. Or flew, rather, as fast as the engines could carry them. Up through breathing tunnels and shifting stone and grasping golden light, out into open air, into the sky, into the dark —

BOOM.

The sound reached them a moment after the light. Ego's world came apart from the inside, a slow and brilliant unraveling, golden light flowering outward as the ancient being finally fell.

The Expansion died with him. Every tendril on every planted world went still and cold in the same moment, all across the galaxy.

Peter Quill felt his Celestial power leave him as Ego collapsed — a warmth he had only just discovered, gone — and grief passed over his face for a moment. But he was alive. And Ego's plan would never touch another world.

The Milano sailed clear of the debris, engines humming, and the Guardians were quiet for a while.

Baby Groot climbed up onto Rocket's head and settled there comfortably.

Rocket let him stay.

Some victories cost something. But they were still victories. And they were still together. And that was enough.
