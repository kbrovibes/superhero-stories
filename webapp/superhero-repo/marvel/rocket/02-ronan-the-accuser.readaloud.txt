All right, buckle up — because this story has a really, really, REALLY bad villain in it. His name is Ronan. Say it with me: ROH-NAN. Good. Now picture this: black armor as heavy as a car, a hammer the size of a park bench, and a voice like a thunderstorm arguing with another thunderstorm. That is Ronan the Accuser, and he has just gotten his hands on something called the Power Stone.

The Power Stone is one of the six most dangerous objects in the entire universe. It is small and violet and it GLOWS, and it contains more raw energy than you can possibly imagine. And Ronan — who has decided that he alone gets to judge which planets deserve to exist — is pointing it straight at Xandar.

Xandar! A beautiful world of white towers and long, bright plazas where people go about their days in peace. It is home to the Nova Corps — the galaxy's most important peacekeeping force. Rocket has no time to admire the architecture, because the clock is TICKING.

What does Rocket do? He does what Rocket always does.

He builds something.

CLANG! CLATTER! SNAP! For forty straight minutes, Rocket tears apart every spare part in the Milano — blasters, engines, circuits, anything he can reach — and assembles a device that can blast a hole straight through Ronan's giant warship, the Dark Aster. The other Guardians take their fighters and dive into the battle above Xandar. WHOOOOSH — fighters weaving through laser fire! Explosions blooming like terrible flowers! BOOM — Rocket's shot hits home!

The Dark Aster comes DOWN. KRAAASH! It crashes into Xandar's surface in a wave of fire and dust. The Guardians rush through the wreckage, calling to each other through the smoke.

But Ronan is still standing.

He climbs out of the smoke with the Power Stone blazing violet in his fist, angrier than ever. His great hammer raises. The air around him crackles.

Then Peter Quill steps forward and challenges Ronan to a DANCE-OFF.

No, really. He does. With full commitment. Peter Quill starts dancing in front of the most dangerous being in the galaxy.

Ronan is so confused that he just... stops. His enormous head tilts. And in that pause — LUNGE! — Rocket, Drax, Gamora, and Star-Lord all grab for the Stone at the same time. The violet light EXPLODES through all four of them at once. It is enormous. It is impossible. It should not work.

They hold on.

Ronan falls.

Xandar is saved. The Nova Corps locks the Power Stone somewhere very, very safe. And Rocket, slightly singed and extremely pleased with himself, decides that saving whole planets is actually a pretty good use of a Tuesday.
