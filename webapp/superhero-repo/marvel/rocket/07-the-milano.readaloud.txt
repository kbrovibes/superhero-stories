Okay. You want to talk about the coolest spaceship in the galaxy?

I am going to talk about the Milano.

The Milano is the Guardians' ship. It belongs — technically — to Peter Quill, who found it during his early Ravager days and flew it all the way across the galaxy. It is roughly the size of a city bus, shaped like a sleek silver bird with wings that fold for atmospheric flight. It is blue and silver and has been through approximately twelve times more than any reasonable ship should have to survive.

And it is as good as it is because of Rocket.

Because yes, technically it is Peter's ship. But Rocket has been INSIDE every single system. He has rebuilt the engines. Twice. He has pushed the drive output to the maximum safe level and then a little bit past that, because "maximum safe level" is, in Rocket's view, more of a suggestion. He has rewired the thrust-vectoring array so that the Milano can bank at angles that should tear it apart — but don't, because Rocket calculated it exactly right. The Milano can hit sublight speed in EIGHT SECONDS. It can pull turns that make passengers hold on to their seats and wonder about their life choices.

And if things get REALLY urgent, Rocket has a secret setting called — and I am quoting him here — "oh no mode." You press one button, the engines scream, and the Milano goes faster than it was ever, ever supposed to go.

He and Peter have a recurring argument about who really owns the Milano. It goes like this: Peter says he found it. Rocket says he FIXED it. Peter says finding counts. Rocket says fixing more than counts. This argument has never been resolved. It probably never will be.

Inside, the ship has a cockpit with two seats, a navigation computer that has strong opinions, a cargo bay that is absolutely packed with things nobody has sorted, and Rocket's workshop in the back.

Oh, and one more thing. Near the engine bay, where the ship runs warmest? Rocket engineered a spot that is just exactly the right temperature for a certain tall tree-person to sit comfortably.

He has never mentioned this to anyone.

But Groot always goes straight to that spot, settles in with a small happy sigh, and Rocket — from the cockpit, pretending not to notice — goes just slightly quieter.

A ship is just a machine. That is what Rocket would tell you.

But there is no machine in the galaxy he has given more of himself to.
