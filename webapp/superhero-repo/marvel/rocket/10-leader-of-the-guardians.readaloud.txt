Here is something Rocket would never say out loud, but that everyone who has flown with the Guardians already knows:

Rocket is the leader.

Oh, he would argue with you. He would tell you he's just the pilot. Just the one who keeps the ship running. Just the one who builds all the weapons and does all the planning and notices everything that needs noticing before it turns into a disaster. Just the one everyone goes to when they need a real plan. Just that.

But that IS leadership, isn't it? That is exactly what it is.

After the Infinity War, after Endgame, after all of it — the Guardians changed. Some crew went their own ways. New people joined. The team shifted. And at the center of it, keeping the whole thing flying, was Rocket. Always had been, if you looked closely.

He would sit in the pilot's seat of the Milano and run through the pre-flight sequence — engines, shields, navigation, life support — and make sure every system was exactly right. He knew each Guardian's strengths. He built his plans around those strengths. He noticed when someone was having a bad day before they noticed themselves. He stayed up when everyone was sleeping to make sure the next mission was solid.

He never called it caring. But it was caring. All the way through.

And here is the thing Rocket finally understood, after all these years:

Being different is not the problem. Being different is the POINT.

Everything that was done to him on Halfworld — the enhancements, the implants, the way his mind worked, the way his hands worked — he spent a long time not wanting to think about it. Not wanting to be reminded. But then he looked back at what those things had let him DO. The forge at Nidavellir. The bomb inside Ego. The Time Heist hardware. The Milano. All of it. Built by someone who existed differently from everyone around him, and who was exactly right for every single one of those problems.

He was still sharp-tongued. Still loud. Still convinced that most beings were making avoidable mistakes, and honest about it.

Good. The galaxy needed that too.

Groot stood at his shoulder. Nebula flew beside them. The crew was new and old and a little weird in all the right ways.

Rocket settled into the pilot's seat, hit the engine sequence — HMMM — felt the Milano rise beneath him, and knew what he knew.

Family is not the people who made you.

Family is the people who stayed.
