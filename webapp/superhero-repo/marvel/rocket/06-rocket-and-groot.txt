In the entire Guardians of the Galaxy, no bond is stronger than the one between Rocket and Groot. They are opposites in almost every way: Groot is enormous and Rocket is small. Groot is made of wood and grows branches and produces soft bioluminescent light when he is happy; Rocket is built of flesh and cybernetics and produces a great deal of loud commentary. Groot speaks three words; Rocket speaks approximately all of them. And yet these two understand each other better than anyone else in the galaxy understands either of them.

They met in the middle of a bounty job that went wrong — which, again, is a theme for Rocket. Groot was working the same job from a different angle, and when the job collapsed around both of them and the Nova Corps showed up, they ended up in the same detention center, which eventually led to the Kyln prison, which eventually led to the Guardians. But even in those first messy hours, something clicked. Groot simply saw Rocket — not what he looked like, not what he'd been through, not the sharpness he used to keep people at arm's length — and was unbothered by all of it. Groot offered his company without conditions. Rocket, who had been alone for a very long time, did not know what to do with this, and so accepted it, grumbling.

The measure of the friendship came during the Battle of Xandar. When the Dark Aster crashed and it became clear the Guardians might not survive the impact, Groot wrapped his long wooden arms around all four of his teammates, growing his body into a sphere of living wood that would absorb the crash. It would cost him nearly everything. Rocket grabbed his arm. "What are you doing?" he demanded, and Groot looked down at him with that particular warm look and said, very simply, "We are Groot." The plural. All five of them, together.

Afterward, Rocket sat alone with the pot of mulch that was all that remained of Groot, holding the tiny cutting he had saved. He planted it. He carried it with him everywhere, protecting that little pot with more care than he gave his most valuable weapon, talking to it quietly in the dark when no one could hear. Baby Groot grew back slowly, leaf by leaf, expression by expression, until he was fully restored. Rocket watched every millimeter of that growth with a fierce and private relief.

The friendship between Rocket and Groot is not built on being the same. It is built on something more durable: the knowledge that you are seen completely, and wanted completely, exactly as you are.
