There is no one in the galaxy who builds things the way Rocket builds them. He can walk into a room full of unrelated scrap — torn engine casings, broken navigation chips, old food-prep machinery, one extremely confused robot arm — and walk out twenty minutes later carrying a weapon that could crack the hull of a warship. Other engineers need blueprints, specialized tools, and weeks of careful work. Rocket needs a problem to solve and whatever happens to be lying on the floor.

His personal arsenal at any given moment is a subject of deep admiration among some people and deep alarm among others. He has carried plasma blasters sized for beings four times larger than himself, strapped to his back with jury-rigged harnesses of his own design. He once built a prototype graviton cannon during a prison break, using components removed from the cell's own locking mechanism. He is fond of anything that produces a satisfying BOOM and never fires the same weapon twice if he can help it, because there is always something new to figure out.

The technology does not just run in one direction. Rocket is an exceptional pilot — arguably the best the Guardians have, though he will accept this compliment very grudgingly. He has flown the Milano through asteroid fields, through the wreckage of a collapsing space station, and straight into the hangar bay of an enemy warship at a speed that made even Star-Lord close his eyes. He built significant improvements to the Milano's drive systems, retrofitting the engine coils with modifications that increased sublight speed by roughly thirty percent and gave the ship a maneuverability profile that should not have been possible for a vessel its size.

The cybernetics that were given to him on Halfworld — the implants and enhancements the High Evolutionary's scientists put inside him — he does not talk about. But they are part of why his hands work the way they do, why his processing is faster than any purely organic mind, why he can keep track of a dozen simultaneous calculations and still be having an argument with Drax at the same time. He did not choose those implants, but he has made them his. He has made everything about himself his own.

His weapons workshop aboard the Milano is approximately the size of a large closet and contains more potentially dangerous material per square meter than the armoury of most mid-sized planets. Groot is the only crew member allowed to browse in there unsupervised, mostly because Groot has demonstrated perfect patience in not pressing any buttons without asking first. Rocket considers this an admirable quality, and the highest compliment he knows how to give.
