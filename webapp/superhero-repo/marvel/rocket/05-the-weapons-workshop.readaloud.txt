I need to tell you about Rocket's workshop.

Actually, first I need you to think about the most amazing LEGO set you have ever seen — the biggest one, the one with the most pieces, every single piece useful, all of them waiting for someone clever enough to put them together in the exact right way.

Now make it floating in space. And make most of the pieces technically capable of exploding.

That is Rocket's workshop.

Rocket can build ANYTHING. You hand him a broken toaster, three circuit boards, some engine parts, and an old robot arm, and come back twenty minutes later. He will be standing there with a weapon that can punch through the hull of a warship, which he will be holding with one hand like it weighs nothing. CLICK! He snaps the power cell in. WHIRR! He runs the targeting calibration. SNAP! The last component locks into place. BAM!

He is not joking around. He has never joked about weapons in his life.

His personal collection of weapons changes constantly, because he is always designing something new. Right now, somewhere in the Milano's cargo bay, there is a plasma blaster so large it is technically a piece of artillery, and Rocket has strapped it to his back with a harness he made himself. There is also — and this is my personal favorite — a graviton cannon. He built it during a prison break. Using parts from the prison.

He is also — and he will grumble if you say this too enthusiastically — one of the BEST pilots in the galaxy. He flew the Milano straight through an asteroid field once. He flew it INTO a collapsing space station. He flew it at full speed directly into the hangar bay of an enemy warship while Star-Lord was covering his eyes. The Milano can do things it was never designed to do, because Rocket spent about three months modifying the engine coils until the ship was THIRTY PERCENT FASTER and could turn on a point that should be physically impossible.

Now. The workshop itself. It is about the size of a large closet. It is absolutely stuffed with equipment. If you are NOT Rocket and you go in there unsupervised, you will almost certainly touch something you shouldn't, and things will get complicated very quickly.

Groot, however, is welcome anytime. Because Groot never presses buttons without asking. Rocket thinks this is the single most impressive quality a person can have.

Which tells you a lot about Rocket. And everything you need to know about Groot.
