After the Infinity War and its aftermath, the Guardians of the Galaxy changed. Some members left to find their own paths. New people joined — Nebula and others — and a new version of the team formed around a new center of gravity. Rocket became its leader.

He would not have described it that way. He would have said he was just the one who knew where the ship was pointed and how to fix the engines when they stopped. But leadership, for Rocket, looked exactly like that: keeping the operation running, knowing every person's strengths, planning around them, staying up when everyone else slept to make sure the next mission was ready. He was the one others came to when they needed a plan. He had been doing this, quietly, for years.

The lesson he had carried through Halfworld and bounty hunting and Nidavellir and the long five years after the snap was this: the things that make you different are the things that make you capable. He had spent years resenting what had been done to him — the implants, the changes, the way he looked, the way he existed differently from everyone around him. And then he had watched himself use every one of those differences to do something that mattered: build devices no one else could, fly ships no one else could, hold his team together through things that should have broken them.

He still had sharp edges. He still said exactly what he thought. He still had little patience for beings who overestimated themselves. These things did not go away. But the Guardians had never asked him to be different. They had needed him exactly as he was — strange and fierce and relentless — and that had been the thing that changed everything.

Groot stood beside him, tall and restored, bark warm and green. Nebula flew alongside them, finding in the Guardians a steadiness she had never had before. There were new crew, new missions, new corners of the galaxy that needed the particular brand of determined, wisecracking heroism that only this team could provide.

Rocket sat in the pilot's seat of the Milano, engines warm beneath him, the crew behind him on their various consoles, and understood something he had been circling for his entire life. Family is not the people who made you. Family is the people who stayed.
