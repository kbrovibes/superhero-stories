This is the story of where Rocket came from — and it is the most important story of all.

The being who made Rocket had a title: the High Evolutionary. Big name, right? He was a scientist who believed it was HIS job to improve the universe — to take living creatures, change them with science, and make what he called Higher Species. He did this work on a space station called the Arête, out in the deep dark of space, where no one could tell him to stop.

The High Evolutionary made Rocket. He gave him intelligence. He gave him engineer's hands. He gave him the most powerful brain in three star systems. But he did not do it because he cared about Rocket. He did it because Rocket was a subject. An experiment. Property.

Rocket spent years not thinking about this too hard.

But now the High Evolutionary has come looking for him. He wants his research back. He doesn't see Rocket as a person — just something he built and wants returned. He sends teams after the Guardians. And Rocket — tough, brave, NEVER-BACKS-DOWN Rocket — gets badly hurt.

And while he's hurt, he remembers.

He remembers the lab on the Arête. The cold lights. And three others — animals like him, changed like him. He had called them friends. More than friends. He had called them family. The Evolutionary had called them test subjects.

That quiet sadness? Rocket had been carrying it his whole life.

But here is what the High Evolutionary did NOT count on: Rocket's actual family — his REAL family — was not going to let this go.

Peter Quill! Nebula! Drax! Mantis! Groot! All of them tearing through the Arête, fighting through the Evolutionary's guards and creatures, searching for the code buried in his systems that would save Rocket's life. They did not stop. They did not give up. Not for one second.

And Rocket got back on his feet.

The Guardians reached the High Evolutionary. They freed every animal caged inside the station — every single one. They took apart everything he had built with so little regard for the beings inside it.

Rocket stood there at the end, his chin up, his eyes clear.

He had come from a lab. He had been made by someone who didn't care about him. But standing here, now, surrounded by the people who would burn down a space station to save him?

He knew exactly who he was.

The most dangerous, most brilliant, most LOVED raccoon in the entire galaxy.

And that was no one else's experiment. That was just him.
