Do you have a best friend? Someone who gets you — really GETS you — even when you're grumpy or having a bad day?

Rocket has one of those. His name is Groot.

Now, here is the thing about Rocket and Groot. They are just about the most different pair of best friends you could possibly imagine. Rocket is small. Groot is enormous — tall as a house, wide as a doorway, made entirely out of living wood. Rocket says everything that comes into his head, loudly, at top speed. Groot says three words: "I am Groot." Those three words mean many things depending on HOW Groot says them, and Rocket is the only being in the galaxy who understands every single meaning.

They met during a job that went sideways. There was a bounty, a misunderstanding, the Nova Corps at exactly the wrong moment, and suddenly both of them were heading for the Kyln — one of the most serious prisons in the galaxy. But in those first chaotic hours, Groot looked at Rocket. Really looked. He saw the sharp jokes and the crossed arms and all the ways Rocket kept people at a distance — and he wasn't bothered by any of it. He sat next to Rocket and offered to be there.

Rocket didn't know what to do with that.

So he said something sarcastic and then let Groot stay. Which is, for Rocket, basically the same as a hug.

But the story that shows you what this friendship really means — truly, completely means — happened during the Battle of Xandar. The Guardians were in the Dark Aster when it came down, and there was no time, no way out, nothing between them and the crash.

Groot knew exactly what to do.

He wrapped his arms around all four Guardians and grew — his body extending outward, wood weaving over wood — until he was a sphere of living, growing tree that curved around every one of them. Rocket grabbed his arm. "What are you doing? What are you DOING?"

Groot looked down at him. Those warm, quiet eyes.

"We are Groot," he said.

Not I. We.

KRAASH — the crash hit. Groot's body took all of it.

Afterward, Rocket sat with what was left — a small pot of mulch and one tiny cutting. He planted it. He carried that little pot with him everywhere. He talked to it in the quiet of the night. And leaf by leaf, expression by expression, Groot grew back.

Rocket watched every single millimeter.

Because that is what you do for your best friend. You hold on. You watch them grow. You let them see all the parts of you that you usually keep hidden.

And you say, even when it's hard: we.
