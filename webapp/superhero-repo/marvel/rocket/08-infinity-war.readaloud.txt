Ready for the biggest story in the whole galaxy?

It starts with a distress call in deep space — and the Guardians pull aboard a very large, very singed, very grateful warrior from Asgard named Thor. And Thor has terrible news.

His name is Thanos. Say it quietly, okay? He is an enormous, purple-grey titan who has decided that the universe would be better off with half its people gone. And he is collecting the six Infinity Stones to make that happen. He already has two. He needs four more.

The Guardians split up. Star-Lord, Gamora, Drax, and Mantis race off in one direction. And Rocket? Rocket goes with Thor and Teen Groot — Groot, now tall and lanky and mostly concerned with his video game — to a place called NIDAVELLIR.

Nidavellir is one of the most incredible places in the universe. It is a forge built AROUND a dying star. Not near a star — around one. A giant craftsman named Eitri once made the legendary hammer Mjolnir here, along with dozens of other weapons powerful enough to shake whole worlds. But when they arrive, the forge is dark. Cold. Thanos had already been here. Eitri is there, frightened and helpless, his hands destroyed so he could never build again.

Rocket examines the forge. His eyes go sharp. His hands start moving. CLICK. CLANK. SNAP. He gets down into the ring mechanism and starts working through the damaged components, rerouting, rebuilding, making the impossible functional again. He cannot fix all of it. He fixes enough.

But to actually RESTART the forge, someone has to stand in the ring and hold it open against the full power of a dying star's output — a beam of energy that should destroy any living being.

Thor does it.

He steps into that blazing beam, jaw set, eyes glowing with fierce light, and he HOLDS IT. The pain is tremendous. He does not stop. The forge roars back to life. Eitri casts the molten Uru metal — BOOM — WHOOOOSH — Stormbreaker, a new battle-axe, takes glowing shape. Groot grows his own arm into the handle to finish it.

They fly hard and fast toward Wakanda. ZOOM — but they are not in time.

Thanos snaps his fingers.

Grey ash. Silence.

Half the universe — gone. Including Teen Groot. In the last moment, Groot looks at Rocket.

And then he is just... gone.

Rocket stands on the grass in the quiet, small and still, one of the ones left behind.

He has survived every fight he has ever been in. He is going to survive this one too.

And he is going to fix it.
