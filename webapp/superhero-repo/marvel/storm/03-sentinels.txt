They came at dusk, moving between the clouds above Westchester, New York — three enormous Sentinel robots, each one ten stories tall, built from purple and grey steel and programmed for one purpose: to capture mutants. Their eyes glowed red. The robots had been built by the Sentinel Program, a government operation that feared mutants and believed they could be rounded up and controlled. The X-Men had faced Sentinels before, but tonight the machines had a new targeting system that could track Storm's electromagnetic signature across fifty miles.

Storm rose to meet them alone at first, letting Cyclops and Wolverine maneuver into position on the ground below. She called down a lightning strike that crackled across the lead Sentinel's chest plate and left a scorch mark the size of a school bus. The machine barely staggered. It raised one massive fist, and Storm banked hard to the left, diving through the cloud layer, using the mist to break the Sentinels' line of sight. She was fast in the air — faster than anything that large had any right to be — but three at once was a serious problem.

The second Sentinel fired a containment blast that clipped her cape and spun her sideways through the cold air. Ororo felt her control slip for one terrible second, the wind deserting her as she tumbled. She remembered her training at the Xavier Institute: breathe, feel the air, find the pressure beneath your feet. She found it. She steadied. A freezing headwind whipped past her ears as she righted herself and summoned a fog bank thick enough to swallow the entire valley. The Sentinels' optical sensors went blind.

That was what Cyclops had been waiting for. From the ground, his visor's optic blasts punched through the fog and struck the joints of the third Sentinel at precisely the angles Storm had described to him during their training drills. Metal groaned, and the machine went down. Wolverine had already scaled the second Sentinel's leg while it was blinded, his Adamantium claws shredding the servos in its knee until it folded.

That left Storm and the first Sentinel. She rose above the cloud line where the evening sky was clear and violet, took a slow breath, and built a charge that lit the clouds from within. Then she released it all at once — a thunderbolt so precise it traveled down the machine's antenna, through its head, and into its core systems without touching a single building below. The Sentinel went dark and fell like a toppled statue.

Cyclops surveyed the damage quietly. Three Sentinels in eight minutes. Storm landed lightly beside him, her white hair loose in the aftermath of the storm, and allowed herself one small, satisfied smile.
