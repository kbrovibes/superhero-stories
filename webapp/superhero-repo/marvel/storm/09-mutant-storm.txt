The worst the Sentinel Program ever threw at the X-Men came on a winter morning when a fleet of thirty Mark VII Sentinels deployed simultaneously across the northeastern United States, targeting every known mutant location at once. The Xavier Institute's alarms went off at 5:14 in the morning. Within three minutes, every X-Man was awake and moving. Professor Xavier's face, as he read the incoming reports, was very grave. This was not a skirmish. This was an attempt to end the conversation about mutants in the worst possible way.

Storm took command without being asked. Cyclops coordinated ground defenses. Jean Grey established a telepathic network among mutants across the region so no one would be caught alone and unprepared. Wolverine stationed himself at the Institute's perimeter. Storm flew straight up into the iron-grey winter sky, where the temperature was minus twenty and the wind was forty miles an hour, and the clouds above were low and thick with unshed snow.

What she built over the next four hours was the largest sustained weather event she had ever created — a nor'easter shaped and directed with an intention no natural storm ever possessed. She layered the snowfall to ground the Sentinels relying on aerial surveillance. She drove ice and fierce wind into the joints and sensors of the ground units. She called lightning in careful sequence, each bolt calculated rather than scattered. She was burning through energy reserves she rarely touched, and she knew it, and she kept going anyway.

By hour two she was exhausted. By hour three she was running on will and training alone — not physical endurance, but the ability to maintain precise control when tired, frightened, and absolutely certain it had to be her. Jean Grey's telepathic voice came through: sixteen down. Then twenty-four. Then six remaining.

The last six Storm brought down personally — dropping through her own storm, visibility near zero, the Sentinels' targeting systems scrambled by her electromagnetic field. She took three near misses from containment blasts and a glancing hit that spun her through a snowbank at speed. She stood up. She finished the fight.

When it was over she descended through clearing clouds to find the Xavier Institute standing, its windows full of warm light. Professor Xavier, Cyclops, Jean, and Wolverine were on the front steps, safe and whole. Ororo landed in the snow and stood in the quiet aftermath, the storm dispersing gently around her.

"Are you all right?" Xavier asked.

"Yes," she said. And she was.
