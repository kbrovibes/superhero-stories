Picture two teenagers crossing the wide green landscapes of East Africa. One is a girl with long white hair, just beginning to understand that she can make it rain. The other is a young prince who is quietly, seriously training to become the king of the most extraordinary country in the world.

Their names are Ororo Munroe and T'Challa — and years later, they would become Storm and the Black Panther, two of the mightiest heroes alive. But in that first meeting, they were just two young people, wide-eyed and brave, walking through a world full of possibilities.

Then life happened. Storm joined the X-Men. T'Challa became the Black Panther and king of Wakanda — the most ADVANCED nation on the entire planet, hidden in the mountains of East Africa, powered by a miraculous mineral called Vibranium that no other country has. Wakanda's technology makes the rest of the world look like it's still in history class.

When T'Challa and Ororo found each other again as adults — years and years later — all that time just… dissolved. Like morning mist when the sun comes up.

T'Challa brought her to the Great Mound — the sacred place deep in Wakanda's earth where Vibranium lies — and he asked her to marry him.

She said YES.

Oh, the WEDDING. Dignitaries from all across Africa traveled to Birnin Zana — the GOLDEN CITY, Wakanda's beautiful capital. Music. Ceremony. Color everywhere. Professor Xavier was there. Cyclops, Jean Grey, Wolverine — every X-Man in attendance, watching Ororo walk forward as the new Queen Consort of Wakanda.

Storm wore white and gold.

And the sky above Birnin Zana that day? People still talk about it. The most beautiful sky anyone could remember — all rose and lavender and deep, warm blue.

Not a coincidence.

As queen, Storm brought something new to Wakanda. She had grown up in Cairo's bazaars and the Serengeti's wide plains and the Xavier Institute's busy halls. She knew the whole wide world. She encouraged Wakanda to open its doors wider, to share its gifts with the people who needed them.

But she was still Storm. Still an X-Man. When duty called her back to Westchester, she went. T'Challa never once complained — he was the same kind of person.

The Serengeti girl from Cairo and the warrior-king of the most advanced nation on Earth. Side by side on Wakanda's great escarpment, looking out over a continent that was home to both of them.

What a love story. WHAT a love story.
