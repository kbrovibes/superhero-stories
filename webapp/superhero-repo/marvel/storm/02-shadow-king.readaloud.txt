Okay. Lean in close, because this villain is SNEAKY. He doesn't punch. He doesn't smash. He creeps in through your DREAMS.

His name is the Shadow King. His real name — before he became a terrible astral monster — was Amahl Farouk, and he used to be the most dangerous crimelord in all of Cairo. Professor Xavier beat him once, but Farouk's mind survived and went on living in the astral plane — that invisible world that exists behind reality, made entirely of thoughts and feelings.

And now? He had found Storm.

He slid into her dreams like smoke under a door. He showed her the worst memories she had: being trapped. Darkness pressing in from every side. Walls too close, air too thin. You see, Storm had one great fear — claustrophobia, which means being afraid of tight, enclosed spaces. It was left over from a terrible day in Cairo when she was small.

The Shadow King grabbed that fear and SQUEEZED.

One bad night. Two bad nights. Three nights in a row, Storm woke up gasping, heart POUNDING, certain the ceiling was falling toward her. By day three she refused to sleep at all.

But Wolverine — with his incredible nose that can smell a feeling before it reaches a person's face — noticed immediately. He sat beside her on the roof of the Xavier Institute and said: "You don't have to fight alone."

Jean Grey reached out with her telepathy and FOUND the Shadow King, coiled around Storm's mind like a cold vine. And Professor Xavier strode straight into the astral plane to fight him there — mind against mind, BOOM!

The Shadow King pushed back HARD. He flooded Storm's mind with dark tunnels, locked rooms, falling rubble — all the closed-in fears from her worst memories.

Storm felt herself getting smaller and smaller.

And then she remembered.

She was the woman who walked across the SAHARA alone. She was the Goddess of the SERENGETI. She was STORM.

She threw her arms wide — in her mind, in her dreams — and the open sky came rushing in. The Serengeti wind, enormous and free, so wide and bright that the Shadow King's darkness had NO ROOM left to live in.

CRACK — Xavier struck. The Shadow King scattered like smoke in a gale.

Storm's eyes opened. Sunlight. Real sunlight, warm on her face.

Wolverine held out a cup of tea. He didn't say anything. He didn't need to.

Storm laughed — bright and clear as a lightning bolt — and drank every drop.

BOOM! That's how you beat a Shadow King.
