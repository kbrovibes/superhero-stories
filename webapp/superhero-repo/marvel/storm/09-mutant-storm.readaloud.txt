Five-fourteen in the morning. Everyone is asleep.

BEEP BEEP BEEP BEEP BEEP.

Every single alarm in the Xavier Institute goes off at once.

Thirty Sentinel robots — THIRTY — have just launched simultaneously across the northeastern United States. Mark VII models, the newest and most powerful yet, targeting every known mutant location all at the same time. This is the biggest, most coordinated Sentinel assault ever attempted.

Professor Xavier reads the reports. His face is very, very serious.

Storm reads the reports beside him.

She doesn't wait to be told what to do.

"CYCLOPS — ground defenses! JEAN — telepathic network, warn every mutant in the region! WOLVERINE — perimeter!"

She walks out the front door and flies straight up.

Into the grey winter sky. Past the treeline. Past the low clouds. Into air that is MINUS TWENTY DEGREES with forty-mile-per-hour winds. Her white hair streaming. She spreads her arms and feels the whole atmosphere around her — the cold Arctic air pressing down from the north, the warm wet Atlantic rolling up from the east, all that energy a nor'easter is built from.

She reaches for it.

What Storm builds over the next four hours is the biggest weather event she has ever attempted. A nor'easter — not random, but DIRECTED. Layered snowfall to blind the flying Sentinels' cameras. Ice and fierce wind into ground units' sensors. Lightning strikes in careful sequence — CRACK, CRACK, CRACK — each bolt precisely placed.

Hour one: exhausting but manageable.
Hour two: really exhausted.
Hour three: running on pure training and pure will. The same will that walked the Sahara. That crawled through the dark tunnel to reach Skids. That pushed back the Shadow King. THAT will.

Jean Grey's voice comes through the telepathic network: "Sixteen down." Then: "Twenty-four." Then: "Six remaining."

Storm drops through her own storm.

Visibility: nearly zero. Wind: screaming. The last six Sentinels are somewhere in the blinding snow.

WHOOSH — a blast misses her by two feet!
WHOOSH — she banks hard left!
CLANG — a glancing hit sends her tumbling through a snowbank.

She gets up.

She puts her arms back up.

BOOM. BOOM. BOOM. Three. BOOM BOOM BOOM. Six.

Done.

Storm descends through the breaking clouds. The Xavier Institute appears below — windows lit, warm, intact.

Cyclops. Jean. Wolverine. Professor Xavier on the front steps.

Safe. All safe.

She lands. Snow crunches under her feet.

"Are you all right?" Xavier asks.

She looks at the clearing sky. She looks at her people.

"Yes," she says.

THAT is Storm.
