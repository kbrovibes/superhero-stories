Okay, quick question: what IS Storm's power exactly?

Just "makes it rain"? Just "calls lightning"? Oh no. It is SO much more than that. Let Storm explain!

One Tuesday morning, Professor Xavier asked Storm to take the newest X-Men students outside and show them what she could really do. They walked down to the big lawn by the Hudson River. Storm took off her shoes and stood barefoot in the cool grass — because she thinks better when she can feel the earth.

"I don't control weather the way you flip a light switch," she told them. "I FEEL it. Every current of air. Every charged particle. Every change in pressure and temperature. The atmosphere is like a living thing — and I speak its language."

She said she could feel a thunderstorm building three hundred miles away the way you feel a room go quiet when someone's upset. Not with her eyes. With her whole self.

"Let me show you."

WHOOOOSH — she raised a warm updraft beneath three students and they lifted two whole feet off the ground! Up like balloons! She held them there for five seconds — everyone laughing — then set them back down gentle as feathers.

Then she pulled moisture from the air until a tiny perfect cloud appeared above the lawn. She turned her hand palm-down.

Rain fell on ONE square yard of grass. Just that patch. The square right next to it? Completely dry. The students sitting in the wet patch yelped and everyone else laughed so hard.

But Storm also explained the serious part.

Her power is connected to her FEELINGS. Anger makes storms grow wild. Fear makes the wind go erratic. Professor Xavier trained her for years with meditation and breathing — not to get rid of her feelings, but to keep them steady so the weather follows her intention and not her mood.

"The most dangerous storm," she said, "is one I didn't mean to call."

And flying! She doesn't fly under her own power — she rides a column of air she generates beneath her feet, constantly adjusting like a surfer reading a wave. On a clear day? SIXTY miles per hour. With a tailwind? NINETY!

A student raised a hand. "Do you ever get tired of it?"

Storm looked up at the sky for a long, quiet moment.

"Never," she said. "Every sky is different."

WHOOOOSH — a playful gust sent every notebook on the lawn tumbling across the grass. She walked back inside smiling.

THAT is the power of Storm!
