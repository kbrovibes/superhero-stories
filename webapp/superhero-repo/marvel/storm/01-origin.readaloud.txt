Are you ready? Because this story begins in Cairo, Egypt — one of the oldest, busiest, most magnificent cities on the entire planet — and it ends with a woman who can call down LIGHTNING with her bare hands and ride the wind like a wave!

Her name is Ororo Munroe, and when she was just six years old, she lost her parents in a terrible accident. Six years old! Alone in a giant city, with no one to help her. What would you do?

Well, Ororo was brave. She found a clever street thief named Achmed el-Gibar who taught her how to survive in Cairo's crowded bazaars. She slipped through the marketplace like a shadow. Quick fingers, sharp eyes, always watching — she was the best pickpocket in all of Cairo.

But something STRANGE was happening inside her. When the wind rose up, she felt it in her chest like a song. When thunderclouds gathered over the Nile, her eyes flashed silver. Something was waking up. Something BIG.

When Ororo was a teenager, her feet just… started walking. South! Past the city, across the VAST golden Sahara Desert — hundreds and hundreds of miles — until she reached a place called the Serengeti plain in East Africa.

And there? WHOOOOSH. She raised her arms — and the rain came DOWN. She called to the wind — and it lifted her feet right off the ground! The people of a nearby village watched with wide, wide eyes and called her something wonderful: Goddess.

For years Ororo kept the Serengeti green and safe, steering storms away from villages and watering the dry earth. She loved it. She belonged there.

And then one day, a man with a kind face and a bald head and a wheelchair arrived. His name was Professor Charles Xavier. He said: "You are a mutant, Ororo. Your power is real. You are not alone. Come — there are others like you."

OTHERS like her! CYCLOPS, who fired energy beams from his eyes! JEAN GREY, who could lift anything with her mind! WOLVERINE, who had razor-sharp Adamantium claws and a nose like a bloodhound!

Ororo took a deep breath of Serengeti air and whispered goodbye to the golden plain. Then she flew — actually FLEW — north to Professor Xavier's school in Westchester, New York.

She chose a new name for herself: STORM. Because that's what she was. A living storm, dressed in white, ready to protect the world.

CRACK! Goes the lightning!
WHOOOOSH! Goes the wind!

STORM has arrived!
