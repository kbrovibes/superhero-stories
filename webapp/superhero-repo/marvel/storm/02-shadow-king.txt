Deep within a hidden corner of the astral plane — the invisible world of minds and dreams that exists behind ordinary reality — lurked a being of pure psychic malice called the Shadow King. His real name was Amahl Farouk, and he had once been a crimelord who ruled the streets of Cairo from the shadows. Professor Charles Xavier had defeated him years ago, but Farouk's mind had survived, living on as a hungry, formless thing that fed on human fear. Now he had targeted the X-Men, and his first victim would be Storm.

The Shadow King slipped into Ororo's dreams without making a sound. He showed her the worst memories she carried: the day she lost her parents, the cramped, broken building that had fallen around her in Cairo, the blind dark underground where she had been trapped as a child. Her claustrophobia — her deepest, most private fear — was the door he pried open. For two nights Storm woke sweating, gasping, certain the walls were closing in. By the third day she refused to sleep at all.

Wolverine was the first to notice. His senses were sharp enough to smell fear before it showed on a person's face, and Storm's fear had grown into something immense. "You don't have to fight alone," he told her, sitting beside her on the roof of the Xavier Institute while the Hudson River shimmered in the distance. Ororo did not answer right away, but she did not leave either. When Jean Grey reached out with her telepathy and found the Shadow King coiled around Storm's mind like a dark vine, the X-Men finally understood what they were facing.

Professor Xavier entered the astral plane himself, matching Farouk mind against mind in a battle that Storm could feel behind her eyes like distant thunder. But Xavier needed time. He needed Storm to hold on. The Shadow King pressed down on her with the weight of every closed space she had ever feared — dark tunnels, locked rooms, the rubble of Cairo — and Ororo felt herself shrinking.

Then she remembered who she was. She was the woman who had walked across the Sahara alone at fourteen. She was the Goddess of the Serengeti. She was Storm. She pushed back against Farouk's illusions with the one thing he could not fake: open sky. In her mind she spread her arms and felt the wind, enormous and free, and the darkness shrank. Xavier struck in that moment of weakness and scattered the Shadow King's consciousness across the astral plane.

Ororo sat up, breathing hard, with sunlight warm on her face. Wolverine handed her a cup of tea without saying anything, and she laughed — a real laugh, bright as lightning — and drank it.
