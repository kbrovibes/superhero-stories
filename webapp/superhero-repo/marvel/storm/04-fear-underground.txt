The Morlocks were mutants who lived in the tunnels beneath New York City, hidden from a world that feared them. When their distress signal reached the Xavier Institute, Professor Xavier asked Storm to lead the rescue team. She agreed without hesitation. But as she stepped into the narrow maintenance shaft and the stone walls pressed in on either side, the old fear arrived like a cold hand on her chest.

Claustrophobia was Storm's most closely guarded secret. It had been born in Cairo, when she was a child and a building collapsed around her, trapping her in rubble for two days before rescuers found her. The darkness and the weight of the stone had left something behind — a deep dread of enclosed spaces that no weather power could fix, because the sky was the one thing she could not bring underground.

She kept moving. Behind her, Cyclops navigated by the faint blue glow of his visor. Every few feet the ceiling lowered a little more. Ororo counted her breaths the way Xavier had taught her — not to calm the fear, because that was not possible, but to act alongside it. Fear was not failure. Fear was information. She could be afraid and she could keep going.

They found the Morlocks in a broad underground chamber. Callisto, the Morlocks' fierce leader, had organized her people behind a collapsed section of tunnel, but a second rockfall had trapped a young mutant boy named Skids on the wrong side. The only passage to reach him was three feet wide and twenty feet long.

Storm dropped to her hands and knees and crawled in.

The darkness was absolute. Stone pressed against her back and the floor pressed against her palms. There was no sky. No wind. Her heart hammered so loud she was certain it was bouncing off the walls. She kept going. One arm forward, one knee forward, breathe. One arm forward, one knee forward, breathe. She reached Skids in forty seconds. She checked he was unhurt, spoke to him quietly, and they crawled back out together — the boy following her voice in the dark, calm because she was calm.

When they emerged into the chamber, Callisto stared at Storm with something that looked very much like respect. Storm stood up straight and exhaled until her hands stopped shaking. Nobody mentioned the fear. She did not either. They led the Morlocks out through the tunnels, and when the first breath of cold outdoor air hit Ororo's face, it tasted like the open sky.

It always did.
