Here is the best kind of story: the kind where the hero becomes the teacher.

Three new students arrived at the Xavier Institute and they were scared. Not of villains — of THEMSELVES.

SURI could change the temperature of anything she touched. Sounds amazing! But she'd burned through three sets of doorknobs and was very tired of apologizing.

EMILE could call gusts of wind — but had no idea how to turn them OFF. His room was basically a permanent indoor weather event.

PETRA was twelve, and she could sense electrical fields in everything around her. It was so loud and so new that she'd spent a whole week not touching anything at all, just in case.

Professor Xavier asked Storm to help them.

First thing she did? Took them outside. They sat on the grass by the Hudson River and she told them the real story. Six years old and alone in Cairo. Learning to survive in the bazaars. The long walk across the Sahara. Standing on the Serengeti as the rain came down for the very first time. The claustrophobia she still carries. The Shadow King.

"You can be frightened AND powerful AND still figuring it out — all at the same time," she said. "Those things are not fighting each other. They're all true at once."

The three students went quiet.

Then: training!

For SURI — Storm explained that temperature is a SPECTRUM, like a dimmer switch. Not on or off. They practiced tiny increments, up and down, until one afternoon Suri shook Storm's hand without incident. She started crying a little. Storm said: "Excellent work." Because it was.

For EMILE — Storm took him to the roof. "Wind is pressure," she said. "You don't force it to stop — you equalize the pressure around it." Emile tried. A small miracle happened. The gust quieted.

For PETRA — Storm did something different. She sat with her for an HOUR by the Hudson River. No talking. No training. Just both of them feeling the river's electrical hum. Quietly. Like it was just an ordinary thing.

Petra's shoulders relaxed. She realized: feeling something isn't the same as being SWALLOWED by it.

In December, all three gave a demonstration for the whole school.

Suri held a SNOWFLAKE on her palm for thirty seconds. Perfect.
Emile lifted TWO INCHES off the grass on his own air column and held it.
Petra mapped the Hudson's entire electrical field from memory — accurate within ten feet.

Storm watched from the back. She didn't clap or cheer. Just a quiet, full smile.

Afterward Petra found her.

"How did you know we could do it?"

Storm looked at the three of them — uncertain, new, already becoming something extraordinary.

"Because I was you," she said.

And she was.
