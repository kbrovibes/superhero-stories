• Professor Xavier asks Storm to explain her powers to new students on the lawn by the Hudson River
• Storm explains she doesn't control weather like a switch — she feels the whole atmosphere as a living language she speaks fluently
• Demo: she lifts three students on a warm updraft, then sets them gently back down
• She creates a tiny cloud and makes it rain on one square yard while the next yard stays completely dry
• She explains her powers are tied to emotions — anger or fear can make weather go erratic
• Xavier trained her with meditation and breathing to keep feelings steady so weather follows intention, not mood
• She describes flying as riding a self-generated air column like surfing a wave — 60 mph on a clear day, 90 with a tailwind
• A student asks if she ever tires of it; she says: "Never. Every sky is different"
