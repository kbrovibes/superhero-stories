Okay, imagine this. The sun is going down over Westchester, New York. The sky is turning orange and purple. Birds are heading home. Everything is peaceful and quiet.

And then — BOOM BOOM BOOM — the ground starts SHAKING.

Three enormous robots appear out of the clouds. SENTINELS. Purple and grey steel, each one ten stories TALL, with glowing red eyes and feet that crack the road with every step. They were built by the Sentinel Program — a group that feared mutants and wanted to catch them all and lock them away.

And their target tonight? STORM.

She FLEW to meet them — straight up into the orange sky — while Cyclops and Wolverine got into position on the ground below. That's a team. That's tactics!

CRACK! Storm hit the first Sentinel square in the chest with a lightning bolt! The scorch mark it left was the size of a SCHOOL BUS. The machine barely even wobbled.

Uh oh.

BOOM — the second Sentinel fired a containment blast that spun Storm sideways! She tumbled through the cold air, the wind slipping away from her. For one awful second it felt like falling.

But she remembered her training. Breathe. Feel the air. Find the pressure. She FOUND it — steadied herself — and immediately called up a FOG BANK so thick and so white that the entire valley just… disappeared. The Sentinels blinked their red eyes. Their sensors went dark.

NOW!

PEWWW — Cyclops fired his optic blasts through the fog, hitting the joints of the third Sentinel EXACTLY where Storm had told him to aim during their practice drills. CRASH — down it went!

SHING SHING SHING — Wolverine had already climbed up the leg of the second Sentinel while it was blind! His Adamantium claws sliced right through the knee joints like scissors through paper. KRRRUNCH — that one fell too!

One left.

Storm rose ABOVE the clouds into the clear violet evening sky. She breathed slowly. She gathered the charge — you could see the clouds flickering from inside, like a lamp being turned up higher and HIGHER and HIGHER. She aimed with complete precision.

KABOOOOOM!

A single perfect thunderbolt. Down the antenna. Through the head. Into the core. The last Sentinel went dark and fell like a slowly toppling statue.

Three Sentinels. Eight minutes.

Cyclops looked up with wide eyes. "Eight minutes," he said quietly.

Storm landed beside him, her white hair loose in the storm wind, and smiled the tiniest, most pleased smile you have ever seen.

BOOM! Storm wins!
