There was a rivalry between Storm and Wolverine from the beginning. Not a hostile one — more like two strong instruments in the same orchestra learning to play together. Wolverine's instinct was to act first and explain later. Storm's was to observe, assess, and then act with precision. When they disagreed, they disagreed loudly, and the walls of the Xavier Institute had absorbed more than a few of those arguments.

But the friendship that grew from that rivalry was one of the deepest in the X-Men. It was tested during a mission to the island of Genosha — a place with old wounds in the mutant community, where the X-Men had gone to retrieve a young mutant named Lila Cheney. When the extraction went wrong, Storm and Wolverine found themselves separated from the team, deep inside a fortified Genoshan complex.

The facility had power-dampening technology that blunted Storm's connection to the atmosphere. She could still feel the weather — that sense never fully left her — but she could not reach it. Her arms felt heavy. The air felt dead. It was not unlike the feeling of claustrophobia, and she recognized it as something she could tolerate even if she could not enjoy it. Wolverine, for his part, was as capable as always — his Adamantium skeleton and retractable claws were biological in a way that technology could not easily block.

They worked through the complex the old-fashioned way: quietly, methodically, Wolverine cutting through the obstacles while Storm read the layout and directed them toward the quickest route. She found she trusted his instincts in close quarters the same way she trusted her own instincts in the open air. He did not second-guess her when she said "left," and she did not second-guess him when he said "now." There was a rhythm to it that surprised her.

They reached Lila in a lower chamber and were moving toward extraction when a security lockdown sealed three consecutive blast doors between them and the exit. Storm stood in front of the doors, placed her hands flat against the cold metal, and concentrated. Even through the power dampening, a thin thread of her ability remained — the smallest current, barely enough to light a bulb. She fed it into the door's electronic lock with absolute focus, and after forty seconds it clicked.

Wolverine looked at the open door. He looked at Storm. "You're not totally useless down here," he said.

She smiled. "Noted."

They came out into bright Genoshan sunlight with Lila between them, and Storm took one deep breath of open air and felt the sky fill her lungs again like something returning home.
