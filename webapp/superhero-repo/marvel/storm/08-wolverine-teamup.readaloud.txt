Okay. Important question. What happens when two very strong, very different X-Men get locked in a bad situation with NO backup?

You're about to find out.

Storm and Wolverine have always had a bit of a rivalry. Not a mean one — just… two strong personalities figuring out how to be on the same team. Storm likes to OBSERVE first. Assess. Then act with precision. Wolverine likes to act first and maybe explain later. Their arguments could get pretty loud. The walls of the Xavier Institute have definitely heard some things.

But underneath all that? Deep, genuine friendship.

And BOTH of those things got tested on a mission to the island of Genosha.

Genosha had a dark history — a place that had done terrible things to mutants — and the X-Men had gone to rescue a captured young mutant named Lila Cheney. But the mission went sideways! Storm and Wolverine got separated from the team inside a FORTIFIED GENOSHAN COMPLEX, and that complex had one very nasty trick: power-dampening technology.

What does that mean? It means: Storm's connection to the weather? BLOCKED. She could still feel the atmosphere far above — that sense never really leaves her — but she couldn't REACH it. Her arms felt heavy. The air felt dead and still. No lightning. No wind. No flight.

Wolverine, on the other hand? His Adamantium skeleton and retractable claws are pure biology. You can't block those with a machine.

SHING.

So here's the team: Wolverine handles the physical obstacles — doors, guards, locks. Storm reads the facility layout and navigates — pointing left, calling the fastest route, watching for traps. They find their rhythm FAST, and it surprises both of them. She trusts his instincts in the dark. He trusts her directions in the maze. No second-guessing. No arguments.

Just: good.

They find Lila. They start heading for extraction.

And then — CLUNK CLUNK CLUNK — three blast doors seal shut between them and the exit.

Storm put her hands flat on the cold metal of the first lock. She closed her eyes. She searched for whatever tiny thread of her power was still accessible through the dampening. It was barely there — thinner than a whisper — but it was THERE. She fed every last bit of focus and attention into it. Forty seconds. Thirty. Twenty. Ten.

CLICK.

The door opened.

Wolverine stared at it. He stared at Storm. He made a face that for Wolverine was basically a standing ovation.

"You're not totally useless down here," he said.

"Noted," said Storm.

They walked out into bright Genoshan sunlight with Lila safe between them, and Storm tipped her face straight up to the open sky and breathed in — all of it, the whole enormous atmosphere, right back where it belonged.

THAT is friendship. That is TEAMWORK.
