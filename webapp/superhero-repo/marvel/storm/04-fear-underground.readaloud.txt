Have you ever been afraid of something — and done it anyway?

That is exactly what this story is about.

The Morlocks were mutants who lived in the tunnels beneath New York City — hidden underground, far from a world that didn't understand them. They sent a distress signal to the Xavier Institute. Someone was trapped. The only way in was through narrow subway shafts: tight tunnels, low ceilings, grey walls pressing close from every side.

Professor Xavier asked Storm to lead the rescue. She said yes immediately.

But the moment she stepped inside — flashlight on, Cyclops behind her — the old fear arrived. Right there in her chest. Cold and heavy and completely real.

Storm has a secret fear: CLAUSTROPHOBIA. The fear of tight, enclosed spaces. It started in Cairo when she was a child and a building collapsed around her, trapping her for TWO whole days. Ever since, she's been afraid of places where the walls press in and the sky is nowhere to be found.

And underground? There is NO sky.

But Storm kept walking. She counted her breaths — one, two, three — the way Professor Xavier had taught her. Not to make the fear disappear. You can't just switch fear off! But you CAN act even while you're afraid. Being afraid and being brave is not a contradiction. You can be BOTH at the same time.

One step. Two steps. Three.

They found the Morlocks in a big underground chamber. Their leader — fierce, sharp-eyed Callisto — had everyone sheltering behind a rockfall. But a young mutant boy named Skids was trapped on the wrong side. The only way to reach him? A passage THREE FEET wide and twenty feet long.

Tight. Dark. No sky anywhere.

Storm looked at the passage. Her heart was POUNDING.

She dropped to her hands and knees and crawled in.

The darkness was absolute. Stone pressed against her back. The floor was cold and damp. No wind. No sky. Just her flashlight, her heartbeat, and her breathing.

One arm forward. One knee forward. Breathe.
One arm forward. One knee forward. Breathe.

Forty seconds. She reached Skids! He was cold and scared but unhurt.

"I've got you," said Storm. Calm as a clear sky. "Follow my voice."

Back they crawled — Storm first, Skids right behind, following her steady voice in the dark.

When they emerged, Callisto stared at Storm with wide eyes. Pure respect.

Storm stood. She breathed out — long and slow — until her hands stopped shaking. She didn't mention the fear. She didn't need to.

When the first rush of outdoor air hit her face? It tasted like sunshine. It tasted like the open sky.

THAT is brave.
