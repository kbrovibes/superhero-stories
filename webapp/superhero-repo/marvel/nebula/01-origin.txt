The galaxy is full of stars, and somewhere among them, a small child was taken from everything she knew. Thanos, the most feared warlord in the universe, adopted her alongside another girl named Gamora and raised them both in the cold halls of his warship, Sanctuary. He called them daughters. But Thanos was not a gentle father. He was a conqueror, and he trained Nebula and Gamora as warriors — pitting them against each other to forge them into weapons.

Thanos played a cruel game. Whenever Nebula lost a sparring match to Gamora, he replaced a part of her with a machine: a hand replaced with plated metal, an eye swapped for an implant that could see in the dark, whole sections of her arm and shoulder rebuilt with circuits and steel. He told her this made her stronger. But Nebula was brave enough to feel the truth — that the surgeries left her feeling hollowed out, as if the real Nebula was being replaced with someone harder and colder than she ever wanted to be.

She learned to fight in every way a body could fight. She mastered combat techniques, Kree battle strategies, and the piloting of every ship in Thanos's fleet. She trained until her mechanical fingers could bend steel and her reflexes were faster than most organics could follow. Still, she lost to Gamora again and again, and with each loss, more of her was replaced with circuits and plating. What Thanos never understood was that all those mechanical parts could not remove her heart.

Nebula eventually broke free of Thanos's command. Her path was long and difficult and full of wrong turns. She clashed with the Guardians of the Galaxy on Knowhere and Xandar. She fought people who had done nothing wrong, because she had been told her entire life that strength was all that mattered. But deep in the stars, she began to see clearly. She was not what Thanos had made her. She was something he had tried and failed to erase.

The day she stood beside Gamora — not as a rival, but as a sister — was the day Nebula truly began. All those mechanical parts, all those replacements? They were not marks of failure. They were proof that she had survived everything, and had chosen something better. The galaxy, it turned out, had been waiting for her all along.
