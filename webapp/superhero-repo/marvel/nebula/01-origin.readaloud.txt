Are you ready? Because this story starts with one of the most determined, bravest heroes in the entire galaxy — and she didn't start out a hero at all!

Her name is Nebula. Say it with me: Neh-BYOO-lah. She has brilliant blue skin, gleaming mechanical arms, and an eye that can see through walls. She looks like someone built her in a workshop — and honestly? Part of her was. But that's only part of the story!

A long, long time ago, far across the stars, a terrible tyrant named Thanos took little Nebula away from her home and brought her to his enormous warship called Sanctuary. He gave her an adopted sister named Gamora, and together they were trained to be the two most dangerous fighters in the cosmos.

Here's the thing Thanos did that wasn't fair at all: every time Nebula lost a practice fight against Gamora — CLANG! — he replaced part of her body with a mechanical piece. An arm. An eye. A shoulder. He said it made her stronger. But Nebula knew something he didn't: no amount of metal can replace what's inside your heart!

She practiced every single day. WHAM! Kick. CRASH! Block. WHOOSH! Leap across the training deck. She flew every starship in Thanos's fleet. She learned every battle move ever invented. She was incredibly, amazingly brave.

But bravery isn't just about fighting. It's also about choosing who you fight FOR.

One by one, Nebula started asking hard questions. Why was Thanos pitting her against her own sister? Why did he only care about winning? Was this really the life she wanted?

And then she made the hardest, most important choice of her life — she walked away from Thanos.

STOMP, STOMP, STOMP — right down the ramp of his warship and out into the wide, wild galaxy!

It wasn't easy. She made mistakes. She picked the wrong side a few times. But every step took her further from Thanos and closer to who she really was.

Eventually, she found her sister Gamora again. This time, instead of a sparring match, she reached out her mechanical hand — and Gamora took it.

CLICK! The sound of a real family, finally coming together.

Nebula the hero had arrived. And she was only just getting started!
