Get ready — because today we are meeting one of the scariest villains Nebula ever had to deal with: Ronan the Accuser!

Ronan was a Kree warrior — tall as a tower, wrapped in black armor from head to toe, with a face like thunder and a war hammer called the Universal Weapon that was bigger than most doors. He stomped around his massive warship, the Dark Aster, barking orders at everyone, and nobody — NOBODY — ever said no to him.

Nebula was assigned to stand beside Ronan as his enforcer. She had her cybernetic arm, her razor-sharp eye, and a whole lot of attitude. Together they were a pretty terrifying team.

Ronan's plan? Find the Power Stone — a glowing orb hidden on the abandoned planet Morag — and use its incredible energy to destroy an entire world. Thanos had promised to help him, and Ronan had promised Thanos the stone. Simple enough, right?

WRONG.

Because the Guardians of the Galaxy were on the case!

Star-Lord! Gamora! Rocket! Drax! Groot! They charged into action to protect the shining city-planet Xandar and all the people who lived there.

BOOM — the Dark Aster swooped in from above, the sky turning dark with its shadow!

CLANG — Nebula and Gamora crashed into each other in mid-air, trading punches above the golden towers of Xandar. WHAM! POW! Neither sister was holding back even one tiny bit.

But the Guardians had a plan. Ronan had seized the Power Stone, and it was glowing so hot and bright it could shatter an entire planet — KRAKOOM!

And then Star-Lord — yes, goofy, music-loving Peter Quill — grabbed the Stone with his bare hands.

"You're all crazy!" Rocket yelled.

But the five Guardians held hands and shared the Stone's power together, and then — BOOM — they blasted Ronan with everything they had!

The Dark Aster fell. CRASH! Right into the streets of Xandar.

Ronan was gone.

Nebula grabbed a piece of wreckage and rode it down to the surface. She stood up, dusted off her metal arm, and looked back at Xandar — still standing, still gleaming in the sunlight.

She had been on the wrong side. She knew it. She FELT it, right in the middle of her chest.

But you know what? Sometimes knowing you were wrong is the bravest first step in the world.

Nebula walked away, alone, into the stars. Not running away this time.

Walking toward something better.

And the stars? They were very, very big. But Nebula was ready for every one of them.
