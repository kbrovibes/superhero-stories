Five years after Thanos's snap, the remaining Avengers conceived a plan so ambitious it seemed almost impossible: travel back through time using the Quantum Realm, recover the Infinity Stones from the past before Thanos ever assembled them, and use them to restore everyone who had vanished. They called it the Time Heist. Nebula was part of the team, assigned to travel back to 2014 — to the planet Morag, where the Power Stone was hidden in a silver orb — alongside War Machine, James Rhodes.

The mission went wrong in a way nobody had anticipated. Nebula's cybernetic neural network was still partially synced with Thanos's systems — a legacy of his rebuilding work — and when past-Thanos's own ship detected a quantum signal, he reached across time and into Nebula's memory files. He saw everything: the Avengers, the Time Heist, the plan, the future. He captured present-Nebula and sent the past version of herself back in her place, turning her own timeline against her.

The past version of Nebula — the one who had not yet made her choices, who was still under Thanos's complete control — was used to help bring Thanos's 2014 fleet forward through time to the present. When the Avengers Compound erupted into chaos and Hulk used a new Gauntlet to restore the vanished, Thanos arrived with his entire army. The battle for the fate of the universe was fought in the ruins of the compound and the fields around it, and Nebula fought in it on the right side.

The hardest moment came when she faced the past version of herself. Looking at that Nebula — closed-off, furious, devoted to Thanos — was like looking at a mirror from a different life. She did not enjoy it. But she made her choice clearly and without hesitation, the way she had been training herself to do for years. She chose the future.

The Avengers won because of one extraordinary convergence: every hero, restored by Hulk's snap, came through portals from across time and space to stand on that field together. Nebula was already there, already standing on her own two feet, already on the right side, when the sky opened and the cavalry arrived. No one gave her a speech about deserving to be there. She simply was there. That was enough.
