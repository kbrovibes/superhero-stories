Not every world in the galaxy is simply a rock spinning around a star. Some worlds are alive. Peter Quill's biological father, a Celestial who called himself Ego the Living Planet, was the most extraordinary — and most dangerous — of them all. Ego had shaped himself into a beautiful planet, covered in rolling golden hills and blue flowers, with a handsome human form he used to walk among his visitors. He invited the Guardians to his surface and greeted Peter like a long-lost son. It seemed, for a moment, like something wonderful.

It was not wonderful. Ego had spent millennia trying to spread his consciousness across the universe, seeding distant worlds with his own living tissue. He called this plan the Expansion, and he wanted Peter to join him in powering it. Nebula arrived during the chaos that followed when Ego's true nature became clear — she had been a prisoner on the Ravager ship Eclector and escaped at the peak of the crisis. She found a galaxy in desperate need of her particular skills.

The surface of Ego changed the moment he revealed himself. The golden hills rippled. Tendrils pushed up through the soil. The blue flowers were not flowers at all — they were nodes of his expanding mind, spreading fast. Nebula moved through the chaos with precision, using her cybernetic eye to map the terrain. She found Mantis — an empath with antennae and extraordinary abilities — sheltering near the Milano. Together they worked to pull the wounded clear of the planet's growing tendrils.

The key to stopping Ego was deep underground at his living core. Rocket fashioned a bomb from the ship's power source, and the Guardians carried it to the heart of the planet. Nebula coordinated the evacuation on the surface, making sure everyone had a path to a ship before the countdown ended. This was not a celebrated moment — no one stood and applauded the blue cyborg moving methodically through the chaos — but without her work, people would have been left behind.

When the planet shook apart and the sky filled with light, the Milano — and every person aboard — flew free. Nebula stood at the rear viewport watching Ego's world dissolve into rings of golden energy. She thought about Thanos, who had also believed he had the right to reshape the universe. She thought about the difference between being powerful and being worth something.
