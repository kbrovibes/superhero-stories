The moon of Titan was once a paradise, an ancient and thriving world. By the time Nebula arrived there, it was ruins: shattered towers, cratered plains, the remnants of a civilization that had not survived its own power. It was on this broken moon that the largest battle of Nebula's life unfolded — a moment when heroes from across the galaxy converged in a desperate attempt to stop Thanos before he could use the Infinity Gauntlet.

Iron Man — Tony Stark — had arrived on Titan in pursuit of Thanos and was already building a plan when the Guardians' ship appeared from another direction. The two groups had never met, and their introduction was immediately a disaster: Star-Lord, Drax, and Mantis attacked Tony, Spider-Man, and Doctor Strange, each side believing the other was working for Thanos. By the time they sorted out that they were all on the same side, Thanos was already arriving. Nebula, who had been captive on the Sanctuary II, made her escape in the confusion and joined the assembled heroes on Titan's surface.

What followed was one of the most coordinated improvised battles in galactic history. Doctor Strange used the Time Stone — set in the Eye of Agamotto — to view fourteen million possible futures and identify the single path to victory. Tony built weapons on the spot with Stark nanite technology. Spider-Man used his webs to pin Thanos's arm. Mantis sat on Thanos's shoulders and used her empathic powers to hold him still. Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Star-Lord, Drax, and Nebula grabbed the Gauntlet from multiple angles, pulling and wrenching at fingers that held enough power to reshape reality.

They were so close. The Gauntlet's fingers moved — one came free — and for one agonizing heartbeat it seemed possible. Nebula braced every servo in her body and pulled with everything she had. But the hold broke, and Thanos, freed from Mantis's empathic grip, overwhelmed them.

Thanos won that day. But the battle of Titan mattered. It had shown Nebula something important: she had fought alongside Earth's finest hero, the galaxy's Sorcerer Supreme, and a teenager from Queens — and she had not been the weakest person there. She had been exactly what she needed to be. That knowledge would carry her through everything that came next, all the way to the final battlefield on Earth, where the last chapter of this war would be written.
