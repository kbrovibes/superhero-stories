Okay. Take a deep breath. Because this is the biggest story Nebula has ever been part of — and it involves the scariest villain in the entire universe.

THANOS.

Thanos was enormous and powerful, with a purple chin like a mountain range and eyes that showed no mercy at all. He had been hunting the six Infinity Stones for years — the Power Stone, the Space Stone, the Reality Stone, the Soul Stone, the Time Stone, and the Mind Stone — and he wanted to set all six into a golden glove called the Infinity Gauntlet.

His plan? Snap his fingers and make half of all living things disappear. Just like that. SNAP.

And Nebula — Nebula was his prisoner aboard the Sanctuary II.

She was locked up while Thanos tore memories straight from her cybernetic mind and used them as a weapon. Her own thoughts, her own files — turned against the people she cared about. Can you imagine anything worse? Nebula was absolutely FURIOUS.

Meanwhile, on the dusty, broken moon of Titan, the most amazing team-up was happening! Iron Man in his blazing red armor! Doctor Strange with his golden Eye of Agamotto glowing bright! Spider-Man swinging across alien ruins! Star-Lord, Drax, and Mantis — all fighting together!

They got SO close to pulling the Gauntlet off Thanos's hand. CLANG — one finger free! HEAVE — almost, almost!

And then it slipped away.

SNAP.

The universe changed. Half of everything — gone. Like stars winking out one by one. Nebula stood alone in the silence on Titan's broken surface, and the grief and the anger inside her fused into something new: a promise.

"I will make this right," she said to the empty sky.

And she DID.

Years passed. Hard, quiet years. Nebula worked alongside the Avengers — the ones who were still there. She went back through time, argued with herself, came VERY close to ruining everything — but she held on, because she always holds on.

And when the day finally came — the big, roaring, extraordinary day when everyone came back — Nebula was there on the battlefield.

Facing Thanos.

Not as his prisoner. Not as his weapon. Not as a daughter who would never be good enough.

As Nebula: a hero, standing her ground, every metal joint locked and every circuit humming with something even Thanos could not program into her.

Courage.

CLANG! BOOM! And she did not flinch. Not even once!

Because that is what heroes do. They show up. They stand their ground. And they never, ever give up — not even when things get the hardest they have ever been.
