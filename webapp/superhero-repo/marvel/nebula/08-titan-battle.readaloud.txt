Alright — I want to describe the most INCREDIBLE team-up you will ever hear about. This is the Battle of Titan, and it has everyone!

Titan was once a beautiful moon. By now it was just ruins — broken towers, dusty craters, a world that had seen better days. And it was about to see its most dramatic day yet.

From one direction: Iron Man! His red-and-gold nanite armor assembled around him with a thousand tiny CLICKS. Spider-Man, swinging between broken columns! Doctor Strange, with the Eye of Agamotto glowing at his chest!

From another direction: the Milano! Star-Lord, Drax, Mantis, and — having just escaped from the Sanctuary II — Nebula!

And then the two groups looked at each other and immediately started fighting each other because NOBODY HAD TOLD ANYBODY. CRASH! THWIP! "Wait, wait, wait — are you the bad guys or are we the bad guys?!" "WE ARE THE GOOD GUYS!" "SO ARE WE!" "...Oh."

Once that was sorted out, they made a PLAN.

Doctor Strange used the Time Stone inside the Eye of Agamotto to look at — hold on — FOURTEEN MILLION POSSIBLE FUTURES. He saw every way the fight could go. FOURTEEN MILLION!

"There is one way we win," he said.

ONE way. Out of fourteen million. Okay then!

Thanos arrived. THOOM — he landed on Titan like a mountain stepping down from the sky.

And the heroes went to work. Tony built weapons on the spot — WHOOOOSH, nanites forming blades and shields! Peter Quill dove in from above! Spider-Man spun webs around Thanos's arm — THWIP — and held it fast! Drax grabbed a leg! Nebula grabbed a hand!

And Mantis — incredible Mantis — sat on top of Thanos's massive shoulders and pressed her hands to his temples. Her antennae glowed. Thanos went STILL. Just like that! Held by the power of empathy!

They pulled at the Gauntlet. CLANK! One finger moved. Almost. ALMOST!

But it fell apart. And Thanos broke free.

That day, Thanos won.

But here is what Nebula knew afterward, standing in the quiet ruins of Titan:

She had fought beside Tony Stark and Stephen Strange and a boy from Queens named Peter Parker. She had pulled with every servo she had. She had been exactly what was needed.

Not Thanos's weapon. Not his tool. Not his punished daughter.

A hero. On the right side.

That truth was going to carry her all the way to Earth, to the final battle, to the day everything changed.

Hold on tight — because that day is coming!
