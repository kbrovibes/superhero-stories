Hey. I want to ask you something important.

Have you ever gotten started on the wrong foot? Maybe you made a mistake, or did something you regret, and you wondered — can I still make it right?

Nebula has an answer for that. And it is the best answer I have ever heard.

Here is the scene: the Milano is cruising through space. Stars everywhere. Rocket is in the engine room making extremely happy clanking sounds. Groot is growing flowers on the windowsill. Peter Quill has got the music going — something bouncy from his Awesome Mix tape. Gamora is flying. And someone — one of the younger heroes along for the ride — asks Nebula the question.

"How did you change? You were working for Thanos. Now you are a Guardian. How does that HAPPEN?"

Nebula is quiet for a moment.

Then she says: "I never stopped noticing."

Even when she was chasing the Guardians across Knowhere, enforcing commands on Ronan's warship, doing everything Thanos wanted — she was watching. Comparing. Asking herself: is this right?

And she kept coming back to one comparison.

When Nebula lost a fight under Thanos? CLANK — he replaced a piece of her. No comfort. No help. Just a punishment.

When the Guardians lost a fight? They helped each other back up. Every single time.

That difference mattered.

The change wasn't one big dramatic moment, she says. It was small ones. A thousand of them. Groot leaving her a flower without expecting anything back. Rocket fixing something just because it needed fixing. Gamora saying "you were always brave" and meaning it.

"Change is not a door you walk through once," Nebula says. "It is a direction you keep choosing."

And then she says the most important thing of all:

"The quality of your beginning does not determine the quality of your story."

Oh wow. Are you hearing that?

Nebula was taken from her home. She was shaped by a tyrant. She spent years getting things wrong. And NONE of that is what defines her. What defines her is every moment she saw clearly and chose differently. The courage to say: this isn't right. And the determination to do something about it.

Her metal arm? Hers. Her precision? Hers. Her loyalty — fierce as a solar flare once she decides you're worth it? ALL HERS.

She built her story herself.

And so can you.

WHIRR. CLICK. The Milano hums. The stars go by. And somewhere in space, the most determined hero in the galaxy is choosing — again, right now — to be exactly who she wants to be.

Pretty incredible, right?

Yeah. I think so too.
