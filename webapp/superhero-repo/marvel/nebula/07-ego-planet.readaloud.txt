I need to tell you about the strangest place Nebula has ever been. Are you ready?

Imagine a whole planet. Rolling golden hills. Shimmering blue flowers everywhere. A sky full of light. Beautiful, right?

Now imagine the planet is ALIVE. And it is ANGRY.

That is Ego the Living Planet!

Ego was a Celestial — an ancient, enormously powerful being — who had shaped himself into a world. He even had a human body he walked around in, with a big smile and kind eyes. He invited the Guardians to visit and told Peter Quill, "I am your father." GASP!

But Ego had a terrible secret. His plan — the Expansion — was to spread his living consciousness to EVERY planet in the universe. He had been planting seeds of his own body on thousands of worlds for millennia, and he wanted Peter to help him power the whole terrifying project.

When the Guardians discovered the truth? CHAOS!

Nebula had just escaped from a Ravager ship called the Eclector — she had been their prisoner — and she arrived on Ego's surface at the exact worst moment. The golden hills were RIPPLING! Huge tendrils were pushing up from the ground! The blue flowers were turning out to be pieces of Ego's mind!

Nebula went straight to work.

Her cybernetic eye scanned the terrain — BZZT, mapping every path. Her blade cleared tendrils blocking the way. She found Mantis — a gentle empath with long antennae who could feel other people's emotions — near the Milano, looking very frightened.

"Let's go," Nebula said.

Together they pulled people away from the growing chaos and got them to safety. Methodical. Precise. Completely unstoppable.

Meanwhile — underground — Rocket had built a BOMB from the Milano's power source! The Guardians were carrying it to the glowing core at the center of Ego's living brain. BOOM was coming!

Nebula kept the evacuation moving. Every person, every path, every ship — she made sure no one was left behind.

And then the countdown ended. KRAKOOM!

Ego's planet shook. And shook. And came apart in expanding rings of golden light, beautiful and enormous and final.

The Milano shot free of the explosion — everyone aboard, everyone safe.

Nebula watched from the rear viewport as the rings of light spread and faded.

She thought about Thanos. She thought about Ego. Both of them believed they should control everything.

And she thought: being a person — small, complicated, choosing your own way — is much better than being a tyrant.

She was pretty sure about that now.
