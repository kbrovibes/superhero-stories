There is a question that the youngest members of the Guardians of the Galaxy eventually ask Nebula, usually during a long flight between star systems when the ship is quiet and there is nothing to do but talk. The question is: how did you change? You were on the wrong side, and now you are on the right side. How do you do that?

Nebula usually takes a moment before answering. She is not someone who answers quickly when the question matters.

The honest answer, she tells them, is that she never stopped noticing. Even when she was doing what Thanos wanted — chasing the Guardians, enforcing his commands, fighting on Ronan's warship — she was watching and comparing and asking herself, very quietly, whether any of it was worth it. She did not like the answers she kept getting. She kept going back to the same comparison: on one side, Thanos, who replaced pieces of her whenever she fell short. On the other, the Guardians, who got knocked down and helped each other back up.

The change was not a single moment. It was a thousand small ones. It was every time Groot left her a flower without expecting anything back. It was every time Rocket fixed something just because it needed fixing, with no benefit to himself. It was every conversation with Gamora that peeled away one more layer of old armor. Change, Nebula says, is not a door you walk through once. It is a direction you keep choosing.

She tells them something else, too, which matters for anyone who has ever felt like they got started on the wrong foot: the quality of your beginning does not determine the quality of your story. She was taken from her home and shaped by a tyrant and spent years believing his version of her. None of that is what defines Nebula. What defines her is every moment she saw clearly and chose differently. The cyborg parts? They are hers now. The fighting skill? Hers. The stubbornness, the precision, the loyalty so fierce it could bend steel? All hers, earned through choices that had nothing to do with Thanos.

The Milano hums around her. Gamora is in the cockpit. Rocket is in the engine room doing something that makes a sound like a very happy drum. Groot is growing flowers for the windowsill. Peter Quill has put on a song from his Awesome Mix that Nebula has now heard forty times and does not entirely object to.

This is her life. She built it herself, one decision at a time, out of the difficult materials she was given. She thinks it is, on the whole, excellent.
