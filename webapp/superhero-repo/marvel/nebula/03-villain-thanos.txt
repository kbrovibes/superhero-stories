Of all the battles Nebula ever fought, the hardest was not against a Kree fleet or a rival army. It was against the man who had raised her: Thanos. He had placed the Infinity Gauntlet on his fist — a golden glove set with six colored Infinity Stones, each one controlling a different force of reality — and he was about to do something unthinkable. He intended to use the Gauntlet to erase half of all living things in the universe, just like that, with a snap of his fingers.

Nebula had been captured aboard Thanos's ship, the Sanctuary II, and she had a painful, first-row seat to what the man she had called father truly was. He had torn memories directly from her mind to use as a weapon against the Guardians on the planet Titan. She had been rebuilt and rewired against her will, her own cybernetic files used to betray the people she was beginning to care about. Sitting in chains, watching Thanos move through the universe as though nothing and no one could stop him, Nebula felt the old rage flare up — not the blind, helpless rage of a girl who had lost a sparring match, but the clear, focused anger of someone who knows exactly what is wrong and what needs to be done.

On the ruined moon Titan, the Avengers and the Guardians laid a desperate trap. Iron Man, Doctor Strange, Spider-Man, Star-Lord, Drax, and Mantis fought together against Thanos, coming impossibly close to removing the Gauntlet from his hand. Nebula joined the fight the moment she could, driving her blade and her determination against the tyrant who had made her. She was not afraid of him. She had never been afraid of him, not really — she had been afraid of being nothing without him, which was a different thing entirely. And she was not nothing. She was Nebula.

The battle on Titan was lost. Thanos snapped his fingers, and the universe changed in an instant. But Nebula did not disappear. She was still there, still standing on the rubble of Titan, still breathing. And she made a promise to herself in that terrible silence: she would make things right.

That promise carried her across years and light-years. It carried her to work alongside the remaining Avengers, to push through grief and doubt, and finally to the moment when the snapped lives were restored and she faced Thanos across a ruined battlefield on Earth. She stood her ground. She did not flinch. Every piece of metal in her body held steady, and so did the heart beneath it all.
