The planet Sakaar was a harsh, unforgiving world, a place where the strong thrived and the weak were discarded like refuse. It was here that the Hulk found himself after being exiled from Earth by the Illuminati, a group of heroes who believed he was too dangerous to remain. He was no longer a hero or a monster; he was a slave, forced to wear a disc that dampened his strength and compelled him to fight in the Great Arena for the amusement of the Red King. The Hulk, however, was not easily broken. He rose through the ranks of the gladiators, his green skin and unmatched ferocity earning him a new name: the Green Scar. For the first time in his life, he wasn't just hated; he was revered as a warrior who could challenge the gods themselves.

In the pits of Sakaar, the Hulk found something he had never truly had on Earth: a family. He formed a bond with his fellow gladiators—Miek, Korg, and Hiroim—and together they became the Warbound. They swore an oath to stand by one another until the end, a brotherhood forged in blood and sand. The Hulk’s journey from slave to savior began as he led a rebellion against the tyrannical Red King, eventually toppling the empire and taking the throne for himself. As king, he brought peace to Sakaar, marrying the warrior Caiera and finally finding the happiness that had eluded Bruce Banner for decades. For a brief moment, the Hulk was not a curse, but a king who had built a paradise for his people.

But tragedy followed the Hulk like a shadow. An explosion from the shuttle that brought him to Sakaar devastated the planet, killing millions, including his beloved Caiera. The grief that consumed the Hulk was more powerful than any rage he had ever felt. He believed that the heroes of Earth had intentionally planted the bomb, and he swore a vow of vengeance that would shake the foundations of the universe. The "Green Scar" was gone, replaced by a version of the Hulk whose power was so great that every step he took threatened to crack the tectonic plates of the planet. He gathered his Warbound and set a course for Earth, intending to make his former friends pay for what they had taken from him.

The story of Planet Hulk is a saga of transformation and the search for belonging. It showed that the Hulk was more than just a mindless beast; he was a leader, a husband, and a man capable of building a civilization. Sakaar provided him with a mirror of his own internal struggle, a world where violence was a way of life but where honor could still be found. Even after the destruction of his kingdom, the memory of Sakaar remained a pivotal part of the Hulk’s identity. It was the place where he finally stopped running and started fighting for something larger than himself. The Green Scar’s legacy would haunt the Marvel Universe for years to face, a reminder that even the strongest hero can be pushed to the breaking point by the loss of everything he loves.
