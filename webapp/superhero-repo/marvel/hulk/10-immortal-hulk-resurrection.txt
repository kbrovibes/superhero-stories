The concept of the Hulk changed forever with the realization that he is not just a biological mutation, but a creature of supernatural significance. This is the era of the "Immortal Hulk," a time when Bruce Banner discovered that no matter how many times he died, the Hulk would always bring him back. Death was no longer the end; it was merely a revolving door. Every time Banner’s heart stopped, he would find himself in the "Below-Place," a hellish dimension of green light and cosmic horror. When night fell, the door would open, and the Hulk would emerge, stronger and more vengeful than ever before. This new understanding transformed the Hulk from a superhero story into a psychological horror saga, exploring the dark, eldritch roots of gamma radiation.

The Immortal Hulk is a creature of the night, a being whose power is tied to the phases of the moon and the depths of the human psyche. He is more articulate than previous versions of the Hulk, possessing a cruel sense of humor and a philosophical bent. He views himself as the "Devil Hulk," a protector of Bruce Banner who is willing to destroy the entire world to keep his human host safe. This version of the Hulk isn't interested in being a hero; he wants to expose the hypocrisy of humanity and the systems that oppress the weak. He is a force of judgment, a green-skinned reaper who haunts the shadows of the world, dealing out a brutal form of justice to those who deserve it.

This period of the Hulk’s life was also marked by the return of many of his greatest enemies, all of whom had been changed by their own experiences with death and the Below-Place. The Leader, in particular, sought to harness the power of the "Green Door" to become a god-like entity. The battle for the soul of Bruce Banner reached a cosmic scale, involving entities like the One-Below-All, the dark mirror of the Creator. Banner was forced to confront the truth about his father, his trauma, and the origin of his rage. He realized that the Hulk wasn't just an accident; he was a necessary part of the universe's balance, a creature of destruction that was required for the eventual rebirth of the world.

The Immortal Hulk redefined what it meant to be a gamma-mutate. It showed that radiation wasn't just energy; it was a connection to a deeper, more terrifying reality. Bruce Banner finally accepted that he and the Hulk were one, an eternal entity that would outlive the stars themselves. The horror of their existence was tempered by a sense of profound purpose. They were the world's ultimate survivors, the ones who would stand at the end of time and turn out the lights. The story of the Immortal Hulk is a journey through the heart of darkness, a reminder that even in our most terrifying moments, there is a strength that can never be broken. The green door remains ajar, a symbol of the eternal struggle and the infinite resilience of the strongest one there is.
