When the Hulk returned from Sakaar, burning with grief and fury, his power had grown to something the world had never seen before. He was now called the World Breaker. His footsteps shook the ground. His roar silenced the sky.

He arrived first at the moon, where the powerful Inhuman king Black Bolt challenged him. One single blow, and it was over. Then the Hulk turned toward New York City.

One by one, the greatest heroes on Earth stepped forward to stop him. Iron Man flew in wearing a mighty Hulkbuster suit of armour — torn apart like paper. Doctor Strange called on his most powerful spells — not enough. The Hulk forced them into a makeshift arena right inside Madison Square Garden, demanding they feel what it had been like to be helpless, the way he had been helpless on Sakaar. Even the Sentry — the strongest individual on Earth — joined the battle, and their clash sent shockwaves crashing across the entire Eastern Seaboard.

But then, right at the very peak of the war, the truth came out. The bomb that had destroyed Sakaar and taken Caiera from him — it had not been planted by the Earth's heroes. It had been planted by Miek, one of the Hulk's own Warbound, who had wanted to keep the Hulk forever furious.

And do you know what that knowledge did to the World Breaker? It broke him. Not his body, but his heart. He had nearly destroyed a whole city over a lie.

From that day on, the heroes of Earth understood something important: the Hulk was the strongest one there is. But more than that — he had a soul worth fighting to save, and a lesson to teach about the cost of letting people down.
