If the Hulk is the strongest there is, what about the cleverest? That is where the villain called the Leader comes in — and he is one of the most dangerous minds the Hulk has ever faced.

Samuel Sterns used to be an ordinary person, working a simple job in a chemical plant. Then one day, radioactive waste splashed over him, and something extraordinary and frightening happened. Instead of growing larger and stronger like the Hulk, Sterns' mind exploded with power. His skull swelled to hold a brain thousands of times more efficient than any normal person's. His skin turned green. And he gave himself a title: the Leader.

The Leader does not punch or kick. He plots. He can imagine millions of different futures in just a second, planning out exactly what his enemies will do before they even know what they are planning themselves. He built secret organisations, created strange weapons, and even grew his own army of gamma-powered creatures called Humanoids to do his bidding. All of it aimed at one goal: capturing the Hulk and unlocking his secrets.

But here is what the Leader never managed to understand, no matter how brilliant he was. He thought the world ran on logic. He forgot about something the Hulk had plenty of — raw, real, unstoppable feeling. And Bruce Banner, hiding inside the Hulk, had a scientist's mind of his own.

Do you know what happened every time the Leader built his perfect trap? The Hulk smashed his way out of it anyway, doing something the Leader's brilliant brain had never once predicted.

And that is how heart and fury kept defeating pure cleverness, again and again and again.
