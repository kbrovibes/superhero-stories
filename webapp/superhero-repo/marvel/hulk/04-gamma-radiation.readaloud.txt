Everything about the Hulk begins with a kind of energy — a very special, very powerful kind of light called gamma radiation. Gamma rays are the most energetic waves in the whole universe. They can travel through almost anything, and normally, if they passed through a human body, the result would be very bad indeed.

But Dr. Bruce Banner was different. Hidden inside his DNA was something scientists call a Gamma-Gene — a tiny dormant switch that had been there his whole life, just waiting. When the G-Bomb exploded and flooded Bruce with gamma energy, instead of ending him, the radiation flipped that switch. His body began to change, and the Hulk was born.

The science of it is remarkable. Every time Bruce transforms, gamma energy surges through his cells, making his bones denser and his muscles unimaginably large. His skin turns that famous green colour — a permanent sign of the radiation that courses through him like liquid fire. And the more furious the Hulk becomes, the more energy floods his body, and the stronger he grows.

But here is the most curious part. Gamma radiation does not just change the body — it seems to reach inside a person and bring out what is deepest within them. For Bruce, that was his hidden rage and fear. For others who were touched by gamma energy, like the Abomination and the Leader, it brought out cruelty and hunger for power.

Do you know what Bruce eventually understood, after years of trying to undo what had happened to him? The gamma energy was not something separate from him. It was part of him — as much a part of him as his brilliant mind, or his kind heart.

And that is how one invisible wave of energy changed the world forever.
