There was once a dangerous man named Emil Blonsky who looked at the Hulk and saw something Bruce Banner never wanted to see — opportunity. Blonsky was a spy who believed that power was everything, and he wanted the Hulk's power for himself.

He sneaked into the gamma testing facility and deliberately bathed himself in a huge dose of gamma radiation — far more than Bruce had ever received. He expected to become something magnificent. Instead, he became the Abomination: a scaly, reptilian monster with ridges running along his skull and a ferocious strength that matched the Hulk's own. And unlike the Hulk, Blonsky never changed back. He was trapped in that monstrous form forever. That fact made him very, very angry.

When the Abomination and the Hulk first clashed, the ground shook for miles around. Every punch landed like an earthquake. The Abomination laughed and taunted the Hulk, because at first he was winning. He was bigger, colder, designed purely for fighting.

But here is the secret the Abomination never understood: the Hulk is not like any other fighter in the world. The angrier the Hulk gets, the stronger he gets. And the more Blonsky mocked and sneered, the more the Hulk's rage grew — and grew — and grew.

Do you know what happened next? The Hulk's fists became unstoppable. Blow after massive blow cracked the Abomination's thick hide, until at last the villain went flying through a mountain range and the ground went quiet.

And that is how the Hulk won — not because he planned cleverly, but because a good heart, even a very furious one, will always outlast pure cruelty.
