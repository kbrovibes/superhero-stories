Psssst — lean in close, because I want to tell you about the day someone tried to STEAL the Hulk's power. And it did NOT go the way he planned!

His name was Emil Blonsky — a sneaky, cold-hearted spy who watched the Hulk from the shadows and thought: "I want THAT." Not to help anyone. Not to protect anyone. Just to have the power for himself. Can you imagine anything more selfish?

Blonsky crept into a secret gamma testing facility and found a machine that could bathe someone in radiation. And here is the wild part — he deliberately turned it up to an even HIGHER setting than the one that had changed Bruce Banner! He thought more radiation meant more power. He wasn't wrong. But he also didn't count on the part where he would turn into something truly terrifying.

ZZZZAP! CRACKLE! SIZZLE!

His body twisted and stretched. Scales erupted across his skin! Ridges grew along his skull! When it was over, Emil Blonsky was gone — and in his place stood the Abomination: a scaly, reptilian monster twice the size of a person, with enormous strength and a very, very bad temper.

And — this is the part that made the Abomination especially scary — unlike the Hulk, he NEVER changed back. He was stuck in that monstrous shape forever and ever. No time off. No rest. Just monster, all day long.

When the Abomination charged at the Hulk, the ground SHOOK for miles! Every punch landed like a miniature earthquake. WHAM! CRACK! BOOM! The Abomination laughed and sneered because at first — he was winning! He had been built purely for fighting, with sharper wits and no need to control his temper.

But here is the secret that Emil Blonsky, for all his calculating intelligence, completely missed. The Hulk gets stronger the angrier he gets. And the more Blonsky mocked and taunted and sneered — "You're nothing but a mindless brute!" — the more the Hulk's fury grew. And grew. And GREW.

Are you ready for this? The Hulk's power surpassed everything Blonsky had calculated! The next swing cracked the Abomination's thick scaly hide! CRUNCH! And the swing after that sent him flying through an entire mountain range — CRASH! — until the dust settled and the ground went beautifully, wonderfully quiet.

The Abomination was the dark mirror of everything the Hulk could have been — if Bruce had been selfish instead of brave. Instead, the Hulk won. Because courage and fury together? They beat cruelty every time.
