• Discovery: the Hulk is not just a biological mutation — he is supernatural; each time Banner dies, the Hulk brings him back
• Banner finds himself in the "Below-Place" — a hellish dimension of green light and cosmic horror — every time he perishes
• At night, the "Green Door" opens and the Immortal Hulk emerges, stronger and more vengeful than before
• This version is more articulate — possesses a cruel humor and philosophical bent; calls himself the "Devil Hulk"
• Views himself as a protector of Banner willing to destroy the world to keep his human host safe
• Not interested in heroism — wants to expose the hypocrisy of systems that oppress the weak
• The Leader returns, seeking to use the "Green Door" to become a god-like entity
• Cosmic battle involving the One-Below-All, dark mirror of the Creator, and the soul of Bruce Banner
• Banner forced to confront truth about his father, his childhood trauma, and the origin of his rage
• Final revelation: Hulk was not an accident; he is a necessary part of the universe's balance — born for the rebirth of all things
