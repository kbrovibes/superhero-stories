Out in the vast New Mexico desert, beneath a blazing sun, a very clever and quiet scientist named Dr. Bruce Banner stood watch over his greatest invention — a powerful device called the Gamma Bomb. Bruce had studied gamma radiation his whole life, dreaming of using its incredible energy to help people. But today, the military wanted it used as a weapon, and that sat heavily on his heart.

Then, just as the countdown began, something terrible happened. A young man named Rick Jones drove his old jeep straight onto the testing range. He had no idea what was about to happen. Bruce did not hesitate for even one second. He ran out of the safety bunker as fast as his legs would carry him, grabbed Rick, and threw him into a deep protective trench.

But Bruce himself had no time to reach safety. The Gamma Bomb went off in a blinding wall of green light, and every single one of Bruce's cells was flooded with gamma radiation.

He should not have survived. But he did — changed completely. That very night, the pain came. His bones stretched, his muscles swelled, and where Dr. Bruce Banner had stood, a massive gray-skinned creature roared up at the moon. The Hulk had arrived.

General Thunderbolt Ross and the whole military immediately began chasing the Hulk, not understanding what was really inside that enormous, frightening shape. And do you know what that was? A good man who had given everything to save a stranger — and who still, even as the Hulk, only wanted to protect the people who could not protect themselves.

And that is how a single act of bravery began the most complicated story of any hero on Earth.
