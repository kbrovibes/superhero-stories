Are you ready to hear about the day a brilliant scientist became the BIGGEST, GREENEST superhero on the whole planet? Then hold on tight — because this story starts with a BOOM!

Dr. Bruce Banner was one of the smartest people in the entire world. He worked at a secret testing site called the Los Diablos Missile Base, way out in the New Mexico desert, and he had built something truly amazing — and truly dangerous. It was called the Gamma Bomb. On a bright, blazing day, the countdown was about to begin.

But wait! Can you imagine what happened just before the blast? A teenager named Rick Jones came ZOOMING onto the restricted range in his car! Rick didn't know about the bomb. He just drove right onto the testing field! And do you know what Bruce Banner did when he spotted him? Did he hide safely in his bunker?

No! He SPRINTED across that desert — boots pounding the sand — and he threw Rick into a deep protective trench to keep him safe.

But then... BOOM! KABOOM! KRAKATHOOM!

The Gamma Bomb exploded, and a wall of glowing green light washed over Bruce like a sizzling wave. Gamma radiation — the most powerful kind of energy in the universe — soaked into every single one of his cells. Any ordinary person would have been in terrible trouble. But hidden deep inside Bruce's DNA was something special called a gamma gene, and it saved his life in the most astonishing way possible.

That very first night, something incredible happened. Bruce's skin turned gray. His muscles swelled as big as boulders. He stood up — and up — and UP — until he towered over everything! A massive creature made of pure power had taken his place. The Hulk had been born!

General Thaddeus "Thunderbolt" Ross — a tough military commander with a booming voice — immediately decided the Hulk was dangerous and sent soldiers chasing after him. Poor Bruce was stuck: a quiet, gentle scientist by day, and an enormous force of nature by night.

And here is the most extraordinary thing. Rick Jones — the very teenager Bruce had saved — became the only person who could calm the raging creature and help it settle back into being Bruce. Rick felt so responsible for what happened to his friend that he stayed right by his side.

But do you know what the whole world missed, when they saw only the monster? Deep inside all that roaring green muscle, Bruce Banner's heart was still beating. Still kind. Still desperate to protect the people who couldn't protect themselves.

A hero and a monster, all in one enormous, thundering package. And that hero? He was just getting started.
