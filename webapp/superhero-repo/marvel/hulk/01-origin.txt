The desert sun beat down on the New Mexico testing site, but for Dr. Bruce Banner, the heat was the last thing on his mind. He stood in the reinforced bunker, his eyes fixed on the countdown clock. In just minutes, the Gamma Bomb—his life's work—would detonate, changing the landscape of modern warfare forever. Banner was a man of science, a quiet soul who preferred the sanctuary of a laboratory to the chaos of the outside world. He believed that gamma radiation, if harnessed correctly, could unlock the secrets of the universe, providing clean energy and medical miracles. But today, it was a weapon of mass destruction, and the weight of that responsibility sat heavy in his chest.

Suddenly, a flash of movement caught his eye. A young man, barely out of his teens, had driven a beat-up jeep onto the restricted testing range. It was Rick Jones, a teenager who had no idea he was standing in the shadow of a nuclear disaster. Without a second thought, Banner bolted from the bunker, his lab coat flapping in the wind. He sprinted across the sand, his heart hammering against his ribs. He reached the boy just as the countdown hit zero. With a desperate heave, Banner threw Rick into a protective trench, shielding him from the coming blast. But as he turned to dive for safety himself, the horizon exploded in a blinding wall of emerald light.

The world vanished in a roar of pure, unfiltered energy. Banner felt every cell in his body screaming as the gamma rays tore through his DNA, rewriting the very essence of his being. He didn't die; instead, he was reborn in a crucible of radiation. When the dust finally settled and the military teams arrived, they found a crater of glass and a man who was no longer just a man. That night, as the moon rose over the desert, the first transformation occurred. The pain was unlike anything he had ever imagined, a grinding of bone and a stretching of muscle that forced a guttural roar from his throat. The mild-mannered scientist was gone, replaced by a gray-skinned behemoth of pure rage.

This new creature, the Hulk, looked upon the world with eyes full of confusion and anger. He was the manifestation of everything Banner had suppressed: the fear, the loneliness, and the righteous fury against a world that didn't understand him. For years, the Hulk would be hunted by the very military Banner had served, led by the relentless General "Thunderbolt" Ross. The tragedy of Bruce Banner was that his greatest act of heroism—saving a stranger's life—had cursed him to live as a monster. Yet, within that monster lay a heart that still sought to protect the innocent, even if the world only saw the destruction he left in his wake. The legend of the Hulk had begun, a story of two souls trapped in one body, forever at war with themselves and the world around them.
