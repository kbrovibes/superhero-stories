Dr. Bruce Banner was a brilliant scientist who spent his life studying gamma radiation, a powerful form of energy. He was a kind and quiet man who wanted to use his knowledge to help the world. One day, during a high-stakes experiment, an accident occurred. Bruce rushed to save a young man who had wandered onto the testing grounds, but in doing so, he was caught in a massive blast of gamma rays.

Instead of causing him harm, the radiation changed Bruce’s very DNA. He discovered that whenever he became overwhelmed by intense emotions, especially anger or stress, he would undergo a startling transformation. His skin would turn emerald green, his muscles would swell to incredible proportions, and he would become the Incredible Hulk, a being of nearly limitless physical strength.

In the beginning, Bruce was frightened of the Hulk. He saw the green giant as a monster that he couldn't control. He traveled the world looking for a way to reverse the change, hoping to return to his normal life. However, he soon realized that the Hulk wasn't just a force of destruction. When innocent people were in danger, the Hulk was often the only one strong enough to save them.

The Hulk's power is tied directly to his emotions—the angrier he gets, the stronger he becomes. This makes him one of the most powerful beings on Earth. Bruce eventually learned that he and the Hulk were two parts of the same person. Instead of fighting the change, he began to look for ways to work with his stronger half. Together, they became a hero who uses massive strength to protect those who cannot protect themselves.
