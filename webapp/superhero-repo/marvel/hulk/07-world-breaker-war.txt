The Hulk’s return to Earth following the destruction of Sakaar was not a homecoming, but a declaration of war. He arrived at the moon, defeating the powerful Inhuman king Black Bolt with a single, earth-shattering blow, before turning his sights on New York City. This was the "World Breaker," a version of the Hulk whose anger had reached a level of cosmic intensity. He didn't just want to fight the heroes who had exiled him; he wanted to humiliate them. He demanded that the members of the Illuminati—Iron Man, Mr. Fantastic, and Doctor Strange—face him in open combat. The city was evacuated as the green giant marched through the streets, his footsteps sounding like the tolling of a funeral bell for the age of heroes.

One by one, the Earth's mightiest champions fell before the World Breaker’s relentless assault. Iron Man’s Hulkbuster armor was torn apart like tinfoil, and even the mystic arts of Doctor Strange were insufficient to contain the Hulk’s raw, irradiated fury. The Hulk forced his former friends to fight in a makeshift gladiator arena in Madison Square Garden, mirroring the cruelty they had subjected him to on Sakaar. He wanted them to feel the helplessness of being a slave, to understand the weight of the betrayal he had suffered. The conflict reached its peak when the Sentry, the most powerful being on Earth, intervened. Their battle was so intense that the shockwaves threatened to destroy the entire Eastern Seaboard, a clash of two suns that left the city in ruins.

During the final moments of the war, the truth was revealed: the bomb that destroyed Sakaar had not been planted by the Illuminati, but by Miek, one of the Hulk’s own Warbound, who wanted to keep the Hulk in a state of perpetual rage. The revelation shattered the Hulk’s resolve. The World Breaker didn't just want to kill anymore; he wanted to die. He began to release his energy in a massive, uncontrolled burst, begging the heroes to stop him before he leveled the entire continent. It was a tragic end to a war that had been fueled by a lie, a reminder that the Hulk’s greatest enemy was often the very rage that gave him strength.

World War Hulk remains one of the most significant events in the history of the Marvel Universe. It redefined the power scale of the Hulk, showing that he was truly the strongest one there is. But more importantly, it was a story about the consequences of arrogance and the fragility of trust. The heroes of Earth had tried to solve a problem by throwing it away, only to have it return with a vengeance they could never have imagined. The scars of the World Breaker’s war would take years to heal, both for the city of New York and for the relationships between the heroes. The Hulk had proven that he could break the world, but in the process, he had also broken himself, leaving Bruce Banner to pick up the pieces of a life that would never be the same.
