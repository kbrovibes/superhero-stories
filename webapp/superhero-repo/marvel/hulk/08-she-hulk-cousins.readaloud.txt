Bruce Banner had a cousin named Jennifer Walters, and she was brilliant. She was a lawyer who worked hard every day to make sure people were treated fairly. Her life was very different from Bruce's — no monster inside her, no military chasing her down. Just a steady, purposeful life helping others.

Then one terrible day, Jennifer was badly hurt. Bruce was the only one who could help. He gave her a blood transfusion — his own gamma-irradiated blood, the same kind of blood that carried the power of the Hulk.

Jennifer survived. But she changed. She became the She-Hulk — tall, powerful, and very, very green. Here is the remarkable part: where Bruce sometimes struggled against the Hulk inside him, Jennifer embraced what she had become. She was still herself, still clever and funny and passionate about justice. She just happened to also be incredibly strong.

Bruce and Jennifer became something very special to each other — the only two people in the whole world who truly understood what it felt like to have gamma radiation rewrite every cell in their body. Jennifer helped Bruce remember that the change did not have to be a curse. Bruce helped Jennifer stay connected to the deeper responsibilities that came with her power.

They did not always agree. Jennifer believed in following the law. Bruce's life often took him far outside it. And do you know what? Sometimes that meant they ended up on opposite sides, which was genuinely heartbreaking for both of them.

But they always found their way back to each other. Because they were family. And that bond — built from a single moment of desperation and love — was something even gamma radiation could never break.
