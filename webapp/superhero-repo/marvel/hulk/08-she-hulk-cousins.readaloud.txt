Did you know the Hulk has a cousin? A tall, brilliant, green, absolutely amazing cousin — and her story is one of the most remarkable in all of Marvel! Let me tell you about Jennifer Walters, who became the incredible She-Hulk!

Jennifer was Bruce Banner's cousin, and she was brilliant in a completely different way from Bruce. While Bruce studied gamma radiation and physics, Jennifer studied the law. She worked as a lawyer, standing up in courtrooms and fighting to make sure people were treated fairly. She was fierce and funny and incredibly good at her job.

Her life was very different from Bruce's — no monster inside, no military chasing her. Just purposeful, important, satisfying work.

And then one terrible day, Jennifer was seriously hurt by criminals who wanted to stop her from doing that very work. She needed a blood transfusion immediately — and the only person with her rare blood type anywhere nearby was her cousin Bruce. He didn't hesitate for a single moment.

But Bruce's blood was different from any other person's blood on Earth. It was gamma-irradiated — carrying the same wild, transforming energy that had created the Hulk.

When criminals came to the hospital to finish what they had started — WHOOOOSH! — something green and enormous rose up from that hospital bed! Jennifer Walters was gone, replaced by the She-Hulk: seven feet tall, incredibly strong, and still completely, wonderfully herself.

That is what made Jennifer so different from Bruce. Where the Hulk was Bruce's buried anger bursting free, the She-Hulk was Jennifer's confidence and passion for justice made BIG. She kept her cleverness, her humor, and her determination. She just also happened to be able to throw a car.

Jennifer often chose to STAY as She-Hulk even when she could change back, because she loved who she was in that form. She went right back to being a lawyer — an enormous, green, formidable lawyer that no opposing attorney ever wanted to argue against!

Bruce and Jennifer had a bond unlike anyone else on Earth: the only two people who truly understood what it felt like to have every cell in their body rewritten by gamma radiation. Jennifer helped Bruce see that the change wasn't only a burden. Bruce helped Jennifer carry the weight of responsibility that came with that kind of power.

They didn't always see things the same way. Jennifer believed in the system. Bruce's life had taken him far outside it. Sometimes that meant they stood on opposite sides — which was heartbreaking for both of them. But they always found their way back.

Because that's what family does. And gamma radiation, for all its wildness, could never break that.
