When the Avengers first assembled to face the mischievous and dangerous god named Loki, not everyone was sure about one of their new teammates. He was enormous, and green, and known more for knocking down buildings than for following plans. But when Loki opened a great hole in the sky above New York City, and an army of aliens called the Chitauri came pouring through it, Captain America looked at the Hulk and said something very simple.

"Hulk," he said. "Smash."

And do you know what? The Hulk smashed. He batted aside enormous creatures called Leviathans with a single swing of his fist. He stood between the aliens and the people of New York like a living wall of muscle and courage, taking blows that would have vaporised anyone else. The Avengers realised something important that day: they needed the Hulk.

But being on the team was not always easy for the Hulk, or for Bruce Banner inside him. The chaos of battle sometimes pushed the Hulk toward the edge of his control. That is why Black Widow learned a special calm technique called the Lullaby — a gentle way to help the enormous green giant settle back into being Bruce again.

And slowly, a wonderful thing happened. The Avengers stopped looking at the Hulk with fear. They started looking at him the way you look at a teammate. With trust. With respect. With something that felt very much like love.

For the first time in his life, the Hulk was not being chased. He was being counted on. He had a home, and a family, and a reason to fight that was bigger than anger alone.

From that day on, the green giant stood proudly among Earth's mightiest heroes — their strongest member, and one of their most important hearts.
