Picture this: a sky above New York City torn wide open, an alien army pouring through like a thunderstorm, and Earth's greatest heroes standing together for the very first time. And right in the middle of them — big, green, and absolutely ready — was the Hulk!

It all started because of Loki, the sneaky, mischievous god of trickery, who had a plan to take over the world. No single hero could stop him alone. But Rick Jones — remember him, the teenager Bruce Banner had saved way back on that desert testing range? — accidentally sent out a call for help, and one by one, the heroes came together: Iron Man, Thor, Black Widow, Captain America, Hawkeye... and the Hulk. The Avengers were born!

Now, not everyone was sure about the big green fellow at first. The Hulk had a reputation for smashing things without asking questions. Could someone like that really be a team player?

And then the Chitauri army came pouring through a portal in the sky, and Captain America looked straight at the Hulk and said two of the most famous words in superhero history: "Hulk — SMASH!"

WHOOOOOM! WHAM! CRASH! ROAR!

The Hulk leaped into the air and swatted aside enormous alien creatures called Leviathans — creatures as big as skyscrapers — with a single punch. He stood between the alien soldiers and the frightened people of New York like a living, roaring, unstoppable shield. Blasts hit him. Aliens swarmed him. He just kept going, getting angrier with every attack, and therefore stronger with every attack.

But fighting wasn't the only skill the Hulk needed on the team. After the battle, when the fury faded and the Hulk needed to calm back down into Bruce Banner, there had to be someone he trusted completely. That person was Black Widow — Natasha Romanoff. She developed a special, gentle technique called the Lullaby: a quiet approach, a soft touch, and the words "the sun's getting real low," spoken in a calm, steady voice. And do you know what? It worked.

Slowly, bit by bit, the Avengers became more than teammates. They became the Hulk's family. For the first time in his entire life — spent running from General Ross and the military, living on the outside of every city — the Hulk had people who were glad he was there.

He wasn't just the strongest one there is. He was their strongest. And that made all the difference in the world.
