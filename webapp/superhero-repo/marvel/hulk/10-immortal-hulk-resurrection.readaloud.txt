Hold on — because this story starts with a secret so big, so strange, and so ASTONISHING that it changes everything you thought you knew about Bruce Banner. Are you ready?

The Hulk cannot die.

Oh, Bruce Banner can be hurt. He can fall. His heart can stop. But every single time that happens, somewhere deep beneath the world we know — beneath the ground, beneath the sky, beneath even the realm where nightmares live — there is a place called the Below-Place. And when Bruce arrives there, he finds a glowing green door waiting for him.

The Green Door.

And when night comes back to the world above? The door swings open with a long, slow CREEEEEAK — and out walks the Hulk. Bigger than before. Angrier than before. Stronger than before.

This isn't the cheerful, thundering Hulk you might expect. This version of the Hulk is different. He thinks carefully. He speaks slowly and deliberately, with a dark wit. And he calls himself the Devil Hulk, because he has given up pretending to be anyone's friend. His only purpose — the single thing he cares about — is protecting Bruce Banner. Whatever the cost. Whatever it takes.

Can you imagine? A guardian who lives in the space between life and what comes after, bursting back into the world every night through a glowing green door to make sure his human is safe!

But the Devil Hulk wasn't the only one who knew about the Green Door. The villain called the Leader — Samuel Sterns, with his enormous brain and his hunger for power — had discovered its secret. He wanted to use the Green Door to make himself into something like a god.

The battle that followed reached further than any fight the Hulk had ever had. It stretched all the way to a terrifying entity called the One-Below-All — the darkness at the absolute bottom of existence, the dark mirror of everything good in the universe.

And through all of it, Bruce Banner was forced to look honestly at the deepest, most painful parts of himself. His complicated childhood. His relationship with his father. The true roots of the rage that had lived inside him long before any gamma bomb ever went off.

Do you know what Bruce found at the end of all that searching?

He found that the Hulk was not a mistake. Not an accident. Not a curse. The Hulk was meant to exist — a necessary part of the great balance of everything. Born, in some profound cosmic way, for the renewal of all things.

And for the first time in his life, Bruce Banner stopped fighting the Hulk. He stopped trying to be rid of him. They were not two beings at war. They were one — complicated, powerful, still healing, and finally, finally at peace.
