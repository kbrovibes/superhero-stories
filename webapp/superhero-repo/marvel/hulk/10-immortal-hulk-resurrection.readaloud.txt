There is a chapter in the Hulk's story that turns the page on everything you thought you knew about Bruce Banner and his green companion. Because it turns out that the Hulk is not simply a very strong creature made of gamma radiation. He is something older, deeper, and far more mysterious than that.

Whenever Bruce Banner's heart stops — whenever the worst happens — he does not simply disappear. He finds himself in a strange, unsettling place called the Below-Place, a realm of eerie green light that exists beneath the world we know. And when night falls, a door — the Green Door — swings open, and the Hulk walks back out into the world, angrier and stronger than ever before.

This version of the Hulk is different. He thinks carefully, speaks deliberately, and calls himself the Devil Hulk. He says his only job is to protect Bruce, no matter what the cost. He is not interested in being anyone's champion or hero. He is the guardian of one person, and he will not be stopped.

The villain called the Leader came back too, hungry to control the Green Door and use its power to become something like a god. The battle that followed reached all the way to a terrifying entity called the One-Below-All — a darkness at the very bottom of creation.

And through all of it, Bruce Banner was forced to look honestly at the most painful parts of his past — his difficult childhood, his complicated feelings about his father, the true root of his rage.

Do you know what he found at the end of that journey? Peace. The Hulk was not a mistake. He was meant to exist, as part of the great balance of the universe.

From that day on, Bruce Banner and the Hulk were no longer fighting each other. They were, at last, one.
