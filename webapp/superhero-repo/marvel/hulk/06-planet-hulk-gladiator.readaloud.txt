Fasten your seatbelts, because we are leaving Earth and heading to a planet far, FAR across the galaxy! A wild, dusty, dangerous world called Sakaar — and it's where the Hulk becomes something he never expected to be!

A group of powerful people on Earth called the Illuminati had made a very difficult decision: they believed the Hulk was too strong and too unpredictable to stay on their planet. So they tricked him onto a spacecraft — and shot him into deep space! Can you imagine waking up on a rocket ship, flying away from everything you've ever known?

The spaceship crashed on Sakaar. And things did not start well. The Hulk was captured immediately, fitted with a painful device that dampened his strength, and thrown into a gladiator arena ruled by a cruel and greedy king called the Red King. The Red King made warriors fight each other for the entertainment of cheering crowds. He thought the Hulk would be just another prize fighter.

Oh, how wrong he was!

CRASH! BOOM! ROAR! The Hulk fought his way through every challenger in the Great Arena — and the crowds began to chant a new name: the Green Scar! Warriors who had been forced into the arena as prisoners started looking at the Green Scar and seeing something they hadn't felt in a very long time: HOPE.

Three of those fighters — Korg, a big gentle warrior made of living rock; Miek, a quick insect-like creature; and Hiroim, a warrior of shadows — became the Hulk's closest friends. Together they called themselves the Warbound, and they made an oath sworn in loyalty: no one left behind, ever.

And do you know what the Warbound did next? They REBELLED! They toppled the Red King's empire, freed the people of Sakaar, and the Hulk — who had spent his whole life being hunted — became a king himself! He married a brave and brilliant warrior named Caiera, and for the first time in his life, both the Hulk and Bruce Banner inside him felt something they had almost forgotten: peace.

But then, without any warning — BOOM! — a terrible explosion tore through the city. Caiera was gone. The happiness they had built together was gone.

The Hulk's grief was heavier than any mountain. He gathered the Warbound, pointed the ship toward Earth, and made a vow so powerful it shook the stars. He was going back. And this time — he wasn't running.
