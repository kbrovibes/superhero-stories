Far across the stars, there is a harsh and ancient planet called Sakaar, where only the toughest survive. It was to this distant world that the Hulk was sent — not by his own choice, but by a group of powerful people who believed he was too dangerous to stay on Earth. He arrived as a prisoner, with a device clamped onto him that dampened his strength, and was forced to fight in a great arena for the entertainment of a cruel ruler called the Red King.

But you cannot keep the Hulk down for long.

The Hulk rose through every fight, earning himself a new name among Sakaar's people: the Green Scar. Warriors from all over the arena began to respect him. Three of them — Miek, Korg, and Hiroim — became his closest companions. Together, they called themselves the Warbound, swearing an oath to stand by each other no matter what.

And do you know what happened next? The Hulk led his Warbound in a rebellion. They toppled the Red King, freed the people of Sakaar, and the Hulk — once hunted across Earth — became a king. He married a brave warrior named Caiera, and for the first time in his long, difficult life, Bruce Banner and the Hulk were both at peace.

But sorrow came without warning. A terrible explosion destroyed much of Sakaar, and Caiera was gone. The grief was heavier than any blow the Hulk had ever received. He gathered his Warbound, set his eyes toward Earth, and made a vow. He was coming back — and this time, he was not running away.

From that day on, the legend of the Green Scar showed the world that the Hulk was far more than a monster. He was a king, a husband, and a man capable of building something beautiful — even if it was taken from him.
