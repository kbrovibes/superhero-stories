The Statesman, the Asgardian refugee ship, drifted through the dark with all the survivors aboard — Thor, Loki, Valkyrie, Heimdall, and what remained of the people of Asgard. The brothers had just reconciled. Thor was looking out the window and smiling. The future, for a few quiet hours, looked open.

Then a shadow crossed the stars. A huge ship blocked out the lights ahead. And the voice that boomed across the Statesman was a voice Loki had hoped never to hear again — Thanos. The purple titan had come, this time, for the Tesseract. Loki had taken it from Asgard's vault before the city burned, and now it was in his pocket. Thanos already had the Power Stone. He wanted the Space Stone next. And he had brought his terrible Children with him — Ebony Maw, Cull Obsidian, and the others.

The fight on the Statesman was short. Thor was held down. Hulk was beaten. Heimdall, with his last act, sent Hulk away to Earth to warn the others. Loki, surrounded, did the cleverest thing he could think of — he offered Thanos the Tesseract. Pretending to surrender. Looking for one chance, just one chance, to do something brave.

He stepped forward. He held up the glowing blue cube. He said, with a small bow, "You should know — when you mess with our people..." And then he tried, in one fast desperate motion, to drive a small hidden dagger into Thanos's throat. It was a clever plan. It was a Loki plan. But Thanos was wearing the Power Stone and the Space Stone both, and he caught Loki's wrist as easily as catching a leaf in the wind.

What happened next was not a hero's loud death. It was quiet. Thanos held Loki up by the throat. Loki looked at Thor — bound on the floor, watching, helpless. And Loki said, with the last of his cleverness, "You... will never... be a god." Then it was over. Thor wept. Loki was still.

It was the bravest thing Loki had ever done. He had known — he must have known — that he would not survive trying to stab Thanos. He had done it anyway, to give Thor a chance. To stand between the monster and the brother he loved. The boy who had spent so many years scheming for thrones had, in the end, traded everything for one small act of family. And though Thor would not know it for a long time, the universe was not quite finished with Loki yet. A different timeline. A different chance. A story still being written.
