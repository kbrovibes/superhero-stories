Okay so. You know how the Avengers traveled back in time to get the Infinity Stones? Well, while they were doing that, they accidentally let a YOUNGER version of Loki escape with the Tesseract. POOF! He vanished. Like a Loki-shaped firework.

That Loki — the one from the Battle of New York, still smug, still wearing the green and gold cape, still carrying the Mind Stone scepter — landed in a sandy desert he didn't recognize. He brushed himself off. He started to give a speech to no one in particular about how he was now going to conquer this new world too.

And then the doors slid open behind him.

WHOOSH.

A squad of officers in ORANGE jackets came marching out. They were carrying glowing batons. Click! They pointed the batons at Loki. And TIME ITSELF stopped moving around him. He was frozen in place — except for his eyes, which were going very wide.

"You are under arrest," one of them said, "by the Time Variance Authority."

The what?

THE TIME VARIANCE AUTHORITY. The TVA. The strangest, dustiest, weirdest organization you have ever heard of. They were time COPS! They protected the "sacred timeline." And Loki, by escaping with the Tesseract, had just done something HUGELY illegal.

They marched Loki through endless beige corridors. There were buzzing fluorescent lights overhead. BZZZ. There were waiting rooms full of bored-looking Variants — people who had also fallen off the sacred timeline. There was a metal scanner that he walked through, and a robotic voice announced, "You are not a robot." Thank you, robot voice.

On the wall was a cartoon clock. A friendly little cartoon clock named MISS MINUTES. She was orange and round and smiling, and she TICK-TOCKED right up to Loki and explained, in the cheeriest, friendliest voice imaginable:

"Hi! Looks like you broke the sacred timeline! Whoopsie! That means the TVA will now have to PRUNE you from existence. Have a great day!"

PRUNE? Like, like a garden? They were going to snip him out of the universe like a wilted flower?

Loki was given an orange jumpsuit. On the back it said one word — VARIANT.

He was marched into a cluttered little office. Magazines and pens everywhere. And behind a desk sat a man with a soft tie and very calm eyes. His name was Mobius M. Mobius. He was a TVA analyst, and he had been doing this job for a LONG time.

"Hi, Loki," Mobius said. "Have a seat."

Mobius didn't yell. Mobius didn't threaten. Mobius just looked at Loki like Loki was interesting. Like Loki was a puzzle worth figuring out.

"Listen," Mobius said. "There's a dangerous Variant out there causing problems for us. And here's the funny part. The Variant — is also a Loki."

A different Loki?? Out there??

Mobius offered him a deal. Help catch the other Loki. In exchange, no pruning.

The other TVA officers were NOT happy. There was a tough one named Hunter B-15 who scowled at Loki the whole time. There was a friendly bumbling clerk named Casey who didn't even know what a FISH was. They thought Mobius was crazy for trusting a Loki.

Mobius did it anyway.

And here is the thing. Mobius started asking Loki questions nobody had EVER asked him before.

"What do you actually want?"

"Are you sure?"

"Why?"

Loki opened his mouth to give one of his clever answers — and then closed it. Because he didn't know. He had been so busy pretending and scheming and trying to impress Odin that he had never stopped to ask himself what HE actually wanted.

The orange jumpsuit was kind of itchy. The fluorescent lights were buzzing. Miss Minutes was bobbing past on her cartoon orbit going "BOOP! TICK! TOCK!"

But for the first time in his long, twisty, lonely life — somebody was curious about Loki.

And that was the start of him becoming himself.
