Picture this. Loki and Mobius are racing through TIME itself, chasing a mysterious dangerous Variant who keeps escaping them. She has a trick — she hides in DISASTERS. Like inside a sinking apartment building. Inside a castle right before it gets struck by lightning. Inside a planet right before — uh oh — it gets hit by a meteor. Because if she does anything sneaky in a place that's about to be destroyed anyway, the TVA doesn't notice.

CLEVER.

Loki and Mobius finally tracked her down. The year? 2050. The place? A giant superstore called Roxxcart, on a planet that was about to be smashed by a hurricane. Wind was howling outside. WHOOOOSH! Cans were flying off shelves. THUD!

And there — at the end of the cereal aisle — stood the Variant.

She had blond hair. Sharp green eyes. TWO curved horns coming up from her head. And she did not look one bit happy to see them.

Her name was Sylvie.

She was a Loki.

But not Loki's Loki. A different Loki. From a different timeline. The TVA had snatched her up when she was just a little girl, accused her of being a Variant for reasons she never even understood, and Sylvie had been ON THE RUN her whole entire LIFE.

Sylvie was angry. Sylvie was suspicious. Sylvie did not trust anybody, and she ESPECIALLY did not trust some other version of herself wearing a TVA jumpsuit.

She zapped Loki and herself away with a stolen TemPad — ZWOOOOOOP! — and the two of them landed, of all places, on a CRUMBLING MOON called Lamentis-1.

Lamentis-1 was DOOMED. A whole giant planet was going to crash into the moon in about twelve hours. CRACK! Pieces of sky were already falling. The ground was shaking. Cars were tipping over.

Loki and Sylvie were stuck together. They had no choice but to talk.

And here is the thing, listener. Loki took one look at her — and felt this WEIRD jolt in his chest. Like a recognition. Oh, he thought. OH. So THIS is what it's like to meet myself.

She wasn't exactly like him. She was blunt and grumpy. He was flowery and dramatic. She was practical. He was theatrical. But underneath all those differences? They were the same. Stubborn. Lonely. Always one step ahead. Always sure that nobody was on their side.

They hid in a train car together. Outside, the sky was falling. Pieces of the planet up above were raining down in fire. WHOOSH! ZAP! KABOOM! And inside the train car, in the warm shaky light, the two Lokis started to TALK.

Sylvie told him about being taken from her family as a kid.

Loki told her about being adopted, about being a Frost Giant, about Frigga's library.

They argued. They laughed. They almost trusted each other.

And outside, on a cliff edge, watching the planet break apart above them, Loki realized something that surprised him.

He CARED about her.

As a friend. As something even bigger than that. As the first person who had REALLY, TRULY seen him for what he was.

This — wow. The lights of the universe FLICKERED. Some kind of strange new BRANCH in the timeline started to grow. The TVA's alarms back at HQ went BWEEEEEP BWEEEEEEP BWEEEEEP. A Loki, in love with a Loki, was a "nexus event" — a thing the sacred timeline DID NOT KNOW WHAT TO DO WITH.

And the TVA came charging in with everything they had.

Loki and Sylvie ran. But for the first time in his entire long twisty lonely life, Loki was not running for himself.

He was running for someone else.

And that, friends, is when his real story FINALLY started.
