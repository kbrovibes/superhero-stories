Okay listener. Today's story has the BIGGEST surprise of Loki's whole journey. Are you ready?

You remember Sylvie. The other Loki. The one with the horns and the green eyes. The one Loki had grown to care about more than anyone in the multiverse.

Both of them had been captured by the TVA and dragged back to headquarters. But Sylvie was not the kind of person who stayed captured. She had ONE QUESTION she wanted answered, and she was going to get to the bottom of it.

WHO was secretly running the TVA?

WHY were they pruning anyone who stepped off the sacred timeline?

Sylvie wanted to find that person. Punch them. Get answers.

And this time, Loki was right with her.

Together they fought their way out of the TVA. With surprising help from Hunter B-15 — yes, the tough scowling officer! It turned out, deep down, she also wanted answers — they stole a TemPad and aimed it at the strangest coordinate in the whole multiverse. The Citadel at the End of Time.

The Citadel was not on any planet you can find on a map. It floated past the edge of the universe, on a tiny rocky island in a swirling purple void.

To get to it, the two Lokis first had to cross THE VOID. The Void was a junkyard at the end of everything. Where did all the pruned things go? Here. Pruned cars, pruned ships, pruned buildings, pruned people. And circling overhead — uh oh — was a HUGE purple cloud-monster named ALIOTH. RAAARRRRR! Alioth ate anything that didn't run fast enough. THWOOOOOM!

They survived. Barely. Then they climbed a long stone staircase that wound up to the front gates of the Citadel.

CREEEEAK. They pushed open a tall heavy door.

And inside?

There was no army.

There was no glowing throne.

There was just — a tired man, sitting at a table, eating an apple.

CRUNCH.

He looked up. He smiled in a polite, friendly way. "You finally made it," he said. "Hi. I'm He Who Remains. Pull up a chair. There's a lot to talk about."

WHAT.

He Who Remains was an old, old man who had been holding the whole multiverse together by himself, all alone, for a long, long time. Watching every single branch of every single timeline. Pruning the dangerous ones with his TVA. Keeping one peaceful "sacred timeline" running, while all the wild branches were snipped before they could grow into wars between universes.

The TVA. The orange jumpsuits. The pruning. Miss Minutes. All of it. It was HIS system. He was the one in charge.

"Now," he said, leaning forward, "you have a choice. And it's a real choice. Listen."

He held up one finger.

"Option one — you kill me. The multiverse blossoms into millions of wild branches. Some are good. Some are TERRIBLE. There will be war. There will be other versions of me, less friendly versions, who try to come back and take over."

He held up a second finger.

"Option two — you take my chair. You sit here. You keep the system going. You keep ONE timeline safe, and you stop everyone else."

Sylvie was furious. Her whole LIFE had been ruined by this man. Her whole life of running. She wanted to drive her sword through him right then and there.

But Loki — Loki, our Loki, the trickster who used to LOVE chaos — Loki hesitated. Because he had grown up a little. He had spent his time with Mobius and Sylvie. And he had started to understand something important.

Chaos has a COST. Other people pay it.

The two Lokis argued. Loud. Hard. Sylvie said, "He has to die." Loki said, "Wait. WAIT. We don't know what comes next."

And then — Sylvie made her choice.

She looked at Loki with sad eyes. She stepped close. She kissed him — gently, quickly, sadly. She pushed him backward through a portal that snapped open behind him — WHOOSH! And the last thing he saw was Sylvie raising her sword and bringing it down on He Who Remains.

CLANG. KABOOM. The multiverse SPLIT.

Branches shot out in every direction. Timelines began to grow wild. The walls of the Citadel started to crumble. KKRRRRRRRACK.

Loki landed back in the TVA.

But it wasn't the TVA he had left.

There were new statues in the lobby. Statues of someone he had never seen before — but who looked a LOT like He Who Remains. Different officers in different uniforms. Mobius didn't know him. Hunter B-15 didn't know him.

The whole place had changed.

A new war was beginning. And Loki was right in the middle of it.

The story wasn't over. Oh no, dear listener — it was only beginning.
