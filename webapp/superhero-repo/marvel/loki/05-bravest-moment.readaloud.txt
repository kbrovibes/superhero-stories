Okay, today's story is a big one. It's a brave one. And it's the moment when Loki — the trickster, the schemer, the boy who spent years trying to prove he was clever — became something else. Something bigger. A real hero. Listen closely.

The Asgardian people had just escaped from Hela. They were all riding on a big refugee ship called the Statesman, drifting through space. There was Thor, looking out the window with a small smile. There was Loki, sitting beside him. There was Valkyrie, the fierce warrior. There was Heimdall, the gatekeeper. And there was Hulk, still big and green from the arena on Sakaar.

For a few quiet hours, the future looked... almost peaceful.

Then a shadow crossed the stars.

A HUGE ship loomed up in the window. It was bigger than the Statesman. WAY bigger. The whole sky outside went dark. And a deep, slow, rumbling voice came over the speakers.

It was Thanos.

The purple titan had come back. And this time he wanted what Loki had in his pocket — the Tesseract, the glowing blue cube, one of the six Infinity Stones. Loki had slipped it out of Asgard's vault before the city burned. He had been carrying it. And now Thanos was right outside.

Thanos's huge ship rammed the Statesman. CRASH! BANG! Pieces of the Asgardian ship flew everywhere. Thanos's terrible Children stepped through the hole — Ebony Maw, who could move things with his mind, and Cull Obsidian, who carried a giant hammer. They marched in.

Thor swung Stormbreaker — wait, he didn't have Stormbreaker yet — he swung his old sword. WHACK! It wasn't enough. Cull Obsidian knocked Thor down. Hulk roared and charged — RAARRGH! — but Thanos himself stepped forward and bonked Hulk on the head and even HULK went down. THUD!

Heimdall, with his last bit of magic, used the Bifrost to send Hulk all the way to Earth, to warn Doctor Strange and Iron Man that Thanos was coming.

And then it was Loki's turn.

Loki was surrounded. Thor was held on the floor. There was no way out.

But Loki was Loki. He always had a plan.

He stepped forward, slowly. He held up the glowing blue Tesseract. "Hold on," he said. "I have a deal. Take this. Let my brother live."

Thanos smiled, slow and patient.

Loki bowed his head a little. He said in his most polite voice — "And you should know... when you mess with our people..."

And then — FAST AS LIGHTNING — Loki pulled a hidden dagger from his sleeve and lunged at Thanos's throat!

But.

Thanos had the Power Stone and the Space Stone. He caught Loki's wrist before the blade ever got close. Easily. Like catching a falling leaf.

Loki was held up in the air. He looked over at Thor — Thor, bound on the floor, watching, eyes wide.

And Loki, with the very last of his cleverness, looked Thanos right in the eye and said:

"You... will never... be a god."

It was his last brave little line.

Then it was over. Loki went still.

Thor wept. He yelled. He shook. He could not save his brother.

But here is what you have to understand, listener. Loki KNEW. He had to have known. Trying to stab Thanos with a tiny dagger — Loki had to have known he wasn't going to win that fight. He did it anyway. He did it to give Thor a chance. To stand between the monster and the brother he loved.

The boy who had spent his whole life scheming for thrones, who had been mind-controlled by a scepter, who had pretended to be dead so many times Thor stopped believing it — that boy, when it really counted, traded everything for one small act of family.

And here's the secret about Loki, the surprise the universe was saving for later — Loki's story wasn't quite over yet. Somewhere, in a different timeline, in a different version of events, the trickster god was about to wake up in a very strange new place.

But that, my friend, is the NEXT story.
