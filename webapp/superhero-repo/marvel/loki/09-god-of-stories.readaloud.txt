Okay listener — this is THE big one. This is the story of how Loki, the trickster, the schemer, the boy who had spent so long trying to grab a throne, FINALLY got a throne — but in the most surprising way you could ever imagine.

Are you ready? Take a deep breath. Here we go.

The multiverse was BREAKING. New branches were growing everywhere. Wild. Fast. Faster than the TVA could keep up with. And nobody — NOBODY — knew how to fix it.

Loki ran around the TVA looking for answers. He found a kind, dusty old inventor named OUROBOROS — yes, Ouroboros, like the snake eating its own tail — and Ouroboros explained the truth.

Underneath the whole TVA, hidden behind a big sliding door, was the most important machine in the multiverse. The TEMPORAL LOOM.

The Loom was huge — bigger than a stadium, glowing with golden light, with thousands of strings of pure energy flowing through it. CHUNGGGG! WHIRR! Each string was a branch of the multiverse, and the Loom wove them all together into ONE safe pattern.

But now there were too many strings. The Loom was overheating. SSSSS! ZZZZZ! It was about to burn out.

And if it burned out — every branch would collapse. Every Variant. Every person on every universe. Gone. Forever.

Loki had to fix it.

He tried everything! He used his magic. He learned how to SLIP THROUGH TIME with just his MIND. POOF! He could blink from one second to another with no machine at all, just by thinking really hard. He saved Mobius. He saved Hunter B-15. He saved his friends over and over.

He even snuck off and found Sylvie. She had finally escaped to a normal life — she was hiding out in a little diner in OKLAHOMA, in 1982, eating pancakes, working a normal job, having normal days. She was happy.

Loki begged her to come help. She said no. They argued. They cared about each other. But she had finally found peace, and she couldn't bear to give it up.

Loki understood.

He went back to the TVA. He tried again. He used his magic to step out onto the Loom itself, walking out among the wild bright strings, trying to fix them by hand. The energy almost tore him apart. WHISH! ZAP! CRACK! He had to slip back. He tried again. He failed. Again. He failed.

And then — slowly, in the quiet between failures — Loki understood the truth.

The Loom could not be saved.

Not by a machine. Not by a quick fix. Not by anything clever.

The only way to save the multiverse — the only way to save every single branch, every Variant, every person, every story — was for ONE PERSON to step out onto the Loom, grab every single branch with their bare hands, and HOLD them.

Forever.

Alone.

At the end of time.

That was the deal. The math. The truth. And the math did not care who did it. Somebody had to.

Loki looked around at his friends. Mobius with his soft tie and his magazines. Hunter B-15 who had become a real friend. Casey, who had FINALLY figured out what a fish was — "Wait, those are fish?! In the WATER?! AMAZING!" Sylvie in her diner. Thor, somewhere out in the universe, holding Stormbreaker. Every person in every universe, all of them safe in their own version of the story.

The trickster who had spent his whole life trying to claim a throne understood — finally — that the THRONE wasn't the point.

The PEOPLE were the point.

Loki stood up straight. He brushed off his green-and-gold coat. He breathed deep.

And he walked out onto the burning Loom.

The wild energy whipped around him. WHIIIIISH! ZAP! CRACK! It tried to tear him apart. He kept walking. He reached out his hands. He grabbed every branch — every single one — and he HELD them.

The dying machine — the burning Loom — transformed.

It became a great beautiful TREE OF LIGHT. Huge. Golden. Glowing. With branches of pure timeline reaching out in every direction.

And Loki sat at the center of the tree. Not on a flashy fancy throne. Just a quiet chair. At the root of every story. Holding everything together. Forever.

He had become the GOD OF STORIES.

The new keeper of the multiverse.

By choice. Not by trickery. Not by force. Because the people he loved needed somebody to do it, and HE WAS THE ONE who could.

His friends would never quite see him again. Mobius would go on with his life. Sylvie would stay in her diner. But every story — every branch — would be safe. Because Loki was sitting at the center, holding on.

That, dear listener — THAT — is how the trickster finally became the hero.

And the boy who used to turn apples into snakes in his mother's library? He was, at last, exactly who he had always been meant to be.
