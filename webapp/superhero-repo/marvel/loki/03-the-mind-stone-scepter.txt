In the lonely time between Asgard and what came next, Loki fell through the cold spaces between worlds. When he was found, it was by someone far worse than himself — a purple titan named Thanos, who lived on a rocky throne in the dark. Thanos handed Loki a long golden scepter with a glowing blue stone set into its blade. He told Loki to take the scepter to Earth, find the glowing blue cube called the Tesseract, and conquer the small planet. In exchange, Thanos would give him an army of Chitauri warriors.

What no one knew at the time — not even Loki, not really — was that the scepter was helping with the bossing. The Mind Stone inside it whispered. It was very good at telling a hurting heart what it wanted to hear. You are above them. You were born to rule. They are ants beneath you. Loki, lonely and angry and freshly heartbroken about Asgard, listened.

So he arrived on Earth in New York City, stepping out of a glowing portal as the Tesseract crackled overhead. He pointed the scepter at Hawkeye and made him a temporary ally with a touch. He smiled his sharpest smile at a crowd in Stuttgart. And then he met the Avengers — Iron Man, Captain America, Black Widow, Thor, Hulk, and Hawkeye — for the very first time, and they did not behave like ants at all. They argued, they fought, they yelled. But when the Chitauri came pouring through the sky over Manhattan, they STOOD.

The Battle of New York did not go the way Loki had planned. The army was driven back. The portal closed. And in a moment that became one of the funniest in Avengers history, Loki gave a long villain speech to Hulk in Stark Tower. Hulk grabbed Loki by the ankles and smacked him into the floor — THUD THUD THUD THUD THUD! — left him in a Loki-shaped crater, and walked off muttering, "Puny god."

The Mind Stone scepter was taken away. Loki was taken away. Years later, when he understood more about what had really happened — when he learned that Thanos had been twisting his mind the whole time — Loki would feel a kind of complicated shame. He had done wrong, yes. But he had also been used. And the boy who once made apples turn into snakes in Frigga's library was still in there, waiting to find his own story again.
