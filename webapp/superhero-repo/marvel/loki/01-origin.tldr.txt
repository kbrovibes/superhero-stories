• Loki grows up in Asgard as the second prince, younger brother to Thor and son to Odin and Frigga
• Thor is loud and golden — the kind of son who throws an axe and laughs when it comes back
• Loki is the quieter one — a reader and a watcher, drawn to illusions instead of broadswords
• Frigga teaches him magic in her library — bending light, turning bowls of apples into snakes
• Asgardians respect thunder and weapons more than illusions, so Loki always feels a little out of step
• He tries too hard to prove himself — telling tall tales, pranking Thor, performing for Odin's approval
• Loki has been measuring himself with the wrong ruler — trying to be Thor instead of Loki
• On quiet nights, Frigga still tucks both brothers in and tells them stories about tricksters
• She doesn't yet know one of her sons will grow up to become both god AND trickster
