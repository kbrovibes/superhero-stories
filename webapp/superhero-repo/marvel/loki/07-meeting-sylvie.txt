The dangerous Variant turned out to be hiding in the most disasters in history — places like a sinking apartment block, a ruined castle, a doomed planet — because in a real disaster, anything she did would be erased by the catastrophe and the TVA couldn't track her. Loki and Mobius chased her across centuries. They finally caught up to her in 2050, in a Roxxcart superstore on a planet about to be hit by a hurricane.

Her name was Sylvie. She had blond hair, sharp green eyes, two long curved horns on her head, and she was furious. She was a Loki from a different timeline — taken from her family as a child by the TVA, accused of being a Variant for reasons she had never understood, and on the run her entire life. She was clever, suspicious, lonely, and very VERY tired of being chased.

Loki took one look at her and thought, with a strange jolt — oh. Oh, this is what it's like to meet myself. It was odd. She was nothing like him in many ways — she was blunt where he was flowery, practical where he was theatrical — but she was him in all the ways that mattered. Stubborn. Lonely. Always one step ahead. Always sure that nobody was on her side.

They ended up trapped together, hopping across timelines in a stolen TemPad, fleeing the TVA, arguing the whole way. They hid in a train car on the doomed moon of Lamentis-1 as the moon crumbled into a planet above. They stood on a cliff and watched the sky fall around them. And in that strange quiet moment, Loki — who had never really cared for anyone in his life the way other people seemed to — looked at Sylvie and realized something. He cared about her. As a friend. As something even bigger than that. As the first person who had really, truly seen him.

This caused a problem the TVA had never seen before. A Loki, in love with a Loki, was apparently a "nexus event" — a thing the sacred timeline did not know how to handle. The lights of the universe flickered. Some kind of strange new branch in time began to grow. And the TVA, alarmed, came down on Lamentis-1 with everything they had, ready to drag Loki and Sylvie back to face whatever was waiting for them at the top of the TVA tower. Loki, for the first time in his life, was not running for himself. He was running for someone else.
