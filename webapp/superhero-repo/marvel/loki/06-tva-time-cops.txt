Here is where Loki's story bends in a direction nobody saw coming. During the Avengers' time-traveling adventure to undo Thanos's snap, a younger version of Loki — the one from the Battle of New York, still smug and still wearing the Mind Stone scepter — accidentally got his hands on the Tesseract again and ZAPPED himself out of the timeline. POOF. He landed in a sandy desert he did not recognize, brushed off his coat, and started monologuing to nobody in particular.

That is when the doors slid open and a squad of strange officers in orange jackets marched out, aimed glowing batons at him, and froze time around him with a click. He had just been arrested by the Time Variance Authority. The TVA. The strangest, dustiest, most rule-loving organization in the multiverse.

They marched Loki through endless beige corridors lit by buzzing fluorescent tubes. They made him wait at a counter. They put him through a metal scanner that announced, in a bored voice, "You are not a robot." A cartoon clock on the wall, named Miss Minutes, blinked at him cheerfully and explained — in the friendliest way possible — that he had broken the "sacred timeline" by escaping with the Tesseract, and that the TVA would now have to "prune" him from existence. The orange jumpsuit they handed him said simply VARIANT on the back.

In a cluttered office sat Mobius M. Mobius, an analyst with a soft tie, a magazine collection, and the calm patience of someone who had seen a hundred Lokis before. Mobius offered Loki a deal. There was a dangerous Variant on the loose — another Loki, in fact — and Mobius needed a Loki to help catch a Loki. The other officers, including stern Hunter B-15 and the unhelpful but very loyal Casey, did not love the plan. Mobius did it anyway.

For the first time in Loki's long, lonely life, somebody was looking at him not as a villain or a brother or a disappointment, but as a person worth being curious about. Mobius asked him questions Loki had never been asked. "What do you actually want?" "Are you sure?" "Why?" Loki, who had been built out of clever answers, found he had to think for a minute before he could reply. The orange jumpsuit was, in its own strange way, the start of Loki becoming Loki for the very first time.
