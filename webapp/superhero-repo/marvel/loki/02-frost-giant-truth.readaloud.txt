Okay, listener — settle in. Because today we're going to learn the BIGGEST secret in Loki's whole life. A secret nobody had ever told him. A secret that, when he found out, changed EVERYTHING.

Loki was a grown prince by now. He and Thor were walking through the vault of Asgard — that's a big special room where Odin kept all the magical treasures from old battles. CLINK! CLANK! Their boots echoed on the polished floor.

And there, standing on a pedestal in the middle of the room, was something the brothers had known since they were little — the Casket of Ancient Winters. It was a blue, glowing box, COLD as the heart of a snowstorm. BRRRR! Their breath puffed white when they got close to it.

This time, Loki reached out his hand. And he TOUCHED it.

And here is where the surprise happens.

His hand should have hurt. The cold should have stung him. But it didn't. Instead — POOF! — his skin started turning BLUE. Deep, beautiful, ocean-storm blue. Little ridges raised up along his fingers. He caught his face in the polished floor and his EYES were glowing red.

He wasn't an Asgardian.

He was a Frost Giant.

WHAT?!

Loki ran. He ran through the long golden hallways of Asgard, his cape flapping behind him — WHOOSH! — and he found his father Odin in the throne room. Odin was very old and very tired. He was about to fall into the Odinsleep, which is what Asgardians call it when their king rests for a hundred years. But Loki shook him gently and asked the question.

"Am I a Frost Giant? Tell me the truth."

And Odin, with the kindest and saddest voice you ever heard, told him the story.

A long time ago, there had been a big battle on a cold, icy world called Jotunheim. King Laufey ruled the Frost Giants there. After the battle, Odin walked through the empty temple of Jotunheim and he heard a tiny sound. A baby crying. The baby was so small — much smaller than other Frost Giant babies — and his own family had left him behind because they thought he was too little to be a warrior.

Odin picked the baby up. The baby's blue skin slowly turned pink in Odin's warm hands. And Odin carried that baby home to Asgard. He gave him a name. He raised him as a prince.

That baby was Loki.

Loki sat very still. "So I was just a... a tool? A bargaining chip for later?"

"No," said Odin. "I took you because you needed a family."

But Loki couldn't hear that yet. His whole life felt PAINTED a different color. The color of frost. And the saddest part — the very saddest part — was that Loki had grown up hearing scary bedtime stories about Frost Giants. He had been raised, without anyone meaning to, to be afraid of what he actually WAS.

Loki was hurting.

But then — and this is important — his mother Frigga came in. She had heard everything. She walked straight to Loki and she hugged him. She hugged him EXACTLY the same as before. Not gentler. Not weirder. Just the same. Just like a mom.

"You are my son," she said.

And those five small words were the start of something big. Loki didn't believe them right away. It would take a LONG time, and many, many adventures, before he understood. But somewhere deep down, he heard her.

And the heart of Loki — the real Loki, blue or pink, Frost Giant or Asgardian — was still beating.
