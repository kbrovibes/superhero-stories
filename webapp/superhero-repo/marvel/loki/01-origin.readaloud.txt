Have you ever felt like you didn't quite fit in? Like everyone around you was good at one thing, and YOU were good at something different? Well, that's exactly how a boy named Loki felt growing up in Asgard. And his story — oh, his story is one of the most twisty, surprising stories in the whole Marvel universe!

Asgard was a golden city floating high above the Nine Realms. It had a rainbow bridge — WHOOOSH! — that shimmered all the colors at once. It had a throne room with ceilings so tall you couldn't see the top. And it had two royal brothers — Thor and Loki.

Thor was the LOUD one. He was tall and blond and strong, and he loved throwing axes and arm-wrestling the Warriors Three. CRASH! BANG! THUMP! When Thor walked into a room, EVERYONE looked.

Loki was the quiet one. He was thin and dark-haired and clever, and he loved books and tricks. He could wave his hand — POOF! — and turn a bowl of red apples into a bowl of wiggly green snakes. SSSSSS! Everybody jumped. Loki giggled.

Their mother, Frigga, was a sorceress. She taught Loki magic in her library, where the candles never burned out. "You have a real gift, my son," she would say. And she meant it.

But here's the thing. Asgardians LOVED thunder. They LOVED big strong heroes with big strong hammers. They didn't always know what to do with a boy who made things vanish and reappear somewhere else. WHISH! Where did the apple go? IT'S BEHIND YOUR EAR!

So Loki started trying too hard to prove he belonged. He told stories that made him sound braver than he really was. He played pranks on Thor in front of Sif and the Warriors Three. He wanted his father Odin to look at him the way Odin looked at Thor — with that big proud smile. But Odin? Odin was a hard one to impress.

And here's the secret Loki didn't know yet — and it's a BIG secret, so listen closely. Loki had been measuring himself with the WRONG ruler. He kept trying to be a Thor. But the universe had something much, much stranger and more interesting in mind for HIM.

In those early days, though, the brothers were just brothers. They sparred — CLANG! CLANG! They argued. They sneaked into the vault late at night to look at the Casket of Ancient Winters, all blue and freezing cold. BRRRR! And on quiet evenings, Frigga would tuck them BOTH into bed and tell them old, old stories about gods and tricksters.

She had no idea — none at all — that one of her sons would grow up to become BOTH. A god AND a trickster. The most surprising hero in all the Nine Realms.

And that, little listener, is just the beginning of Loki's story.
