Loki's tricks, in the end, never went away. He did not become a different person. He stayed a trickster all the way through. He still turned a sandwich into a snake when nobody was looking. He still made an illusion of himself stand in a doorway just to make Mobius jump. He still shape-shifted into the most inconvenient person possible in a serious meeting. The mischief was part of him, and he was not going to apologize for it.

What changed was what he used the tricks FOR.

The boy who once used illusions to make Thor look foolish in front of Sif learned, very slowly, to use illusions to protect his friends. When Mobius was almost pruned, Loki used a shimmering double of himself to draw the Hunters away. When Sylvie was being tracked across the timeline, Loki used a clever rumor — planted in the right ear at the right moment — to throw the TVA off her trail. When Casey needed help opening a stuck door at the TVA, Loki shape-shifted into a wrench. Casey almost dropped him. The tricks got used for kindness instead of pride.

Even his old habit of faking death — which he had done so many times Thor had stopped believing him — turned out to have something gentle hidden in it. It had always been a way to escape the people who wanted him to be someone he was not. By the end, he did not need to escape anymore. He could stay. He could be himself, with no costume, no illusion, no false name. He sat at the root of the great tree of stories. He WAS himself. Finally. All the time.

There is no neat lesson at the end of Loki's life. He was a brother. He was a Frost Giant. He was a Mind-Stoned villain. He was a fake Odin. He was a "get help" projectile. He was a brave little dagger in front of Thanos. He was an orange jumpsuit. He was a friend to Mobius. He was someone Sylvie kissed once and walked away from. He was the keeper of the multiverse. The trickster grew up, but he never stopped being a trickster. He learned that being clever was not the same as being good — but that being clever, when used for the right reasons, could be a small kind of love. Frigga would have been proud. And somewhere, in some branch of the wild beautiful timeline, Loki was still her son.
