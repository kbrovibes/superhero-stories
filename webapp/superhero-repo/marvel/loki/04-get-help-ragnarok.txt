By the time of Ragnarok, Loki had pulled off one of his strangest tricks yet. He had pretended to be dead, snuck back to Asgard, disguised himself as Odin, and quietly run the kingdom for a couple of years from the throne — eating grapes, watching plays about his own heroic life, and generally enjoying being king without anyone knowing. It was the kind of mischief that was VERY Loki. But it had a cost. By neglecting Asgard's defenses, he had let trouble grow at the edges of the realm.

Then Thor came home, found him out, and dragged him by the collar to Earth to find the real Odin. The two brothers ended up on a junkyard planet called Sakaar, where Thor was forced to fight in the arena and Loki — being Loki — quickly charmed the planet's strange ruler, the Grandmaster. When Thor begged Loki to help him escape and save Asgard, Loki at first said no. Old habits. Old hurts. Working with Thor felt like losing.

But the new villain was their sister Hela, the Goddess of Death, freshly escaped from her long imprisonment. She was stronger than either of the brothers alone. To even get OFF Sakaar, Thor and Loki had to sneak into the Grandmaster's hangar past a wall of guards. And that is where one of the funniest moments in Marvel happened — the "get help" gag. It was a trick they used to do as kids. Thor would pretend Loki had been hurt, walk up to a group of strangers shouting "MY BROTHER, HE'S DYING — GET HELP, PLEASE, GET HELP," and then — when the strangers got close — heave Loki at them like a green-and-gold bowling ball. STRIKE!

"I hate it," Loki said, walking down the corridor with Thor's arm around his shoulders. "It's HUMILIATING."

"It works every time," Thor said.

It worked again. The brothers stole a ship called the Commodore and rocketed off Sakaar to face Hela together over Asgard. Loki, on the bridge, did one of the bravest things he had ever done — he convinced Thor to let Surtur destroy Asgard itself, to defeat Hela once and for all. The land burned. The people lived. And as the refugee ship Statesman drifted away into the dark, Thor looked at Loki and grinned.

"You know," Thor said. "It's good to have you back."

Loki almost smiled. For once, he meant it.
