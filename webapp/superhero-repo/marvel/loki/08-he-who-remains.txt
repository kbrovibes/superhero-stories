Sylvie was not done. Even captured, even hauled back to the TVA, she had a plan — find whoever was secretly running the place, and ask the only question that mattered. Why? Why was the TVA pruning anyone who stepped off the "sacred timeline"? Whose rules were these? Loki, this time, was right beside her. Together they fought their way past Hunter B-15 (who, after some surprising moments, became an ally), commandeered a TemPad, and aimed it at the strangest coordinate in the multiverse — the Citadel at the End of Time.

The Citadel was not on any planet. It floated past the edge of the universe, on a craggy little island in a swirling purple void. To get to it, the two Lokis first had to cross a place called the Void, where pruned things were sent — a junkyard at the end of the multiverse with an enormous purple cloud-monster named Alioth circling overhead, eating anything that did not get out of its way. They survived. They climbed the long stone stairs. And at the top, they pushed open a tall door.

Inside was not a throne. There was no army. There was just an old castle hall — and a man eating an apple at a table. He looked tired. His name, he said, was He Who Remains. He had been holding the multiverse together by himself for a very long time, all alone, watching every branch of every timeline so that none of them could grow into a war between universes. The TVA, the sacred timeline, the prunings — they were all just his system. Awful, yes. But, he claimed, the alternative was much worse.

"You have a choice," he told them, very calmly. "Kill me. The multiverse blossoms into a million wild branches. Or take my chair, and keep doing what I've been doing." Sylvie wanted to kill him. She had spent her whole life on the run because of him. Loki — Loki, of all people — found himself hesitating. Because the trickster who had loved chaos had grown up enough, in his time with Mobius and Sylvie, to understand that sometimes chaos has a cost.

The two Lokis argued. Sylvie made her choice. She kissed Loki — gently, sadly — pushed him back through a portal to the TVA, and ran her sword through He Who Remains. The multiverse split. Branches shot out in every direction. The walls of the Citadel began to break. And Loki, returned to the TVA, found that nothing was quite the way he had left it. New statues in the lobby. Different officers. A whole different version of the place. The first war was already beginning.
