Now today's story is one of the FUNNIEST in all of Loki's adventures. And it involves a trick the brothers had been using since they were KIDS. Get ready, because this one ends with Thor literally THROWING Loki like a bowling ball.

But first — a setup.

You remember Loki had been brought back to Asgard in chains after the Battle of New York. Well, here's what you didn't know. After a while, Loki pulled off one of his sneakiest tricks EVER. He faked his own death. Then he snuck back to Asgard, used his magic to disguise himself as Odin — POOF! — and SAT ON THE THRONE for YEARS.

Yes! Loki was the secret king. He ate grapes. He watched plays about himself where actors pretended to be the heroic Loki who died saving Asgard. He was having a TIME.

But — uh oh — he wasn't paying much attention to actually running the kingdom. Trouble was building at the edges.

Then Thor came home. Thor sniffed around, suspicious. Thor poked Odin-who-was-secretly-Loki with the tip of his sword. POOF! Loki turned back into himself. Busted!

Thor dragged Loki off Asgard by the collar — "WE NEED TO FIND DAD." But everything went sideways and they crash-landed on the WEIRDEST planet you have ever heard of. Sakaar. It was a junkyard planet. All the trash in the universe washed up there. And the ruler was a glittery old fellow called the GRANDMASTER, who made people fight in his big colosseum for fun.

Thor was thrown into the arena. Loki, of course, started CHARMING the Grandmaster, sipping fruity drinks on a balcony.

But then they got bad news. Their SISTER — Hela, the Goddess of Death — had escaped her long prison and was attacking Asgard. The brothers HAD to work together.

And that meant they needed to escape Sakaar. Which meant they needed to get past the GUARDS in the spaceship hangar.

Time for an old trick.

Thor put his arm around Loki and started walking down the corridor toward the guards. Loki was groaning and limping and acting like he was DYING.

"OH NO!" Thor shouted, his voice cracking dramatically. "MY BROTHER! HE'S DYING! GET HELP! PLEASE! SOMEBODY GET HELP!"

The guards looked up. Thor staggered closer. Loki groaned, "I am unwell..."

And then — at exactly the right moment — Thor LIFTED Loki up.

WHHHHOOOOOSH!!

And THREW HIM. Like a green-and-gold BOWLING BALL.

Right into the guards.

CRASH! BANG! STRIKE!

The guards went tumbling. Loki bounced and groaned. "I hate this trick," he mumbled from the floor. "I HATE it. It's HUMILIATING."

Thor grinned and helped him up. "It works every time."

"It does NOT work every time. It works on YOU SOMETIMES."

"Get help. Classic."

They stole a ship called the Commodore — VROOM! — and rocketed off Sakaar to go face Hela.

When they got home, Asgard was in trouble. Hela was too powerful to defeat normally. So Loki — clever, sneaky LOKI — had an idea. He brought back Surtur, the fire demon, and let Surtur destroy Asgard itself. Because Asgard, Thor said, was not a place. It was a PEOPLE. And the people lived.

Afterward, on a refugee ship called the Statesman, drifting in space, Thor looked at Loki sitting next to him on the bridge.

"You know," Thor said. "It's good to have you back."

And Loki — for once, for REAL — almost smiled.

Because today, instead of being the trickster against his brother, he had been the trickster WITH his brother. And it turned out, that was a whole lot more fun.
