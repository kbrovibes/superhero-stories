HYDRA was the secret organization that had created the Winter Soldier, and for seventy years they had used him to keep the world bending in the directions they wanted. Their headquarters was buried inside S.H.I.E.L.D. itself, hidden in plain sight ever since Operation Paperclip had quietly imported Arnim Zola into the United States after the war. By the time Steve Rogers came out of the ice, HYDRA was everywhere — in Congress, in the World Security Council, in the basement of the Triskelion. Their plan, called Project Insight, was to use three new Helicarriers to target and eliminate, in a single afternoon, every person on Earth they considered a future threat.

When Steve, Sam Wilson, and Natasha Romanoff finally pieced the plot together, HYDRA sent their best asset to stop them — the Winter Soldier. The first time Steve fought him on a highway in Washington, D.C., Bucky punched a knife between his hands so fast Steve barely caught it. The mask fell off in the middle of the fight. Steve froze in the middle of the street, looked at his oldest friend in the world, and said, in a small voice, "Bucky?" The Soldier looked back, blinking. "Who the hell is Bucky?" he said. HYDRA pulled him out of the field that same afternoon and put him back in the chair.

The Triskelion fell three days later. Sam and Steve fought their way through the Helicarriers as Project Insight launched, and Steve found Bucky waiting for him inside the third one. They fought across catwalks and gantries while the ship tilted and burned around them. Steve refused to swing his shield at his friend. He kept whispering Bucky’s name. "You’re my mission," the Soldier kept saying, slamming Steve into the metal deck. The Helicarrier hit the Triskelion. Glass shattered everywhere. Steve, broken-jawed, looked up at the Soldier and said, "Then finish it. Because I’m with you to the end of the line."

Those words — the same words Bucky had said to a small, sick Steve in Brooklyn fifty years before HYDRA — cracked the conditioning. The Soldier stopped. Steve fell through the broken hull of the Helicarrier into the Potomac River, and the Soldier — somewhere in his programming, Bucky Barnes — dove in after him. He pulled Steve out of the water onto the riverbank, stared at his unconscious face for one long, confused moment, and disappeared back into the city. HYDRA was finished. The Winter Soldier was gone. And somewhere in Bucharest, in a small apartment with a stack of stolen notebooks, a man with a metal arm began the slow, terrified work of trying to remember who he had been before all of this.
