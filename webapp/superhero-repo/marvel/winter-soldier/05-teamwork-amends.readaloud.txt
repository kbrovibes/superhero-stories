Bucky Barnes had a small notebook, and in it was a long list of names. They were the people the Winter Soldier had hurt while HYDRA was driving. Bucky had promised his therapist, Dr. Raynor, that he would go through every name — not to undo what had happened, but to be honest about it.

Some people on the list were not ready to hear from him. Some were. And some did not even know what had happened to them or why.

One of the names belonged to a man named Yori Nakajima, who ran a small sushi restaurant in Brooklyn. The Winter Soldier had taken the life of Yori's son, RJ, long ago. Bucky began going to that restaurant for dinner. He listened to Yori talk about RJ. He made sure Yori was not alone on the holidays. He did not say who he was or what he had done.

Dr. Raynor told him, gently, that keeping someone company did not count as making amends. Bucky walked Yori to a bench one evening and told him the truth.

Yori cried. He told Bucky to leave. Bucky left. And standing outside, Bucky understood something he had not quite known before: making amends was not about being forgiven. It was about being honest enough to risk not being forgiven.

And do you know what happened next? Sam Wilson asked Bucky to be his partner. And Bucky, who had spent decades without a real life, surprised himself by wanting to say yes.

Sam reminded Bucky every day that he was a person, not a weapon. Bucky reminded Sam that not every fight could be won with a smile. They argued constantly and trusted each other completely.

From that day on, Bucky Barnes had something he had not had in seventy years: a morning routine, a good friendship, and the slow, strange, wonderful feeling of beginning again.
