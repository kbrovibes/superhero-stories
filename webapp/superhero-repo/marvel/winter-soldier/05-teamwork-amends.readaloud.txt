Bucky Barnes had a notebook. Not a fancy one — just a small, ordinary notebook. And in it was a long, long list of names. Every person the Winter Soldier had hurt while HYDRA was in control. Every one.

Bucky had promised his therapist, Dr. Raynor, that he would go through every single name. Not to undo what had happened — because nothing could undo it. But to be honest. To face it. To do the right thing even when it was terrifying.

Some people on the list were not ready to hear from him. They told him to leave, and he left. Some people were ready, and those conversations were hard and sad and important. And some didn't even know what had happened to them or why.

One of the names belonged to a man named Yori Nakajima who ran a small sushi restaurant in Brooklyn. The Winter Soldier had taken the life of Yori's son RJ long ago. So Bucky started going to that restaurant. He listened to Yori talk about RJ — about his laugh, his favorite foods, his big plans. He made sure Yori wasn't alone on the holidays. He did it for months. He never said who he was.

Dr. Raynor told him, gently: this doesn't count as making amends. Bucky knew she was right.

So he walked Yori to a park bench one evening and told him the truth.

Yori cried. He told Bucky to leave. Bucky left. And standing there on the sidewalk outside, Bucky understood something he hadn't quite known before: making amends isn't about being forgiven. It's about being honest enough to RISK not being forgiven. The truth matters even when it hurts — especially when it hurts.

That's a big idea for a very brave person.

And then Sam Wilson asked Bucky to be his partner. Bucky — who hadn't had a real life in seventy years — surprised himself completely by wanting to say yes.

Working with Sam was different from any mission HYDRA had ever given him. Sam reminded Bucky every single day that he was a PERSON, not a weapon. And Bucky reminded Sam that not every fight could be won with a smile and a good speech.

They argued constantly. They trusted each other completely. WHOMP! FWOOOOSH! Bucky on the ground, Sam in the sky!

And after the missions, Bucky helped fix Sam's sister Sarah's boat down in Louisiana, ate dinner with Sam's nephews, and sat at the table like a person with a family.

That — right there — was the real victory. Bucky Barnes, kid from Brooklyn, learning what it felt like to begin again.
