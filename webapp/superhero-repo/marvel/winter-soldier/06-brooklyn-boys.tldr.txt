• Bucky and Steve Rogers grew up a few blocks apart in Brooklyn — brownstones, clotheslines, corner shops
• First met when Bucky stepped in to stop a bigger kid bullying Steve behind the Brooklyn Heights library
• Bucky's family looked out for Steve when his mother Sarah Rogers worked night shifts as a nurse
• They walked Flatbush Avenue to school together; loved the Brooklyn Dodgers; Bucky paid for Steve's tickets in secret
• Coney Island summers — Steve threw up on the Cyclone and Bucky never told anyone
• Friday night movies at the local theater — adventure serials with men in hats doing the right thing
• When Sarah Rogers passed away, Bucky sat on the funeral home steps next to Steve, no words needed
• That's when Bucky first said it: "I'm with you to the end of the line, pal"
• Those words would carry both of them through World War II, through ice, through seventy lost years, and home again
