The arm was the first thing anyone noticed about Bucky Barnes, and the hardest thing to understand. HYDRA's version was enormous — dark gray Soviet metal bolted to his shoulder with thick bolts, cold enough to ache in winter, strong enough to stop a moving truck. It was, really, a weapon shaped like a limb. Every time he raised it, some old part of his mind still waited for an order.

After the battle between the Avengers, a man named T'Challa — the King of Wakanda — made Bucky a remarkable offer. Come to Wakanda. Rest. Heal.

Shuri, T'Challa's brilliant younger sister, took one look at the HYDRA arm and laughed. Then she removed it carefully, put it in a glass box, and told Bucky he could live without it for a while. For nearly two years, he worked on a goat farm at the edge of the Wakandan jungle. He ate proper meals. He slept under a mosquito net. The children in the nearby village started calling him White Wolf, because of the way his face looked when he smiled. Shuri, meanwhile, was very quietly building something new.

The arm she made was pure Wakandan vibranium — the same extraordinary metal in Steve's shield and Black Panther's suit. Matte black, with a small white star painted on the shoulder. It weighed almost nothing. It absorbed the shock of every impact instead of sending pain up into his shoulder.

And do you know what Bucky did the first night he wore it? He lay down on his left side — something the HYDRA arm had never let him do comfortably. He cried very quietly. Then he slept.

The HYDRA arm had been built to remind him of what they had done to him. The Wakandan arm was something else entirely. It was a gift from people who had decided he was worth helping.
