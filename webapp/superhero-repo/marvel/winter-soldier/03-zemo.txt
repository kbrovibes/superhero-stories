Helmut Zemo was a quiet, polite man with a Sokovian accent and a thin red scarf that he always wore even indoors. He had lost his wife, his son, and his father in the floating-city disaster the Avengers had stopped over Sokovia. He did not blame Ultron for those deaths. He blamed the Avengers. And he was patient enough — and clever enough — to wait years and build a plan that did not require an army or a single weapon. All it required was for the Avengers to fight each other.

Zemo’s plan centered on Bucky. He hunted down the old HYDRA notebook with the ten Russian trigger words. He arranged for the bombing of a United Nations conference in Vienna and made sure the security cameras caught a man in a black hoodie with a metal arm. By the time Steve Rogers found Bucky hiding in his Bucharest apartment, the entire world was hunting them both. Zemo, dressed as a psychiatrist, walked into a holding cell in Berlin where Bucky was being kept, read the ten words aloud, and the Winter Soldier came back to life inside Bucky’s head like a flame in a dry forest.

Bucky tore through the Berlin facility, took down half a dozen guards, and escaped on a motorcycle through a city he did not recognize. Steve and Sam Wilson finally chased him down on a riverside highway, dragged him into a warehouse, and waited for the trigger words to wear off. When Bucky finally came back to himself, panting and afraid, he told Steve everything Zemo had asked him about — a secret HYDRA bunker in Siberia where five other Winter Soldiers had been kept frozen for decades. Zemo, they realized, was not trying to wake them up. He was trying to make sure the Avengers thought he was.

The real plan was a single, terrible video. In the Siberian bunker, Zemo had not even bothered to wake the other Soldiers. He had shot all five of them in their pods. What he had brought Tony Stark there to see was an old security tape from 1991 — the night the Winter Soldier had stopped a car on a dark road and ended the lives of Howard and Maria Stark to recover a case of Super Soldier Serum. Tony watched his parents die at Bucky’s metal hand, and the fight that broke out in the bunker tore the Avengers in half. Zemo, watching it on a small screen from a rooftop above, finally smiled. He had not needed to defeat the Avengers. He had only needed to wait long enough for them to defeat themselves.
