Have you ever heard the saying "you don't need an army if you're clever enough"? That is exactly what a man named Helmut Zemo believed. And he was really, really clever. Frighteningly clever.

Zemo didn't have superpowers. He didn't have a giant robot. He didn't even wear a scary costume! He wore a thin red scarf and spoke politely in whatever room he walked into. But inside, he was carrying something heavy: he had lost his wife, his son, and his father in the disaster over Sokovia. And he blamed the Avengers.

So Zemo made a plan that didn't need weapons. It only needed one small notebook — the old HYDRA notebook with the ten Russian trigger words that turned Bucky into the Winter Soldier.

STEP ONE: Zemo arranged an explosion at a United Nations building in Vienna. He made sure the security cameras caught someone with a metal arm. The whole world started hunting Bucky.

STEP TWO: Zemo dressed as a psychiatrist, walked calmly into a Berlin holding cell where Bucky was being kept, and read all ten Russian words aloud.

WISHING. FURNACE. DAYBREAK. SEVENTEEN...

CRASH! Like a fire in dry wood, the Winter Soldier came back into Bucky's mind! He tore through the facility — BANG WHOOOM CRASH! — and thundered through Berlin on a motorcycle before Steve and Sam Wilson finally ran him down. When the words faded and Bucky came back to himself, shaking and horrified, he told them what Zemo had asked about: a HYDRA bunker in Siberia with five more Winter Soldiers frozen in pods.

So Steve, Bucky, and Sam headed to Siberia. But Tony Stark — following his own investigation — arrived too.

And do you know what was actually waiting in that bunker? Zemo had already shot all five Winter Soldiers while they slept. He hadn't come to wake them. He had come to show Tony Stark one thing: a security video from 1991. The night the Winter Soldier stopped a car on a dark road and ended the lives of Howard and Maria Stark — Tony's parents.

Tony watched his parents. And then he looked at Bucky. And then at Steve, who had known for a while.

BOOM! The Avengers tore apart. Steve fighting Tony. Tony fighting Bucky. Winter Soldier losing his arm in the blast. Everything broken.

That was ALL Zemo had wanted. No army. No weapons. Just one notebook, one video, and the Avengers' own pain — turned against themselves.
