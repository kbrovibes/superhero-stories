Helmut Zemo was a quiet man who always wore a thin red scarf and spoke politely in whatever room he walked into. He had lost everything in the disaster over Sokovia — his wife, his son, his father. He did not blame the robot named Ultron for those deaths. He blamed the Avengers. And he was patient enough, and clever enough, to spend years planning something that did not need a single weapon or an army. It only needed one notebook.

Zemo found the old HYDRA notebook with the ten Russian trigger words. Then he arranged for an explosion at a United Nations conference in Vienna, and made sure the security cameras caught a figure in a dark hood with a metal arm. By the time Steve Rogers found Bucky hiding in his apartment in Bucharest, the whole world was hunting them both.

Zemo dressed as a psychiatrist, walked into a Berlin holding cell, and read all ten words aloud.

The Winter Soldier came back into Bucky's mind like a fire in dry wood. Bucky tore through the facility, escaped, and thundered through Berlin on a motorcycle before Steve and Sam Wilson finally ran him to ground. Then they waited in a warehouse while the trigger words faded. And when Bucky came back to himself, he told them what Zemo had asked about: a HYDRA bunker in Siberia with five other Winter Soldiers frozen in pods.

And do you know what was waiting in that bunker? Zemo had already shot all five Winter Soldiers in their sleep. He had not come to wake them. He had come to show Tony Stark a videotape from 1991 — the night the Winter Soldier had stopped a car on a dark road and taken the lives of Howard and Maria Stark to retrieve a case of Super Soldier Serum.

Tony watched his parents die. And the Avengers tore themselves apart. That was all Zemo had ever wanted.
