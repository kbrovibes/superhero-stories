• After the Blip, Bucky returned to Brooklyn with a new arm, a clean mind, and a very long list of wrongs to right
• Dr. Christina Raynor, his court-appointed therapist, gave him a small spiral notebook and one rule: write every name, then set each wrong right
• Bucky bought a black pen at the bodega and filled both sides of the page — Yori Nakajima, Leah Behrens, Aaron Cleary, a scientist in Stuttgart, a janitor in Odessa
• The rule: cross off a name only when the work is really done — not when he has merely done something
• Some names could be helped directly; some quietly in the background; some required the hardest amends — telling the person the truth
• Dr. Raynor caught him once skipping the honest part — "That's the whole work," she said; he put the name back on the list
• He carried the notebook in his inside jacket pocket everywhere he went
• Some names had a line through them and a quiet note; some had a line and nothing — the silence of people who said leave forever
• The job was never absolution; it was truth, told kindly, one name at a time — until Bucky walked all the way through his past and out the other side
