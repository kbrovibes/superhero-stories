Before there was ever a Winter Soldier, there was a kid from Brooklyn named Bucky Barnes. And this kid had a best friend — a small, scrappy, stubborn little guy named Steve Rogers who kept getting into fights he couldn't win. Can you guess who always stepped in to help him? BUCKY!

James Buchanan "Bucky" Barnes was tall and funny and easygoing. He looked after Steve the way a good big brother does — even though they weren't brothers at all, just two kids from the same neighborhood who decided to stick together forever.

Then World War II came. BOOM! Bucky shipped out with the 107th Infantry Regiment, fighting in France and Italy — while Steve, still small and thin, was turned away from every single recruitment office in the country. Every one! Bucky fought through frozen forests and terrible battles while his best friend couldn't even join up.

Until HYDRA agents captured Bucky near a place called Azzano, Italy. They dragged him to a cold steel laboratory run by a scientist named Arnim Zola. And Zola injected Bucky with an early version of the Super Soldier Serum. He should not have survived. NOBODY had survived it before. But Bucky? Bucky did.

And then — CRASH! BANG! — a blond stranger in a star-spangled suit came bursting through the door. Steve Rogers. Finally transformed. Finally Captain America. The two friends walked right out of that HYDRA factory, side by side, grinning.

But the fall came months later. High above the Alps, on a train speeding across a mountain gorge, Bucky was knocked from the open side car. Steve LUNGED for his hand — fingers almost touching — and MISSED. Bucky fell into the snow far below.

The world believed he was gone. He was not gone.

HYDRA found him. They replaced his missing left arm with a heavy metal prosthetic — CLANK! They erased his memory with a special chair full of electrical current. And they planted TEN Russian trigger words inside his mind. Words that, spoken in the right order, turned him into something else: the Winter Soldier. Their secret weapon.

Here's something that will send a shiver down your spine: for SEVENTY YEARS they froze him, woke him for a mission, then froze him again before he could remember his own name.

But somewhere underneath all the ice and all the dark — very quietly, very patiently — a kid from Brooklyn was still waiting.
