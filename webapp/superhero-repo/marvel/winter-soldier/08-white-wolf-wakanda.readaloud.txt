Have you ever needed somewhere quiet to go? Somewhere with no noise, no rushing, no one yelling? Just a soft bed, a warm meal, and time to figure things out?

That is exactly what Bucky Barnes needed. And the person who gave it to him? A KING.

Let's set the scene. After all the fighting at the airport in Leipzig — POW! WHAM! BOOM! — and the even harder fight in a snowy bunker in Siberia, Bucky was in trouble. The world's governments wanted to lock him up. They thought he was dangerous. And here is the sad part: he kind of WAS dangerous. Because HYDRA had left ten Russian trigger words in his head — secret words that could turn him into the Winter Soldier again whenever someone said them in the right order.

Bucky knew it. That's why he needed help.

That's when T'Challa stepped in. Who is T'Challa? He is the BLACK PANTHER! King of a hidden country called WAKANDA! And after everything had settled down, he looked Bucky right in the eyes and said, very calmly, "I can help you. Come home with me."

So off they went, all the way to Africa.

Now listen, because Wakanda is AMAZING. From the outside it just looks like jungle and mountains. But that's because Wakanda hides itself with the most advanced cloaking technology in the entire WORLD. WHOOOSH! You fly through what looks like a cliff face, and suddenly — there it is! Silver cities! Grass villages! Herds of long-horned cattle wandering everywhere! MOOOOOO!

T'Challa took Bucky to a small thatched-roof hut at the edge of a quiet little village. There was a stream nearby. A grove of acacia trees. No electric lights. Just a bed, a wooden table, and a window that opened toward the sunrise.

For the first time in SEVENTY YEARS — say that out loud, seventy! — no one was going to put Bucky in a HYDRA chair ever again.

Then we meet the real hero of this story. Princess SHURI! T'Challa's little sister! She is SIXTEEN years old and one of the smartest scientists on planet Earth. (Sorry, Tony Stark.) Shuri looked at Bucky and was NOT impressed. She studied his brain scans for months. Then she built a special machine that could find the trigger words HYDRA had hidden inside his head — and turn them OFF, one at a time. CLICK. CLICK. CLICK.

While Shuri worked, Bucky lived in the village. He helped the farmers take care of a small herd of goats. BAAAAA! He learned a little of the language — it's called Xhosa, and it has the most amazing clicking sounds. He woke up at dawn. He went to bed at sunset. He stopped having nightmares.

The village kids gave him a new name. They called him "Ingcuka Emhlophe." Say it like this: in-GOO-kah em-SHLO-pay. It means "WHITE WOLF." Because of his pale skin and the calm, steady way he watched over them all.

Then one ordinary morning, Shuri called him into her lab. She sat him in a soft chair. NOT a HYDRA chair. Just a chair! She looked at him and said: "Try the words."

She read each Russian trigger word out loud, in the exact right order.

Wishing. Furnace. Daybreak. Seventeen. Benign. Nine. Homecoming. One. Freight car.

Bucky listened all the way to the end.

And nothing happened.

NOTHING!

Bucky blinked. He looked at his hands. And then — oh, this part is so good — he SMILED. A real, full, Brooklyn smile! The kind he hadn't used in seventy years!

"It's gone," he said.

Shuri grinned. She fist-bumped him. She called him White Wolf. And outside that lab, in a country half a world away from Brooklyn — for the FIRST time in his whole long, lost life — Bucky Barnes was finally, completely, gloriously FOUND.
