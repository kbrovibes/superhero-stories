What if the scariest thing wasn't a monster hiding under the bed — but something hiding right inside the organization meant to protect everyone? That is what Steve Rogers discovered. And it is very, very scary.

HYDRA had been hiding for a LONG time! After World War II, a scientist named Arnim Zola was secretly brought into the United States — and HYDRA grew inside S.H.I.E.L.D. like roots growing under a house. Invisible! Patient! Spreading quietly for DECADES! By the time Steve came out of the ice, HYDRA had people in Congress, in the World Security Council, in the very building Steve worked in every day.

And their plan? Project Insight: use three enormous flying Helicarriers — giant warships in the sky — to target and eliminate every person HYDRA considered a threat. All at once. One afternoon. Done.

Steve Rogers, Natasha Romanoff, and Sam Wilson uncovered the plot! And HYDRA sent their most dangerous weapon to stop them: the Winter Soldier.

Their first meeting was on a highway in Washington D.C. CRASH BANG WHOOOM! The Winter Soldier moved so fast that Steve barely caught the knife aimed at his face. Then the Soldier's mask came off — and Steve froze in the middle of the highway.

"Bucky?" Steve said, in a voice so small it barely existed.

The Soldier stared back with cold, empty eyes. "Who the hell is Bucky?" HYDRA pulled him back and put him in the chair.

Their final battle was on the third Helicarrier as it fell from the sky, burning and breaking apart around them. Steve refused to raise his shield at his oldest friend. He just kept saying Bucky's name — over and over — even when he was hit. Even when he was knocked down. Even when he could barely stand.

The Helicarrier crashed into the Triskelion — KAAABOOOM! Everything broke. Steve looked up from the ruins, battered and barely breathing. "Then finish it," he said quietly. "Because I'm with you to the end of the line."

Those words. Those seven words. They were words a small, sick Steve had said to BUCKY in Brooklyn, a long, long time ago.

CRACK. Something deep inside the Winter Soldier broke open.

Bucky pulled Steve from the river. Then he disappeared into the city — and somewhere in Bucharest, in a tiny apartment with a stolen notebook, a man with a metal arm began the slow, frightened work of trying to remember who he was.
