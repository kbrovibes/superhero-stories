HYDRA had been hiding for a very long time. After World War II, a scientist named Arnim Zola was quietly brought into the United States through a program called Operation Paperclip, and HYDRA grew inside S.H.I.E.L.D. like a root beneath a house — invisible, patient, and spreading. By the time Steve Rogers came out of the ice decades later, HYDRA had people in Congress, in the security council, in the very building Steve worked in. And they had a plan called Project Insight: use three enormous Helicarriers to target and eliminate every person they considered a threat — all at once.

Steve Rogers and his friends Natasha Romanoff and Sam Wilson uncovered the plot. HYDRA sent their most dangerous asset to stop them: the Winter Soldier.

The first time they met on a highway in Washington D.C., Bucky punched so fast that Steve barely caught the knife. Then the mask came off. Steve froze in the middle of the street and said, in a very small voice, "Bucky?" The Soldier looked back with empty eyes. "Who the hell is Bucky?" HYDRA pulled him away and put him back in the chair.

Their final fight was on the third Helicarrier as it fell and burned around them. Steve refused to raise his shield against his oldest friend. He just kept saying Bucky's name. The ship hit the Triskelion. Everything broke. Steve looked up from the wreckage, battered and barely able to speak, and said, "Then finish it. Because I'm with you to the end of the line."

Those words — words a small, sick Steve Rogers had once heard in Brooklyn — cracked something deep inside the Soldier.

Bucky pulled Steve out of the Potomac River. Then he disappeared. Somewhere in Bucharest, in a small apartment with a stolen notebook, a man with a metal arm began the slow, frightened work of trying to remember who he used to be.
