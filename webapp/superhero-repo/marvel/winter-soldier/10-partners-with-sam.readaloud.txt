Have you ever been forced to do a group project with someone who was NOT your first choice? Maybe you wanted to work with your best friend, but the teacher paired you with someone else? And at first you were like… "ohhhh nooo"… but by the end you were like, "wait, actually, this person is GREAT?"

That is EXACTLY what happened with Sam Wilson and Bucky Barnes. And it might be the best partnership in all of the Avengers.

Now, who is Sam Wilson? Sam used to be a hero called the FALCON. He flew around with these incredible mechanical WINGS. WHOOOOSH! He had been an Air Force pararescue — that means he used to fly into dangerous places and rescue people. And after Steve Rogers retired, you know what Steve did? He handed Sam his SHIELD. He looked Sam in the eye and said, "You're the next Captain America."

That is a HUGE responsibility!

Bucky? Well, you know Bucky. One hundred and six years old. Vibranium arm. Just out of seventy years of HYDRA programming. A man who would rather eat a plum standing over the sink than make small talk with anyone.

Steve had left these two to each other. Steve loved them both. Steve was like — "you'll figure it out." That's it! That was the plan!

The first time Sam invited Bucky to dinner, Bucky said "no" and walked away. Sam laughed all the way home to Louisiana. He was used to this guy by now.

But here is where it changed. There was a bad situation in Europe — a group called the Flag Smashers, led by a young woman named Karli Morgenthau. Sam and Bucky had to team up to stop them.

And oh, they ARGUED. So much! All the time!

Sam wanted to TALK to Karli, to reason with her. Bucky wanted to take her down right now. CLANG!

Sam ate balanced meals like a sensible person. Bucky? Plums. Over the sink. Standing up. ALL the plums.

Sam loved jazz music. SMOOTH JAZZ! Bucky? Bucky liked the Brooklyn Dodgers. (Tell Bucky the Dodgers don't even play in Brooklyn anymore. Just try it. He'll be UPSET.)

They drove from Munich to Riga in a tiny tiny rental car together arguing the WHOLE WAY. PUTT PUTT went the engine. BLAH BLAH BLAH went the two heroes.

But by the end of that trip? They actually LIKED each other. They just had to discover it by yelling about strategy for ten hours.

Now here is the BEST part of the whole story.

Sam's sister, Sarah Wilson, lived in a little Louisiana town called DELACROIX. She ran the family fishing business — there was a big boat that had been in their family for years. And the boat was falling APART. The bank wanted to take it.

So Bucky drove down to Louisiana one weekend. He rolled up his sleeves. His vibranium arm GLEAMED in the sun. And he started patching the hull. THUNK THUNK THUNK went his hammer.

Sarah's two boys — Cass and AJ — climbed ALL OVER him! They asked him every question you can think of about his arm. "Is it cold?" "Is it heavy?" "Can it pick up the WHOLE BOAT?" Bucky answered every single one. Seriously. Like a teacher.

He fixed a stuck part of the boat. He carried a HUGE motor across the dock like it was a loaf of bread. WHEW! He stayed for dinner. He did the dishes! By Sunday night, the Wilson family had decided: Bucky was family now. Nobody asked Bucky's opinion. (He didn't mind.)

Then King T'Challa of Wakanda sent Sam a present — a brand new Captain America suit, made from Wakandan VIBRANIUM. It had wings AND the shield, all built in. WHOOSH! ZING! Sam put it on and he was Falcon and Captain America at the same time.

And from that day on, the new team had a rhythm.

Sam in the SKY!
Bucky on the GROUND!
Sam doing the talking!
Bucky standing just close enough that nobody tried anything funny!

They argued about jazz. They argued about plums. They argued about whether wings counted as a superpower. But they never EVER argued about who had whose back.

And in the quiet Louisiana evenings — with the bayou frogs going RIBBIT-RIBBIT, and Sarah's gumbo on the table, and Cass and AJ asleep upstairs — the old kid from Brooklyn and the new Captain America would sit on the dock with their feet in the water. And Bucky would think:

After EVERYTHING. After all the lost years.

I am, finally, finally, finally HOME.
