Are you sitting down? Good — because this story starts in the mountains of Bavaria, Germany, and it is about the most unusual, acrobatic, wonderful hero you have ever heard of.

His name is Kurt Wagner. And from the very first second of his life, Kurt was different.

His skin? Indigo blue — like the deep sky just before the stars come out. His hands had THREE fingers each. His feet had TWO toes. He had pointed ears, glowing yellow eyes, and — wait for it — a long, curling, prehensile TAIL!

And he could also do THIS: BAMF! He would disappear in a puff of brimstone smoke and reappear somewhere else entirely! BAMF! Gone! BAMF! Back! There one second and somewhere completely different the next, teleporting like lightning deciding where to land!

When Kurt was very small, his mother — a shape-changing mutant named Mystique — made a heartbreaking choice to leave him in the forest. But a kind circus woman named Margali Szardos found him and brought him home. The circus folk adored Kurt immediately. They had never seen a performer like him! He could flip on the trapeze, swing on his tail, and tumble in ways that left audiences breathless. They called him — and you have to say this with flair — "the Incredible Nightcrawler!"

But one night, in a town near Stuttgart, a frightened crowd surrounded Kurt. They pointed and shouted. They called him a monster. Kurt had done nothing wrong, but he was alone.

And THEN — a calm, warm voice cut through the noise.

Professor Charles Xavier rolled forward in his wheelchair — a brilliant man who could communicate with his own thoughts — and he said, clearly and without any hesitation: "You are not a monster. You are a gift to this world."

WHOOSH — like a fresh wind through everything.

Xavier offered Kurt a place at his school in Westchester, New York — Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters — where mutants were welcome, celebrated, and trained to be heroes.

Kurt said YES.

He suited up in a blue uniform and trained in the Danger Room alongside Wolverine, Storm, and Colossus — crashing through obstacles, teleporting past laser grids, growing stronger every day. He took the name NIGHTCRAWLER.

He read poetry in the library after dinner. He practiced swordsmanship with a silver rapier in the courtyard on quiet mornings — SWISH, SWISH, the blade tracing arcs in the dawn air. He led gentle prayers in the school's small chapel at the end of the east wing. He smiled — that wide, warm, magnificent smile — in every single mission briefing.

BAMF! There goes Nightcrawler — the bravest, most acrobatic, most kind-hearted blue hero in the whole Marvel universe!
