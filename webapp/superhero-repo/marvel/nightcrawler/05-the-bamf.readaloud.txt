Okay — hands up if you have ever wondered: when Kurt Wagner goes BAMF and disappears — WHERE DOES HE GO?

Because he doesn't just flicker. He doesn't vanish like a light switching off. He goes somewhere. There's smoke! There's a sound! Something is definitely happening!

So Kurt drew a diagram on the chalkboard in the Xavier Institute's briefing room and explained the whole thing, and we are going to explain it to you right now.

When Kurt teleports, he steps — just for a fraction of a second — into somewhere called the BRIMSTONE DIMENSION. Not this world. Not another planet. A corridor between places. A secret in-between.

The purple-grey SMOKE? That's residue from the Brimstone Dimension sneaking through into our world. The loud BAMF sound? That's all the air rushing back in to fill the space where Kurt just was.

BAMF! He's gone! The air goes CRACK. The smoke hangs in the air for just a moment. And then, somewhere else entirely — BAMF! — Kurt appears.

Now for the rules — because even teleportation has rules!

Rule one: Kurt can jump about THREE KILOMETERS in one hop before it gets too tiring. Beyond that, things get wobbly.

Rule two: he must be able to SEE or clearly REMEMBER where he's going. You do NOT want to materialize inside a wall. Kurt is very clear about this.

Rule three: he CAN carry passengers! But each extra person is extra work — it's like running while someone sits on your shoulders.

Rule four: after eight or ten rapid jumps in a row, he gets dizzy and needs a rest. (Even superheroes need a rest sometimes!)

In the Danger Room — the X-Men's training space full of moving walls and robot opponents — Kurt showed everyone what all of this looks like at full speed. BAMF-BAMF-BAMF: three people safely evacuated from a simulated collapsed building in under ten seconds! BAMF: on the back of a training robot, disabling its sensors before it even turned around! And in a tight corridor, he used short rapid hops to bounce off walls and ceiling like the most acrobatic pinball you have ever seen!

But you know what Kurt says is the most important part?

It's not the speed. It's not the Brimstone Dimension.

It's the COURAGE to jump when you can't fully see where you're going.

Every teleport is a leap of faith. Aim carefully, trust yourself, and MOVE. Doubt in the middle of a jump? That's the one thing that can make it go wrong.

Professor Xavier told Kurt that this is the best lesson his power has to teach: commitment matters more than certainty. You don't always know exactly what's on the other side. But you prepare, you pay attention, and then —

BAMF!

You leap with your whole heart.
