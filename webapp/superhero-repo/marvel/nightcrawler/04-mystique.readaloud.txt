Have you ever had that feeling where something familiar is nearby — but you can't quite name it? Kurt Wagner had that feeling the moment a woman walked through the door of a Bavarian mountain lodge in Germany.

She looked ordinary. Brown hair. Grey coat. A rucksack. Nothing unusual at all.

But something pulled at Kurt's chest, like the feeling before a BAMF — like his whole body was pointing at something important.

Her name was Raven Darkhölme. And most of the world knew her as MYSTIQUE — a mutant who could change her face and body into any person she wanted. Right now she was wearing an ordinary disguise, but underneath? Her natural skin was blue. Indigo blue.

Sound familiar?

Mystique had come with important information. A dangerous device had been stolen from a research station in a place called Genosha, and she knew where the thieves were hiding it. She told the X-Men everything she knew.

Her information was good. Really good.

The X-Men flew to Genosha, found the stolen device, and in the middle of a crumbling, collapsing building, Kurt did what Kurt does best. BAMF — a hostage yanked clear! BAMF — a second one! BAMF — BAMF — a third! All three safe in the courtyard before the roof came down!

After the mission, while the dust was still settling, Mystique walked up to Kurt alone. She stood in the rubble and looked at him — and for a moment, she let her disguise slip. Her skin went indigo blue.

The same color as his.

"I am your mother," she said.

Kurt stood very, very still.

He had wondered. Deep down, in the quiet part of himself, he had wondered. Two people with the exact same impossible blue skin, both from Germany, both with powers — it wasn't hard to work out.

But he had Margali Szardos. He had grown up in her circus, in her wagon, surrounded by her children and her warmth. She was his mother in every way that mattered.

He looked at Mystique for a long moment.

"I believe you had reasons," he said quietly. "I do not need to know them all. Margali Szardos is my mother. She has always been my mother. That is true."

Mystique's face was hard to read. But something in it — just a little — softened.

Kurt put out his three-fingered hand. She took it. They shook.

It wasn't a fairy-tale reunion. It was something more real than that: two people, being honest with each other, right where they stood.

Kurt walked back to the X-Men.

He didn't feel smaller. He felt like himself — exactly himself — more clearly than ever before.

BAMF! There he goes.
