The Sentinels arrived without warning. Three of them descended on Salem Center, New York, on a grey Tuesday morning — enormous purple-and-pink robots, each one forty feet tall, their flat faces lit by cold white scanner beams. They had one purpose: to hunt mutants. Their designer, a scientist named Bolivar Trask, had built them to do exactly that, and the Sentinels were very good at their job. Their scanners swept the Xavier Institute grounds in seconds.

Kurt Wagner was in the courtyard practicing swordsmanship with his rapier when the first scanner beam found him. He felt the cold light travel across his skin and heard the mechanical voice boom down from above: "MUTANT DETECTED. ACQUIRING TARGET." He dove sideways — BAMF — and reappeared on the roof of the boathouse forty meters away, panting behind a chimney stack.

The other X-Men scrambled to respond. Storm summoned a curtain of fog to blind the Sentinels' optical sensors, and Wolverine charged the nearest robot's ankle with his adamantium claws sparking. But the Sentinels were adaptable. They had been programmed to counter mutant abilities, and they adjusted their tactics with every attack.

Kurt's role was speed and distraction. He understood that his teleportation could not harm a Sentinel directly, but it could confuse one completely. BAMF — he appeared on a Sentinel's shoulder. BAMF — he vanished before it could grab him. BAMF — he reappeared behind its skull plate and jammed his rapier through a ventilation seam, prying out a fistful of sensor wires. The giant robot lurched and stumbled, its head swinging uselessly from side to side.

While the first Sentinel was blinded, Colossus drove both metal fists into its knee joint, and the machine toppled with a ground-shaking CRASH into the sports field. Storm called down a focused lightning strike on the second Sentinel's central processor — a precise bolt that she aimed with extraordinary care, threading it between dormant trees. The robot froze mid-step and went dark.

The third Sentinel turned and fled, presumably to report back to its handlers. Kurt watched it climb toward the clouds and felt a complicated sadness. They would return. Sentinels always returned. But today the Xavier Institute was safe.

Back in the common room, Professor Xavier addressed the team. He was not triumphant. "Bolivar Trask fears what he does not understand," Xavier said quietly. "Our work is not only to defeat machines. It is to show the world there is nothing to fear from us." Kurt nodded, turning his rapier thoughtfully in his three-fingered hands. That second battle, he knew, would be harder than the first. But it was the one that mattered most.
