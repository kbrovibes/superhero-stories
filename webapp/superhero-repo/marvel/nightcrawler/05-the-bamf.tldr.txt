• New X-Men trainees ask the question Kurt has answered a hundred times: where do you go when you teleport?
• Kurt explains: he steps into the Brimstone Dimension — a warm, dark corridor between places — for less than one second
• The purple smoke is residue from that dimension; the BAMF sound is air rushing back in
• Limits: three kilometers per hop; must see or clearly remember the destination; carrying passengers adds effort
• After eight to ten rapid jumps in a row, he grows dizzy and needs rest
• In the Danger Room he evacuates three colleagues in ten seconds, disables a training robot, and ricochets through a tight corridor mid-air
• The part no diagram captures: every teleport is a leap of faith — doubt in the middle is the one thing that makes it go wrong
• Professor Xavier says Kurt's power teaches the best lesson: commitment and clarity matter more than certainty
