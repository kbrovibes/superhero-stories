Do you want to know a secret about Kurt Wagner?

He has a sword.

Not a big crashing heavy sword — a rapier. Thin, balanced, elegant, with a swept hilt that catches the light. He has had it since he was TWELVE YEARS OLD, when Margali Szardos pressed it into his three-fingered hands on the bank of the Rhine river and told him: "A swashbuckler without a blade is only half a swashbuckler."

Since that morning, Kurt has practiced every single day. In meadows. In parking lots. On rooftops in various countries. He is, quietly and without making a big deal of it, one of the finest fencers in Western Europe.

Now you might think: Kurt, you can TELEPORT. Why do you need a sword?

The answer is: the rapier is for precision. And nothing proves this better than the mission in Madrid.

Thieves had broken into the Prado Museum — one of the most famous art museums in all of Spain — and stolen a small painting by a master named Francisco Goya. A portrait of a woman in a red dress. Small enough to carry under one arm. Worth an enormous amount to the people of Spain.

The thieves had rigged the security grid so that any large burst of energy — lightning, adamantium claws — would bring the ceiling down on hundreds of irreplaceable paintings.

Storm looked at Wolverine. Wolverine looked at Storm. They both looked at Kurt.

Kurt smiled.

He moved through the darkened galleries alone. His two-toed feet were completely silent on the cool marble floors. He BAMF-ed past laser beams. He found the first guard — tap, flat of the blade, BAMF — sitting in confused surprise with empty hands. Second guard, same. Third. Fourth.

Not a single painting rocked on its hook.

He found the stolen Goya, wrapped in padded cloth in a back room. He carried it to the exact wall where it belonged — he had memorized the position from the mission briefing — and hung it back, perfectly level.

He was in the courtyard watching the moths circle the streetlamps when the police arrived.

Afterward, Storm asked: "With everything you can do, why do you still carry the rapier?"

Kurt cleaned the blade with a cloth. "Margali gave it to me," he said. "It reminds me where I came from. The circus teaches you early: the most powerful tool you own is not always the right one for the job." He sheathed it with a click. "Also — honestly — it is a great deal of fun."

Storm laughed.

The rapier lay between them on the table, catching the lamplight — two stories at once.

BAMF! En garde!
