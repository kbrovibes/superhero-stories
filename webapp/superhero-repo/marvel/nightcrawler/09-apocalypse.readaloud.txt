Alright — this is the BIG one.

The X-Men's supersonic jet — the Blackbird — is streaking south through the night sky toward Cairo, Egypt. The pyramids are down there somewhere in the dark. And in the middle of Cairo, a communications tower is blinking its lights.

His name is En Sabah Nur. He calls himself APOCALYPSE. He is the oldest mutant alive — thousands of years old — and he believes only the strongest should survive. He has seized the tower and is broadcasting a signal that will disrupt electronic systems across three continents.

The X-Men land at Cairo's edge. Cyclops gives the briefing.

The tower is ringed by automated energy cannons that fire instantly at anything that moves. Apocalypse also has four powerful lieutenants — called Horsemen — guarding the corners.

The plan: Storm and Cyclops make noise on the north and south sides to pull the Horsemen away. Jean Grey uses her telepathy to fog the awareness of the human operators. That opens a path to the east side of the tower.

But someone has to thread through those energy cannons first.

Every eye turns to Kurt.

He's already studying the surveillance photos. The cannons track physical mass. His Brimstone Dimension hops fall below the threshold. He can get through — if he's fast and precise.

BAMF. BAMF. BAMF. He threads through the cannon ring like a needle through silk, each hop landing in a shadow between arcs.

He materializes in the signal room.

Apocalypse is waiting. Seven feet tall. Armored. Ancient. He felt Kurt arrive.

"You are brave, little acrobat," he says. "But bravery is insufficient."

Kurt stands very still. He can't win a direct fight — he knows this. But he doesn't need to win a fight. He needs ninety seconds for Storm to reach the roof array.

So he talks.

He talks about Professor Xavier. He talks about the children at the Xavier Institute — kids who were frightened when they arrived, who found a home. He talks about what mutants could mean to the world. He speaks clearly and calmly, without performing anything.

Seventy-eight seconds.

CRACK-BOOM. Storm's lightning comes through the roof array. The transmitters go dark. Signal stopped.

Apocalypse looks at the dead equipment. Then at Kurt. Something in the ancient face shifts — almost respect. He withdraws without a word.

On the Blackbird flying home, the stars thick and bright above the Mediterranean, Kurt is quiet.

He stood in a room with the most powerful mutant alive, armed with nothing but words.

The words were enough.

Sometimes, he thinks — that is the whole lesson.

BAMF.
