It was October, leaves blowing past the windows in orange and red, and a new suitcase had appeared in the Xavier Institute's front hallway.

On the suitcase sat an eleven-year-old girl named Petra. Arms crossed. Not happy.

Her mutation appeared three days ago: she could walk through solid walls. She had done it at two in the morning and frightened her neighbors, and now she was here, and she didn't want to be here.

The first person she encountered was Kurt Wagner.

He came around the corner humming to himself, stopped when he saw her, and she gave him the most suspicious look an eleven-year-old face can make. Blue skin, pointed ears, curling tail, glowing eyes — she had not expected this.

Kurt looked back at her.

And then he sat down on the floor. Just sat, right there in the hallway, a few feet away. No fuss.

"I understand that look," he said. "I wore it myself once."

She said nothing.

"When I was close to your age," he continued, "a mob in a town near Stuttgart cornered me against a wall. I had done nothing. They were afraid. I had no idea there was a school like this, or that people existed who understood." He paused. "Then Professor Xavier arrived and said something I needed to hear. Everything changed." He looked at her. "I am glad someone told me. I think eventually you will be too."

She looked at him sideways. "You teleport?"

"BAMF," he agreed — and vanished in a small friendly puff of smoke, reappearing on the bench at the end of the hall.

She flinched. Then almost smiled.

That was the beginning.

Over the following weeks, Kurt became her guide — not her teacher, but the person who understood the loneliness of a power you can't turn off. He told her what Margali Szardos and the circus had taught him: an ability that feels like a problem, used with skill and purpose, becomes your greatest strength. He told her about the mob in Stuttgart and every X-Man who had chosen to stay.

He told her: the hardest thing isn't mastering the power. It's learning you deserve to be in the room.

By November, Petra was eating with the other students. By December she was training in the Danger Room. By spring she was one of the school's best young mutants.

On the last evening before winter break, she found Kurt in the chapel. She sat beside him. After a while she said: "I'm glad someone told me too."

The candle burned gold. The pine trees outside held their first snow. Everything was exactly as it should be.

BAMF.
