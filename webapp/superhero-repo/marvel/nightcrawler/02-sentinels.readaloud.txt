Picture this: a grey Tuesday morning at the Xavier Institute. Birds singing. Leaves rustling. Kurt Wagner in the courtyard, swooshing his rapier through the air — SWISH! SWISH! —

And then — BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

The ground shook. Birds stopped singing. Three enormous shadows fell across the courtyard. These shadows were FORTY FEET TALL and made entirely of purple and pink metal.

SENTINELS!

Enormous mutant-hunting robots, designed by a scientist named Bolivar Trask. One job: find mutants and stop them. Their flat robot faces swept the school with cold white scanner beams — back and forth, back and forth.

One beam hit Kurt.

"MUTANT DETECTED." The voice boomed like a foghorn. "ACQUIRING TARGET."

Kurt Wagner does not stand still when something that size points at him. BAMF! He vanished in a puff of smoke and landed crouching on the boathouse roof. BAMF! He jumped to a chimney stack. Too fast, too unpredictable — you can't grab what you can't find!

The rest of the X-Men scrambled into action.

WHOOOOSH — Storm raised her arms and a rolling wall of fog came in from the north, blinding the Sentinels' optical sensors. SLASH-SLASH! — Wolverine drove his adamantium claws into a robot's ankle, sparks flying!

But Kurt had a plan. He was already watching the nearest Sentinel carefully, looking for his moment. BAMF — he appeared on the giant's shoulder. The robot swung its arm to grab him — WHOMP — too slow. BAMF — gone. BAMF — behind its head! Kurt wedged his rapier into a ventilation seam and yanked out a whole bundle of sensor wires. SZZT! The robot's head swung uselessly side to side. It couldn't see anything!

CRASH! Colossus hit the blinded Sentinel's knee with both metal fists. Forty feet of robot toppled sideways into the sports field — the most enormous fall you have ever heard!

CRACKLE-BOOM! Storm sent a pinpoint lightning bolt into the second Sentinel's central processor. Every light went dark. The robot froze mid-step.

The third Sentinel looked around at the two fallen machines — and flew away into the clouds.

Kurt watched it go, feeling something complicated. They would return. More would come. Bolivar Trask hadn't built only three.

That evening, Professor Xavier gathered the team. "The hardest fight isn't stopping a robot," he said quietly. "It's changing a fearful heart."

Kurt turned his rapier in his three-fingered hands, thinking about the mob in Stuttgart and the school that had opened its doors. Robots he could handle. Winning trust would take something different. Something more patient.

He was ready for both.

BAMF!
