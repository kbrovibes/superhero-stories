Oh. Oh no. Do you hear that?

BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

That is the sound of the ground shaking. And the ground is shaking because something very, very big is walking through the woods of Westchester, New York — and it is heading straight for the Xavier Institute.

His name is Cain Marko. He is the JUGGERNAUT. He wears a massive suit of almost-indestructible armor, and deep in his chest beats the power of the Crimson Gem of Cyttorak — a mystical gem that makes him unstoppable. And he hates Professor Xavier — his stepbrother — and today he has decided to do something about it.

BOOM. BOOM. Trees are falling. Birds are flying away as fast as they can.

The X-Men scrambled out to stop him!

Storm hit him with lightning. CRACK-BOOM. The bolts bounced right off his armor. Colossus ran at him in full steel form — CLANG — and the Juggernaut threw him sideways like a crumpled piece of foil. Wolverine raked his adamantium claws across the breastplate. They barely left a scratch.

Nothing was working.

But Kurt Wagner was standing at the edge of the battle — watching. Thinking. Because Kurt is not just brave, he is clever.

He had noticed something. The Juggernaut's helmet was sealed shut with four huge bolts — Professor Xavier's telepathy couldn't get through the metal. But the back seam? The very back of that helmet? That seam was a little weaker than the rest.

And the Juggernaut, with that enormous helmet on, couldn't turn his head quickly at all.

Kurt waited. He watched. The Juggernaut stopped for one second to shove Colossus out of his path —

NOW.

BAMF — Kurt appeared on the Juggernaut's shoulders! He grabbed the back of the helmet. BAMF — he yanked. A bolt CREAKED. BAMF — he yanked again. Another bolt GROANED. BAMF — BAMF — BAMF! The Juggernaut was spinning, roaring, swatting at empty air, but Kurt was never where the giant hands arrived.

Four bolts. Four pulls. And then — CLANK — the fourth bolt gave way.

The helmet came LOOSE.

Kurt BAMF-ed to the fire escape and clung to the railing, his heart hammering.

Professor Xavier reached out with his mind, through the air, finding the thoughts of Cain Marko. Not anger. Underneath the anger — sadness. Loneliness. An old, old hurt.

Xavier spoke to all of it. Quietly. Without cruelty.

The Juggernaut stopped.

He stood very still for a long moment. Then he turned around and walked back into the woods.

Wolverine walked over to Kurt and put one big, gruff hand on Kurt's shoulder. He didn't say anything. He didn't need to.

Kurt smiled his wide smile.

A giant had been stopped — not by the biggest punch, but by the cleverest mind.

BAMF! On to the next one.
