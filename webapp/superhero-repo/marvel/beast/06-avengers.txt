The call came on a Thursday. Tony Stark was on the other end of the line, which Hank found immediately amusing, because Tony Stark had a habit of opening conversations by assuming the other person was already delighted to hear from him. In this case, Hank was, though he made sure not to show it too quickly. Stark was recruiting for the Avengers. He had a need for a scientist who could also punch things. Hank said he would think about it. He spent forty-five minutes thinking about it, which for him was an unusually long time.

He joined.

The Avengers operated differently from the X-Men. Where Xavier's team moved quietly, working to protect a world that often feared them, the Avengers operated on a grander, louder stage — a team recognized by the world, trusted by governments, expected to handle threats of the highest possible order. The Avengers Mansion on Fifth Avenue in Manhattan was a very different house from the Xavier Institute: brass fixtures, formal meeting rooms, and a rotating cast of the most powerful individuals on the planet.

Hank settled in and immediately began cataloguing everything he could learn. He worked well with Captain America, who had a strategic mind that Hank respected enormously, and with Iron Man, whose engineering talent matched Hank's biochemical expertise in interesting ways. They had long arguments about materials science that the other Avengers quietly escaped by remembering urgent things they had to do elsewhere.

His first major Avengers mission sent him alongside Thor, the Scarlet Witch, and the Vision to address a threat from the Absorbing Man — a criminal named Carl Creel who could transform his body into the material of anything he touched. The Absorbing Man had absorbed the mineral composition of a bridge support beam and grown to enormous size. Physical strikes were useless — hitting him simply transferred the material into the striking surface.

Hank's solution took eleven minutes of rapid thinking and one precise application of a chemical inhibitor he had synthesized in the Avengers Mansion laboratory during the drive over. He explained it to Thor in three sentences on the approach. Thor nodded, swung Mjolnir into the inhibitor charge Hank lobbed — CRACK — dispersing it across the Absorbing Man's surface with perfect force. Creel reverted to human form and went down hard.

"You are a very unusual warrior," Thor said afterward, which was possibly the nicest thing anyone had ever told Hank McCoy.

"Thank you," said Hank. "You are a very good delivery system."
