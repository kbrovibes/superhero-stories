Are you ready? Because this story starts with the biggest hands you have ever seen on a baby — and ends with one of the SMARTEST, STRONGEST, most loveable heroes in the entire Marvel universe!

His name is Henry Philip McCoy. Hank, to his friends. And from the very first day he was born in Dunfermline, Illinois, it was clear that Hank was going to be something extraordinary. His hands were ENORMOUS. His feet were ENORMOUS. The doctors scratched their heads. The nurses exchanged surprised looks. His parents, Norton and Edna McCoy, just laughed and looked at each other and said — "That's our Hank!"

As Hank grew up, those big hands and feet turned out to be absolutely incredible. He could leap across the entire gymnasium in a single bound — WHOOOMP — and land without a sound. He could hang upside down from a chin-up bar and read a thick science textbook at the same time. He set every single athletic record at Waukegan High School AND won the state science fair in the exact same year! His teachers didn't know whether to put him in gym class or the advanced chemistry seminar. The answer, of course, was both.

But one afternoon, some very bad men called the Conquistador showed up at the football game. They grabbed his parents and threatened them, trying to force Hank to do their dirty work. Hank was NOT having that. WHOOSH! He spun into the air. CRACK! He knocked them off their feet one by one, moving faster than any ordinary person could follow. When it was over, he stood in the middle of the football field — breathing hard, his enormous hands still raised — and understood something he'd never quite put into words before: I am a MUTANT.

Professor Charles Xavier had already noticed. Xavier ran a remarkable school at the Xavier Institute in Westchester, New York, for young people with mutant abilities — a place where extraordinary was not strange, it was celebrated. He sent Hank a personal invitation. And Hank — who loved learning just as much as he loved somersaulting off the top of the bleachers — said YES before he had even finished reading the letter.

That was how Hank McCoy became BEAST, one of the very first five X-Men! He put on a blue-and-yellow uniform and joined Scott Summers, Jean Grey, Bobby Drake, and Warren Worthington III — and the X-Men were never quite the same again.

LEAP! SWING! THUD! In battle, Beast was absolutely unstoppable. He bounced off walls. He somersaulted over villains. He hung upside down from impossible places.

And afterward, he would flip open a book and quote a poem to anyone who would listen.

Brains AND muscle — and the biggest, warmest heart on the whole team. That's our Beast.
