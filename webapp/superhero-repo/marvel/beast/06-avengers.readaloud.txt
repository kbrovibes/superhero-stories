One Thursday, Hank McCoy's phone rang.

He was upside down, naturally. Reading a paper on enzyme kinetics.

He answered it.

"Beast," said Tony Stark's voice on the other end. "I need a scientist who can also punch things. You in?"

Hank thought about it for forty-five minutes. Then he said yes.

That is how Henry Philip McCoy became an Avenger!

The Avengers Mansion was on Fifth Avenue in Manhattan — a big, formal building with brass fixtures and proper meeting rooms and a roster that included Thor, Captain America, the Scarlet Witch, Iron Man, and the Vision. It was very grand. It was very loud. Hank loved it.

He and Tony Stark had ENORMOUS arguments about materials science that went on for hours and sent everyone else in the mansion fleeing toward the exits. He and Captain America — Steve Rogers — talked strategy late into the night, two careful minds who both believed in planning ahead.

And then came the Absorbing Man.

His real name was Carl Creel, and his ability was astonishing: he could transform his entire body into whatever material he touched. Touch concrete? He becomes concrete. Touch steel? He becomes steel. That afternoon he had grabbed a bridge support beam and become iron — enormous, heavy, sixty feet tall.

You cannot punch someone who is made of iron. You just hurt your fist.

This was not a problem for Hank. Because Hank didn't lead with his fist.

He led with his BRAIN.

On the drive to the scene — in the back of the Avengers' Quinjet — he synthesized a chemical inhibitor. A substance that, if it covered Creel's surface, would interrupt his ability to hold a transformed state. He filled it into a small round capsule. Like a fizzing bath bomb, but for defeating villains.

He turned to Thor.

Thor was sitting across from him, massive and golden-haired, holding Mjolnir on his knee. Hank explained the plan in exactly three sentences.

Thor smiled. He likes a good plan.

They flew in over the bridge. Hank lobbed the capsule — TOSS — into the air. Thor's eyes tracked it perfectly. Mjolnir swung — CRACK — and struck it dead center, shattering it into a fine mist that settled all across the Absorbing Man's enormous iron surface.

Carl Creel lurched. He shrank. He returned to human size and sat down in the middle of the bridge looking extremely confused.

The battle was over in twelve minutes.

"You are a very unusual warrior," Thor told Hank, with genuine admiration.

Hank straightened his glasses.

"Thank you," he said. "You are a very good delivery system."

Thor laughed for approximately the next five minutes. It was loud enough to rattle the bridge cables.

Hank smiled his wide, wide smile.

Being an Avenger was going to be excellent.
