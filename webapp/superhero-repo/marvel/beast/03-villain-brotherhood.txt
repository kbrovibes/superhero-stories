Magneto believed the world would never accept mutants — and he had decided that meant mutants should rule the world instead. He had formed the Brotherhood of Evil Mutants, gathering dangerous allies: Mystique, who could become any person she chose; Toad, who could leap great distances and had a striking, adhesive tongue; and the Blob, whose enormous body could not be moved by any force. When the Brotherhood attacked a United Nations summit in New York City, the X-Men raced to respond, and Hank found himself facing Toad in the marble corridors of the UN building.

Toad was faster than he looked. He launched himself off a pillar, his tongue shooting out — CRACK — and wrapping around Hank's ankle, yanking him off his feet. Hank rolled with it, using the pull to build momentum rather than fight it, and came up in a handspring that let him close the distance. He was not surprised that Toad was quick. He was surprised by the look on Toad's face — something between fury and genuine pain. Being part of Magneto's Brotherhood was not, Hank suspected, a joyful life.

But they still had to be stopped. Hank feinted left, went right, and caught Toad by his coat collar, pinning him firmly against the wall. Across the chamber he could see Cyclops and Jean Grey holding off the Blob, and further back, the unmistakable silhouette of Magneto himself — silver-haired, regal in his red-and-purple armor — using magnetic force to tear the metal railings from the balcony and redirect them toward the X-Men.

"Professor Xavier taught you better than this," Hank said to Toad, calmly. "You don't have to be here."

Toad just sneered. But he stopped struggling.

At the far end of the room, Magneto and Cyclops clashed in a spectacular duel — magnetic force against optic blasts — until Jean Grey shifted a steel support beam directly into Magneto's magnetic field, absorbing his energy and throwing him off balance. He retreated, the Brotherhood with him, slipping away before the authorities arrived.

The summit was saved. Hank filed a seventeen-page report on the structural weaknesses of the UN's security layout, suggested twelve improvements, and then went to the library. The Brotherhood would return — Magneto always did. But today, the X-Men had answered with reason, with discipline, and with Beast's extremely well-timed handspring.
