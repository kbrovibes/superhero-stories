Picture this — the desert. Miles of sand and rock. The sun blazing overhead. And then, rising up on the horizon, something ENORMOUS and purple and terrifying.

BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

That's a Sentinel — a thirty-foot-tall robot built for one single purpose: hunting mutants. A man named Bolivar Trask had built them at his research complex in New Mexico. He thought mutants were dangerous. He thought people like Hank McCoy needed to be stopped. So he built machines the size of buildings to do the stopping.

The X-Men stood in that desert and looked up — WAY up — and Hank McCoy felt something deep in his chest. Not terror. Something quieter. A sadness. Because someone had actually built a machine specifically to hunt people like him.

But there was no time to feel sad. There were THREE Sentinels — and they were moving FAST.

Cyclops — ZZZAP! — fired an optic blast right at the knee joint of the first one and made it stagger. Iceman threw a wall of ice in front of the second — CRAAACK — blocking it for a few precious seconds.

But the third one had already found Jean Grey's mutant energy signature and was TURNING toward her.

Hank did not hesitate for a single second.

LEAP! He launched himself off a boulder — soaring twenty feet through the air — WHAM — and landed on the Sentinel's shoulder. Right up there on the shoulder of a thirty-foot robot. Like it was no big deal.

He looked down. He looked at the seam where the neck armor met the head. He grabbed it. He PULLED.

CRACKKK-RRRRRIP!

The sensor array came away in his hands. The Sentinel lurched. Its targeting screen went dark. For thirty whole seconds — thirty glorious seconds — it couldn't find a single mutant.

That was all Jean needed. She lifted every rock, every piece of metal, every loose stone within a hundred yards and FLUNG them all at once into the Sentinel's chest. BOOM! Down it went, leaving a crater ten feet deep in the New Mexico sand.

The third Sentinel ran away, but not before sending all its data back to Trask's laboratory.

Hank picked up a piece of the defeated Sentinel's armor and looked at it carefully.

"They'll build more," Cyclops said.

"I know," said Hank. "So let's make sure we understand these things better than the people who built them."

He tucked the fragment in his pack and smiled. Because the smartest person on the team was already one step ahead.
