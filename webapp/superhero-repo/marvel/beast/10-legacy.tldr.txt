• Every year at the Xavier Institute, Hank teaches an unofficial final lesson: he asks students to share the hardest thing about being a mutant
• He listens without fixing — being stared at, losing control, feeling different from family, not knowing who to trust
• Then he shares his own hardest story: three real days alone in the Brand Corporation lab after his mutation became permanent
• He doesn't promise it stops being hard — he promises it gets lighter as you build people, work, and self-knowledge
• He asks them to look around the room and see the community already built among them
• Hank reflects: original X-Man, Avenger, two doctorates, Senate speaker for mutant rights
• He sat up all night with a student who accidentally caused earthquakes, and was there when they stopped
• He closes every lesson the same way — "Go read something" — they groan; he is always smiling
