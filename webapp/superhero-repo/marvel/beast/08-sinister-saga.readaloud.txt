Deep beneath New York City — a very dangerous man was building a very dangerous machine.

His name: Mister Sinister. Real name: Nathaniel Essex. Pale skin. Red diamonds on his forehead. He had spent years collecting genetic data on mutants, building secret files on every X-Man alive. Now he had built the Genetic Sequencer: a machine that could permanently rewrite mutant DNA. His plan? An army of controlled mutants who would follow his orders forever.

The X-Men found out. They were NOT going to let that happen.

Hank spent three long days reconstructing stolen engineering schematics — matching partial blueprints, filling in the gaps, cross-referencing every detail — until he had a complete map of Sinister's underground laboratory beneath Manhattan. He handed copies to Cyclops, Jean Grey, Storm, and Gambit on the Quinjet. They dropped into the tunnels and followed his map through the dark.

Sinister's lab was guarded by his Marauders — ruthless mercenary fighters. In a narrow stone corridor, Hank came face to face with Sabretooth. Enormous. Feral. Very sharp claws. Very bad attitude.

Hank assessed: the width, the ceiling height, the stone walls.

Then he moved.

LEAP — off the wall! FLIP — over Sabretooth's arm! BOUNCE — off the ceiling! Three directions in two seconds, and Hank was already on the radio: "Gambit — northeast clear. Storm — gap on the upper level. Jean — thirty seconds!"

Because Hank never just fought. He was always doing five things at once.

When Sabretooth stumbled — frustrated, grabbing at nothing — Hank redirected him neatly into the wall. Sabretooth sat down and looked confused.

Across the lab, Jean Grey reached the Genetic Sequencer. She raised her hands.

Then Mister Sinister stepped from the shadows. Cool. Pale. Fast. He hit Jean with a focused psychic counter-blast. She staggered backward.

Hank was already running.

Three bounds — low, shoulder first — WHAM — Sinister hit the wall and slid down looking very surprised.

Six seconds. That was all Jean needed. The Sequencer's power core rose, then tore free with a sound like a thunderclap.

KZZZT — POP — darkness.

Sinister found his escape hatch and was gone. He always had escape hatches. Hank made a mental note to account for escape hatches next time.

Back at the Institute, Hank spent two careful days analyzing every scrap of Sinister's stolen database, ensuring nothing could be salvaged or reconstructed. Then he erased everything and wrote one line in his journal:

"The cost of this work is vigilance, always."

Mister Sinister would build another laboratory. He always did. The X-Men would find it. They always did.

That was the deal.
