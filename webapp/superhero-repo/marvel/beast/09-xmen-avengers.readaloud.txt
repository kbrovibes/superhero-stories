Ready for the biggest team-up in the whole collection? This one has EVERYBODY.

An ancient mutant called Apocalypse — real name En Sabah Nur — had been alive for thousands of years. He believed only the strongest beings deserved to survive. To prove his point, he released a synthetic virus into six cities at the same time: New York, London, Tokyo, Cairo, Sydney, and Buenos Aires.

This virus didn't just make people sick. It made their abilities go completely, dangerously out of control. Mutants and regular people alike became forces they could not turn off. Six cities. Total chaos.

The world needed two teams — and those two teams needed the same person.

Tony Stark called Hank McCoy. Hank was already in his car.

For seventy-two hours — three full days, zero sleep — they worked side by side at Avengers Tower. Enormous whiteboards covered in equations. Complicated chemistry equipment clattering and spinning and beeping at all hours. Major arguments about aerosol dispersal rates. Minor disagreements about music — Tony wanted rock; Hank wanted quiet. They compromised on moderate classical. Neither of them loved it. Neither of them stopped working.

Hour twenty: a promising lead.
Hour forty: a setback. Back to the whiteboard.
Hour sixty: almost there.
Hour seventy-two: DONE.

An aerosol cure so precise it could neutralize the virus with a single breath — completely and safely, in anyone it reached.

The challenge now: six cities. All at once.

Thor stood on the roof of Avengers Tower, Mjolnir in hand. He swung it — WHOOOOOM — and the air shifted. He swung again — CRAAACK — and above the city, clouds gathered and spiraled into a vast, purposeful, magnificent storm.

Jean Grey focused from inside the building, guiding the storm's reach toward all six cities simultaneously. Captain America coordinated ground teams. Cyclops and Storm swept across the infected zones overhead.

Hank stood on the roof with the first canister. Took a breath. Opened it.

The cure went out on the wind — invisible, drifting gently through every street and open window, touching everyone it reached.

Four hours later: contained. Eight hours later: completely gone. Every person affected: safe.

Tony Stark walked into the quiet lab afterward.

"Good work, McCoy," he said.

"You too, Stark," said Hank.

They shook hands. Tony went to find something to eat. Hank sat in the lab for a while, feeling the particular warmth that comes when a very large problem is completely, finally solved.

Then he called the Xavier Institute to check on his students.

World saved. Students first. Always.
