Imagine a beautiful building full of marble floors and big tall windows — the United Nations in New York City, where leaders from every country in the world had come to meet. Now imagine — CRASH — a villain exploding through the front door!

His name: Magneto. Silver hair. Red and purple armor. Eyes that see every piece of metal in the room as a weapon. Magneto believed that mutants would never be treated fairly by regular people — and he had decided the answer was to take over instead.

With him came his Brotherhood of Evil Mutants: Mystique, who could change her face and body to look like absolutely anyone; the Blob, so enormous that no force in the world could push him even one inch; and Toad — a jumpy, leaping, long-tongued fighter who moved like a grasshopper and was considerably nastier.

The X-Men arrived at full speed.

Hank McCoy went straight for Toad in the marble corridor.

THWACK! Toad's tongue — which was long and sticky and fast as a whip — snapped out and caught Hank around the ankle!

Most people would have fallen flat.

Hank did a HANDSPRING.

He used the pull to build speed, spun in the air, landed right in front of Toad, and grabbed him by the coat collar. He pinned him against the wall. Toad blinked.

"You don't have to be here," Hank said, completely calm. "Professor Xavier would help you, if you asked."

Across the great chamber, Cyclops was blasting at the Blob — ZZAP ZZAP — while Jean Grey was moving things with her mind to keep Magneto busy. And Magneto was tearing the metal railings off the balcony — RRRIIIP — and hurling them toward the X-Men like enormous silver javelins.

Jean kept her cool. She picked up a massive steel beam with her mind — one of the HEAVIEST ones in the building — and floated it right into Magneto's magnetic field. His own power got tangled up in the beam and pulled sideways, throwing him off balance. He stumbled. He looked furious. He grabbed his Brotherhood and retreated before the police could arrive.

The summit was saved. All those world leaders were safe.

Hank let Toad go. Toad scrambled away without another word.

Hank wrote a seventeen-page security report on the UN building's weak spots, suggested twelve improvements, and handed it to the director. Then he went to find a library.

That's Beast for you — saving the world, and doing his homework, all in the same afternoon.
