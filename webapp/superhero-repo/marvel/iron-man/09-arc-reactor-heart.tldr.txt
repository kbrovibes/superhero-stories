• The Arc Reactor — originally conceived by Howard Stark and Anton Vanko — is first made real by Tony in a cave in Afghanistan
• Powered by palladium (and later a new synthesized element), the palm-sized device produces enormous energy and keeps shrapnel away from Tony's heart
• The Arc Reactor is both a power source for the Iron Man suit AND Tony's life-support system
• Early reactors poison Tony's blood with palladium; he searches desperately for a safer element to replace it
• The breakthrough: a hidden message from his father Howard, encoded in the blueprints of the Stark Expo — pointing to a new element Tony synthesizes himself
• This discovery is a reconciliation between father and son; Howard always believed Tony would change the world
• Tony builds a giant Arc Reactor at the heart of Stark Industries — a proof of concept for clean, renewable energy for the whole planet
• In his later years, advanced surgery allows Tony to remove the reactor from his chest; he continues wearing a version of it to power his nanotech suits — the blue glow now a symbol of commitment, not vulnerability
