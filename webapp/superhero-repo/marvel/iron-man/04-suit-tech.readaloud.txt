If you ever visited Tony Stark's workshop beneath his home in Malibu, you would find something wonderful. Suits of armor lined the walls — each one slightly different, each one a little more incredible than the last. Some were red and gold. Some were silver. One was enormous, built to contain a rampaging giant. Tony called them his Marks — Mark I, Mark II, Mark III — all the way to the most advanced armor ever made.

Each suit began with a problem Tony needed to solve. The early ones were made from gold-titanium alloy — strong as a tank but light enough to fly. The repulsor rays in the palms could launch Tony into the sky or blast through a bank vault door. The Unibeam in his chest blazed like a small sun. And guiding it all was J.A.R.V.I.S. — a brilliant artificial intelligence who flew alongside Tony like the world's most capable co-pilot, whispering information, tracking targets, and helping Tony stay one step ahead.

Over the years, the suits grew stranger and more wonderful. The Hulkbuster was a massive shell of armor designed to hold a very large and very angry green teammate. The Silver Centurion could absorb energy. One suit worked underwater; another could travel through space.

And then Tony reached the most amazing invention of all. His final armor used nanotechnology — billions of microscopic machines stored inside his own Arc Reactor, living in his chest. With a thought, they could pour out over his body like a shimmering silver wave and become anything he imagined.

And do you know what Tony himself said, on one quiet evening, looking at all those beautiful suits? He said that the armor was the least important thing about Iron Man. The suit was just metal. The hero inside it — that was what truly mattered.
