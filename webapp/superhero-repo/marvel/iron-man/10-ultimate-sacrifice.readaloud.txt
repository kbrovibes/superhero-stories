At the end of all Tony Stark's inventions — at the end of all the suits and the reactors and the great clanging battles — there was a quiet cabin by a lake.

Tony lived there with Pepper and their little girl, Morgan. After so many years of noise and danger and the weight of the world, he had found peace. It was small and warm and full of the smell of breakfast in the morning. He had built something better than any armor: a home.

Then the call came. The friends he thought he had lost — half the people in the whole universe, taken away in a single terrible moment by the villain Thanos — might be brought back. All it would take was time travel, which was considered impossible.

Tony sat alone in his workshop by the lake and did the impossible. He solved it. He kept it secret at first — because he was afraid. He was afraid of losing what he had. But he could not ignore the children who had been taken, the parents, the friends. He thought of Peter Parker. He thought of every ordinary person just trying to live their life.

He handed Pepper the answer he had built and flew to the battle.

The fight was enormous. When everything seemed lost, Tony saw his one chance. He reached out with his nanotech armor and pulled the six Infinity Stones away from Thanos. The power of all six was too much for any living person to survive. Tony knew that. He snapped his fingers anyway.

Thanos and his entire army turned to dust and drifted away.

Pepper knelt beside Tony in the rubble, took his hand, and said the words he had never quite been able to say to himself: "You can rest now."

And that is the story of Tony Stark — a man who walked into a cave carrying nothing, and changed everything.
