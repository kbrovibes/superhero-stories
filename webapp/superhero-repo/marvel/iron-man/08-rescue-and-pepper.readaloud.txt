Behind every great inventor is usually someone making sure the inventor remembers to eat dinner. For Tony Stark, that person was Pepper Potts.

Pepper began as his executive assistant at Stark Industries, and from her very first day, she was the most important person in the building. She managed meetings and contracts and phone calls. She also, when necessary, walked into Tony's workshop and told the world's most brilliant man — very firmly — when he was being completely ridiculous. He listened. Eventually.

Their friendship grew slowly into something much more beautiful. They fell in love the way real people do: gradually, honestly, through a thousand small moments of trust.

Tony worried about Pepper all the time. That was simply the truth of it. He spent countless late nights in his workshop building something he called the Mark 49 — the Rescue armor. It was purple and silver, and it wasn't built for war. It had the strongest shields he could design, and its weapons were gentle ones. He built it for her and hoped with all his heart she would never need to wear it.

But when the terrible villain Thanos threatened every living thing in the universe, Pepper put it on. She stepped into the armor. And when the final battle came, there they were — Tony in his red-and-gold Iron Man suit and Pepper in her purple-and-silver Rescue armor, fighting side by side.

It was the most beautiful thing Tony Stark had ever seen.

And that is how Pepper Potts — kind, brave, brilliant Pepper — proved that the heart of the Iron Man story was never the armor at all. It was the people inside it.
