Picture this: race cars zooming around a famous track in Monaco, the sun blazing, the crowd going wild. Beautiful, right? Now picture a man walking ONTO that track — wearing a glowing harness and swinging two electrified whips that crackle like lightning. ZZZZAP! CRASH! Not so beautiful anymore. Let's find out who this is!

His name was Ivan Vanko, and he was brilliant — dangerously brilliant. His father, Anton Vanko, had once worked alongside Howard Stark to create the very first Arc Reactor. But things had gone terribly wrong for the Vanko family. Anton had passed away poor and forgotten, far from home in Russia. Ivan never forgave the Stark name. So he built his own Arc Reactor with his own hands, and connected two long plasma-charged whips to it — whips that could slice through reinforced steel like scissors through paper. CRACK! ZAP! ZZZZRAK!

He disguised himself as a racing mechanic to sneak onto the Monaco Grand Prix track. And do you know what Tony Stark was doing at that exact moment? Racing a Formula One car! No armor. No JARVIS. Nothing but a helmet and a steering wheel. Uh oh.

Ivan swung those whips — CRASH CRASH CRASH — and race cars exploded like firecrackers all around the track. Tony was in real trouble. But then — WHOMP! — a silver suitcase came sliding across the tarmac. His loyal friends Pepper Potts and Happy Hogan had come running! Tony popped the case open, and out unfolded the Mark V, a special suit designed to pack flat like a suitcase and spring open in seconds. It locked around Tony's body — CLANK CLANK CLANK — just in time. Tony stopped Ivan, but those whips left deep gouges in the armor. This enemy was no joke.

Ivan was caught. But then a jealous, scheming man named Justin Hammer broke him out. Hammer thought he could control Ivan and use him to build an army of remote-controlled military drones. He could not. Ivan was not the kind of person anyone controlled. He took Hammer's drones and turned every single one of them against the crowd at the grand Stark Expo. WHOOSH! BUZZ! ZAP!

Iron Man launched into the sky. His best friend James Rhodes — also known as War Machine, wearing a tremendous gray-and-silver armor — launched right beside him. They blasted through the drone fleet together. And then Tony remembered something clever: if he and Rhodey fired their repulsor beams at each other at exactly the same time, the combined blast would create a massive explosion right in the middle. They aimed. They fired. KA-BOOOOM! Ivan's plans ended in a brilliant flash.

Tony looked over at Rhodey hovering beside him and thought: this is what real partnership looks like. Not just machinery. Not just firepower. Two friends who trust each other completely. Now THAT is a superpower.
