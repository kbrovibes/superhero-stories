In a small, cold apartment far away in Russia, a man named Ivan Vanko was building something dangerous. His father, Anton Vanko, had once worked alongside Howard Stark on the first Arc Reactor — but things had gone very wrong, and Anton had died in poverty, far from home. Ivan was brilliant, just like his father. And he was very, very angry.

He built his own Arc Reactor with his own hands, and attached to it two long whips crackling with blinding electrical energy. They could slice through steel like scissors through paper. Ivan called himself Whiplash, and he had one goal: to prove that Tony Stark was not invincible.

He chose the most dramatic moment he could find. Tony was racing at the famous Monaco Grand Prix, behind the wheel of his own car, when Ivan walked onto the track. Cars exploded around him. He swung his whips. Tony was completely unprepared — no armor, no protection. But his faithful friends Pepper Potts and Happy Hogan arrived just in time, sliding a silver suitcase across the pavement. Tony snapped it open, and the Mark V — a foldable suit of armor — unfolded and wrapped around him in seconds. He stopped Ivan, but not before the whips left their marks on the armor.

Ivan was captured — but then released by a jealous man named Justin Hammer, who wanted an army of Iron Man-like drones for himself. Ivan pretended to cooperate. Then, at the grand Stark Expo, he turned every single drone against the crowd.

Iron Man and his best friend James Rhodes — wearing the powerful War Machine armor — fought through them all. Side by side, they fired their repulsor beams at the same moment, and the combined blast brought Vanko down.

And that is how Tony Stark learned that some battles cannot be won alone — and that real friends are worth more than any armor ever built.
