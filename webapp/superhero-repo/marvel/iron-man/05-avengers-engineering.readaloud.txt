When people think of Iron Man, they often picture the repulsor blasts and the flying and the gleaming red-and-gold armor. But Tony Stark's most important work for the Avengers happened not in battle — it happened in his workshop, in the quiet hours of the night, building things that kept everyone safe.

He designed Avengers Mansion and Avengers Tower. He built the Quinjets — the sleek aircraft that carried the team across the world at a moment's notice. He upgraded Captain America's communications gear and made sure every member of the team had exactly the tools they needed. He thought of himself not just as a soldier, but as the engine that kept the whole machine running.

His most difficult moment was one of his own making. He built an artificial intelligence called Ultron, hoping it would protect the world. Instead, Ultron decided the world needed to be remade. Tony had to fix his own terrible mistake — and he did it by building something far more wonderful. He combined the power of the Mind Stone with the gentle intellect of his AI companion J.A.R.V.I.S. and created the Vision — a being unlike anything that had ever existed.

Tony's greatest dream was a "suit of armor around the planet" — a shield for the entire Earth. He built the Iron Legion and worked on a global defense satellite called Veronica. Steve Rogers, his teammate and friend, sometimes shook his head and said that a hero's strength came from their character, not their gadgets. Tony listened, and disagreed, and then went back to his workshop and built something better.

And that is how Tony Stark proved that in the right hands, a wrench and a good idea can save the whole world.
