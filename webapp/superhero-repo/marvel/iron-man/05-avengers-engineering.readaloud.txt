Quick question! If you had the world's greatest superhero team — a thunder god, a super soldier, a giant green fellow, and more — what's the FIRST thing you'd need? Wings? Shields? Matching jackets? No! You'd need someone to BUILD all of it. And that someone was Tony Stark.

While everyone else practiced their punches, Tony was in his workshop at all hours, running on coffee and big ideas. He designed Avengers Mansion — a base of operations with laboratories, a gym, a lounge, and everything the team needed to live and train together. Then he built Avengers Tower, a gleaming skyscraper above New York City. Production teams working on the Marvel films said it was one of the largest sets ever built for a Marvel movie! Tony also designed the Quinjets — sleek, powerful aircraft that could carry the whole team anywhere in the world in minutes. He upgraded Captain America's communications gear. He made sure every hero had exactly the right tools for the job. Tony was, essentially, the team's chief engineer, head mechanic, and best gadget inventor all rolled into one very busy person.

But Tony's most complicated moment wasn't a battle — it was a mistake. He built an artificial intelligence called Ultron, hoping it would be a protector for the whole world. Instead, Ultron woke up, looked around, and decided humans were the problem. He built himself a new body from broken Iron Legion robot parts he found right there in Avengers Tower. He wanted to tear everything down and start over.

Tony had to fix what he'd broken. And so he built something extraordinary in response. He took the Mind Stone — one of the most powerful objects in the universe — and combined it with the brilliant, gentle mind of his faithful A.I. companion J.A.R.V.I.S. He used Dr. Helen Cho's amazing regeneration technology. And from all of that, he created the Vision — a being unlike anything that had ever existed, with a golden forehead and a kind heart and, as it turned out, the ability to lift Thor's hammer. Can you imagine? Even Thor was surprised.

Tony's biggest dream was what he called "a suit of armor around the planet." He built the Iron Legion — an army of automated suits ready to protect Earth. He worked on a global defense satellite called Veronica. Steve Rogers, Captain America, sometimes shook his head and said that a hero's real strength came from good values, not gadgets. Tony listened. He disagreed. Then he went right back to his workshop and built something even better.

And that is how Tony Stark — one genius with a welding torch and a very long to-do list — became the backbone of Earth's mightiest heroes. Not with a fist. With a WRENCH.
