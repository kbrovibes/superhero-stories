There are some gifts that look wonderful from the outside but cost too much on the inside. This is a story about one of those gifts.

A man named Aldrich Killian had discovered something he called Extremis — a treatment that could rewrite the instructions inside a person's body. People who took it could heal from almost any wound. They could generate extraordinary heat from within. They were faster and stronger than any ordinary person. To Killian, this was progress. To Tony Stark, watching one of those people break through his best armor with bare hands, it was a problem that needed solving.

So Tony did what Tony always did. He thought about it for a long time. Then he injected a modified version of Extremis into his own body.

The results were astonishing. He could call his armor with a single thought — the pieces would fly across the room and wrap around him before he could even speak a word. He could see through satellites. He could feel the entire world's networks humming like strings on an instrument. It felt like becoming a new kind of being.

But something was going wrong. The more connected Tony became to his machines, the more he worried about everything. The more he worried, the more he tried to control. And the more he tried to control, the more he pushed his friends away. And do you know what happened next? He realized that Extremis was making him less like himself, not more.

He removed it. It was difficult, and it hurt, but he did it.

From that day on, Tony Stark understood that the measure of a good hero is not how powerful they can become — it is how wisely they choose to use the power they already have.
