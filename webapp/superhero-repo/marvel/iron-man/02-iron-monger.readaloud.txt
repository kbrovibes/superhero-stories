Psst. Lean in close. This story has a secret, and the secret is: the most dangerous villain isn't always the one who looks scary. Sometimes it's the one who smiles and pats you on the back.

Tony Stark called Obadiah Stane a friend. Stane was older, calm, and steady — the kind of man who seemed to have an answer for everything. He'd helped run Stark Industries for years and years, ever since Tony's father Howard Stark was gone. He seemed so trustworthy. And that was exactly the problem.

The moment Tony came home from Afghanistan and shut down the weapons division, Stane's smile went cold on the inside. Because Stane LIKED the weapons business. He liked it very much. So he did something sneaky and terrible: he sent people to the desert to collect the crushed wreckage of Tony's old Mark I armor. He handed the pieces to a team of engineers and said, "Build me something BIGGER."

And they did. They built the Iron Monger — a massive, looming suit of steel, taller than a truck, heavier than a tank, built to crush and conquer. CRASH. STOMP. CRASH.

But Stane wasn't done. He came to Tony's Malibu home and did something truly awful: he reached out and stole the Arc Reactor right out of Tony's chest — the glowing blue device keeping Tony alive. Tony crumpled to the floor, barely breathing.

Here's the thing about Tony Stark, though. He does not give up. He crawled — actually crawled — across that floor to where his older, backup Arc Reactor sat on a shelf. He snapped it into place. He bolted on the Mark III armor. And he flew straight for Stark Industries.

WHOOSH! CLANG! BOOM! The two suits collided over the city — bigger versus smarter, brute force versus brain power. Tony lured Stane onto the rooftop of the great Arc Reactor building and called out to Pepper Potts, the bravest person in the whole building. Pepper overloaded the reactor — KA-BOOOOM! — a tower of brilliant blue energy blazed up from below and put an end to the Iron Monger for good.

The next day, Tony walked up to a bank of cameras and microphones. Everyone expected him to deny everything. Instead, he looked straight into the cameras and said five words: "I am Iron Man."

The whole world stopped. Then the whole world cheered. And Tony Stark's new life — as a protector, not a weapons maker — began that very moment.

Did you know? In comics, Obadiah Stane was so obsessed with strategy that he modeled his whole life on chess. He even led a group of bad guys called the Chessmen. But Iron Man turned out to be a move he never saw coming.
