Tony Stark had always trusted Obadiah Stane. Stane was older, calm, and steady — the man who had helped run Stark Industries since Tony's father Howard Stark was gone. He called Tony brilliant. He seemed proud. But deep inside that calm exterior, something very different was growing.

When Tony came home from Afghanistan and closed down the weapons division of Stark Industries, Stane smiled politely. And then, quietly, he sent people to the desert to recover the wreckage of the Mark I armor. He had it reverse-engineered by a team of engineers into something enormous — the Iron Monger, a towering suit of steel far bigger and heavier than anything Tony had made.

Then Stane did something truly cold. He came to Tony's home in Malibu and stole the Arc Reactor right out of Tony's chest. The glowing blue device — the one keeping Tony alive — gone. Tony lay on the floor of his own house, barely breathing.

But Tony Stark does not give up. He crawled to the shelf where he kept his original, older reactor — less powerful, but enough. He snapped it into place. He put on the Mark III. He flew to Stark Industries.

Their battle was huge and thunderous, two suits of armor crashing through the city and onto the rooftops. Stane was bigger. But Tony was smarter. He lured Stane onto the roof of the great Arc Reactor facility and called to his friend Pepper Potts, his assistant and the bravest person in the building. She overloaded the reactor — and a tower of brilliant blue energy erupted and ended the fight.

From that day on, Tony stood before the cameras and said the words that changed everything: "I am Iron Man."
