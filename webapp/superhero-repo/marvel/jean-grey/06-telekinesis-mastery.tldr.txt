• Jean's telekinesis feels more natural than telepathy — immediate and instinctive, like an extension of her own hands
• Professor Xavier pushes her from small objects to heavy ones, requiring both speed and precision at the same time
• She trains constantly until catching falling objects becomes pure reflex, like a musician whose hands move without thinking
• She develops telekinetic shielding: an invisible barrier that deflects blasts, projectiles, and debris — confusing villains who expect contact and find only resistance
• During a mission in New Mexico, she holds a dome over the entire X-Men squad for eleven minutes while Storm works overhead
• She discovers telekinesis can work at microscopic scale — mending torn paper by pressing fibers back together without touching it
• She can disassemble a lock without a key, adjust tiny machine parts, and fix delicate objects with precise mental touch
• Jean never uses telekinesis carelessly; every use is a deliberate choice guided by the judgment behind the power
