• Every year Jean returns to the Xavier Institute to help new students in their first weeks of training
• She remembers being new: the noise, the fear that her gift was more burden than blessing
• Twelve-year-old Maya from Chicago has raw empathic powers; sitting in the crowded cafeteria makes her cry from absorbing thirty people's feelings
• Jean recognizes Maya immediately — the closest thing to her own beginning she has ever seen
• Jean meets Maya in Professor Xavier's garden and teaches her the same exercises she once learned: find one thing, hold it softly, step back
• Maya keeps a journal; Jean asks about it daily, knowing that being asked makes progress feel real
• Jean tells Maya: the goal is never to stop feeling — empathy is strength, and the noise is useful information
• By week eight Maya works independently; Jean watches and feels the circle complete — student becomes teacher
