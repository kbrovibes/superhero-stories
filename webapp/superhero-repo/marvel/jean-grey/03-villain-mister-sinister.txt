Mister Sinister was not like other villains. He wore a long black coat with a high collar, and his skin was pale as chalk, and in the center of his forehead gleamed a red diamond that seemed to catch every bit of light in a dark room. His real name was Nathaniel Essex, a scientist who had spent decades studying mutant genetics, convinced that within the X-Men lay the blueprint for the most powerful mutants who would ever live. He was calm, precise, and utterly certain he was right — which made him far more unsettling than anyone who simply shouted and threw things.

His great obsession was Scott Summers and Jean Grey. He had studied their genetic profiles from a distance for years, convinced that together they represented something extraordinary. He wanted samples, data, information — and he was willing to take it whether they agreed or not. He sent his operatives, a group of engineered soldiers called the Marauders, to intercept the X-Men during a mission in the tunnels beneath New York City known as the Morlock Tunnels.

Jean sensed the ambush before it came. Deep underground, in the dark and the narrow stone passages, she felt the sharp, purposeful minds of the Marauders converging from three directions at once. "They're coming," she told Scott, and the X-Men had just enough time to shift position before the Marauders arrived.

The fight was quick and fierce in the narrow tunnels. Jean held the center, using her telekinesis to seal off passage after passage, stacking stone and debris like doors slamming shut — BOOM, BOOM, BOOM — buying time for her teammates to regroup. When two Marauders broke through, she caught them in a telekinetic grip and held them still long enough for Storm to deal with both.

Mister Sinister himself appeared at the far end of the tunnel, composed and unhurried, as if watching an experiment conclude. He told Jean calmly that she was exceptional and that he meant her no real harm — only study. Jean fixed him with a steady look. She reached into his mind with her telepathy, not to hurt, but to send one clear message: leave, and do not return here.

Mister Sinister's expression shifted — just barely, just for a moment — before he turned and walked away into the dark. The Marauders followed. The X-Men emerged from the tunnels into the night air of New York, tired but whole.

Jean knew Sinister would return someday. He was too patient and too focused to give up. But she also knew that when he did, she would be ready — and she would not be alone.
