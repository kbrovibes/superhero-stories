Have you ever had a friend who just — gets it? Who understands you without you having to explain? Jean Grey had one of those. His name was Scott Summers, and he was the first person at the Xavier Institute who made her feel completely at home.

Scott had been there longer than Jean. He was serious and steady, the kind of person who thinks before he speaks and means every word he says. He always wore his ruby-quartz glasses — special lenses that contained the powerful optic beams he could fire from his eyes — and Jean never once thought of those glasses as strange. They were just Scott. Part of what made him him.

They trained together in the Danger Room, and WOW, what a team they made! Jean would sense danger before it arrived — WATCH OUT, Scott! — and Scott would be already moving before she finished the thought. Other X-Men sometimes watched them sparring and said it was like watching two people share the same brain. Hank McCoy joked that their teamwork was either telepathy or just knowing each other very, very well.

Jean always smiled at that joke. "A little of both," she said.

Here is the thing about Scott Summers that Jean loved most: he was never, ever strange about her telepathy. Some people got self-conscious around Jean. They watched their own thoughts the way you catch yourself watching your reflection in a shop window — suddenly very aware of what you look like. Not Scott. He just went on being himself, completely and naturally, as if her ability to read minds was as normal as being tall.

He was the most honest person she had ever met.

And here is the most important thing — something that shows exactly who Jean Grey is:

She could have known, at any time, how Scott felt about her. She could have reached into his mind and checked, the way you flip a page to see how a story ends. She never did. Some things belong to the person who is feeling them, and they deserve to tell their own story, in their own words, in their own time.

Scott told her on a quiet evening in the garden of the Xavier Institute, when the sky was turning the color of warm embers over the Hudson Valley. He spoke carefully, as he always did.

Jean had known for a long time. She listened to every word — out loud, the way he meant her to hear it — and then she told him exactly how she felt.

It was, as she always knew it would be, a very good conversation.

Some stories are even better when you wait for them!
