Some villains are loud and wild and wear very dramatic costumes. And then — there is Mister Sinister.

Mister Sinister does NOT shout. He does not stomp. He speaks in a quiet, careful voice, and that — honestly — is what makes him so creepy. His real name is Nathaniel Essex, and he wears a long black coat with a tall collar and his skin is pale as a winter moon, and right in the middle of his forehead is a glowing red diamond that catches every single speck of light in a dark room.

Mister Sinister's hobby? Mutant science. His favorite subject? Jean Grey and Scott Summers. He had been studying their mutant genetics from a distance for YEARS, and now he wanted something closer than a file — he wanted the real thing.

So he sent his team of engineered soldiers — the Marauders — down into the Morlock Tunnels beneath the streets of New York City, where the X-Men were on patrol.

He thought it was going to be easy.

He had not counted on Jean.

DEEP underground, in the narrow stone tunnels, Jean felt them coming. Her telepathy picked up three groups of sharp, determined minds moving fast, converging from all directions.

"They're coming," she told Scott. "Now."

The X-Men shifted into position — and CRASH, the Marauders arrived.

The tunnel was tight and dark, not a great place for a fight. But Jean was ready. BOOM — she slammed a telekinetic wall across one passage, cutting off half the Marauders. BOOM — another passage sealed with a heap of rubble. BOOM — a third blocked so fast the Marauders on the other side barely knew what happened.

Two got through. Jean caught them both in a telekinetic grip and held them perfectly still while Storm finished the job.

And then — at the far end of the longest tunnel — a pale figure appeared. Very calm. Very composed. Absolutely unbothered.

Mister Sinister.

"You are exceptional, Jean Grey," he said in his quiet, precise voice. "I mean you no harm. I only want to study you."

Jean looked at him steadily. Then she reached into his mind with her telepathy — not to hurt, just to speak, clearly, directly, unmistakably.

Leave. Do not come back here.

For one moment, Mister Sinister's calm cracked — just a little, just a flicker. Then he turned and walked away into the dark.

Jean let out a long breath.

He would come back someday. She knew that. But when he did — she would be ready. And she would still not be alone.

Watch out, Mister Sinister. You picked the wrong telepath!
