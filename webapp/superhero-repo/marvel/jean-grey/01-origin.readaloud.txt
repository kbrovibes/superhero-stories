Are you ready? Because this story starts with the biggest surprise a ten-year-old girl ever had — and ends with one of the greatest heroes the X-Men have ever known!

Her name is Jean Grey, and she grew up in a town called Annandale-on-Hudson, right beside a wide, beautiful river in New York. Jean was quiet and kind and a very good student. She had a best friend named Annie Richardson, and life was good — until the afternoon everything changed in an instant.

WHOOSH — a car. Annie. And then Jean felt it.

Not with her ears or her eyes — with her MIND. Jean felt every single thing Annie was feeling, as if someone had thrown open a door inside her brain that was never supposed to open. Happiness, fear, pain — all of it rushing in like a wave. Jean's telepathy had switched on, just like that, and it would not switch off.

Her parents were scared. Jean was more scared than anyone. But then — a knock at the door.

Professor Charles Xavier. Calm voice. Kind eyes. He sat beside Jean and said, very quietly: "Your mind is not broken. It is extraordinary."

He invited Jean to his Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters in Westchester. A school for kids with powers! Jean packed her bags and said YES.

At the Xavier Institute, she met Scott Summers — serious, steady, always wearing those red glasses — and big-brained Hank McCoy. They became her best friends in the whole world. Together they trained in the Danger Room, day after day, learning to use their gifts without hurting anyone.

Jean practiced reaching out with her mind CAREFULLY — like picking up one single feather instead of the whole pillow. FOCUS. BREATHE. REACH.

And then one morning she found something else. She looked at a pencil across the room — and it floated straight into her hand. ZOOM! Telekinesis! Moving things with just her thoughts!

Jean Grey had TWO mutant superpowers: a mind that could speak to any mind in the world, and hands that could move anything without touching it.

Professor Xavier gave her a uniform — gold and blue, clean and bright — and a name to go with it: Marvel Girl.

The very first time she stood with the X-Men, ready to go out and protect a world that was still learning not to be afraid of people like her, Jean Grey stood tall in her gold-and-blue uniform and smiled. This was exactly where she was supposed to be. This was what all the hard work had been for.

Watch out, world. Marvel Girl is here!
