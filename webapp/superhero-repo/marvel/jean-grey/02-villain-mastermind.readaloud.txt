Okay — imagine the sneakiest villain you have ever thought of. Got one? Good. Now make him sneakier. That is Mastermind!

His real name was Jason Wyngarde, and his power was creating illusions — fake things that looked one hundred percent real. He could make you see a door that wasn't there. He could make you hear a voice from an empty room. He could make you think you were somewhere you absolutely were NOT. And he decided to use that nasty trick on Jean Grey.

It started small. Jean would walk down a hallway at the Xavier Institute and the walls would look... wrong. A little longer than usual. A little darker. She shook her head. She told herself she was tired. But Mastermind was patient, and every day the illusions got bigger.

BLINK — a strange shadow in the corner.
BLINK — a door she'd never seen before.
BLINK — her own reflection staring back from nowhere.

Professor Xavier noticed immediately. "Jean's mind is being pulled," he said, and Scott Summers was already standing up before the Professor finished the sentence.

The X-Men tracked Mastermind to Manhattan, where he had built his biggest, cleverest illusion yet: a maze of mirrors around Jean in the middle of the city! She could hear Scott calling her name — but every direction she turned, she saw only herself reflected back. Mastermind laughed from somewhere in the fake shadows.

"Let's see your pretty telepathy help you now!" he said.

Jean took a deep breath. She closed her eyes.

She couldn't see through the lie with her eyes — so she didn't try. Instead, she reached out with her mind, feeling for real things. There was Scott — warm and brave and steady as a rock. There was Mastermind — his concentration wound up tight like a coiled spring, holding all those fake mirrors in place.

Jean reached for that spring and — SNAP — she pushed.

CRACK! The illusions shattered all at once like a thousand broken mirrors hitting the floor.

Mastermind stumbled backward, eyes wide, jaw open. He had never once met a person who could reach right through his illusions and pull them apart from the inside. He turned and RAN.

Jean stood in the sunny streets of midtown Manhattan, Scott right beside her, and she took one long, quiet breath.

She hadn't seen through the trick. She'd done something better — she'd reached past it to find what was real.

Not bad for a day's work, Marvel Girl!
