Okay — two superpowers. Jean Grey has TWO. Telepathy we covered. Now let's talk about the other one — the one that makes people stare with their mouths wide open.

Telekinesis!

Moving things with your mind.

Jean didn't even fully realize she had this power at first. She was reaching for a pencil one afternoon at the Xavier Institute — her arm stretched out, fingers spread — and the pencil ROSE OFF THE TABLE and floated right into her hand before she got there. She just blinked at it for a moment. Then she laughed.

Professor Xavier started her small. A book. A cup. A training disc. Then bigger. A chair. A heavy training beam. Then — watch this — a car. WHOOOMP. Up it went, steady and smooth.

But it is not just about lifting heavy things! The really cool part is the precision.

Jean practiced and practiced until catching a falling object became pure REFLEX. A teammate slips? Jean's already got them. A ceiling panel cracks loose? Jean's already holding it. She didn't have to think about it any more than you think about catching something someone tosses to you.

She invented something she called telekinetic shielding. By wrapping telekinetic energy around herself or a teammate, she could create an invisible barrier — a force field — that DEFLECTED blasts and projectiles and debris. Villains would fire something at her and it would just — stop. No impact. No contact. Just resistance, like running into a wall that isn't there.

Confusing? Oh, very.

One mission in New Mexico was really something. The whole X-Men squad was pinned down and Jean threw a telekinetic dome over all of them — a bubble of force protecting everyone at once — and held it for ELEVEN whole minutes while Storm worked to clear a dangerous weather situation above them. Hank McCoy calculated afterward that she had been stopping hundreds of kilograms of incoming debris the whole entire time.

And get this — Jean's telekinesis works at TINY scales too. She once mended a torn page in one of Professor Xavier's very old books by pressing the individual paper fibers back together — without touching the page at all. She can pick a lock with her mind. She can fix tiny machine parts.

Jean never, ever uses her telekinesis carelessly. Every time she lifts or stops or shields, she pauses — just for a breath — and asks herself: is this right? Is this what the moment needs?

That's not fear. That's wisdom.

And wisdom, plus telekinesis? Basically unstoppable!
