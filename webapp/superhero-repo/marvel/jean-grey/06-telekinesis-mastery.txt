Jean's telekinesis — the ability to move objects using only her mind — had always felt more natural to her than telepathy. Where telepathy required constant vigilance and deliberate restraint, telekinesis felt like a physical extension of herself, as immediate and instinctive as reaching out her hand. But instinct and mastery are different things, and Jean worked for years to understand the full range of what she could do.

In the beginning she could move small objects cleanly: a book, a cup, a training disc. Professor Xavier pushed her further, asking her to move heavier objects with more precision. A car required concentration but not great effort. A collapsed section of the Danger Room's ceiling, caught mid-fall and lowered gently to the ground, required both speed and control — two qualities that are harder to hold simultaneously than they seem. Jean learned to hold them. She practiced catching falling things so often that it became reflex, the way a trained musician's hands move without the musician having to think.

Her most important development was what she called telekinetic shielding. By wrapping telekinetic energy tightly around herself or around a teammate, Jean could deflect incoming attacks — blasts, projectiles, debris — the way a shield deflects a blow. The barrier was invisible, which made it confusing for opponents who expected contact and found only resistance. During one mission in New Mexico, she held a telekinetic dome over the entire X-Men squad for eleven minutes while Storm worked to clear a dangerous storm system overhead. Hank McCoy estimated afterward that she had been deflecting several hundred kilograms of debris the whole time.

She also discovered that telekinesis could be applied with surgical precision at the microscopic level — a discovery she made almost by accident when she mended a torn page in one of Professor Xavier's oldest books by pressing the fibers back together without touching the paper. She could adjust a machine's smallest moving parts. She could disassemble a lock without a key. This fine-detail control, combined with her telepathy, made her one of the most versatile problem-solvers on any team she joined.

Jean never used her telekinesis carelessly. She understood that great power is only as good as the judgment behind it. She had learned that from Professor Xavier, had seen it proved in her own life a hundred times. Every time she lifted something, stopped something, or shielded someone, she thought: is this the right use right now? Not out of fear, but out of the kind of respect that comes from knowing exactly how much your hands can hold.
