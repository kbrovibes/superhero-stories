Here's a question for you. Can you feel what the person next to you is thinking right now? No? Not even a little? That's completely normal.

Jean Grey could feel every mind on her entire city block. At once. Without meaning to.

THAT is why training at the Xavier Institute was so important.

Professor Xavier had a special room for this — his private study, shielded by a device called Cerebro's auxiliary dampener, which blocked outside noise from getting in. He and Jean would sit quietly in that room and he would say, very gently: "Just one, Jean. Find one mind and hold it."

One. Not the whole block. One.

Jean reached out like you reach for something in the dark. Carefully. A bird on the windowsill. A student reading on the second floor. Just one thread. Just one.

FOCUS. HOLD. BREATHE.

She kept a training journal. Early pages: "Too much again. The whole cafeteria came in at once!" But further along? "Held one. Two. THREE different people for six whole minutes!"

Day by day. Practice by practice. Getting better.

But the hardest lesson Professor Xavier ever taught her had nothing to do with numbers. He called it empathy without losing yourself. It means: you can feel what someone else feels — their sadness, their fear, their excitement — and stay completely clear about who YOU are. You don't become them. You understand them.

That sounds simple. It is actually one of the hardest things in the world.

But Jean learned it.

By the time she was a seasoned X-Man, her telepathy could do incredible things. She could scan a whole building and sense which minds inside were thinking dangerous thoughts. She could reach across the city and speak directly into a teammate's mind — no phone needed! She could wrap her own thoughts in a shield so powerful that not even another telepath could peek inside.

And sometimes — in a moment when people were scared and the world felt very big and loud — Jean could send a quiet, warm feeling into a crowd. Not a command. Just a gentle presence, like someone lighting one small candle in a very dark room.

She believed with her whole heart that telepathy, used with care, was one of the kindest gifts a person could ever have. Not just because it helped in battles — but because truly understanding someone else's feelings? That made her the best teammate, and the most loyal friend, the X-Men had ever known.

Now THAT is a superpower worth training for!
