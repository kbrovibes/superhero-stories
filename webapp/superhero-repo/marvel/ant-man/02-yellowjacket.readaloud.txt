Not everyone who learns about amazing technology uses it wisely. Darren Cross had once been the student of Dr. Hank Pym, but Pym had seen something dark in him — a hunger for power that made the shrinking technology too dangerous to share. Cross never forgot that. He spent years working in secret, and at last he built his own shrinking suit. He called it the Yellowjacket.

The Yellowjacket was nothing like the Ant-Man suit. It had plasma cannons, mechanical stingers, and powerful wings built for war. But the formula Cross used to shrink himself was unstable, and little by little it twisted his mind, filling him with anger and fear until he was hardly himself at all.

And do you know what he did? He went after the one person Scott Lang loved most — his daughter, Cassie.

Scott fought Cross through places that had become something else entirely at their tiny scale. A bedroom floor stretched into a canyon. A toy train set became a roaring, crashing locomotive. At one point, their entire battle raged inside a briefcase! Scott knew he could not simply outpunch the Yellowjacket. He had to be smarter, steadier, braver. He dove deeper into the microverse than he had ever gone, risking his own existence to protect Cassie.

In the end, it was Cross's own unstable power that defeated him. The very technology he had stolen and twisted turned against him, and the Yellowjacket fell. And that is how Scott Lang learned that wearing a powerful suit is not enough. The person inside the suit — their heart, their choices, their love — is what truly matters.
