Picture two tiny fighters, smaller than a grain of rice, battling through a world that most people never get to see. Welcome to the most claustrophobic, most incredible showdown you have ever heard about — the fight between Ant-Man and the Yellowjacket!

Darren Cross was once the prized student of Dr. Hank Pym. He was brilliant, no doubt about that. But Pym noticed something unsettling in Cross — a hunger for power that had nothing to do with helping people. So Pym turned him away. And Cross never, ever forgot it.

For years, Cross worked in secret inside his own company, Darren Cross Enterprises, obsessed with one goal: building a shrinking suit even more powerful than the Ant-Man suit. And he did it. He called it the Yellowjacket. BUZZ! Out popped mechanical stingers. ZAP! Plasma cannons crackled to life. The suit had wings built for war and weapons built to destroy.

But here is something Cross did not count on. The formula he used to shrink himself was unstable — and bit by bit, it warped his mind, twisting his thoughts, stoking his anger until he was barely himself at all. In the Marvel comics, the original Yellowjacket identity was actually invented by Hank Pym himself during a mental breakdown — so there is something almost poetic about the name attracting unstable minds.

And do you know what Cross did that was truly terrible? He went after Cassie Lang — Scott's little daughter. That was his biggest mistake.

CRASH! The battle began in the strangest arenas imaginable. A bedroom carpet stretched out like a vast purple desert. A toy train set thundered around them like a real locomotive, sparks flying, carriages smashing. POP! A battle inside a briefcase, walls pressing in on every side. Scott had to outTHINK Cross, not just outfight him, because the Yellowjacket's weapons were bigger and flashier than anything Ant-Man carried.

Scott went deeper into the microverse than he had ever dared — smaller and smaller, past what any normal person would risk — all to protect Cassie.

In the end, it was Cross's own unstable technology that brought him down. The Yellowjacket suit tore itself apart from the inside, unable to handle the power of a mind consumed by anger and pride. And Scott landed back in Cassie's room, breathing hard, and smiled at his daughter.

The lesson? A suit full of weapons means nothing if the person wearing it has lost their way.
