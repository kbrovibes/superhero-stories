Quick question for you — and think carefully before you answer. If you could have any army in the world, what would you pick? Knights in shining armor? Robot warriors? A fleet of spaceships?

Scott Lang chose ants. And I promise you, by the end of this story, you will completely understand why.

Ants are, without any exaggeration, some of the most extraordinary creatures on planet Earth. A single ant can lift and carry an object fifty times its own weight. FIFTY! If a human could do that, they could lift a small car over their head. Ants build cities, farm fungus underground, care for their young, communicate through chemical signals called pheromones, and coordinate thousands of individuals without a single spoken word. They have been on Earth for over one hundred million years — they watched the dinosaurs come and go and kept right on building their hills.

Scott's cybernetic helmet taps directly into this ancient communication system. By sending pheromone signals and electromagnetic pulses, Scott can share goals and intentions with his ant companions — and then he steps back and lets them find the best way to achieve those goals themselves. He does not micromanage. He trusts them. And that trust? It is the reason his ant army is so effective.

Here is the team. Carpenter ants — the big ones, the ones with wings — serve as Scott's aerial cavalry. BUZZ! Ant-thony, the very first carpenter ant Scott bonded with, was his most loyal steed, carrying him through the air on missions that would have been impossible any other way. Fire ants are the builders and soldiers. In the real world, fire ants can link their bodies together to form living rafts that float on water — and in Scott's world, they build living bridges across impossible gaps and swarm enemies in coordinated waves. Crazy ants are the saboteurs. Real crazy ants actually seek out electrical equipment and can disrupt it with their own bodies — they are naturally drawn to electronic signals. In Scott's missions, crazy ants have short-circuited security panels, disabled alarms, and knocked out surveillance systems without making a sound.

Scott named his closest companions. Ant-thony. Ant-tonio. Ulysses. He mourned every one he lost. Because to Scott, these were not tools. They were teammates. They were friends.

And do you know the most magnificent thing these tiny friends ever did? In the final battle against Thanos, when the sky was filled with alien ships and the ground shook with the thunder of an impossible war, the ant army was there. Thousands upon thousands of six-legged heroes, surging across the battlefield alongside Thor and Captain America and Iron Man.

Nobody noticed them right away. But they made a difference. They always make a difference.

That is the truth about Scott Lang's army: heroism doesn't have a size requirement. It just needs a big heart — and good friends at your side. Even if those friends have six legs, no voice, and a wingspan the size of a postage stamp.
