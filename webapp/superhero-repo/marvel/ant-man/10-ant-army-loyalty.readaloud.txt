To most people, an ant is something you find at a picnic. To Scott Lang, ants are the most loyal, most disciplined, most extraordinary teammates in the world.

Using his cybernetic helmet, Scott can send messages through pheromones and electromagnetic signals — a special language he shares only with his six-legged friends. And the ants listen. Not because they have to, but because Scott treats them with real respect. He does not just bark orders. He shares the goal, and then trusts the ants to find the very best way to reach it.

His army has many kinds of heroes. Carpenter ants spread their wings and carry Scott through the sky like the finest aerial steed in any hero's stable. Fire ants link their bodies together to build living bridges across impossible gaps. Crazy ants short-circuit alarms and fry electronics without a single visible move. Scott has named his closest companions — Ant-thony, Ant-tonio, Ulysses — and he mourns every one he loses in battle.

And do you know what thousands of ants together can do? They can swarm a security system and shut it down before a single guard notices. They can build a living shield around Scott in the heat of a fight. They can make the ordinary world — a kitchen floor, a briefcase, a ventilation shaft — into a perfect battlefield.

In the great final war against Thanos, the ant army fought too. Thousands of tiny heroes, side by side with the mightiest Avengers on Earth.

And that is how Scott Lang proved it: heroism does not have a size requirement. All you need is a large heart — and good friends at your side.
