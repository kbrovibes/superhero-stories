Most people know that Ant-Man can shrink. But here is a secret not everyone has seen: he can also grow.

Inside the Ant-Man suit is what scientists call the Giant-Man protocol. When Scott Lang activates it, the Pym Particles work in reverse — and in a few enormous heartbeats, a man who could fit in your palm becomes sixty feet tall. Buildings shrink to toy blocks. Cars become pebbles. Fighter jets buzz around his head like annoying flies.

The first time Scott tried this was at an airport in Germany, in the middle of a battle between two groups of Avengers. Nobody was expecting it. One moment, a small figure was running across the tarmac. The next moment — thundering footsteps. Everyone stopped to stare. And in that moment of surprise, Scott's friends had exactly the window they needed to escape.

But do you know what Giant-Man feels like from the inside? It is not easy. Every step shakes the ground. Every movement takes enormous effort. Scott's heart has to work harder than it ever has, and if he stays too large for too long, the strain is almost too much to bear. He calls the Giant-Man form a last resort — a "break the glass in case of emergency" power. He only uses it when nothing else will do.

In the final great battle against Thanos and his army, Giant-Man stood alongside the Avengers and crushed enormous alien leviathans beneath his boots.

From that day on, the world knew: Scott Lang had no fixed size. He was exactly as big as the moment required him to be.
