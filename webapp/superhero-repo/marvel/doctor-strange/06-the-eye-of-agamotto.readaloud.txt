Around Doctor Strange's neck hangs a small golden amulet — round and ancient, carved with symbols older than any written language. It is called the Eye of Agamotto, and it was made by the very first Sorcerer Supreme, a great master named Agamotto himself.

The Eye is not just a piece of jewelry. It is alive, in its own quiet way. It chooses its master. You cannot take it. You cannot inherit it. It decides. And it chose Strange because he had the right mixture of curiosity, courage, and hard-won humility.

Inside the Eye sleeps the Time Stone — one of six Infinity Stones that govern the deepest powers in existence. With it, Strange can do something astonishing: he can touch time itself. He can look forward and see the paths the future might take. He can reach back and undo a terrible moment that has already happened. And when an enemy is too powerful to face directly, he can trap them in a loop, spinning the same moment over and over, until they agree to stop.

But the Eye does more than bend time. It shines a light that nothing can hide from. Creatures wearing disguises, sorcerers concealing dark intentions, invisible threats drifting through the air — the Eye of Agamotto sees them all, stripping away every illusion and shadow. When Strange opens the Eye fully, a warm golden radiance pours out, and malevolent things recoil from it like a flame from the wind.

Strange wears it every day, feeling its gentle pulse against his chest. It reminds him of every sorcerer who came before him, all the way back to Agamotto.

And that is how one small glowing amulet became the most important piece of armor in the universe.
