Okay, I need you to imagine the most magical house in all of New York City. From the outside, it just looks like a tall old townhouse at 177A Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village. People walk right past it every single day. They don't notice it. But if you looked really closely at the big round window near the top — the one that glows with a faint golden shimmer — you would know something extraordinary is happening inside.

That, my friend, is the Sanctum Sanctorum!

WHOOSH! Step through the front door and the ordinary world disappears. Staircases curl up toward ceilings that don't exist. Rooms appear that couldn't possibly fit inside the building. Books float off shelves all on their own. Some hallways become labyrinths that twist and turn to confuse any sneaky intruder who wanders in without an invitation. The whole building seems to be alive — and honestly? It kind of is!

Doctor Strange keeps the most extraordinary collection of artifacts here. One whole chamber is the Chamber of Relics, where magical objects from across the multiverse rest behind protective glass. And the most incredible relic of all? It's not even on a shelf.

Are you ready for this? The Cloak of Levitation is a magnificent deep red cape — and it has its own personality! It isn't just something Strange wears. It CHOSE Strange, swooping right off its hook the very first time they met and wrapping around his shoulders like a big proud hug. It can fly, whisking Strange up above the rooftops whenever he needs to soar. It wraps around enemies and holds them tight — FWOOM! — like a very determined hug they did NOT ask for. And if Strange ever gets knocked out, the Cloak acts all on its own to keep him safe.

Standing beside Strange in the Sanctum is his steadfast partner Wong. Wong is a Master of the Mystic Arts who helped found the Sanctum's library and knows every dangerous tome on the shelves. Together, Strange and Wong repair the magical wards, guard the artifacts, and make sure nothing sinister slips through from another dimension.

And do you know what the big round window at the top is for? That is the Seal of Vishanti — a great glowing symbol that projects a protective aura over the whole building, keeping it hidden from magical detection. The Sanctum is a fortress and a library and a home, all at once.

What a house! What a cloak! What a wonderful, world-saving pair of guardians.
