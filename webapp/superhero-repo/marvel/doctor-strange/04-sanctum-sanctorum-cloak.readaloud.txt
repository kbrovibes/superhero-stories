On a quiet street in Greenwich Village in New York City, there is a townhouse that most people walk right past. It looks unusual — the windows are shaped differently, and there is something about the air around it that makes people walk a little faster. But if you looked very carefully at the large circular window near the top, you might notice that it glows, just faintly, with a warm golden light.

That is the Sanctum Sanctorum, and it is anything but ordinary. Inside, staircases lead to rooms that could not possibly fit in the building. Books float off shelves on their own. Artifacts from a thousand different worlds rest behind glass, humming with old and powerful energy. The whole building seems alive, its rooms rearranging themselves to welcome a friend — or confuse an intruder.

The most remarkable resident is not Doctor Strange. It is his cloak.

The Cloak of Levitation is a deep crimson cape, and it has a personality all its own. It chose Strange — swooped down and wrapped around his shoulders the first time they met, as if it had been waiting for him. It can fly, pulling Strange up into the air whenever he needs to go higher. It wraps around enemies and holds them still. And if Strange is ever knocked out, the Cloak will act on its own to protect him.

Wong, Strange's steadfast companion and fellow guardian, keeps the Sanctum running. Together, they maintain the wards, repair the protective enchantments, and make sure that nothing dangerous from the other side of reality sneaks through.

From that day on, the Sanctum Sanctorum stood as New York City's secret shield — a warm light in a dark window, always watching.
