The world of Doctor Strange is filled with amazing and mysterious magical objects, but none are as legendary as the Eye of Agamotto. This ancient golden amulet, worn around the Sorcerer’s neck, houses the incredibly powerful Time Stone. With it, Strange can see into the distant future, rewind time to fix dangerous mistakes, or freeze a specific moment in place. It requires great wisdom and skill to use, as messing with the flow of time is very dangerous, but it is the ultimate tool for protecting our reality from collapse.

Then there is the Cloak of Levitation, which is much more than just a piece of red clothing. This magical cape has a personality all its own! It is incredibly loyal, brave, and sometimes even a little bit sassy or playful. It can fly on its own, wrap itself around enemies to trip them up during a fight, or even gently wipe away Strange’s tears if he’s feeling overwhelmed. The Cloak chose Stephen as its owner because of his hero's heart, and the two have been inseparable companions ever since his training.

The Sanctum Sanctorum is the magical headquarters for all these adventures. Located at 177A Bleecker Street in New York City, the house looks perfectly normal on the outside but is much bigger and more complex on the inside. It is filled with moving staircases, hidden doors that lead to different planets, and vast libraries containing every magic book ever written in history. The large, beautiful circular window at the top, called the Seal of Vishanti, protects the building from being seen or entered by dark forces.

Together, the Eye, the Cloak, and the Sanctum form the foundation of the Sorcerer Supreme’s immense power. The librarian Wong helps manage the Sanctum’s vast collection of ancient relics, ensuring that dangerous items don't fall into the wrong hands. For a seven-year-old fan, imagining a house that can take you anywhere in the universe and a cape that can think for itself is the most magical part of Doctor Strange’s incredible world.
