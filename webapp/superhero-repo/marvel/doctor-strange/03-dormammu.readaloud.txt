Beyond the edges of our world, past every star and every sky, there is a place called the Dark Dimension. It is ruled by a being unlike anything in our universe — a vast, hungry presence named Dormammu. He is not made of flesh or bone. He is pure power, pure darkness, and he has one endless desire: to swallow every world and every reality until nothing exists but him.

Strange faced him, and he quickly learned that throwing spells at Dormammu was like throwing pebbles at the sea. No magic was big enough. No fight could be won that way. So Strange did something truly unexpected. He used the Eye of Agamotto — the golden pendant that holds the Time Stone, one of the most powerful objects in existence — to pull time into a loop.

Dormammu killed him. Time snapped back. Dormammu killed him again. And again. And again. Each time, Strange returned, saying the same words: "Dormammu — I've come to bargain."

Do you know what took true courage? Not the fighting. It was the enduring. Strange let himself lose, over and over, because he knew that as long as he kept returning, Dormammu was stuck. He could not move on to the next dimension. He could not eat the next world. He was trapped in a loop with one very stubborn doctor.

Finally, Dormammu agreed: leave Earth alone, take his servants away, and never return.

From that day on, Strange guards the thin walls between our world and the Dark Dimension with quiet, steady vigilance — knowing that the dread lord is always out there, waiting and watching, held back by nothing more than one man's refusal to give up.
