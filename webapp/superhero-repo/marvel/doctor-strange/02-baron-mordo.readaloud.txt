Have you ever had a friend who got jealous — so sure they deserved something that they couldn't be happy when someone else got it instead? Well, Doctor Strange has a story just like that, and the friend's name is Baron Karl Mordo!

Karl Mordo was already at Kamar-Taj when Stephen Strange arrived, shuffling in all cold and bedraggled from the Himalayan snow. Mordo had studied the Mystic Arts for years and years. He could conjure spells with razor-sharp precision. He was powerful, disciplined, and absolutely certain that one day the Ancient One would name HIM the next Sorcerer Supreme. ZAP! He seemed like the perfect student.

And then along came Strange. The new student with the surgeon's mind zoomed through lessons that took others a decade. The Ancient One's eyes lit up whenever Strange was nearby. And Karl Mordo felt something cold creep into his heart — resentment.

Now, Mordo had a special weapon: the Staff of the Living Tribunal, a gleaming staff humming with ancient power. He wielded it with the elegance of someone who has practiced ten thousand hours. He was brilliant — truly! But here is a fun fact worth knowing: in the very first Marvel comic he ever appeared in — Strange Tales #111, back in 1963 — Mordo was already trying to trick Strange and the Ancient One. He has been letting his jealousy get the better of him for a very, very long time!

And do you know what Mordo decided? He invented his OWN rules: that he would hunt every sorcerer in the world he thought was using magic the wrong way. CRACK! He called it protecting the multiverse. But really, his heart had gone all cold and brittle, like ice that snaps when you step on it.

Strange was heartbroken. He had looked up to Mordo as a mentor and almost a friend. Losing him was like watching a beautiful book get torn page by page.

But here is what Doctor Strange learned from Baron Mordo: being gifted is wonderful, but being humble is more powerful than any staff or any spell. The greatest magic isn't what you wield — it's what you choose to do with your heart.
