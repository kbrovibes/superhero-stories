Long before Stephen Strange ever came to Kamar-Taj — longer ago than any book in the library could say for certain — there was the very first Sorcerer Supreme. His name was Agamotto.

Agamotto was extraordinary, even by the standards of extraordinary people. He studied the Mystic Arts with a dedication that turned decades into centuries, and he understood reality at a level most minds could not approach. And at the height of his power, he did something remarkable: he created a vessel. A talisman. A container for one of the most powerful objects in the universe.

The Time Stone.

The Time Stone is one of six Infinity Stones — six ancient, primordial jewels, each one governing a fundamental force of existence. The Space Stone controls where things are. The Mind Stone controls thought. The Reality Stone controls what is real. The Power Stone is raw, devastating force. The Soul Stone governs something deeper and more mysterious than any of the others. And the Time Stone?

The Time Stone controls when.

Agamotto placed the Time Stone inside a golden amulet, sealed it with layers of protective sorcery, and named it the Eye of Agamotto after himself. Because the Eye would know its own master — it was sentient in the way that truly ancient, truly powerful objects sometimes are. It would choose who wielded it, and it could not be used by someone whose spirit was not prepared for the responsibility.

Strange wears it at his throat, its golden chains curved against the Cloak of Levitation's crimson fabric. He doesn't use it casually. Using the Time Stone — rewinding moments, peering into possible futures, locking a specific moment in an inescapable loop — takes concentration and intention and the absolute certainty that you are not making the universe's situation worse in ways you cannot currently predict. Strange sometimes sits with the Eye for an hour before deciding whether to use it. Wong, watching from the library doorway, considers this appropriate caution.

The Eye does more than bend time. It projects what the texts of Kamar-Taj call the "All-Revealing Light of the Vishanti" — a beam of pure, clarifying illumination that strips away disguises and deceptions the way bright sunlight burns through fog. Extra-dimensional parasites hiding in human hosts flicker into visibility. Hidden motives become clear. Dark magic that has been weaving itself into the fabric of a place for years is suddenly laid bare, its threads visible and traceable. Beings that feed on darkness flee from this light because they cannot hide within it.

Can you picture Strange holding the Eye aloft in a dark room — its golden light spreading outward in all directions, revealing the shapes of things that were lurking unseen in the corners? And the corners suddenly empty as whatever was hiding there decides to be elsewhere?

It is also a symbol. Every Sorcerer Supreme who has carried the office since Agamotto has worn or protected the Eye — an unbroken chain stretching back to the beginning. When Strange wears it, he carries that lineage. He is the most recent link in a very long tradition of guardians who chose this work, who studied these arts, who stood between the world and the chaos beyond its edges.

He is responsible for what it contains. For what it reveals. For using it wisely, which means often choosing not to use it at all. The temptation to simply rewind every mistake, to peer around every corner of the future before walking forward, to fix everything — this temptation is real. Strange knows it. Every Sorcerer Supreme has known it.

The answer to that temptation is restraint. Clarity. The understanding that in a universe of infinite possibilities, the greatest gift is not being able to control everything — but being able to see clearly, act truthfully, and trust in the present moment even when you can glimpse the future.

The Eye of Agamotto pulses gently against Strange's chest. Patient. Waiting. Choosing, as it always has, the right moment.

He has learned to trust it.
