The Dark Dimension is a frightening place — all churning colors and impossible shapes, ruled by the hunger of Dormammu. But deep within it, there was someone who did not belong to that darkness. Her name was Clea, and she was Dormammu's own niece. Her father was Prince Orini, and she had grown up surrounded by power and chaos and a cruelty she never wanted any part of.

When Strange first ventured into the Dark Dimension, Clea helped him. She was risking everything — her safety, her place in that world, the anger of her terrible uncle — because she believed in what Strange was trying to do. That kind of courage is rare in any dimension.

Strange did not forget it. He taught her the Mystic Arts, and she proved to be a gifted student. Her understanding of the Dark Dimension was something Strange could never have learned from any book. She showed him that even in the most frightening realms, there were people who wanted something better. Strange gave her something in return: a clear and steady sense of why power should be used for protection, not domination.

Their friendship grew into something deeper. It was not an easy love — they lived in different worlds, with different responsibilities pulling them apart. There were long separations. There were misunderstandings. But the trust between them endured.

In time, Clea took her uncle's place as ruler of the Dark Dimension, working to bring order and kindness where there had only been cruelty.

From that day on, the two sorcerers kept watch over their separate worlds and supported each other across the void — proof that even the universe's darkest corners can hold a light.
