One quiet evening in the city, a series of bright green sparks lit up the sky. The mischievous Green Goblin was back, but this time he wasn't looking for a fight; he was looking for a laugh. He zipped through the streets on his high-tech glider, dropping small, harmless pumpkin-shaped balloons that released colorful glitter and silly string. While it seemed like a harmless prank, the sticky mess was causing traffic jams and confusing the local citizens. Natasha Romanoff, the Black Widow, was immediately dispatched to handle the situation with her usual tactical precision.

Natasha knew that chasing the Goblin in a straight line would be useless against his agile glider. Instead, she used her gymnastics expertise to scale the side of a skyscraper, positioning herself at a perfect vantage point. She monitored his flight pattern, noticing that he always looped back to the city square to admire his handiwork. With a calm and serious expression, she prepared her Widow’s Bite wrist-gauntlets. She didn't want to hurt him, just to stop the chaotic pranks that were disrupting the city's peace.

As the Goblin zoomed past, Natasha launched a series of small, adhesive tracking discs. These weren't weapons, but clever gadgets designed to gently disrupt the glider's steering. The Goblin let out a surprised yelp as his glider began to drift toward a large, soft safety net she had pre-positioned between two buildings. With a graceful leap, Natasha descended from the rooftop, landing softly on the ground just as the Goblin’s glider came to a controlled stop inside the net.

The Goblin looked around, covered in his own silly string, and realized he had been outsmarted. Natasha stood before him, her black suit shimmering under the streetlights. "The city isn't your playground," she said firmly but kindly. She helped the prankster out of the net, ensuring he was safe before handing him over to the local authorities for a stern talking-to. Her quick thinking and non-violent approach had saved the day, proving that a master spy always knows the best way to end a joke.
