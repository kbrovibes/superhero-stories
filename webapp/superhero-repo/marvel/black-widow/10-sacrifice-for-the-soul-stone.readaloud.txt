Take a deep breath, little ones. This is the bravest story of all. It is the story of Natasha Romanoff's greatest moment — and it happened on a planet at the very edge of the universe, under a cold and starless sky. Are you ready? Here we go.

When the terrible villain Thanos snapped his fingers and made half of all living things disappear, the Avengers refused to give up. They hatched an incredible plan: travel through time, collect six powerful objects called the Infinity Stones, and bring everyone back. Six stones. Six teams. Six missions happening at the same time across the whole universe!

Natasha Romanoff and her dearest friend in all the world — Clint Barton, Hawkeye, the man from Budapest who had once offered her a second chance — were sent together to a desolate planet called Vormir. The winds there were cold. The rocks were grey. And a mysterious figure called the Red Skull waited for them with a terrible piece of news.

To claim the Soul Stone, the universe demanded a sacrifice. Not a thing, not a treasure. A soul freely given. Someone you truly, deeply love.

Natasha and Clint looked at each other — and both said the exact same thing at the exact same moment. "It should be me."

And do you know what happened next? They ARGUED. Not about who should fight — but about who should sacrifice themselves so the other could live! Clint ran for the cliff. WHOOSH — Natasha grabbed him back! She scrambled, she leapt, she would not let him be the one. Because for Natasha, this was something she had wanted her whole life without ever quite having it: a choice. A real, free, completely her own choice. Not one made for her by the Red Room. Not one made by an order from S.H.I.E.L.D. This one was hers.

She made it. Freely. Bravely. With a kind of peace that surprised even her.

Clint returned to Earth with the Soul Stone, and the Avengers used it to bring back everyone Thanos had taken. Half the universe, breathing and laughing and alive again. Natasha Romanoff made that possible.

She had spent her whole life trying to clear the red in her ledger. In the end, she filled the whole ledger with gold.
