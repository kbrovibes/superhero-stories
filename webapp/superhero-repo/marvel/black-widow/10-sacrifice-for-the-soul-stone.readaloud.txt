There is a lonely planet called Vormir, swept by cold winds at the edge of the universe, where a very old and terrible price must be paid. To claim the Soul Stone — one of the six most powerful objects in all of existence — the universe demands a sacrifice. Not of something small. Of someone truly beloved.

Natasha Romanoff and Clint Barton travelled to Vormir together. They had been friends since Budapest, since the day Clint had looked at her and offered her a second chance when no one else would. They knew, when they arrived, what the Soul Stone would require. And they both immediately said the same thing.

"It should be me."

They argued at the edge of that vast, silent cliff. They fought — not against enemies, but against each other's love. Each one trying to be the one to fall so the other would not have to. Clint was strong. But Natasha was stronger, and her resolve was unbreakable. She had spent years trying to balance all the harm done before she became a hero. This was the moment. This was the choice that was entirely, completely hers.

She made it freely. With courage, and with love, and with a kind of peace that surprised even her.

Clint returned with the Soul Stone, and the Avengers used it to bring back everyone Thanos had taken. Half the universe, restored. Natasha Romanoff had given everything for that.

And that is how a girl who was once shaped into a weapon — who never had a choice about anything — made the most powerful, the most generous, the most freely chosen decision of her entire life.
