All right, let me ask you something. What if you could walk into a room and everyone inside thought you were a completely different person? Different face, different voice, different name — but secretly, on the inside, still completely, totally YOU? That is exactly what Natasha Romanoff can do. And it is one of the most brilliant spy tricks you will ever hear about!

Her secret tool is called the Photostatic Veil — and oh, it is a remarkable piece of S.H.I.E.L.D. technology. It is a super-thin layer of programmable, microscopic cells that fits over Natasha's face like a second skin. When she turns it on, it projects a completely different face — different colours, different features, different contours — AND it even changes her voice to match! She can walk right into a meeting of dangerous criminals, disguised as their own trusted colleague, and not a single one of them will know who she really is. First used in a daring mission against the World Security Council, the Veil has fooled some of the most suspicious, careful people on the planet. Can you imagine having that kind of disguise in your back pocket?

But the Photostatic Veil is only one piece of what makes Natasha the greatest infiltration expert on Earth. She is also an absolutely extraordinary hacker. She can sit down in front of an encrypted computer — one with a password so complicated it would take most computers months to crack — and unlock it in minutes. CLICK CLICK CLICK! Information, downloaded. Secrets, revealed. Mission, accomplished.

And before any of this happens? Natasha studies. She pores over floor plans, learns every guard's routine, memorises where every camera points, and maps every possible route in and out. Then she makes backup plans for every single thing that could go wrong. And backup plans for THOSE. By the time she walks in, she has already solved the mission a dozen times in her head.

Do you know what her enemies notice, by the time they figure out something is wrong? Nothing. Silence. An empty corridor. Because Natasha Romanoff has already slipped past them like a breeze through an open window — carrying exactly what the Avengers needed, and leaving no trace behind at all.
