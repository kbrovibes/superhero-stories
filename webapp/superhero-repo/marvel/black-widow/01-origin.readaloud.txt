When Natasha Romanoff was very young, she was taken from her ordinary life and brought to a secret place deep in Russia called the Red Room. It was not a school in any way you would recognise. The Red Room trained girls to be spies and fighters — and it did so by taking away everything that made them themselves.

Natasha was brilliant and determined. She learned every skill they taught her — how to move in silence, how to disappear in plain sight, how to think three steps ahead of any enemy. She became the finest operative the Red Room had ever trained, and they gave her a codename: the Black Widow.

But as the years passed, a quiet discomfort grew inside her. She was very, very good at her work. And yet — she was being used as a weapon for people who did not care about protecting anyone. That did not sit right with the person Natasha truly was, deep down.

Then one day, a S.H.I.E.L.D. agent named Clint Barton arrived on a mission with orders to stop her. Do you know what he did instead? He looked at Natasha Romanoff and saw the person behind the Black Widow. He offered her something no one had offered before — a choice.

She took it. She joined S.H.I.E.L.D. and spent every mission afterwards trying to balance the things she regretted, to protect more lives than she had ever put at risk. She became one of the most valuable agents the world had ever seen.

And that is how Natasha Romanoff — once shaped into a weapon — chose instead to become a guardian, a founding member of the Avengers, and one of the most extraordinary heroes on Earth.
