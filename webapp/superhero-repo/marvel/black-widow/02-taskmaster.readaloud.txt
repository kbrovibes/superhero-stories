Most enemies fight back with their own moves. Taskmaster is very different. He uses yours.

Taskmaster is a dangerous mercenary with a very unusual gift called photographic reflexes. The moment he watches someone fight, he can copy their every move perfectly — their kicks, their flips, their throws — all of it. He uses this remarkable ability to train villains and hired thugs in the techniques of the world's greatest heroes, and he is paid very well for it.

So imagine how strange it felt for Natasha Romanoff — the Black Widow — to find herself facing an enemy who matched her every attack exactly. Every spin, every strike, every clever combination she attempted, Taskmaster countered with flawless precision. It was like fighting a mirror.

During a dangerous mission to shut down a covert smuggling operation, Taskmaster had been hired to guard the criminals inside. The fight was exhausting. Every technique Natasha knew, he knew too.

And do you know what she did? She stopped fighting the way she had been trained. She moved unpredictably — chaotic, surprising, creative — inventing brand new movements in the middle of the fight that Taskmaster had never seen and could not anticipate. Her mind worked faster than his ability to copy.

Because here is the truth about Taskmaster. He can steal a person's moves. He cannot steal their heart, or their quick thinking, or their unbreakable will. Those things belong to Natasha alone.

From that day on, every time she faced Taskmaster, she became a little bit harder to predict — and he never quite managed to catch up with the real Black Widow.
