Have you ever looked in a mirror and seen yourself wave back? Now imagine your REFLECTION could also kick, flip, spin, and punch exactly like you do — and it was trying to catch you! That is what it feels like for Natasha Romanoff when she faces the villain called Taskmaster. Scary? Absolutely. Impossible to beat? Not a chance!

Taskmaster has the most unusual superpower you have probably ever heard of: photographic reflexes. That means the instant he WATCHES someone fight, he can copy their every single move with perfect, flawless precision. A backflip? Copied. A spinning kick? Stored in his brain forever. He once injected himself with an experimental serum that unlocked this ability — but do you know the sneaky catch? Every new fighting move he copies, he loses a little bit of his own memory! He trades his past for someone else's skills. That is a pretty wild trade.

So when Taskmaster set his sights on Natasha, he had already studied her moves. He had watched recordings of every battle she had ever been in. THUNK — her punch was his punch! SNAP — her kick was his kick! During a mission to shut down a dangerous smuggling gang, he stood between Natasha and the criminals she was after, mirroring her every attack with eerie, robotic perfection.

And do you know what happened next? Natasha grinned. Because she had figured out his weakness.

Taskmaster can copy what he SEES. But he cannot copy what he has NEVER seen before. So Natasha stopped fighting the way she was trained. She started making up brand new moves — wild, unpredictable, chaotic combinations she had invented in that very moment. He had no recordings of those. He had no memory of those. And his perfect copy machine of a brain just could not keep up! ZAP — her surprise attack caught him totally off guard!

Here is the lesson: having all the moves in the world is not enough if you do not have the clever, creative, unstoppable MIND behind them. That mind belongs to Black Widow, and Taskmaster can never, ever copy that.
