When Natasha Romanoff faces enemies with super-strength, or armour made of metal, or powers she cannot match with her hands alone, she has a secret. It is strapped to her wrists, and it crackles with electricity — the Widow's Bite.

The Widow's Bite was designed by the brilliant engineers at S.H.I.E.L.D. specifically for Natasha and her fighting style. These sleek gauntlets fit perfectly under her suit and can deliver a sharp electrical shock to any opponent — from a gentle stun to a burst powerful enough to stop even a superhuman enemy in their tracks. She can adjust the level in an instant, depending on the situation.

But the Widow's Bite does more than just shock. It fires grappling hooks so Natasha can swing through the air. It releases clouds of tear gas to disorient groups of enemies. It even emits concentrated blasts of sound to throw off anyone who gets too close. She can also use it to communicate with computers and unlock electronic systems — which turns out to be very useful in a world full of encrypted doors and hidden data.

Do you want to hear about the most impressive moment the Widow's Bite ever had? Natasha found herself alone in a dim industrial warehouse, surrounded by a whole squad of heavily armoured mercenaries. She waited for the right moment, then released a wide electrical burst that short-circuited every single suit of armour at once. Then, quick as thought, she fired her grappling hook, swung up into the rafters, and picked off the rest of them one by one.

From that day on, the Widow's Bite was more than just a weapon. It was a promise — that Natasha Romanoff would always find a way.
