Sam Wilson grew up in Harlem and learned to fly because the Air Force needed people who would go where no one else wanted to go. He wore a remarkable piece of military hardware called the EXO-7 Falcon — a metal backpack that unfolded into a pair of mechanical wings, letting him soar and dive and rocket through the air at speeds no parachute could match. His job was to fly into dangerous mountain valleys and bring injured soldiers home. He never thought of himself as a hero. He thought of himself as a man who got his friends home.

Then one mission went wrong. Sam's wingman, Riley, was hit by a rocket in mid-flight. Sam was right there — six feet away — and could do nothing. He came home to Washington and put the wings away. He spent his days at the VA Hospital, helping other veterans find their way back.

Then one morning, during a jog around the National Mall, a man in a hood kept passing him on the running path. "On your left," the man said, every single time. The man's name was Steve Rogers.

And do you know what happened over a long, quiet breakfast after that run? Sam told Steve about Riley. Steve told Sam about his friend Bucky. Two men who had both lost someone important — one from the past and one from last year — found in each other a kind of friendship that does not need many words.

When HYDRA threatened to use S.H.I.E.L.D.'s Helicarriers to kill millions of people in a single afternoon — a plan called Project Insight — Steve showed up at Sam's door. Sam pulled a photo of the EXO-7 wings from a folder on his table. "Tell me where to find a flight suit," he said.

From that day on, the Falcon was back. And this time, his wings belonged to something bigger than any one mission.
