WHOOOOSH! Imagine strapping on a backpack — and that backpack UNFOLDS into giant mechanical wings and LAUNCHES you into the sky at the speed of a jet! That is Sam Wilson's whole deal. And it is INCREDIBLE.

Sam Wilson grew up in Harlem and became an Air Force pararescue specialist — one of the toughest jobs you can imagine. Pararescue means you fly into dangerous, scary mountain valleys to bring injured soldiers home. Nobody wants to go there. Sam went every time!

His wings were called the EXO-7 Falcon. Metal backpack, two huge articulated wings that snap open — FWOOOOSH! — and suddenly Sam is diving between canyon walls at unbelievable speed. He could fly faster than a jet but twist and turn like a real falcon. ZOOM! BANK! SWOOP!

Then one terrible mission, Sam's wingman — his flight partner, his friend Riley — was hit by a rocket-propelled grenade in mid-flight. Sam was right there. Six feet away. He could do nothing. Riley was gone.

Sam came home to Washington D.C. and put the wings away. He went to the VA Hospital instead, helping other veterans find their way through hard times. He jogged the National Mall every morning, quiet and steady.

One morning during his run, a man in a hood kept passing him on the path. "On your left," the man said. Again: "On your left." AGAIN: "On your left." Then the man ran off so fast it was almost unfair.

The man's name was Steve Rogers. Captain America.

Can you imagine accidentally making friends with Captain America on your morning jog?! They grabbed breakfast together afterward. Sam told Steve about Riley. Steve told Sam about his friend Bucky — a friend lost long ago, someone Steve still missed every day. Two men who'd both lost someone important found each other. That's how the best friendships work.

Then HYDRA threatened to use three massive Helicarriers — giant flying warships — to identify and eliminate millions of people in a single afternoon. A plan called Project Insight. Steve showed up at Sam's door. Sam pulled out a photo of the EXO-7 Falcon. "Tell me where to find a flight suit."

And just like that, the wings were back. SNAP! FWOOOOSH!

Because Sam Wilson was always a pararescue specialist who flew toward the trouble. Captain America just helped him remember that the trouble was worth flying toward.
