• After the Flag Smashers were stopped, Sam Wilson and Bucky Barnes drove from a New York rooftop down to Delacroix, Louisiana in a borrowed pickup truck loaded with tools
• Did not speak for the first three hundred miles — finally a team, no need for words
• Sarah Wilson met them on the porch with her two boys; handed Bucky sweet tea and pointed at the chore list on the fridge
• Top of the list: a leaky bilge pump on the family fishing boat, the Paul and Darlene
• Three weeks of repairs: Bucky scraped barnacles, fixed a broken winch, rehung a screen door, ate gumbo and corn bread
• Cass (Sam's nephew) asked endless questions about the vibranium arm — Bucky answered every one
• Walked the bayou at sunset with Sam, watched herons; did not check his phone, did not write new names in his notebook
• First time in seventy years Bucky was simply present in his own body, in a family, in a town
• On the last morning, Bucky couldn't find words for Sarah — she hugged him and said "Come back whenever, there's always a list"
• Driving out of Delacroix, Sam glanced over and saw Bucky asleep against the truck door — finally, peacefully, for the first time in years
