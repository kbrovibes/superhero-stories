Okay, here is a fun fact: Sam Wilson and Bucky Barnes absolutely could not stand each other at first. At ALL. And they became the best partnership in the whole Marvel universe! How does that happen?!

Sam had read Bucky's Winter Soldier file. He knew every mission, every mission HYDRA had sent him on. That's a lot to get past. And Bucky? Bucky decided Sam was a breezy Air Force kid who liked his wings too much. They were polite to each other for Steve Rogers's sake. Polite is NOT the same as friendly.

Their first real mission together sent them chasing the Flag Smashers across three countries. Three! They sat side by side in a cargo plane arguing about EVERYTHING.

Sam: Bucky needs to apologize to the families of people the Winter Soldier hurt.
Bucky: That won't undo anything!
Sam: THAT'S NOT THE POINT!

Then Bucky went quiet. And he admitted, very softly, that he hadn't slept a full night in three years. Three years! Sam didn't lecture. He didn't say anything at all. He just slid a notebook across the bench so Bucky could write the names down properly. That was the moment things began to shift.

The more missions they ran together, the more they needed each other. Bucky had a way of stopping Sam from second-guessing himself — just a look, a word, a steadying hand. Sam had a way of pulling Bucky out of his memories before he got stuck inside them. They even went together to ask for help from Helmut Zemo — the man who had BROKEN the Avengers — because they needed what only he knew. Desperate times!

And do you know how the final battle in New York looked? BUCKY covered the ground — WHOMP CRASH CLANG! SAM covered the sky — FWOOOOSH! Bucky threw the shield. Sam caught it. SNAP! They finished each other's plans without a single word.

After the battle, Bucky drove all the way down to Louisiana — where Sam's sister Sarah Wilson was trying to fix the family boat — and just... helped fix the boat. He ate dinner with Sam's nephews. He sat at the table. He let himself be a person again, not just a soldier or a weapon.

Sam looked at him across the table and finally smiled.

That's how two people who couldn't stand each other became something better: partners, friends, and — though neither of them would ever say it out loud — family.
