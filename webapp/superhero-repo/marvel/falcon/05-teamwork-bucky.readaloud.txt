Sam Wilson and Bucky Barnes did not like each other. They were polite for Steve Rogers's sake, which is not the same thing at all. Sam had read Bucky's file as the Winter Soldier. Bucky had decided Sam was a breezy Air Force kid who thought too highly of his own wings. They were stuck with each other, and both of them knew it.

Their first real mission together took them across three countries, sitting side by side in a cargo plane, arguing about everything. Should Bucky apologize to the families of the people the Winter Soldier had hurt? Sam said yes. Bucky said it would not undo anything. Sam said that was not the point.

Then Bucky went quiet for a moment. And he admitted, very softly, that he had not slept a full night in three years. Sam did not say anything. He just slid a notebook across the bench so Bucky could write the names down properly. That was the moment things began to change.

The closer they got to the end of the mission, the more they needed each other. Bucky had a way of pulling Sam back from doubt. Sam had a way of pulling Bucky out of his memories before he got stuck inside them. They even went together to ask for help from Helmut Zemo — the man who had once torn the Avengers in half — because they needed what only he knew.

And do you know how the final battle in New York went? Bucky covered the ground. Sam covered the sky. Bucky threw the shield. Sam caught it. They finished each other's plans without a single word.

Afterward, Bucky helped fix Sam's sister Sarah's boat in Louisiana. He ate with Sam's nephews. He sat at the table and let himself be a person again. Sam looked at him over his glass and finally smiled.

That is how two people who couldn't stand each other became the best partnership anyone could ask for.
