ZAP! WHOOOOSH! CRASH! Welcome to one of the wildest chase scenes in the sky — where Sam Wilson proved something really important: you don't need a shield to be a hero!

Sam was hunting a group called the Flag Smashers. But first? First he had to handle a very theatrical French mercenary named Georges Batroc. Batroc was famous for fighting with savate — a French style of kickboxing that uses LOTS of fancy spinning kicks — WHAP WHAP WHAP! He had once gone punch for punch with Captain America himself on a cargo ship. He lost. He did NOT forget.

Now Batroc was on a turboprop plane flying at twenty thousand feet above the desert between Libya and Tunisia, with a hostage on board. Twenty thousand feet! That's really, really high up. Like, clouds-below-you high up.

Sam strapped on his new wings — a sleek Wakandan-designed set, slimmer and faster than the old EXO-7, built from Vibranium technology. CLICK CLICK FWOOOOSH! He launched off and banked between the plane's engines and the desert cliffs below — SO close the air shook behind him! WHOOOOSH!

Batroc's men parachuted out the back and tried to shoot Sam in mid-flight. MID-FLIGHT! Who does that?! Sam deployed his Redwing drone — a small mechanical bird of prey — CLICK! — and sent it zipping after the jumpers while he dove into the open back of the plane himself.

He found the hostage — a Tunisian intelligence officer — grabbed them, and jumped right back OUT of the plane into open sky!

PULL UP! Sam yanked his wings six feet above the sand dunes. The ground EXPLODED around them in a huge plume of sand — WHOOOOOM! The officer looked at Sam like he'd just been pulled out of a dream.

Batroc escaped to cause trouble another day. But something shifted inside Sam. He hadn't been wearing Captain America's shield. He hadn't been fighting for a title. He'd just been Sam Wilson — a pararescue specialist from Harlem who always, always flies toward the trouble.

And you know what? That's always been exactly enough.
