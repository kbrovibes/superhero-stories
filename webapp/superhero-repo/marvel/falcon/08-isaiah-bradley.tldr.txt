• Isaiah Bradley: a Black soldier who had been given the Super Soldier Serum during the Korean War and sent on missions no history book published
• As strong and brave as Steve Rogers — but the government locked him away for thirty years and ran experiments on him instead of awarding a medal
• The world was told he was dead; he was not — he lived quietly in Baltimore with his grandson Eli
• Bucky Barnes brought Sam to Isaiah's door without warning, knowing Sam needed to hear the story unprepared
• Isaiah refused to be called Captain America — told Sam to leave the shield on the floor of a museum
• Sam carried Isaiah's story through every Flag Smasher fight, every council meeting, every press conference
• After the final battle in New York, Sam went to the Smithsonian Institution and asked the curators to build a new wing
• The new exhibit: a glass case with a uniform, a photograph, and a name — Isaiah Bradley — in clean white letters
• Sam brought Isaiah and Eli to see it on a cold afternoon; Isaiah stood in front of his own name and finally cried
• Sam could not undo what the country had done — but he could refuse to let Isaiah be invisible any longer
