Now, here is a question for you. If Sam Wilson is Captain America now — who flies the wings? Who is the new Falcon?

His name is Joaquin Torres. And he is wonderful.

Sam met Joaquin in Tunisia. Joaquin was a young Air Force lieutenant stationed at a small base in the desert, and he had been chasing tips about the Flag Smashers all by himself for a whole month before Sam ever showed up. Joaquin was sharp. He was funny. And he could FLY anything with a control stick.

True story: one time Joaquin landed a helicopter on a runway with only ONE working rotor. Most pilots would say that is impossible. Joaquin said, "I had options." Then he laughed.

Sam liked him from the very first conversation.

He liked him even more after Joaquin got knocked around in a fight with a Flag Smasher and ended up in a Tunisian hospital. Sam walked into the hospital room expecting to find a sad, bandaged-up lieutenant. Instead, Joaquin was sitting up in bed eating crackers and grinning, and he said — calmly, like he was asking what was for lunch — "So, when are we going back out?"

Sam knew, right then, that this kid was going to be a problem. The good kind.

When Sam became Captain America, he kept Joaquin close by. They built a partnership the same way Sam had built one with Bucky — slowly, with lots of arguments, with shared takeout food on long flights, with Joaquin slowly learning to read Sam's silences.

Sam took Joaquin to WAKANDA. Yes, Wakanda! Joaquin's eyes were as big as dinner plates the whole trip. Sam introduced him to the Royal Engineering Corps. Sam took him to Delacroix, Louisiana, to meet Sarah Wilson and the boys. (Cass had a LOT of new questions to ask.) And Sam took Joaquin up into the air, again and again, and showed him exactly how the vibranium wings caught a crosswind. WHOOOOSH. SNAP. CLICK.

Joaquin was a good student. Sam was a patient teacher.

And then, one day, the day finally came.

Sam was Captain America now. The shield needed both of his hands. So Sam said: "Joaquin. The wings are yours."

Joaquin had been training in his own pair of Wakandan-tech wings for almost a year. The suit had been built specially for him — sleeker than Sam's old one. The chest plate was deep green and gold. Beautiful. And right at the collar, in a tiny gold clip, there was an Air Force pin. Because Sam knew Joaquin wouldn't feel right flying without it.

Joaquin pulled on the helmet. He turned and looked at himself in a polished hangar wall. He stared for a long moment.

Then he said, very quietly: "Oh, this is gonna be a problem."

Reader, it WAS a problem.

But ONLY for the bad guys he was about to chase through the sky.

Joaquin took to being the Falcon the same way Sam had — with skill, with humor, and with a complete refusal to leave any friend behind. He worked alongside Sam now as a real partner. Not a student. Not a sidekick. A partner.

Sam flew with the shield on his arm. WHOMP. CLANG.

Joaquin flew with the Redwing drone darting at his shoulder. BZZZZ. PING. WHIRR.

They flew together over Washington. Over Wakanda. Over Delacroix.

And one day, Sam watched Joaquin bank into a clean dive at three hundred miles an hour — and Sam finally understood what Steve Rogers must have felt the day he handed Sam the shield by the lake.

Some gifts, given at the right time, to the right person — fly even higher than they did before.

That's Joaquin Torres. The new Falcon. Watch the sky.
