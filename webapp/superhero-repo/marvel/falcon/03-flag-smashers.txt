After the snap was reversed, half of humanity returned home to find that the other half had spent five years rebuilding the world without them. Borders had moved. Apartments had new tenants. Refugees had become citizens. A young woman named Karli Morgenthau, who had grown up in a global support shelter during those five years, watched all of those refugees lose their new homes overnight when the original tenants returned. She and her friends formed a group called the Flag Smashers, and they took a serum that made them as strong as Captain America. Their goal was not to hurt anyone. Their goal was a single world without borders. The trouble was, they were willing to hurt anyone who stood in their way.

Sam Wilson met Karli first in Munich, in a warehouse full of stolen relief supplies. She fought him at super-soldier strength while her team escaped down a stairwell, and she could have ended him with a single punch. Instead, she stopped, looked him in the eye, and asked him whether he understood what it meant to be invisible to the people in power. Sam, who had grown up Black in America, told her quietly that he understood that better than she did. Then he asked her how breaking other people’s ribs was supposed to fix any of it. Karli didn’t have a good answer. She left him standing in the warehouse with his Redwing drone hovering at his shoulder.

The fight kept escalating across continents. The Flag Smashers blew up an aid building. They kidnapped the leader of a global resettlement council and threatened to drop her off a balcony onto a crowd. Sam Wilson, in a brand-new red, white, and blue suit forged by Shuri in Wakanda, finally caught Karli on a New York rooftop. He did not punch her. He talked to her. He told her that she was not wrong about the problem, only about the solution, and that the world had not asked her to choose violence to be heard. For the first time, Karli faltered. Sharon Carter, hidden across the street, did not. A single shot ended the fight before Sam could finish his sentence.

Afterward, Sam stood in front of the cameras with the council members all gathered around him. He did not give an angry speech. He gave a careful one. He said that doing the right thing should not require having superpowers, and that the people in power could choose, every single day, to do better. He named Karli once, not as a villain, but as a young person who had been failed by every adult in her life. When the cameras turned off, Sam quietly folded his wings, lifted the shield, and walked away as the new Captain America. He had not won by being the strongest. He had won by being the most patient.
