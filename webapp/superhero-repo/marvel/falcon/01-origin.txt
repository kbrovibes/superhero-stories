Sam Wilson grew up in Harlem and learned how to fly because the Air Force needed pararescue specialists for missions no one else wanted. He spent his twenties strapped into a piece of military hardware called the EXO-7 Falcon — a pair of mechanical wings that unfolded out of a metal backpack and let him glide, dive, and rocket through the air at speeds no parachute could ever match. He flew night runs into mountain valleys to lift injured soldiers off the ground, his wings catching updrafts that smaller drones could not even register. He never thought of himself as a hero. He thought of himself as a guy who got his friends home.

The mission that changed his life was the one that did not end the way it should have. Sam’s wingman, Riley, was hit by a rocket-propelled grenade in mid-flight. Sam saw it happen in real time, his own wings spread wide six feet away, and could do nothing. After that, he came home to Washington, D.C., hung up the EXO-7, and started counseling other veterans at a clinic at the VA Hospital. He spent his weekday mornings jogging laps around the National Mall, telling himself that ordinary life was enough. It was, until one morning a man in a hood passed him on the running path so quickly that Sam laughed out loud. "On your left," the man said, every single time he lapped him. The man’s name was Steve Rogers.

The friendship between Sam Wilson and Steve Rogers was instant. Sam told Steve about Riley over a long, quiet breakfast. Steve told Sam about Bucky over an even longer one. When HYDRA tried to assassinate Nick Fury and turn S.H.I.E.L.D. into a tool for global control, Steve showed up at Sam’s door and asked, without quite asking, for help. Sam took him into his living room, opened a folder on a coffee table, and showed him a single photo of the EXO-7 wings hanging in a museum vault. "Don’t need to know what we’re doing," Sam said with a tired smile. "Tell me where to find a flight suit."

The Battle of the Triskelion was Falcon’s first day as an Avenger, and he was extraordinary at it. He flew between three crashing Helicarriers, dodged HYDRA fire, and helped Steve replace the targeting chips that ended Project Insight before it could end half the world’s leaders. When the smoke cleared, Sam Wilson stood next to Steve Rogers on a dock by the Potomac, looked at the sky over a wounded city, and decided to keep flying. The Falcon was back, and this time, his wings would belong to a team.
