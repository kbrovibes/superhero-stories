Okay — are you ready for the coolest room in all of comic books? Get comfortable, because we are going UNDERGROUND!

Beneath Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters — past the reinforced steel doors, down the private elevator — is a room that looks empty when you first walk in. White walls. Smooth floor. Totally boring.

And then — WHOOOOOM — it ACTIVATES!

The Danger Room was designed by Professor Xavier and Hank McCoy, also known as Beast, and it can turn into ANYTHING. A crumbling building — CRASH! A thunderstorm — BOOM! A holographic forest where robotic opponents jump out from behind every tree — SNIKT! Everything looks real. Everything feels real. Your brain genuinely cannot tell the difference!

The first time Logan walked in, a forest appeared all around him — trees, roots, river, the works. Three holographic opponents came at him from three directions. There was a river crossing with no obvious safe route. There was a falling tree heading straight for him — TIMBER!

Logan finished the whole thing in FOUR MINUTES.

Cyclops was up in the control room watching. He raised one eyebrow behind his visor. Most recruits take TWELVE minutes.

But the Danger Room's best trick was teaching the X-Men how to work TOGETHER.

One day, the scenario was a collapsing underground tunnel. Five holographic hostages needed rescuing. GO!

Storm — WHOOSH — funneled wind through a gap in the debris to clear it! Jean Grey — BZZZZT — held up an entire section of falling ceiling with her mind! Cyclops called out from above: "Logan — passage three — left side — NOW!"

Logan opened his mouth. He was going to argue. He could feel it coming.

He went left instead.

The passage opened. The hostages escaped. Mission complete!

When the lights came back up, Logan looked around at his team. Jean, who had held up a CEILING. Storm, as precise as a surgeon. Cyclops, who had seen the whole picture at once.

Logan nodded slowly.

He had just learned something important. You can be the toughest person in the room — and still need the people beside you.

That is what the Danger Room teaches. That is what the X-Men know.

SNIKT — now, who wants to run the simulation again?
