All right. This is the last story in our Wolverine collection, and it might be my favorite one. Ready?

Every year at Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters, Professor Charles Xavier holds something called the Gathering. Students, X-Men, teachers — everyone sits together in the great main hall, and they talk about what the year has taught them. What they learned. What mattered.

This year? Professor Xavier asked Logan to speak.

Logan stood at the front of that big hall and looked out at all those young faces — new students who'd just arrived, older students who'd been there for years, teammates he had trained alongside. And he thought: what do I actually want to say?

He told them about Muir Island.

Muir Island is off the coast of Scotland, and on it lives Dr. Moira MacTaggert — one of the greatest scientists in the world, and a close friend of Professor Xavier's. One stormy night, a systems failure at her research facility trapped three of her staff in the lower labs. Automated systems weren't working. The North Sea weather was terrible. CRASH — BOOM — a full-on storm.

The X-Men arrived.

BOOM — Cyclops had a plan in thirty seconds flat. WHOOSH — Storm steadied the weather so the Blackbird could land safely. Jean Grey linked everyone telepathically — they could all hear each other's thoughts, no need for radio. And Logan?

Logan went into the lower labs. The deepest section. The most difficult part.

He went because that was the most useful thing he could do. And he was — though he did not say this out loud to the students — afraid. Not of the danger. But of failing. Of getting it wrong. Of letting his teammates down.

What happened? He followed his training. He followed his team. He used his claws exactly when they were needed — SNIKT — and retracted them the instant they weren't — SNIKT again. He moved through those dark, damaged labs and he found all three researchers and he brought them out.

Dr. MacTaggert met him on the tarmac in the rain. She shook his hand with BOTH her hands. He wasn't sure what to do with that.

He looked out at the hall. Students were very quiet.

"I spent a long time," he said, "believing the only thing I had to offer was the ability to take a hit and keep going."

He paused.

"The X-Men showed me that taking a hit and keeping going is only worth something because of what it protects. It's never the point by itself." He took a breath. "The point is the people."

Professor Xavier smiled.

The hall was quiet for a long, warm, wonderful moment.

SNIKT. That's Wolverine. And now you know his whole story.
