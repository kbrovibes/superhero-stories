Long before he was the hero called Wolverine, there was a small, sickly boy named James Howlett who grew up on a sprawling estate in the rolling hills of Alberta, Canada. The year was the 1880s, and James spent most of his days inside, too frail for rough games, watching the wide open wilderness from the windows of his family's grand house. His closest friend was a girl named Rose, and together they explored the quiet corners of the estate while the wind swept down from the Rocky Mountains to the west.

One stormy night, a violent confrontation shattered James's world completely. In the chaos and fear of that moment, something extraordinary happened — bone claws burst through the skin between his knuckles for the very first time. Three on each hand, sharp as thorns, solid as rock. It hurt. It frightened him. But it was also the first sign of a gift that would define the rest of his life. James had a healing factor, a power hidden deep in his blood that could mend any wound faster than the human eye could follow.

James fled the estate with Rose and took a new name: Logan. He was quiet about his claws and quieter still about the way cuts and bruises vanished from his skin overnight. He worked in the lumber camps of British Columbia, swinging axes and hauling timber, growing stronger every season. The other workers called him a tough kid. They had no idea. Logan's bones were denser than normal, his senses sharper — he could track a deer through the forest by scent alone, hear a whisper from fifty paces, and see through darkness the way a wolf can.

Over the long decades that followed, Logan traveled the world. He fought in great wars alongside ordinary soldiers, doing what he could to protect people who had no healing factor, no bone claws, no second chances. Each conflict left its mark on his memory, even when no scar remained on his skin. He learned to be a fighter, but more importantly he learned when not to fight.

By the time Logan crossed paths with a remarkable man named Charles Xavier, he had lived more lives than most people could imagine. Professor Xavier saw the goodness in Logan — the quiet protectiveness, the fierce loyalty — and offered him a place among the X-Men. Logan hesitated. Then he thought of every person he had failed to protect. He said yes.
