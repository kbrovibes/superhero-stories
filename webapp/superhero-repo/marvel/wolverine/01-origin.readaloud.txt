Are you ready? Because this story starts in the wild, windy forests of Canada — and it is one of the most extraordinary stories you have ever heard!

His name was James Howlett, and he grew up on a big, beautiful estate in Alberta, Canada, way back in the 1800s! James was small and quiet and a little bit sickly — not the kind of boy anyone would expect to become a legend. His best friend was a girl named Rose, and the two of them spent their days exploring every corner of the estate while the Rocky Mountains gleamed in the distance.

And then, one stormy night — SNIKT! — three bone claws punched right through the skin between James's knuckles. THREE. ON EACH HAND. Sharp as a hawk's talon and solid as rock. It hurt! It scared him! But here was the amazing part — a moment later, the skin around the claws was already healing. His body could fix itself FASTER than you could snap your fingers!

James took a new name — LOGAN — and ran off into the wild. He worked in the lumber camps of British Columbia, chopping wood and hauling timber. The other workers thought he was just a tough young man. They did not know that Logan could smell a campfire from three miles away. They did not know he could hear a pine cone drop in the forest at night. They did not know his cuts and bruises were gone by morning!

WHOOSH — Logan traveled the world! He fought in great wars, watching out for soldiers who did not have his gifts. Each battle made him wiser, tougher, more careful. He learned that fighting is sometimes necessary — but it is never fun. He saw that ordinary people needed protecting.

Then one day, a man in a wheelchair rolled into his life. Professor Charles Xavier had a school in New York, a school for people with special gifts just like Logan's. Xavier looked at the gruff, weathered man with the wild hair and the sideburns and the crossed arms — and saw something nobody else had seen in a long time.

Xavier saw a HERO.

Logan stared back. He thought about all the times he had been too late, all the people he wished he could have kept safe. He uncrossed his arms. He nodded.

And just like that — Wolverine was born!
