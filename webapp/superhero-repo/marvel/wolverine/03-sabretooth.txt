Of all the enemies Wolverine has ever faced, none is more personal — or more dangerous — than Victor Creed, the man called Sabretooth. Like Logan, Creed has bone claws, a healing factor, and senses sharp enough to track prey across a dozen miles. But where Logan fights to protect people, Sabretooth fights for domination. He is bigger, louder, and cruelest when he thinks he has won — which makes him exactly the kind of enemy Wolverine refuses to back down from.

Their rivalry stretches back through more years than most people can count. Sabretooth has crossed paths with Logan at some of the worst moments in his life, always appearing exactly when Logan is at his most raw, his most uncertain. Creed seems to take particular pleasure in reminding Logan of his past — the lost memories, the long wandering years, the things that were done to Logan without his consent. It is a cruel tactic, and Logan has had to learn not to let it shake him.

One of their fiercest encounters happened on the grounds of Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters. Sabretooth arrived at the mansion not to negotiate but to cause mayhem, crashing through the garden gate with a roar that rattled the windows. Students scattered. Alarms rang out. The X-Men mobilized immediately — but Logan was already moving. He had smelled Creed's approach a full minute before anyone else, and he was waiting.

Their battle tore up the lawn and brought down a stretch of garden wall. Sabretooth's claws scraped against Logan's adamantium blades in a shriek of metal on metal. SNIKT — CRASH — CLANG — back and forth they pushed each other, strength against strength. What separated them was not raw power. It was will. Sabretooth wanted to prove he was greater. Logan only wanted to protect the people behind him. That difference decided everything.

Logan drove Sabretooth back step by step until Creed finally retreated into the trees, snarling and defeated. The students came back out slowly, eyes wide. Logan stood at the edge of the garden, claws still extended, watching the darkness. Then he retracted them with a quiet SNIKT, turned around, and asked if everyone was all right. They were. Because he was there.
