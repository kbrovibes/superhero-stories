The claws are the first thing most people notice about Wolverine, and understandably so. Three on each hand, springing from between his knuckles with a sound that has become one of the most famous sounds in all of comics — SNIKT. When extended, they catch the light like polished mirrors. When retracted, they vanish so completely that Logan can shake hands with a stranger and no one suspects a thing.

But those claws have a long and layered history. They began as pure bone — natural mutations that appeared the same stormy night Logan's healing factor first activated. For decades, the bone claws were his secret, a last resort he rarely needed to reveal. They were strong, but they were also breakable, and Logan had learned to be careful with them. Bone is impressive. Bone is not adamantium.

Adamantium is a metallic alloy so dense and durable that almost nothing in the world can damage it. The scientists of Weapon X coated every bone in Logan's skeleton with it — and the claws came out the other side transformed. Where once they were a natural ivory, they emerged gleaming silver. Where once they could chip or fracture, they could now score grooves in steel. Where once they were a remarkable biological feature, they were now among the most formidable physical weapons on Earth.

Logan has used those claws in service of others in countless ways. He has cut ropes to free trapped allies. He has sliced through metal doors when no key existed. He has deflected energy blasts by holding his arms up like shields. In the frozen forests north of Westchester, he once cut a fallen tree blocking a mountain road with a few minutes of precise work — faster than any chainsaw, cleaner than any axe.

What matters most is the choice behind the claws. Every time Logan extends them, it is a decision. He chooses when, and why, and in whose defense. Professor Xavier understood this better than anyone. He never trained Logan to hide the claws or be ashamed of them. He trained Logan to understand that a weapon in the hands of someone with good judgment is not frightening — it is protective. The SNIKT sound that echoes through the halls of Xavier's school is the sound of a guardian saying: I am here. No one will be hurt on my watch.
