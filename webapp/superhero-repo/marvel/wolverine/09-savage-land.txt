Deep in the heart of Antarctica, far past the ice sheets where maps go blank, there is a place that should not exist: the Savage Land. Hidden inside a vast crater warmed by volcanic vents, it is a lush, prehistoric wilderness — towering jungle, warm rivers, and creatures that vanished from the rest of the world sixty-five million years ago. The X-Men had been briefed on it. Reading about it and landing in it were entirely different things.

The team arrived via the Blackbird after a distress beacon activated deep in the Savage Land's interior. A group of researchers from the Xavier Institute's extended network had gone in to study the region and gone silent. Wolverine, Cyclops, Jean Grey, and Storm deployed into the jungle while the Blackbird held at altitude. Within minutes, the forest had swallowed all sight of the sky.

This was where Logan's senses became the team's most essential asset. In the deep jungle, visibility dropped to a dozen feet. Electronic instruments misfired in the magnetic interference thrown by the volcanic vents. Cyclops's tactical maps were useless. But Logan could smell the river three miles north, the researchers' trail two days old, the distinctive scent of something large moving parallel to the group fifty yards east. He navigated without hesitation, leading the team through terrain that would have taken twice as long to cross any other way.

The complication arrived on the second day: the researchers had not merely gotten lost. They had stumbled into the territory of Garokk, an ancient being whose domain extended across the central plateau, and his forces had penned them into a riverbed canyon. The canyon walls were too steep to climb and too densely guarded to rush.

Logan's solution was not to attack. It was to navigate. While Storm created a localized weather event over the plateau — drawing Garokk's forces' attention upward — Logan slipped along the canyon rim, found the unguarded eastern access, and guided the researchers out in single file, leading by scent and memory in near-total darkness. Jean maintained a psychic veil over the group, softening their presence to anyone scanning the area.

They reached the Blackbird at dawn, all researchers present and unharmed. Standing at the edge of the jungle, watching the extraordinary green of the Savage Land stretch away behind them, Logan allowed himself a brief moment of quiet satisfaction. Some problems were solved with force. Some were solved with patience, care, and the willingness to trust what your senses told you when all the instruments were dark.
