Okay — time to meet the most COMPLICATED villain in the whole X-Men world!

His name is Erik Lehnsherr, but most people call him MAGNETO. He wears a flowing cape and a deep red helmet — that helmet blocks all telepathy, so Jean Grey cannot read his mind. And his power? He can control METAL. Any metal. Any size. ANYWHERE.

He can bend steel beams. He can pull iron bridges apart. He can levitate cars and throw them like tennis balls.

And Logan? Logan's entire skeleton is covered in adamantium — one of the most magnetic metals in the Marvel Universe.

You can see why this is a problem!

One stormy afternoon, the X-Men arrived by Blackbird jet at an offshore research platform off the coast of Nova Scotia. Magneto had taken it over — threatening to send a catastrophic power surge through undersea cables. Storm was controlling the weather overhead, BOOM, lightning flickering across the clouds. Cyclops was running the plan. Jean was shielding. Logan was in front.

And Magneto appeared on the upper deck — cape swirling — and the very FIRST thing he did was lift Logan straight into the air!

WHOOOOSH — up Logan went, thirty feet off the deck, hanging there by the pull of his own skeleton!

Magneto was making a point: I am in control here.

Logan hung in the air. Claws out. Totally calm. SNIKT. He had been in worse places.

But here is what Magneto did NOT think about — what was everyone ELSE doing?

BLAM — Cyclops fired a precise optic blast right through the control housing of the main antenna!

CRACK — Storm sent a focused lightning bolt into the backup generator!

Jean Grey, quietly and quickly, moved every member of the research crew to the safe lower deck. Every single one. All fine.

While Magneto was busy showing off — the X-Men had taken apart his entire plan!

Magneto looked around. His leverage was gone. He lowered Logan back to the deck — slowly, carefully — which was, in its own way, a kind of grudging respect.

Logan landed. Claws back in. He looked at Magneto.

"It doesn't have to be this way, Erik."

Magneto looked at him for a long moment. Then — whoooosh — he was gone, vanished into the cloud cover.

Platform safe. Crew safe. X-Men: one. Magneto: a long, long time thinking about his choices.

SNIKT — that is what you call a team victory!
