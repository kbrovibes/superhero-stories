Picture this: the front door of the biggest, grandest school you have ever seen swings open — and in walks Logan. Jacket. Stubble. Crossed arms. Not exactly your typical new student!

Welcome to Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters, Westchester, New York — the most extraordinary school on Earth!

Professor Charles Xavier was in a wheelchair, but he had the warmest smile and the sharpest mind of anyone in the room. He walked Logan — well, wheeled — through the grand hallways. And what did Logan see? A girl moving a book across the room without touching it! A boy leaving a trail of ice on the floor tiles and then melting it super fast before a teacher noticed — whoops! Students with gifts unlike anything Logan had seen gathered in one place before.

Professor Xavier introduced the team. First, Scott Summers — CYCLOPS — cool as ice, wearing his ruby red visor, the kind of team leader who has a plan for everything. Then came Jean Grey, warm and incredibly powerful — a telepath who could read thoughts AND move objects with her mind at the same time! Then Ororo Munroe — STORM — who could call lightning from a sunny sky. BOOM! And Hank McCoy — BEAST — brilliant as any scientist and acrobatic as a gymnast.

Logan? He crossed his arms a little tighter.

He was NOT used to teams. He was not used to rules. And he was DEFINITELY not used to getting along with Scott Summers, who had different ideas about strategy than Logan did. Those two clashed like — well — like adamantium claws on a metal door!

CLANG!

But then Logan started to watch. Really watch.

He saw how Jean checked on every teammate. He saw how Storm stayed calm when things got difficult. He saw Xavier look at every single student like they were someone extraordinary worth believing in.

And something in Logan went quiet. Something old and guarded and tired began to let go.

The day he first put on his blue-and-yellow X-Men uniform, Logan stood in front of the mirror. He looked at himself for a long moment. Not at the claws. Not at the stubble. But at the symbol on the chest.

He had spent decades looking for something worth fighting for. Something real.

He had found it.

SNIKT — welcome to the X-Men, Logan!
