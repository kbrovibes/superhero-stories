Once upon a time, in the wide green grounds of Xavier's School for Gifted Youngsters, the air smelled of cut grass and morning dew and something else — something that made Logan set down his coffee cup very, very slowly.

He had learned to trust that nose of his. A lifetime of living close to the wilderness will do that. And what his nose was telling him right now was not good.

Victor Creed was coming.

Now. If you have not yet heard of Victor Creed — also known as Sabretooth — here is what you need to know. He was tall, broad, and very loud. He had claws just as sharp as Logan's, a healing factor just as powerful, and senses almost as keen. In a way, the two of them were alike — born with the same kind of extraordinary gifts, shaped by the same extraordinary world.

But that is where the likeness ended.

Logan used his gifts to stand between danger and the people he cared about. Sabretooth used his gifts to remind the world how dangerous he could be. He did not protect. He did not build. He appeared, and he caused chaos, and he seemed to genuinely enjoy it.

And worst of all — he had known Logan for a very long time. He knew exactly which old memories to poke at, which old sadnesses to drag into the light. It was a mean trick. The meanest kind, really.

So when Sabretooth came crashing through the iron gate of the school grounds with a roar that scattered every bird from every tree within half a mile — Logan was already on his feet.

The students heard the alarm bells. BWEEEEP — BWEEEEP — the bright urgent sound echoing across the courtyard. They ran for the halls, glancing back over their shoulders with wide eyes. The other X-Men were pulling on their gear, checking with each other in the quick, calm way that a well-trained team does.

But Logan was already at the garden gate.

He stood in the morning light, feet apart, arms loose at his sides. He breathed in the sharp cold air. He watched the enormous figure of Sabretooth stop on the torn-up gravel path, eyes narrowing.

"Logan," Creed said — the way a thundercloud says hello.

SNIKT. Three claws, bright as mirrors, extended from Logan's right hand. Then three more from his left. He said nothing at all.

What followed was the kind of fight that leaves marks on the landscape for months afterward.

They moved fast — faster than the eye can follow if you blink. CLANG — the first strike, metal on metal, so sharp and loud it rang across the whole courtyard. CRASH — through the rose beds, petals flying in all directions. WHAM — against the old stone garden wall, which gave a deep groan and shed half its bricks to the ground.

Sabretooth was fierce, and Sabretooth was furious, and Sabretooth was enormous. He hit like a falling tree. His claws bit into Logan's costume and he snarled with something close to joy.

But Logan did not stop.

Here is why. Sabretooth wanted to win. That was the whole point for him — to be the bigger, stronger, better one. Every move he made was for himself.

Logan wasn't thinking about himself at all. Behind him, students were watching from the tall windows of the school. People he had promised to keep safe. Professor Xavier trusted him. His teammates trusted him. Every student in that building had come here because they believed they could be protected.

Logan was NOT going to let any of them down.

He pressed forward. And forward. And forward.

Creed stumbled over the fallen bricks. His snarl became uncertain. He slashed, and Logan deflected with his forearm and kept coming. He roared, and Logan went quiet and kept coming. Step by step, inch by inch, the great big bully of a man was pushed backward — through the broken gate, across the torn gravel, to the very edge of the lawn where the dark trees began.

There Sabretooth stopped. He glared at Logan with golden eyes full of fury.

"This isn't over," he snarled.

"It is for today," said Logan.

Creed held the stare for one long moment. Then he turned and vanished into the trees like a shadow that had somewhere else to be.

Logan stood at the edge of the lawn for a while longer, watching the forest, breathing slowly, waiting to be certain. The birds had not yet come back to the trees. When they did — when the sparrows settled on the branches and the robins started up again — he let out a long breath.

SNIKT, SNIKT — in came the claws.

He turned around. A dozen faces peered out from the windows of the school. He looked up at them.

"Is everyone all right?"

Nods. Thumbs-up. One very relieved wave from a girl on the second floor.

Logan nodded back. He picked up a fallen rose from the crushed garden bed, set it on the low bit of remaining wall, and walked back inside to finish his coffee.

Some days, that is exactly what heroism looks like.

Sleep well, little one. The good ones always show up in time.
