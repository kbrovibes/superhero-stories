OKAY! This is a big one! A whole team! An abandoned desert base! BOOBY TRAPS! Let's go!

The Reavers are not mutants. They are humans who have upgraded their bodies with cybernetic implants — metal arms, targeting systems wired directly into their brains, armor under their skin. They are fast. They are tough. And they have been causing the X-Men serious trouble for years.

When intelligence arrives that the Reavers are regrouping at an old HYDRA base in the Mojave Desert — the big hot desert in California — with enough weapons to start a small war, Cyclops calls the team together.

GAMBIT. WOLVERINE. STORM. ROGUE. CYCLOPS. JEAN GREY.

Six X-Men boarding the Blackbird — the X-Men's supersonic jet — and flying WEST.

The base is dug into a desert mesa. Concrete corridors. Hangar bays. And booby traps on every single entrance.

You can't just fly in the front door.

So Cyclops sends Gambit ahead to SCOUT.

Remy goes in alone. Dark. Silent. His glowing red eyes narrowed to thin slits so they don't give him away. He moves through four corridors exactly the way Marcel taught him in New Orleans, his feet finding the floor without a sound.

And what does he find? THREE pressure plates. TWO tripwires. A weight sensor hidden under a metal floor panel.

He radios all of this back to Cyclops — every distance, every position, every guard timing — and then he guides the team in on his route.

Cyclops directs the team like a chess grandmaster. Wolverine: heavy hitting! Storm: air cover! Jean Grey: telepathic communication! Gambit: in the lead, holding softly charged cards like lanterns, using tiny micro-charges to snap the tripwires without triggering their sensors. SNIP. SNIP. SNIP.

They reach the main hangar.

Thirty Reavers. Automated gun systems along the CEILING.

X-Men enter from THREE different doors at the SAME MOMENT.

BZZZZT! Cyclops sweeps his optic blast across the ceiling guns — they're gone in one arc! CRACK-BOOM! Storm calls lightning down and the WHOLE POWER GRID shorts out! WHOOSH — Rogue dives in from above, absorbs the Reavers' cybernetics, uses their own systems AGAINST them! And Gambit and Wolverine come in from opposite sides — CRACK CRACK KRAKOOM — SNIKT SNIKT — two different angles, completely coordinating without even talking, because they have trained together long enough to know.

Four minutes. FOUR MINUTES. And the Reavers are done.

On the Blackbird flying home, Wolverine — not famous for compliments — sits next to Remy.

"You're better in a team than you let on," he says.

"I'm a thief," Remy says. "We don't like people knowing how good we are."

The lights of home glitter in the dark ahead.

MISSION COMPLETE!
