Okay, here is something you need to know about Gambit: the playing cards are NOT the superpower. The playing cards are just his FAVORITE way to use the superpower. There is a difference, and it is a VERY cool difference. Let me explain!

Gambit's mutant ability is called kinetic energy charging. Kinetic energy is the energy that things have when they move — the energy inside a spinning top, a rolling ball, or a flying Frisbee. When Remy touches an object, he can PUMP kinetic energy into it, like inflating a balloon, faster and faster, more and more, until — if you let go — BOOM! All that energy explodes outward in a bright, precise flash. Pink. Purple. Crackling like a miniature lightning storm.

He could charge ANYTHING. A coin — BANG! A pebble — CRACK! A doorknob, a piece of chalk — POW! Once, in training, he charged a single grape and it went off like a tiny firecracker and everyone in the Danger Room lost their minds laughing. Even Wolverine smiled. A little.

But playing cards are special. Light as feathers. They slice through the air flat and fast. And Remy has been flicking cards from his fingers since he was a kid in New Orleans — the same wrist-snap he used to deal in the Thieves Guild. When he charges one, it glows that gorgeous pink-purple and crackles, and when it hits — KRAKOOM! — it explodes with exactly as much force as he decided to put into it. Precise.

He keeps two decks on him at all times. The worn one in his LEFT pocket — that's his practice deck, for training his concentration. The fresh one in his RIGHT pocket — emergency use only. His favorite card? The ace of diamonds. He says it flies truest. Nobody argues with him about this, because nobody else can throw a glowing ace of diamonds forty feet and hit a doorknob dead center, so.

In the Danger Room, Remy practices things even cooler than big explosions. He practices FLASH charges — just enough energy for a blinding light, no boom at all. He practices DELAY sequences — charging five cards with a tiny time gap between each one, creating a chain of explosions that walks across the floor like footsteps. And he practices his most important trick: draining the charge BACK out of an object. Defusing his own explosive. Because sometimes, being brave means putting something dangerous to sleep instead of setting it off.

"The cards aren't the weapon," he once told Bobby Drake after a training session.

"What is the weapon, then?" Bobby asked.

Remy tucked the deck back into his pocket and smiled.

"I am," he said. "The cards are just how I introduce myself."
