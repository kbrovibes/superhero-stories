The Reavers were not mutants. They were human fighters enhanced by cybernetic implants — metal limbs, targeting systems wired into their nervous systems, armor plating under synthetic skin — and they had attacked the X-Men at the mansion once before and been driven off, but never fully defeated. When intelligence reached Cyclops that the Reavers were regrouping at an old HYDRA base in the Mojave Desert with enough firepower to overwhelm a small army, he assembled the full X-Men roster. Gambit, Wolverine, Storm, Rogue, Cyclops, and Jean Grey boarded the Blackbird and flew west.

The base was a maze of concrete corridors and hangar bays dug into a mesa, and the Reavers had booby-trapped every entrance. This was not a problem that could be solved by flying in the front door. Cyclops sent Gambit ahead to scout, because there was nobody better at moving through a space quietly and reading what was inside it.

Remy mapped four corridors in darkness, his glowing eyes dimmed to slits, his feet finding the floor silently the way Marcel had taught him when he was nine. He marked three pressure plates, two tripwires, and a weight sensor hidden under a metal floor panel. He relayed everything to Cyclops through a comm earpiece — distances, positions, estimated guard timing.

The team went in together on Gambit's route. Cyclops directed them like a chess player — Wolverine as the heavy, Storm providing aerial support where the corridors opened into larger spaces, Jean managing communications and providing early warning of any minds moving toward them. Gambit moved ahead of the formation, lighting their path with softly charged cards held like lanterns, clearing tripwires with precisely aimed micro-charges that snapped them without triggering the sensors.

The Reavers' main force was in the largest hangar. Thirty of them, plus automated gun systems along the ceiling. The X-Men entered from three different access points simultaneously — Cyclops's optic blast took out the ceiling guns in a single sweeping arc, Storm's lightning shorted the power grid, and Gambit and Wolverine came in low and fast, each from a different angle, while Rogue flew in from above, absorbing the Reavers' cybernetic enhancements temporarily and using those same systems against them.

It was over in four minutes.

On the Blackbird home, Wolverine sat next to Gambit and stared at the darkness outside the window for a long time.

"You're better in a team than you let on," Logan said finally. Not a compliment, exactly. An observation.

Remy smiled. "I'm a thief," he said. "We don't like people knowing how good we are."

Outside, the Mojave slid past below them, and ahead, the lights of the country they were flying home to glittered in the dark.
