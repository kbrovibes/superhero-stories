Hey — have you ever held a playing card in your fingers and wished it could do something absolutely incredible? Well, our hero can make it EXPLODE! But let's start at the very beginning.

His name is Remy LeBeau, and he grew up in New Orleans, Louisiana — a city full of jazz music drifting out of open windows, the smell of fresh beignets on every corner, and the wide brown Mississippi River rolling past like it owns the whole state. Remy had no mom or dad he could remember. Instead, he was raised by the Thieves Guild — a secret crew of super-skilled thieves who were VERY good at not being seen.

The Guild taught Remy all kinds of things. How to pick a lock — CLICK! How to move through a room in perfect silence — shhhhh. How to spin a bo staff so fast it goes WHOOOOSH and becomes a blur of wood and air. And how to smile charmingly at anyone, in English, in French, AND in Cajun — which is its own whole thing in Louisiana.

But Remy had a power the Guild could NEVER have taught him. When he picked up an ordinary object — a coin, a pebble, one playing card from a deck — and concentrated really hard, the thing began to GLOW. Pink. Purple. Crackling with energy, like a tiny thunderstorm in his hand. And when he let go — BOOM! — it exploded in a brilliant flash of kinetic energy! KRAKOOM!

His eyes glowed too — red irises, black around the edges — and sometimes that scared people. But Remy was kind. Actually, he was SO kind that he started secretly putting stolen things BACK into people's pockets. That's right. The Guild trained him to steal, and he used those skills to return things. Pretty sneaky — but in a GOOD way!

Then one morning, a man named Charles Xavier appeared in the French Quarter of New Orleans and told Remy the truth. His power was a mutant ability. And Xavier had a school in Westchester, New York — a school for people exactly like Remy, where nobody would be afraid of glowing eyes.

Remy packed a travel bag. He tucked his best deck of cards into his coat. He slung his bo staff over his shoulder. He got on a train heading NORTH.

At Xavier's school, he chose a name: GAMBIT. It's a chess word — it means a bold, brave opening move. And that is exactly what Remy LeBeau made.

The game was ON!
