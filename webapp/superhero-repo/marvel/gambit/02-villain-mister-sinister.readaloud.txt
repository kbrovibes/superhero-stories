Okay, are you ready for a scary — but exciting — story? Let me tell you about the creepiest villain Gambit has ever faced. His name is Mister Sinister, and just his NAME sounds like trouble, doesn't it?

Mister Sinister — real name Nathaniel Essex — is OLD. Like, very old. He has been scheming since the Victorian age, which was back when people wore top hats and had no TV. He wears black armor and has a red diamond on his forehead, and his face is as pale as a winter moon. His hobby? Collecting information about mutants. Which is NOT a nice hobby at all.

But here's the part that Gambit knows — and doesn't talk about much. A long time ago, before Remy joined the X-Men, Sinister found the young thief from New Orleans and made him a horrible bargain. Remy made a mistake. He has felt bad about it ever since.

So when the X-Men discovered that someone was capturing mutants in the Louisiana bayou — a dark, swampy forest full of cypress trees and Spanish moss — and brought them to a secret research facility for experiments, Remy took one look at the building and went very quiet.

He knew this place.

He told Cyclops and Storm everything. Then he got to work.

BZZZT! Cyclops fired his optic blast and knocked out Sinister's power systems. CRASH — BOOM! Storm called lightning from the sky and took out three robotic sentinels like they were tin cans in a thunderstorm!

And Gambit? Gambit moved like the thief he had been trained to be. Quiet as a cat. Reading every room before entering. Finding every prisoner — including a girl named Louisa who could create heat and had been locked away for three whole weeks.

Then Sinister appeared. Tall. Pale. Calm as if he were merely annoyed about a small inconvenience.

"You made a mistake coming back to Louisiana," Gambit said.

"Did I?" said Sinister, sounding bored.

Gambit flicked his wrist.

KRAKOOM! The whole lab exploded in light and energy!

Sinister vanished into his secret escape tunnel — because that's what Sinister does — but every prisoner was free. The facility was empty. Louisa was breathing clean bayou air for the first time in weeks.

Outside, under a wide sky full of Louisiana stars, Remy looked toward the warm orange glow of New Orleans on the horizon. His city. His home. The city he had grown up in, and that he would always protect.

Sinister was still out there. He always had an escape plan. But tonight, every single prisoner was free, and the good guys had won.

GAMBIT: 1. SINISTER: 0!
