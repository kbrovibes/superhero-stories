Alright, it's time for a heist story! But not the bad kind of heist — this is the kind where the HERO is doing the stealing, and he's stealing something BACK.

Let me set the scene!

It's Tuesday in October. The air smells like jazz music and the river. Gambit gets a phone call from a man named Etienne Marceaux — the Master of the Thieves Guild in New Orleans. And Etienne ONLY calls when things are serious.

Here's the situation: a rogue group of thieves called the Crimson Tide has stolen something from a university research lab on Tulane Avenue. A sealed container. Inside that container? A compound — a special chemical — that can TEMPORARILY take away mutant powers when put in a water supply. And the Crimson Tide is about to sell it to someone who wants to use it against the X-Men.

Remy LeBeau puts on his coat, grabs his bo staff, tucks his cards in his pocket, and gets on a plane to New Orleans. He does not tell Cyclops. He will apologize for that later. But this is HIS city and HIS history.

He walks the French Quarter as evening falls — warm streets, jazz drifting from open doorways, the smell of beignets. HOME.

Step one: find the Crimson Tide.

He locates the first member — a man called Thierry — in a GAMBLING HALL above a restaurant on Bourbon Street. Does Gambit fight him? No! He SITS DOWN, DEALS A HAND OF CARDS, and wins the whole game graciously while Thierry TELLS HIM EVERYTHING without even realizing he's doing it. That is world-class information gathering right there.

Step two: get the container.

The other three Crimson Tide members are guarding it in a warehouse in the Warehouse District. They are NOT interested in a polite card game.

Gambit goes through the SKYLIGHT.

CRACK — charged card, smoke and light! CRACK CRACK — two more, while everyone scrambles! He sweeps two guards off their feet with his bo staff — THWACK THWACK — and gets to the container before the third one even knows what direction he came from.

He takes the container.

He leaves each guard one playing card. An ordinary one. Uncharged. Just tucked into their collar.

So they know he was there. So close.

Step three: make it safe.

He brings the container straight to Beast — Professor Henry McCoy — at Xavier's mansion. Hank spends three days figuring out the compound and neutralizing it completely. Safe. Gone.

Then Remy calls Etienne.

"The Crimson Tide is out of the Guild," he says. "New Orleans is safe."

Etienne says, "You didn't have to go yourself."

Remy says, "Yes, I did."

Because New Orleans isn't just a place. It's his home. And protecting home isn't a job.

It's love.

HEIST COMPLETE! GAMBIT WINS!
