Before there were glowing pink playing cards, before there were explosions, before there was an X-Men uniform — there was a stick.

Okay, not just any stick. A REALLY good stick. But let's start at the beginning!

When Remy LeBeau was not yet ten years old, growing up in New Orleans with the Thieves Guild, his trainer — a man named Marcel — handed him a bo staff for the very first time. A bo staff is a smooth hardwood pole, about six feet long. No blade. No trigger. No flashing lights. Just a beautifully balanced piece of wood.

"Any fool can fire a gun," Marcel told young Remy. "A bo staff requires thought. It makes you better before it makes you dangerous."

Remy practiced. And practiced. He practiced sweeps — WHOOSH, low and fast, aimed at the ankles. He practiced spinning blocks — WHIRRRRR, the pole becoming a blur. He practiced vaults — planting the staff and flying over obstacles. For years, until the staff felt like a sixth finger. Part of him.

His staff was made by Celestine Arceneaux, a Guild artisan on Magazine Street in New Orleans. White ash wood, shaped on a lathe, finished with warm oil that kept it grippy even in rain. Near one end, she burned a small fleur-de-lis — the symbol of New Orleans — into the wood. Remy has carried it for years.

Now here's why the staff matters so much when you already have exploding playing cards:

Cards are GREAT from a distance. Throw, glow, BOOM! Perfect! But what about when someone is RIGHT in front of you? What about someone much stronger, like Sabretooth? You can't just wave cards around up close!

That's where the staff comes in. Its six-foot length means Gambit can REACH people without being close enough for them to grab him. Swing it in a big arc and suddenly everyone nearby has to step back. Use it as a lever. Use it as a pole to jump OVER someone entirely. The staff and the cards together make Gambit one of the most adaptable fighters anyone has ever faced.

And his favorite move with the staff? The sweep.

Low. Fast. Perfectly timed. Aimed at the feet right when your opponent's weight pushes forward.

He used it on Sabretooth in Audubon Park. Creed hit the dirt like a falling tree!

He used it on four Weapon X soldiers in a corridor in Manitoba. THUD. THUD. THUD. THUD.

He used it on Wolverine once, during a training session.

Wolverine says it was a slip.

Nobody else says it was a slip.

WHOOOOSH! THWACK! That's the sound of a good stick in the right hands!
