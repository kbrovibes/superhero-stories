Now here is a very unusual story, because this time, Gambit cannot throw a single card and cannot swing his staff even once. This time, the battle happens somewhere else entirely.

Ready? Let's go.

The Shadow King is not a person you can punch. He is not a robot you can zap. His real name is Amahl Farouk, and he exists in the psychic realm — the invisible world of thoughts and minds — creeping between people's feelings like a shadow under a door. He can slide into your mind and take control of it, quietly, without anyone noticing until it's too late.

And one autumn evening, he crept into Xavier's mansion in Westchester, New York, and started turning off the X-Men one by one.

Storm stood in the garden and stared at the sky. Not in a "wow, pretty clouds" way. In a blank, empty, nobody-home way. Wolverine sat at the kitchen table, turning a fork over and over and over and saying absolutely nothing. Wolverine. NOTHING. That is how you know something is very wrong.

Gambit noticed. Because a thief trained to read rooms can feel when a room has changed.

But what could he DO? He charged a card — and who was he going to throw it at? The air? You can't throw a card at a psychic shadow. He raised his bo staff — and who was he going to sweep? A feeling?

Remy put his weapons down.

He sat cross-legged in the center of the mansion's main hall. He closed his eyes.

And he FELT things. On purpose. As hard as he could.

He thought about New Orleans. The river. The jazz music. The beignets. The smell of rain on warm cobblestones. He thought about Beau Mireaux, safe in the park. He thought about Louisa, lifting her face to the stars. He thought about Rogue — and that feeling was BIG and warm and fierce and wonderful and complicated in the best possible way.

BOOOM.

Not a card explosion. Something bigger. Something like love and loyalty and home all pressing outward from inside him at once.

The Shadow King SHRIEKED — not out loud, but right inside Remy's skull, like a glass breaking — and the pressure rushed OUT of the mansion, room by room, corridor by corridor.

Storm blinked. Wolverine put down the fork. Jean Grey came sprinting from upstairs, eyes clear and wide.

"Someone just shook the psychic plane like a TAMBOURINE!" she said. "Was that YOU?!"

Remy opened his eyes. He looked mildly pleased.

"I felt things at him," he said. "Seemed rude to hold back."

THAT is how Gambit fights a villain he can't even see.

With his whole, warm, completely unstoppable heart!
