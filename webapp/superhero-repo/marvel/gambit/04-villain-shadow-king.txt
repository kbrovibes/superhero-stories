The Shadow King was not like any enemy Gambit had faced. He was not a man with claws or a scientist in a lab. The Shadow King — Amahl Farouk — existed in the psychic realm, a dark presence that could slip into people's minds and control them. He had fought Charles Xavier himself, in a battle fought entirely inside the mind. He had survived. When he targeted the X-Men at their mansion in Westchester, he did not announce himself with explosions. He was already inside before anyone knew he had arrived.

Gambit was the first to notice something wrong, because a thief trained in silence knows when a room feels different. The other X-Men had gone quiet in a wrong way — not peaceful, but distant. Storm stood in the garden and stared at the sky without moving. Wolverine sat in the kitchen and turned a fork over and over in his hand, saying nothing. Their eyes had gone flat, like the light inside them had been dimmed.

Remy could not reach the Shadow King with charged cards. He could not sweep him with a bo staff. The Shadow King had no body, no collar to press a glowing ace of spades against. He existed between thoughts.

But Remy had one advantage. Jean Grey had once told him that strong emotions — sharp, concentrated feeling — could create turbulence in the psychic realm, the way a stone thrown into still water makes rings spread outward. And Remy LeBeau, born in New Orleans, raised on music and heat and loyalty, was a person who felt things very deeply.

He sat down in the center of the mansion's main hall and closed his eyes. He thought about New Orleans — the river, the oak trees, a trumpet drifting through the Quarter at dusk. He thought about Beau Mireaux's face when Sabretooth walked away. He thought about Louisa lifting her face to the bayou stars. He thought, with great care, about Rogue — that warm, complicated, fierce feeling pulsing through him like a charged card on the verge of going off.

The Shadow King shrieked.

Not out loud. But Remy felt the shriek inside his skull, like a mirror cracking, and then the pressure left the mansion room by room. Storm blinked. Wolverine put down the fork. Jean Grey came running from the upper hall, eyes clear, and said, "Someone just shook the psychic plane like a tambourine — was that you?"

Remy opened his eyes. "I felt things at it. Seemed rude to hold back."

The Shadow King had retreated — driven off not by force, but by the one thing he could not consume: someone who knew exactly who he was and cared fiercely about the people around him.
