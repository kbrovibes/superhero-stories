Okay, I need you to picture a big park in New Orleans — ancient oak trees dripping with Spanish moss, the wide Mississippi River just beyond the levee, and the smell of warm grass in the air. Beautiful, right?

Now put a SNARLING, CLAWED VILLAIN in it.

His name is Sabretooth — real name Victor Creed — and he is big, mean, and not at all interested in nice conversations. He has retractable claws like knives, superhuman strength, and he heals so fast that he almost never stays hurt. He has been fighting Wolverine for decades. He is, in short, not someone you want to run into in a park.

But a seventeen-year-old courier named Beau Mireaux from the Thieves Guild DID run into him — and Sabretooth had him cornered against a wall of ancient tree roots, claws out, grinning in that unpleasant way he has.

And THAT is when Gambit showed up.

"Put him down, Creed," Remy said, pulling his bo staff off his back and settling it firmly in both hands. "He's a kid."

Sabretooth grinned wider. And LUNGED.

WHOOSH — the bo staff swept up to block! CRACK — a charged card hit the ground between them in a puff of smoke! CRUNCH — Sabretooth's claws raked right through an oak tree that Gambit had already spun away from! Those were DEEP scratches in that tree, people!

The fight moved through the park like a storm. Sabretooth was stronger. Faster. He healed every scratch in seconds. But Remy — Remy knew these streets like the back of his hand. He knew which roots to leap over and which branches hung just right and exactly how much space was between every bench and path.

He charged five cards at once. CRACK CRACK CRACK CRACK KRAKOOM! A trail of explosions peppered the ground around Sabretooth like tiny fireworks — smoke and light everywhere!

Creed roared. And charged again.

Remy WAITED. He let Creed's big body carry him exactly two steps too far — and SWEPT his bo staff low in a wide arc!

BOOM! Two hundred and sixty pounds of angry mutant hit the dirt.

Remy crouched beside him and pressed one glowing ace of spades against the back of Creed's collar. It pulsed pink-purple. It crackled.

"You want to finish this," Gambit said, calm as a Sunday morning, "or you want to walk away?"

Sabretooth growled.

Then he walked away.

Beau Mireaux kept his envelope. The park went quiet again, the Spanish moss swaying gently in the river breeze.

Gambit put away his cards. He rolled his shoulders. He looked at young Beau and said, "You okay?"

Beau nodded, eyes wide as dinner plates.

"Good," said Remy. "Let's go get beignets."

THAT is how a hero handles Sabretooth!
