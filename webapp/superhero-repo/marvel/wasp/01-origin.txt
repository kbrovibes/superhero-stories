Hope van Dyne grew up in a beautiful old house in San Francisco that always felt a little too quiet. Her mother, Janet van Dyne, had been the first Wasp — a brilliant scientist and Avenger who flew on tiny wings beside her husband, Dr. Hank Pym. When Hope was only seven years old, Janet went on a dangerous mission to stop a Soviet missile. To save millions of lives, she had to shrink smaller, and smaller, and smaller — past the subatomic barrier, into a strange place called the Quantum Realm. Janet did not come back.

For years, Hope and her father lived under that silence. Hank Pym was a serious man with kind blue eyes and a heavy heart, and he poured himself into his work at Pym Technologies. Hope learned to read in his laboratory, learned to ride a bike on the lawn at the Pym estate, and learned, very early, that her father loved her even when he could not find the right words to say so. She studied harder than anyone in her class. She studied to be near him.

By the time Hope was a young woman, she had earned degrees in physics and business, and she had grown into a sharp, precise, formidable person. She knew the Pym Particle research better than anyone alive except her father. She knew the design of the original Wasp suit. She knew exactly how the wings unfolded and exactly how the bio-electric stingers were calibrated. What she did not know was why Hank refused to let her wear it.

The answer was love. Hank had already lost Janet to the science. He could not bear the thought of losing Hope too. So he kept the Wasp suit locked away in a vault behind a fingerprint scanner, and he asked an unassuming ex-thief named Scott Lang to wear the smaller Ant-Man suit instead. Hope was furious. She trained Scott herself, drilled him in combat, and watched him stumble through skills she had mastered as a teenager.

But Hank was not just being stubborn. He was being a father. And in time, after Scott proved himself an Avenger, Hank walked Hope to a quiet workshop, set a sleek red-and-gold helmet on a table, and said the words she had waited her whole life to hear. "I have something for you."
