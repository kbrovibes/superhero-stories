The first time Hope van Dyne met Scott Lang, she did not like him very much. He was a goofy, tall, ex-thief her father had hand-picked to wear the Ant-Man suit instead of HER. He fumbled his shrinking. He cracked jokes when she was trying to be serious. He once shrank himself by accident in his bathtub. Hope, who had trained since childhood and knew the Pym Particle research inside and out, did not understand why Hank Pym had chosen Scott. It took her a long time to figure out the answer.

Scott was the partner she needed, not the partner she expected.

What Hope eventually discovered, after years of working with Scott as Ant-Man, is that they balance each other in ways neither of them would have predicted. Hope is the technical lead — she does the calibrations, designs the gear, plans the missions, leads the science. Scott is the heart of the partnership — he keeps her laughing, he reminds her to eat, and he brings a kind of unpredictable, scrappy creativity that Hope's careful brain would never have invented on its own. When Hope says, "We need a way into the building," Scott will say, "What if we shrink a guy and put him in a tic-tac box?" And then, somehow, that will be the answer.

In combat, they move like a single creature. Hope dominates the air with her wings and stingers. Scott dominates the ground with the ant army he commands through the cybernetic helmet — carpenter ants for flight, fire ants for swarming, crazy ants for shorting out electronics. Hope provides the precision; Scott provides the chaos. Hope hits one target perfectly; Scott hits ten targets messily. Bad guys cannot defend against both at once. They have to choose. They almost always choose wrong.

Hope still does most of the technical work. Scott knows this and is the first to admit it. The Pym disc Scott uses to shrink came from Hope's lab. The helmet Scott uses to talk to ants was upgraded by Hope. The reading on the dial Scott is supposed to remember is one Hope has reminded him of approximately a thousand times. ("Is it red to shrink or blue to shrink?" "RED, Scott. RED to shrink. We have had this conversation since 2015.") Scott takes the teasing with a grin. He knows he is lucky.

The partnership matters in another way too. Scott has a daughter, Cassie, who looks up to Hope as a mentor. Hope, who lost her own mother for thirty years, takes that responsibility seriously. She teaches Cassie the Pym Particle math, helps her build her first suit, and tells her not to be afraid to be the smartest person in the room. Together, Hope and Scott and Cassie form an extended family that defends the world a little smaller — and a little tighter — than anyone else.
