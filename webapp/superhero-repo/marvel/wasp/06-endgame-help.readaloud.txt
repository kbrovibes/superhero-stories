Have you ever waited and waited and WAITED to come home? This is one of those stories. This is the story of the time Hope van Dyne was gone — really gone — for five whole years, and how she made it back just in time for the biggest fight in history.

It started with something terrible called the Snap. Thanos — a giant purple villain — gathered all six Infinity Stones and snapped his fingers. SNAP. And in that single moment, HALF of every living thing in the entire universe simply... vanished. POOF. Like dust on the wind.

Hope vanished. Hank Pym vanished. Janet van Dyne — who had only just come HOME from the Quantum Realm! — vanished too. The whole Pym family was gone in a heartbeat.

Scott Lang did not vanish. But that was almost worse. Scott was inside the Quantum Realm at the exact second of the Snap. From his point of view, only about five hours went by. But on Earth, the clock kept ticking. Five whole years went by without Hope. Five years.

And here is the funny part — the part you would never guess.

Scott got OUT of the Quantum Realm by accident. He was trapped inside a deactivated tunnel at the back of an old van in a dusty warehouse. And one day a HUNGRY RAT scampered across the controls and stepped on exactly the right buttons — chomp, sniff, click — and the tunnel turned back on. Scott popped out, dizzy and confused, and stumbled into a world he did not recognize.

He ran to the Avengers. And he had a wild, brilliant idea. What if the Quantum Realm — the place where time gets all twisty — could be used as a TIME TUNNEL? What if they could go BACK to the past and collect the Infinity Stones BEFORE Thanos got them? Then they could undo the Snap and bring everyone home.

Tony Stark looked at the math. He thought about it. He shook his head. Then he thought about his little daughter. And then he said, "Let's do it."

For weeks, Tony and Bruce Banner — the gentle green giant — built a brand-new Quantum Tunnel big enough to send the whole Avengers team back through time. They called the mission the TIME HEIST. They scattered to different moments — past battles, past worlds, past their own younger selves — and they collected all six Infinity Stones.

Then Bruce, brave Bruce, put on a borrowed gauntlet, and SNAP — he undid the disappearance. The Stones glowed. The Quantum Tunnel hummed. And all across the universe, the missing half of everything came back.

Hope van Dyne came back too.

She blinked back into the world standing exactly where she had been the day she vanished — at the controls of her father's portable laboratory, right next to a quiet pier in San Francisco. The fog rolled. Seagulls cried. Five years had passed in the blink of her eye. Her parents were standing next to her, blinking just as hard. They turned to each other in disbelief.

"What just happened?" Janet asked.

But there was no time to explain. A distress signal was blinking on the Pym Tech screens. The Avengers were under attack at their old compound, and Thanos — a younger, angrier Thanos pulled in from the past — was about to attack with his whole alien army.

Hope did not hesitate. She suited up — wings unfolding, stingers humming. Janet pulled on her own Wasp suit. Hank shrank the entire laboratory into a suitcase, threw it in the back of the van, and drove. The Pym family flew across the country and arrived in the middle of the largest superhero army the world has ever seen.

There were portals open in the sky. There were wizards and aliens and Wakandans and warriors pouring through them. And there, weaving through the sky together, were two Wasps — Hope on the left, Janet on the right. Mother and daughter. The OLD Wasp and the NEW Wasp. Both of their stinger gauntlets were glowing. Both of their wings were a humming blur. They flew in perfect formation, weaving through falling debris, zapping enemies out of the sky one after another. ZAP! ZAP! ZAP!

Far below, Scott Lang — back in his Giant-Man size, towering like a skyscraper — was smashing alien war machines into the ground with two enormous fists. WHAM! BOOM! The Pym family had come back from beyond worlds, beyond time, beyond the Snap itself.

And there was NO way they were going to lose now.
