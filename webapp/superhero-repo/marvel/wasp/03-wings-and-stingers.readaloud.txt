Okay, settle in — because Hope van Dyne is about to take FLIGHT, and you are not going to want to miss it. The Wasp suit has two secret weapons that nobody else in the Avengers has. Are you ready? Because we are about to count them off!

Secret weapon number one: WINGS. Real, working, dragonfly-thin wings. The first time Hope tried the new Wasp suit, her father pushed a little button on the back of the shoulder — and FWUMP — two pairs of clear, shimmering wings unfolded out of the suit. Like a hummingbird. Like a dragonfly. And then Hope lifted right off the floor of the workshop. BRRRRZZZZ — they hum so fast you cannot even see them blur. She did not even need a running start. She just THOUGHT about flying, and up she went.

Compare that to Scott Lang. Scott — bless his heart — has no wings on his Ant-Man suit. None! He has to find a flying carpenter ant named Ant-thony, climb onto its back, and hold on for dear life. Hope just flies. Whoosh!

Secret weapon number two: STINGERS. Built right into the wrists of the Wasp suit are two bio-electric blasters that Hope designed herself. Press the button, and YELLOW LIGHTNING zips out of her wrist. ZAP! ZAP! She can use them at full size, like a regular soldier with a blaster, OR she can shrink down to ant size and zap a tiny target precisely. ZIP — a security camera shorts out. ZAP — a guard gets a static-shock and slumps over for a nap. ZWWWWAP — a steel door gets a perfect circle blasted right through it. Scott does not have stingers. Hope DESIGNED them.

But none of this gear would work without years and years of training. Hope did not become the Wasp by accident. Since she was little, she studied martial arts. Boxing — bonk! Jiu-jitsu — flip! Escrima with little sticks — clack-clack-clack! At night, when nobody was watching, she would sneak into the Pym Tech parking garage and practice shrinking and growing over and over, like a metronome — click, small; click, big; click, small. She trained until her shrinking was as precise as a dancer's step.

So when she finally put on the new red-and-gold Wasp suit, all those years came together. Her combat style is something brand new. She hovers up by the ceiling. ZIP — she drops behind a thug. SHRINK — she dodges his swing by going thumb-sized. GROW — she pops back to full size with her elbow at the back of his head. BONK! Fight over. Sometimes she will shrink her whole body but keep her fist FULL-SIZED for just a half-second — and land a giant punch that knocks a goon flying. WHAM!

Scott has a nickname for it. He calls it her "tactical music." He once told her she is the only person in the world who can do calculus while throwing a kick. Hope smiled a little, but she didn't argue.

Once she flew through a whole warehouse full of bad guys to grab a stolen Pym disc — never touched the ground, never broke a sweat. She came home with the disc, a bruised elbow, and her father waiting at the kitchen table with a hot cup of tea.

Hank looked at his daughter, in her sleek red-and-gold suit, with the wings still folded along her back, and he said something he had waited a lifetime to say.

"Your mother would be very proud."

Hope didn't say anything. She didn't have to. Some things are bigger than words.
