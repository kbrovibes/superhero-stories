Hold on tight, because this one is BIG. This is the story of the time the whole Pym family fell into the Quantum Realm and went face-to-face with one of the most dangerous villains in the whole Marvel multiverse. His name is Kang the Conqueror.

But first — a secret.

Hope did NOT know this for a long time, but Janet had a secret from her thirty years down in the Quantum Realm. While she was lost down there, Janet had met a stranded warrior named Kang. Kang was a soldier from a far-future timeline — exiled to the Quantum Realm after losing a war against, of all things, OTHER VERSIONS of himself. (The multiverse is weird. We will explain another day.)

At first, Janet thought Kang was just another stranded survivor. He was kind to her. He was clever. He needed help repairing his crashed ship. Janet, being a brilliant scientist, helped him. But as she worked on his ship, she started to notice things. She found logs of his old battles. She saw what he had done to other worlds. She realized — way too late — that if Kang ever got OUT of the Quantum Realm, he would burn galaxies. He would conquer everything.

So she did the bravest, hardest thing she could. She SABOTAGED Kang's ship. She tore out the power core. She stranded him forever in the Quantum Realm. Then she escaped home... and kept the whole thing a secret. Not even Hope knew.

Until one day, a few years after Janet had come home, Cassie Lang — Scott's daughter — was running an experiment in Scott's basement. She had built a little device to send a signal INTO the Quantum Realm to map it. Janet saw the device. Janet went WHITE. "Turn it off!" she shouted. "Don't send anything down there!"

But it was too late. The signal had gone out. And something had answered.

WHOOOSH! The whole family — Scott, Cassie, Hope, Hank, and Janet — was sucked DOWN into the Quantum Realm. SHRINK! SHRINK! SHRINK! Past the subatomic barrier. Through the swirling blue-and-purple light. And into a world they had never imagined.

The Quantum Realm was full of strange, beautiful things. There were underground cities with crystal towers. There were rivers that flowed UP instead of DOWN. There were people with neon-blue skin and floating heads with no bodies, and friendly creatures the size of small dogs that scampered along on six legs. The whole place glowed.

But Janet knew exactly where they had landed. They were inside Kang's territory. The Conqueror had spent years rebuilding. He had built an army. He had built a throne. He had built a fortress shaped like a giant spiked crown. And he was searching, desperately, for one missing piece — the power core Janet had hidden — that would let him escape back to the regular universe.

The Pym family fought back. Janet led them through hidden tunnels she had memorized decades ago. They met freedom fighters who had been resisting Kang for years. Hank Pym, of all people, rode into battle on the back of an intelligent giant ant. Cassie discovered that with the right Pym disc, SHE could grow into a giant too, and she stomped through the battlefield as a hero in her own right. Scott shrank down to nothing and slipped past Kang's guards.

And in the air? Hope and Janet flew side by side. Two Wasps. Wings humming. Stingers flashing. Hope's yellow-gold. Janet's bluish-white. They wove through the glowing blue caves of Kang's throne room, dodging bursts of energy, zapping his soldiers out of the sky.

In the end, the Pym family did the impossible. Scott shrank himself smaller than ever before — past the smallest possible point — and snuck into the heart of Kang's power core. He tore it apart from the inside. CRACK! BOOM! Hope and Janet pinned Kang himself with a perfectly coordinated stinger volley — yellow lightning and bluish lightning crisscrossing right onto his armor. ZAP-ZAP! He stumbled back. He fell off his throne.

The family climbed home through a brand-new Quantum Tunnel they built with the help of the Quantum freedom fighters. They were exhausted. They were bruised. Hope's wings were dented. Scott's helmet was cracked. They were all very, very glad to see the fog of San Francisco again.

But Hope had learned something she would carry forever. Her mother was not a saint. Her mother had made hard, secret choices down there in the Quantum Realm — including stranding a man, even a dangerous man, to save the universe. And now her mother had trusted Hope with that secret. That was the moment Hope stopped being only Janet's daughter. From then on, she was Janet's full PARTNER — equals, side by side, two Wasps holding up the family together.
