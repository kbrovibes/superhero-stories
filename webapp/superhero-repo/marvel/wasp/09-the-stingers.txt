If you want to understand what makes Hope van Dyne unique among the Avengers, you have to study her stingers. The bio-electric blasters built into the wrists of the Wasp suit are her own invention, designed during her years running the Pym Tech engineering lab. Other heroes have repulsors, hammers, shields, magic. Hope has stingers — focused arcs of yellow electricity that can be fired full size, ant size, or anywhere in between. Even her mother Janet's original suit had stingers, but Hope's are the next generation, smaller, faster, and twice as accurate.

The stingers fire from emitter rings on the gauntlets. A short burst is non-lethal — enough to stun a guard, short a security system, or knock the gun out of a thug's hand. A sustained burst can punch through a steel door or melt a lock. Hope can also rapid-fire, sending out three or four pulses in a second, weaving them through a moving crowd like a sharpshooter. The energy comes from a microbattery in the suit that recharges from the same Pym Particle reaction that powers the wings.

The other half of the Wasp gear is, of course, the wings themselves. Two pairs of thin, clear panels fold out of housings on Hope's back, vibrating fast enough to hum like a chord. They give her flight even at full human size — something Scott Lang's Ant-Man suit cannot do. To go anywhere, Scott has to ride on the back of a flying carpenter ant named Ant-thony. Hope just lifts off. The wings let her hover, dart, change direction in midair, and dive on a target at speeds the ant-riders can only dream about.

But the real secret of Hope's combat style is the way she combines everything. She shrinks, she grows, she flies, she stings, all in patterns Scott has never quite been able to match. She can shrink to thimble size, dart between two attackers, grow at full size in the gap between them, land an elbow on one and a stinger on the other, and shrink again before either of them hits the floor. She can fire a stinger from ant size — a yellow zip of lightning out of a body too small to see — and the bolt hits with the same precision as if it had come from a full-size gauntlet.

When new Avengers train with Hope for the first time, they almost always make the same mistake. They watch her wings and forget about her stingers. They watch her stingers and forget about her size. By the time they have figured out which Hope to watch, she has already won. The Wasp is small, fast, and beautiful in flight — but the secret of her power is that she is the most precise fighter on the team.
