Okay, gather close — because this is one of the happiest stories Hope van Dyne ever lived through. This is the story of what happened AFTER Janet came home. This is the story of two Wasps in one house.

The first months were a little bit funny, actually. Janet van Dyne had been gone for THIRTY YEARS. THIRTY! Think about that. She had never seen a smartphone. She had never sent a text message. She had never even HEARD of the internet. The first time Hope handed her mom a phone, Janet held it the way you hold a tiny mysterious lobster — carefully, with two fingers, just in case it bit. Hope laughed so hard she almost cried.

"Mom," Hope said, "you tap it. With your finger. Just tap it."

"This is what people DO now?"

"Yes."

"All day?"

"...mostly, yes."

Janet shook her head. She had a lot to learn.

So Hope became her mother's teacher. Every morning at breakfast, they would sit at the big sunny table in the kitchen. Hope would explain emojis. Hope would explain Wi-Fi. Hope would explain that peanut butter now came in twelve different flavors. And in return, Janet would explain what she had figured out during her thirty years in the Quantum Realm. She had learned things no living scientist on Earth knew. She had walked across landscapes that existed before atoms. She had befriended strange glowing creatures who spoke only in patterns of light. Hope listened with her mouth open. They both filled notebooks. They learned each other.

It did not take long before they started working TOGETHER. Hank Pym went down to the basement of Pym Tech and pulled Janet's original Wasp suit out of storage. It was the one she had worn back in the early days of the Avengers, all those decades ago — a faded red-and-black uniform with thin glassy wings and a sleek round helmet. Hank gently fixed up the cracks. He restored the wings. He recharged the stingers. And when Janet finally stepped into the workshop in her old suit, with her wings folded along her back, Hope's breath caught right in her throat.

Two Wasps. One old. One new. Mother and daughter. Both of them glowing in the same workshop light. It was one of those moments Hope knew she would remember for the rest of her life.

They made an INCREDIBLE team. Janet led the science. Hope led the tactics. When they fought side by side, Janet would set the rhythm — calm and steady and almost graceful — and Hope would zip in and punch the holes. Janet's stingers glowed BLUISH-WHITE. Hope's stingers glowed BRIGHT YELLOW. The two colors made a beautiful pattern in the air when they worked together. Bad guys did not know which Wasp to look at. They got confused. They lost.

One time, a bunch of arms dealers stole a Pym disc from a shipping yard. Hope and Janet flew over together. Janet shrank through a keyhole — ZIP! Hope flew through a vent — ZOOM! They met in the middle of the warehouse. Janet zapped the guard on the left. ZAP! Hope zapped the guard on the right. ZAP! They cleared the whole place in two minutes flat. The arms dealers never even saw what hit them. The Pym disc went home in Hope's belt pouch.

But honestly? The missions weren't even the best part.

The BEST part was the small things.

Walking through the garden together in the morning, while Janet sniffed every flower she had not smelled in thirty years. Cooking pasta together on a rainy night, with Janet trying to explain that the Quantum Realm has NOTHING like spaghetti. Janet brushing Hope's hair on a Sunday afternoon — something she had not been able to do since Hope was seven years old.

For thirty long years, the house in San Francisco had been a little too quiet. There had been a missing voice at the dinner table. There had been an empty chair in the garden.

Now there were two voices and two chairs and two Wasps and one very happy father, finally — FINALLY — sitting in his kitchen with his whole family back in it. And Hope, who had spent her whole life waiting, smiled the kind of smile that only comes when something you waited for forever turns out to be real.
