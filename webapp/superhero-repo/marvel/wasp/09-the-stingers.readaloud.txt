Time to get into the GEAR. Buckle up, because Hope van Dyne's equipment is some of the COOLEST in the whole Marvel universe. Let's break it down piece by piece.

First, the stingers. Oh, the STINGERS. Have you ever seen lightning shoot out of someone's wrist? Because that is what a stinger looks like. ZAP! ZWWWAP! ZAP!

The stingers are bio-electric blasters built right into the wrists of the Wasp suit. They were designed by Hope herself — she invented them in her Pym Tech engineering lab. Each gauntlet has a little emitter ring around the wrist. When Hope flicks her thumb against her palm, the emitter ring lights up YELLOW and shoots a burst of focused electricity. ZAP! And the burst can do all KINDS of things depending on how Hope sets it.

A short burst is non-lethal — it just stuns. Perfect for knocking out a guard, or shorting out a security camera, or popping a gun out of a thug's hand. ZAP — guard down. ZAP — camera off. ZAP — gun goes flying.

A sustained burst, where Hope holds the stinger on a target for a couple of seconds, can melt a metal lock or punch a clean smoking hole right through a steel door. ZWWWWWAP! Take THAT, locked door!

And there is a third mode — rapid-fire. Hope can shoot three or four stingers per second, weaving the pulses through a moving crowd like a sharpshooter. Some bad guys try to run. They do not get far. ZAP-ZAP-ZAP-ZAP — and they are all on the floor.

The stingers don't run on bullets or batteries you can buy at the store. They run on a special microbattery powered by the same Pym Particle reaction that runs the wings. Brilliant! Engineering at its finest.

Speaking of WINGS — let's talk about those, because they are the OTHER half of what makes the Wasp special.

Two pairs of clear, thin, glassy wings fold out of little housings on Hope's back. When Hope wants to fly, she presses a button and FWUMP — the wings unfold. Then they start to vibrate, faster and faster and faster, until they hum a soft chord — BRRRZZZZ — and Hope rises straight off the ground.

Now, here is the important thing — Scott Lang's Ant-Man suit does NOT have wings. Scott has to ride on the back of a flying carpenter ant named Ant-thony. Hope just LIFTS OFF. Imagine if you had to wait for your friend to find a flying bug every time you wanted to take to the air. Sorry, Scott. Hope wins this round.

Her wings let her hover, dart sideways in midair, dive down on a target at top speed, and change direction in a heartbeat. Hummingbirds wish they were as agile as the Wasp.

But here is the REAL secret. The thing nobody figures out about Hope until it is too late. She combines EVERYTHING. The wings. The stingers. The shrinking. The growing. All at once.

She'll shrink down to thumb-sized — POP — dart between two thugs, GROW back to full size in the gap between them — BOP — elbow on one — BOP — stinger on the other — ZAP — and shrink again before either of them hits the floor. She can fire a stinger from ANT size. A tiny yellow lightning bolt from a tiny tiny dot, zipping across a room with the same precision as a full-sized gauntlet. Wild!

New Avengers train with Hope sometimes. They always make the same mistake. They watch her wings and forget about her stingers. Then they watch her stingers and forget about her size. By the time they figure out which Hope to look at — the flying one, the shrinking one, or the zapping one — she has already won the spar. Every. Single. Time.

The Wasp is small. The Wasp is fast. The Wasp is beautiful in the air. But the secret of her power isn't any of those. The secret is that she is the most PRECISE fighter on the entire Avengers team.

Stay sharp like the Wasp, friends. Bring it all together.
