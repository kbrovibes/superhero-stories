Have you ever waited a really, really long time for something? Like waiting for your birthday, or waiting for a letter, or waiting for someone you love to come home? Hope van Dyne knows ALL about waiting. Her story starts with a quiet house in San Francisco, and a mother who flew away on tiny wings.

Hope's mom was named Janet van Dyne. And she was not an ordinary mom. Janet was a brilliant scientist AND a founding Avenger. She was the very first WASP — buzzing through the sky in a red-and-black flying suit, fighting alongside her husband, Dr. Hank Pym. They were a team. They were heroes.

But one day, when little Hope was only SEVEN years old, something terrible happened. An enemy fired a giant missile at the United States. There was no time to stop it from the outside. So Janet did the bravest, scariest thing imaginable. She shrank herself down — smaller, and smaller, and smaller — right through the missile's metal shell. Tinier than an ant. Tinier than a speck of dust. Tinier than an ATOM. And she disabled the missile from the inside. ZAP! Saved!

But shrinking that small comes with a terrible cost. Janet shrank past something scientists call the subatomic barrier, into a strange place called the Quantum Realm. And she did not come back.

Hope's dad, Hank, was heartbroken. He buried himself in his laboratory at Pym Technologies. He worked all day and most of the night. He loved Hope very, very much — but he was so sad he could not always find the right words. So Hope grew up in a quiet house, with a quiet father, and a missing mother.

She got SMART. Whoosh, very smart. She earned a degree in physics. She earned another in business. She learned EVERYTHING about her parents' Pym Particle research. She knew the original Wasp suit inside and out — the wings, the electric stingers, the shrinking dials. There was just one little problem.

Her father wouldn't let her wear it.

Hope was FURIOUS. She marched into Hank's lab. She glared at him. She demanded answers. And then Hank brought in a goofy ex-thief named Scott Lang, and gave HIM the Ant-Man suit instead. Hope rolled her eyes so hard you could practically hear them. SHE was the one who should be a hero. SHE knew the science. SHE had earned it.

But Hank wasn't being mean. Hank was scared. He had already lost Janet to the Pym Particles. He could not bear the thought of losing Hope too. That was the secret behind his stubborn rules — a father's love, wrapped up in worry.

Hope trained Scott anyway. She drilled him in combat. She knocked him onto the mat — bonk! — over and over until he learned. And after Scott proved himself a hero with the Avengers, Hank finally led Hope into a quiet workshop. He set a sleek red-and-gold helmet on the table. The wings unfolded with a soft hum. The stingers glowed.

"Hope," he said. "I have something for you."

And just like THAT, after a lifetime of waiting, the new Wasp was born.
