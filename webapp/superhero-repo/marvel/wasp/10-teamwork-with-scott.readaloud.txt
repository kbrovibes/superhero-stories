Last story. Best story, maybe. This is the one about Hope and Scott — about the funny way two completely different people became one of the best teams in the entire Avengers.

The first time Hope van Dyne met Scott Lang, she did NOT like him. Like, at all. Hope had trained her whole life to be the next Wasp. She knew the Pym Particles. She knew the engineering. She had practiced her shrinking in the parking garage at midnight until her timing was perfect.

And then her father went and gave the suit — the Ant-Man suit, the smaller one — to some goofy, tall, ex-thief who could not even keep his dials straight. Scott Lang. The first time Hope watched Scott try to shrink, he overshot the dial, popped down to the size of a thimble in the wrong direction, and somehow ended up stuck inside his own bathtub. Bonk. The water drain almost ate him.

Hope rolled her eyes so hard she practically pulled a muscle.

"He's the new Ant-Man?" she said to her father, deadpan. "That guy?"

"Yes," Hank said.

"Why?"

And Hank, in his quiet way, said, "Because he is the right kind of person for the job. Not the smartest. The right kind."

Hope did not get it for a long time. She trained Scott herself. She drilled him in combat. She knocked him onto the mat, bonk, over and over. She corrected him when he got the colors wrong on the Pym discs. ("Red to shrink, Scott. Red. R-E-D. The color of a stop sign.") Scott took the teasing with a grin. He kept coming back. He kept trying.

And eventually, slowly, Hope started to figure out what her dad had seen.

Scott was the partner she NEEDED, not the partner she had expected.

See, Hope is the technical genius. She runs the calibrations. She designs the gear. She plans the missions. She leads the science. Without Hope, the gear does not work. Without Hope, the math does not add up.

But Scott? Scott is the HEART of the partnership. He cracks jokes when Hope is too serious. He brings her snacks when she has worked for sixteen hours. And — and this is the secret — Scott is incredibly creative in a way Hope's careful brain would never invent on its own.

Hope will say, "We need a way into the building."

Scott will say, "What if we shrink a guy and put him in a Tic Tac box?"

And then, somehow, that will be the answer.

In a fight, they are unstoppable. Hope owns the air — wings humming, stingers flashing, zipping in tight little patterns above the battlefield. Scott owns the ground — leading a whole army of ants! Carpenter ants for flying transport! Fire ants for swarming attacks! Crazy ants for shorting out electronics! Hope is precision. Scott is chaos. Hope picks one target perfectly. Scott swarms ten targets messily. Bad guys cannot decide which one to defend against. They almost always choose wrong.

But here is the part Hope still teases Scott about. She does most of the technical work. Scott is the first to admit it. The Pym disc he uses to shrink came from her lab. The helmet he uses to talk to ants was upgraded by her. And the colors of the dial?

Hope, deadpan: "Red to shrink, Scott."

Scott, every single time: "Right, right. Red to shrink. Got it."

Hope: "Or is it blue?"

Scott: "Wait, blue?"

Hope: "RED, SCOTT. RED. We have had this conversation since 2015."

He grins. He knows he is lucky.

And the very best part of this team is the third member of it — Cassie Lang. Scott's daughter. A brilliant, fierce kid who looks up to Hope as a mentor and a hero. Hope, whose own mother was lost when she was small, takes that very seriously. She teaches Cassie the Pym Particle math. She helps her build her first suit. She tells her not to be afraid to be the smartest person in the room.

Together — Hope and Scott and Cassie — they make a tiny, tight extended family. The world has Avengers who can move planets. The world has Avengers who can summon thunder. The world also has the Pyms and the Langs, who can shrink to the size of a thimble, ride an ant, fire a stinger, and laugh at each other's jokes in the kitchen on a Saturday morning.

And honestly? That family might be the best Avengers team of them all.
