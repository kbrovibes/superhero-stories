The first months after Janet van Dyne came home were the strangest of Hope's life. Her mother had been gone for thirty years. Janet did not know what cell phones were. She did not know what the internet was. She had never tasted modern peanut butter. Hope, who had grown up in a quiet house missing one of its people, suddenly had a mother to introduce to her own world. They spent long mornings at the breakfast table while Janet learned about emojis and Hope learned how the Quantum Realm actually worked.

It did not take long for Hope and Janet to start working together. Janet's old Wasp suit was still in storage at Pym Technologies — a faded red-and-black uniform with thin glassy wings — and Hank restored it gently in the workshop. The first time Hope saw her mother in full Wasp gear standing next to her in the laboratory, both of them with their wings folded, Hope's breath caught in her throat. Two Wasps. One old, one new. Mother and daughter, both glowing in the same workshop light.

They made an extraordinary team. Janet had thirty years of Quantum Realm survival behind her. She had learned things about quantum energy no living scientist on Earth knew. Hope had thirty years of training, engineering, and combat skill. When the two of them worked a mission together, Janet would lead the science and Hope would lead the tactics. When they fought, Janet would set the rhythm — slow, considered, almost graceful — and Hope would punch the holes. Crooks who had once feared the new Wasp learned to fear the pair of them twice as much.

On one early mission, a small group of arms dealers had stolen a Pym disc from a shipping yard. Hope and Janet went together. Janet shrank down through a keyhole. Hope flew through a vent. They met in the middle of the warehouse and worked through the room in two minutes flat — Janet zapping with bluish-white stingers, Hope zapping with bright yellow ones, both of them weaving in the air. The dealers did not know which Wasp to track. They lost.

But the best part of having her mother back was not the missions. It was the small things. Walking through the garden together. Cooking pasta on a rainy night. Janet brushing Hope's hair the way she had not been able to do since Hope was seven years old. The world had given Hope thirty quiet years without her mother. Now, every day, the house in San Francisco was a little less quiet — and the new Wasp had the original Wasp at her side, finally, where she had always belonged.
