After the Snap, when half of all life in the universe vanished in a single moment, Hope van Dyne was one of the people who disappeared. So were her father, Hank, and her newly returned mother, Janet. Scott Lang, trapped in the Quantum Realm at the exact second of the Snap, did not even know it had happened. He floated in tiny stillness for what felt to him like five hours. On the surface of Earth, five years passed without Hope.

When Scott finally escaped — by accident, when a hungry rat in an abandoned warehouse stepped on the controls of the Quantum Tunnel — he stumbled out into a quiet, broken world. Half of everyone was gone. He could not find his family. He could not find Hope. But he found the Avengers, and he brought them an idea: the Quantum Realm could be used as a time tunnel. They could go back. They could undo the Snap.

For Scott, the math was a wall. For Tony Stark and Bruce Banner, the math was a long, hard week. They built a Quantum Tunnel platform big enough for the whole team, calibrated to send Avengers into specific moments in time to collect each of the six Infinity Stones. The mission was called the Time Heist. And when the Heist succeeded, when Bruce snapped the Stones in a borrowed gauntlet and undid the Snap, Hope van Dyne came back.

She returned exactly where she had been standing on the day she vanished — at the controls of her father's portable laboratory by a quiet pier in San Francisco. The world around her had aged five years. Scott was different. The Avengers were short-handed. And somewhere across the country, a vengeful Thanos was preparing for one final battle.

Hope did not hesitate. She gathered her parents. She suited up. She flew to the ruined Avengers compound and joined the largest superhero army the Earth had ever assembled. In the final battle, Hope and Janet — mother and daughter — fought side by side in the sky, two Wasps weaving through falling debris while a returned Scott in his Giant-Man form smashed alien war machines into the ground below. The Pym family had come back from beyond worlds and beyond time, and they were not about to lose now.
