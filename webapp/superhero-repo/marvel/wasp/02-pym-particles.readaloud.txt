Okay, scientists, gather around — because Hope van Dyne is about to give you a lesson in the most extraordinary physics in the Marvel universe. Are you ready? Because Hope does NOT slow down for anybody.

The big secret behind both the Wasp and the Ant-Man suits is something called a Pym Particle. Hope's father, Dr. Hank Pym, discovered it before Hope was even born. So Hope basically grew up speaking Pym Particle like other kids speak English. By the time she was a tiny girl, she could already recite the equations off the lab chalkboard. Whoosh, brilliant!

Here is what makes the Pym Particle so amazing. It changes the SPACE between atoms — without changing how heavy a thing is, or how strong it is. So when Hope shrinks down to the size of a bee — WHOOSH, tiny — her PUNCH still has the same power as a full-grown adult human. Tiny fist, ENORMOUS thump. Bonk! That is why a shrunken Wasp can knock out a security guard twice her size.

And there's a color code, did you know that? In the comics it works like this: RED Pym Particles make things shrink. BLUE Pym Particles make things grow. Hope can do both, with a little dial on her wrist. Click — small. Click — big. Click — even bigger. Click — back to normal.

But here is the part Hope NEVER forgets. Pym Particles are also dangerous. If you turn the shrink dial too far, you slip past something called the subatomic barrier — and you fall into a strange, glowing place called the Quantum Realm. People who fall into the Quantum Realm do not always come back. Hope's mother, Janet, was lost there for thirty years. THIRTY. So when Hope works with Pym Particles, she is calm, and precise, and never, ever silly. Whereas Scott Lang — bless him — once shrank himself by accident in a bathtub.

In the Pym Tech laboratory, Hope is the one who actually BUILDS and IMPROVES the gear. She redesigned the Wasp gauntlets. She fine-tuned Scott's Ant-Man helmet. She invented a rolling laboratory the size of a suitcase that can ZOOSH up into a full-sized building right on a San Francisco street corner. Imagine pulling your house behind you on a luggage strap! That is Hope-level engineering.

She also remembers the warnings. Once, a man named Darren Cross — Hank's former student — tried to turn Pym Particles into a weapon called the Yellowjacket suit. After that, the Pym family promised to keep the formula safe. One vial of red particles in the wrong hands could shrink a tank, a missile, even a skyscraper. So Hope guards the science like a treasure.

If you ask Hope how she stays calm with that much power, she just gives you a sharp little smile and quotes her father. "Pym Particles are not a toy. They are not a magic trick. They are physics — and physics keeps its promises." And then ZIP — she shrinks, and gets back to work.
