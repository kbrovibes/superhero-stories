Close your eyes for just a moment. Imagine you go to sleep tonight in your cosy bed — and when you wake up, seventy years have gone by. Your house is gone. Your street is gone. Everyone you ever knew is part of history. And the world outside your window is a totally different place, full of strange glowing screens and thundering machines and more people than you can count.

That is almost exactly what happened to Steve Rogers.

During the final days of World War II, Captain America steered a massive aeroplane full of terrible weapons away from the cities it was aimed at. He had no choice — he had to bring it down in the freezing Arctic Ocean. SPLASH! KA-BOOM! The plane went into the ice, and Steve went with it.

Now here is the astonishing scientific secret of why Steve survived: the Super-Soldier Serum in his body did something extraordinary. Scientists at S.H.I.E.L.D. believe it worked a little like the way wood frogs survive winter — his body created a natural cryoprotectant, like a special syrup that kept his cells safe and whole deep inside the ice. His heart kept beating, very, very slowly. And there he stayed, perfectly preserved, while the world above him kept spinning.

When S.H.I.E.L.D. found him and brought him back in the 21st century, Steve woke up in a room that looked like the 1940s — but he could tell something was wrong. He raced outside and found himself in the middle of New York City, staring up at the lights of Times Square, surrounded by sounds and sights that had no place in anything he remembered. Can you imagine that feeling?

The hardest part was not the skyscrapers or the telephones or the incredible technology everywhere. The hardest part was looking around and not seeing a single familiar face. But then Steve Rogers did what he had always done: he got up, he looked forward, and he found new people to fight alongside — Tony Stark, Natasha Romanoff, and the rest of the Avengers.

And do you know what he brought that none of them could find anywhere else? The wisdom of someone who had already seen the world at its very worst — and its very best. His outsider eyes cut right through the noise and saw what truly mattered. In a world spinning faster every day, Captain America was the steady compass everyone needed.
