• Steve Rogers frozen in the Arctic for nearly seventy years; wakes up disoriented in the 21st century
• His Brooklyn of childhood replaced by glass towers and digital billboards — everything familiar is gone
• Struggle: the clear good-vs-evil simplicity of World War II replaced by complex political gray areas
• Key insight: while the surface changed, humanity's core desire for freedom and justice remained the same
• New allies include Tony Stark and Natasha Romanoff — methods clash with his values, forcing him to grow
• His outsider perspective becomes a strength: provides grounded wisdom the modern era often lacks
• Reminds Avengers of human cost and the importance of staying true to the mission
• Witnesses long-term progress — civil rights, technology, international cooperation — built on his generation's sacrifice
• No longer just a history-book figure: becomes a vital bridge between generations
• The 21st century is not just where he ended up — it is where he is most needed
