Not every villain waves a sword or breathes fire. Some are dangerous for a different reason — they believe the wrong things with all their heart. That was the Red Skull, a man named Johann Schmidt who thought that only the strongest and most ruthless people in the world deserved to be in charge.

Johann Schmidt discovered a mysterious glowing blue cube called the Tesseract — a source of incredible cosmic power. Most people would have used such a thing to help others. But Schmidt only saw a weapon, a way to make everyone in the world obey him. His face had turned a deep, frightening crimson red, like a skull, and he wore it like a mask of terror, hoping that just the sight of him would make his enemies give up before the fight even began.

Captain America — brave Steve Rogers from Brooklyn — was everything the Red Skull was not. Where Schmidt used fear, Steve used kindness. Where Schmidt pushed people away, Steve brought them together. And do you know what? The Red Skull could never understand that. He thought friendship and fairness were silly ideas, and that was his greatest mistake.

Every time the Red Skull hatched a new scheme — hiding in mountain fortresses, reaching for power across the stars — Captain America and the Avengers stood in his way. The Skull would rage and plot and try again, but courage and truth kept defeating him.

And that is how the world learned that a man who fights for the people will always outlast a man who only fights for himself. The Red Skull could never take that away from Captain America, no matter how many plans he made.
