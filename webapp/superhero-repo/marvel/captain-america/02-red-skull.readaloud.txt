Are you ready to meet Captain America's most frightening enemy? Take a deep breath — because the Red Skull is as scary as his name sounds!

His real name was Johann Schmidt, and he was the leader of a villainous organisation called HYDRA. Now, what made Schmidt so dangerous wasn't his fists or his weapons — it was what he believed. He believed that only the most ruthless, most fearsome people deserved power. He thought kindness was weakness, and friendship was foolishness. Can you imagine anything sillier? And more dangerous?

Schmidt discovered something that changed everything: a glowing blue cube called the Tesseract. Oh, this was no ordinary object! The Tesseract crackled with cosmic energy — power so enormous it could level entire cities. Most people would have locked it away in the safest place they could find. But not Johann Schmidt. He saw it as the perfect weapon to make the whole world obey him. CRACKLE! BOOM! He built enormous machines to harness its energy, hiding them in secret mountain bases high above the clouds.

And his face — oh dear! Something happened to Johann Schmidt long ago that turned his face a deep, terrible crimson red, like the surface of a skull. He wore that face without a mask, without shame, hoping that just the sight of him would make his enemies give up before the fight even started. He called himself the Red Skull, and he meant every terrifying bit of it.

But do you know who was not afraid? Steve Rogers — Captain America, the boy from Brooklyn! Where the Red Skull used fear, Cap used courage. Where Schmidt tore people apart, Steve brought them together. And that drove the Red Skull absolutely up the wall!

Their battles blazed across hidden fortresses and roaring aeroplanes high above the Atlantic Ocean. CLANG! WHOOSH! CRASH! Every time Captain America raised his shield, the Red Skull learned the same lesson — one that his cold heart could never quite accept: fear can win a battle, but it cannot win a war. Not against someone fighting with courage and truth.

In the end, the Tesseract itself had the last word — and the Red Skull found himself whisked far, far away from everything he had tried to conquer. And the world breathed a very big sigh of relief.
