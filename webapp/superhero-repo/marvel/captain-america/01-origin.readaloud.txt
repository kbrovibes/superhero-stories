Picture the scrawniest kid in all of Brooklyn, New York — so thin the wind could tip him over, so small his hand-me-down jacket practically swallowed him whole. That kid wanted nothing more than to stand up for what was right. And do you know what? That kid became the mightiest hero the world had ever seen.

His name was Steve Rogers.

Steve grew up in Brooklyn during a time when the whole world was in trouble. Terrible people were doing terrible things, and Steve wanted to help stop them. So he tried to join the United States Army. Are you ready for this? He was turned away — not once, not twice, but again and again! Too short. Too skinny. Too sick. But Steve Rogers never quit trying.

A kind scientist named Dr. Abraham Erskine had been watching Steve very carefully. And do you know what Dr. Erskine saw? He didn't see weak muscles. He saw a BIG heart. He saw someone who would never bully anyone, never lie, and never stop fighting for the little guy. Dr. Erskine was looking for a GOOD man — not just a strong one. And Steve Rogers was the best man he had ever found.

So Dr. Erskine invited Steve to take part in something extraordinary: Project Rebirth! Steve stepped into a shimmering chamber and received the Super-Soldier Serum. Then came the Vita-Ray radiation — a burst of energy that lit up the room like a tiny sun! ZAP! WHOOOOSH! When the chamber opened, out stepped a Steve Rogers who was taller, stronger, and faster than any person alive. His reflexes became ten times quicker than a regular person's. Can you imagine catching a ball before you even see it coming?

But here is the most important thing, and you must remember this: the Super-Soldier Serum made Steve's body stronger, but it was his heart that made him a hero. Dr. Erskine always said the serum does not create something new — it only makes more of what is already there. And what was already inside Steve Rogers? Courage. Kindness. A fierce love of justice.

Then, in a terrible moment, a villain struck and Dr. Erskine was gone. The formula was lost forever. Steve Rogers was the one and only Super-Soldier the world would ever have.

He put on a red, white, and blue uniform. He picked up a round shield with a bright white star. And the world came to know his name — CAPTAIN AMERICA. From tiny Brooklyn to the front lines of World War II, the scrawniest kid who ever lived proved something wonderful: the greatest power in the universe is the goodness already inside your heart.
