Before Steve Rogers was ever Captain America, he had a best friend. His name was Bucky Barnes, and together, the two boys from Brooklyn laughed and looked out for each other through everything. Even on the most dangerous front lines of World War II, Bucky fought alongside Steve in a brave group called the Howling Commandos.

Then one terrible day, during a mission on a high-speed train, Bucky fell. Steve searched and searched, but his friend was gone. For years, Captain America carried that sadness quietly in his heart, believing he had lost Bucky forever.

But Bucky had not perished. A secret and very dark organisation had found him, and they did something terrible. They wiped away his memories, replaced one of his arms with a powerful cybernetic arm made of metal, and turned him into a silent, frightening assassin called the Winter Soldier. He remembered almost nothing of who he used to be.

And do you know what happened when Steve finally came face to face with the Winter Soldier? He recognised his friend's eyes. Right then, Steve made a choice. He would not give up on Bucky Barnes. Not ever.

It took a long time and it was very hard — harder than any physical battle Steve had ever fought. There were moments of real sadness. But Steve kept returning, kept reminding Bucky of Brooklyn, of their friendship, of the good man he had always been. Slowly, memory by memory, Bucky began to come back.

From that day on, Bucky Barnes stood beside Captain America once more — not as a soldier following orders, but as a friend who had found his way home.
