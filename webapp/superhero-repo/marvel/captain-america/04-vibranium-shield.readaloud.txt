Captain America carries many things with him into every battle — courage, kindness, and a steady heart. But he also carries something you can actually see and touch: the most remarkable shield in the world.

That shield is made from Vibranium, a very special metal that can only be found in one place on Earth — the hidden kingdom of Wakanda. Vibranium is extraordinary because of something magical about the way its tiny atoms are arranged. When anything hits the shield — a bullet, a bolt of energy, even a blow from a mighty god — the shield simply soaks it up. The force just disappears, like dropping a stone into still water. Captain America doesn't even feel the impact.

And do you know what makes it even more wonderful? Cap can throw it. He has practised for years, learning every angle and bounce, so the shield can sail through the air, ricochet off walls, and come zooming right back to his hand, sometimes knocking down several enemies along the way — like the world's most heroic boomerang.

The shield has a star in the middle, ringed with red, white, and blue. People all over the world recognise that pattern. When they see it, they know that Captain America is near and that someone brave is standing between them and danger. Cap has also used it to climb walls, cushion a fall from great heights, and shelter ordinary people caught in the crossfire.

From that day on, every scratch and dent on that Vibranium circle became a little badge of honour — the mark of a threat that was faced, and a life that was saved, by the shield and the good man who carries it.
