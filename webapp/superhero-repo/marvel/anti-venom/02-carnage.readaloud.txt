Okay, deep breath — because this story has the SCARIEST symbiote of all. Ready?

His name is Carnage. And guess what color he is? Not black. RED! Red as a fire truck, red as a warning light. Carnage is made of living goo, just like Venom, but Carnage only ever wants one thing: to make a big, loud, chaotic MESS.

His tendrils go SHINK — sharp as blades! He can throw pieces of himself like darts — ZIP! ZIP! And one day, Carnage broke loose in New York City.

Even Spider-Man gulped. "I can't stop THIS one by myself!"

But guess who was in the city now?

That's right — Anti-Venom! Our white hero! And here's the amazing thing: Anti-Venom can FEEL other symbiotes. When Carnage got close, Eddie felt a prickle, like a splinter in his finger. "Found you," he growled.

WHOOSH! Anti-Venom swung across the sky on shiny white webs, chasing that red streak all the way to the middle of Manhattan.

Spider-Man landed on the same rooftop — and jumped! "Ahh! A symbiote!"

"Easy, web-head," rumbled Eddie. "I'm not the Venom you remember. Today, we're on the SAME side!"

And oh, they needed each other.

Carnage laughed and swung his red whips — THWACK! THWACK! But Spider-Man's spider-sense went TINGLE, and he flipped away every time. Flip! Flip! Whoosh!

Then Anti-Venom got close. Real close. And do you know what happens when white antibodies touch red symbiote goo?

FSSSS! The red goo hissed and curled away! Because Anti-Venom is the CURE — and no symbiote can stand its own antidote! Every time Eddie's big white hands brushed the red goo, Carnage got weaker and weaker. Fsss! Fsss! Like an ice cube shrinking in the sun.

"No fair! No fair!" Carnage shrieked, waving his skinny red tendrils. "Get away from me!"

But Anti-Venom kept coming. Step. Step. STEP. Because a bully like Carnage is only brave until somebody bigger and kinder stands up to him.

And then — like every bully who finally loses — Carnage RAN. He slithered off into the shadows, all the way back toward Ravencroft, to sulk and lose another day.

Manhattan was safe! The city lights glowed steady and bright again.

Spider-Man looked at the big white hero next to him. He was quiet for a second.

"You're really trying to be one of the good guys," he said.

Anti-Venom gave a little nod.

Two old rivals. One rooftop. One win. And you know what?

It was a very, very good beginning.

THWIP — there they go!
