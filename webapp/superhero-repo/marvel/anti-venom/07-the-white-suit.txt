If you saw Anti-Venom swinging across the rooftops of New York City for the first time, you might gasp — because at a glance he looks a little like Venom. But look again. Where Venom is black, Anti-Venom is white. Where Venom is a shadow, Anti-Venom is a light. He is the mirror-opposite of the monster he used to be, and every part of his look tells that story.

His skin is living goo, glossy and gleaming white, like fresh snow or polished pearl. It ripples and flows as he moves, because the symbiote is always alive. Threading across that white are swirling black tendrils and veins, curling along his arms and shoulders like the branches of a tree drawn in ink. Spread across his broad chest is a black spider shape — the same emblem Venom wears, but flipped: black on white instead of white on black. Two enormous oval eyes, pure white and rimmed in black, sit wide upon his face, and his grin is wide and toothy, though somehow friendlier than Venom's ever was.

That white color is not just for show. It is the color of the healing Lightforce that made him, the sign of the antibodies swirling inside him, the flag of a symbiote that cures instead of harms. When frightened people first see his size, they sometimes shrink back — until they notice the difference, until they see the light instead of the dark, and understand that this creature has come to help.

The living goo can do remarkable things beyond simply looking striking. It flows to cover Eddie completely or peels back to show his human face when he wishes to speak plainly. It can stretch and shape itself, sprouting extra tendrils to grab a falling person or forming a shield against a blow. And it can shift its color to melt into a wall or a shadow, letting a creature the size of Anti-Venom disappear as quietly as a whisper.

Eddie is proud of the white suit, and not because it looks fearsome. He is proud because it is the truest picture of who he has become. The black Venom goo had always reflected his anger, his hunger, his need for revenge. The white Anti-Venom goo reflects the opposite — his mercy, his healing, his second chance. When Eddie catches his reflection in a darkened window, he no longer sees a monster staring back. He sees a guardian, dressed in the color of the light that saved him.
