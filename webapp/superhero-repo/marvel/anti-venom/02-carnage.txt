Of all the black symbiotes in the world, the most dangerous was red. His name was Carnage, and his host was a troubled man named Cletus Kasady. Where the Venom symbiote had once held a spark of noble purpose, Carnage held nothing but a love of chaos. His red tendrils could sharpen into blades, and he could hurl pieces of himself like darts. When Carnage broke loose in New York City, even Spider-Man knew he could not stop this one alone.

But now there was someone new in the city — a white figure who was the natural enemy of every symbiote alive. Anti-Venom felt Carnage the way you feel a splinter, a wrongness prickling at the edge of his senses. Eddie Brock did not hesitate. He swung across the skyline on strands of white webbing, following the red streak of destruction toward the heart of Manhattan.

Spider-Man arrived at the same rooftop, ready to fight — and then froze at the sight of the towering white creature beside him. "Easy, web-head," Eddie rumbled. "I'm not the Venom you remember. Today, we're on the same side." Spider-Man had learned long ago never to fully trust a symbiote. But he had also learned to recognize help when it arrived, and against Carnage, he needed all the help he could get.

The fight was fast and loud. Carnage laughed and lashed out with crimson whips — THWACK! — but Spider-Man's spider-sense sang, and he flipped clear every time. Anti-Venom pressed in close, and that was the thing Carnage feared most. Because when Eddie's white antibodies touched the red goo, the symbiote screamed and curled away, weakening wherever the white light burned it. Carnage was fast, but Anti-Venom was his cure, and no symbiote can bear its own antidote.

Cornered, Carnage did what bullies do when they finally lose — he ran. He peeled away into the shadows and slithered back toward the walls of Ravencroft, beaten but not broken, saving the rest of the battle for another day. Manhattan was safe. The lights of the city glowed steady again.

Spider-Man stood on the rooftop, catching his breath, studying the strange white hero beside him. "You're really trying to be one of the good guys," he said slowly. It was not quite a thank-you, but it was close. Anti-Venom gave a small nod. Two old rivals had fought side by side and won. It was, Eddie thought, a very good beginning.
