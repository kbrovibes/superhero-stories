Picture this. You look up at the New York rooftops, and WHOOSH — a huge goo creature swings past!

You gasp. "AH! Is that… VENOM?!"

Look again, friend. Look closer.

Because this one isn't black. This one is WHITE!

Meet the white suit of Anti-Venom — the mirror-opposite of Venom in every single way!

Where Venom is black, Anti-Venom is white. Where Venom is a shadow, Anti-Venom is a LIGHT. Everything about the way he looks tells the story of the hero he became.

His skin is living goo — glossy, gleaming, shiny white! Like fresh snow. Like a polished pearl. And it ripples and flows when he moves, because the goo is ALIVE. Squish, squish!

Swirling across all that white are wiggly black tendrils and veins, curling down his arms like tree branches drawn in ink. Cool, right?

And on his big broad chest? A black spider shape! It's the very same spider Venom wears — but flipped! Black spider on white, instead of white spider on black!

Two ENORMOUS white eyes, wide as dinner plates, sit on his face. And his grin is big and toothy — but somehow way friendlier than Venom's ever was. A little scary, a lot kind.

Now here's the important part. That white isn't just for looks!

White is the color of the healing Lightforce that MADE him. It's the color of the antibodies swirling inside. It's like a flag that says: "I'm a symbiote who CURES instead of hurts!"

So when scared people first see how big he is, sometimes they shrink back. But then — they notice. They see the light instead of the dark. And they realize, "Oh! This one came to HELP!"

And oh, what that living goo can do!

It can flow up to cover Eddie completely — or peel back to show his real human face when he wants to talk. It can stretch out extra tendrils to catch a falling person — GOTCHA! — or make a shield to block a hit — CLANG! And it can change color to melt right into a wall, so a giant creature can vanish quiet as a whisper. Shhh!

But you know why Eddie loves the white suit most?

Not because it looks tough.

The old black goo showed all his anger and his need for payback. But the white goo shows the OPPOSITE — his kindness, his healing, his second chance.

So when Eddie sees himself in a dark window now, he doesn't see a monster anymore.

He sees a guardian — dressed in the color of the light that saved him.
