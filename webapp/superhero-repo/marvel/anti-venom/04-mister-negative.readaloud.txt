Here's a tricky question for you. What if the person who SAVED your life turned out to have a big, secret, spooky other side?

That's exactly what happened to Eddie Brock. Buckle up!

You remember Martin Li, right? The kind man with the healing white Lightforce? He's the one who cured Eddie's sickness AND made the white Anti-Venom goo. Martin ran a soup kitchen called F.E.A.S.T. He fed hungry people. He smiled at everybody. To Eddie, Martin was the nicest guy in ALL of New York City.

But Martin Li had a secret. A BIG one.

In the daytime — sweet, gentle Martin. But hidden inside him? A cold, silver-haired shadow-man named MISTER NEGATIVE!

Ooooh.

Where Martin's light HEALED things, Mister Negative's dark power — called Darkforce — twisted them all wrong and turned them black. And Mister Negative was a sneaky crime boss with a whole gang of masked fighters called the Inner Demons!

Poor Eddie. He couldn't believe it. "How can the man who saved me ALSO be a bad guy?"

Down in the tunnels under the city, the Inner Demons swarmed him — WHAM! THWIP! THUD! But Anti-Venom was strong. His white webs went SNAP-SNAP-SNAP and stuck those masked fighters right to the walls!

Then out of the shadows stepped Mister Negative himself, hands crackling with cold Darkforce — crackle, crackle, ZZZT!

And Eddie… stopped.

Because that was Martin's face. That was the hand that made him well.

"You healed me," Eddie said softly. "You gave me everything. Why are you like this?"

But Mister Negative just smiled a chilly little smile. Because the shadow-half didn't remember any of the kind things the good-half had done.

So they fought. But here's the amazing part — Eddie fought WITHOUT any hate in his heart. He couldn't bring himself to hurt the man inside the villain. He blocked the Darkforce — BLOCK! He tangled up the Inner Demons — TANGLE! And he scattered the whole gang back into the tunnels!

Mister Negative slipped away, the way sneaky villains do.

And Eddie climbed back up into the night, thinking and thinking.

People are complicated, he realized. The very same man can hold a healing light AND a cold shadow. That didn't make the kindness fake. And it didn't make the schemes okay, either.

It just meant everybody is still finding their way.

Eddie owed Martin his life. Both things were true at the very same time! He could be grateful to Martin AND still stand against Mister Negative — every single time the shadow showed up.

Because that, friend, is what heroes do. They say thank you when someone helps them. And they still do the right thing, no matter how tricky it gets.

Tricky, tricky — but Eddie figured it out.
