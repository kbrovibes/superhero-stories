Eddie Brock owed everything to Martin Li. It was Martin's healing Lightforce that had washed away Eddie's serious sickness and given birth to the white Anti-Venom symbiote. To Eddie, Martin was the kindest man in New York — a gentle soul who fed the hungry at his F.E.A.S.T. shelter and asked for nothing in return. So imagine how Eddie felt when he learned Martin Li's biggest secret.

Martin Li had two sides. In the daylight, he was the good and generous man everyone loved. But hidden inside him lived a shadow-self — a cold, silver-haired figure who called himself Mister Negative. Where Martin's Lightforce healed, Mister Negative's Darkforce corrupted, turning things black and twisting them wrong. And Mister Negative was a crime boss who commanded a gang of masked fighters known as the Inner Demons.

Eddie could hardly believe it. How could the man who saved his life also be the villain lurking in the city's dark corners? When Anti-Venom finally faced the Inner Demons in the tunnels beneath New York, his heart was tangled up with feelings he could not sort out. The masked fighters swarmed him — WHAM, THWIP, THUD! — but Anti-Venom was strong, and his white webs snapped them to the walls one by one.

Then Mister Negative himself stepped from the shadows, his hands crackling with cold Darkforce. Eddie hesitated. This was Martin's face. This was the touch that had made him whole. "You healed me," Eddie said. "You gave me everything. Why are you like this?" But Mister Negative only smiled a chilly smile, for the shadow-self did not remember the kindness the good half had done.

They fought, and it was a strange battle, because Eddie fought without hate. Even now, he could not bring himself to harm the man inside the villain. He blocked the Darkforce, tangled the Inner Demons in webbing, and scattered the whole crew back into the tunnels. Mister Negative slipped away, as villains do, and the fight ended without a true winner.

Eddie climbed back up into the New York night, thinking hard. People, he realized, are complicated. The same man could hold both a healing light and a cold shadow. It did not make Martin's kindness fake, and it did not make Mister Negative's schemes forgivable. It only meant that even the people we look up to are still finding their own way — the same way Eddie was finding his. He owed Martin his life. And he would still stand against Mister Negative, every single time.
