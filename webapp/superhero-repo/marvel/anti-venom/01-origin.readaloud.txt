Have you ever heard of a bad guy who became a HERO? Then lean in close, because this is that story!

His name was Eddie Brock. And once — a long time ago — Eddie was Venom. You know Venom! The big black goo monster with the giant white eyes who used to chase Spider-Man all over New York City. That was Eddie.

But Eddie got tired of being scary. So he let the black symbiote go, and he tried to live a nice, quiet life.

Except… Eddie got sick. A serious sickness. And no doctor, in any hospital, in the whole big city, could make him better. Eddie got weaker and weaker. Uh oh.

Then — knock knock! — Eddie met a kind man named Martin Li.

Martin ran a soup kitchen called F.E.A.S.T., where anybody hungry could get a warm bowl of soup and a friendly smile. And Martin had a SECRET. Deep inside him glowed a gentle white light called the Lightforce.

Martin reached out his hand. He touched Eddie's shoulder. And — WHOOSH! — warm white light poured right into him!

The bad illness? GONE. Eddie stood up straight. He took a big, deep breath. He was well again!

But wait — something ELSE was happening. Can you feel it?

Deep down in Eddie's blood, tiny little bits of the old Venom goo were still hiding. And when that healing light touched them — ZAP! — the light and the goo swirled together and made something brand new!

White goo bubbled up all over Eddie. Not black — WHITE! Shiny, gleaming white, with wiggly black tendrils and a big black spider on the chest. Huge white eyes, wide as dinner plates!

This was Anti-Venom. The mirror-opposite of Venom!

And here is the best part. This new goo wasn't hungry for revenge. It was made of HEALING. Its white antibodies could wash sickness and yucky poison right out of a person. And its touch could melt away ANY other black symbiote goo — fsss! — like water on sugar.

Eddie looked in the mirror at the white hero staring back. Big white eyes! Black spider on the chest! Tendrils wiggling like happy little snakes! He remembered every mistake he ever made. He remembered all the times people were scared of him. And right then and there, he made a promise.

"This time," Eddie said, "I don't scare people. I protect them. I heal them. I keep them safe."

The bad guy was gone. A hero stood in his place. Eddie Brock — the Lethal Protector of New York City!

And his greatest power of all? Being kind enough to heal.
