This is the BIG one, friend. The greatest, bravest story of them all. Are you ready? Here we go!

One day, all of Manhattan started to CHANGE.

A wicked villain called the Jackal had made a sneaky spider-virus — and he set it loose all over the city! And it spread from person to person, just like a cold. Achoo!

At first? It seemed kind of FUN! People started crawling up walls! Sticking to ceilings! Feeling super strong and super fast! Whee!

But uh oh — the virus didn't stop there. Slowly, it started turning people into spider-creatures! The WHOLE city was in trouble. And no regular medicine could fix it.

This was the giant disaster everybody would remember forever. They called it… SPIDER-ISLAND.

Spider-Man raced to help! So did Reed Richards, the smartest scientist in the Fantastic Four, and every single hero they could round up. They searched and searched for a cure while more and more of Manhattan changed. Tick, tick, tick — time was running out!

And then Reed's science found the answer. And guess what?

The cure was ALREADY in the city. The cure was… Anti-Venom!

That's right! Eddie's white antibodies — the ones that wash away sickness and melt black goo — were the PERFECT medicine for the spider-virus!

Everywhere Anti-Venom touched a sick person — WHOOSH — the virus melted away, and the spider-creature turned back into a happy, grateful human being!

So Eddie became the living cure. He moved through the whole city, laying his healing hands on person after person after person. Touch! Cured! Touch! Cured!

But… do you remember his limit?

The healing is NOT endless.

Every person Eddie cured drained a little more of his white power. And there were THOUSANDS of people who needed him! Eddie could feel his glow getting dimmer and dimmer, like a candle burning down. Flicker… flicker…

And now Eddie had the hardest choice of his whole life.

He could save himself. Or he could save the city.

Eddie didn't wait. Not even a second.

He poured out EVERYTHING he had — every last drop of his precious cure — until the whole island was healed and Manhattan was itself again!

And when it was all over… the white symbiote was gone. Eddie had given it away to save a city full of strangers.

He stood there, plain old Eddie once more, tired all the way to his bones — and smiling.

Because he'd gotten a second chance… and he'd spent it on everybody else.

And friend? There is no braver thing a hero can EVER do.
