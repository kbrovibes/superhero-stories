Every hero has a job. And do you know what Eddie Brock's job was? Not being famous. Not being on TV.

His job was PROTECTING people. All of them!

Eddie called himself a Lethal Protector. But you know which word mattered to him? Not "lethal." PROTECTOR! Because more than anything, Eddie was there to keep people safe.

So he didn't go swinging around the bright, fancy part of the city where the cameras were. Nope!

Eddie guarded the FORGOTTEN corners. The dark alleys. The little shelters. The quiet blocks where regular people lived and no other hero ever bothered to look.

Night after night — WHOOSH — he swung across New York on shiny white webs, keeping watch over everybody down below.

Did a robber try to scare a shopkeeper? STOMP — not tonight, buddy!

Did a little kid wander toward the busy street? SWOOP — Eddie scooped them up just in time!

Did a heavy beam fall on a worker? HRRRNGH — Eddie lifted it right off! And when the worker was hurt — WHOOSH — Eddie laid a healing hand on him too!

Other heroes fight and zoom away. But Anti-Venom? He always stayed a little longer, just to make sure everybody was really and truly okay.

Then one night — guess who he bumped into?

SPIDER-MAN!

Now these two had a LONG, complicated history. Once upon a time, enemies. Then teammates against Carnage. But New York is a BIG city with LOTS of trouble to share!

And that night, a gang of crooks had way too much firepower for one hero alone.

So — team-up time!

Spider-sense went TINGLE — "Ambush!" Anti-Venom's strength went CRASH — right through the locked door! THWIP! THUD! BAM! Those crooks didn't stand a chance!

When it was all over, the two heroes sat up on a water tower and looked out at the twinkly city lights.

And Spider-Man said something he'd been thinking for a while.

"You know what? You're not so bad to have around."

And from the hero who used to be the MOST scared of Eddie? That meant a LOT.

Anti-Venom chuckled a big, happy, rumbly laugh.

Two men who spent YEARS as enemies… were slowly, surely becoming friends.

And Eddie swung home over the sleeping rooftops, thinking: "I'm not a perfect hero. But I'm a faithful one."

And for the people nobody else remembers to watch over? A faithful protector is EXACTLY what you want.

So look up at the rooftops tonight, friend. Maybe — just maybe — a big white hero is up there right now, swinging quietly through the dark, making sure every single person gets home safe and sound.

Even you. Sweet dreams, and THWIP — there he goes!
