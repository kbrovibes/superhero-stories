Remember the old black Venom goo? The one Eddie used to wear? Well — it didn't disappear. Oh no. It found somebody NEW to wrap around.

His name was Mac Gargan. And Mac was NOT nice.

Once upon a time, Mac was a villain called the Scorpion. Now he wore the black Venom symbiote, and he used it to scare people and take their stuff. He was bigger and meaner than ever — a whole new Venom stomping around New York City. STOMP! STOMP!

But guess who knew that black goo better than anybody in the whole world?

Eddie Brock! Because he used to wear it! And now, as Anti-Venom, Eddie had the ONE power the goo was truly scared of — a touch that could melt it right off. FSSS!

They met during some dark and stormy days. Spider-Man was already fighting a whole TEAM of bad guys, and Mac's Venom was the biggest, hungriest one of all.

Then Anti-Venom showed up — and everything changed!

But here's the surprising part. Eddie didn't want to HURT Mac. He wanted to FREE him! Eddie thought, "If I can peel that black goo off, I can give Mac a second chance — just like Martin Li gave one to me!"

CRASH! THWIP! BOOM! The fight bounced across rooftops and alleyways!

Mac swung his black tendrils — grab! snatch! — but everywhere they touched Anti-Venom's white skin — SIZZLE! — the goo shrank back and squealed!

Eddie walked RIGHT through the attacks. His white antibodies burned the black goo away, layer by layer by layer. The Venom symbiote screamed. It was being CURED, and it did NOT like that one bit!

"Hold still," Eddie growled. "I'm helping you — even if you don't want it!"

He grabbed on tight and pushed the healing white light in DEEP. And the black goo bubbled and thinned and started peeling off Mac's body like old paint — flake, flake, gone!

For one shining second, Eddie almost — ALMOST — washed the whole thing clean away!

But oh no — the other villains grabbed Mac and yanked him free! The black goo hung on, weak and wounded, and Mac vanished into the storm.

Eddie stood in the rain, huffing and puffing.

He didn't save Mac that day. But he learned something HUGE about himself.

"I'm not a monster who hunts monsters," Eddie said. "I'm a HEALER."

Because to Eddie, even the scariest black goo isn't something to smash.

It's something to CURE.

And that, my friend, is what makes a real hero. Not the biggest muscles. Not the loudest roar. The kindest heart! Eddie didn't win that day — but he found out exactly who he wanted to be. And that's a win that lasts forever.

FSSS — go get 'em, Anti-Venom!
