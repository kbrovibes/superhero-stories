Before he was a hero, Eddie Brock had already lived two very different lives. He had been Venom, the huge black symbiote that once battled Spider-Man across New York City. Then he had walked away from the symbiote, hoping for a quieter life. But quiet did not come. Eddie had fallen ill with a serious sickness, one that no doctor at any hospital in the city seemed able to fix. Day by day, he grew weaker, and he began to believe that his story was nearly finished.

Then Eddie met a kind man named Martin Li. Martin ran a soup kitchen in Chinatown called the F.E.A.S.T. shelter, a warm place where people who had nowhere to go could find a meal and a friend. Martin carried a rare and secret power inside him — a gentle white glow he called the Lightforce. When Martin reached out and laid his hand on Eddie, that healing light poured into him. The bad illness melted away. For the first time in a long while, Eddie could breathe deeply and stand up straight.

But something unexpected happened, too. Deep in Eddie's blood, tiny leftover traces of the old Venom symbiote were still hiding. When Martin's Lightforce touched those traces, the light and the goo mixed together and became something brand new. A fresh symbiote bloomed across Eddie's body — not black, but glossy, gleaming white, laced with swirling black tendrils and a black spider shape stretched across the chest. Its eyes were enormous white ovals. It was the mirror-opposite of Venom in every way.

This new creature was called Anti-Venom. And it was not hungry for revenge like the old suit had been. It was made of healing. Its white antibodies could wash sickness and poison out of a person's body, and its touch could dissolve any other black symbiote goo like water melting sugar. Eddie had spent years as a danger to the city. Now, without meaning to, he had become its cure.

Eddie stood in front of a cracked mirror and stared at the white figure looking back at him. He remembered every mistake he had ever made. He remembered being feared. And he decided, quietly and firmly, that this second chance would not be wasted. He would use these strange new gifts to protect the innocent people of New York — to guard the ones nobody else was guarding. Eddie Brock, once a villain, once sick and hopeless, pulled the white symbiote around himself and became a Lethal Protector.
