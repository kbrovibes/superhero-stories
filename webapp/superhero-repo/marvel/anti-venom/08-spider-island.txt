The greatest test of Eddie Brock's new life came the day all of Manhattan began to change. A wicked villain called the Jackal had cooked up a sneaky spider-virus and set it loose across the island. It spread from person to person like a cold, and one by one, ordinary New Yorkers started sprouting spider-powers. At first it seemed almost fun — people crawling up walls, sticking to ceilings, feeling strong and fast. But the virus did not stop there. Slowly, it began to turn people into spider-creatures. The whole city was in danger, and no ordinary medicine could cure it. This was the terrible event that people would forever call Spider-Island.

Spider-Man raced to stop it, alongside Reed Richards of the Fantastic Four and every hero he could gather. They searched and searched for a cure, working against the clock as more and more of Manhattan changed. And then Reed's science found the answer, and the answer was astonishing. The cure was already walking the streets of New York. It was Anti-Venom.

Eddie's white antibodies — the same ones that washed away sickness and dissolved black symbiotes — turned out to be the perfect medicine for the Jackal's spider-virus. Wherever Anti-Venom touched an infected person, the virus melted away and the spider-creature became a frightened, grateful human being once more. Eddie became the living cure, moving through the chaos, laying his healing hands on person after person after person.

But you remember his limit. The healing was not endless. With every single person Eddie cured, a little more of his white power drained away — and there were thousands of people in Manhattan who needed him. Eddie could feel himself running low, the glow inside him dimming like a candle burning down. He had a choice to make, and it was the hardest choice of his life. He could save himself, or he could save the city.

Eddie did not hesitate for long. Working with the other heroes, he poured out everything he had — every last drop of his precious cure — until the whole island was healed and Manhattan was itself again. And when it was over, the white symbiote was spent. The Anti-Venom power was gone, given away to save a city full of strangers. Eddie Brock stood there, ordinary once more, tired to his bones, and quietly at peace. He had been given a second chance, and he had spent it on everyone else. There is no braver thing a hero can do.
