Once upon a time, at the end of a long and gentle story, there was a lesson waiting — the kindest and most important lesson of all.

Come close, dear one, and I will tell it to you, for it is the true heart of everything Anti-Venom ever was.

The lesson is this: everyone deserves a second chance. And healing is the very greatest power there is.

Now, to understand why, you must remember where Eddie Brock began.

He had been Venom — a great, dark creature that the whole city feared. He had made real mistakes, and he had frightened people, and for a long, long time he believed with all his heart that his story could only ever end in darkness. He thought there was no way back for someone like him.

But then, one gray day, a kind man named Martin Li looked at Eddie Brock.

And do you know what Martin saw, dear one?

He did not see a monster. He saw a person. A person who was hurting, and tired, and worth saving.

And Martin gave him a second chance.

That single act of mercy changed absolutely everything. It washed away Eddie's serious sickness. It gave birth to the gleaming white Anti-Venom symbiote. And it turned an old, feared villain into a faithful Lethal Protector, who spent his long nights healing the forgotten people of New York City, one gentle touch at a time.

And that, dear one, is exactly why Eddie fought the way he did.

He never wanted to destroy his enemies. He wanted to cure them. He tried and tried to peel the black goo away from Mac Gargan, hoping to set him free. He faced the shadowy Mister Negative without a drop of hate in his heart. And when the terrible Spider-Island threatened to change all of Manhattan, Eddie gave away his entire symbiote — every last bit of it — to heal a whole city full of strangers.

For Eddie understood something that very few heroes ever truly learn. He understood that the whole point of a second chance is to pass it on. You heal others because you yourself were healed. You forgive others because you yourself were forgiven. Kindness, you see, is meant to keep moving, from one hand to the next.

And now, dear one, here is the most beautiful part of the whole story. So listen closely.

Even after Eddie gave up his precious symbiote on Spider-Island, the Anti-Venom power did not die away. Oh no. It lived on.

In time, a brave soldier named Flash Thompson took up the gift and carried it forward. And here is a wonderful surprise — this was the very same Flash Thompson who, long ago, had once bullied a shy young boy named Peter Parker. But Flash had grown up. Flash had become a hero himself. And now he carried the Anti-Venom cure into the world as Agent Anti-Venom.

Do you see what happened, dear one?

The gift of healing was passed from hand to gentle hand, again and again. From Martin Li, to Eddie Brock. From Eddie Brock, to Flash Thompson. Like a single candle lighting another candle, and that candle lighting yet another, until a whole room glows warm and bright.

A second chance, it turns out, can travel a very, very long way.

So the next time you think of Anti-Venom, do not only picture the big white creature with the huge white eyes and the black spider spread across his chest.

Picture, instead, what he stands for.

Picture a person who once did wrong, and then chose, with all his heart, to do better. Picture a hero whose strongest weapon was mercy, and whose greatest victory of all was healing. Picture a whole life that became living proof of one simple, hopeful, shining truth:

No matter what came before, tomorrow can always, always begin again.

Everyone — everyone, dear one — deserves a second chance.

And the very kindest thing you can ever do with yours… is to turn around and give one to somebody else.

So close your eyes now, little one, and hold that warm thought close as you drift off to sleep. Be gentle. Be brave. Forgive, and heal, and pass the kindness on.

Goodnight, Eddie. Goodnight, Flash. Goodnight, every heart that ever chose to begin again.

Sleep well. The end.
