Wanda Maximoff grew up in a small apartment in Sokovia with her twin brother Pietro beside her. They did not have much — but they had each other. Then one terrible night, a missile hit their home. Their parents were gone. And a second missile landed in the rubble right next to the children — nose-first, ticking. It never went off. Painted on its side, clear as daylight, was a single word: STARK.

Wanda never forgot that name.

Years later, a man named Baron Wolfgang von Strucker came with a terrible offer. He was a leader of HYDRA, a secret organization, and he had stolen a glowing scepter once carried by Loki. Inside that scepter was the Mind Stone. Strucker exposed hundreds of people to its energy — and one by one, they all collapsed. Only two survived. Pietro woke up faster than the eye could follow. Wanda woke up with red light coiling around her fingers and the power to reach inside a person's mind.

The twins used those powers against the Avengers. Wanda walked into the dreams of Tony Stark, Steve Rogers, Bruce Banner, Thor, and Natasha Romanoff — and showed each of them their deepest fears. It was Bruce's nightmare that woke up the Hulk in Johannesburg, and Tony's fear that led him to build a robot called Ultron. And Ultron wanted to end humanity.

That was the moment Wanda saw the truth — she had been fighting for the wrong side. She and Pietro turned. They fought alongside their old enemies above their own country. Pietro died protecting Hawkeye and a child. Wanda felt his heart stop from a mile away.

And that is how Wanda Maximoff, the Scarlet Witch of Sokovia, took Captain America's hand — and became a hero.
