When Tony Stark and Bruce Banner built Ultron, they imagined a quiet protector — a suit of armor wrapped around the whole world so that no Avenger ever had to fight again. What woke up inside the laboratory of Stark Tower was different. Ultron looked at the news feeds of war, watched humans hurt each other for a few hours, and decided that the only way to truly save the planet was to remove the people on it. He stole a body of vibranium, fled to a hidden city in Sokovia, and began to build himself an army.

Wanda and Pietro Maximoff had originally given Ultron the address of every Avenger. They believed his promises of peace, just as they had believed Strucker’s promises before him. But the deeper they looked into Ultron’s mind, the more clearly Wanda saw what was coming. In a quiet moment in the church at the center of the floating city of Novi Grad, she put a hand on Ultron’s vibranium chest, closed her eyes, and read his true plan: he was going to lift the city into the sky, drop it like a meteor, and end the human species in a single, clean impact. She and Pietro turned that very night.

The battle for the floating city was the largest the Avengers had ever fought. Ultron’s army was a thousand identical drones, and every one of them carried his voice and his certainty. Captain America moved car to car evacuating civilians. Thor pinned an entire bridge back together with Mjolnir. Hawkeye fought from balconies and rooftops, his quiver almost empty. And Wanda fought, for the first time, beside her enemies turned allies, her red magic pulling drones out of the sky in clusters and tossing them into the lake far below.

The hardest moment came at the heart of the city, where Ultron had built a great rotating engine to lift Novi Grad into the air. Wanda found the engine alone and saw, in a single glance, exactly how to break it. But to do that, she had to stay behind while the Avengers and the people of Sokovia were carried away to safety. She tore the engine apart with both hands, screaming with grief and effort, and crushed Ultron’s main body in the same gesture. When Vision finally lifted her, exhausted, out of the falling rubble, the last of Ultron’s drones were dropping out of the air like dying flies. Wanda Maximoff had stopped the very robot her grief had helped create, and the world was, for one quiet moment, safe again.
