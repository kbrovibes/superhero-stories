Have you ever read an old, old book that told a story about something SCARY that hadn't happened yet? Well, the witches of the world have been writing about Wanda Maximoff for HUNDREDS of years. They didn't know her name. They didn't know she would be born in Sokovia. But they DID know she was coming. And they were AFRAID. This is the story of how Wanda finally found out about that old prophecy. And how she decided what to do about it.

A long, long time ago, the witches of the world wrote a name in their oldest books. They called this person the SCARLET WITCH. The Scarlet Witch wouldn't study magic the way other witches did. The Scarlet Witch wouldn't borrow her power from another dimension the way Doctor Strange does. The Scarlet Witch would simply MAKE magic, all by herself, the way an ordinary person makes a smile. The witches were SO scared of this future person that they wrote her name in RED INK and underlined it THREE TIMES! Whoa! Three times! That's a big warning.

Now meet Agatha Harkness. Agatha is THREE HUNDRED AND FORTY years old. Can you believe that? She was alive when George Washington was alive! She was alive way back during the Salem witch trials, hundreds of years ago. She survived the witch trials by — uh oh — STEALING the magic of the other witches. She kept her power tidy and hidden. She lived in nice houses. She baked yummy snickerdoodle cookies. SMELLS GOOD!

And Agatha was always — ALWAYS — watching the world. So when she felt Wanda's red glow ripple across the whole East Coast of America, Agatha packed her bags. She drove to a little town called Westview in New Jersey. She rented the house right next door to Wanda. She wore pearls. She baked cookies. She called herself "Agnes." She smiled all the time. SMILE SMILE SMILE.

For weeks, Agatha pretended to be a nice friendly neighbor. She watched Wanda. She listened. She measured exactly how big and how strong Wanda's magic was. And then — when Wanda was tired and sad and tangled in her own story — Agatha pulled back the curtain! WHOOOSH! Her cozy basement suddenly turned into a candlelit witch's cellar with runes drawn all over the walls. Her eyes flashed bright PURPLE. And she sang her famous little song. "It was Agatha all alooooong!" CRACKKK! The whole house shook.

Then Agatha told Wanda the saddest, scariest thing. She said, "Wanda. Did you know about the prophecy? The witches were afraid of YOU. You're the Scarlet Witch. You're the one they wrote about. You aren't supposed to EXIST."

For one long, long moment, Wanda almost believed her. The witches were scared. Should Wanda be scared of herself too? Should she give her power away?

But here is what Wanda decided. Listen carefully. She had ALREADY been scared of herself for too many years. She didn't need a three-hundred-year-old witch with purple lightning to tell her she was dangerous. She had decided in Sokovia, and in Wakanda, and right here in Westview, that SHE would choose what to do with her own magic. Nobody else.

So Wanda lifted her hand. Red light poured out of it. She painted Agatha's own runes on the ceiling of the Hex bubble. CRACK! Agatha froze. Her purple lightning fizzled out. Wanda lifted slowly into the air. Her hair grew long and dark. A beautiful scarlet headpiece formed around her brow like a CROWN. And the Scarlet Witch — the one the old books had warned about for hundreds of years — opened her eyes for the very first time. And she chose, very quietly, to be good.
