After Wanda Maximoff lost Vision, her grief became something no one had ever seen before. It spread out from her like a glowing red bubble, and it swallowed an entire town in New Jersey called Westview. Inside the bubble, Wanda gave herself the life she had always wanted: a cozy house, a husband, two little boys named Billy and Tommy. It was warm and quiet and perfect. And it was not real.

Outside the bubble, a woman named Monica Rambeau and the agents of S.W.O.R.D. stood at the shimmering boundary, trying to understand what was happening and how to help.

Inside, Wanda had a neighbor named Agnes. Agnes wore pearls. Agnes baked snickerdoodles. Agnes was always, always smiling.

Agnes was not what she seemed. Her real name was Agatha Harkness, and she had been alive for several hundred years. She had survived the Salem witch trials by stealing the magic of the witches around her. And she had come to Westview because she recognized Wanda's power. The old books of witchcraft had spoken of a force called the Scarlet Witch — a magic so strong it could reshape reality itself. Agatha wanted it.

The fight that followed was unlike any before it. Agatha fired bolts of purple lightning. Wanda answered with walls of red force. They crashed through the streets of Westview while Billy and Tommy watched from the porch.

And do you know how Wanda won? She turned Agatha's own runes against her — painting them in red light across the inside of the bubble so that only Wanda could use her magic within it. Agatha froze, powerless.

From that day on, Wanda Maximoff finally accepted what she had become. The Scarlet Witch opened a glowing book on her cabin table and began to understand herself.
