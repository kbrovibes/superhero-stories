• After losing Vision twice, Wanda drives to a New Jersey town called Westview, to an empty lot Vision once bought, with a note in his handwriting: "To grow old in"
• Her grief overflows and her chaos magic builds a glowing red bubble — the Hex — around the whole town
• Inside the bubble, Westview becomes a black-and-white 1950s sitcom, complete with a smiling Vision and laughing neighbors
• The decades pass quickly — 50s to 60s to 70s to 80s to 90s — with new wallpaper, new clothes, and new music every era
• Wanda discovers she is pregnant with two impossible little boys: Billy (who can read minds) and Tommy (who can run incredibly fast, like his uncle Pietro)
• Outside the bubble, a group called S.W.O.R.D. and Captain Monica Rambeau press against the boundary, trying to figure out who is running the show
• When Wanda realizes the real people of Westview are trapped inside her story, hurting, she does the brave thing
• She kisses Billy and Tommy goodnight, lets Vision dim, and closes the Hex — because real people matter more than any dream she could make
