The red light that curls around Wanda Maximoff’s fingers is not the same magic that the sorcerers of Kamar-Taj study in their dusty libraries. Doctor Strange’s spells come from disciplined practice — hand positions called mudras, ancient texts written in Sanskrit and Vishanti, and the slow shaping of energy borrowed from other dimensions. Chaos magic is different. It does not borrow; it makes. It does not balance the rules; it bends them. It belongs to one person at a time, and for now, that person is Wanda.

For most of her life, Wanda thought she was just an enhanced human with telekinesis and mind-reading, two gifts unlocked in her by the Mind Stone in Sokovia. But the Mind Stone had not given her magic. It had only unlocked the magic she had always carried. Ancient witches had written about a kind of magic that did not appear in their lifetime — a magic strong enough to redraw the laws of reality. They called the wielder of that power the Scarlet Witch, and they prayed she would never be born. Wanda Maximoff, child of Sokovia, was the prophecy they had been afraid of.

Doctor Strange explained the difference to her one evening at Kamar-Taj. He pulled out a copy of the Book of the Vishanti, which contained every spell the universe’s good sorcerers had ever written down. Then he pulled out the Darkhold, the dark mirror book that Agatha Harkness had warned her about. "Vishanti is borrowed power," he said. "Darkhold is taken power. Chaos magic, your kind of magic, is grown. You are the source." He told her, gently, that this is what made her the most dangerous person on Earth, not because she wanted to be — but because she had no one to study with, and no one who could pull her back if her grief ever pulled her too far.

The hardest part about chaos magic is that it answers feelings as much as it answers thought. When Wanda is calm, her hands form careful spirals of red and her objects float in straight, neat lines. When she is afraid, her magic shoves the world away from her in jagged red shards. When she misses her brother, her power sings in a high, sad pitch that only she can hear. Doctor Strange told her that one day, she would have to choose what to do with so much power that came so easily. Wanda nodded, and folded her hands, and decided in that moment that she would be the Scarlet Witch who used her chaos to protect, not to take. The red light around her fingers, very gently, agreed.
