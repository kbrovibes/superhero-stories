Wanda Maximoff grew up in a small apartment in the Eastern European country of Sokovia, with her twin brother Pietro at her side and not very much else. When she was ten years old, a missile struck their home and killed their parents. A second missile fell into the rubble next to them, its nose half-buried in the floor, and for two days the twins watched it tick. It never went off. Painted along its side, in clear, factory-stenciled letters, was a name Wanda would never forget: STARK. From that moment, she and Pietro grew up holding a quiet, furious grudge against Tony Stark and the country he came from.

Years later, when a man named Baron Wolfgang von Strucker offered the orphaned twins a chance to gain real power, they took it. Strucker was a leader of the secret organization HYDRA, and he had stolen Loki’s scepter from the wreckage of the Battle of New York. Inside that scepter, glowing softly, was the Mind Stone. One by one, hundreds of test subjects were exposed to its energy, and one by one, they all collapsed. Only two survived. Pietro became impossibly fast, faster than the eye could follow. Wanda woke up with red light coiling around her fingers and the ability to push thoughts, objects, and even time itself out of place.

At first the twins fought against the Avengers, blaming them for everything Sokovia had lost. Wanda used her powers to walk into the minds of Tony Stark, Steve Rogers, Bruce Banner, Thor, and Natasha Romanoff and show each of them their worst fears. It was Bruce Banner’s nightmare that triggered the Hulk in Johannesburg and gave Tony the panicked idea that became Ultron — a peacekeeping robot that wanted to wipe out humanity. Wanda and Pietro realized too late that they had been working for the wrong side. They turned, and they joined the Avengers in the sky above their own country to stop Ultron from dropping a floating city onto the world.

On that day, Wanda learned what being a hero costs. Pietro Maximoff threw himself in front of Hawkeye and a child to absorb a volley of Ultron’s gunfire. He died on the bridge with a small, exhausted smile, and Wanda felt his heart stop from a mile away. Her grief tore Ultron’s primary body apart with a single, screaming gesture of her hand. When the Sokovian sky finally went quiet, Steve Rogers stepped to her side and offered her the same thing he had offered Tony years before — a place on the team, and a chance to use what she had been given for someone other than herself. Wanda Maximoff, the Scarlet Witch of Sokovia, took his hand.
