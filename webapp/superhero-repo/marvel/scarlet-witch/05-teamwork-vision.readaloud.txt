Did you know that Wanda Maximoff and Vision were connected even BEFORE they ever talked to each other? It's true! And this is the most beautiful — and most heartbreaking — friendship in all of the Avengers.

The Mind Stone did something extraordinary. It flooded into Wanda's mind in HYDRA's laboratory and unlocked her chaos magic. ZAP! And it also powers the yellow gem that glows in Vision's forehead. The SAME stone. The same golden energy. Two very different people, linked by one tiny ancient gem.

So when they became friends, it felt like puzzle pieces clicking together! CLICK!

Vision tried to cook Wanda a Sokovian dish called paprikash — a warm stew with red peppers that smelled like home. Did he get it right? Nope! The sauce was too thin. Not enough paprika. Every single time. But Wanda always laughed. And Vision always tried again. Can you think of a sweeter friendship than that?

Then the Sokovia Accords came — big rules about how and when heroes could act — and CRACK! They split the Avengers right down the middle. Wanda chose to fight with Captain America. Vision was asked to keep Wanda at the compound. He did it sadly, reluctantly. When they met again on an airport battlefield in Leipzig — on opposite sides — they tried SO hard not to hurt each other.

But the hardest moment came in Wakanda, the greatest nation on Earth.

Thanos — the most dangerous being in the universe — was collecting all six Infinity Stones. He already had five. The sixth was the yellow Mind Stone glowing in Vision's forehead. To get it, he would have to end Vision.

Vision looked at Wanda with calm, steady eyes. "You have to destroy the stone," he said quietly. "Even if I'm still attached to it."

Think about what he was asking. Think about it! He was asking his best friend — the person he loved most in the world — to do the most painful thing imaginable. And why? To save everyone else.

Wanda put her hand on the yellow gem. She was crying. With one hand she held back Thanos's entire army — WHOOOOSH! — while with the other she destroyed the stone. CRACK! Vision closed his eyes.

Thanos arrived anyway and reversed time with another stone. He took the Mind Stone. But the lesson was still there, shining bright.

Real teamwork doesn't always look like winning. Sometimes it looks like trusting your friend enough to do the hardest, most painful thing — and trusting them to do the same for you.

Wanda and Vision were linked by a yellow gem neither of them had chosen. And they chose each other anyway.
