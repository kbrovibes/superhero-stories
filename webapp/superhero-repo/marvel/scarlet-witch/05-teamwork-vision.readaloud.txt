Wanda Maximoff and Vision met in the strangest of ways. He was born on the same day her brother died — lifted out of a cradle in a laboratory when Thor's lightning struck it and woke something new. Vision floated into the air with a yellow gem glowing in his forehead. It was the Mind Stone — the same stone that HYDRA had once used to unlock Wanda's powers in Sokovia. They were strangers connected by one small, ancient, extraordinary gem that neither of them had asked for.

They could have been afraid of each other. Instead, they became friends. Vision would sit in the kitchen with Wanda and try to cook paprikash, a Sokovian dish she loved. The recipe never came out right. Wanda always laughed anyway. Slowly, in small, quiet ways, two of the strangest people in the world became each other's favorite person.

But the world was not always kind to them. When the Avengers split over the Sokovia Accords, Vision stayed behind and Wanda went away. They fought each other in an airport in Leipzig — gently, as gently as two people can fight — and Wanda flew off into the sky and Vision watched her go, feeling something new: the ache of missing someone.

And do you know what the hardest moment of all was? In Wakanda, when Thanos came hunting the Mind Stone in Vision's forehead, Vision took Wanda's hand and asked her — very quietly — to be the one to destroy it. She wept. She placed her hand over the gem and pushed her red magic into it until the stone shattered. Vision closed his eyes.

From that day on, Wanda carried the truth of what real teamwork means: sometimes love asks you to do the most painful thing imaginable — and you do it anyway, because that is what it means to truly be there for someone.
