Have you ever wanted something SO much that you started to believe it had to be true? Even when, deep down, a quiet little voice in your head told you that wanting wasn't the same as being right? This is the story of the hardest test Wanda Maximoff ever faced. And how a small boy in another universe finally helped her find her way back.

After Wanda became the Scarlet Witch in Westview, she went away to be alone. She found a tiny cabin high up in the mountains, surrounded by fir trees and quiet snow. She brought just one thing with her. A book. It was called the Darkhold, and it had once belonged to Agatha Harkness. The Darkhold's cover glowed a soft, sickly GREEN. CRACKLE went its pages. And the Darkhold whispered to Wanda every single night by candlelight.

The Darkhold told her something amazing and dangerous. It told her that the universe was not just one universe. The universe was actually MILLIONS of universes! All floating side by side like the bubbles in a bubble bath. And somewhere in one of those other universes, the book whispered, there lived ANOTHER Wanda Maximoff. A Wanda who had never lost her babies. A Wanda who tucked Billy and Tommy into bed every night and read them bedtime stories. The Darkhold promised — listen, this is very tricky — the Darkhold promised that Wanda could REACH that other family. And have her boys back at last.

Wanda almost believed it. She read the Darkhold every night. The book's words crept into her dreams. They made her sleepy. She started to forget the Wanda who had walked away from Billy and Tommy in Westview ON PURPOSE because real people mattered more.

That's when there was a KNOCK KNOCK KNOCK on the cabin door. It was Doctor Strange! And with him was a brave young girl in a denim jacket named America Chavez. America had the most AMAZING power. She could kick the air with her sneakers — KAPOW! — and open a STAR-SHAPED PORTAL to any other universe in the multiverse! WHOOSH! Wow!

The Darkhold whispered something dark to Wanda. It said, "If you take America's power for yourself, you can travel to your children." Doctor Strange begged Wanda not to listen. He said, "The Darkhold ALWAYS has a price, Wanda. And the price is always too high!" But Wanda had been listening to grief for so long that she couldn't quite hear anything else.

So Doctor Strange and America RAN. WHOOSH! Through a green-glass universe where the buildings were made of leaves! WHOOSH! Through a black-and-white universe with paint instead of sky! Strange tried spells from Kamar-Taj. He called on the Book of the Vishanti. CRACK! WHOOSH! But nothing was strong enough to stop the Scarlet Witch when the Darkhold was whispering in her ear.

And then, finally — Wanda did it. She found another universe. She found another Billy and Tommy. They were playing on a sofa with their other-universe mom. Wanda burst in. She walked up to them. She said, "It's okay. It's Mama. I'm here."

But here is what happened. The boys looked up at her — and they didn't know her. They saw a stranger with red magic glowing in her eyes. They got SCARED. They cried. They called for their REAL mother.

And in that moment — listen carefully — the REAL Wanda Maximoff, the one underneath all the Darkhold's whispers, woke UP. She heard her own little boys being frightened of her. And it cracked her heart open. She set them down gently. She kissed their foreheads. She whispered, "I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I'm so sorry." And she walked away.

Then Wanda Maximoff did the BRAVEST thing she had ever done in her whole life. She lifted her hands. Red light filled the whole multiverse. And she pulled every single copy of the Darkhold in every single universe DOWN on top of itself. CRACKKK! SHATTERRR! The whole book crumbled into dust forever. No other grieving person would ever, EVER be tempted by it again. Wanda had saved the multiverse from the worst book in it. And she had saved herself.
