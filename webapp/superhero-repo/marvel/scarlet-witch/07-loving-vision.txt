Vision and Wanda Maximoff first became friends in the kitchen of the new Avengers compound. She had lost her brother Pietro. He had been alive for only a few months. Neither of them quite knew what to do with the strange, gentle space between them, so they did the only thing two lonely people could think of: they tried to cook. Vision had read every cookbook ever written, but he had never actually tasted food. Wanda taught him to chop onions, to brown the paprika, to stir the goulash until it sang. He could never quite get it right. She always laughed at him anyway.

When the Sokovia Accords split the Avengers in half, Vision was asked to keep Wanda inside the compound for her own safety. He didn't agree with the order, but he didn't know how else to keep her from the world's anger. Wanda still slipped away with Captain America, because she had decided years ago that she would never be locked inside any room again. The next time she and Vision saw each other was on a battlefield in Leipzig. They tried very hard not to hurt each other. When Wanda boarded Steve Rogers's quinjet and flew off into the clouds, Vision watched her leave and felt, for the first time in his short life, the soft, strange pain of missing someone.

For two quiet years, while the Accords kept the team apart, Wanda and Vision met in secret. They walked through old European cities at dusk — Edinburgh, Prague, a small market in Budapest. Vision wore a disguise that made him look like an ordinary man with brown hair and brown eyes. Wanda held his hand in hers and tried to remember what peace felt like. They were two of the strangest people on the planet, and somehow they had found the warmest, quietest thing in it.

Then Thanos came. He came for the Mind Stone in Vision's forehead — the same yellow gem that had once flooded Wanda's body in HYDRA's cells. In the green forests of Wakanda, with Thanos closing in and no other option left, Vision quietly asked Wanda to be the one to destroy the stone. "It is the only way," he said. He held her hand. She placed her palm over the gem and pushed her red magic into it, weeping. The stone shattered. The man she loved closed his eyes.

Thanos undid it a heartbeat later, but for one shining moment Wanda Maximoff had done the bravest, most loving thing a person can do — she had let go of the one she loved most so the universe could keep its chance to live. That courage stayed inside her always, glowing softly beside her grief like a second heart.
