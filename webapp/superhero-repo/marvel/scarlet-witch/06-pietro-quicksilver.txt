Wanda and Pietro Maximoff were twins, born ten minutes apart in a tall apartment building in Sokovia. Pietro liked to remind Wanda that he was the older one. Wanda liked to remind Pietro that she was the smarter one. They argued about it the way only twins can — quickly, fiercely, and with no real anger underneath. Their mother used to laugh and tell them both they were exactly right.

When their parents were killed by a missile and the second one fell into the apartment without exploding, the twins held each other for two whole days under the rubble. They watched the casing tick. They counted Pietro's heartbeats and then Wanda's. Pietro promised, in a whisper, that he would never leave her alone in a quiet room again. Wanda made him promise twice. She would remember that promise her whole life.

Years later, after Baron Strucker and the Mind Stone, the twins came out of HYDRA's cells with strange new gifts. Pietro could run faster than the eye could follow. The world, to him, looked like it was always standing still. He would zip from one end of a room to the other in less than a heartbeat, pulling a chair out from under Wanda just to make her laugh, then pulling it back before she hit the floor. He could circle the city of Novi Grad before breakfast. He could outrun a bullet. Wanda used to say he ran because he could not bear to sit still long enough for sad thoughts to catch him.

On the day Ultron tried to drop their floating city out of the sky, Pietro fought beside her for the first time as an Avenger. He saved a small Sokovian boy named Costel from the city's collapsing streets. He pulled Wanda out of a burning building. He carried strangers, two at a time, to the rescue boats. When Hawkeye stood out in the open with that little boy in his arms and Ultron began to fire, Pietro was the only one fast enough to reach them. He arrived in a blue blur, threw himself in front of the bullets, and caught every single one.

Wanda felt his heartbeat stop from a mile away. She fell to her knees. The red light around her hands flared and dimmed and flared again, and a small, sad smile broke across Pietro's face as he sank down. "You didn't see that coming," he whispered to Clint Barton, and then he was gone. Years later, when Hawkeye and his wife had their fourth child, they named the little boy Nathaniel Pietro Barton, after the fastest, kindest brother Wanda Maximoff would ever have.
