Have you ever had a brother or sister who could be SO annoying and SO wonderful at the exact same time? That was Pietro Maximoff, Wanda's twin brother. And this is the story of the fastest, funniest, bravest boy in all of Sokovia.

Pietro and Wanda were born ten minutes apart. Pietro NEVER let Wanda forget that he was the older twin. He would tap his wrist and say, "Ten minutes more wisdom, little sister." And Wanda would roll her eyes and say, "Ten minutes more being WRONG, big brother." That's how twins talk to each other. Maybe you know.

When they were ten years old, something terrible happened. A missile fell on their building and their parents were gone. A second missile fell into the rubble next to them — and DIDN'T explode. It just ticked. For two whole days, Wanda and Pietro held each other and watched it tick. Pietro whispered a promise to her. He said, "I will never leave you alone in a quiet room again." He made that promise twice, because Wanda asked him to.

Years later, after HYDRA's scary experiments, Pietro woke up with the most amazing power in the world. He could run FASTER than ANYTHING. Faster than a car. Faster than a bullet. Faster than your eye can blink! WHOOOOSH! To Pietro, when he ran, the whole world looked like it was standing perfectly still. He could zip across a whole city before breakfast. He could pull Wanda's chair out from under her, watch her start to fall, and then ZIP back and put it under her again before she hit the floor. WHOOSH! That was his favorite joke.

Wanda used to say something very smart about her brother. She said Pietro ran so fast because he could not BEAR to sit still long enough for sad thoughts to catch him. Isn't that interesting? Sometimes the people who move the fastest are the ones who feel the most.

On the day Ultron tried to drop the floating city of Novi Grad out of the sky, Pietro became an Avenger. He saved a little Sokovian boy named Costel from the falling streets. He pulled strangers — TWO AT A TIME — to safety. He pulled Wanda herself out of a burning building. WHOOSH! WHOOSH! WHOOSH!

Then came the hardest moment. Hawkeye was out in the open holding the little boy Costel in his arms. Ultron's gun started to fire — CRACK! CRACK! CRACK! Pietro was the only one fast enough to reach them in time. He flew in like a blue blur and threw himself in front of every single bullet. He caught them ALL. To save them.

Wanda felt his heart stop from a mile away. She fell to her knees. Her red magic flickered like a candle in the wind. Pietro looked up at Hawkeye with a small, tired smile and whispered, "You didn't see that coming." And then he was gone.

That was the day Wanda Maximoff lost the bravest boy she ever knew. But here is the most beautiful part of the story. Years later, when Hawkeye and his wife had a brand new baby boy — their fourth child — do you know what they named him? They named him Nathaniel Pietro Barton. Because some heroes you remember every single day for the rest of your life. And Pietro Maximoff was that kind of hero.
