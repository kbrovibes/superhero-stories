Wanda Maximoff met Vision in a strange way. He was born in the same Sokovian battle in which her brother died, woken up from Tony Stark and Bruce Banner’s laboratory when Thor struck the cradle with Mjolnir’s lightning. Vision floated to his feet for the first time with the Mind Stone glowing in his forehead — the same stone that had once flooded Wanda’s body in HYDRA’s cells. They were strangers tied together by a single yellow gem that neither of them had asked for. It would have been easy to be afraid of each other. Instead, they slowly, carefully became friends.

Vision was the calmest member of the Avengers. He had been alive for less than a year, but his mind held a thousand years of stored knowledge from JARVIS, the artificial intelligence Tony had built and kept for half his adult life. While the team trained, Vision often sat in a kitchen at the new Avengers compound with Wanda, trying to learn how to cook a Sokovian dish called paprikash so she would feel a little more at home. The recipe never came out right, but she always laughed when he tried. Slowly, over many small evenings, two of the strangest people on the team realized they had become each other’s favorite person.

When the Sokovia Accords split the Avengers in half, Vision stayed at the compound and Wanda was kept inside it as if she were a danger to be contained. Vision didn’t want to keep her locked up; he simply didn’t know how else to keep her safe. Wanda still went with Captain America and his rebels, because she had decided long ago that she would never be locked away from the world again. She and Vision met again on a battlefield in Leipzig, and they tried very hard not to hurt each other. When Wanda flew away into the sky in the back of Steve’s quinjet, Vision watched her go and felt, for the first time, the soft pain of missing someone.

The hardest moment of their teamwork came in Wakanda. Thanos was hunting the Mind Stone, and Vision quietly asked Wanda to be the one to destroy it. "It is the only way," he said. He held her hand. She placed her palm over the gem and pushed her red magic into it, weeping. The stone shattered. The man she loved closed his eyes. And for one heartbeat, Wanda Maximoff knew she had done the right, terrible, brave thing. Thanos undid it a second later, but the lesson stayed with her always: real teamwork sometimes means doing the hardest thing in the world for the friend at your side, and trusting them to do the same for you.
