After Westview, after Agatha, after Wanda Maximoff became the Scarlet Witch, she went away to a small cabin in the mountains to be alone. She brought one book with her. It was the Darkhold — a glowing green book of dark magic that had once belonged to Agatha. The Darkhold whispered. It promised. It told Wanda that somewhere in the great branching multiverse — in one of the millions of other Earths floating beside this one — there lived another Wanda Maximoff who had never lost her babies. A Wanda who tucked Billy and Tommy into bed every single night. The book promised she could find that other family, and reach them, and have her children back at last.

Wanda almost believed it. She read the Darkhold every night by candlelight. The book's words got into her dreams. They softened her edges. She started to forget the Wanda who had said goodbye to Billy and Tommy in Westview and walked away on purpose. She started to think the universe owed her her children back.

That is when Doctor Strange knocked on her cabin door. He brought with him a brave young girl named America Chavez, who had a remarkable gift — she could open star-shaped portals between every universe in the multiverse, just by kicking the air with her sneakers. The Darkhold had told Wanda that if she took America's gift for herself, she could travel to her children. Strange begged Wanda not to listen to the book. He told her that the Darkhold had a price, and the price was always too high. Wanda did not listen. She had been listening to grief for so long that she could not quite hear anything else.

Wanda chased Strange and America across worlds. They ran through a green-glass universe where the buildings were made of leaves. They ran through a black-and-white universe with paint instead of sky. Strange did everything he could think of. He tried spells from Kamar-Taj. He called on the Book of the Vishanti. Nothing was strong enough to stop the Scarlet Witch when she had the Darkhold whispering in her ear.

In the end, it was a little boy who saved her. Wanda did finally find another universe with another Billy and Tommy in it. She reached them. They looked up at her and saw, not their mother, but a stranger with red magic in her eyes. They were frightened of her. They cried for their real mother. And Wanda — the real Wanda, the one underneath the Darkhold's whispers — heard the sound of her own boys being afraid of her, and woke up from the longest dream of her life. She set them back down. She kissed their foreheads. She walked away. And then, with the last red light in her palms, she pulled every copy of the Darkhold in every universe down on top of itself and crushed the book into dust forever, so no other grieving person would ever have to choose what she had almost chosen. It was the bravest thing she had ever done.
