Close your eyes for a moment. Can you picture a small apartment in a faraway country called Sokovia, where two children named Wanda and Pietro Maximoff lived with their parents? It was a cozy home — books on the shelves, the smell of dinner on the stove, the muffled sound of the city outside. It was a good and ordinary life. And then, one terrible night, everything changed.

BOOM.

A missile fell from the sky and struck their home. Wanda and Pietro hid beneath a collapsed table, holding each other very, very tightly. They were only ten years old. The world they knew was gone in an instant — but Wanda would not be gone with it. She looked at the wall in front of her, and she noticed something on the side of the missile that had landed nearby, quietly, without exploding. Two words stamped into the metal: STARK INDUSTRIES.

She burned those words into her heart, because even at ten years old, Wanda knew that someday she would want to understand why.

Years passed. Wanda and her twin brother Pietro grew up in the rubble of Sokovia, surviving, searching for a way to make things better for their broken country. That search led them to a man named Baron Wolfgang von Strucker, who worked for a shadowy organization called HYDRA. Strucker made them a promise — he would give them the power to protect their people. All they had to do was volunteer for his experiments.

The experiment involved a magical object called the Mind Stone, a glowing yellow gem that carried some of the oldest and deepest power in the universe. Hundreds of other people had tried before Wanda and Pietro. None of them survived. But Wanda stepped forward anyway — because she had a will as strong as iron and a heart that burned like a star.

And something extraordinary happened.

The yellow light of the Mind Stone washed over her — and instead of breaking her, it opened something. Can you picture it? Like a door swinging wide inside her chest, letting out a bright red glow she had been carrying all along without knowing. The Mind Stone didn't give Wanda her power. It unlocked the power that was already hers, waiting quietly since the day she was born.

Pietro came out the other side moving as fast as lightning — WHOOSH — a silver blur that lapped the whole city in seconds. And Wanda? She raised her hands, and red energy swirled around her fingers like ribbons of smoke and flame. She could move things without touching them. She could reach into someone's mind and show them their deepest fears. She could bend reality itself, as if the whole world were made of soft clay and she was the sculptor.

They called her the Scarlet Witch — though she didn't know that name yet. She and Pietro joined together, fighting for Sokovia's future, even if the path they chose was crooked at first. But the important thing is this: from the very beginning, Wanda Maximoff was something the world had never quite seen before. A girl from a small apartment in a small city, who survived the unimaginable — and came out the other side glowing red and magnificent.

She had lost so much. But she had found something too: a power born not in a laboratory, but in her own heart. Ancient witches across the centuries had written about a great Scarlet Witch, a figure of chaos magic so deep it could redraw the rules of reality itself. They had predicted she would come. They just didn't know she would be a girl from Sokovia who once hid under a table with her brother.

Can you picture it? A ten-year-old Wanda, shaking in the dark. And then years later, that same Wanda, standing tall with red light blazing from her palms — not because someone gave her power, but because she had always carried it, waiting for the right moment to shine.

That is how the Scarlet Witch came to be. And her story was only just beginning.
