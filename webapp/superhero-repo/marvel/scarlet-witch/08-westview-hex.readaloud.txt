Have you ever wanted something SO badly that you imagined exactly what it would look like — your perfect house, your perfect family, your perfect day? Well, this is what happened when Wanda Maximoff's wishes got SO big that they spilled right out of her and made themselves real. It's called the story of the Westview Hex. And it might be the most beautiful, most heartbreaking story Wanda ever lived.

After all the battles, after losing her brother Pietro and her best friend Vision, Wanda was very, very sad. So one day she got into a small car and drove all by herself to a sleepy little town in New Jersey called Westview. She stopped at an empty lot. Vision had bought it for them, before he was gone. In Wanda's hand was a folded piece of paper. On it, in Vision's neat handwriting, were four little words: "To grow old in." When Wanda read those words, her heart got so big and so sad and so full that her magic just — WHOOOSH! — spilled out of her in a glowing red bubble. The bubble grew and grew until it covered the WHOLE town.

Inside the bubble, something MAGICAL happened. Westview turned into a black-and-white television show! Like the kind your grandparents might have watched a long, long time ago. There was a sweet little house with a white picket fence. There was a Vision — a smiling, happy Vision! — coming through the front door to say, "Honey, I'm home!" The doorbell played a cheerful little tune. DING-DONG! The neighbors all wore aprons. They all laughed at exactly the right moments. HA HA HA! For the first time in years, Wanda was not alone.

And here's the most amazing part. The decades inside the bubble FLEW by. One day it was the 1950s in black and white. The next day it was the 1960s with color and groovy music! Then the 1970s with bell bottoms! Then the 1980s with HUGE hairdos! Then the 1990s with bright shirts and silly jokes! Wanda's little house got new wallpaper every time. Vision got new sweaters. The neighbors got new haircuts. WHOOSH! The years just zipped right by.

And then — oh, this is the best part — Wanda discovered something incredible. She was going to have BABIES. Not one but TWO. Two impossible little boys named Billy and Tommy. They were born one magical afternoon, and by dinnertime they were already TODDLERS. By the weekend they were TEN YEARS OLD. Because Wanda's magic doesn't run on regular calendars. It runs on LOVE. Billy could read minds, like his mom. And Tommy could run super-duper fast — WHOOSH! — just like his Uncle Pietro. Wanda had her whole little family at last.

But here is the sad part. Outside the red bubble, the real people of Westview were trapped inside Wanda's story. They were playing parts in a show they didn't want to be in. They couldn't go home. They couldn't hug their own families. A group called S.W.O.R.D., and a brave woman named Monica Rambeau, pressed against the boundary and tried to figure out who was making this happen.

When Wanda finally understood what she had done — when she saw the real people of Westview frozen and hurting — she did the bravest, hardest thing she had ever done. CRACKKK went the bubble as it started to come undone. She kissed Billy and Tommy on the forehead and tucked them in for bed. She held Vision's hand one last time. She told them she loved them. And then she let the Hex go. The bubble vanished. The real town blinked back into the warm sunlight.

And Wanda Maximoff walked away from the warmest dream she would ever have. Because real people, she decided, matter more than any dream — even the most beautiful one in the world.
