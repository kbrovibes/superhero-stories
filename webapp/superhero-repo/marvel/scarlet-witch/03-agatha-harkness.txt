After the universe lost Vision in the snap and got him back as a quieter, paler version of himself, Wanda Maximoff drove to a small New Jersey town called Westview and accidentally rewrote it. Her grief was so large that her chaos magic spread out from her in a glowing red bubble, building her a perfect black-and-white sitcom life inside it, complete with a new Vision and two impossible little boys named Billy and Tommy. The people of Westview were trapped inside her story, playing parts in a show she didn’t know she was writing. From the outside, S.W.O.R.D. agents and Monica Rambeau pressed their faces against the boundary and tried to figure out who, exactly, was in charge.

A neighbor named Agnes lived next door inside the bubble. She wore pearls, baked snickerdoodles, and was always, always smiling. What Wanda did not realize was that Agnes was actually Agatha Harkness, a witch who was several hundred years old and had been watching Wanda’s power from a distance for a very long time. Agatha had survived the witches of Salem by stealing their magic, and she recognized that Wanda’s chaos magic was something the books of witchcraft had warned about for centuries — the power of the Scarlet Witch.

When Agatha finally revealed herself, the entire street rewrote itself for a moment, every house collapsing into a single dark basement filled with candles and runes. "It was Agatha all along," she sang to a stunned Wanda, and the fight that followed was unlike any the Avengers had ever seen. There were no fists and no shields. Agatha hurled purple lightning. Wanda built a red wall of force. They tore through the streets of Westview as Wanda’s boys watched in terror from the porch of her own house.

Wanda won the way she so often did — by being more clever than her enemies expected. She used Agatha’s own runes against her, painting them in red light across the inside of the hex bubble so that no witch trapped within could use her magic except for Wanda herself. With Agatha frozen and powerless, Wanda took back the chaos magic Agatha had tried to steal, and finally accepted what she had become. The book Agatha had warned her about — the Darkhold — appeared on the table of her little cabin afterward, glowing with a soft green light. Wanda Maximoff sat down with it, opened the first page, and began, for the first time, to truly understand the strange, sad, magnificent witch she had grown into being.
