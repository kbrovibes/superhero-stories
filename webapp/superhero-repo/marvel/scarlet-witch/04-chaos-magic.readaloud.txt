There is magic in this universe that wizards study in great libraries, turning pages and practicing hand gestures called mudras for years. Doctor Strange learned his power that way, at a school called Kamar-Taj, reading from a sacred text called the Book of the Vishanti.

Wanda's magic is different. It does not come from any book or any school. It does not borrow power from another dimension. It grows from inside her, as natural as breathing — and as hard to control as a thunderstorm. It is called chaos magic.

For years, Wanda thought the Mind Stone in Sokovia had given her powers. But Doctor Strange sat with her one evening and explained something that changed everything. The stone had not created her magic. It had only unlocked what was already inside her. Ancient witches had written about this for centuries — a kind of power so strong it could redraw the laws of reality. They gave the person who carried it a name: the Scarlet Witch. They prayed she would never be born.

And do you know what chaos magic is like? When Wanda is calm, her hands form neat spirals of red light. When she is afraid, the magic shoves the world away in jagged shards. When she misses her brother Pietro, her power sings a quiet, sad note that only she can hear. Her feelings and her magic are the same thing.

Doctor Strange told her honestly that this made her the most dangerous person on Earth. Not because she wanted to be — but because she had no teacher.

From that day on, Wanda chose what to do with that power. She would be the Scarlet Witch who used chaos to protect. The red light curling at her fingertips, very gently, agreed.
