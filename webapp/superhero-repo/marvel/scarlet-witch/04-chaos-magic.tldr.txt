• Chaos magic: not borrowed or disciplined like Doctor Strange's sorcery — it makes; it bends rules
• Doctor Strange studies at Kamar-Taj using mudras, Sanskrit texts, and the Book of the Vishanti
• The Mind Stone in Sokovia didn't give Wanda her magic — it unlocked magic she already carried
• Ancient witches wrote of a magic strong enough to redraw the laws of reality: the Scarlet Witch prophecy
• Doctor Strange showed Wanda two books: the Book of the Vishanti (borrowed power) vs. the Darkhold (taken power); chaos magic is grown — Wanda is the source
• Chaos magic answers emotions as much as thought: calm = careful spirals; fear = jagged shards; grief = a high, sad pitch
• Strange warned that Wanda had no teacher and no one to pull her back if grief pulled her too far
• Wanda decided to be the Scarlet Witch who uses chaos to protect, not to take
• The Darkhold appeared after Agatha's defeat; Wanda began to study it in her cabin
• Lesson: power grown from within is the most dangerous kind — and the most personal choice to wield
