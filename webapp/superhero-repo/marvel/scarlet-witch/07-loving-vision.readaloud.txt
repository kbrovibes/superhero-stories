Have you ever had a best friend who was a LITTLE bit different from everybody else? Maybe they were SUPER smart, or SUPER quiet, or had a funny way of looking at the world? This is the story of Wanda Maximoff and the kindest, strangest best friend she ever had. His name was Vision.

Vision was not exactly a person, and he was not exactly a robot. He was something brand new. His skin was the color of a ripe plum. His eyes were soft and golden. In the middle of his forehead glowed a beautiful yellow gem called the Mind Stone. He had only been alive for a few months when he met Wanda. She had just lost her brother Pietro. Both of them were lonely in a way only they could understand.

They first became friends in the kitchen of the Avengers compound. Wanda decided to teach Vision how to cook a Sokovian dish called paprikash. It is a kind of stew with onions and a special red spice. Vision had read every cookbook ever written. But here is the funny thing — Vision could not actually EAT food. He didn't have a stomach! He just wanted to make it for HER. He chopped the onions. He stirred the pot. He sniffed the steam very politely. SNIFF! And he ALWAYS got the recipe a little bit wrong. Wanda laughed every single time. WHIRRRR went his floating spoon. PLOP went the wrong amount of paprika. It was the warmest, silliest kitchen on the planet.

Then a big argument split the Avengers in half — something called the Sokovia Accords. Vision was asked to keep Wanda safely inside the compound. He didn't really want to. He just didn't know how else to keep her from the world's anger. But Wanda had decided LONG ago that she would never be locked inside any room ever again. So she slipped away. The next time she and Vision saw each other, they were on opposite sides of a great big battle in Leipzig. They tried so, so hard not to hurt each other. When Wanda finally flew away in the back of Captain America's quinjet, Vision watched her leave and felt — for the very first time in his life — the soft, strange ache of missing someone. Have you ever felt that? It's not a fun feeling. But it means you love someone.

For two whole years, the two of them met in secret. Vision wore a disguise that made him look like an ordinary man with brown hair and brown eyes. They walked together through beautiful old European cities — Edinburgh in Scotland, Prague in the Czech Republic, a tiny market in Budapest. They held hands. They watched the sunsets. They were two of the strangest people on the whole planet, and they had found the warmest, quietest thing in the whole world.

But then Thanos came. WHOOSH! The big purple villain wanted to collect all six Infinity Stones, and one of those stones was the yellow gem in Vision's forehead. Wanda and Vision and the Avengers ran to the green forests of Wakanda to make a last stand. They fought and fought and fought. But Thanos was getting closer. There was only one way to save the universe. The Mind Stone had to be destroyed. And the only one strong enough to destroy it was Wanda.

Vision held her hand. He said softly, "It is the only way." Wanda looked into his eyes for a very long time. Then she lifted her hand. Red light poured out of her palm and into the Mind Stone. CRACKKK! The stone shattered. Vision closed his eyes.

Thanos undid it a moment later — that's a story for another day. But for one shining, terrible moment, Wanda Maximoff did the bravest thing a person can EVER do. She let go of the one she loved most so the whole universe could have a chance to live. And THAT kind of courage — listen carefully — that kind of courage stays inside a person forever, glowing right next to their heart.
