• Long before Wanda was born, the old witches wrote about her in their books — "the Scarlet Witch" — underlined three times in red ink
• They begged the universe never to let her exist, because the Scarlet Witch would not borrow magic like sorcerers — she would MAKE it
• Agatha Harkness has been alive for 340 years; she survived the Salem witch trials by stealing other witches' magic
• When she feels Wanda's red glow ripple across the East Coast, she rents the house next door in Westview, calls herself Agnes, and bakes snickerdoodles
• She watches Wanda for weeks, measuring her power, before pulling back the curtain in her candlelit basement
• "It was Agatha all along!" she sings — and shows Wanda the old prophecy: "You weren't supposed to exist"
• For one long moment Wanda almost believes the witches were right to be afraid of her
• Wanda paints Agatha's own runes in red light across the Hex ceiling, freezes her — and lifts into the air as the Scarlet Witch with a scarlet crown, choosing quietly to be good
