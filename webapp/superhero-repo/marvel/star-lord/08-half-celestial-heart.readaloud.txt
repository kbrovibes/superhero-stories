Imagine you found out you had a SECRET SUPERPOWER. Imagine you could make a glowing ball of light appear right between your hands — woosh, just like that — and you could use it to do almost anything. Wouldn't that be exciting? Wouldn't you want to use it ALL the time? Well. Listen to what Peter Quill did. Because this is a story about a kid who got a magical secret superpower, and then chose, very carefully, what to do with it.

Peter Quill had grown up thinking he was just a regular Earth boy. A regular Earth boy who had been kidnapped by space pirates, sure. A regular Earth boy who lived in a spaceship and stole stuff for a living, fine. But still — just a regular kid on the inside. No glowing powers. No magic.

Then one day his father Ego showed up — Ego, the Celestial, the very old very powerful being from outer space — and Ego told Peter something that changed everything. "You are MY son, Peter. And you have my power inside you. Hold out your hands."

Peter held them out. Ego put his own glowing palms underneath. And between Peter's hands — POOF! — a little ball of blue-and-gold light appeared. WHOOSH! GLOW! HUM! It pulsed gently, like a heartbeat. Peter laughed out loud. It was the coolest thing he had EVER done.

He could feel the power inside him now. He could imagine all the things he could do with it. He could save planets! He could light up whole galaxies! He could be a SPACE SUPERHERO with GLOWING HANDS! How cool was that?

But then Peter found out something terrible. His father Ego didn't want them to use the glowing light to save planets. Ego wanted to use it to DESTROY them. To take over every world in the galaxy and replace them with little pieces of himself. And Ego wanted Peter to help. Uh oh.

So Peter had to make a choice. On a hillside of golden ground, with the planet shaking around him, with Ego whispering, "Be a god with me, Peter, be a GOD with me," Peter looked down at the small glowing ball in his hand.

And he thought about Yondu. The blue-skinned pirate who had raised him.

And he thought about his mom. Who had made him the Awesome Mix tape.

And he thought about Rocket the raccoon, and Groot the tree-friend, and Gamora and Drax and Mantis, who were all flying toward him in a Ravager ship to come save him.

And Peter Quill made the bravest choice of his whole entire life.

He chose THEM. He chose to be a normal human boy from Missouri with a cassette tape. He let the glowing ball of light shrink down — smaller, smaller, almost gone. Just a tiny warm ember in his chest. Just enough to remember it was there.

Here is the thing about Peter's choice. It is way harder than you would think. The hardest power in the whole universe is not the power to make things go BOOM. The hardest power is the power NOT to. The power to say, "I could do this — but I'm not going to."

Years later, his friend Mantis asked him, quietly, if he ever missed the glowing light. Peter thought for a long time. He looked out the window of his spaceship at the stars going by. And he shook his head.

"That part wasn't mine," he said softly. "That part was Ego's. The Missouri-boy part — the part that loves my mom and Yondu and all of you — THAT part is mine. That's the part I want to keep."

Mantis nodded. She put her gentle hand on his sleeve. And she did not have to say a word, because she could feel that he was telling the truth.

What part of YOU is your favorite part? Hold onto that part. It is your superpower too.
