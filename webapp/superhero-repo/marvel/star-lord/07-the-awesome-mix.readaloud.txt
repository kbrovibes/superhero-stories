Do you have a favorite song? One that makes you want to dance around the kitchen, or jump on the couch, or wiggle your toes inside your shoes? Music is one of the most magical things in the whole universe. And in this story, music is going to be exactly that — magical.

Way back in 1988, when Peter Quill was just a little boy on Earth, his mom Meredith made him something very special. It was a cassette tape — that's a little plastic box that holds music inside, and you put it into a thing called a Walkman to play it. Meredith called the tape AWESOME MIX VOL. 1. She wrote the title in her own neat handwriting on the little label.

She recorded every single song onto that tape, one at a time, from her own radio. CLICK. RECORD. "Come and Get Your Love" by Redbone. CLICK. STOP. CLICK. RECORD. "Hooked on a Feeling" by Blue Swede. The whole tape had twelve songs on it. Twelve songs that Meredith Quill loved most in the whole world.

The night she died in the hospital, the tape was wrapped up in a little package on her bedside, with Peter's name written on it. He took it with him when the Ravagers' blue beam pulled him up, up, up into space. WHOOOSH! And from that day on, that tape went EVERYWHERE with him.

He played it in the cockpit of his spaceship the Milano while flying through asteroids. WHIZZ! BOOM! He played it during prison breakouts. CLANK! ZAP! He even played it in the middle of fights — dancing across broken stones on the planet Morag, twirling little space lizards around by their tails like dance partners. DOO DOO DOO, DOO-DOO-DOO!

Rocket the raccoon hated it. "These songs are ancient, Quill!" Drax tried to learn the words and got them all wrong. "I AM SINGING THE EARTH SONG OF LOVE!" Baby Groot, though — baby Groot LOVED the tape. He bobbed his tiny wooden head perfectly on the beat. Wiggle-wiggle, BOP-BOP-BOP!

One day Peter's old Walkman finally broke. CRACK. SNAP. Uh-oh. Peter almost cried. But the Nova Corps — the space police on Xandar — gave him a brand new music player called a Zune. It held FIVE HUNDRED SONGS! Peter scrolled through it like he had never seen anything so fancy. "Earth music has gotten WEIRDER, you guys," he told his team. He still kept his mom's original cassette tape stuck to the back of the Zune, just to keep it safe.

Now here is the most special part of the story, so listen close.

Many, many years later, after a HUGE adventure, Peter sat alone in his ship one quiet night. And he opened a second package. His mom had made another tape. AWESOME MIX VOL. 2. She had hidden it inside the first package long ago and written on it, "For when you're a little older."

Peter pressed play. He heard a song called "Father and Son" by Cat Stevens. He heard a song called "Brandy" by Looking Glass. And he understood something amazing. Even though his mom had been gone for almost thirty years, she was still — somehow — taking care of him. She was still picking out songs to help him grow up.

Because that is what love does. Love is bigger than time. And sometimes — sometimes — love comes wrapped up in a little plastic box full of music.
