Okay — what if I told you that Peter Quill's dad was an entire PLANET? Like, the whole planet WAS his dad. The mountains? Dad. The golden waterfalls? Dad. The glowing core at the center? Definitely dad. Can you even imagine?!

Peter had wondered about his father his whole life. Then one day — ZOOM! — Ego arrived in a golden ship with a warm smile and the most extraordinary explanation ever given. He was a Celestial — one of the oldest, most powerful beings in the universe. So ancient and powerful that his body had grown into a beautiful, enormous world. He invited Peter, Gamora, and Drax to come home with him.

And the planet WAS beautiful! Golden light everywhere. Strange flowing rocks. Waterfalls that glowed. Peter gasped!

Then Ego showed him something incredible: you are my son, and you carry Celestial power inside you. He showed Peter how to form a glowing ball of light between his palms. Peter made one — SHWOOM! — and laughed out loud. He finally had a dad!

But there was someone at Ego's side named Mantis — a quiet, gentle empath with antennae on her head — who pulled Gamora aside and whispered the truth.

Ego's plan? He had been seeding pieces of himself on every planet in the galaxy for thousands of years, waiting to grow and take over everything. He had spread children across space for millennia hoping one would carry the Celestial gene he needed to power this expansion. None had. And when they couldn't help him? He had ended them. Their bones were in a cave beneath the planet. Thousands of children, gone forever.

And one more terrible thing: Meredith Quill — Peter's mother — had been given the illness that took her away. By Ego. Because he had started to love her. And loving someone made him feel weak. So he ended her.

Peter reached for his Quad Blasters. His hands were shaking. He pointed them straight at his father's face.

BOOM! Rocket and Groot dived deep into the planet and planted explosive crystals in the glowing core! WHOOOOSH! Peter and Ego fought in great bursts of gold and blue Celestial light on a hillside!

Then Peter made his choice. He let the Celestial power go. He chose to be human — because humans are the kind of creatures who don't do those things to people they love.

And Yondu — the blue-skinned pirate who had stolen Peter as a boy and kept him safe all those years — put his own space suit on Peter and floated away into the cold black sky. Gone forever.

"He may have been your father, kid," Yondu had said. "But he wasn't your daddy."

And that made Yondu the greatest dad in the galaxy.
