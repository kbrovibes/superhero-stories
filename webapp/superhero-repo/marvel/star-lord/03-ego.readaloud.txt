Peter Quill had wondered about his father for most of his life. And then, one day, his father appeared. Ego arrived in a golden ship with a warm smile and the most extraordinary explanation: he was a Celestial — one of the oldest beings in the universe, so ancient and so powerful that his body had grown into an entire planet. He took Peter and Gamora and Drax home to that planet, and it was beautiful in every direction. And he told Peter the most wonderful, most dangerous thing: you are my son, and you carry my Celestial power inside you.

Ego showed Peter how to form a ball of glowing light between his palms. Peter laughed out loud. He had a dad.

But then Mantis — a quiet, gentle empath who had lived at Ego's side her whole life — found Gamora alone and told her the truth. Ego's beautiful planet was seeded with pieces of himself waiting to grow across the entire galaxy. He needed a Celestial heir's power to make it happen. He had scattered thousands of children across space over thousands of years hoping one would inherit the gene. They had not. And when they could not help him, Ego had ended them. Their bones were in a cave beneath the planet.

And there was one more thing. The illness that had ended Peter's mother, Meredith Quill — Ego had given it to her. Because he had felt himself falling in love with her. And he could not allow that.

Peter reached for his Quad Blasters and pointed them at his father. Rocket and Groot dropped exploding crystals into the planet's core. Peter and Ego fought in great bursts of Celestial light on a golden hillside. Then Peter made his choice: he let the light go. He chose to be human again.

Yondu Udonta, the blue-skinned pirate who had taken Peter as a boy, put his own space suit on Peter and floated away into the cold of space.

"He may have been your father, kid," Yondu had said. "But he wasn't your daddy."
