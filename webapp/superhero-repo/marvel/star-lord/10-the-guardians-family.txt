By the time the Guardians of the Galaxy settled on Knowhere — the hollowed-out skull of an ancient Celestial floating in deep space — Peter Quill's small family had grown into a real one. There was Rocket, who had recently survived the worst journey of his life. There was Groot, no longer a sapling but a tall, broad, branching teenager with deep growling words. There was Drax, who had finally learned (mostly) what jokes were. There was Mantis, who had become the brightest and bravest member of the team. There was Nebula, Gamora's sister, who now ran the day-to-day operations of Knowhere with surprising kindness. There was Kraglin, the Ravager who had inherited Yondu's red metal fin, still learning to whistle the yaka arrow into flight. And there was Cosmo — a beautiful golden Soviet space dog with telepathic powers and the saddest, most loyal eyes in the galaxy.

Peter had thought, after the loss of Gamora to Thanos, that his family was broken forever. He had thought that the team would never feel the same. He had been wrong. The new Guardians had grown in ways he had not predicted. Rocket, after the long, painful adventure of facing his old creator the High Evolutionary, had brought a small entourage home with him — a memory of his cage-mates from the laboratory. Lylla the otter, Teefs the walrus, and Floor the rabbit had not survived to leave with him. Rocket carved their names into the inside of his workbench so he would never forget them, and Peter let him cry about it for as many nights as he needed to. That was the kind of family they were.

The Benatar had been replaced, and replaced again. Their newest ship was the Bowie, named after one of the singers on Awesome Mix Vol. 1. It was a sleek, fast vessel painted in cosmic blue and orange, with enough bunks for everyone, a kitchen big enough for Drax to cook for the whole team, and a music system Rocket had wired to start playing whenever Peter walked into the cockpit. Cosmo curled up on the captain's chair when Peter was not in it, his big golden tail thumping softly against the leather, and projected gentle thoughts into the minds of whoever was sad that day. Mantis kept a herb garden in the back. Nebula ran a small data station from the engine room.

When Peter finally went home to Earth — back to Missouri, back to the small farm where his grandfather still lived — the whole family came with him. Grandpa Quill made pancakes. He cried when he saw Peter alive. He made twenty extra pancakes for Drax. He fed bacon to Cosmo. He let Groot help with the dishes. He stood on the porch as the Bowie lifted back off into the sky and waved until the ship was gone. Peter Quill, half-Celestial pirate, leader of the Guardians of the Galaxy, sat in the cockpit with his strange, wonderful family around him and finally understood: the only thing better than finding a family in space is bringing them home.
