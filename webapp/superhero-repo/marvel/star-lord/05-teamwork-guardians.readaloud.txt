The Guardians of the Galaxy did not start as a team. They started as five strangers locked in the same prison block on a space station called the Kyln. A half-human pirate. An assassin. A man with no sense of humor. A talking raccoon. A tree who could only say three words.

On paper, they should have destroyed each other before breakfast. Instead, somewhere between escaping the Kyln and saving the planet Xandar, they became something nobody planned for: a family.

The strange thing about the Guardians was that nobody was really in charge. Peter Quill acted like the leader because he had the ship and the loudest voice. Gamora actually was the leader in any moment that needed a plan. Rocket was in charge of anything with an engine. Drax was in charge the moment the only answer was to charge forward. And Groot was the one who, when things got darkest, wrapped his branches around everyone and held them together.

They argued about every single plan. Then they carried it out perfectly.

And do you know what happened when the universe's largest threat — Thanos — came to their corner of the galaxy? The Guardians crashed into the Avengers. Thor fell onto the Benatar's windshield. Peter Quill and Tony Stark nearly argued themselves off a planet called Titan. Peter made a mistake driven by grief — and it cost everyone dearly. He had to learn, the hardest way, that being a Guardian meant staying steady even when your heart was breaking, and finding the grace to forgive and keep going.

The Benatar flew on. Peter's mother's mixtape played on. Mantis, who had joined them after Ego, translated little Groot's three words for everyone.

And that is how Peter Quill, the lost boy from Missouri, finally understood what his mother had meant all along. She had called him her Star-Lord first. Now he was theirs.
