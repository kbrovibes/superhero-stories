Ronan the Accuser was one of the most feared warriors in the Kree Empire. He carried a long, two-pronged hammer called the Cosmi-Rod, painted his face with black war paint, and believed, sincerely, that anyone who disagreed with the Kree should be removed from the universe. When Thanos offered Ronan a deal — bring me the Orb hidden on Morag, and I will give you the planet Xandar — Ronan agreed. The trouble was, when Ronan opened the Orb and saw the glowing purple Power Stone inside, he decided he did not need Thanos at all. He pressed the stone into the head of his own hammer, and the Cosmi-Rod sang.

Ronan’s ship, the Dark Aster, descended on Xandar like a closing fist. The Nova Corps scrambled hundreds of small fighters into the sky to form a glowing yellow net of energy below the ship, trying to slow its drop into the city of Xandar Prime. Star-Lord and his strange new crew — Gamora, Drax, Rocket, and Groot — flew the Milano straight through Ronan’s fleet, blasting his necromonger ships out of the sky two at a time. Peter Quill, for the first time in his life, was not trying to escape a fight. He was trying to win one.

When the Milano crashed into the bridge of the Dark Aster, Peter and his crew faced Ronan in person. Ronan struck the floor of the ship with the Cosmi-Rod and the entire bridge cracked. Drax screamed and charged. Gamora’s blades flashed. Rocket fired everything he had. Groot wrapped his branches around all four of his friends, formed a leafy ball, and absorbed the impact of the Dark Aster crashing into the streets of Xandar Prime. He sacrificed himself to keep them alive. When the dust settled, only one twig of Groot remained, and Ronan was rising out of the rubble with the Power Stone still glowing on the head of his hammer.

Peter Quill, who had never been a planner, suddenly had the only plan that mattered. He started dancing. He sang loud, off-key, on purpose. He pointed at Ronan and challenged him to a dance-off in the middle of the smoking street. Ronan, stunned, lifted the Cosmi-Rod to end him. In that one heartbeat of distraction, Rocket fired a beam straight at the head of the hammer and knocked the Power Stone loose. The purple gem rolled across the cobblestones. Peter dove for it. The stone’s energy nearly tore him apart — until Gamora, Drax, and Rocket all caught his hand and shared the power between the four of them. They turned it on Ronan as a single, glowing fist, and the Accuser dissolved in a wave of purple light. The Guardians of the Galaxy had won their first impossible day, and not one of them, not even Peter Quill, had quite understood how.
