Peter Quill was eight years old in the year 1988 when his world ended. He was sitting in a Missouri hospital waiting room listening to a Walkman cassette his mother had made him. Inside the room, his mother Meredith Quill was dying. She held out her hand, and he was too scared to take it. He ran out into the night, where the stars were bigger than the streetlights, and a beam of blue light reached down from a long, silver ship hovering above the trees. A green-skinned Ravager captain named Yondu Udonta had been hired to deliver Peter to his alien father, Ego. Yondu took one look at the small, sobbing boy on the deck of his ship and decided, instead, to keep him.

Peter grew up among the Ravagers, a rough, multispecies pirate crew who stole everything that was not bolted down. Yondu trained him with a yaka arrow that flew on the sound of a whistle. The crew taught him how to negotiate a deal, how to lose at cards on purpose, and how to fly any kind of ship at all. By the time Peter was a young man, he was wandering the galaxy alone in a beat-up M-ship called the Milano, calling himself Star-Lord — a name his mother had given him as a child that no other crew member ever took seriously. He listened to her mixtape every single day. It was the only thing he had left of Earth.

The job that changed his life seemed small at the start. A mysterious buyer wanted Peter to retrieve an artifact called the Orb from the ruins of a planet called Morag. Peter slipped down to the surface, dancing across the wet stones in his red boots to the music in his headphones, and pried the Orb out of its pedestal. He had no idea what was inside. The next week, he was in a prison called the Kyln on the wrong side of a galactic bounty, locked in a cell with a humorless assassin named Gamora, a tree-shaped giant named Groot, a sarcastic raccoon called Rocket, and a literal-minded warrior named Drax the Destroyer.

Peter Quill made a deal with all of them. They would break out of the Kyln together. They would sell the Orb together. They would split the money five ways and never see each other again. The plan worked beautifully on every level except one. Somewhere in the breaking-out and the selling and the running, the five strangers became friends, and then, accidentally, a family. When the Orb turned out to contain the Power Stone and Ronan the Accuser tried to use it to destroy Xandar, the same five misfits stood between him and the planet, holding the most dangerous object in the universe between their five linked hands. They saved the day, and somewhere in the cheering crowd, Peter Quill — the lost kid from Missouri — finally heard his mother’s name and realized he had become someone worth her singing about.
