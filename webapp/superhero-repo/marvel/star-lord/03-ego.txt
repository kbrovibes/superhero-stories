A year after defeating Ronan, Peter Quill finally met the father he had always wondered about. Ego appeared on a Sovereign planet in a shining golden ship, smiling kindly at the Guardians and claiming, very calmly, to be a Celestial — one of the oldest beings in the universe. He took Peter, Gamora, and Drax to his home planet, a beautiful place that looked like a postcard from every direction. Ego explained that he had loved Peter’s mother on one of his many trips to Earth, and that Peter was his half-Celestial son with a piece of the same eternal power inside him. He showed Peter how to make a glowing ball of light between his palms. Peter laughed out loud. He had a dad.

What Peter did not know was that Ego was not just a Celestial. Ego was the planet they were standing on. His core — a bright pulsing brain at the center of the world — was using its energy to seed every other planet in the galaxy with little pieces of Ego, waiting to grow. The only problem was that growing required power. Ego had needed an heir who carried his Celestial DNA, and he had spent thousands of years scattering his children across the galaxy hoping one of them would inherit it. None of them had. Until Peter.

Mantis, a quiet empath who had grown up at Ego’s side, finally told Gamora the truth. Ego had ended his other children once he learned they could not help him. Their small skeletons were stored in a cave deep beneath the surface of the planet. Worst of all, Ego himself had given Peter’s mother the tumor that had ended her life back on Earth, because he had felt himself falling in love with her and could not stay. Peter listened to all of this and did the bravest thing of his life. He pulled out his Quad Blasters and pointed them at his father.

The battle for the planet was the biggest fight the Guardians had ever fought. Rocket and Groot dropped exploding crystals into the planet’s core. Drax, Mantis, Gamora, and Nebula pulled the survivors out of the collapsing palaces. Peter Quill faced Ego on a hillside of golden light and fought him with a glowing fist of Celestial power for the first and last time in his life. When the seed at the center of the planet finally cracked, Ego’s body began to fade. Peter dropped his fists, stopped using the light, and chose to be human again. The planet dissolved into stardust beneath their feet, and the Milano rose into the night sky with everyone — almost everyone — safe. On the way home, Yondu Udonta, the blue-skinned pirate who had stolen Peter as a boy, sat with him at the edge of the open cargo bay and whispered, "He may have been your father, kid. But he wasn’t your daddy."
