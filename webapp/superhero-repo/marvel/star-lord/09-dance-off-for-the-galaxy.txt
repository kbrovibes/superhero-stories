Ronan the Accuser was standing in the middle of a smoking crater on Xandar Prime, the Cosmi-Rod glowing purple in his fists with the Power Stone embedded in its head, and he was about to wipe out an entire planet. Groot had just been broken into hundreds of tiny twigs in the crash of the Dark Aster. Drax was unconscious. Gamora was bleeding. Rocket was sobbing into his hands over Groot. There was nothing left between Ronan and the destruction of three billion people on Xandar except one half-human idiot named Peter Quill — and Peter Quill had no plan at all.

He had a few seconds. He had no working blaster. He had no allies who could stand up. And then, in a flash of genius that nobody could have predicted, he had an idea so ridiculous and so brave that it might just work. Peter pulled the headphones off his neck, looped them under his collar, plugged them into the small cassette player on his belt, and pressed PLAY. The opening guitar of "O-o-h Child" by The Five Stairsteps drifted up out of the rubble. And Peter Quill, in his red boots and his battered jacket, in front of the most dangerous warrior in the Kree Empire, started to dance.

He pointed at Ronan. He sang the words. He shouted at the Accuser to look at HIM, not anywhere else, just at him. "I'm distracting you, you big turd blossom!" he yelled, twirling on one foot. Ronan was so dumbfounded that he forgot to attack. His Cosmi-Rod dropped just slightly. His glowing purple gaze locked on the idiot dancing in front of him. Behind Ronan, exactly out of his line of sight, Rocket Raccoon was crawling toward a fallen weapon. Drax was waking up. Gamora was breathing again. The dance, it turned out, was not a panic move. It was the plan.

Rocket fired the salvaged weapon at the head of the Cosmi-Rod. The Power Stone burst loose and rolled across the cobblestones of Xandar Prime. Peter dove for it. The stone's energy nearly tore him apart — the kind of power that could vaporize a planet was now running through one half-Celestial body — but Gamora caught his hand, and Drax caught hers, and Rocket caught Drax's. Four friends, four hands linked in a glowing purple chain, shared the burden between them. They turned the stone on Ronan as a single, glowing fist of light, and the Accuser dissolved into purple dust. The dance-off, in the end, had saved an entire galaxy. Peter Quill would never let anyone forget it for the rest of his life.
