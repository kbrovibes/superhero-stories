Every great adventurer needs a great ship. Peter Quill had the Milano.

She was not grand or shining. She was a beat-up wedge-shaped M-class starship with a worn red paint job, two stubby wings, and the words "Knowhere Cantina" still half-painted on her belly from a previous owner. Peter had won her in a game of Sakaaran dice at a moon-base bar when he was nineteen years old. He named her after a girl he had seen on Earth television when he was small — Alyssa Milano, his second-grade crush. He never told the rest of the crew where the name came from. They thought it sounded space-y.

The Milano had two pulse cannons and one sleeping bunk and was not built for combat at all. But in Peter's hands, she was extraordinary. He flew her through asteroid fields without a scratch. He once landed her on the back of a flying creature near Knowhere while "Come and Get Your Love" played through his speakers.

Over time, the Milano became the Guardians' home. Rocket hot-wired extra pieces onto her engines without asking. Drax stuck his knives into the panel above his bunk. Gamora kept the medical bay perfectly tidy. Groot lived in a flower pot in the cockpit — and after his great sacrifice on Xandar, Baby Groot stood in that same pot and danced every time the ship lifted off.

The Milano did not survive the battle against Ego. She was torn in half in the upper atmosphere and Peter watched her fall. He cried about it for two days.

Then the Guardians built a new ship — the Benatar — and painted her red.

And that is how Peter Quill learned: a home is not the walls around you. A home is the people inside.
