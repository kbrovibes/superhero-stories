Every great adventurer needs a great ship! Batman has the Batmobile. Cinderella had a coach. And Peter Quill — Star-Lord, hero of the galaxy — had the MILANO!

VROOM! Is she big and shiny and fancy? Absolutely not! The Milano was a beat-up wedge-shaped M-class starship with worn red paint, two stubby little wings, and the words "Knowhere Cantina" still half-painted on her belly from a previous owner. Not exactly impressive-looking, right?

But here's the secret: Peter won her in a game of Sakaaran dice at a moon-base bar when he was just nineteen years old! And he named her after his second-grade crush from Earth television — Alyssa Milano. He never told the crew. They all thought it sounded space-y. WHOOPS!

In Peter's hands, that beat-up ship was EXTRAORDINARY. Did she have two pulse cannons and only one sleeping bunk? Yes. Was she built for combat? Absolutely not. Did that stop Peter? NEVER! He flew the Milano through asteroid fields without a single scratch — WHOOOOSH! He drifted sideways between giant cargo cruisers! He once landed her on the back of a flying creature near Knowhere while "Come and Get Your Love" blasted through the speakers! BOOM BOOM BOOM!

Over time, the Milano became the Guardians' HOME. Rocket hot-wired extra pieces onto the engines without asking anyone — ZZZT CLANK! Drax stuck his knives into a panel above his bunk. Gamora kept the medical bay perfectly tidy. And Groot? Groot lived in a flower pot right in the cockpit! And after his big sacrifice saving everyone on Xandar, Baby Groot stood in that same little pot and danced every time the ship lifted off. Every. Single. Time.

Can you picture it? A tiny dancing baby tree in a flower pot, with stars going WHOOOOSH outside the window? Perfect!

But then came the battle against Ego. And the Milano didn't make it. She was torn in HALF in the upper atmosphere — CRASH! — and Peter watched her fall, spinning and broken, into the clouds below. He cried about it for two whole days.

Then the Guardians built a new ship from spare Sovereign Quadrant parts and Ravager hauler pieces. They called her the Benatar. And they painted her red — because of course they did.

Because a home isn't really the walls around you. A home is WHO is inside with you. And as long as the Guardians were together, any ship would do just fine.
