Have you ever, in your whole entire life, used DANCING to solve a problem? I mean really. Have you ever shaken your hips, twirled on one foot, and SAVED an entire planet? Probably not. But Peter Quill has. And this is the day he did it.

Things were going VERY BADLY for the Guardians of the Galaxy. They were on a planet called Xandar — a peaceful planet, with three billion good and ordinary people living on it. And Ronan the Accuser had just crash-landed on Xandar with the most powerful weapon in the entire universe. The POWER STONE. It was glowing purple inside his big two-pronged hammer called the Cosmi-Rod, and Ronan was about to use it to destroy the WHOLE PLANET. Uh oh.

It got worse. Groot — the tree-shaped friend, the gentle giant — had wrapped himself into a leafy ball to protect everyone when the ship crashed. WHAM! CRASH! He had saved the others, but he had broken into hundreds of tiny little twigs. Drax was knocked out cold. Gamora was bleeding. Rocket the raccoon was sobbing into his hands because he had just lost his best friend in the world.

Only Peter Quill was still standing. And his blaster wasn't working. And his ship was on fire. And Ronan was lifting up the glowing Cosmi-Rod to end everything.

Peter had about three seconds to come up with a plan.

So Peter Quill did the most Peter Quill thing he had ever done in his whole life. He plugged in his little cassette tape player. He pressed PLAY. CLICK. And music drifted up out of the rubble — "O-o-h Child" by The Five Stairsteps. La-la-LA-la. La-la-LA-la.

And Peter started to DANCE.

Right there. In front of Ronan. In his red boots and his battered jacket. He twirled. He pointed. He sang the words out loud, off-key, on PURPOSE. "OOH CHILD, THINGS ARE GONNA GET EASIER!" He pointed at Ronan and shouted, "Look at ME, you big turd blossom! I'M distracting you!"

Ronan just STARED. His giant purple eyes were as wide as dinner plates. His mouth fell open. He literally could not believe what he was looking at. The Cosmi-Rod sagged in his hands. He completely, totally, one hundred percent forgot to attack. Because nobody — NOBODY — had ever danced at the Accuser before.

And that was the WHOLE POINT. While Ronan was staring, the rest of the team was waking up. Rocket crawled across the rubble toward a giant salvaged blaster. Drax sat up and rubbed his head. Gamora started to breathe again. Peter just kept dancing, kept singing, kept Ronan's attention locked on his red boots.

Rocket aimed the giant blaster at Ronan's hammer. ZAP! KA-BLAM! The Power Stone burst right out of the Cosmi-Rod and rolled across the street. KLINK-KLINK-CLATTER!

Peter dove for it. He scooped it up. His body started to glow purple — because nobody, NOBODY, is supposed to hold the Power Stone by themselves. It would have torn him into a million sparkly pieces.

But then Gamora's hand caught his hand. And Drax's hand caught Gamora's. And Rocket's hand caught Drax's. Four friends. Four hands. Linked together in a glowing purple chain.

They shared the power. They turned it on Ronan together. WHOOOOM! A single glowing fist of purple light blasted out, and Ronan dissolved into a cloud of purple dust right in front of them.

Xandar was saved. Three billion people were saved. The Guardians of the Galaxy had won — and they had won because Peter Quill, in the worst moment of his life, had been brave enough, and weird enough, and STAR-LORD enough to start DANCING.

He never let any of them forget it. Not ever. Not even once. Would YOU?
