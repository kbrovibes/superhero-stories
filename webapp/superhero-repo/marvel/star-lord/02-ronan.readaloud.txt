Ronan the Accuser was one of the most feared warriors in the galaxy. He carried a great hammer called the Cosmi-Rod and believed, with absolute certainty, that anyone who disagreed with his Kree Empire should be erased from the universe. Thanos had promised him an entire planet — Xandar — in exchange for one small glowing artifact called the Orb. Ronan agreed. Then he opened the Orb and saw what was inside.

The Power Stone. One of the six Infinity Stones. A purple gem of staggering, terrible power. Ronan pressed it into the head of his Cosmi-Rod and decided he did not need Thanos anymore.

His great ship, the Dark Aster, descended on the city of Xandar Prime like a fist closing. The Nova Corps filled the sky with hundreds of fighters, forming a glowing energy net to slow the ship's fall. Star-Lord and his friends flew the Milano straight into Ronan's fleet, blasting their way through until they crashed into the Dark Aster's bridge.

The fight that followed was desperate and enormous. Groot wrapped all four Guardians inside his branches and absorbed the terrible impact of the Dark Aster crashing into Xandar's streets. When the dust cleared, only one small twig of Groot remained. And Ronan, terrible and certain, was rising from the rubble with the Power Stone blazing on his hammer.

Peter Quill did the only thing he could think of. He started dancing. He pointed at Ronan and challenged him to a dance-off.

And do you know what? Ronan stopped. He stared. And in that one bewildered heartbeat, Rocket fired a beam straight at the hammer — and the Power Stone knocked loose. Peter grabbed it. The energy nearly tore him apart. Then Gamora, Drax, and Rocket each took his hand, and together they aimed that terrible purple power straight at Ronan.

From that day on, the Guardians of the Galaxy had saved a whole world — and none of them quite understood how.
