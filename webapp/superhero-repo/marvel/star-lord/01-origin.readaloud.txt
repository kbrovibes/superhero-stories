Peter Quill was eight years old and sitting in a hospital waiting room when his world fell apart. His mother, Meredith Quill, was dying in the room behind him. She held her hand out. He was too scared to take it. He ran outside into the night — and a beam of blue light reached down from a ship in the sky and carried him away.

A green-skinned Ravager captain named Yondu Udonta had been hired to deliver Peter to his alien father. But Yondu looked at the crying boy on his ship's deck and made a different choice. He kept him.

Peter grew up among the Ravagers — a rough and colorful crew of space pirates who flew between the stars and took what they wanted. Yondu trained him with a yaka arrow that flew on the sound of a whistle. The crew taught him to fly any ship and to survive in a universe that was not easy or gentle.

By the time Peter was grown, he flew alone in a beat-up ship called the Milano and called himself Star-Lord — a name his mother had given him as a child. Every single day, he played the mixtape she had made him. It was the only piece of Earth he had left.

Then came a job that seemed simple: retrieve an artifact called the Orb from the ruins of a planet named Morag. Peter had no idea what was inside it. He danced across the wet stones in his red boots to the music in his headphones and pried it loose.

A week later, in a prison called the Kyln, he shared a cell with four strangers: a deadly assassin named Gamora, a giant tree called Groot, a sarcastic raccoon named Rocket, and a warrior called Drax the Destroyer.

And that is how the Guardians of the Galaxy began.
