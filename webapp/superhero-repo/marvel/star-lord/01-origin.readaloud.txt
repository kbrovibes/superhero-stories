WHOOOOSH! One moment, eight-year-old Peter Quill was standing outside a hospital in Missouri. The next? A beam of blue light reached DOWN from the sky and carried him right up into a spaceship! Can you imagine that?!

Peter had just run outside because he was too scared to hold his mother Meredith Quill's hand while she was sick. He was crying. He was alone. And then — ZAP — space!

The ship belonged to blue-skinned Ravager captain named Yondu Udonta. Yondu had been hired by someone to deliver Peter to his alien father. But Yondu looked at the sobbing little boy on his deck... and kept him instead.

Growing up among the Ravagers was wild! They were a multispecies crew of space pirates who flew between the stars taking whatever they wanted. Peter learned to fly every kind of ship — ZOOM! He learned to negotiate with alien traders. He learned to fight. And Yondu trained him with the most amazing weapon: a yaka arrow that flew wherever Yondu WHISTLED it! FWEET! WHOOSH! CRASH!

By the time Peter grew up, he flew a beat-up ship called the Milano all by himself. Want to know a secret? He named her after a girl he liked in second grade — Alyssa Milano from Earth TV. He never, ever told the crew.

He called himself Star-Lord. His mother had called him that when he was tiny. It was the one piece of her he kept.

And every single day, no matter where in the galaxy he flew, Peter played the mixtape of songs his mother had made for him. It was the only piece of Earth he had left. COME AND GET YOUR LOVE — boom boom boom!

One day, Peter took a job that seemed simple: grab a mysterious artifact called the Orb from the ruins of a wet, flooded planet called Morag. He danced across the stones in his red boots — BOOM BOOM BOOM — to the music in his headphones and grabbed it. No big deal, right?

WRONG. The Orb contained the Power Stone — one of the six most powerful objects in the entire universe!

Soon Peter was in a prison called the Kyln, sharing a cell with four very strange strangers: a deadly assassin named Gamora, a tree-creature called Groot who only said three words, a very sarcastic raccoon named Rocket, and a warrior called Drax the Destroyer.

Five strangers. Zero reason to trust each other. One wild escape plan.

And THAT — right there in that prison cell — is where the Guardians of the Galaxy began.
