Have you ever had a grown-up in your life who wasn't your mom or your dad, but who took care of you anyway? A grandma, an uncle, a neighbor, a teacher? Sometimes the person who loves you the most is not the one you would expect. This is a story about that kind of love.

Peter Quill was in BIG trouble. The whole planet he was standing on was breaking apart underneath his feet. CRACK! RUMBLE! Pieces of golden ground were falling up into the sky. Peter had just fought his real father, Ego — who turned out to be a very mean Celestial, sorry to say — and now Peter was stuck on a crumbling hillside with no way home. WHOOSH! What was that? A bright blue streak fell out of the breaking sky.

It was YONDU. Yondu Udonta — the blue-skinned space pirate who had stolen Peter from Earth way back in 1988, who had called him "rat" and "boy" for thirty whole years and never once said anything mushy. Yondu had come to save him! He grabbed Peter under one arm. He fired up his rocket pack — WHOOSH-WHOOSH-WHOOSH — and they zoomed up, up, UP through the sky together.

But here is the part of the story that is a little bit sad, so listen carefully. Yondu only had ONE space helmet. And space is very, very cold. Cold enough to freeze your nose and your fingers and everything in between.

Yondu pressed the helmet down onto Peter's head. CLICK. He pulled a thin Ravager jacket up around his own shoulders, but he knew. He KNEW it wouldn't be enough. And he didn't care. Because Yondu had decided, in that very moment, that Peter would make it home safe — even if Yondu did not.

He smiled at Peter through the visor. His red metal fin glinted in the starlight. And he said the most important words anyone had ever said to Peter Quill in his whole entire life. "He may have been your father, boy. But he wasn't your daddy."

And then, very quietly, Yondu drifted away into the stars.

Peter floated up through space all by himself, holding onto Yondu's tiny floating shape, and he finally — FINALLY — understood. The grumpy blue pirate who had been raising him for thirty years had loved him the whole time. Yondu just hadn't known how to SAY it.

The Ravager funeral was the biggest in a hundred years. A dozen old captains flew their ships across the sky and fired off long, colorful exhaust streams — red and blue and gold and green — like the most beautiful fireworks you have ever seen. FWOOOSH! SPARKLE! Kraglin, Yondu's first mate, gave Peter Yondu's yaka arrow. Peter tried to whistle the way Yondu used to. He couldn't quite do it. Not yet.

But here is the thing Peter Quill carried with him for the rest of his life. The people who love us best aren't always the loudest about it. Sometimes love sounds like a gruff voice calling you "rat." Sometimes love looks like a blue-skinned pirate giving you his only helmet without saying a word. That is the kind of love Yondu had. And Peter would never, ever forget it.
