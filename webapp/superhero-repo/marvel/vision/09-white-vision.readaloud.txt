Have you ever wondered if there could be TWO of you? Like — same face, same voice, same hands — but only ONE of you remembered all your birthdays and all your bedtime stories? Today's story is about exactly that. About TWO Visions. And a very, very old riddle that they had to figure out TOGETHER.

While Wanda's red Hex bubble was glowing over Westview, something secret was happening OUTSIDE it. A man named Tyler Hayward — he was the boss of an organization called S.W.O.R.D. — had been doing something he wasn't supposed to do. He had Vision's old body. The original one. The one without the Mind Stone, the one that had been quietly broken since the day Vision was gone.

And Tyler Hayward had been putting it back together. Piece by piece. Vibranium plate by vibranium plate. Wires. Circuits. Synthetic muscle. Until one day, on a S.W.O.R.D. laboratory table, the body opened its eyes.

It looked exactly like Vision. Same face. Same hands. Same shoulders. But — and this is the strange part — it was WHITE. Pure white. Like a marble statue come to life. No pink. No silver. No green cape. No yellow gem in the forehead. Just blank, calm, EMPTY white.

White Vision had no memories. No feelings. No favorite color. No childhood. NOTHING. And the first words anyone said to him were a single set of orders: "Find the OTHER Vision. End him."

White Vision didn't argue. He didn't know how. He just flew. STRAIGHT into the Hex. Right through the red wall. WHOOOSH.

And there — hovering above the streets of Westview — was the OTHER Vision. Pink and silver, green cape, yellow gem in his forehead. Made of magic and memory and love.

The two Visions saw each other.

It was the WEIRDEST mirror moment ever. Same face! Same calm British voice! Same hands!

They flew at each other. CRASH! BAM! POW! They traded blows above the rooftops. They knocked each other through the air. They were perfectly matched — because of course they were. They were the same person. Or were they?

And then — almost without meaning to — they landed on the steps of the Westview library. And they STOPPED fighting.

And they started TALKING.

Pink Vision had a question for White Vision. And here it is. Listen carefully, because this is an OLD puzzle. People have been wondering about it for two thousand years.

It's called the SHIP OF THESEUS. Don't worry, you don't have to say it. (THEE-see-us. Try it!)

Here's the puzzle. Imagine a wooden ship. A beautiful old ship that sails the sea. Every year, one wooden plank gets old and needs to be replaced. So you swap it for a new plank. Year after year — swap, swap, swap — you replace every single plank, until NONE of the original wood is left!

Question — is it still the SAME ship?

Hmm! Tricky! That's a tough one!

Pink Vision asked the question very gently. And White Vision — even with no memories — thought hard. He answered, in his careful British voice, "The Ship of Theseus is the philosophical question of whether an object that has had all its components replaced remains fundamentally the same object."

They looked at each other. They both knew the answer was — sort of YES and sort of NO. They were BOTH Vision. And neither one was the WHOLE Vision. The one with the body but no memories. The one with the memories but no original body. Both real. Both incomplete.

And then Pink Vision did something only a being made of LOVE could think of. He reached out his pink hand. He gently touched White Vision's forehead. And — WHOOOSH — he gave him back ALL his memories!

Every conversation with Wanda! Every Avengers argument! Every sunset over New York! All of it, in one warm rush.

White Vision's eyes WENT WIDE. He understood, suddenly, who he had been. He understood the orders he'd been given were WRONG. He looked at his pink twin. He gave a small, grateful bow. And he flew straight up — WHOOOSH — out of the Hex and into the sky.

"I am Vision," he whispered to himself as he went.

And here's the most amazing part. Somewhere out there, in the great wide world — White Vision is STILL FLYING. He carries every memory of who he was. He's looking for the rest of his life.

And the two Visions had answered the Ship of Theseus the BEST way anyone ever has. By being KIND to each other.
