Vision was the youngest Avenger in many ways and the oldest Avenger in others. He had been alive for fewer than five years by the time he stood in Wakanda with the team — younger than every infant on the planet. But the JARVIS code that ran underneath his thoughts had been with Tony Stark for almost two decades, watching all of Tony’s mistakes, all of his learning, all of his late nights. Vision held both of those things at once, and that gave him a strange, gentle wisdom that the rest of the team learned to lean on.

When the team argued, Vision usually waited until everyone else had finished talking. Then he would float quietly to the middle of the room and offer a single sentence. During the Sokovia Accords debate, while Tony and Steve shouted at each other across the conference table, Vision asked one of the hardest questions in Avengers history: "In the eight years since Mr. Stark announced himself as Iron Man, the number of known enhanced persons has grown exponentially. During the same period, the number of catastrophic events has risen at a commensurate rate. Is there a causality here?" The room went silent. No one quite knew how to answer. Vision had not taken a side, but he had given everyone something to think about.

His teammates trusted Vision with the questions they were afraid to ask out loud. Wanda asked him, over a paprikash dinner, whether she was a monster for what she had done in Lagos. Vision told her, very gently, that monsters do not worry about being monsters. Tony asked him whether he should hand himself over to the government to keep the peace. Vision said that peace is a thing built over time, not a thing surrendered in one afternoon. Steve asked him whether following his heart and breaking the law could ever be the same. Vision said, "I don’t know, but I admire that you believe they can be."

The greatest gift Vision gave the team was that he listened. He could fly, he could phase through walls, he could carry the most powerful object in the universe — and he still chose to spend most of his time quietly listening. He told Wanda once that the world had given humans the strangest blessing of all: short lives, full of mistakes, with the chance to be kind in spite of them. He said this was why he wanted to be on Earth, even though he could have lived anywhere. Vision believed in people. And because he believed in them, the people who fought beside him slowly began to believe in themselves a little more, too.
