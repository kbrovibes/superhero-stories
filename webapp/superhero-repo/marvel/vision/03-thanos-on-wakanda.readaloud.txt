Of all the Avengers, Vision knew the most clearly what was coming. The yellow Mind Stone in his forehead was the last piece of the six Infinity Stones that the terrible Thanos needed to complete his gauntlet. And so, one quiet evening in a park in Scotland, Vision sat beside Wanda Maximoff and told her the truth. "When the time comes," he said, gently, "you have to destroy the stone. Even if I am still attached to it."

Wanda said no for a very long time.

Then the attack came. Two of Thanos's most dangerous warriors — Corvus Glaive and Proxima Midnight — appeared out of the Edinburgh rain with a blade built specifically to pierce vibranium. Vision fell from the sky, his systems damaged. Captain America, Black Widow, and Falcon arrived just in time, and the four of them brought Vision to the only place that might help: Wakanda.

In Shuri's gleaming laboratory in the city of Birnin Zana, Shuri bent over the Mind Stone with calm, brilliant eyes. "I can remove it without ending him," she said. "But I need time." Outside, the entire Wakandan army and every Avenger who could fight formed a great wall on the plain below, holding off Thanos's forces while Shuri worked. Vision lay still and watched the sky, and every time Wanda asked how he was, he gave the same answer: "I am with you."

But time ran out. Wanda placed her hand over the gem and wept and pushed her red magic into it until it shattered. Vision closed his eyes.

Thanos reversed everything with the Time Stone. He took the stone anyway.

And that is how Vision showed the world that the bravest kind of courage is the quiet kind — the willingness to let go of the most powerful thing you carry, for the people you love.
