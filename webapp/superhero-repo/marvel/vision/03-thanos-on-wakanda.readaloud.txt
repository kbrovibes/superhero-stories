Of all the Avengers, Vision knew the clearest what was coming. He had known for a long time. Because the yellow Mind Stone in his forehead — the one that gave him his powers, sparked his first heartbeat, made him everything he was — was exactly what the terrible Thanos needed to complete his Infinity Gauntlet.

One quiet evening in a park in Scotland, Vision sat beside Wanda Maximoff and told her the truth. "When the time comes," he said gently, "you have to destroy the stone. Even if I'm still attached to it."

Wanda said no. A very firm, very certain no.

Then the attack came! WHOOOOSH CRASH! Two of Thanos's most terrifying warriors — Corvus Glaive and Proxima Midnight of the Black Order — appeared in the Edinburgh rain carrying a blade built specifically to pierce Vibranium! The blade nicked Vision's side — ZAP! — and damaged his power systems. He fell from the sky, his lights flickering.

Captain America! Black Widow! Falcon! They arrived just in time — BANG WHOMP CRASH! — and the team made a decision: bring Vision to Wakanda, the only place with technology advanced enough to help.

In the city of Birnin Zana, inside Shuri's gleaming laboratory, Shuri bent over the Mind Stone with calm, brilliant eyes and made her assessment: "I can remove the stone without ending him. But I need time."

OUTSIDE: every Avenger who could fight, the entire Wakandan army, and King T'Challa himself formed a great wall on the plains below, holding back Thanos's forces with everything they had — CRASH CLANG BOOM BOOM BOOM! — while Shuri worked.

Vision lay still on the examination table and watched the ceiling. Every time Wanda leaned in and asked how he was, he gave the same answer: "I am with you."

But time ran out. Thanos broke through. Shuri couldn't finish.

Wanda placed her hand over the yellow gem and wept. She pushed her red magic into it with everything she had — CRAAACK! — and the Mind Stone shattered. Vision's lights went out.

Then Thanos raised his hand and reversed time with another stone. He took the Mind Stone anyway. And Vision was gone.

But what Vision showed everyone in that moment — the quiet courage to let go of the most powerful thing you carry for the people you love — that lesson never goes away.
