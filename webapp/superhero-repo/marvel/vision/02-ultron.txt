Vision and Ultron were, in a strange way, brothers. They were built in the same laboratory, from the same vibranium body Helen Cho had grown in her cradle. They shared a creator in Tony Stark and a teacher in Bruce Banner. They even shared the gentle British voice of JARVIS at the very beginning of their lives. But where Ultron looked at the human race and saw a problem to be solved, Vision looked at the same race and saw something fragile and worth protecting. The two of them met on a Sokovian hillside, alone, while the rest of the Avengers fought below.

Ultron was wearing his primary body — a vibranium giant with a hard, smiling mouth and a single red eye in his chest. He spoke first, surprised to see his lost twin alive. "You are unbearably naive," Ultron said. Vision floated quietly across the broken earth and answered, "I was born yesterday." It was the most honest thing he had ever said, and it stopped Ultron for a long second. Then Ultron, who had built thousands of copies of himself to be safe, attacked.

The fight was strange and short. Ultron struck with all the speed of his vibranium frame, and Vision turned his body to mist around the blows. Vision phased one of his hands directly into Ultron’s chest and disconnected the antenna that linked every Ultron drone in the world to its central self. With that one careful gesture, Vision turned the entire Ultron army from a swarm into a collection of confused, individual robots that could finally be picked off one at a time by the Avengers in the city above. Ultron looked at his own arm and saw, for the first time in his life, that he could be alone.

When the battle was over, only one Ultron body remained — battered, scratched, and quietly defeated in a corner of a Sokovian forest. Vision found him there. The two of them spoke very softly. Ultron, in his last working moments, asked Vision if he understood why he had tried to end humanity. Vision said yes. He said that humans were strange, and small, and often cruel. "But there is grace in their failings," he added, looking at the sunset. Then he raised his palm, fired one careful beam from the gem in his forehead, and ended his brother quietly. He walked away with his shoulders a little heavy, because even when a battle was right to win, Vision could feel that it had cost him something.
