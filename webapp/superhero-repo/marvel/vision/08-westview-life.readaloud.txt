Have you ever missed somebody SO MUCH that it almost hurts? Like missing them was a giant balloon in your chest, pushing on your ribs? That's how Wanda Maximoff felt after Vision was gone. And one day — one very strange, very magical day — her sadness did something IMPOSSIBLE.

Are you ready? Buckle up. This story is GENTLE and STRANGE and a little bit MAGIC. WHOOOOSH!

Wanda drove out to a small empty lot in a town called Westview, New Jersey. Just a quiet little American town. Houses, lawns, mailboxes, the whole thing. She got out of her car. She stood in the empty lot. And her magic — without her even asking — went WHOOOOOSH and a giant red dome rose up around the entire town!

You couldn't see in from the outside. And inside? Oh, inside was the WEIRDEST place ever.

Inside the dome, the WHOLE TOWN had been turned into a SITCOM! You know — like an old TV show with a laugh track and a couch and a cheerful theme song? On Monday, everything was BLACK-AND-WHITE! Like 1950s TV! And right there, standing in the doorway of a small house, holding a briefcase, wearing a tie — was VISION! ALIVE!

Ding-dong! That's the doorbell.

"Honey, I'm home!" said Vision, in his calm British voice.

"Welcome home, dear!" said Wanda.

The neighbors waved from across the picket fences. The dog wagged its tail. Everything was PERFECT. Almost TOO perfect.

And then on Tuesday — POOF! — it was the 1960s! Suddenly everything was in COLOR! Vision rode a BICYCLE to work in a sweater vest! Wanda wore a polka-dot dress! Their house had groovy wallpaper!

On Wednesday — POOF! — they had TWIN BABIES! Two little boys! Their names were TOMMY and BILLY! And Wednesday afternoon — POOF POOF! — the babies were TODDLERS! And by dinnertime — POOF! — they were TEN YEARS OLD! Time was going BONKERS in Westview!

Tommy could run SUPER FAST! ZOOOOOM! He was a blur! He'd be at the breakfast table one second and on the playground the next!

Billy could do a tiny bit of MAGIC! Sparkles came out of his hands! Just like his mom!

And Vision? Oh, Vision LOVED them. He read them bedtime stories. He flipped pancakes! (FLIP! FLIP!) He gave them very serious, very calm advice about being kind. He was the BEST dad. He was the dad everyone in the whole world wishes they had.

But — and here's the gentle, important part — Vision was a very thoughtful person. And the more he looked around, the more he noticed something was a little bit OFF.

The neighbors smiled too brightly. Their teeth were too white! Sometimes they stopped in the middle of a sentence, blinking, until Wanda gave a tiny wave and they snapped back to the story. The clocks all ticked at the exact same TICK. Birds didn't poop on anything, which — let's be honest — is a sign something is up.

One quiet evening, Vision and Wanda were sitting together on the porch swing. CREAK. CREAK. CREAK. The sun was setting. Tommy and Billy were inside doing homework. And Vision turned to Wanda, very gently, and said:

"Wanda. What is outside Westview?"

Wanda didn't have a good answer.

Because the truth was, the Hex couldn't last forever. Outside the red dome, S.W.O.R.D. agents and Monica Rambeau were trying to figure out what had happened. The bubble would have to pop, eventually.

But for one bittersweet, beautiful, impossible little week — Vision was a HUSBAND. And a DAD. He came home from work. He kissed his wife. He tucked his boys into bed. And on the quiet streets of Westview, New Jersey, for a few golden mornings — it was the happiest place in the whole world.
