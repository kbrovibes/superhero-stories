This is the softest story I'm going to tell you today. So we're going to slow down. Take a breath. Maybe snuggle in a little. Ready?

Wanda's big red Hex bubble over Westview, New Jersey — the magical bubble where Vision was alive again, where they had two beautiful sons named Tommy and Billy, where every day was a different sitcom — was starting to come apart.

You could see it happening. The walls of the house were flickering. The wallpaper was peeling at the edges. Sometimes the sky outside the window blinked from blue to staticky gray and back to blue again. The Hex was tired. It couldn't hold on anymore.

And Vision — the gentle, magic-made husband, the one who had been reading bedtime stories to Tommy and Billy all week — knew it.

He found Wanda standing alone in the living room of their little house at 2800 Sherwood Drive. He walked over to her. He took her hand. Just took it. The way he had taken it once, a long, long time ago, on a quiet bench in Scotland.

Tommy and Billy were upstairs in their pajamas. They were watching from the top of the stairs. Two little faces peeking through the railings. Quiet as mice.

Now — earlier in the week — those two boys had asked a question. They had asked their dad, "Why does Mom seem so sad sometimes? Even when she's smiling?"

And Vision had given them an answer that — listen to this — is maybe the kindest sentence anybody has ever said in any Marvel story EVER. Are you ready?

He said, "What is grief, if not love persevering?"

Wait. Let's slow down. Because that's a big sentence, and it's an important one. Let me unpack it for you.

Grief — that's the SAD feeling you get when you miss somebody. When you miss them so much it almost hurts inside. That's grief. Maybe you've felt it. Maybe you've missed a grandparent, or a friend who moved away, or a pet, or a person you loved a whole lot.

Persevering — that means it KEEPS GOING. It keeps showing up. It doesn't give up. Like a little flame that just won't blow out.

So what Vision was saying was — being SAD about missing someone is not weakness. It is not being broken. It is not anything wrong with you at ALL.

It is just LOVE. Love that has nowhere to go. Love that keeps on loving, even after the person is gone.

Isn't that the kindest thing? "What is grief, if not love persevering?"

Wanda heard him say it from the next room. And she cried. Not because she was sad, exactly. But because she had been CARRYING that love for a very long time without knowing what to call it. And now she had a name for it.

It was just love. The whole time. Just love.

OK. Back to the living room.

The walls were flickering. The Hex was almost gone. Vision was holding Wanda's hand. He smiled at her — that calm, patient, pink smile she knew so well.

"We have said goodbye before," he said gently. "So it stands to reason —"

And Wanda — soft and crying and brave — finished his sentence for him.

"— we'll say hello again."

The Hex came down. WHOOOSH! The red walls dissolved. The sitcom dissolved. The little house dissolved. And Tommy and Billy faded — quietly, peacefully, no scary noises, no sad endings — back into the gentle magic they had been made of. Vision faded too, still smiling, still holding Wanda's hand right until the very last second.

And the very last thing Wanda Maximoff carried out of Westview, into the rest of her life, was that one sentence. Like a tiny candle she could keep in her pocket forever.

What is grief, if not love persevering?

Just love. The whole time.

That's it. That's the whole story. That's what Vision left behind.

Sweet dreams.
