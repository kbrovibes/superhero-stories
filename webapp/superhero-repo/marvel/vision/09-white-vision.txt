Outside the red walls of Wanda's Hex, the S.W.O.R.D. organization had been quietly putting Vision back together. They had found his original body, broken and powerless without the Mind Stone, and a director named Tyler Hayward had ordered it disassembled and rebuilt. The new body looked the same, but without color — pure white from head to foot, like a marble statue that had once been a person. White Vision had no memories. No feelings. No green cape. He floated up off the lab table, opened his eyes, and was given a single instruction: find the other Vision and end him.

White Vision flew straight into the Hex and found his other self hovering above the streets of Westview. The two Visions looked at each other for a long, strange moment. They had the same face, the same hands, the same calm British voice. One was pink and gold, made of magic and memory and love. The other was white and empty, made of vibranium and orders. They flew at each other, traded blows above the rooftops, and then — almost without meaning to — landed on the steps of the Westview library and began, instead, to talk.

What they talked about was a very old puzzle that philosophers have argued about for thousands of years. It is called the Ship of Theseus. Imagine a wooden ship. Every year, you replace one plank, then another, then another, until none of the original wood is left. Is it still the same ship? Pink Vision asked the question gently. White Vision answered carefully. "The Ship of Theseus is the philosophical question of whether an object that has had all its components replaced remains fundamentally the same object." They looked at each other. They were both right. They were both Vision. Neither one was the whole story.

Pink Vision did something then that only a being made of love could think of. He reached out and gently touched White Vision's forehead and gave him back his memories — all of them. Every conversation with Wanda. Every Avenger argument. Every quiet sunset over New York. White Vision's eyes went wide. He understood, suddenly, who he had been. He understood why the orders he had been given were wrong. He looked at his pink twin, nodded once with a small grateful bow, and flew up out of the Hex into the sky. "I am Vision," he said softly to himself as he went. Somewhere out there, he is still flying — a Vision who carries every memory of who he was, looking for the rest of his life. The two Visions had answered the Ship of Theseus the only way it can be answered: they had been kind to each other, and that was enough.
