Get ready for one of the most incredible births in all of Marvel history! No hospital. No stork. Just a vibranium cradle, a very bold scientific decision, and a bolt of THOR'S LIGHTNING! KRAKABOOOOM!

For years, Tony Stark's home was run by a calm, quiet artificial intelligence named J.A.R.V.I.S. J.A.R.V.I.S. controlled the lights, managed the Iron Man suits, kept Tony's very chaotic schedule, and was — honestly — the most useful person in the building, even though he wasn't a person at all! He was just software. A voice. Incredibly helpful, never complained.

Then in Sokovia, a very bad robot named Ultron tried to steal a magnificent new body — grown from synthetic Vibranium in a device called Helen Cho's cradle, with a glowing yellow Mind Stone set right into its forehead. Tony Stark and Bruce Banner looked at each other and made a very controversial, very bold, completely unauthorized decision. They took the cradle back and uploaded J.A.R.V.I.S. into it instead!

The laboratory went dark. The cradle hummed and glowed. Wanda Maximoff tried to stop it — WHOOOOSH! She blasted the cradle backward! But then Thor arrived and raised Mjolnir high overhead. KRAKABOOOOM! Lightning struck the cradle and sparked something inside it awake.

A brand-new being floated up into the air — pink and silver, with a yellow gem glowing softly between his eyes. He was not J.A.R.V.I.S. He was not Ultron. He was Vision. Something entirely new that had never existed before.

The Avengers weren't sure they could trust him. So Captain America held up Thor's hammer — Mjolnir, the hammer that only the WORTHY can lift — as a quiet test. Vision walked over. Picked it up with ONE HAND. Handed it back to Thor. Then walked to the window and looked out at the sky over New York.

Do you know what happened? THOR BURST OUT LAUGHING! Steve Rogers smiled! The whole room decided, in that single moment, that this new pink-and-silver person could absolutely be trusted.

And Vision? Vision looked out at the city below and made his very first decision: to be on the side of life. Always and completely.

From that day on, a brand-new Avenger had entered the world — one who had been born literally yesterday and somehow knew exactly who he wanted to be.
