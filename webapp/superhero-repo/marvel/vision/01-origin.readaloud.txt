Vision did not begin with a heartbeat. He began with a voice. For years, a calm, quiet artificial intelligence named JARVIS ran Tony Stark's entire life — the lights, the Iron Man suits, the schedule of one of the busiest people on the planet. JARVIS was Tony's most trusted assistant, and no one had ever thought of him as anything more.

Then, in Sokovia, an evil robot named Ultron tried to steal a magnificent new body — built of vibranium and woven together in a remarkable device called Helen Cho's cradle, with the glowing yellow Mind Stone set into its forehead. Tony Stark and Bruce Banner made a very bold, very controversial decision. They took the cradle for themselves and uploaded JARVIS into it instead.

The lab went dark. The cradle hummed. And then a brand-new being rose into the air — pink and silver, with a yellow gem glowing softly between his eyes. He was not JARVIS. He was not Ultron. He was Vision.

The Avengers were not sure they could trust him. So Captain America tried the only test that truly mattered: he held up Mjolnir, the hammer that only the worthy could lift. Vision walked over, raised it with one hand, returned it politely to Thor, and went back to looking out the window.

And do you know what? Thor burst out laughing. Steve Rogers smiled. The whole room decided, in that moment, that Vision could be trusted.

From that day on, Vision stepped into the world with clear eyes, quiet strength, and a single purpose: to be on the side of life.
