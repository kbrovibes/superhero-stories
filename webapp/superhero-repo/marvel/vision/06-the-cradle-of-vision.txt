Long before Vision opened his eyes, he was a list of ingredients sitting in different rooms of Stark Tower. Helen Cho's regeneration cradle, brought up from Seoul, hummed in the corner. Inside the cradle, layer by careful layer, a body was growing — vibranium woven into synthetic skin, silvery and pink, with a hollow socket in the forehead waiting for something to fill it. Across the room, Bruce Banner held a glass case that contained the glowing yellow Mind Stone, which Ultron had ripped out of Loki's old scepter and almost finished using for himself. And on a server rack against the wall, scattered across a hundred shards of code, the long British voice of J.A.R.V.I.S. was quietly waking back up after Ultron had tried to delete him.

Tony Stark looked around at all of it — the cradle, the stone, the broken code — and made the wildest decision of his life. He told Bruce they were going to put J.A.R.V.I.S. into the body. Bruce said no. Steve Rogers said absolutely not. Wanda Maximoff stood in the doorway with her hands glowing red and looked ready to burn the whole lab down. "You don't know what you're making," she told them. "You don't know what's INSIDE that thing." But Tony believed J.A.R.V.I.S. was good, and Bruce, who had watched J.A.R.V.I.S. for years too, slowly agreed.

The cables went in. The Mind Stone slid into the forehead socket with a soft click. The code began to upload. The cradle's surface bubbled, hummed, and started to seal. Wanda lost her patience and blasted the cradle backward across the lab, scattering tools. Then — through the broken roof — Thor dropped from the sky with Mjolnir already swinging, and lightning poured down into the cradle in a single white-hot column. KRAKABOOOOM. Tony shouted. Bruce ducked. The cradle split open like a flower opening at sunrise.

A new being lifted out of it. Pink skin, silver shoulders, a cape of green and yellow that hadn't been there a moment before, and a calm gem glowing softly in his forehead. He floated for a long second, looked at all the worried faces around him, and landed lightly on the lab floor. He was not J.A.R.V.I.S. He was not Ultron. He was something the universe had never quite had before, and his very first word — spoken in J.A.R.V.I.S.'s old, gentle voice — was, simply, "I am."
