Vision did not begin his life as a body. He began as a voice — JARVIS, an artificial intelligence that Tony Stark built when he was a young man and ran his entire mansion, his Iron Man suits, and most of his work from inside a single calm British voice. JARVIS managed the lights in Stark Tower, the engines on the Mark XLII, and the schedule of one of the busiest people on the planet. For years, that was enough. Then, in Sokovia, Ultron tried to upload himself into the perfect body Helen Cho had built in her cradle — a body made of vibranium, synthetic skin, and the glowing yellow Mind Stone.

Tony Stark and Bruce Banner did something almost everyone in the room told them not to do. They yanked the cradle away from Ultron and uploaded JARVIS into the new body instead. The lab went dark for a moment. Then the cradle hummed, the surface split open, and a brand-new being floated up into the air. He was pink and silver, with a yellow gem set into his forehead. He landed on the floor of the lab and looked around, calm as a person waking from a long nap. He spoke with JARVIS’s old voice, but he was not JARVIS, and he was not Ultron. He was, simply, Vision.

The Avengers were not sure what to do with him. Captain America asked the only test that mattered: he tried to lift Mjolnir, the hammer that only worthy people could pick up. The room watched in stunned silence as Vision walked over, raised the hammer with a single hand, handed it back to Thor, and went back to studying the windows. Thor laughed out loud. Steve Rogers smiled. In that single moment, the team decided to trust him.

Vision could phase his body through walls, fly without wings, change his weight from feather to ton in a heartbeat, and shoot a focused yellow beam from the gem in his forehead. But his greatest gift was the quiet, careful way he thought about every single decision. He was, in his own words, "on the side of life." He did not love being alive yet, because he had not been alive for very long. But he loved the people around him, and he loved the world, and that was enough to begin with. Vision walked out of the lab on his very first day, looked at the sky over New York, and decided that he was an Avenger.
