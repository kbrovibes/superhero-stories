Of all the Avengers, Vision had the most personal reason to fear Thanos. The Mind Stone — the yellow gem in his own forehead — was the last piece Thanos needed to complete his Infinity Gauntlet. Vision knew this long before the rest of the team did. He sat on a quiet bench in a park in Scotland with Wanda Maximoff one evening and told her, as gently as he could, that he had decided what he wanted her to do. "When the time comes," he said, "you have to destroy the stone, even if I am still attached to it." Wanda said no for a very long time before she said yes.

The attack came in Edinburgh that same night. Two of Thanos’s Black Order — Corvus Glaive and Proxima Midnight — appeared in the rain and tried to cut the Mind Stone out of Vision’s head with a long, curved blade. Vision phased his body away from the strike, but the blade had been built specifically to pierce vibranium, and a thin slice of it nicked his stomach and damaged his power systems. He fell out of the sky. Captain America, Black Widow, and Falcon found them just in time, and the four of them brought Vision to the only place on Earth where a synthezoid body might still be saved: Wakanda.

In Shuri’s laboratory in Birnin Zana, Shuri studied the Mind Stone’s lattice with quiet excitement. "I can extract the stone without ending him," she said. "But it will take time." Outside, the rest of the Avengers and the entire Wakandan army formed a wall on the great plain. Vision lay on the slab and watched the sky through the open ceiling, while Wanda held his hand and asked him over and over how he was. He answered, every time, the same way: "I am with you."

When Thanos finally arrived, time ran out before Shuri could finish. Wanda, weeping, placed her hand over the gem in Vision’s forehead. Vision smiled at her. "It is the only way," he said. She destroyed the stone. He closed his eyes. For one heartbeat, Vision was gone. Then Thanos used the Time Stone to reverse her work, pulled the Mind Stone back out of Vision’s head, and ended him for real this time. The other Avengers watched in silence, unable to help. Vision’s last gift to the world was that he had been willing to let the stone die for the people who loved him — a quiet, complete kind of courage that even gods like Thanos could not understand.
