Here is something remarkable: Vision was the NEWEST Avenger. Born yesterday, practically! He had been alive for less than five years when he sat in the same room as heroes who had been fighting for decades. And yet — and YET — Vision was often the wisest one in the room. How does that work?!

Well, it's because underneath Vision's thoughts ran the J.A.R.V.I.S. code — and J.A.R.V.I.S. had spent nearly two decades watching Tony Stark. Every mistake. Every great idea. Every late night and every hard lesson. Vision held all of that quietly inside, like a library with two decades of very good, very complicated books.

But here's what made Vision special: he didn't LECTURE. He waited — patiently, quietly — until everyone finished shouting and arguing and making their points. Then he offered one sentence. Just one! And it was always the truest, most useful thing in the room.

When Tony Stark and Steve Rogers were having their BIGGEST argument about the Sokovia Accords — should Avengers have government oversight or not? — Vision floated to the middle of the room and simply asked: had the rise of powerful people in the world made the world MORE dangerous, not less? The room went silent. Nobody could answer. Vision hadn't taken a side. He had given everyone something real to sit with.

Do you know what he told Wanda when she thought she was becoming a monster because of her powers? He said: "Monsters do not worry about being monsters." Think about that! Only a good person worries about being good. A monster never does.

He told Tony that peace is built slowly, day by day — not given away in a single afternoon. He told Steve that he didn't know if a person could follow their heart and break the law at the same time — "but I admire that you believe they can." How kind is that!

Vision could fly. He could phase through walls. He could carry the Mind Stone. He could think ten thousand thoughts a second. He could lift Mjolnir with ONE HAND!

And what did he choose to spend most of his time doing? Listening. Just listening to the people around him, because he thought they were worth it.

That is the real lesson Vision gave the Avengers — and gives all of us: wisdom isn't having all the answers. It's making room for everyone's questions. And believing, with your whole heart, that the people you're listening to are worth the effort.
