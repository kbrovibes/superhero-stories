The yellow gem in Vision's forehead was not put there as decoration. It was one of the six Infinity Stones — older than stars, older than planets, older than almost anything in the universe. And it had traveled a long and winding road before it came to rest in Vision's forehead.

It had once lived inside Loki's scepter during the Battle of New York. Before that, it had been held by creatures called the Chitauri in the deep darkness of space. Before that — no one quite remembered. The Mind Stone simply existed, glowing softly wherever it rested, waiting for someone worthy of carrying it.

Its power was the power of mind itself. It could read thoughts. It could change feelings. It could build an entirely new mind out of nothing — which is exactly what it did when Tony Stark and Bruce Banner connected it to a body made of vibranium and JARVIS's code. It became the spark that started Vision's heart.

Because of the stone, Vision could shoot a golden beam from his forehead powerful enough to carve through the toughest materials. He could think ten thousand thoughts at once. He could phase through solid walls like they were made of air. He was, in a real sense, made of mind.

And do you know what? All of that also made him the most hunted being on Earth. Thanos was collecting every stone and would not rest until the gauntlet was complete.

Tony Stark once asked Vision quietly if he ever wished the stone had landed in someone else's forehead. Vision thought for a long time and said, "No. I am glad it is mine."

And that is how Vision carried the most powerful thing in the universe — not with pride, but with perfect, steady courage.
