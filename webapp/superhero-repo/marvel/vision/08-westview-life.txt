After Vision was gone, Wanda Maximoff missed him so much that her grief did something extraordinary. She drove out to a small empty lot in a town called Westview, New Jersey, and her magic — without her quite asking it to — wrapped a great red dome around the whole neighborhood. Inside the dome, time worked differently. The streets rearranged themselves into a black-and-white sitcom. The neighbors became cheerful characters with painted-on smiles. And right at the center of it all, standing in the doorway of a small suburban house, was Vision. Alive again. Wearing a suit and tie. Coming home from work.

The Hex moved through the decades the way a fast-forward button moves through a long movie. On Monday they were a 1950s couple in black-and-white, with Vision pretending to be a quiet office worker named Mr. Hart and Wanda trying to cook a dinner she had never made before. On Tuesday they were 1960s newlyweds in color, with Vision riding a bicycle to work in a sweater vest. On Wednesday the kids arrived — twin boys named Tommy and Billy, born so quickly that the whole neighborhood barely had time to bring casseroles. Tommy could run fast. Billy could do small bits of magic. They grew from babies to little boys to ten-year-olds in a single afternoon, because Westview's clock did not work the way the rest of the world's did.

Vision loved them. He loved them with the same calm, steady love he had once given the Avengers, only stretched bigger to fit a family. He read bedtime stories. He flipped pancakes. He gave Tommy and Billy serious advice about kindness. But Vision also knew, somewhere inside, that something was wrong. The neighbors smiled too brightly. The clocks ticked too smoothly. People sometimes stopped in the middle of a sentence, blinking, until Wanda nudged them back into the story. One quiet evening, on the porch swing with Wanda, Vision said the gentlest question he could think of. "Wanda. What is outside Westview?" She did not have a good answer.

The Hex could not last forever. Outside its red walls, S.W.O.R.D. agents and Monica Rambeau were trying to figure out what had happened, and inside, the truth was slowly leaking through. But for a little while — a precious, impossible little while — Vision got to be a husband, and a dad, and a man who came home for dinner and helped his sons with their homework. Decades of ordinary life, all squeezed into one bittersweet week, on a quiet street in a quiet town that, for a few golden mornings, was the happiest place in the world.
