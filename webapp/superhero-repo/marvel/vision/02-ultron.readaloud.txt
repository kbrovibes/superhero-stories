In a strange way, Vision and Ultron were brothers. They had both been born in the same laboratory, from the same vibranium body that Tony Stark and Bruce Banner had found in Helen Cho's cradle. They had even started with the same voice — the gentle, British tones of JARVIS. But they had grown in completely different directions.

Ultron looked at the human race and saw a problem. Vision looked at the same race and saw something worth protecting.

They met at last on a Sokovian hillside while the Avengers battled below. Ultron was enormous — a vibranium giant with a rigid smile and a red eye glowing in his chest. He looked at Vision with something like surprise. "You are unbearably naive," he said. Vision floated forward across the broken earth and answered, simply, "I was born yesterday." It was the truest thing he could have said.

Then Ultron attacked. He was fast and strong and certain. But Vision turned his body to mist around the blows, reached one phased hand directly into Ultron's chest, and found the single antenna that connected every Ultron drone in the world to one central mind. He severed it. In an instant, a thousand identical robots went from a coordinated army into a scattered, confused collection of individuals — and the Avengers could pick them off one by one.

Later, Vision found the last Ultron body alone in a forest. The two of them spoke quietly. Vision said he understood why Ultron had tried what he had tried. "But there is grace in their failings," he added, watching the sunset.

Then Vision raised his hand and said goodbye to his brother.

And that is how Vision won the hardest fight of his life — not by hating his enemy, but by understanding him.
