Here is one of the strangest things in all of Marvel: Vision and Ultron were sort of brothers. Both built from the same Vibranium body! Both started with J.A.R.V.I.S.'s voice! Same lab, same cradle — and completely, totally OPPOSITE ideas about the world. Can you imagine?!

Ultron looked at the human race and decided: problem. Vision looked at the exact same people and decided: worth protecting. Two beings made from the same parts, going in completely different directions!

They finally met face to face on a Sokovian hillside while the Avengers fought Ultron's drone army in the burning streets below. Ultron had his primary vibranium body — enormous and hulking, with a rigid, terrifying smile and a single red eye glowing in his chest.

Ultron looked at Vision floating toward him across the broken ground and said, in his sneering robot voice: "You are unbearably naive."

And Vision floated closer and answered simply: "I was born yesterday."

The most honest thing he could have said! BOOM!

Then Ultron attacked. CRASH BANG WHOOOM! He was fast, powerful, and completely certain he was right. But Vision turned his body to MIST around every blow — phasing right through the attack like it was made of air! Then he reached one phased hand directly into Ultron's chest and found the single antenna linking every Ultron drone in the entire world to one central mind. SNAP! He severed it.

In that one careful gesture, a thousand Ultron robots across the globe went from a coordinated army to a confused, scattered mess of individuals — and the Avengers could pick them off one by one! WHOMP CLANG CRASH!

Later, Vision found the last Ultron body alone in a quiet forest. Just the two of them, away from all the noise. Ultron sat in the rubble looking at the sunset. Vision sat beside him and said he understood why Ultron had tried what he tried. "But there is grace in their failings," Vision said, watching the golden light.

Then Vision raised his hand and said goodbye.

And that is how Vision won the hardest fight of his very short life — not by hating his enemy, but by understanding him, and choosing something different anyway.
