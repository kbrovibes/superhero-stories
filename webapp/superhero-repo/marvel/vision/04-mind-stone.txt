The yellow gem in Vision’s forehead was not jewelry. It was one of the six Infinity Stones — older than any galaxy and far more powerful than the body that carried it. The Mind Stone had a long and strange history before it reached Vision. It had once lived inside Loki’s scepter at the Battle of New York. Before that, it had been kept by the Other and the Chitauri in deep space. Before that, no living being could quite remember. The Mind Stone simply existed, glowing softly in whatever box or hand or skull contained it.

The stone’s power, as its name suggested, was the power of mind. Anyone who held it could read thoughts, push thoughts, change moods, and in the right hands, build entirely new minds out of nothing. When HYDRA’s Baron Strucker shone its energy onto the Maximoff twins, the stone gave Wanda and Pietro their gifts. When Tony Stark and Bruce Banner connected the stone to Helen Cho’s synthetic body, the stone became the spark of life that started Vision’s heart and woke up his brain. Vision was, in a real sense, made of mind itself.

For Vision, the gem was both his greatest power and his deepest weakness. He could fire a focused yellow beam from it that could carve through tanks. He could think faster than anyone on the planet, calculating ten thousand outcomes a second to find the kindest one. He could phase his body through walls because the stone tied his molecules to a strange other dimension where solid things became suggestions. But the same stone made him a target, because Thanos had been collecting them all and would not stop until he had every one. Tony Stark once asked him quietly if he wished it had been someone else’s forehead the stone had ended up in. Vision thought about that for a long time and finally said, "No. I am glad it is mine."

When Wanda destroyed the Mind Stone in Wakanda, the yellow gem cracked into bright dust and Vision’s lights went out for the first time. Thanos undid the moment with the Time Stone, but the lesson the stone had taught him stayed with everyone. Power is not something you keep. It is something you carry until you can put it down for the right reason. Vision had carried his stone with a steady hand, and when the time came to give it up, he gave it up without flinching. The Avengers would remember that, the next time any of them held something too powerful to keep forever.
