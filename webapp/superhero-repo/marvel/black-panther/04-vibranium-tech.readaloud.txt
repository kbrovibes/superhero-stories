A very long time ago, a star fell from the sky and landed in the heart of Africa. It left behind a miracle — a shining metal called Vibranium. The people who would become the Wakandans found it, studied it, and over many, many years, they used it to build something truly extraordinary.

Their capital city is called Birnin Zana, and it is unlike any city in the world. Towers of Vibranium-laced steel glow softly blue at night, each one carved with swirling patterns honoring ancient Wakandan tribes. Quiet trains glide above the streets on magnetic Vibranium rails. Sleek Talon Fighters and Dragon Flyers weave silently through the sky. And underneath it all, the Great Mound hums gently — a vast deposit of Vibranium that powers every lamp and every machine in the entire country without a single puff of smoke.

The Black Panther suit that Shuri designed is perhaps the most wonderful thing of all. It looks like smooth dark cloth, but it is actually Vibranium mesh — tougher than anything. When someone punches or blasts T'Challa, the suit drinks up that energy, stores it, then releases it back in a great wave. His retractable claws are made of anti-metal Vibranium that can cut through almost anything.

And do you know what? Shuri uses Vibranium to heal people too. In their hospitals, tiny tools called Kimoyo Beads help mend terrible injuries in just a few hours. T'Challa shares these gifts through Wakandan International Centers so people all over the world can be helped.

From that day on, Wakanda showed the world that the most powerful technology is the kind that protects the planet and lifts everyone up together.
