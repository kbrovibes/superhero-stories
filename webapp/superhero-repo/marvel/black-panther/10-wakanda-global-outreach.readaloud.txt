The great hall of the United Nations went very quiet when T'Challa rose to speak. Leaders from every country in the world were watching. And what he told them surprised them all.

He said that Wakanda would no longer hide. That his nation's greatest treasure was not Vibranium itself, but everything Vibranium had helped them learn — and that it was time to share those lessons with the whole world.

The first thing Wakanda built was an Outreach Center in Oakland, a city in America. T'Challa chose that place carefully: it was the same neighborhood where his uncle N'Jobu had once lived, the place where Erik had grown up — the place where hurt had started. Now, instead of hurt, there would be help. Doctors trained there. Children learned there. People who had been suffering found medicine that Wakandan science had made possible — treatments for illnesses that no one else had been able to cure, and prosthetic arms and legs that worked better than the original ones.

It was not always easy. Some people in the world heard "Wakanda is sharing" and thought: "How can we take?" T'Challa had to stay watchful, protecting his nation's deepest secrets even while opening its hands.

But something beautiful was also happening. Young Wakandans were meeting young people from everywhere on Earth. They were trading ideas and stories and dreams. The Golden City began teaching its children about the whole wide world.

From that day on, Wakanda proved something that T'Challa had always believed in his heart: the bravest thing a king can do is not just protect his own people, but stretch out his hand and lift everyone up.
