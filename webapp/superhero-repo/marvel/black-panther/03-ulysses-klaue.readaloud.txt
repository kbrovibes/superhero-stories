There was a man the world's secret agents whispered about with dread. His name was Ulysses Klaue, and he had one very dangerous obsession: Vibranium. He had once snuck into Wakanda, stolen precious metal, and barely escaped — leaving one arm behind in the process. He replaced that arm with a stolen Wakandan mining tool, a powerful sonic cannon that could shatter steel walls and send shockwaves crashing through the air.

Klaue sold Vibranium on the black market to anyone with enough gold to pay, and he laughed and danced while he did it, pretending to be silly so people would not notice how dangerous he truly was. But T'Challa noticed. For King T'Challa, hunting Klaue was not just a mission — it was a matter of honor for every Wakandan who ever lived.

The chase took them all the way to the city of Busan, where T'Challa, General Okoye, and their team tracked Klaue to an underground casino. When Klaue fired his sonic cannon, T'Challa's Vibranium suit absorbed the blast, storing every bit of that energy — and then released it right back. Back and forth they fought, through the casino, out onto the streets, with Shuri back in Wakanda remotely guiding a speeding vehicle to help cut Klaue off.

They caught him. And even though Klaue was later taken away by Erik Killmonger, T'Challa never forgot what the chase taught him: Vibranium's power makes the whole world curious, and the best way to protect Wakanda's greatest treasure is not walls and secrecy, but wisdom.

From that day on, T'Challa worked harder than ever to build a world where Wakanda's gifts would be shared — not stolen.
