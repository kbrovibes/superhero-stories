High above the jungles of Wakanda, where the air turns cold and snow covers the peaks of the Gorilla Mountains, lives a tribe unlike any other. They are the Jabari, and for as long as anyone could remember, they kept to themselves. They did not use Vibranium. They did not live in shining towers. They prayed to Hanuman, the gorilla spirit, and believed that true strength came from the body, from the mountain, and from each other.

Their leader, M'Baku, was a giant of a man with a booming voice and a laugh that shook the hills. He and the Jabari were proud. And for a long time, the Golden City and the mountain tribe looked at each other with suspicious eyes, like two great cats circling a fire.

But then came the dark day when Killmonger seized the throne. T'Challa's family had nowhere to go — and it was M'Baku who opened the Jabari gates and gave them shelter. He did not have to. He chose to. And when the time came to fight for Wakanda, M'Baku led his warriors down from the frozen peaks and into the battle, wooden weapons cracking against armor, mountain strength against high-tech fury.

They won. Together.

After that, everything changed. M'Baku took a seat at Wakanda's royal council, his gruff wisdom balancing the scientists and generals. T'Challa trained alongside him in the old mountain style, learning new moves that no enemy could predict.

From that day on, the mountains and the Golden City were no longer two separate worlds. They were one Wakanda — and Wakanda, united, was stronger than anything.
