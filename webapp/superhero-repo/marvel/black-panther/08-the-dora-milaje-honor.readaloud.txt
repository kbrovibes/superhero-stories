Wherever T'Challa walks, a ring of fierce and graceful warriors walks with him. They are the Dora Milaje — Wakanda's most honored protectors — and they are extraordinary.

Every woman who joins the Dora Milaje trains for years and years. They master martial arts. They study strategy. They practice with their Vibranium spears until those spears feel like extensions of their own arms — each one custom-made, perfectly balanced, able to slice through almost any material on Earth and send shockwaves booming through the air. Their red armor gleams in the sun like embers, and their shaved heads show the world they have given themselves completely to their duty.

At their head stands General Okoye. She is calm in the middle of hurricanes. She is quick and precise in a fight, and she is absolutely, completely loyal — not just to the king, but to Wakanda itself. Her friendship with T'Challa was forged through hundreds of battles fought side by side.

When the Dora Milaje move together in combat, it is something to behold. Each warrior knows exactly what the others will do next. They flow like one creature, one purpose, one unbreakable will. And when they raise their spears and cry "Wakanda Forever," the sound fills every heart with pride.

They protect the Golden City. They travel the world on secret missions. They stand between danger and the people they love without flinching.

From that day on, every child in Wakanda knew: the greatest strength is not one warrior alone, but many warriors who trust each other completely — and fight as one.
