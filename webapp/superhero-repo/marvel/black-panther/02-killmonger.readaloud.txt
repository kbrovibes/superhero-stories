Far away in America, a boy named N'Jadaka grew up with a heavy sadness in his heart. His father, Prince N'Jobu, had been the brother of King T'Chaka — which meant N'Jadaka had royal Wakandan blood. But he had been left behind, never knowing the beautiful kingdom his father loved. He grew up angry, training every day, going by the name Erik Stevens, then Killmonger, determined to one day return and take what he believed was his.

And one day, he did. He arrived in Wakanda and challenged T'Challa to ritual combat — a sacred tradition that said any member of the royal bloodline could fight for the throne. They clashed at Warrior Falls, a great waterfall roaring around them, and Killmonger's fierce warrior skill proved stronger. He took the crown, consumed the Heart-Shaped Herb, and began preparing Wakanda for war.

But T'Challa was not finished. On the Ancestral Plane, he spoke honestly with his father's spirit, learning truths about the family's past that he had never known. That honesty made him stronger. He returned and challenged Killmonger again, this time deep inside the Great Mound, where Vibranium's energy crackled through the air and flickered both their suits dark.

T'Challa won. But when it was over, instead of anger, he felt something softer. He carried his cousin to the edge of the mountain so Erik could see the Wakandan sunset — golden and purple and wide — for the very first time.

From that day on, T'Challa opened Wakanda to the whole world, building outreach centers in the very neighborhoods where Erik had grown up. Because a great king knows that healing old wounds is the bravest battle of all.
