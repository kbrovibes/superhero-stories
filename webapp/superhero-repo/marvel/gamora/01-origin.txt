Long before she became one of the greatest heroes in the galaxy, Gamora was the last of the Zen-Whoberi — a peaceful people who lived on a quiet world far from the wars of the stars. When Thanos came to Zen-Whoberi, the planet fell silent. A small girl with green eyes and steady hands was the only one he took with him, not as a prisoner, but as something he called a daughter. Her name was Gamora.

Growing up aboard Thanos's ship, the Sanctuary, Gamora trained harder than anyone around her. Thanos was not a warm father. He taught through challenge and competition, always pitting Gamora against her adopted sister, Nebula. The two girls sparred every morning on the training deck while stars drifted past the porthole windows. Gamora learned to use every weapon in the arsenal — blades, staffs, her own quick feet — and she became so skilled that warriors across the galaxy whispered her name in hushed, careful voices: the deadliest woman in the galaxy.

But the more Gamora learned, the more she understood what Thanos truly was. She had been trained to serve his plans. His goals were not protection or peace — they were power, and something far darker. The realization settled in her chest like cold starlight. She had spent years becoming a weapon for someone she could not follow. So, quietly and bravely, she walked away from everything she had known.

It was not an easy choice. Nebula stayed, and the distance between the sisters became an old wound that never quite healed. Gamora carried her twin blades — most famously the long sword called Godslayer — and turned her skills toward something better. She followed her own conscience across star systems, standing against the plans she had once been trained to carry out.

The universe had other ideas about where she would end up. In a prison called the Kyln, she met Peter Quill, who called himself Star-Lord, and a ragged band of misfits: Rocket the sharp-tongued raccoon, the gentle giant Groot, and Drax the fierce warrior searching for his own justice. None of them trusted each other at first. But when danger threatened everyone around them, Gamora fought shoulder to shoulder with strangers who soon became something new: family. The Guardians of the Galaxy were born not from a plan, but from a choice — the same kind of brave, simple choice Gamora had made when she left Thanos behind. She was no longer a weapon. She was a Guardian.
