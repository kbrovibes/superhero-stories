Let me tell you about the most unusual family in the whole entire galaxy.

They live on a spaceship called the Milano. It is not a fancy spaceship. Rocket's half-finished gadgets are EVERYWHERE. Drax has claimed the biggest chair on the bridge and absolutely will not share it. Groot keeps a little collection of interesting rocks on the navigation console. And Star-Lord plays his music SO LOUD that it bounces off every wall.

It is a mess.

Gamora loves it more than any quiet, clean place she has ever lived.

Now — who ARE these Guardians of the Galaxy, exactly?

STAR-LORD — Peter Quill — grew up in Missouri and was then accidentally swept up to the stars as a kid by a crew of space pirates called the Yondu Ravagers. He has been bouncing around the galaxy ever since, with tremendous confidence and slightly less competence, which is somehow part of his charm. He leads with big heart and even bigger ideas.

ROCKET is a raccoon-like creature who is possibly the smartest person on the ship and will ABSOLUTELY build a weapon out of anything you leave unattended for ten minutes. He acts tough, but underneath? He cares so much it might break your heart.

GROOT just says "I am Groot." Three words, every single time. But somehow those three words carry EVERYTHING — happiness, worry, bravery, kindness, surprise. Groot shows up when you need him most, every time, without making a fuss about it.

DRAX is a big fierce warrior who lost his family and is slowly, slowly learning that the best response to that kind of sadness is not just fighting — it is also letting people in.

And GAMORA is the one who holds the whole beautiful mess together.

She is the planner. The strategist. The one who actually READS the mission briefing before they land. When Star-Lord's big talk falls short? Gamora says three calm sentences and the negotiation is over. When Rocket gets lost in his own clever ideas? Gamora sets the new heading.

But most importantly — she sees every single one of them, clearly, the way you see people you love. She knows that Rocket hides scared behind funny. She knows that Groot rustles when something's wrong. She knows when Drax needs company and when he needs silence.

She learned all of that the same way she learned to use Godslayer: by paying attention, every day.

The Guardians of the Galaxy didn't start as a family. They started as a bunch of strangers stuck in a prison together, with nothing in common.

But sometimes that is exactly how the best families start.

And Gamora? Gamora is home.
