Okay, this is the story of the most complicated rivalry in the ENTIRE galaxy — and it starts with two girls who grew up on the same cold ship, eating breakfast at the same table, training side by side every single morning.

One of them was Gamora. Green skin, steady hands, the best fighter Thanos had ever trained.

The other was Nebula. Blue skin, part metal, part circuits, part something that felt a lot like hurt feelings that had been building up for a VERY long time.

Here's the sad part. Thanos made them compete. Every. Single. Time. And every single time Nebula lost — which was a lot, because Gamora was INCREDIBLY good — Thanos replaced a piece of Nebula with a machine part. An eye. An arm. Circuits in her brain. Until Nebula looked in the mirror and saw more metal than person staring back.

And she blamed Gamora for every single one.

So when Nebula tracked Gamora across the galaxy in her sleek, blue-black ship, she wasn't just coming to fight. She was coming to WIN, finally, after all those years.

She was fast — ZOOM — and relentless and she knew every move Gamora had ever used because she had trained against them for years. In the fight over Knowhere, CLANG CLANG BANG, it was back and forth and back and forth and Nebula got the upper hand —

And THEN?

Rocket skidded in from one side. Drax arrived from the other. Not to fight Nebula — just to give Gamora one single second to breathe.

One second was enough.

Gamora got back to her feet. She planted those boots. She looked Nebula straight in the eye.

And something happened that surprised everybody, including Gamora. Nebula's fury — all that blazing, hot, unstoppable fury — flickered. And underneath it, Gamora could see what had always been underneath it: sadness. The kind that lives in you when you think nobody is paying attention to you. The kind that comes from always being compared to someone else and always feeling like you came up short.

Nebula retreated.

Gamora stood there and breathed and thought about it. She thought about those breakfast tables on the Sanctuary. She thought about two little girls training in the cold, both of them just trying to survive.

She hoped — quietly, to herself — that someday Nebula might stop running and turn around. That someday there might be room for something other than this.

Because beneath all the metal and all the anger, Nebula was still her sister.

And Gamora never forgot that.
