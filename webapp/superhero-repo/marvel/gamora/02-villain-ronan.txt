Of all the threats the Guardians of the Galaxy have faced, few were as fearsome as Ronan the Accuser, a towering Kree warrior who carried a massive stone war-hammer called the Universal Weapon. Ronan wore heavy blue-black armor and painted his face like a warrior of old, and his voice echoed through corridors like a hammer on stone. He had made a deal with Thanos — deliver an Infinity Stone called the Power Stone, hidden inside a small silver orb, and receive a terrible revenge upon his enemies. But Ronan grew too hungry for power. He decided to keep the Stone for himself.

The Power Stone, when wielded by someone without protection, could destroy whole planets. Ronan embedded it directly into the handle of his war-hammer, and the energy inside it turned the weapon into something the universe had never seen. He aimed his ship, the Dark Aster, at the planet Xandar, home of the Nova Corps — the brave space-police force that protected countless worlds. If Ronan reached Xandar and brought that hammer down, an entire civilization would end.

Gamora had faced Ronan before. She knew his power and his fury. But she also knew something he did not: a ragged band of unlikely heroes was flying directly into his path. Aboard the Milano, their battered spacecraft, Gamora stood with Star-Lord, Drax, Rocket, and Groot and worked through every option they had. Their spaceship was outgunned. Their crew was small. Their chances were not good.

"We are all going to die," Rocket said, surveying the Dark Aster's enormous hull.

"Not today," Gamora replied, already moving to her console.

The Guardians fought brilliantly. Rocket led the aerial assault with every jury-rigged weapon he could bolt to the Milano. Drax charged directly at Ronan's soldiers aboard the Dark Aster. Groot wrapped himself around his friends to shield them when the ship began to fall. And Star-Lord did something no one expected — he grabbed the Power Stone with his bare hands.

The energy tore through him like a storm. But Gamora grabbed his hand without hesitation, and then Drax and Rocket grabbed on too, four Guardians sharing the impossible weight of a Stone that could destroy worlds. Together, they held it. Together, they turned it on Ronan.

KRACOOM — the Accuser fell.

The planet Xandar survived. The Nova Corps swept up the pieces, and the Power Stone went into their safekeeping. The Guardians landed on Xandar's surface, wind-burned and exhausted and laughing. Gamora looked at her teammates — this strange family that had walked into the impossible together — and felt something she recognized at last: trust.
