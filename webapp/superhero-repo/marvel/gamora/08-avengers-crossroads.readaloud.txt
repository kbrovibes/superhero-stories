Okay — what happens when the Guardians of the Galaxy bump into the Avengers? You are about to find out, and it is SPECTACULAR.

It started with a signal. Gamora picked it up on the Milano's instruments — a pulse of cosmic energy near the ruins of a planet called Titan. Orange skies, broken towers, dust drifting in the air like old gold. And the energy signature? Gamora knew it immediately. She had studied every frequency an Infinity Stone could emit, and this was one of them.

"We should investigate," she said.

"Obviously," Rocket agreed, already adjusting the heading.

ZOOM — the Milano dropped out of warp near Titan.

And there was ANOTHER ship there.

Out stepped Tony Stark. Iron Man. Wearing a suit so extraordinary that Rocket spent four seconds admiring it before mentally taking it apart. Behind him came Doctor Strange, Sorcerer Supreme, whose cloak moved on its own and who carried the Eye of Agamotto — housing its own Infinity Stone. And bounding over with enormous energy — Spider-Man, who immediately started talking.

"Are you really the deadliest woman in the galaxy? Is the green skin natural? What does Godslayer weigh? Have you tried a taco?"

Gamora stared at him.

"Yes. Yes. I don't know. No."

"You should really try a taco."

Meanwhile, Gamora and Tony Stark had looked at each other and understood two things instantly: same goal, no time to waste. Tony was smart and quick and had no interest in showing off when showing off wasn't useful.

"Tell me everything you know about Thanos," Tony said.

"Tell me everything you know about the Gauntlet," Gamora replied.

And they got to work.

FAST. Gamora laid out Thanos's patterns — when he negotiates, when he charges, what slows him down. Tony explained the Gauntlet's design and what each Stone did when combined. Doctor Strange listened with laser focus, already calculating five moves ahead. Even Spider-Man contributed — fresh eyes sometimes see what tired ones miss.

For one afternoon above the ruins of Titan, two teams who had never met became something more. Not just allies. A combined force with combined knowledge, each team filling in what the other didn't know.

That is what great teamwork looks like. Not one person having all the answers. Everyone bringing what they know, trusting each other with it, building something none of them could build alone.

The battle over Titan was fierce. CLANG — CRASH — BOOM! But the heroes fought it with sharper maps than anyone had ever carried into that fight.

Because on the crumbling stones of an orange world, two families of heroes had looked at each other and said: we are on the same side.

Let's act like it.
