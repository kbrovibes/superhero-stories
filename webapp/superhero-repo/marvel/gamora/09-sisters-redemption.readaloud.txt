Some stories take a long, long time to reach their ending.

This is one of them.

You remember Nebula, right? Blue skin, metal parts, twin blasters, and a whole cargo hold's worth of frustration directed mostly at Gamora? The sister who trained alongside her on the Sanctuary every morning, who Thanos kept pitting against her, who lost those fights and paid for them with pieces of herself?

Well. After everything that happened with Thanos — after the battles and the chaos and the long, hard work of setting things right — the universe needed time to heal. And in that time, something happened that Gamora had quietly, stubbornly hoped for without saying it out loud.

Nebula came around.

Not all at once. Not in a big dramatic moment. Slowly, the way a river slowly carves through stone. Nebula had lived inside Thanos's plan. She had been part of it. She had seen the full shape of what it was. And somewhere in that seeing, she made a decision that was entirely her own: she wanted to be on a different side.

Then came the battle above Earth — the biggest, loudest, most complicated fight you have ever imagined — and in the middle of all that chaos and smoke and sound, two familiar people ended up standing side by side.

Back to back. Swords up.

Covering each other without even thinking about it. Just PURE MUSCLE MEMORY from all those mornings sparring on the Sanctuary's training deck.

"You have my back," Nebula said. She sounded genuinely surprised.

"I always did," Gamora said.

Nebula was quiet. The battle thundered around them. Then she said — carefully, like she was carrying something she didn't want to drop — "I blamed you."

"I know," Gamora said.

"It was not entirely your fault."

"I know that too."

CRASH — the battle moved on and pulled them back in. But something had changed. That old crack between them — years old, Sanctuary-shaped — did not vanish. Cracks don't just vanish. But it wasn't empty anymore.

Nebula was not suddenly a simple person. She had experienced too much and carried too much for that. But she was making her OWN choices now. Her own side. Her own fight. Not the fight Thanos had aimed her at.

Gamora knew exactly what that felt like. She had done the same thing.

After the battle, they sat together for a while in the quiet that follows big fights. Two sisters. The long way around. A road that had been years long and crossed half the galaxy and gone through some very difficult territory.

But it had, at last, come home.

Family can grow in unexpected directions, Gamora thought.

Even the ones you thought were lost.
