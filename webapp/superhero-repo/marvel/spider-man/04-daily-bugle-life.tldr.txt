• The Daily Bugle — New York City's most aggressive newspaper — runs a relentless campaign declaring Spider-Man a public menace
• Editor J. Jonah Jameson, with his flat-top haircut, demands "Pictures of Spider-Man!" to fuel headlines like "Spider-Man Attacks Local Bank!"
• Peter Parker works as a freelance photographer for the Bugle — secretly selling photos of himself in action to pay rent and help Aunt May
• Peter builds his most important relationships inside the newsroom: mentor Robbie Robertson and reporter Betty Brant
• Jameson's core belief: true heroes don't hide behind masks — police, firefighters, and soldiers deserve the credit
• Peter realizes he doesn't need public approval to be a hero; the truth matters even when no one believes it
• The Bugle moves from print newspaper to digital news empire but Jameson's anti-Spider-Man crusade never wavers
• Peter regularly defends the Bugle staff from his own enemies — protecting the very people who mock him
