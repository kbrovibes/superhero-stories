There was once a very clever scientist named Dr. Otto Octavius. He worked with dangerous materials, so he built himself four long mechanical arms that he could move with nothing but his thoughts. He was proud of his work — perhaps too proud.

One day, something went terribly wrong. A radiation leak caused a massive explosion in his laboratory, and when the smoke cleared, the mechanical arms had fused to his body. The accident changed his mind as much as it changed his body. The kind scientist was gone, and in his place stood the villainous Doctor Octopus — four arms snapping, and a heart full of anger at the world.

Doc Ock, as the city came to call him, used his mechanical limbs to terrorize Manhattan. He could strike from four directions at once and move with a terrifying, crawling speed. Spider-Man had to push every bit of his spider-sense and his quick reflexes just to stay in one piece. Their battles roared across rooftops and even on top of a moving subway train, the steel arms crashing and clanging through the city.

And do you know what happened next? Peter Parker thought hard — harder than his fists could ever hit. In the final clash, he used Octavius's own technology against him, short-circuiting the harness and bringing the rampage to an end. Doc Ock was defeated. But as Peter looked at the broken man lying there, he didn't feel triumphant. He felt sad. He saw what a brilliant mind could become when it forgot about kindness.

And that is how Spider-Man learned that the greatest battles are sometimes fought not with strength, but with the wisdom to know that every great mind carries a great responsibility.
