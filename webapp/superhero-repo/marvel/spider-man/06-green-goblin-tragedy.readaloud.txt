Sometimes the most dangerous enemy isn't a stranger. Sometimes it starts as a friend.

Norman Osborn was one of the most powerful men in New York City — a rich, sharp-minded businessman and the father of Harry Osborn, Peter's best friend. Norman had everything: money, influence, a son who loved him. But Norman wanted MORE. He wanted to be the smartest, the strongest, the most powerful person alive. So he created a secret formula — the Goblin Formula — brewed in a hidden laboratory, designed to supercharge a human being.

It worked. Norman became faster and stronger than any normal person could be.

But here's the terrible thing about taking a potion to make yourself more powerful: it also made whatever was already dark inside him much, much darker. The Goblin Formula cracked his mind like a walnut. And out of the cracks came the Green Goblin — a cackling, glider-riding nightmare in a purple-and-green costume, with glowing eyes and a bag full of weapons shaped like pumpkins.

Those pumpkin bombs were no joke. Norman first used them in only his second appearance in the comics, and he kept upgrading them — some made smoke, some made fire, some could even scramble Spider-Man's spider-sense. And the Goblin Glider he flew? It started as a broomstick before Norman improved it into a high-speed flying platform armed with missiles, blades, and launchers. It was terrifying and also, if you weren't the one being chased, slightly impressive.

But the Goblin's most powerful weapon wasn't any of that. It was knowledge.

Somehow, Norman discovered that Spider-Man was Peter Parker. He knew exactly who Peter loved. And he used that knowledge with a cruelty that made Peter's heart turn to ice.

He took Gwen Stacy — brave, brilliant Gwen, the most important person in Peter's heart — and flew her to the top of the George Washington Bridge. High above the cold, dark river. Peter swung after them with everything he had, terror in his chest, webs firing as fast as his hands could work.

He reached for her. His web caught her. But the fall had been too far and the stop too sudden, and Gwen was taken from him forever.

Do you know what the Green Goblin wanted most after that? He wanted Peter to become angry and cold and cruel — to fight the way the Goblin did, without mercy. He pushed and goaded and sneered.

Peter never gave him that. Even with his grief huge and heavy, Peter kept his heart. He stayed himself.

That choice — to remain good even when everything hurts — was the bravest thing Peter Parker ever did.

The Goblin was defeated. But his shadow lasted. Harry Osborn, Peter's best friend, would later fall into the same darkness. The Osborn family became the defining sorrow of Peter's life.

And yet — Peter never stopped swinging.
