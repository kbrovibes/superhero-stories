The creature known as Venom was actually two beings bonded together: a black, liquid-like alien symbiote from space and a human host named Eddie Brock. Eddie had once been a reporter at the Daily Bugle, just like Peter Parker, but he often felt misunderstood. When the symbiote bonded with him, it granted him powers very similar to Spider-Man’s—including the ability to stick to walls and create its own organic webbing. Venom was enormous, with a massive white spider-symbol on his chest and a wide, toothy grin that looked scary but often hid a very confused personality.

One chilly evening, Venom decided that the giant bell in the St. Jude’s Cathedral tower was far too dusty. He didn't realize that the symbiote was incredibly sensitive to loud, vibrating sounds. He began swinging around the bell, accidentally knocking it with his heavy, ink-like tendrils. Each "BONG" of the bell sent waves of discomfort through the symbiote, causing it to wiggle and ripple like black pudding. Venom started shouting at the bell to "BE QUIET," which only made the situation more chaotic and attracted a crowd of concerned citizens below.

Spider-Man arrived, having heard the rhythmic clanging from three blocks away. He knew that the symbiote's weakness to high-pitched frequencies was the key to helping Eddie. "Venom! You're literally ringing your own doorbell!" Peter called out, clinging to the stone gargoyles of the tower. He didn't want to fight; he wanted to stop the noise. Peter used his web-shooters to create a "sonic muffler" by weaving a thick, sound-dampening web-nest around the giant bronze clapper of the bell. As the clanging stopped, the symbiote immediately calmed down, settling smoothly back onto Eddie’s shoulders.

Venom looked at the silenced bell, then at Spider-Man. "Quiet now," the creature rumbled, its voice dropping to a low hum. Peter landed on the ledge beside him. "The symbiote needs peace and quiet sometimes, Eddie. Maybe stay away from the percussion section for a while?" Eddie looked down at his hands, which were no longer rippling. "It was... very loud," he admitted. Spider-Man nodded, knowing that even the biggest creatures have things they’re afraid of. He stayed with Venom for a while, watching the city lights until the "WE" felt calm again.
