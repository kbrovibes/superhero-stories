The Avengers were often called to handle "Global Threats," but Captain America believed that teamwork was a skill that needed constant practice, even for small problems. One afternoon, Steve Rogers invited Spider-Man to the Avengers’ Helicarrier—a massive, flying aircraft carrier that served as their mobile headquarters. The mission was a "Search and Recovery" drill: a small, experimental drone had lost its flight-path and was lodged in the Helicarrier’s massive cloaking-fins. "It's a delicate spot, Peter," Cap said, looking over the mission maps. "We need your agility and Iron Man's technical support."

Spider-Man felt a surge of pride. He wasn't just the "kid" anymore; he was a specialist. He leaped off the flight deck, free-falling for a moment before firing a web-line that snagged a stabilization rail. "I've got eyes on the drone, Tony!" he called through his comms. Iron Man hovered nearby in his Mark 85 armor, using his repulsors to create a steady shield of air so the wind wouldn't blow Peter away. "Careful, Spidey. Those fins are made of a Vibranium-alloy. Don't scratch the paint; it's expensive," Tony joked, though his sensors were locked on Peter’s every move.

The drone was wedged deep between two shifting panels. Peter realized he couldn't reach it with his hands without the panels closing. "Tony, I need you to use a low-power heat beam to expand the outer rail by just three millimeters!" Peter shouted. Iron Man fired a precise, orange beam, and the metal groaned and shifted. Seizing the moment, Spider-Man used his "sticky" fingers to pluck the drone from the gap while simultaneously firing a web-net to catch a loose bolt that had fallen toward the engines. It was a perfect display of coordination.

Back on the flight deck, Captain America handed Peter a bottle of water. "Great work, son. You saw the solution before we did." Peter beamed under his mask. He realized that while Tony had the power and Steve had the strategy, his own unique perspective was what saved the day. Teamwork wasn't about everyone doing the same thing; it was about different people bringing their best "facts" to the table. As the Helicarrier cruised over the Atlantic, Peter Parker felt like he was finally part of a much larger, more incredible world.
