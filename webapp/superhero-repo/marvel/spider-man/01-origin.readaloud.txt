Once upon a time, in a quiet neighborhood called Forest Hills in Queens, New York, there lived a boy named Peter Parker. He was quiet and bookish, very good at science, and he lived with his kind Aunt May and his gentle Uncle Ben. Life wasn't always easy for Peter — a bully named Flash Thompson gave him a hard time — but home was full of warmth and love.

Then one day, on a school field trip to a research laboratory, something extraordinary happened. A tiny spider, touched by a particle beam, fell from the air and bit Peter on the hand. It stung for just a moment. But by the time Peter walked home that afternoon, he could feel the whole world had changed. He could see without his glasses. He could feel a strange tingling in the back of his head — a spider-sense that warned him of danger. He could climb walls with his bare hands. And he was strong. Very, very strong.

For a while, Peter thought this was his chance to be famous. He put on a costume and became "Spider-Man," winning money in a wrestling ring. But one terrible night, he let a thief run past him — and that same thief broke into the Parker home and took Uncle Ben away from him forever. And do you know what happened next? Peter tracked the man down, and the grief he felt was the heaviest thing he had ever carried.

In the silence of that night, he remembered the words Uncle Ben had always said to him: "With great power comes great responsibility." Those words became the most important promise of his life.

From that day on, Peter Parker put on the red-and-blue mask not for fame or money, but to protect the people he loved — every person in every corner of Queens and Manhattan — so that no one else would ever lose someone the way he did.
