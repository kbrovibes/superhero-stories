Are you sitting down? Good — because this story starts with the tiniest little spider you have ever heard of, and ends with the greatest superhero in all of New York City!

His name was Peter Parker, and he lived in Forest Hills, Queens — a quiet neighborhood full of tree-lined streets and friendly porches. Peter was fourteen years old, a little shy, a LOT brainy, and he lived with the two people he loved most in the whole world: his Aunt May and his Uncle Ben. Uncle Ben had a saying he repeated so often that Peter could say it in his sleep: "With great power comes great responsibility."

Peter didn't know yet just how important those words would become.

One bright afternoon, his class at Midtown High School piled onto a bus for a field trip to a science laboratory. Peter pressed his face to every single display. While the other kids fooled around, Peter was reading about particle accelerators and genetic research. Science was his superpower even before he had any superpowers!

And then — OUCH! — a tiny spider, the size of a coat button, dropped from the ceiling and bit the back of Peter's hand. This wasn't just any spider. The scientists had been experimenting with radioactive energy, and somehow that little creature had soaked up more than its fair share. Peter barely noticed at the time.

But the next morning? Oh boy. WHOOSH — he slapped his palm on the bathroom wall and it STUCK. He could climb like a spider — up walls, across ceilings, upside down above the kitchen table! He felt fast as lightning and strong enough to bend a lamppost. And tingling like electricity behind his eyes: his brand-new spider-sense, warning him of danger before danger even knew he was there.

Peter was clever enough to build his own web-shooters — little wrist devices that fired sticky silk threads strong as steel cables. THWIP! He could soar between buildings, swooping over Queens like a human pendulum. It felt like flying. It felt like magic.

But then came the hardest night of his life. Peter had let a thief slip past him, just not wanting to get involved. That same thief later found his way to Forest Hills — and took Uncle Ben away from Peter forever.

Peter caught the thief. But catching him didn't fix anything.

Standing alone in the dark of an old warehouse, the words hit him like a thunderclap. With great power comes great responsibility. He finally understood every single syllable.

From that night on, Peter Parker became the Friendly Neighborhood Spider-Man — swinging through Queens and Manhattan, protecting everyone he could, honoring Uncle Ben with every single web he fired into the sky.

THWIP! There he goes!
