Peter Parker had been swinging through Queens for years when a long black car pulled up and a very famous face leaned out of the window. Tony Stark — Iron Man himself — was looking for a young man from Queens with extraordinary gifts. And he had found exactly the right one.

Tony didn't just hand Peter a shiny new suit, though he did do that. He gave Peter the remarkable "Iron Spider" armor, a red-and-gold masterpiece of technology. But far more importantly, he gave Peter something harder to build: guidance. He sat with Peter and talked about choices. He told him about the mistakes he had made. He showed him what it meant to think about the big picture — not just the street in front of you, but the whole wide world.

For Peter, Tony became the kind of mentor that changes your whole direction. He was tough and funny and occasionally impossible, but he believed in Peter the way that only someone very wise can believe in someone very young.

When the terrible villain Thanos arrived and the world changed overnight, Tony's deepest fear was losing Peter. And for a while, he did. But when the Avengers found a way to set things right, their reunion was one of the warmest moments in all their long history together.

And do you know what happened in the end? Tony made the greatest sacrifice of all — and the world was saved. Peter was left standing in a world without his mentor, holding Tony's technology and trying to figure out how to carry on.

From that day on, Peter Parker walked a little differently. He wore the responsibility on his shoulders the way Tony had always taught him to — not as a burden, but as a promise. Tony Stark's greatest invention was never a suit of armor. It was the hero Peter Parker became.
