The day Peter Parker was invited to join the Avengers was the most exciting and most terrifying day of his young life. One moment he was the Friendly Neighborhood Spider-Man, swinging over Queens and catching purse-snatchers. The next moment he was standing in Avengers Tower, looking up at Captain America and Thor, wondering if someone had made a very big mistake.

Peter felt like the youngest person in every room — because he was. He cracked jokes when things got serious, and sometimes his teammates stared at him with slightly puzzled expressions. Tony Stark, the brilliant inventor known as Iron Man, didn't stare. He saw exactly what Peter was. "You remind me of me," Tony told him once, and that was the highest compliment Peter had ever received.

What made Peter special on the team wasn't just his spider-sense — that wonderful tingling that warned him of danger before anyone else saw it coming. It was the way he never forgot about the ordinary people below. When a battle raged on the rooftops, Peter was the one who stopped to carry a frightened child to safety. He was the bridge between the world of gods and billionaires and the everyday world of the people they protected.

The battles grew bigger. One fight even took Peter across the stars to an alien planet, far from Queens, fighting against the terrible Black Order. It was a very long way from home. But even there, Peter was still Peter — brave, quick, and ready with a well-timed joke.

And that is how a boy from Queens proved that you don't need to be a god or a billionaire to belong among Earth's Mightiest Heroes. You just need a big enough heart.
