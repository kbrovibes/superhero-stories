There are stories about battles, and there are stories about something much harder — love. This is one of the most important stories in the whole life of Peter Parker, and it begins with a smile and seven words.

"Face it, Tiger, you just hit the jackpot."

That was what Mary Jane Watson said the first time she properly met Peter. She was bright and warm and funny, the kind of person who lit up every room she walked into. And Peter, who was serious and careful and always a little worried, wasn't sure what to make of her at first. She seemed so different from him.

But Mary Jane was paying very close attention. She saw the way Peter disappeared at odd moments. She noticed the tired shadows under his eyes. She understood, before he ever told her, that the boy she was getting to know was carrying something enormous. And she chose to stay.

Their life together was not simple. Every time Peter put on the mask and swung out into the night, MJ sat with the quiet worry that he might not come home. Villains sometimes came after her. The newspapers said terrible things about Spider-Man. But she never asked Peter to stop. She knew why he did it. She understood the promise he had made to Uncle Ben, even when it cost them both dearly.

They were married on a bright, hopeful day — and when the hard times came, they faced them side by side.

And that is how Peter Parker learned that the greatest power of all has nothing to do with spider-webs or super-strength. It is the courage to love someone, and to let them love you back, even when the world keeps trying to pull you apart.
