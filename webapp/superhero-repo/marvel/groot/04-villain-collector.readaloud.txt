Now, most villains in this story want to destroy things. But today's villain? He wants to COLLECT things. Specifically — he wants to collect GROOT!

His name is Taneleer Tivan, but everyone calls him the Collector. He lives inside Knowhere — the space station built inside an enormous dead Celestial's skull — and his museum fills miles and miles of corridor. Alien weapons under glass! Exotic creatures in glowing cages! Parts of extinct civilizations, frozen in amber and crystal! The Collector is ancient and very strange and he wants every rare thing in the galaxy on a shelf in his museum.

And Groot? A living Flora Colossi tree-creature? Very, VERY rare. Almost the rarest thing there is.

So when the Guardians came to Knowhere on business, the Collector sent a polite little invitation. Rocket sniffed trouble immediately. Groot put a calm bark hand on his shoulder. "I am Groot," which meant: be cool, please, at least until we know what he wants.

They went in. The Collector greeted them in purple robes with his white hair swept back — very fancy, very creepy smile. He walked them through the exhibits slowly and proudly. A Symbiote specimen in a sealed case! A Chitauri soldier frozen in a column of blue light! A Brekk-spider wing, six feet long and iridescent! Fascinating! Unusual! And then, very smoothly, he said he'd pay ANYTHING they named. For the tree.

Now. Here is the moment that matters.

Groot looked to his left. In a cage right next to him was a tiny bird-creature — small as a child's fist, feathers pressed flat, eyes huge and full of sadness, wings folded tight as if it had forgotten what wings were for.

Groot looked at it for one long moment.

Then Groot reached out — SNAP — and broke the cage latch off like a dry autumn stick.

The bird EXPLODED upward! Wings wide! SINGING — straight up to the high vaulted ceiling of the museum, loop after glorious loop, absolutely wild with joy!

Three guards IMMEDIATELY drew weapons. Groot rose to his full tremendous height, arms spread wide, bark creaking — and put himself between those guards and every single one of his friends.

"I am Groot."

Translation: I. Am. Not. For. Sale. And neither is anyone else in this room.

Then Rocket — very helpfully — explained in precise technical detail what their weapons could do to the museum's ceiling, the floor, and the Collector's prized exhibits if he was in the mood to demonstrate. The Collector thought about it carefully.

He put the guards away.

The Guardians walked out with everything they came in with. The little bird sang from the rafters. A cage sat open and empty behind them.

You cannot collect a guardian. Some things are just too alive for any cage.
