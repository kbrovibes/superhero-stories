Okay. This one is important. Are you listening? Really listening?

The Dark Aster — Ronan's enormous warship, black and angular and the size of a small city — is going down. Falling. Crashing toward the surface of Xandar with the Guardians still inside it. There's no weapon that can fix this. There's no clever plan that Rocket can come up with in the next thirty seconds. There is a very large ship and a very fast descent and the ground coming up FAST.

Groot looks at his friends.

He looks at Star-Lord, who never gives up on anything. He looks at Gamora, who fought so hard to find people she could trust. He looks at Drax, who laughs more than he used to. And he looks at Rocket — his best friend, the only person in the universe who understands every word he says.

And then Groot grows.

He spreads his arms WIDE — CRRRREEEEAK — and his branches multiply and spread outward and upward, arching over his friends in a great rounded dome of living wood. Branch locks into branch. Twig weaves into twig. The bark thickens. His roots drive down into the ship's floor to hold it in place. He pours everything he has into every layer.

A cocoon. Made of Groot. Around ALL of them.

Rocket grabs his arm. "No — Groot — you'll —"

Groot looks at him.

"I am Groot."

Three words. Quiet. Warm. Certain as the oldest tree in the oldest forest. His eyes are soft.

Rocket understands. He always understands.

BOOM.

The crash hits. The force slams into the outside of the cocoon — SMASH SMASH SMASH — and the outer bark layers crack and splinter and break away, one after another. But the INSIDE stays. The inside HOLDS. The core, the innermost layers that Groot poured himself into, holds strong and steady around the people he loves.

When Star-Lord and the others push through the wreckage to the surface of Xandar, they are alive. All of them. Every single one. The sky is full of dust and light.

And around them, in the broken earth, are the scattered pieces of a gentle giant — bark chips and twigs and fragments of the creature who made this possible.

Rocket does not move for a while. Then he starts to pick up the pieces. Every twig. Every chip of bark. He finds a pot. He plants them.

Groot grows back. HE ALWAYS GROWS BACK — because that is the nature of Groot. The season ends, and then it begins again. That's the whole beautiful truth of it. That's who he is.

But those three words — quiet and warm and certain in the dark of a falling ship — those words stayed with everyone who heard them.

Forever.

I am Groot.
