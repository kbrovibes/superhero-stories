When the Guardians of the Galaxy intercepted a distress signal from a ship under attack, they found a half-destroyed vessel carrying two Asgardian survivors and information that changed everything: Thanos was on the move, and the Infinity Stones were his target. The signal led them, by a series of improbable events, into alliance with heroes from a small blue planet called Earth — Tony Stark, who built himself a suit of iron, and Stephen Strange, a sorcerer who carried one of the very Stones Thanos sought.

They converged on Titan — the ruined moon of Saturn, a dead world of crumbling architecture and yellow dust, which had been Thanos's original home. Strange had opened a sling-ring portal there, and suddenly the Guardians and the Iron Man team were face to face in the ruins of an ancient city, circling each other with weapons raised and questions flying in six directions at once.

Groot stood quietly in the middle of it, watching. He had seen enough encounters to know which ones were about to become fights and which ones were about to become something else. This one had the shape of the second kind.

Star-Lord and Tony Stark argued for a while, which was inevitable. Rocket explained to a man called Spider-Man, with considerable impatience, exactly what the Infinity Stones were and why obtaining all six would be catastrophic. Groot sat on a ruined column and listened to Strange's description of Thanos's plan, and felt the particular weight that deep trouble carries when it is real.

When Thanos arrived on Titan, the combined team moved as a unit with surprising effectiveness. Groot's role was structural: he anchored the team's position, created barriers and gripping vines to restrict Thanos's movement, and grew a lattice of branches across a gap in the ruins that three team members needed to cross simultaneously. When Thanos turned his focused attention on him — those terrible golden fingers, that relentless certainty — Groot held his position, roots driven deep into Titan's cracked stone floor, for long enough that it mattered.

They did not succeed that day. Thanos was too powerful, the stones too many. But every moment of resistance was a delay, and every delay was a chance.

Groot stood in the dust of Titan, watching the Earth heroes and his own team and the ruined Titan skyline, and felt the specific sadness of battles that are not yet over. He grew a small flower from his palm — just a small one, a private gesture — and then closed his fingers around it.

Some things you keep for yourself. Some things you keep for later, for when the hard season turns.
