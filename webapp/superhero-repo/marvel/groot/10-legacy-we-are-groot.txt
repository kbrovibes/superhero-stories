There is a young Flora Colossi somewhere in the galaxy who grows a little taller each day, learning the universe one day at a time. He is known to the Guardians as Groot — the same Groot, regrown from the twigs Rocket collected after the crash on Xandar, and yet somehow also new, carrying forward the essential nature of who Groot has always been: the kindness, the patience, the unshakeable steadiness under pressure.

Star-Lord once asked Rocket whether the new Groot was really the same Groot — whether all those memories, all that history, came back as he grew. Rocket's answer was sharp and immediate. "He's Groot," he said. "That's enough."

And it was. Because the thing that defines Groot is not his memories alone, but his character — the choices he makes in moments when no one has told him what to do. Across dozens of planets and a hundred dangerous situations, Groot has consistently chosen the same things: protect the people near him, be kind to small creatures, hold on when the pressure is greatest, and trust that the people he loves are worth holding on for.

He has taught the Guardians things they needed to know without ever delivering a lecture. He showed Star-Lord what steadiness looks like, on the days when Star-Lord's confidence was covering up something more uncertain. He showed Gamora what it means to give protection without demanding anything in return. He showed Drax that strength and gentleness are not opposites — that the most powerful tree in the forest is also the one the birds nest in. He showed Rocket, day after day, that he was worth the loyalty he had never expected.

The Guardians call themselves a team. What they mean is something older and more specific: they mean a family, assembled by circumstance and held together by choice. And in that family, Groot is what the oldest tree is in an old-growth forest — the presence that makes the whole system possible, the roots that hold the soil, the canopy that shelters the smaller life growing in its shade.

When the newest Groot learned to say his three words — when the small shoot became a small tree and the small tree put together the sounds that he had always known — he said them to Rocket first. And Rocket, who had been waiting a very long time without admitting it, heard them with his whole chest.

"I am Groot."

Three words. A whole universe.

We are all Groot, when we choose to be.
