Here is the last question. The most important question. The one Star-Lord asked Rocket one evening on the Milano, looking at a small green plant in a little pot in the corner of the cargo bay:

"Is that really Groot? Is it actually the SAME Groot?"

Rocket thought about this for approximately one second. One second — which, for Rocket, is quite a long time to think before answering.

"He's Groot," said Rocket. "That's enough."

And you know what? That IS enough. Because here's the great and true thing about Groot: he is not defined by which exact memories live in which exact branch. He is defined by what he DOES. By the choices he makes when nobody has told him what to choose. By the things he reaches toward — the way a green shoot reaches for light before it even has eyes to see it.

And every time — on every planet, in every fight, in every quiet moment on the Milano when something small and hurt needed a warm place to rest — Groot has chosen the same things.

PROTECT the people near you. Be KIND to every creature, no matter how small. HOLD ON when the pressure is greatest. And TRUST that the people you love are worth every single thing you have to give.

He never gave Star-Lord a speech about steadiness — he just WAS steady, day after day after day, until Star-Lord's own shoulders came down from around his ears. He never told Gamora about protection without conditions — he just wrapped his bark arms around her in every battle, no price, no negotiation. He never lectured Drax about strength-and-gentleness being the SAME thing — but Drax watched a creature who could bend steel beams carry a tiny sparrow in the palm of his hand, and he understood. And laughed more.

And Rocket — Rocket already knew. Rocket knew from day one on Xandar. He was just grateful, every day that followed, that he had been right.

The Guardians are a TEAM. But what they really are — what they REALLY are — is a family. Made by accident and kept by choice. And Groot is the oldest tree in their forest. The roots under the whole system. The canopy where the others shelter and grow.

When the new Groot said his three words to Rocket for the very first time — small and new and absolutely certain —

Rocket heard them with his entire chest.

"I am Groot."

Three words.

A whole universe.

We are ALL Groot, when we choose to be. When we are patient where it is hard. When we are kind before we are asked. When we hold on for the people we love.

You know how to be Groot. You already do.
