Okay! Do you know what's even better than a team of heroes? TWO teams of heroes, meeting for the first time, on a ruined moon, with the most powerful villain in the universe on his way!

Here's how it happens. The Guardians intercept a distress signal out in the far reaches of space. They find a ship under attack and pull out two Asgardian survivors. And from those survivors, they learn: Thanos is moving. The Infinity Stones are the target. ALL SIX. And if he gets all six — every gem of impossible power gathered into that golden Gauntlet — things will be very, very bad for everything that is alive in the galaxy.

A chain of wild, fast-moving events brings the Guardians to TITAN — the ruined moon of Saturn, Thanos's original home world, all crumbled stone towers and yellow dust and a silence that has been going on for a very long time. And waiting there in the ruins? A man in a red-and-gold iron suit named TONY STARK. And a sorcerer named DOCTOR STRANGE, who is holding one of the very Stones Thanos wants around his neck on a chain.

The two teams see each other. Weapons rise. OBVIOUSLY.

Groot sits down on a broken column and watches the whole thing. He has seen enough of these moments to know: this one is going to turn into something useful.

Sure enough — after Star-Lord and Tony Stark argue about who's in charge of what (classic), and after Rocket explains the Infinity Stones to a young hero in a red-and-blue suit called SPIDER-MAN with quite a lot of impatience — they settle down. They make a plan. They are one team now.

THANOS ARRIVES.

And they fight together — with actually impressive coordination for people who met twenty minutes ago! Groot grows vine restraints around Thanos's arms to slow him down — CREAK CREAK WHOMP — and builds a bridge of living branches across a twenty-foot gap in the ruins so three heroes can cross it at once. When Thanos turns those heavy golden-gauntlet eyes DIRECTLY on Groot?

Groot drives his roots into the cracked stone floor of Titan. GRIP GRIP GRIP. He is not moving.

He doesn't.

He holds.

In the end, Thanos is too powerful that day, and the battle is not won. But every vine grown, every barrier raised, every second he was delayed — that was a chance. A crack in the impossible. A seed in hard ground.

Groot stands in the dust of Titan when it's over. He opens his palm.

From it grows a small flower. Pale gold. The colour of afternoon light.

He closes his fingers gently around it.

Some things you save for later. For when the hard season turns.
