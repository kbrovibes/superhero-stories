Taneleer Tivan called himself the Collector. He lived inside Knowhere — the ancient mining colony in the skull of a dead Celestial — in a museum so vast and strange that its corridors seemed to go on forever. He filled it with every rare and unusual thing the galaxy could offer: alien weapons sealed in glass, exotic creatures in glowing cages, artifacts from extinct civilizations. The Collector was ancient, eccentric, and dangerous in the specific way that people who want to own everything are dangerous.

When word reached him that Groot — a living Flora Colossi, one of the rarest botanical beings in the known galaxy — was on Knowhere, the Collector became very interested. He sent his assistant, a pale and unhappy person named Carina, to deliver an invitation. The Guardians had come to Knowhere to sell an object, not to be collected. Rocket smelled the trap immediately. Groot had to put a gentle bark hand on his shoulder to keep him from saying so loudly.

They went to the Collector's museum. Tivan greeted them in purple robes, his white hair swept back, his smile warm in the way that only very cold things can be warm. He gestured at the exhibits: a Symbiote specimen, a Chitauri soldier frozen in stasis, the wing-casing of a giant Brekk-spider. And then, very casually, he mentioned that he would give them whatever price they named for the tree.

Groot looked at a creature in a cage beside him — a small avian being, feathers flat against its body, eyes wide with misery. He looked at it for a long time. Then he broke the cage. Just reached out and snapped the latch like it was a twig, and the bird-creature flew immediately for the high vaulted ceiling, singing.

Three of the Collector's guards drew weapons. Groot stood between them and his friends, arms spread, bark creaking.

"I am Groot," he said, in a voice that meant: I am not a collection piece.

The standoff ended when Rocket reminded Tivan — loudly and with considerable technical detail — exactly what their weapons were capable of. Tivan reconsidered. The Guardians left with their payment and their dignity. The bird-creature watched from the rafters as they went.

The Collector got no Flora Colossi that day. What he got, instead, was a broken cage and a reminder that some things in the galaxy cannot be owned — only known, if they choose to let you.
