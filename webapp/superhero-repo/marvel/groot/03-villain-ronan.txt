Ronan the Accuser was a Kree warlord who carried a Universal Weapon — a great stone hammer called the Cosmi-Rod — and used it to enforce his belief that the universe owed him justice. He wore black armor that covered him from boots to crown, and his eyes burned with certainty. When Ronan obtained the Power Stone, one of the six Infinity Stones, he aimed it at the planet Xandar with every intention of destroying it entirely. The Guardians of the Galaxy were the only crew close enough, reckless enough, and stubborn enough to stop him.

Ronan's warship, the Dark Aster, was enormous — a black, angular fortress that cut through the atmosphere above Xandar like a blade. The Guardians flew the Milano into its underside, and Groot punched through the hull with his bare hands, vines and branches spiraling out to tear gaps in the metal plating. Rocket had built half a dozen improvised weapons from salvaged ship parts, and he was firing all of them. Star-Lord, Gamora, and Drax fought through corridor after corridor of Ronan's Sakaaran soldiers.

They reached Ronan on the bridge. He was more powerful than any of them, the Power Stone flooding his entire body with energy that no one being should survive. He moved through the team like a storm, batting them aside with his Cosmi-Rod. It was Groot who kept returning — knocked down, cracked, splintered — and kept rising again. A tree does not stay down.

The final move was chaos. The Guardians grabbed the Power Stone together — four hands and one enormous set of bark-covered fingers — and channeled the immense energy between them. It should have been impossible. It very nearly was. Groot held on.

When it was over, Ronan was defeated and Xandar was saved. The Nova Corps — Xandar's space police force — arrived to secure the Power Stone and officially declare the Guardians allies of the planet they had just protected.

Groot stood in the rubble and watched the light of Xandar's twin suns fall across the rooftops. He reached down and picked up a single petal from a flower that had survived the crash. He held it in his bark fingers for a long moment.

"I am Groot," he said.

Rocket, who was bleeding from his left ear and completely out of ammunition, looked up. "Yeah," he said. "We did."
