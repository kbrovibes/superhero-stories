Have you ever heard a name so scary that whole PLANETS went quiet when someone said it? Well, buckle up — because that name is THANOS!

Thanos is the Mad Titan. He lives in a dark fortress called the Sanctuary and he wants ALL SIX Infinity Stones — six magical gems of unbelievable power — so he can control the entire universe. Not just one planet. Not just one solar system. Not even just one galaxy. EVERYTHING. Every world. Every creature. All of it, in his enormous golden fist.

And when his ships and his generals — the Black Order — started moving toward Xandar, the Guardians looked at their dashboard scanner, looked at each other for about two seconds, and said: let's go.

The Milano blasted through the stars toward a place called Knowhere. Can you picture it? Knowhere is a mining colony built inside the SKULL of an enormous dead ancient giant called a Celestial. The whole place echoes when you walk. The walls are made of bone. It is extremely dramatic and we absolutely love it.

BOOM — Thanos's forces hit the hull! His generals arrived: Proxima Midnight! Corvus Glaive! Ebony Maw! All three, charging through the bone corridors like a thunderstorm carrying weapons.

Groot moved through the dark passages with his roots gripping the metal floor — GRIP, GRIP, GRIP — quiet as a tree in the dead of winter. His bark absorbed every vibration. He was listening for his friends.

Then — Proxima Midnight had Gamora cornered in a narrow tunnel. Blocked. Nowhere to go.

CRRRASH! Groot burst THROUGH the wall — arms spread wide, branches everywhere — and knocked Proxima Midnight completely off her feet. She landed hard and did not get up quickly.

"Nice timing, buddy," Rocket said, already swinging into position and firing two overcharged blasters into the ceiling — BOOM BOOM — sealing the tunnel. Route blocked. Team protected.

The Guardians couldn't beat Thanos completely that day. He was too powerful. His army was too large. But they held the line. They protected each other. They covered the retreat and got every single person back to the Milano alive.

And that? That was a WIN.

As the Milano punched back into space — alarms still blinking, hull a little singed — Groot sat quietly in the back, already growing new twigs where his fingers had snapped off in the fight. Patient. Calm.

"I am Groot," he said.

Rocket looked back. "Yeah, buddy," he said. "We're still here."

Some days you can't win everything. But everyone comes home — that's everything.
