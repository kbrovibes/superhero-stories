On the planet X, deep in a galaxy of rustling forests and amber skies, there lived a creature unlike anything in the known universe. His name was Groot — a tall, ancient being made entirely of living bark, roots, and branches. His arms were wide as oak limbs. His eyes glowed with soft violet light. And though his vocabulary consisted of exactly three words — "I am Groot" — those words carried every emotion he had ever felt: warmth, curiosity, grief, and joy.

Planet X was a world of sentient flora, and Groot was one of its greatest sons. The Flora Colossi, as his people were called, were proud and powerful. Groot himself had always been different. Where the others sought status, Groot sought friendship. Where they stood rigid as oaks, he bent toward the light. He cared deeply for smaller creatures — the insects that nested in his branches, the birds that rested on his shoulders — and that gentleness made the elders of Planet X uneasy.

When Groot was still young by the long reckoning of his species, he was cast out from Planet X for a simple act of defiance: he had sheltered a small human child who had wandered into the sacred groves. The elders declared it a disgrace. Groot stepped into the stars carrying nothing but himself.

He drifted through the galaxy for what felt like ages, working odd jobs at space stations, loading cargo on transport ships, until the day a bounty hunter named Rocket found him in a cantina on the planet Xandar. Rocket was a raccoon-like creature with a mechanical frame and a mouth that never stopped moving. He had plans, schemes, and sharp elbows for every crowd. He and Groot were, by every measure, opposites. Within a week they were inseparable.

Together they joined a ragged band of misfits who became the Guardians of the Galaxy — Star-Lord, Gamora, and Drax the Destroyer. They were not the heroes anyone expected. They argued constantly. Half their plans went sideways. But when the galaxy needed saving, they showed up.

Groot held the group together the way great trees hold hillsides together: quietly, from underneath. He carried the injured. He grew walls of bark when enemies fired their weapons. He stretched his branches across impossible gaps so his friends could cross. And in every moment of danger, those three familiar words rang through the chaos — "I am Groot" — full of steadiness, full of love.

The gentle giant from Planet X had been cast out for caring too much. Among the Guardians, that was exactly what made him irreplaceable.
