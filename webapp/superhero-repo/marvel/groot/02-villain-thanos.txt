The Guardians of the Galaxy had faced dangerous enemies before — space pirates, rogue warlords, weapons dealers who traded in stolen star-drives — but none of those threats compared to the name that made entire star systems go quiet: Thanos. The Mad Titan ruled from his fortress in the Sanctuary, commanding armies across a dozen galaxies. His goal was to collect all six Infinity Stones and use their combined power to bend the universe to his will. And when his forces swept through a sector near Xandar, the Guardians flew straight toward the storm.

Groot had seen the reports on the Milano's dashboard — worlds stripped bare, space stations left silent. Thanos did not fight small. He brought the Black Order: Corvus Glaive, Proxima Midnight, and Ebony Maw, generals who attacked without hesitation. When their ships appeared on the Milano's scanner, the team barely had time to argue about the plan before the first volley of energy beams lit up the hull.

The Guardians landed on the surface of Knowhere, the ancient mining colony carved from the skull of a Celestial, hoping to outmaneuver Thanos's forces. Groot moved through the narrow tunnels of Knowhere with remarkable quiet for a creature so large, his roots gripping the metal floors, his bark absorbing the vibrations of each nearby explosion. When Proxima Midnight cornered Gamora in a side passage, it was Groot who crashed through the wall beside her, his massive arms spreading wide and knocking the general off her feet.

Rocket, perched on Groot's shoulder, fired two overcharged blasters into the ceiling to seal the tunnel. "Nice timing, buddy," he said. Groot replied — "I am Groot" — in what Rocket later described as the most satisfied voice he'd ever heard.

The Guardians could not defeat Thanos that day. He was too powerful, and his forces were too many. But they protected what they came to protect, covered their retreat, and pulled each other out when it counted. As the Milano punched back into space, alarms still blinking, Star-Lord looked around at his crew: bruised, bark-scorched, exhausted, and alive.

Groot sat in the back, quietly growing new twigs where his fingers had broken. The galaxy was still in danger. Thanos still moved through it like a storm. But the Guardians were still together, and Groot — steady as any tree that had ever bent without breaking — was still right there with them.
