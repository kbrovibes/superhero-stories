Let me tell you about the coolest spaceship in the whole galaxy. It's called the MILANO — and it's home!

The Milano belongs to Star-Lord — that's Peter Quill, the Guardian who grew up on Earth and cannot stop playing music from his cassette player. The Milano is an M-class quadrant vessel, which sounds fancy, but actually it's been patched in FOURTEEN different places, the engine fault has supposedly been fixed six times by Rocket and isn't fixed, and the whole thing smells a little bit like burnt wiring and old socks. It is WONDERFUL.

Now here's a question: where does a giant tree-creature sleep on a spaceship?

CARGO BAY! The cargo bay is the only room tall enough for Groot. He folds his branches in, tilts sideways through the doorway — CREAK — and settles onto the deck. And here's a secret: at night, Groot roots VERY lightly into the metal floor. Just barely. Just enough to draw tiny trace minerals from the hull's alloy the way trees drink through their roots.

Rocket complained about this. Then he checked the hull and found out Groot's corner is STRONGER and more corrosion-resistant than anywhere else on the ship. Groot is literally making the Milano better by sleeping on it. Rocket stopped complaining.

From the cargo bay porthole, Groot watches the galaxy go by. He's seen things, friends. AMAZING things!

Xandar's double sunset — TWO suns going down at different speeds until the sky turns every colour from amber to deep violet. A PULSAR — a star that spins and sends out a beam of light that sweeps the dark like a lighthouse above the greatest ocean you've ever imagined. The ribbon nebula of the Kyln region — a whole band of starlight in colours that don't have names!

The galaxy is enormous. The Nova Corps, the Kree Empire, the very offended golden people called the Sovereign — everyone has their corner. And between all of them float thousands of worlds and stations, including Knowhere the skull-colony and a hundred other strange places the Guardians have been.

Groot was cast out from his home planet. And yet — somehow — he found THIS. The Milano. The Guardians. Rocket four meters away working on the engine.

One night, the nebula light turned his bark purple and gold.

"I am Groot," he said.

"Yeah, buddy," said Rocket, without looking up. "I know."

Home doesn't always look like you expect. Sometimes it's a patched-up ship with a broken engine and the best friends in the galaxy.
