What happens when the most powerful psychic on Earth gets a headache? It is a VERY serious situation!

A psychic entity — something ancient and alien — had landed in the Canadian Rockies. The X-Men called it the Shadow Mind. It was broadcasting a signal on a frequency that only telepathic minds could receive — and instead of music, the signal caused hallucinations, dizziness, and collapse.

Forty-three mutants across North America went down simultaneously.

And even Professor X — the most powerful telepath on Earth — could barely think straight.

So he made a call. To Avengers Tower. To Tony Stark.

Iron Man landed in the X-Men's Blackbird bay and looked around with that sharp, assessing Tony Stark expression that meant he was already working out what needed to be done. Professor X explained the situation in seven minutes. Tony immediately proposed a solution: a sonic counter-frequency device, built with Stark Industries technology, that could JAM the Shadow Mind's signal just long enough for the X-Men to get close enough to act.

THEY WORKED THROUGH THE ENTIRE NIGHT. Professor X, Tony Stark, and Beast — who contributed a set of calculations that accelerated the whole project by SIX HOURS.

At dawn, Storm and Thor flew the sonic device to the Rockies — Storm calling winds to carry it fast, Thor using Mjolnir to keep them steady in the mountain turbulence. WHOOOOOOSH. Cyclops led the ground team through ice and altitude. And Captain America — calm, precise, efficient — coordinated everyone over the communicator.

The device deployed. The signal stopped.

Silence.

Beautiful, glorious, peaceful silence.

Professor X closed his eyes. He reached through Cerebro. He found the Shadow Mind — vast, strange, unlike anything he had ever contacted before.

And what he found there surprised everyone.

It was not angry. It was not malicious. It was — in PAIN.

The Shadow Mind had no idea it was hurting anyone. It was broadcasting distress the way a lost animal cries out — lost, afraid, no language for what was happening.

For forty-three minutes, Professor X sat in perfect stillness and listened. He reached back. He built, carefully, the beginning of something that could one day become communication.

The signal changed. From noise — to something that might, with patience and time, become a conversation.

Tony Stark watched the whole thing from behind his faceplate without moving — which, for Tony Stark, meant he was astonished.

On the flight home: "I build things that fix problems. You just figured out this wasn't a problem."

Charles replied: "You built the device that gave me the silence to listen. That was essential."

Tony nodded. They understood each other perfectly.

THAT is what happens when the most powerful mind on Earth teams up with the most powerful engineer on Earth.

Not an explosion. A conversation.
