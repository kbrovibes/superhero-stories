Okay — imagine the biggest, most unstoppable thing you can picture. Something that smashes through walls like they are made of cardboard. Something that keeps coming no matter what.

That is the Juggernaut. And here is the twist — he is Professor X's stepbrother!

His real name is Cain Marko, and Cain always resented Charles. He resented his brilliant mind. He resented his calm. So when Cain was on a military mission in Korea and stumbled upon an ancient glowing gem called the Crimson Gem of Cyttorak — and touched it — the power inside it turned him into something terrifying: a living juggernaut, coated in magic armor, impossible to stop by any force on Earth.

And the first thing he did with that power? He pointed it straight at the Xavier mansion.

BOOM. BOOM. BOOM.

Two miles away, the X-Men felt the ground shake. Every student was evacuated to the sublevels in minutes — Cyclops directing traffic in the hallways, Colossus carrying three kids at a time down the stairs. EVACUATE, EVACUATE, EVACUATE!

Professor X watched on the monitors, very still, very focused.

Juggernaut wore a metal dome helmet that completely sealed his mind from telepathy. Charles could not reach in and simply say "stop." He had to think his way through this.

Jean Grey stretched out her telekinesis with everything she had — and managed to hold Juggernaut in place for a whole NINETY SECONDS before the magic overwhelmed her. Beast tried to rip the helmet off. Cyclops piled rubble in the path. The whole team worked together — brave, brilliant, precise.

But Juggernaut kept. coming.

Professor X watched. He thought. And then — quietly — he smiled.

He had noticed something. Cain was marching in a perfectly straight line. He was relying entirely on his helmet to protect his mind. He was not planning at all. He was just — charging, full of anger, not thinking about what came next.

Charles knew Cain. He had known him his whole life.

Anger had made his stepbrother careless.

"Lead him underground," Charles said, calm as a professor explaining a math problem.

The X-Men ran. Juggernaut followed. Into the tunnels, where the ceiling was low and the walls were close. Beast waited — and at exactly the right moment — KA-KRUNNCH — down came thirty tons of reinforced concrete ceiling, right on top of Juggernaut.

The helmet cracked.

Professor X reached in — gently, just for a moment — and suggested: rest now.

The giant went still.

Charles sat in the quiet afterward, and thought about Cain as a boy. Before the Gem. Before all of this.

It was a heavy kind of quiet. But the mansion was safe. His students were safe.

And that was what mattered most.
