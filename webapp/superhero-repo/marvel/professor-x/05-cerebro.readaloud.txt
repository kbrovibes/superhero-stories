Deep, deep beneath the Xavier School — past the gymnasium, past the med bay, past the hallway of student photos — there is a door made of reinforced titanium. And behind that door is the most remarkable machine in the whole world.

It is called Cerebro.

Professor X built it with a brilliant mutant inventor named Forge. Eighteen months of work — late nights, early mornings, hundreds of soldered circuits, dozens of aligned quantum amplifiers. It looked like a chrome helmet sitting in the center of a spherical room forty feet across, wired up to everything.

When Professor X puts that helmet on — everything changes.

You know how far his telepathy reaches normally? About one mile. Enough to sense nearby minds, to communicate, to keep track of who's around. Pretty amazing!

But WITH Cerebro? His mind stretches out across the WHOLE PLANET. Every mutant mind on Earth appears to him at once, like a sky full of stars. He can feel a teenager in São Paulo whose hands have just gone magnetic. He can hear a child in Cape Town thinking, frightened and confused, whose skin is turning blue. He can find them and know them and start planning how to reach them before they have to face the world alone.

WHOOOOOOSH. That is what a planet's worth of minds sounds like when they all arrive at once.

Charles once described it as standing in a river made of thought — a billion minds flowing past at the same time. He can only stay in Cerebro for about forty minutes before the pressure becomes too much.

Now — the Hellfire Club. A sneaky group of villains who decided to use a telepathic disruptor to scramble Cerebro's signal during a very important moment: young mutants were trapped in a government facility in Nevada and needed to be found.

The disruptor hit. KRRZZZZT. Static! Like trying to read in a spinning room. Cold and confusing and very hard to push through.

Professor X held on.

He narrowed. World... state... county... county... building. One building. One by one, he found the minds inside it. One by one, he mapped their exact locations.

Then he opened his eyes and said calmly to Cyclops: "Building four, sublevel two, cells seven through twelve. Take the team now."

Cyclops was gone in sixty seconds. Every single young mutant was rescued within the hour.

Later, Forge offered to add shielding to the helmet to stop future disruptions.

Charles shook his head.

More shielding would shrink the range. And a smaller range might mean missing one child somewhere in the world who needed to be found.

He would rather take the risk himself.

That is Professor X. The most powerful mind on Earth — and also the most stubborn heart.
