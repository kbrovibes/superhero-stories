• Kitty Pryde arrives at the Xavier School at age 13 after accidentally phasing through walls in front of her class
• Professor X spends an hour talking with Kitty about science fiction, hockey, and computers before discussing her powers — she leaves feeling heard, not assessed
• He writes in his notes: she will teach the others as much as they teach her
• In her first year, Kitty improves Cerebro's algorithms, teaches hockey, and masters precise control of her phasing
• She asks the best questions: not "what can my power do?" but "when should I use it?"
• The Xavier School is not a hero factory — it is a community; graduates become X-Men, scientists, doctors, teachers
• The school is living proof that Professor X's dream is real — not theory but a building with lights on
• His most important lesson: "Helping others costs something, and is worth it — especially when it's hard"
