Hela is the Goddess of Death and one of the most powerful beings Thor has ever faced. She is ancient and possesses a strength that rivals even Odin’s. Hela wears a dark, multi-pointed crown and can create weapons out of thin air, which she uses with terrifying skill. She believes that she is the rightful ruler of Asgard and that her power should be used to conquer other worlds, a philosophy that is the exact opposite of everything Thor stands for.

When Hela returned to Asgard, she proved to be a formidable challenge for Thor. She was so strong that she even managed to damage Mjolnir, leaving Thor without his favorite weapon. This was a turning point for Thor, as he had to realize that his power didn't just come from his hammer; it came from within himself. He had to learn how to channel his lightning and his strength without any tools, becoming a true God of Thunder.

The battle for Asgard was a massive struggle that involved all of Thor’s friends and allies. Hela was relentless, using her magic to create an army and her incredible durability to shrug off almost any attack. Thor had to make the difficult decision to put the safety of his people above the survival of the city itself. He realized that "Asgard is not a place, it’s a people," and he focused his efforts on saving every citizen.

By standing up to Hela and finding his true power, Thor showed that a leader’s greatest strength is their dedication to their people. Even though Hela was a massive challenge, Thor’s courage and his willingness to sacrifice everything for his home allowed him to prevail. This encounter made Thor a wiser and more powerful hero, ready to face any threat that might come his way in the future.
