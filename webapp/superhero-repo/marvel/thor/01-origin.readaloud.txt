Picture this: a golden palace floating among the stars, where thunder rolls across the sky every single morning. That palace is Asgard, the greatest of the Nine Realms — and living inside it was a prince named Thor Odinson who was absolutely convinced he was the best at everything.

And you know what? He kind of was! Thor had golden hair, a hammer called Mjolnir that weighed 42 pounds — but ONLY if you were worthy enough to lift it. To anyone else, it was as heavy as a mountain. He adventured with his friends Sif and the Warriors Three, and he laughed at danger like it was a little tickle.

But Thor had one very big problem: his head was full of pride and very short on listening.

One day — ignoring every rule his father Odin had set — Thor marched his friends into Jotunheim, the frozen home of the Frost Giants. BOOM! CRACKLE! Ice shattered. Giants roared. Thor thought he was being heroic. Odin knew he had nearly started the worst war in a thousand years.

Odin did not yell. He was much scarier than that. He reached out, and with a single touch — WHOOSH — pulled every drop of power out of Thor's body. Gone. Just like that. Then he placed an enchantment on Mjolnir: "Whosoever holds this hammer, if they be worthy, shall possess the power of Thor." And then... he threw his son out of Asgard.

Thor fell from the sky and landed — THUD — in the desert of New Mexico. No powers. No hammer. No crown. Just a confused prince sitting in the dust.

A scientist named Jane Foster found him there. She studied the stars and was absolutely brilliant. She helped this strange man figure out how Earth worked. Thor had never needed help from anyone before. Can you imagine how weird that felt?

Then the Destroyer arrived — a massive, clanging metal warrior sent by someone who wanted Thor gone for good. CRASH! It blasted buildings. Everyone ran.

Except Thor. He walked straight toward it with his arms open wide and said: "Leave them alone. Take me."

He wasn't powerful. He wasn't special. He was just willing.

And do you know what happened next? KRAKOOM — Mjolnir shot through the sky like a comet and slapped right into Thor's outstretched hand. His power roared back. He smashed the Destroyer into rubble and every single person in that town went home safely.

Worthiness isn't about muscles or hammers or golden palaces. It's about what you're willing to do for someone else. And Thor finally figured that out — right there in the New Mexico desert.
