• Thor joins the Avengers as a guardian of Midgard (Earth) — not as a ruler but as a protector of a world he learned to love during his exile
• He brings the team unprecedented power: control of the weather, flight, lightning, and near-immortal strength
• Thor's most important contribution: cosmic context — he understands the Chitauri, the Infinity Stones, and the ancient forces threatening reality
• His formal Shakespearean speech and unfamiliarity with modern human culture create warmth and humor within the team
• Deep bonds form with Steve Rogers and Tony Stark; Thor views the Avengers as his new Warriors Three
• Thor is the team's "heavy hitter" — called upon to level a battlefield, stop a falling city, or face the most dangerous threats alone
• His near-immortality makes him acutely aware of the fragility of his mortal teammates; he takes the most dangerous hits to protect them
• After the Blip, Thor's grief is the most profound on the team — he lost his family, his home, and half his people; his teammates help him find his way back
