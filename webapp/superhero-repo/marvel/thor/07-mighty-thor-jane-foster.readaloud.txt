Sometimes the most important stories are about the people who choose to be brave when they have every reason not to be.

Jane Foster was a scientist — a brilliant astrophysicist who had once helped a confused prince from Asgard learn how to be a person. She had always been quietly extraordinary. But she had been given a hard piece of news: she was very ill, and the illness was not going away.

Then, from far across the universe, the shattered pieces of Mjolnir began to stir. The old enchantment — "Whosoever holds this hammer, if they be worthy, shall possess the power of Thor" — had not faded. The hammer sensed something in Jane Foster that met every word of that promise.

The pieces flew together. The hammer was whole. And Jane was transformed.

She became the Mighty Thor — golden hair streaming, lightning in her eyes, Mjolnir spinning in her grip. She faced enemies that would have made lesser warriors turn and run. She used her sharp scientific mind to swing the hammer in ways that surprised even the most seasoned Asgardian warriors.

But every time she transformed, the magic swept the medicine from her body. Every act of heroism cost her something she could not get back. She knew this. She kept going anyway.

And do you know what happened next? Thor watched her. And the man who had carried the name of God of Thunder for centuries stood very still and understood, perhaps for the first time, what it truly meant to be worthy.

Jane made one final choice — to save the gods from a terrible villain called Gorr the God Butcher. She used the last of her strength, and she was gone.

From that day on, the name of Jane Foster was spoken in Valhalla and in every hall of Asgard — the Mighty Thor who chose others before herself, right to the very end.
