Here is something amazing about Mjolnir: even in pieces, it was still listening.

After Hela shattered it, the broken shards of the hammer landed in the ruins of old Asgard — and they stayed there, scattered in the grass, waiting. The enchantment Odin had placed on it long ago was still alive: "Whosoever holds this hammer, if they be worthy, shall possess the power of Thor." The hammer didn't care that it was broken. An enchantment like that doesn't give up.

And across the universe, a woman named Jane Foster felt a pull. A tug. Like the hammer was calling her name.

Jane Foster was a brilliant astrophysicist — a scientist who studied the stars — and she was facing the hardest news of her life. She was very ill, with a sickness that wasn't going away. She was scared. She could have stayed home. She chose instead to follow the pull.

When she reached out her hand toward those broken shards — WHOOOM! CRASH! KLINK! — the pieces flew through the air from every direction and assembled themselves around her. The hammer was whole. And Jane Foster was transformed.

Golden light poured over her. Mjolnir settled in her grip like it belonged there. She became the Mighty Thor — and she looked absolutely magnificent.

Here is the tricky part. Every time Jane lifted Mjolnir and became Thor, the magic of the hammer swept the medicine from her body. The transformation healed her in that moment — but afterward, the illness came back stronger. Are you ready for this? The very thing that made her powerful was making her sick. And she knew it. She knew it every single time she picked up that hammer.

She picked it up anyway. Because she believed the world needed a Thor more than it needed her to be safe.

She used Mjolnir in ways nobody had ever thought of — with a scientist's sharp mind, she discovered new tricks, new angles, new possibilities. Even Thor watched with his mouth open in amazement. He had carried that hammer for centuries. He had never swung it quite like that.

In the final battle against a very scary villain named Gorr the God Butcher, Jane made her last choice. She used every bit of strength she had left to destroy Gorr's terrible weapon, the Necrosword, by driving Mjolnir into it with all the lightning she could call. KRAKOOM! The Necrosword shattered into pieces.

Jane's transformation faded. The illness came back all at once. She held Thor's hand and smiled.

And then she was carried up — in golden light — to Valhalla, the great hall where Asgard's bravest heroes rest forever.

She never called herself a hero. The hammer called her worthy, and she simply agreed.
