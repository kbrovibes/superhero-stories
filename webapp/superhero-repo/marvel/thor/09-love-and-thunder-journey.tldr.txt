• After the battle against Thanos, Thor travels the universe with the Guardians of the Galaxy on a journey of self-discovery
• He sheds his grief-worn persona, regains his strength, and searches for a new identity beyond war and kingship
• The era of Love and Thunder: Thor seeks to understand what kind of hero he still wants to be
• He is reunited with Jane Foster — now the Mighty Thor — and they face a new enemy together: Gorr the God Butcher
• Gorr is a man consumed by grief who believes gods are selfish and indifferent to mortal suffering; he seeks to destroy all gods
• The conflict takes Thor to Omnipotence City (hall of the gods) and to the dark shadowed realm of Eternity
• In the final battle, Thor chooses empathy over rage — he understands Gorr's grief and refuses the cycle of violence
• Thor brings Gorr to Eternity, where any wish can be granted; Gorr wishes for his daughter Love to be resurrected instead of killing gods
• Gorr dies asking Thor to protect Love; Thor becomes her guardian — traveling the stars together as father and daughter
