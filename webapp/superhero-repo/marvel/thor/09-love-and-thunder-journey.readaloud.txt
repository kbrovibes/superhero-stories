After so much loss and so many battles, Thor needed to do something he had never done before: wander. He traveled the stars with the Guardians of the Galaxy, seeing the universe not as a king or a soldier, but simply as himself. It was good for him. He grew stronger again — in his body and in his heart.

Then he heard about Gorr the God Butcher. Gorr was a man who had loved deeply, and lost everything, and decided that all gods were to blame. He carried a terrible weapon called the Necrosword and traveled from world to world, spreading darkness and grief. He wanted to reach a place called Eternity — the one place in the universe where any wish could be granted — and use it to end all gods everywhere.

The quest took Thor to Omnipotence City, a grand and dazzling hall where gods from every corner of the universe gathered. It took him to the dark, shadowed realm where Gorr held children hostage to bring Thor to him. And along the way, it brought him back to Jane Foster — now the Mighty Thor — the woman he had never quite stopped loving.

They fought Gorr together. It was a long, hard road.

And do you know what happened at the very end? Thor stood before Gorr, and instead of swinging his axe, he listened. He heard the grief in this man who had lost his daughter, Love. He understood it. He chose not to fight, but to feel.

He brought Gorr to Eternity. And Gorr — in his last moments — chose love over destruction. He wished his daughter back to life.

As Gorr faded away, he looked at Thor and said one thing: "Take care of her."

From that day on, Thor traveled the stars with a little girl named Love at his side, teaching her everything he knew about thunder, kindness, and why both matter equally.
