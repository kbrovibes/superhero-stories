What do you do after you've saved the world, lost your home, lost your brother, and sat alone for five years being sad? If you are Thor Odinson, you go on the most spectacular road trip in the universe.

He traveled with the Guardians of the Galaxy — a ragtag crew of space adventurers who argued constantly but loved each other fiercely. Rocket Raccoon, Groot, Star-Lord — bouncing from planet to planet, seeing sights no Earth person had ever dreamed of. Thor laughed again. He remembered who he was.

Then a name reached him: Gorr the God Butcher.

Gorr had started as an ordinary man who prayed his whole life to his gods for help. When his daughter Love died and his gods offered only cruelty, something inside Gorr broke. He found a weapon called the Necrosword — an all-black blade that could slay any god — and set out to end all gods everywhere. He was heading for a place called Eternity, where any wish could be granted. His wish? End every god there ever was.

This quest took Thor to Omnipotence City — a shining hall where gods from every civilization in the universe met. Egyptian gods, Mayan gods, Greek gods, and — this is true, I promise — even Bao, the god of dumplings! Zeus himself held court. It was enormous and dazzling and very, very loud.

But none of those gods would help. So Thor went to face Gorr himself, alongside Valkyrie and a very familiar face: Jane Foster, now the Mighty Thor, Mjolnir spinning in her hand.

Are you ready for this next part? In the final moments, Thor stood before Gorr — exhausted and grief-worn — and instead of swinging Stormbreaker, Thor listened. He heard the pain of a man who had loved his daughter more than anything.

Gorr stood at the threshold of Eternity, where any wish was possible. He could have ended all gods.

Instead, he whispered: "I want Love back."

That was his real wish, underneath all the anger. He wished his daughter back to life — and Eternity granted it.

Gorr faded away, and with his last breath he looked at Thor: "Take care of her."

And so Thor — God of Thunder, Avenger, former prince of Asgard — became a dad. He travels the stars now with a little girl named Love at his side, teaching her about lightning and kindness, and why the two of them always go together.
