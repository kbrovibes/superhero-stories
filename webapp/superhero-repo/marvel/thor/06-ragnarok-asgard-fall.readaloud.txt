Every story has an ending written into its very beginning. The old Asgardian prophecies had one name for the ending of their golden realm: Ragnarok.

Thor had known about the prophecy all his life. He had spent years traveling the universe, trying to find a way to prevent it. But some things cannot be prevented — only understood.

When Hela came and shattered Mjolnir and drove Thor to the faraway world of Sakaar, it seemed as if everything was lost. He had no hammer. He had no throne. His beloved home was in the hands of the Goddess of Death.

On Sakaar, something remarkable happened. Thor sat very still and thought about who he really was. And he remembered something his father Odin had tried to tell him: the storm was inside him. The lightning was not a gift from the hammer — it was a part of him that the hammer had merely helped him focus. He reached inward, and the storm answered.

He went back to Asgard crackling with lightning, powerful and determined. But Hela grew stronger every moment she stood on Asgardian soil. Thor could hit her with everything he had and she would simply rise again.

So he made the hardest decision of his long life.

He called upon Surtur — the ancient fire demon of the prophecy — and gave him the power to rise. And Surtur's enormous blazing sword came down, and Asgard burned.

Thor stood on a ship sailing through the stars, watching the flames behind him. His people stood with him, alive and safe and whole. His home was ash.

From that day on, Thor understood what every great king must learn: the place you live is not your kingdom. The people you love are.
