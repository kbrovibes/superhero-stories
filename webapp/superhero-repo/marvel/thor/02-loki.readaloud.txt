Have you ever had a best friend who was also your complete opposite? Thor and Loki were EXACTLY like that — and their story is the most complicated, most heartbreaking, most wonderful friendship in all the Nine Realms.

Thor was LOUD. Thor ran straight at things. Thor hit things with a hammer. BOOM. Problem solved.

Loki was quiet. Loki thought sideways. Loki could change his face to look like anyone — a bird, a fish, a giant, even Thor himself! He could walk through a room and nobody would notice him until he'd already moved every single thing in it. He was the God of Mischief, and oh, was he good at it.

They grew up together in Asgard, sons of King Odin and Queen Frigga. They ran through golden hallways, practiced sword fighting, and drove the palace guards absolutely crazy. For a long time, they were brothers in every way that counted.

But then Loki found out something that shattered his world like a hammer through ice. He was not Asgardian. He was the son of Laufey, king of the Frost Giants — the enormous, icy, intimidating creatures of Jotunheim. Odin had found baby Loki, tiny and abandoned, and brought him home. Are you ready for this? Frost Giants can grow up to twenty-five feet tall — and little Loki had never grown that big. Laufey had hidden him away because he was different. And Odin had kept this secret for years and years and years.

The loneliness Loki had always felt suddenly had a reason. And that loneliness turned into something darker.

He tricked Thor into exile. He tried to claim Odin's throne. He led a whole army of alien Chitauri warriors in an attack on New York City — his way of screaming: "I am worth noticing!" But it only pushed people further away.

And through all of it, Thor kept reaching out his hand. Every time. Because Thor knew the mischievous, brilliant boy underneath all those tricks — and he never stopped believing that boy was still in there.

In the darkest moment, when the terrible Thanos came, Loki stepped forward all alone. He tried to stop him. It was the bravest, most foolish, most Loki thing he ever did. And just like that — he was gone.

Thor carries his brother everywhere now. The God of Thunder and the God of Mischief — two brothers who were nothing alike and could not have been more the same. Thunder and mischief. Two sides of the same golden coin.
