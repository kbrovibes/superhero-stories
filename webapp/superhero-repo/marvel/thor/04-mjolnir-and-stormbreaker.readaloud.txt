Are you ready to hear about the two most incredible weapons in the entire universe? Good. Buckle up.

First: Mjolnir. This hammer was forged in the great workshops of Nidavellir — where master craftsmen called dwarves used the blazing power of a DYING STAR as their furnace. The metal they used was called Uru, and Uru soaks up magic like a sponge. The hammer took seventeen weeks to complete, and when it was done, King Odin placed the most famous enchantment in the universe on it: only the worthy could lift it.

Can you imagine? The most powerful warriors in all the realms couldn't pick it up. But the right person came along — and it practically LEAPT into their hand! WHOOSH!

Mjolnir channeled the storm. When Thor spun it by its leather strap and hurled it, it moved like lightning — because it WAS lightning. And it always came back. Always.

Then Hela grabbed it out of the air and crushed it with one hand. Crumble. Done. Gone.

Thor had to find a new weapon. So he traveled back to Nidavellir, where a dying star was slowly going out, with Rocket Raccoon and the gentle, wooden giant Groot.

They found Eitri, the king of the dwarves — the last survivor of his people. Thanos had come before them and taken everything. But Eitri still remembered how to forge a king's weapon. He chose the most powerful Uru mold and called it Stormbreaker.

Here was the problem: the star's furnace had gone cold. To restart it, someone had to hold the giant iris of the star open with their bare hands — and let the full heat of a dying star pour over them.

Thor stepped forward. ZAP! KRAKOOM! The heat hit him like nothing he had ever felt. His friends screamed his name. He did not let go. He held on until the Uru poured into the mold and the axe began to take shape.

But there was no handle. And then — do you know what happened? Groot quietly reached his long wooden arm into the molten metal, shaped it into a grip, and then cut his own arm free to complete the weapon.

"I am Groot," he said softly.

Stormbreaker was born from two kinds of courage: a god who endured the impossible, and a friend who gave what he could spare.

When Thor arrived at the great plains of Wakanda, the weapon singing with energy in his hand, even Thanos — the most feared being in the universe — had reason to worry.
