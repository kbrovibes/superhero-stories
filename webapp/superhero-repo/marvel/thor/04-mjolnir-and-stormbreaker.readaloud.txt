For thousands of years, Thor carried a hammer. Its name was Mjolnir, and it was forged in the heart of a dying star by the master craftsmen of Nidavellir, from a mystical metal called Uru. But Mjolnir was not just a weapon — it was a promise. The enchantment on it was clear and simple: only the worthy could lift it. And when it returned to Thor's hand, it always felt like coming home.

Then Hela crushed it into a thousand pieces with one hand, and it was gone.

Thor was left with his own power — which was real and great — but without the weapon that had been his companion through centuries. To face the evil Thanos, who threatened all life everywhere, he needed something new. He needed something that could wound a titan.

So Thor traveled to Nidavellir — the great forge at the edge of a dying star — with his new friends Rocket Raccoon and the gentle giant Groot. They found Eitri, the dwarf king who had forged Mjolnir long ago. Thanos had visited Nidavellir before them, and Eitri was the only one left. But his knowledge of the forge was still there, and his hands still remembered their craft.

To melt the Uru, the star's forge had to open. Thor held the iris open himself, standing in the full heat of a dying star, enduring heat that would have destroyed anyone less. The Uru flowed. The axe took shape.

But it still had no handle. And do you know what happened next? Groot — sweet, quiet Groot — reached his wooden arm into the molten metal without hesitation and shaped it into a grip. Then he cut his own arm free to complete it.

Stormbreaker was born from a god's endurance and a friend's love. And when Thor arrived at Wakanda, cracking with lightning, it was the only weapon that Thanos had reason to fear.
