Okay, friends. Lean in close, because this story has a SECRET — a secret so big that all of Asgard had been keeping it for centuries. Ready?

Thor had a sister.

Her name was Hela, and she was the Goddess of Death — Odin's firstborn, the most dangerous warrior in the history of the Nine Realms. She once rode a wolf the size of a house into battle. She had been erased from every painting, every history book, every story told in Asgard's golden halls. She had been locked away in a prison far, far away.

But prisons only work as long as someone keeps them sealed. And when Odin finally passed from the world — WHOOOOOM — the seal broke.

Hela burst back into existence like a storm breaking through a door. She was wearing a helmet made of antlers, and her eyes glowed with power. She reached out and caught Mjolnir — Thor's beloved hammer, forged in the heart of a dying star — right in her bare hand. CRACK! She squeezed. The hammer shattered into a thousand pieces.

Thor stared. In his whole long life, nobody had ever destroyed Mjolnir. Nobody.

She flung Thor and Loki away and headed straight for Asgard. Do you know what was waiting for her there? She had the power to bring back her old undead army from beneath the throne room floor — and she did. RUMBLE! Skeletal warriors marched through the golden streets. And her giant wolf Fenris — the enormous creature who had been her companion for centuries — rose again beside her.

Meanwhile, Thor landed on the junkyard planet Sakaar, powerless and hammer-less. But something happened to him there. He sat quietly and heard his father's voice in his memory: "Are you the God of Hammers?" And just like that — KRAKOOM! — lightning erupted from Thor's hands. His fists. His whole body. The storm was INSIDE him all along. Mjolnir had just been helping him find it!

But even crackling with raw lightning, Thor couldn't beat Hela as long as she stood on Asgardian soil. She drew strength from the very land. Every hit, she got back up. So Thor made the most painful choice a king ever makes.

He let Asgard go.

He sent Loki to find Surtur — the ancient fire giant of legend, whose enormous flaming sword was destined to destroy Asgard in the great prophecy called Ragnarok. BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! Surtur's blade came down, and the golden realm burned bright as a star.

Hela fell with it.

And Thor sailed away on a ship packed with his people, watching the fire behind him. He had lost his home. But Asgard — the real Asgard — was right there on that ship, breathing and alive.

"Asgard is not a place," he said quietly. "It's a people."

And he meant every single word.
