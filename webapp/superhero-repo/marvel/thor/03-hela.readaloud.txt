There are secrets that entire kingdoms keep buried. This is the story of the secret that Asgard had kept for centuries — and of the day it came back.

Before Thor and Loki were born, Odin had another child: Hela, the Goddess of Death. She was fierce and brilliant and hungrier for conquest than anyone in the Nine Realms. She fought at Odin's side as he built his empire. But when Odin chose peace, Hela refused to stop. A great battle followed, and Odin imprisoned her in a dark and distant place, sealing it with his own life force.

When Odin finally died, that seal broke.

Hela burst back into the world with terrible power. She reached out one hand and caught Mjolnir in mid-flight — and crushed it into pieces. Thor stared at the fragments of the hammer he had carried his whole life. Hela barely blinked.

She sent Thor and Loki tumbling away to the faraway junkyard planet Sakaar, and turned her attention to Asgard. Her undead army rose from old graves. Her great wolf Fenris prowled the streets. The golden city trembled.

On Sakaar, Thor closed his eyes and thought about what his father had always told him. His power did not live in the hammer. It lived inside him. He reached for the storm — the real storm, the one that had always been part of who he was — and lightning poured through him like a river.

He went back to Asgard. But he quickly understood: as long as Hela was connected to that land, she could not be beaten. So Thor made the choice that only a true king could make.

He let Asgard burn.

The fire demon Surtur rose and brought the end the old prophecies had foretold. And on a ship sailing away from the flames, Thor stood with his people — alive and free.

From that day on, Thor knew: Asgard was never the golden towers. Asgard was the people sailing beside him.
