Did you know that Asgard used to come to Earth long before superheroes? Thousands of years ago, the Asgardians visited a small town in Norway called Tønsberg — one of the oldest towns on Earth, built by actual Vikings who told stories about thunder gods and wrote them on their walls. The Asgardians even left the glowing blue Tesseract hidden there for centuries. Earth and Asgard have always been connected.

So when the Asgardian survivors needed a new home, Tønsberg felt right. And that is how New Asgard was born.

It was quite different from what came before. No golden towers. No Bifrost bridge. No enormous palace. Just a quiet coastal village where fishing boats bobbed in a cold Norwegian harbor and the neighbors wore very regular Earth clothes. The Asgardians adjusted. They put on sweaters. They learned about grocery stores. A few of them started really enjoying Norwegian waffles.

And one very funny thing: someone opened an ice cream shop and named it Infinity Conez — with a giant replica of Thanos's Infinity Gauntlet holding an ice cream cone above the door. Can you imagine?! Even in sad times, Asgardians found a way to make people smile.

Valkyrie ran things. Brave, fierce, no-nonsense Valkyrie — who had once ridden a winged horse into battle — now ran meetings and fixed problems and made sure thousands of Asgardian refugees had what they needed. She was extraordinary at it. She was exactly the kind of leader New Asgard needed.

Thor was not doing so well.

He had lost Loki. He had lost Heimdall. He had lost half his people. He had held the forge of a dying star open with his own hands and arrived at Wakanda crackling with power — and it still hadn't been enough. The grief sat on him like a boulder.

He stayed inside. He didn't answer the door. He let his hair grow long and tangled. A god who had once called storms across three realms now mostly stayed on his couch.

But New Asgard grew around him anyway. Children played. People rebuilt. Tourists arrived from all over the world to see the most remarkable little village on the planet — actual Norse gods, living in a Norwegian fishing town, sharing the harbor with the local fishing boats. Humans and Asgardians became neighbors. Became friends.

Slowly — the way spring arrives slowly after the longest winter — Thor heard the life around him and decided to be part of it again.

He looked at Valkyrie and understood with perfect clarity: she was already the king. She had been all along.

He passed her the crown and walked back out into the world. New Asgard had given him something Asgard never could — the knowledge that starting over isn't giving up. It's how you keep going.
