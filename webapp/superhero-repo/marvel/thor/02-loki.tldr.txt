• Thor and Loki are raised together in Asgard: Thor is a man of action and raw power; Loki is a master of magic and illusion
• Loki discovers he is not Asgardian — he is the son of Frost Giant king Laufey, adopted by Odin — a truth that shatters his identity
• Loki's resentment at living in Thor's shadow turns to deep, calculating hatred; he becomes the God of Mischief
• Loki orchestrates Thor's exile to Earth, attempts to seize Odin's throne, and later leads an alien army in an invasion of New York City
• Thor never gives up on his brother, always believing in a path to redemption — they are forced to work together against Hela and later against Thanos
• Brief moments of true brotherhood surface during their darkest times, reminding them of the children they once were
• Loki sacrifices his own life trying to stop Thanos — a futile but genuinely brave act; the ultimate confirmation he was a son of Odin
• Thor carries Loki's memory forever; the brothers remain inseparable parts of the Asgardian legend — thunder and mischief, two sides of the same coin
