Loki is the God of Mischief and Thor’s younger brother. While Thor is known for his strength and directness, Loki is known for his cleverness and his ability to create illusions. Loki can change his appearance, make himself invisible, and trick people into seeing things that aren't there. Although they grew up together as brothers, Loki often felt like he was in Thor’s shadow, which led him to play many tricks and sometimes even cause serious trouble.

Loki’s mischief often comes from his desire for attention and his belief that he should be the one to rule. In one famous scheme, Loki used his magic to make Thor believe that an army of giants was attacking, hoping to lead Thor into a trap. Loki enjoys using words and secrets to turn people against each other, making him a very difficult opponent to fight. You never know if the Loki you are talking to is the real one or just an illusion.

Despite Loki’s many tricks, Thor still cares for his brother and always hopes that Loki will choose to be a hero. Their relationship is complicated, as they often find themselves on opposite sides of a conflict. Thor has learned to be very patient and to look past Loki’s illusions to find the truth. He knows that Loki is capable of great things if he uses his brilliant mind for good instead of just for mischief.

Their battles are as much about wit as they are about power. Thor has to use his instincts to stay one step ahead of Loki’s clever plans. Even when Loki causes chaos, Thor tries to show him that being part of a family is more important than having power. The story of Thor and Loki is a reminder that even brothers can be very different, and that true strength is found in being honest and fair.
