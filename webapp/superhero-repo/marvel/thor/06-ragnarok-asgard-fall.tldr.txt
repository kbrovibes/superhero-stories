• The prophecy of Ragnarok — the destruction of Asgard in fire and shadow — has haunted the realm for eons; Thor spends years searching for its source
• With Odin's death and Hela's return, Ragnarok becomes inevitable; Hela is nearly invincible while connected to Asgard's power
• Thor loses his hammer to Hela; he later loses his eye in battle against her undead legions
• Thor reaches within himself and discovers the storm: he becomes a living bolt of lightning — his power was never in Mjolnir
• He realizes he cannot defeat Hela by fighting her — he can only win by destroying the thing she draws power from
• The key insight: "Asgard is not a place; it's a people" — the people matter more than the land
• Thor orchestrates the resurrection of Surtur, the fire demon of the ancient prophecy, whose enormous flaming blade destroys Asgard entirely
• Hela falls as the realm burns; Thor escapes on a refugee ship carrying the surviving Asgardians — a king who chose his people over his home
• Ragnarok is both an ending and a beginning: the old empire burns, and something new can now grow
