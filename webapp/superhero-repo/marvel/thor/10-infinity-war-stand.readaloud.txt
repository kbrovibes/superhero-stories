Let's talk about the most important throw in the history of the universe.

It started with darkness. Thanos — the Mad Titan — attacked the ship carrying the last Asgardian survivors. He was collecting the six Infinity Stones and did not care what stood in his way. Loki and Heimdall were lost. Thor was thrown into the cold empty vacuum of space, tumbling, alone, apparently finished.

The Guardians of the Galaxy found him floating there — barely breathing, but alive. Rocket poked him. Thor opened his eye.

And from that moment, a single purpose filled Thor like lightning fills a storm cloud: stop Thanos.

To do that, he needed a weapon powerful enough to hurt a Titan wielding all six Infinity Stones. No such weapon existed — except one. Eitri the dwarf king of Nidavellir could forge it. Stormbreaker.

Thor traveled to Nidavellir with Rocket Raccoon and Groot. The forge was dark — Thanos had shut it down. To restart it, someone had to hold the dying star's iris open by hand — standing in the full, unshielded blast of a collapsing star.

Thor held on. ZAP! KRAKOOM! The energy scorched him. His friends screamed. He held on until the Uru poured and Stormbreaker took shape. Groot sacrificed his own arm to make the handle. "I am Groot," he said simply.

Thor arrived at Wakanda — where the armies of Earth and Asgard stood against Thanos's forces — descending in a column of Bifrost light that split the sky like a knife. KRAKOOM! Even Thanos, wielding the complete Infinity Gauntlet with all six stones, could not stop what was coming.

Thor drove Stormbreaker deep into Thanos's chest. He had done it.

And then he made one mistake. One moment of anger, one word spoken — and Thanos used that moment to close his fist.

SNAP.

Half of everything that had ever lived... disappeared. For five years Thor sat in New Asgard and carried that weight.

But then came the Time Heist — a wild plan to travel back through time. Thor went back to Asgard, to the days when his mother Frigga was still alive. She found him sitting alone, looking lost, and said the thing only a mother can say: you are not your failures. You are what you do next.

Thor stood up straight. Old Mjolnir — Mjolnir from the past — flew to his hand and hummed with recognition.

He was still worthy.

He went back. He stood shoulder to shoulder with Captain America and Iron Man, the three of them side by side at the end of the world.

And do you know what? That was enough.
