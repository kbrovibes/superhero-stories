Thor has wielded two of the most famous and powerful weapons in the universe: Mjolnir and Stormbreaker. Mjolnir is a magical hammer forged in the heart of a dying star by the legendary blacksmiths of Nidavellir. It is made of a unique metal called Uru and carries a special enchantment from Odin: "Whosoever holds this hammer, if he be worthy, shall possess the power of Thor." This means that only someone with a pure heart and noble intent can even lift it.

Mjolnir allows Thor to fly by swinging it at incredible speeds and letting go, pulling him through the air. It also helps him focus his lightning, allowing him to strike with the precision of a bolt of electricity. When Thor throws Mjolnir, it always returns to his hand, no matter how far it has traveled. For many years, Mjolnir was not just a tool for Thor; it was a symbol of his worthiness and his connection to his father’s legacy.

Later in his journey, Thor worked with Eitri the Dwarf to create a new weapon called Stormbreaker. Stormbreaker is a massive battle-axe that is even more powerful than Mjolnir. It has the ability to summon the Bifrost, the rainbow bridge that allows Thor to travel instantly across the Nine Realms. It is a "kings' weapon," designed to be the ultimate tool for a protector of the universe. Stormbreaker can cut through almost anything and can even resist the power of the strongest cosmic forces.

Whether he is using the hammer or the axe, Thor treats his weapons with great respect. He knows that they are tools to be used for protection and justice. The stories of Mjolnir and Stormbreaker remind us that a hero's equipment is an extension of their character. Just as the weapons are strong and enduring, so is Thor’s commitment to keeping the realms safe from harm.
