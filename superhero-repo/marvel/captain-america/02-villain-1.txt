One sunny afternoon in New York City, a high-pitched cackle echoed through the skyscrapers. The Green Goblin was zooming around on his high-tech glider, but he wasn't looking for a fight. Instead, he was being a total prankster! He was dropping "pumpkin bombs" that didn't explode with fire, but instead popped open to release hundreds of colorful rubber ducks and sticky green slime all over the park. People were surprised and a bit annoyed as the park became a slippery, duck-filled mess.

Captain America was nearby and saw the mischief unfolding. He knew that even though these were just pranks, they were causing chaos and making people feel unsafe. Using his incredible tactical mind, Cap calculated the Goblin's flight path. He waited for the right moment and then threw his Vibranium shield. The shield didn't hit the Goblin; instead, it bounced off a flagpole and a brick wall, precisely knocking the bag of "prank bombs" right out of the Goblin's hands before they could be dropped.

The Green Goblin looped around, surprised that his fun had been interrupted. Cap stood his ground, looking serious but fair. "Mischief might seem like fun, but when it starts bothering people and making a mess of our city, it's not a joke anymore," Cap said firmly. He explained that a person with such amazing technology like the Goblin's glider should use it to help others, rather than just playing annoying tricks that could cause someone to trip or get hurt.

The Goblin stayed silent for a moment, looking at the duck-covered park below. Seeing Captain America’s unwavering sense of justice and fairness, he decided to call it a day. He zoomed off, leaving the ducks behind. Cap didn't just walk away; he stayed behind to help the park rangers clean up the slime and collect the rubber ducks. He showed everyone that a true leader doesn't just stop the problem—they stay to help make things right again.
