Gamma radiation is the invisible force that created both the Hulk and many of his greatest allies and enemies. In the world of science, gamma rays are a real form of high-energy light that comes from space and radioactive materials. In the Marvel universe, however, gamma radiation has special properties that can interact with human biology in amazing and sometimes unpredictable ways. It is a source of nearly infinite energy that can change a person's physical form.

When Bruce Banner was caught in the gamma blast, the energy bonded with his cells on a molecular level. This created a biological reactor inside him. Normally, this much energy would be dangerous, but Bruce’s unique body found a way to store it. This stored energy is what fuels his transformation into the Hulk. It explains why the Hulk can grow so large and become so heavy in just a matter of seconds—he is literally drawing on a well of pure energy.

The transformation itself is triggered by the body’s "fight or flight" response. When Bruce feels a surge of adrenaline, the gamma energy is released, rearranging his bones and muscles into the Hulk's form. This process also gives the Hulk his incredible durability. His skin becomes dense enough to stop most things, and his body can heal from almost any injury incredibly fast. This is all thanks to the constant flow of gamma energy through his system.

Bruce Banner continues to study gamma radiation to this day. He wants to understand how to use it safely to help provide clean energy for the whole world. While it gave him a life he never expected, he also knows it is a powerful tool that requires great responsibility. Understanding the science behind the Hulk helps us see that he isn't magic; he is a marvel of biological engineering and the power of the atom.
