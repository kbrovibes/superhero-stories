In the dark, quiet alleys near the Sanctum Sanctorum, a large, shadowy figure was seen rummaging through recycling bins and making scary, low growling noises. People in the neighborhood were frightened of the creature known as Venom, a black alien symbiote who looked like a giant, toothy version of Spider-Man. However, the wise Doctor Strange sensed that Venom wasn't actually angry or mean; he was just very lost, lonely, and incredibly hungry for his absolute favorite snack: crispy tater tots and sweet chocolate.

Strange approached the creature slowly and calmly, holding out a hand to show he meant no harm at all. Venom hissed and bared his teeth, but the friendly Cloak of Levitation playfully patted the symbiote on the shoulder, which made it pause in complete surprise. Strange used the magical Eye of Agamotto to peek into Venom's thoughts and saw that the symbiote was simply overwhelmed by the loud sirens and bright lights of the busy city. He wasn't a monster; he was just a visitor from another world who didn't know the human rules yet.

Using his powerful magic, Strange created a special "Quiet Zone" around the alley, instantly dampening the sounds of traffic. He then conjured a small, glowing portal directly to a local diner’s kitchen, bringing back a large plate of warm, golden tater tots. Venom’s large, white eyes widened with joy, and he quickly began snacking, his scary grumbles turning into a happy sound that was almost like a cat's purr. The misunderstood creature finally felt safe and understood.

Doctor Strange explained to Venom that he could stay in a secret, peaceful garden within the Sanctum where it was always quiet. Wong even agreed to bring him his favorite snacks every single day as long as he stayed out of trouble and didn't scare the neighbors. Venom realized he didn't have to be scary to get what he needed, and New York was safe once again from the "monster" who just wanted a kind friend and a warm meal.
