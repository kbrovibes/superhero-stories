One bright afternoon in the bustling city of Wakanda, a strange green shadow streaked across the sky. It was the Green Goblin, but today, he wasn't looking for a serious battle. Instead, he was in a particularly mischievous mood, carrying a bag full of "Glitter-Goo Bombs." He zipped through the Golden City on his hover-glider, laughing loudly as he dropped sticky, sparkling messes onto the clean streets and shiny statues. "A little color for the King!" he shouted, narrowly missing a group of surprised tourists.

King T'Challa quickly donned his Black Panther suit. He knew that the Goblin was just looking for attention, but the pranks were becoming a nuisance and could potentially damage Shuri’s sensitive lab equipment. T'Challa tracked the Goblin’s erratic flight path using his suit's advanced sensors. He didn't want to hurt the trickster, just stop the chaos. "Your games end here, Norman," T'Challa projected his voice through his mask. He signaled the Dora Milaje to clear the area so no one would get stuck in the glittery goo.

Using his incredible agility, the Black Panther climbed to the top of the highest spire and waited for the perfect moment. As the Goblin zoomed past, T'Challa leaped into the air, his Vibranium claws catching the edge of the glider. Instead of crashing it, he used his suit's energy-absorbing tech to stabilize the craft and steer it toward a soft landing in a nearby park. The Goblin, surprised by the sudden lack of speed, finally hopped off.

T'Challa didn't scold him harshly; instead, he offered the Goblin a chance to use his engineering skills for something productive—like helping Shuri design new, less-messy fireworks for the upcoming festival. The Goblin, realizing his pranks were no match for the King's patience, agreed to behave for the rest of his visit. It was a victory for diplomacy over conflict, proving that even a mischievous foe can be guided toward a better path with the right leadership. Wakanda remained shining and clean, with just a little extra sparkle in the park.
