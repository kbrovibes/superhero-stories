Deep in the outskirts of Wakanda, a dark, swirling figure was spotted near the Great Mound. It was Venom, the massive symbiote-powered creature. He looked scary with his big white eyes and sharp teeth, but T'Challa noticed something unusual—Venom wasn't attacking anyone. Instead, he seemed to be hiding, looking confused and hungry. The creature was actually searching for a place where he could be safe from the loud noises and bright lights of the city, which often made him feel overwhelmed and grumpy.

The Black Panther approached Venom slowly, keeping his hands visible to show he wasn't a threat. "You seem lost, traveler," T'Challa said calmly. Venom hissed softly, but he didn't strike. T'Challa realized that the symbiote was reacting to the intense vibrations coming from the nearby Vibranium mines. To a creature sensitive to sound and vibration, the mines must have sounded like a loud, crashing thunderstorm. T'Challa knew he had to help the misunderstood giant find some peace and quiet.

T'Challa used his Kimoyo beads to communicate with Shuri. He asked her to create a temporary "silence field" around a secluded cave in the mountains. He then guided Venom toward the cave, using gentle gestures and low-frequency hums from his suit to mask the mining noises. Once inside the quiet space, Venom visibly relaxed, his dark form becoming less jagged and more smooth.

T'Challa left a supply of nutrient-rich snacks for the symbiote, realizing that even the most frightening-looking beings sometimes just need a bit of understanding and a quiet place to rest. Venom gave a small, appreciative nod, proving that he wasn't a monster, just a visitor who had lost his way. The King of Wakanda showed that true strength includes the ability to empathize with those who are different, ensuring that even a creature like Venom could find a moment of sanctuary in his kingdom.
