Deep in the city's library, a strange, gooey shadow was moving between the bookshelves. It was Venom, a large, black, swirling creature made of a friendly but very confused "symbiote." Venom wasn't trying to be mean; he was just very hungry and looking for his favorite snack: extra-dark chocolate! Because he was so big and gooey, he was accidentally knocking over all the books.

Ant-Man arrived and saw the mess. He knew that if he tried to fight Venom, it might just make the creature more nervous. Instead, Scott shrunk down to the size of a crumb and began to speak through his helmet's speakers. "Hey there, big guy! You seem a little lost," Ant-Man said in a calm, friendly voice. Venom tilted his large head, trying to find where the tiny voice was coming from.

Scott guided a team of ants to carry small pieces of a chocolate bar he had in his pocket. To Venom, it looked like the chocolate was walking across the floor! The creature was delighted and carefully followed the trail of treats out of the library and toward a nearby candy shop. Ant-Man grew back to his regular size and explained to Venom that libraries are for quiet reading, not for gooey snacks.

Venom felt a bit embarrassed about the mess he made, so he used his stretchy, gooey arms to help Ant-Man put all the books back on the shelves in record time. They became fast friends, and Venom promised to always check the map before looking for snacks next time. Ant-Man learned that even the scariest-looking creatures sometimes just need a little guidance and a sweet treat.
