When the Avengers needed to go on a very special mission to save the world, they knew they couldn't do it without Ant-Man and his miniature technology. This mission was known as the "Time Heist," and it required the team to travel through the Quantum Realm. Because Scott Lang had spent so much time in the Quantum Realm, he was the expert the team needed to navigate the tiny pathways of time and space.

Ant-Man’s contribution was vital to the success of the mission. He used his Pym Particles to help the other Avengers shrink down so they could enter the Quantum Tunnel. While the bigger heroes handled the heavy lifting, Scott used his tiny size to sneak into places and retrieve important items without being noticed. His ability to hide in plain sight made him the perfect secret weapon for the team’s most delicate operations.

During the heist, Scott also used his engineering skills to help Tony Stark and Bruce Banner build the equipment needed for the mission. He showed them how to use the Pym Particles safely and how to navigate the unpredictable energy of the Quantum Realm. Even though he was often the smallest person in the room, his ideas and his courage were as big as anyone else's. The Avengers learned that having a tiny teammate could be a huge advantage.

The success of the heist proved that every member of the Avengers is important, no matter how their powers work. Ant-Man’s miniature tech and his brave spirit helped save the day and bring everyone back home. Scott felt proud to be an Avenger and to use his unique gifts for such a grand cause. It was a reminder that even a small hero can play a massive part in the greatest adventures imaginable.
