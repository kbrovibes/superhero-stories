Norman Osborn was the brilliant but eccentric CEO of Oscorp, a massive technology company located in a gleaming skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan. After a laboratory experiment with a performance-enhancing formula went slightly wrong, Norman developed a mischievous alter-ego: the Green Goblin. He traded his business suit for a scaly purple-and-green costume and a frightening, but mostly just grumpy, goblin mask. His greatest invention was the Goblin Glider, a vertical-thrust flight platform shaped like a giant bat, capable of zooming between skyscrapers at incredible speeds.

One afternoon, the Green Goblin decided to "redecorate" the Daily Bugle building, where Peter Parker worked as a freelance photographer. The Goblin wasn't looking to hurt anyone; he just thought the building needed more color. He swooped in on his glider, his jet-boots leaving trails of orange smoke in the sky. He began tossing "Pumpkin Bombs"—hollow, sphere-shaped gadgets that, instead of exploding, released hundreds of tiny, mechanical clockwork toys that scurried across the pavement, making loud "HONK HONK" noises and startling the pigeons.

Spider-Man was already on the scene, having seen the orange smoke from his apartment in Forest Hills. He swung across 34th Street, his red-and-blue suit a blur against the glass windows. "Hey, Norman! I think J. Jonah Jameson is grumpy enough without the clockwork ducks!" Peter shouted, landing on the wing of the Goblin Glider. He noticed the glider’s stabilizer was slightly out of alignment—a technical detail his science-brain couldn't ignore. Using a precise "impact web" shot, he gummed up the glider’s intake, forcing it to descend slowly toward a soft landing on a flat-top delivery truck.

The Goblin jumped off the truck, looking frustrated as his mechanical toys began to run out of batteries. Peter landed beside him, offering a small screwdriver from his utility belt. "You know, Oscorp makes the best batteries in the world. Maybe you should focus on that instead of noisy ducks?" Norman looked at the toy in his hand and sighed. "They were quite loud, weren't they?" Spider-Man gave him a friendly pat on the shoulder. "Extremely. Let's get these cleaned up before the editor sees them."
