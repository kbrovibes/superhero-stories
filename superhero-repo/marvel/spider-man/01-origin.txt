Peter Parker was a quiet, brilliant teenager living in a modest Queens household with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben. Since his parents had died years prior, his aunt and uncle were his entire world. Though Peter was a genius in the classroom, he was often lonely, spending his afternoons tucked away in his bedroom tinkering with old electronics.

The turning point occurred during a class trip to a cutting-edge laboratory. While Peter was marveling at a demonstration of radioactive particle accelerators, a common house spider drifted through a glowing beam of energy. Irradiated and dying, the arachnid landed on Peter’s hand and delivered a frantic, stinging bite. The young student barely noticed the tiny wound, but his biology was already changing.

The next morning, Peter’s world was literally upside down. He woke up clinging to his bedroom ceiling, his vision sharp and his muscles rippling with newfound power. He discovered he possessed the proportional strength of a spider and a "spider-sense" that buzzed at the back of his skull whenever danger lurked nearby. Suddenly, the shy bookworm was the most powerful person in the room.

Eager to help his family with their bills, Peter crafted a makeshift disguise using an old sweatshirt and a mask to hide his identity. He entered a professional wrestling tournament, easily defeating a champion named Crusher Hogan. Drunk on his own success and newfound fame, Peter became arrogant. He ignored a security guard’s plea to stop a fleeing thief, simply because he didn't feel like helping.

That selfish choice led to a tragedy that haunted him forever. That same thief later broke into the Parker home, and in the struggle, Uncle Ben was killed. Heartbroken and filled with regret, Peter finally understood his uncle’s mantra: "With great power, there must also come great responsibility." He traded his dummy costume for iconic red-and-blue spandex, becoming New York’s friendly neighborhood protector.
